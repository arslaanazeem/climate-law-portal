Presided by: Justice (R) Nasir Saeed Sheikh
This complaint was initiated by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located at 2-KM, Raiwind Manga Road, Lahore. The complaint, filed on 15th July 2019, seeks action against the Respondent for alleged violations of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (as amended) (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, particularly concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into the environment. The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate upon the matter.
The crux of the Complainant's case rested upon the persistent failure of M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited to manage and treat its industrial wastewater in compliance with the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, the EPA alleged that during multiple routine and surprise inspections conducted between January 2019 and June 2019, the Respondent's effluent discharge points were found to be releasing wastewater containing pollutants significantly exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS. The primary parameters identified as non-compliant included Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. The Complainant further contended that despite numerous show-cause notices issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, and repeated directives to install, upgrade, or adequately operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the Respondent had failed to demonstrate consistent compliance, thereby posing a continuous threat to public health and the environment by polluting land and water bodies. The Complainant sought punitive action, including fines and directives for immediate compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included certified copies of inspection reports dated 18th January 2019, 15th March 2019, and 10th June 2019, detailing the observations of EPA field teams regarding the operational status of the Respondent's ETP and the visual quality of the discharged effluent. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory for effluent samples collected on these dates, which consistently showed parameters such as BOD ranging from 150-250 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD from 400-650 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), and TSS from 200-350 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), with pH variations outside the acceptable range of 6-9. Photographic evidence of the discharge point and the surrounding affected area was also submitted. The Complainant further produced copies of show-cause notices issued on 25th January 2019, 20th March 2019, and 18th June 2019, along with the Respondent's evasive or non-committal replies. Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, also appeared as a witness, affirming the contents of the inspection and lab reports. In response, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel and Production Manager, Mr. Tariq Khan, contested the allegations. They argued that the ETP was fully functional and that any exceedances were temporary due to unforeseen operational glitches or fluctuations in raw material input, which were promptly rectified. They also questioned the methodology of sample collection and the accuracy of the EPA's laboratory analyses, though no independent corroborating lab reports were submitted by the Respondent. The Respondent claimed to have invested significantly in environmental compliance and presented internal maintenance records for the ETP, which, however, did not
conclusively demonstrate consistent compliance with the NEQS, nor did they offer a credible or independently verifiable explanation for the repeated and significant exceedances of pollution parameters observed by the Complainant.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments put forth by both parties and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented. It is evident that the Complainant has presented a strong and consistent body of evidence, including multiple inspection reports, certified laboratory
analysis reports, **and photographic evidence, all of which consistently point towards the Respondent's persistent failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. The laboratory reports, in particular, provide irrefutable scientific evidence of the significant and repeated exceedances of critical parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS), far beyond the permissible limits. These findings are strongly corroborated by the EPA's inspection reports and the visual observations of the field teams regarding the ETP's operational status and effluent quality.**
**The Tribunal finds the Respondent's defence, primarily relying on claims of "unforeseen operational glitches" and questioning the EPA's methodology without providing any independent expert analysis or counter-reports, to be unsubstantiated and lacking in credibility. While internal maintenance records were presented, they do not negate the objective and consistent evidence of non-compliance as established by the EPA's scientific testing over several months. The Respondent has failed to offer a credible or independently verifiable explanation for the repeated and significant exceedances of pollution parameters.**
**It is thus established beyond doubt that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has consistently failed to adequately operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and has discharged untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into the environment, thereby violating the provisions of Section 11 and Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The persistent nature of these violations, despite repeated show-cause notices and directives from the Environmental Protection Agency, demonstrates a clear disregard for environmental regulations and poses a continuous threat to public health and the environment by polluting land and water bodies.**
**Therefore, having found M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited guilty of the alleged violations, the Tribunal hereby issues the following final order and penalty:**
1.  **Immediate Compliance Directive:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and ensure that all discharged wastewater strictly complies with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at all times, effective immediately.
2.  **ETP Upgrade/Optimization:** The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order, submit a comprehensive plan for the upgrade, optimization, or replacement of its existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure consistent and verifiable compliance with NEQS. The implementation of the approved plan shall be completed within a further period of ninety (90) days thereafter, subject to regular monitoring and approval by the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore.
3.  **Environmental Penalty:** In exercise of the powers vested under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal imposes an environmental penalty (fine) of **PKR 3,000,000/- (Rupees Three Million only)** on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for the continuous and persistent violations of environmental laws and the disregard of previous directives. This penalty shall be paid into the designated account of the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
4.  **Compliance Reporting:** The Respondent

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **03 November, 2023**.
