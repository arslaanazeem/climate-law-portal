This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials. The complaint, lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeks redress for alleged violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with statutory requirements. The Tribunal, vested with jurisdiction under Section 22 of the Act, has carefully considered the submissions and evidence presented by both parties to determine the extent of environmental non-compliance, if any, and to issue an appropriate order for the protection of the environment.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent had been consistently violating various environmental laws and regulations. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated/Partially Treated Industrial Effluent:** It was alleged that the Respondent's industrial facility was discharging effluent into the municipal sewerage system, and subsequently into a natural water body, whose parameters consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, particularly concerning Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels.
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding NEQS:** The Complainant contended that emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks and other process vents, particularly particulate matter and sulfur dioxide (SO2), were found to be above the prescribed NEQS limits during routine monitoring exercises.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste:** The EPA alleged that the Respondent failed to properly segregate, store, and dispose of hazardous waste generated from its manufacturing processes, in contravention of the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and the Act. Concerns were raised regarding the potential for soil and groundwater contamination due to inadequate handling.
4.  **Failure to Obtain/Renew Environmental Approval:** It was further alleged that the Respondent had undertaken certain expansions or modifications to its plant without obtaining the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC) from the EPA, as mandated by Section 12 of the Act, thereby operating without due regulatory clearance for specific operational aspects.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, dating from May 2019 to March 2020, detailing observations of environmental non-compliance at the Respondent's facility. These reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis results of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points, confirming exceedances of NEQS for various parameters. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports from stack emissions were submitted, indicating violations of NEQS for air pollutants. Photographic evidence depicting improper waste storage practices and notices of violation issued to the Respondent by the EPA were also placed on record. The Complainant's witnesses, including the Assistant Director and environmental analysts, testified
as to the accuracy of the reports and the persistent nature of the violations observed during their visits. They highlighted the Respondent's failure to take adequate remedial measures despite repeated warnings and notices, emphasizing the potential long-term environmental damage and public health risks posed by the continued non-compliance.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
