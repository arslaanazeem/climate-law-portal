### Judgment
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 7/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, a prominent industrial enterprise, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), and its associated rules and regulations. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized of the matter, exercising its jurisdiction to adjudicate environmental complaints and enforce compliance with environmental laws. The core of the complaint revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to adhere to prescribed environmental standards, specifically regarding the discharge of industrial effluents and emissions, thereby causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and the environment.
The Complainant, representing the provincial EPA, submitted that M/s Ghani Glass Limited, operating its manufacturing facility at [Fictional Address: Sheikhupura Road, Lahore], has consistently operated in contravention of environmental laws. Specifically, the allegations include:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent:** It is alleged that the Respondent's facility discharges industrial wastewater containing pollutants such as Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and heavy metals (e.g., lead, chromium) that exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents.
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding NEQS:** The Com
plainant further alleged that the Respondent's industrial processes release particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and other hazardous air pollutants into the atmosphere at levels significantly above the NEQS for industrial emissions, contributing to ambient air pollution.
3.  **Improper Solid Waste Management:** Allegations also included the improper disposal of hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste generated during the manufacturing process, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination due to inadequate segregation, storage, and treatment practices.
4.  **Failure to Obtain/Update Environmental Approvals:** It was further contended that the Respondent either failed to obtain the necessary No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) for new installations or expansions, or failed to submit Environmental Impact Assessments (EIAs) as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and subsequent regulations, or failed to comply with conditions stipulated in previously granted NOCs, demonstrating a pattern of disregard for regulatory requirements.
The Complain
plainant, therefore, prayed for strict action against the Respondent, including the imposition of appropriate penalties and directives for immediate compliance with environmental laws and standards, to prevent further degradation of the environment and safeguard public health.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was submitted that the Respondent operates its facility in accordance with internationally recognized environmental management practices

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
