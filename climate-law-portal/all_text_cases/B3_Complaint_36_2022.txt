**JUDGMENT**

This complaint was filed on 27 February, 2022 before this Tribunal, which exercises jurisdiction over environmental offences committed within the province of Punjab under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The matter pertains to violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by Reliance Weaving Mills Limited, a Textile & Garments facility located at Lahore. This is a repeat violation by the Respondent, having previously been found in breach of environmental standards, which aggravates the present offence. The Environmental Protection Order (EPO) bearing No. EPA/EPO/2022/036 was issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and the requisite compliance period has since expired without compliance being achieved.

**I. COMPLAINANT'S ALLEGATIONS**

The Complainant alleged that the Respondent is bypassing its installed effluent treatment system and discharging partially treated or untreated wastewater containing high concentrations of organic pollutants, suspended solids and toxic dyes into the local storm water drain, in brazen violation of NEQS (SRO 742(I)/2000).

Specifically, the Complainant's allegations are as follows:

(a) The Respondent's facility has been found discharging/emitting pollutants in excess of NEQS limits in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997;

(b) The Respondent has failed to comply with Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2022/036 issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 within the stipulated compliance period;

(c) The Respondent has not taken adequate remedial and preventive measures despite multiple EPA inspection reports documenting the violations.

**II. EVIDENCE ON RECORD**

**1. EPA Inspection Reports**
   Inspection reports dated 03/03/2022 and 21/07/2022 prepared by authorized Environmental Officers of the Punjab EPA confirming NEQS violations at the Respondent's facility.

**2. Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2022/036**
   EPO issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 directing the Respondent to cease violations and achieve NEQS compliance within the specified period.

**3. Laboratory Analysis Reports**
   Certified laboratory test results (Ref. No. NEQS-LAB/2022/0036) from a PEPA-certified laboratory confirming BOD5, COD, TSS and pH exceedances in the Respondent's final effluent discharge.

**4. Photographic Evidence**
   Photographs and video footage recorded during EPA inspection confirming the observed violations.

**5. Non-Compliance Report**
   Report confirming the Respondent's failure to achieve compliance within the period stipulated in EPO No. EPA/EPO/2022/036.

**III. RESPONDENT'S DEFENSE**

The Respondent's legal representative Mr. Farooq Ahmad Khan submitted that his client had proactively engaged with the Punjab EPA, had submitted a compliance plan, and had commenced installation of upgraded pollution control equipment. The measurements upon which the complaint was based were disputed as reflecting unrepresentative transient conditions rather than routine operations.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, Reliance Weaving Mills Limited, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, Reliance Weaving Mills Limited, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,100,000/- (Rupees Three Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 620,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order, including ensuring full operationalization of the Effluent Treatment Plant / pollution control system, and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months commencing from the date of this order, incorporating certified laboratory test results from a NEQS-certified laboratory.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply with the directions in paragraphs 4 and 5 above within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for a period of one (1) year and to report findings to this Tribunal. The EPA is authorized to seal the facility in the event of continued non-compliance.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear the costs of this proceedings including the costs of laboratory analysis and EPA inspection, assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
