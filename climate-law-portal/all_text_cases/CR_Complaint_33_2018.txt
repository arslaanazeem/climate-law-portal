The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, presided over by Mr
. Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal, having heard the learned counsel for the complainant and the respondent, meticulously reviewed the evidence presented and the submissions made by both parties.
Upon careful consideration of the expert reports, site inspection findings, and the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (as amended), and the rules framed thereunder, this Tribunal finds that M/s Nishat Mills Limited (the Respondent) has failed to adequately comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) in respect of its industrial effluent discharge and has not implemented the necessary measures to control air emissions as mandated by law. The persistent non-compliance, despite repeated notices from the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, demonstrates a clear dereliction of environmental responsibility.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the aforementioned findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is declared to be in violation of Section 12 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with relevant NEQS for industrial effluent and air emissions.
2.  **Environmental Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent shall, within ninety (90) days from the date of this order, install and operationalize an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and upgrade its Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to ensure full compliance with the NEQS. A detailed action plan for these measures shall be submitted to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within fifteen (15) days.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports, including laboratory analyses of treated effluent and air emissions, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of one (1) year following the operationalization of the ETP and upgraded APCDs.
5.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
6.  **Default Clause:** In case of failure to comply with any part of this order, the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is authorized to initiate further legal action, including but not limited to sealing of the industrial unit or imposition of further penalties, as per law.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **09 April, 2022**.
