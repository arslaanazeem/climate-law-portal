**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 1/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Complaint was instituted before this Tribunal on 15th January 2022, asserting the Tribunal’s jurisdiction under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters concerning environmental pollution and impose penalties for non-compliance with environmental laws. The core of the dispute revolves around the Respondent's alleged discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the prescribed NEQS, thereby causing environmental degradation and public nuisance.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies, without conforming to the NEQS for industrial effluents. Specifically, the allegations included: (a) persistent discharge of effluent with high levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values outside the permissible range; (b) failure to operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively or, in some instances, its complete bypass; (c) non-compliance with Environmental Protection Order (EPO) No. EPA/EPO/2021/456 dated 10th November 2021, which directed the Respondent to ensure NEQS compliance; and (d) neglect in submitting regular self-monitoring reports as required under environmental regulations. The Complainant emphasized that these violations posed a significant threat to aquatic life, public health, and the overall ecological balance of the area.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented several pieces of evidence. Key among these were multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 25th October 2021, 1st December 2021, and 5th January 2022. These reports detailed visual observations of the Respondent’s discharge point, noting the effluent’s dark coloration and pungent odor, indicative of untreated industrial waste. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA Environmental Laboratory, corresponding to samples collected during these inspections. These reports consistently showed exceedances of NEQS for industrial effluents: BOD levels were reported as high as 350 mg/L (against NEQS of 80 mg/L), COD at 800 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), TSS at 280 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and pH values ranging from 4
(acidic) and 10 (alkaline) on different occasions, both significantly outside the permissible NEQS range of 6-9. The Complainant further highlighted the Respondent’s failure to respond adequately to the EPO issued in November 2021, which mandated corrective actions and compliance within a stipulated timeframe, indicating a deliberate disregard for environmental directives.

the Respondent, through its legal counsel, initially contested the allegations, primarily arguing that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was operational and designed to meet the NEQS. The Respondent contended that any reported exceedances were either isolated incidents due to temporary operational glitches or attributable to sampling inaccuracies. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
