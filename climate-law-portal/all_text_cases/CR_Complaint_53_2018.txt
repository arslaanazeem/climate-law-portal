**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 53/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials and related products. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations.
The Complainant contended that the Respondent was operating its industrial facility located at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Lahore, in contravention of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Effluents. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which eventually found its way into water bodies, causing significant environmental pollution. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent was not properly managing its hazardous waste, disposing of it in an environmentally unsound manner without obtaining the requisite licenses or approvals from the EPA, as mandated by the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003. These actions, it was argued, posed a serious threat to public health and the environment.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of site inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15.03.2018, 02.05.2018, and 18.07.2018. These reports consistently highlighted the discharge of effluent from the Respondent's premises. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15.03.2018 and 18.07.2018. These reports, prepared by the EPA's accredited laboratory, indicated that the industrial effluent consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS parameters for Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and certain heavy metals (specifically lead and chromium). Photographic and video evidence depicting the nature of the effluent discharge and the alleged improper disposal of solid and hazardous waste in an open area adjacent to the factory premises were also presented. The Complainant further placed on record the show cause notices issued to the Respondent on 22.03.2018 and 25.07.2018, demanding an explanation for the non-compliance, and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies thereto.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, vehemently denied the allegations. Through its authorized representative and legal counsel, the Respondent argued that it is a responsible corporate entity with a strong commitment to environmental stewardship. It claimed to have an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and stated that all efforts were being made to comply with environmental regulations. The Respondent presented its internal environmental monitoring reports, which, contradictorily, often showed compliance or marginal exceedances, attributing any discrepancies to sampling methodologies or transient operational issues. It also submitted evidence of substantial investments made in upgrading its environmental infrastructure, including the ETP, and claimed to have initiated the process for obtaining necessary permits for hazardous waste management. The Respondent further contended that some of the waste depicted in the Complainant's evidence might not be attributable solely to its operations or could be non-hazardous general waste. It requested the Tribunal to consider its ongoing efforts towards achieving full compliance and to exercise leniency.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Key provisions directly relevant to the allegations include:
*   **Section 11 (Prohibition of certain discharges or emissions):** This section prohibits any person from discharging or emitting any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount, concentration, or level that is in excess of the NEQS, or from discharging any untreated effluent or waste.
*   **Section 14 (Handling of hazardous substances):** This section mandates that no person shall import, manufacture, transport, store, process, use, collect, or discharge any hazardous substance except under a license issued by the Agency and in accordance with the conditions of such license.
*   **Section 17 (Powers of the Tribunal):** This section empowers the Tribunal to hear complaints and impose penalties for violations of the Act and its rules.
*   **Section 20 (Penalties):** This section prescribes penalties for contravention or failure to comply with the provisions of the Act, rules, regulations, or conditions of a license.
*   **National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS):** These standards, notified under the Act, specify the permissible limits for various pollutants in industrial effluents and emissions.
*   **Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003:** These rules detail the procedure for obtaining licenses for hazardous substances and their environmentally sound management, including collection, transportation, treatment, and disposal.
**Analysis of Evidence and Findings:**
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the documentary and other evidence placed on record.
The Complainant's case rests primarily on the site inspection reports, laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples, and photographic/video evidence. The consistency of the inspection reports, spanning several months, indicating effluent discharge from the Respondent
's premises. The laboratory analysis reports, prepared by an accredited EPA laboratory, provide compelling scientific evidence that the industrial effluent consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS parameters for BOD, COD, TSS, lead, and chromium on multiple occasions. These consistent exceedances, documented over several months, demonstrate a systemic failure on the part of the Respondent to adequately treat its industrial waste prior to discharge. The photographic and video evidence, while circumstantial regarding the exact source of all depicted waste, corroborates the Complainant's assertion of improper waste management practices, particularly showing accumulated waste in an open area adjacent to the factory, raising serious concerns about the Respondent's adherence to proper disposal protocols for both general and potentially hazardous waste.
While the Respondent claimed to have an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and presented internal monitoring reports, the Tribunal finds that these internal reports lack the independent verification and consistency of the EPA's laboratory analyses. **V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,100,000/- (Rupees Three Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 620,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **10 February, 2023**.
