This complaint, bearing No. 43/2023, was instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, paper, and board, located in Lahore. The complaint was filed under the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), alleging various contraventions of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, having heard the learned counsel for both parties, examined the evidence presented, and considered the relevant legal provisions, proceeds to deliver its judgment.
The Complainant's case rests on the assertion that the Respondent has been operating its industrial facility in violation of environmental norms, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment. Specifically, the allegations brought forth by the Complainant include:
Firstly, the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system, eventually leading to contamination of surface water bodies, with pollutant parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) consistently exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for municipal and industrial effluents, as prescribed under S.R.O. 54
(I)/2000. Secondly, the emission of air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), from its industrial stacks, consistently exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions, without adequate air pollution control devices. Thirdly, the improper storage, handling, and disposal of hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing
processes, failing to comply with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and posing risks of soil and groundwater contamination.
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that it has made substantial investments in environmental management systems, including state-of-the-art effluent treatment plants and air pollution control devices. It was argued that any deviations from NEQS were minor, temporary, or attributable to unforeseen operational challenges, and that the company is committed to continuous improvement and sustainable practices. The Respondent also presented data suggesting efforts towards reducing their environmental footprint and highlighted their significant economic contribution and employment generation in the region.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the inspection reports submitted by the Complainant, which included detailed laboratory analyses of effluent and emission samples collected on multiple occasions. These reports consistently indicated exceedances of NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, PM, SO2, and NOx, as well as deficiencies in hazardous waste management protocols. Expert testimonies further corroborated these findings, highlighting the inadequacy of existing treatment facilities and the lack of proper waste segregation and disposal mechanisms. While acknowledging the Respondent's claims of investment and commitment to environmental stewardship, the overwhelming evidence presented clearly demonstrates a persistent pattern of non-compliance with the mandatory environmental standards set forth under Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Act, read with the relevant S.R.O.s and Rules. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to demonstrate sufficient diligence or effective implementation of environmental safeguards to meet its statutory obligations. The environmental impact of these persistent violations, particularly the discharge of untreated effluent and uncontrolled air emissions, cannot be understated, posing direct threats to aquatic life, human health, and the general environmental quality of the surrounding areas.
Based on the overwhelming evidence and the legal framework, the Tribunal finds M/s Packages Limited liable for contravening the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, specifically Sections 11, 12, and 13, and the rules framed thereunder.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order and penalty:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) Upgrade:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, install, upgrade, or modify its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged effluent consistently meets the prescribed NEQS for municipal and industrial effluents.
3.  **Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) Upgrade:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, install, upgrade, or modify its air pollution control devices (APCDs) to ensure that all industrial gaseous emissions consistently meet the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
4.  **Hazardous Waste Management Plan:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment, develop and submit to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) a comprehensive Hazardous Waste Management Plan, in compliance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003. This plan must detail the proper storage, handling, treatment, and disposal of all hazardous waste generated at its facility. The EPA shall approve this plan within thirty (30) days of submission, and the Respondent shall implement it forthwith.
5.  **Submission of Compliance Reports:** The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of one (1) year, detailing the measures taken to comply with this order, including monitoring data of effluent and air emissions, certified by an EPA-approved laboratory.
6.  **Penalty:** In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 17(1) of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, a penalty of **PKR 500,000/- (Rupees Five Hundred Thousand only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the established contraventions. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
