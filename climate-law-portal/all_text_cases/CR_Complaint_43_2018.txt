**Introduction**
This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and standards. The complaint, registered on 10.05.2018, seeks legal action, imposition of penalties, and remedial measures against the Respondent for causing environmental pollution, specifically air and water pollution, emanating from its industrial operations located in the vicinity of Lahore. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, established under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), is vested with the jurisdiction to adjudicate such matters and enforce environmental protection
laws and standards.
**Procedural History**
Upon the filing of the complaint, notices were issued to the Respondent. The Respondent appeared through its authorized representative and counsel, contesting the allegations. Written statements were filed by both parties, outlining their respective positions. The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented, which included inspection reports from the EPA, laboratory analyses of effluent and emissions samples, expert testimonies, and documentary evidence submitted by both sides. Multiple hearings were conducted, during which arguments were heard from the learned counsel for the Complainant and the Respondent. The Complainant presented evidence demonstrating exceedances of National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both air emissions (particulate matter, sulphur dioxide) and liquid effluent discharge (BOD, COD, TSS) from the Respondent's facility. The Respondent, while acknowledging its operations, largely contended that it had taken reasonable measures for pollution control and that some of the alleged violations were either minor or attributable to factors beyond its immediate control. However, the expert reports and corroborating evidence, particularly the consistent findings of non-compliance over various inspections, strongly supported the Complainant's assertions regarding persistent environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's industrial activities.
**Findings of the Tribunal**
After a thorough examination of the pleadings, evidence, and arguments presented, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has been operating in violation of several provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed thereunder. Specifically, the evidence adduced, including multiple analytical reports from EPA-approved laboratories, unequivocally demonstrates that the Respondent's air emissions and liquid effluent discharges have consistently exceeded the permissible limits, thereby causing significant air and water pollution in the surrounding area. The Tribunal notes the Respondent's failure to install and/or adequately operate effective pollution control devices, or to maintain them in a manner compliant with environmental regulations, despite prior warnings and notices from the EPA. This persistent non-compliance constitutes a clear breach of Sections 11, 13, and 14 of the PEPA, 1997, which mandate adherence to NEQS, prohibit the discharge of untreated effluent, and require proper treatment of emissions, respectively. The environmental damage caused, as evidenced by the deterioration of local air quality and contamination of water bodies, necessitates immediate and stringent action to protect public health and the environment.
**Final Order and Penalty**
In light of the aforementioned findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 read with Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited is found guilty of violating Sections 11, 13, and 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed thereunder, by causing air and water pollution from its industrial operations.
2.  **Imposition of Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ghani Glass Limited for the environmental damage caused and the sustained non-compliance with environmental laws. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Remedial Measures and Compliance:** The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is directed to undertake the following remedial measures immediately:
    *   **Air Pollution Control:** Install and fully operationalize state-of-the-art air pollution control equipment (e.g., Electrostatic Precipitators, Bag Filters, or equivalent technology) for all emission sources within **ninety (90) days** to ensure compliance with NEQS for air emissions.
    *   **Water Pollution Control:** Upgrade and fully operationalize its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) within **sixty (60) days** to ensure that all treated effluent discharges strictly adhere to the NEQS for liquid effluent.
    *   **Monitoring and Reporting:** Submit a detailed compliance report, certified by an EPA-approved third-party environmental consultant, demonstrating full adherence to NEQS for both air and water, within **one hundred and twenty (120) days** from the date of this order. Thereafter, quarterly compliance reports, including analytical results from accredited laboratories, shall be submitted to the EPA for a period of two (2) years.
    *   **Environmental Management Plan:** Develop and submit an Environmental Management Plan (EMP) to the EPA within **forty-five (45) days**, detailing standard operating procedures for pollution control, waste management, and emergency response, along with a schedule for employee training on environmental compliance.
4.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of the penalty or the implementation of remedial measures within the stipulated timelines, shall result in further legal action, which may include the imposition of daily penalties as per Section 17(2) of PEPA, 1997, and/or the closure of the industrial unit or any part thereof, without further notice.
5.  **Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.
6.  **Finality:** This order shall take immediate effect. Any party aggrieved by this judgment may prefer an appeal before the Honourable High Court within the period prescribed by law.
