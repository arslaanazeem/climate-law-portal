This judgment addresses Complaint No. 54/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Lahore. The complaint, lodged on October 23, 2018, seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by the Respondent. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized with the jurisdiction to adjudicate upon environmental complaints and impose penalties as stipulated under the Act.
The Complainant's primary contention is that the Respondent, an industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, has consistently failed to comply with the NEQS regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions, thereby causing environmental pollution. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), despite its existence, was either non-functional, inefficient, or improperly maintained, leading to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into municipal drains, eventually polluting natural water bodies. Furthermore, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial processes, particularly from its boilers and other stacks, emitted air
emissions containing elevated levels of particulate matter (PM), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and sulfur oxides (SOx), exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. The Complainant further submitted that these emissions contributed significantly to ambient air pollution in the vicinity, posing serious health risks to the local population and detrimental impacts on the environment. Several inspections were conducted by EPA officials between 2017 and 2018, and samples of both effluent and air emissions were collected. Laboratory analyses of these samples consistently indicated non-compliance with the NEQS. Despite repeated notices and warnings issued to the Respondent, including Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs), the alleged violations persisted, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was contended that the Respondent is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship. They asserted that a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) were in place, regularly maintained, and operated in accordance with industry best practices. The Respondent submitted that their operations were subject to routine internal monitoring and periodic external audits, which generally confirmed compliance. They further argued that any occasional exceedances of NEQS, if proven, were minor, transient, and not indicative of a systemic failure or deliberate violation. The Respondent also questioned the methodology and accuracy of the samples collected by the Complainant, suggesting potential procedural irregularities or analytical errors that could have led to skewed results. They presented their own internal monitoring reports and certificates from third-party environmental consultants to support their claims of compliance and robust environmental management systems.
During the proceedings, this Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant submitted detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis certificates from EPA-approved laboratories, photographs, and copies of show-cause notices and EPOs issued to the Respondent. The laboratory reports clearly indicated that the discharged effluent consistently exceeded the NEQS for parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Similarly, the air emission reports demonstrated exceedances of NEQS for PM, NOx, and SOx from the Respondent's stacks. The Respondent, in turn, submitted operational logs for their ETP and APCDs, maintenance records, and internal environmental audit reports. However, these internal documents, while demonstrating efforts, did not conclusively refute the independent laboratory findings presented by the EPA. Expert testimony was also considered, which largely corroborated the EPA's findings regarding the efficacy of the Respondent's existing pollution control infrastructure in meeting the stringent NEQS.
Upon careful consideration of the documentary evidence, expert opinions, and arguments advanced by both sides, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Packages Limited has indeed violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Section 11 thereof, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards. The persistent exceedances of NEQS for both industrial effluent and air emissions, as evidenced by multiple independent laboratory analyses, cannot be dismissed as minor or transient. While the Respondent has invested in pollution control technologies, the evidence indicates that these systems have either been inadequately operated, maintained, or are insufficient to consistently achieve compliance with the prescribed standards. The health and environmental implications of such persistent pollution are significant and cannot be overlooked.
Therefore, this Tribunal, in exercise of the powers vested under Section 20 and Section 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, hereby issues the following final order and penalty:
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Packages Limited is hereby declared to have violated Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the aforementioned violations. This penalty shall be paid into the Environmental Protection Fund within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Remedial Measures and Compliance Plan:** The Respondent is directed to immediately undertake comprehensive measures to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the efficient operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) to consistently meet all parameters of the National Environmental Quality Standards.
    a.  The Respondent shall submit a detailed **Environmental Compliance Plan** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment. This plan must outline specific actions, timelines, and financial commitments for achieving and maintaining full compliance with NEQS.
    b.  The plan shall include provisions for regular internal monitoring, third-party verification, and reporting mechanisms to the EPA.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit quarterly compliance reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party environmental consultant, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of two (2) years, commencing from the submission of the Compliance Plan. These reports shall include laboratory analysis results of effluent and air emissions, demonstrating consistent adherence to NEQS.
5.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of the penalty or the timely submission and implementation of the Compliance Plan, shall render the Respondent liable to further penalties, including but not limited to, a daily fine of PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only) for each day of continued non-compliance, and/or sealing of the industrial unit or any part thereof, without further notice, as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
6.  **Costs:** The costs of these proceedings shall be borne by the Respondent.
