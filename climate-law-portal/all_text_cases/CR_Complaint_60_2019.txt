This complaint, bearing No. 60/2019, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited, a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address alleged contraventions of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants, thereby causing environmental degradation and public nuisance. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), holds jurisdiction to adjudicate upon such matters and impose penalties as stipulated by law.
It was primarily alleged by the Complainant that M/s Packages Limited had been operating its industrial units without consistently adhering to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge and air emissions. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. The discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants (such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH) in concentrations exceeding the NEQS, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its associated rules and regulations.
b. The emission of air pollutants (such as particulate matter, Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)) from its boilers, generators, and other industrial processes, which also consistently exceeded the NEQS.
c. Failure to maintain and operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) effectively and continuously, leading to the aforementioned non-compliance.
d. Failure to provide timely and accurate environmental monitoring reports to the EPA, despite repeated directives and notices issued by the Complainant.
e. Causing environmental pollution detrimental to public health and surrounding ecosystems.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, specifically on 15.03.2019, 22.05.2019, and 10.07.2019. These reports detailed observations of the Respondent's ETP and APCDs, noting instances of sub-optimal operation and maintenance. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15.03.2019 and 10.07.2019, which revealed COD levels significantly higher than the NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, BOD levels exceeding the 80 mg/L limit, and TSS also in excess of the 200
mg/L limit. Additionally, air emission monitoring data from samples collected on 22.05.2019 indicated particulate matter concentrations exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial emissions, particularly from the boiler stacks. The Complainant also submitted copies of several show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, dated 20.03.2019, 01.
06.2019, and 15.07.2019, all demanding explanations for the persistent non-compliance and requiring immediate corrective measures.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its authorized representative and legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal on several scheduled dates. While acknowledging certain operational challenges and historical issues, the Respondent largely denied consistent non-compliance, particularly emphasizing recent efforts to upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs). The Respondent contended that significant investments had been made to modernize their environmental infrastructure and that they were actively working towards achieving full National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) compliance. They presented some internal monitoring data, albeit less comprehensive than the EPA's, suggesting improvements in recent months. The Respondent further argued that the EPA's sampling might not always reflect the overall operational efficiency or that some exceedances were temporary due to maintenance activities or unforeseen technical glitches, which were promptly addressed. They requested a lenient view given their commitment to environmental stewardship and their contribution to the national economy.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
