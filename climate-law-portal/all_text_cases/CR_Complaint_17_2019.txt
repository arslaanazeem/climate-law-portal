This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint, lodged under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged contraventions of environmental laws and regulations.
The Complainant's case, as articulated in the complaint and supported by various documents, asserts that the Respondent has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"), thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on multiple dates, including 15.03.2019 and 02.05.2019, the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was found to be either non-functional, improperly operated, or bypassed, leading to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater directly into the sewerage system/drain. Samples of the discharged effluent were collected by the inspection teams and subsequently analyzed at the EPA's accredited laboratory. The laboratory reports, annexed to the complaint, indicated that several key parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeded
the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). These findings conclusively demonstrated a clear violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which mandates that no person shall discharge or emit waste, effluent, or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level which is in excess of the NEQS.
The Respondent, through its legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and presented arguments primarily focusing on their efforts to upgrade the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and their commitment to environmental compliance. They contended that any instances of non-compliance were isolated or due to operational challenges during the upgrade process. However, the Respondent failed to provide convincing evidence or laboratory reports from independent accredited sources to effectively counter the consistent findings of the Complainant's inspection teams and the analytical reports from the EPA's laboratory, which showed persistent exceedances during the specified periods. The Tribunal also noted that despite being issued Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and show-cause notices by the EPA, the Respondent's corrective actions were either inadequate or delayed, leading to continued environmental degradation.
Having meticulously reviewed the complaint, the documentary evidence presented by the Complainant, including the inspection reports, sampling protocols, and laboratory analyses, and having considered the submissions made by both parties, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the Respondent’s repeated and persistent contravention of the NEQS, and consequently, a clear violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The evidence unequivocally points to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent, posing a significant threat to the environment and public health.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
Therefore, in light of the overwhelming evidence of environmental non-compliance and the potential harm posed to the environment and public health, this Tribunal hereby finds M/s Nishat Mills Limited, the Respondent, guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
Accordingly, exercising the powers vested under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal orders as follows:
1.  **Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Nishat Mills Limited for the established contraventions of the National Environmental Quality Standards and the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
2.  **Continuing Contravention Fine:** Considering the persistent nature of the violations, an additional daily fine of **PKR 25,000/- (Rupees Twenty-Five Thousand Only)** is imposed from the date of this judgment until such time as verifiable and sustained compliance with the NEQS is achieved and confirmed by the Complainant through independent laboratory analysis.
3.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent is hereby directed to:
    a.  Immediately ensure the full functionality and optimal operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently meet the prescribed NEQS for all discharged effluent parameters.
    b.  Submit a comprehensive and time-bound compliance plan to the Complainant within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment, detailing the specific measures to be undertaken to ensure sustainable compliance with the NEQS.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
