**Introduction**
This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "the Company") on January 15, 2019. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with statutory obligations regarding pollution control at the Respondent's industrial facility located in Lahore. The core of the complaint revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to adequately manage its industrial emissions and effluents, thereby causing environmental degradation and public nuisance, in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of PEPA, 1997.
**Allegations**
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited are manifold and stem from a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted by the EPA. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's glass manufacturing plant was operating without effective pollution control measures, resulting in:
1.  **Air Pollution:** Excessive discharge of air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SOx), from its furnace stacks, significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. The EPA reported that ambient air quality around the facility was also negatively impacted.
2.  **Water Pollution:** Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater/effluents into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into surface waters. Laboratory analyses indicated that several parameters of the discharged effluent, such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, were consistently beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial effluents.
3.  **Hazardous Waste Management:** Improper handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous waste generated during the glass manufacturing process, in violation of the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and Section 14 of PEPA, 1997.
4.  **Non-compliance with Notices:** Despite repeated notices, warnings, and opportunities for compliance issued by the EPA since late 2018, the Respondent allegedly failed to take concrete and effective measures to rectify the environmental violations. This included a failure
to submit satisfactory compliance plans, install necessary pollution control equipment, or demonstrate a genuine commitment to environmental protection, despite numerous opportunities.
5.  **Failure to Obtain Environmental Approval:** The Respondent was also alleged to have made significant operational changes or expansions without obtaining the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval, as mandated under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and the Environmental Impact Assessment and Initial Environmental Examination Regulations, 2000.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
