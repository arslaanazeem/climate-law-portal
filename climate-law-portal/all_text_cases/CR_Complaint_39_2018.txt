### JUDGMENT
This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. The complaint, registered as No. 39/2018, was lodged on 15.05.2018, alleging severe environmental pollution and non-compliance with the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal has duly considered the pleadings, evidence adduced by both parties, and the arguments advanced by their respective counsels.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent was engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, a process which generates significant quantities of industrial effluent. Following multiple public complaints regarding foul odor and discharge of discolored water into nearby drains and agricultural fields, the EPA conducted a series of inspections. Specifically, on 01.03.2018 and 10.04.2018, EPA teams visited the Respondent's premises, where they observed the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drainage channels. Samples of the effluent were collected from the discharge points on both occasions. Subsequent laboratory analysis of these samples revealed that critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels far exceeded the permissible limits stipulated by the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. The Complainant further alleged that despite repeated verbal warnings and a formal show-cause notice dated 20.04.2018, the Respondent failed to take effective measures to control the pollution, including the installation or proper operation of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). This persistent non-compliance, according to the Complainant, constituted a blatant violation of Section 1
1 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, which prohibits the discharge of effluent or waste into the environment in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is an environmentally conscious organization that has invested significantly in pollution control measures, including the installation of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). It was argued that the samples collected by the EPA might not have been representative, or that occasional exceedances could occur due to operational upsets, which are promptly addressed. The Respondent presented documents purportedly demonstrating their efforts towards environmental compliance, including records of ETP maintenance and internal monitoring reports. They further submitted that they have been in communication with the EPA to resolve any perceived non-compliance and have committed to further upgrading their ETP to meet stricter standards if required. It was also contended that the EPA's inspections were sometimes conducted without prior notice, making it difficult to present a complete operational picture.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the evidence presented by both parties. The inspection reports dated 01.03.2018 and 10.04.2018, coupled with the detailed laboratory analysis reports, unequivocally establish that the effluent discharged by the Respondent on those dates significantly exceeded the NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. While the Respondent claimed to possess an ETP, the consistent exceedances observed during inspections strongly suggest either its improper operation, inadequate design capacity, or a deliberate bypass of the treatment process. The Tribunal finds the argument regarding non-representative samples unconvincing, especially given the repeated nature of the violations and the corroborating public complaints. The show-cause notice dated 20.04.2018 provided ample opportunity for the Respondent to rectify the situation, yet no substantial improvement was demonstrated before the filing of the complaint. The Tribunal emphasizes
that environmental protection is a paramount duty, and industries must operate within the prescribed standards. The evidence on record, particularly the consistent analytical results from independent laboratory tests, leaves no doubt that the Respondent was in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards on multiple occasions. The Respondent's explanations, while acknowledging an ETP, failed to provide a satisfactory account for the persistent and significant exceedances of critical parameters. The Tribunal is therefore satisfied that the Complainant has successfully established the allegations of environmental pollution and non-compliance with Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS.
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal holds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited responsible for the violations as alleged in Complaint No. 39/2018.
### ORDER
After careful deliberation and considering the gravity of the environmental violations and the persistent non-compliance, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Penalty:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, the Respondent, is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment, into the Punjab Environmental Protection Fund.
2.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment, ensure the full functional operation and upgrading of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently meet all parameters specified under the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance report, duly verified by an independent third-party environmental consultant approved by the EPA, demonstrating full compliance with NEQS.
3.  **Regular Monitoring:** The Respondent shall install continuous online monitoring systems for its effluent discharge points, linked to the EPA's monitoring system, within ninety (90) days, to ensure real-time compliance.
4.  **Future Compliance:** The Respondent is strictly warned to ensure continuous compliance with all provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. Any future non-compliance shall be dealt with severely, potentially leading to higher penalties, operational restrictions, or even closure of the facility under the relevant provisions of law.
5.  **Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
