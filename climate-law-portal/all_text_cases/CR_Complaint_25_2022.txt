This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit, for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Complainant invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeking an order for the cessation of polluting activities and imposition of penalties as prescribed by law. The Tribunal commenced proceedings upon the filing of the complaint, ensuring due process was afforded to both parties.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Hudiara Drain, a major natural water body, thereby causing significant water pollution and environmental degradation. Specifically, it was alleged that during a routine inspection conducted by EPA officials on February 28, 2022, samples of effluent discharged from the Respondent's premises were collected. Subsequent laboratory analysis of these samples revealed that several critical parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) stipulated under Section 13 of PEPA, 1997, and the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent had failed to operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively or consistently, despite repeated warnings and prior communications from the EPA. The non-compliance was stated to be a continuous violation, posing a serious threat to public health, aquatic life, and the overall ecosystem, contrary to the prohibitions enshrined in Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence, including:
1.  **Inspection Report No. EPA/LR/IR/2022/035:** Dated March 1, 2022, detailing the observations made by the EPA inspection team on February 28, 2022, including photographic evidence of the discharge point and the visibly polluted effluent.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/LR/LAB/2022/015:** Dated March 10, 2022, issued by the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for BOD (550 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L), COD (1200 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), TSS (350 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), and pH (10.5 against a standard range of 6-9).
3.  **Witness Testimony:** Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Analyst, EPA, Lahore, appeared before the Tribunal and testified under oath, confirming the sampling procedure, chain of custody, and the accuracy of the laboratory findings. He elaborated on the potential environmental impacts of such discharge.
4.  **Correspondence Record:** Copies of prior warning letters, show-cause notices, and meeting minutes between the EPA and the Respondent, dating back to 2021, indicating a history of non-compliance and unfulfilled commitments regarding ETP operation and upgrades.

**Tribunal's Analysis and Findings:**
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the evidence and arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent. The core of the complaint rests on the alleged discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS.
The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the Inspection Report, the Laboratory Analysis Report, and the witness testimony, provides compelling and irrefutable proof of significant environmental violations. The laboratory results, showing BOD at 550 mg/L (against a standard of 80 mg/L), COD at 1200 mg/L (against a standard of 200 mg/L), TSS at 350 mg/L (against a standard of 200 mg/L), and pH at 10.5 (against a standard range of 6-9), demonstrate a gross and alarming exceedance of the prescribed NEQS across multiple critical parameters. These figures are not indicative of minor operational glitches but rather a severe failure in the effluent treatment process. **V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
