This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 14/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, located at [Hypothetical Address, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore], for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal was constituted to adjudicate environmental disputes, ensuring compliance with environmental laws and standards, and this matter falls squarely within its jurisdiction. The proceedings commenced with the filing of the complaint on [Hypothetical Date, e.g., 15th March 2019], followed by issuance of notices to the Respondent, who subsequently appeared through its authorized representative and legal counsel.
The Complainant, through its Assistant Director, alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment, specifically into the municipal sewerage system which eventually leads to natural water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental pollution. The core allegations included:
a. Violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under the Act.
b. Failure to install and/or effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of consistently treating industrial waste to meet NEQS.
c. Non-compliance with previous environmental improvement notices and show-cause notices issued by the EPA on [Hypothetical Date 1, e.g., 10th January 2019] and [Hypothetical Date 2, e.g., 5th February 2019], which directed the Respondent to rectify the environmental non-compliance.
d. Causing degradation of ambient water quality and potential harm to public health and biodiversity due to the discharge of hazardous pollutants.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports. The first inspection report, dated [Hypothetical Date 3, e.g., 20th January 2019], detailed observations of direct discharge from the Respondent's premises. Crucially, samples of the discharged effluent were collected during this and a subsequent inspection on [Hypothetical Date 4, e.g., 8th February 2019]. These samples were then subjected to laboratory analysis by the EPA's accredited laboratory. The laboratory analysis reports, submitted as Exhibit C-1 and C-2, consistently indicated that parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels in the Respondent's effluent significantly exceeded the permissible limits stipulated in the NEQS for industrial effluents. Photographic evidence (Exhibit C-3) showing the discharge points and the color of the effluent was also presented. Furthermore, copies of the show-cause notices (Exhibit C-4) and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies thereto (Exhibit C-5) were placed on record, demonstrating a history of non-compliance and inadequate response.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, while acknowledging
the presence of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) on its premises, vehemently denied the allegations of deliberate non-compliance and discharge of untreated effluent. Through its legal counsel, Mr. Asif Mehmood, the Respondent contended that its ETP was indeed operational and designed to meet the NEQS. It was argued that any exceedances noted in the EPA's reports might be attributed
to occasional technical glitches, maintenance issues, or possibly inconsistencies in the sampling methodology employed by the EPA. Mr. Mehmood emphasized that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is a responsible corporate entity that has made significant investments in environmental protection measures and is committed to sustainable operations. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
