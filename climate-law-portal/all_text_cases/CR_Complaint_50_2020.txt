**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the environmental complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and its allied rules and regulations. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is tasked with adjudicating environmental disputes and imposing penalties for contraventions of environmental laws.
**Introduction**
The present complaint, filed on 15.06.2020, arises from a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, at the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The EPA, as the primary regulatory body responsible for the enforcement of environmental standards, initiated these proceedings following persistent reports and its own findings indicating the Respondent's failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge and air emissions, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. The Respondent, a prominent industrial entity, is expected to operate in strict adherence to environmental safeguards.
**Allegations**
The Complainant, through its Assistant Director, averred that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has consistently operated its textile dyeing and finishing unit in contravention of environmental laws. The specific allegations include:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent:** It is alleged that the Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS parameters directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually leads to surface water bodies, without adequate treatment. This contravenes Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
2.  **Failure to Operate an Effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** Despite having an ETP, the Complainant asserts that it is either non-functional, improperly maintained, or insufficient to treat the volume and type of pollutants generated, leading to non-compliance with NEQS for parameters such as Biochemical

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
