**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 1/2021**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Nishat Mills Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a prominent textile manufacturing unit, alleging serious violations of environmental laws and standards. The complaint, registered on 15th February 2021, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), seeking the enforcement of environmental standards, imposition of penalties, and remedial measures against the Respondent. The Tribunal’s mandate is to adjudicate matters relating to environmental pollution, ensuring compliance with National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and fostering environmental protection within its territorial jurisdiction.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Nishat Mills Limited, located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, was consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the natural water bodies, thereby causing severe water pollution. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent had failed to install and operate an efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating its industrial discharge to meet the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of excessive air emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks, particularly regarding particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, exceeding the permissible limits set by NEQS. The EPA further contended that despite repeated notices and warnings issued to the Respondent, including show-cause notices dated 10th November 2020 and 15th January 2021, the Respondent had failed to take adequate corrective measures, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and the health of the surrounding community and ecosystem.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports compiled by EPA field teams on various dates, specifically 25th October 2020, 5th December 2020, and 20th January 2021. Crucially, these reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis certificates from the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming significant exceedances of NEQS parameters for industrial effluents. For instance, the analysis of samples collected on 5th December 2020 showed Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) levels at 450 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD) at 180 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) at 250 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). Similar exceedances were reported for air emissions, with particulate matter consistently above 200 mg/Nm³ (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/Nm³). Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the dark, odorous discharge from the Respondent's premises into the public drain was also submitted. The Complainant further presented copies of the show-cause notices, official correspondence, and non-compliance reports issued to the Respondent, demonstrating a clear administrative process followed prior to filing the complaint. The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations, asserting that it had an operational ETP and was making continuous efforts to comply with environmental standards. They argued that the samples collected by the EPA might not have been representative, or that the analytical methods used were questionable. The Respondent also claimed that their ETP was undergoing an upgrade during the period in question and that any temporary exceedances were a result of this transitional phase. They presented some internal monitoring reports and invoices for chemicals purchased for the ETP, but these lacked independent
verification or accreditation, and therefore could not sufficiently counter the robust evidence presented by the Complainant.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the entire record, including the documentary evidence, inspection reports, laboratory analyses, and photographic and videographic submissions. It is evident that the Complainant has discharged its burden of proof by presenting a consistent and verifiable chain of evidence demonstrating persistent and significant breaches of the National Environmental Quality Standards by M/s Nishat Mills Limited. The laboratory reports from an accredited EPA laboratory, detailing specific exceedances of COD, BOD, TSS, and particulate matter, are particularly compelling and stand unchallenged by any credible counter-evidence. The Respondent's claims regarding unrepresentative samples or questionable analytical methods were unsubstantiated and lacked any expert testimony or independent audit to support them. Similarly, the assertion of an ETP upgrade as a justification for non-compliance does not absolve the Respondent of its statutory duty to comply with NEQS at all times, especially
given the repeated warnings issued by the EPA over several months. Environmental compliance is a continuous obligation, not one that can be suspended during upgrade periods, particularly when such upgrades are necessitated by prior non-compliance. The photographic and videographic evidence further corroborates the visual observations of the EPA teams, depicting a clear discharge of polluted effluent. The Respondent's internal reports, lacking independent verification, are insufficient to rebut the robust, scientifically validated evidence presented by the Complainant.
Therefore, this Tribunal finds that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has indeed violated the provisions of Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging effluents and emissions exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards, and has failed to take effective measures despite repeated notices from the Environmental Protection Agency. The persistent nature of these violations, coupled with the potential harm to public health and the environment, warrants a stringent response from this Tribunal to ensure the sanctity of environmental laws and to deter future non-compliance.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 21 and Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is found guilty of violating Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and air emissions.
2.  **Imposition of Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is imposed on M/s Nishat Mills Limited for the proven environmental violations. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Remedial Measures and Compliance Directives:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is directed to immediately take all necessary measures to ensure full compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both industrial effluents and air emissions. Specifically, the Respondent shall:
    *   **(a) Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) Upgrade:** Upgrade and operationalize its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently achieve NEQS for all parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS), within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment.
    *   **(b) Air Emission Control:** Install and operationalize appropriate air pollution control devices for its boiler stacks to consistently achieve NEQS for particulate matter and other air pollutants, within a period of **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment.
    *   **(c) Compliance Reporting:** Submit monthly compliance reports, including validated laboratory analysis results from an EPA-accredited laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency for a period of **one (1) year**, commencing one month after the completion of the upgrade/installation deadlines.
4.  **Monitoring and Enforcement:** The Environmental Protection Agency is directed to conduct regular monitoring and inspections of M/s Nishat Mills Limited's premises to ensure strict compliance with this order and the NEQS. Should the Respondent fail to comply with any part of this order within the stipulated timelines, the EPA is authorized to initiate further legal action

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **18 September, 2023**.
