This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged under Section 17(1) read with Section 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleges grave violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and non-compliance with National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal commenced proceedings on 15.01.2020, following the submission of the complaint and accompanying documents, aiming to ascertain the Respondent's culpability and impose appropriate legal remedies. The matter has been heard diligently, with opportunities afforded to both parties to present their respective cases, ensuring adherence to the principles of natural justice.
The Complainant's allegations are primarily centered on the Respondent's failure to adequately manage and treat its industrial wastewater. It is contended that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS into public sewers and ultimately into the River Ravi. Specifically, the complaint details that during routine inspections conducted on 05.08.2019 and 22.10.2019, samples of the Respondent's treated effluent were collected from its discharge points. Subsequent laboratory analyses, performed by the EPA's accredited laboratory, revealed significant exceedances for critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. The Complainant further alleges that the Respondent was operating without a fully functional and compliant Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of meeting NEQS, despite having been issued several warnings and show-cause notices for previous non-compliance. It was also asserted that the Respondent failed to provide satisfactory responses to these notices, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental protection laws and the directives of the regulatory authority. The Complainant sought punitive action, including a substantial fine and directions for immediate remedial measures, to prevent further environmental degradation.
In support of its complaint, the EPA presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included certified copies of the inspection reports dated 05.08.2019 and 22.10.2019, meticulously detailing the observations made by the inspection teams regarding the Respondent's ETP operations and discharge practices. Crucially, the Complainant submitted the original laboratory analysis reports bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/19/08/0012 and EPA/LAB/19/10/0045, which unequivocally confirmed the NEQS exceedances. These reports compared the analyzed parameters with the NEQS for industrial effluents, clearly highlighting the extent of pollution. Furthermore, copies of the show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 20.08.2019 and 05.11.2019, along with proof of their delivery and the Respondent's evasive or inadequate replies, were placed on record. The Complainant also presented photographic evidence depicting the discharge points and the visible discoloration of the effluent, corroborating the visual observations made during inspections. On the other hand, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, initially denied the allegations, claiming that their ETP was operational and designed to meet NEQS. They contended that the samples might have been collected improperly or were not representative of their average discharge. The Respondent submitted some maintenance records for their ETP and a letter indicating their intention to upgrade the plant. However, they failed to produce any independent third-party laboratory analysis reports to counter the EPA's findings, nor could they provide a valid No-Objection Certificate (NOC) for their current discharge operations that explicitly confirmed compliance. During cross-examination, the Respondent's technical representative admitted to periodic operational issues with the ETP and acknowledged that a full-scale upgrade was indeed necessary, indirectly lending credence to the Complainant's assertions.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act,
1997. Specifically, Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, unequivocally prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Section 17 prescribes penalties for contravention of the Act, while Section 17 empowers the Environmental Tribunal to take cognizance of complaints and impose appropriate penalties and
directions to ensure compliance. The Tribunal, having meticulously reviewed the entire record, including the pleadings, documentary evidence, and the arguments advanced by learned counsel for both parties, now proceeds to render its findings.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
