**JUDGMENT**
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, under Section 17 read with Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeks redress against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, established under Section 20 of the Act, is
empowered to hear and decide complaints regarding contraventions of the Act and its rules, regulations, and NEQS, and to impose penalties as prescribed.
The Complainant contended that the Respondent’s industrial operations were discharging untreated or inadequately treated effluent into the surrounding environment, exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS, and emitting air pollutants without proper abatement measures, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and public health hazards. Evidence, including analytical reports of effluent and emission samples collected by EPA officials on various dates between January and March 2023, was presented to substantiate these claims. Notices were duly served upon the Respondent, requiring compliance and explanation.
The Respondent, through its counsel, denied the allegations, asserting that it had installed and was operating an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, and was making continuous efforts to comply with environmental standards. However, during the course of proceedings, it was acknowledged that certain parameters of discharge and emissions had, on occasion, exceeded the NEQS, attributing these to operational fluctuations and maintenance issues rather than deliberate non-compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, including the EPA's monitoring reports, the Respondent's compliance submissions, and heard arguments from both sides. It was established that while the Respondent had indeed invested in environmental management infrastructure, the consistent exceedance of NEQS for several key parameters, as evidenced by multiple independent laboratory analyses, demonstrated a failure to consistently operate these systems effectively or to the required standards. The duty to comply with NEQS is strict, and operational fluctuations do not absolve a polluter of liability if those fluctuations result in environmental harm or non-compliance. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to demonstrate consistent compliance despite repeated warnings and opportunities to rectify the deficiencies.
Therefore, the Tribunal finds that M/s Packages Limited has contravened Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging environmental pollutants in excess of the NEQS, and has failed to take all reasonable measures to prevent or abate pollution, in violation of Section 11 of the Act.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the findings, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  M/s Packages Limited is found guilty of violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
2.  A penalty of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** is imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the aforementioned violations. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  M/s Packages Limited is directed to immediately take all necessary measures to ensure full and consistent compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards for all its discharges and emissions. The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive compliance plan, outlining specific actions, timelines, and responsible personnel, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within fifteen (15) days.
4.  The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, shall conduct fortnightly monitoring of the Respondent's premises for a period of six (6) months to verify compliance. The cost of such monitoring shall be borne by the Respondent.
5.  Failure to comply with these directions shall result in further legal action, including potential daily fines as per Section 17(2)(a) of the Act, and/or sealing of the industrial unit as per Section 16 of the Act, without further notice.
6.  The complaint is accordingly disposed of. No order as to costs.
