**Introduction**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 50/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. The complaint was lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), specifically alleging persistent violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and public nuisance. The Tribunal was seized of the matter following repeated failures by the Respondent to comply with Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued by the Complainant.
**Allegations**
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited include:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent:** The Respondent has been accused of regularly discharging industrial effluent directly into the Hadiara Drain without adequate treatment, which subsequently flows into the River Ravi. Multiple inspections and laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA revealed that the discharged effluent consistently exceeded the NEQS limits for several critical parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (specifically lead and chromium). This discharge is alleged to be in direct contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
2.  **Failure to Operate a Functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** Despite repeated directives and warnings, the Respondent allegedly failed to install and/or effectively operate a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant capable of treating the industrial wastewater to meet NEQS. Inspections indicated either a non-existent, improperly designed, or poorly maintained ETP, rendering it ineffective.
3.  **Emission of Air Pollutants:** The Complainant also alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly from its boilers and process stacks, were emitting gaseous pollutants and particulate matter exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. This was based on observations of dark smoke plumes and occasional ambient air quality monitoring.
4.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs):** The EPA had previously issued multiple Show Cause Notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) dating back to 2019, directing the Respondent to rectify
the aforementioned environmental violations. However, the Respondent allegedly failed to comply with these directives, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and the authority of the EPA, thereby necessitating the filing of the instant complaint before this Tribunal for appropriate legal action and enforcement.
**Respondent's Stance**
Upon being served with the complaint, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, appeared through its
counsel and filed its written statement. In its defense, the Respondent primarily contended that it is a long-standing industrial entity contributing significantly to the national economy and employment. While acknowledging some challenges in meeting the NEQS, particularly concerning the effluent discharge, the Respondent denied any deliberate or persistent disregard for environmental laws. It was argued that:
1.  **Ongoing Efforts for Compliance:** The Respondent claimed to have made substantial investments in upgrading its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. It asserted that the existing ETP was operational, albeit facing technical challenges in consistently achieving the stringent NEQS for all parameters, especially during peak production periods or due to fluctuations in raw material composition. Plans for further technological upgrades and capacity enhancement of the ETP were stated to be in advanced stages.
2.  **Technical and Financial Constraints:** The Respondent highlighted the complex nature of treating chemical industrial effluent and emissions, requiring specialized and costly technologies. It pleaded for understanding regarding the time and financial resources required to implement comprehensive solutions, especially in light of prevailing economic conditions.
3.  **Questioning EPA's Methodology:** The Respondent, without outright denying the violations, raised concerns regarding the frequency and methodology of EPA's sampling and analysis, suggesting that isolated instances might have been extrapolated to represent consistent non-compliance. It also requested a joint sampling and analysis exercise to verify the current status.
4.  **Commitment to Environmental Protection:** The Respondent reiterated its commitment to environmental stewardship and assured the Tribunal of its willingness to comply with all environmental regulations, provided a reasonable timeframe and practical solutions were considered.
**Proceedings**
The Tribunal conducted several hearings, during which both parties presented their respective arguments, documentary evidence, and cross-examined witnesses. The Complainant submitted detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis results from multiple dates, copies of Show Cause Notices, and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) demonstrating a pattern of non-compliance. The Respondent, in turn, submitted technical reports from its consultants, expenditure details on ETP upgrades, and proposed timelines for achieving full compliance. The Tribunal also considered the option of appointing an independent expert for a site visit and assessment, but based on the overwhelming evidence presented by the EPA and the Respondent's partial admissions, it was deemed unnecessary to delay the proceedings further. Attempts at mediation between the parties to arrive at an agreed compliance plan failed, as the Complainant insisted on immediate and full compliance, while the Respondent sought a more extended period.
**Findings of the Tribunal**
Having meticulously reviewed the pleadings, evidence, and arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent, this Tribunal has arrived at the following findings:
1.  **Irrefutable Evidence of Violations:** The documentary evidence, particularly the laboratory analysis reports presented by the EPA, consistently demonstrates that the Respondent's industrial effluent discharge and gaseous emissions have repeatedly and significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various critical parameters. The Complainant's reports clearly indicate high levels of BOD, COD, TSS, pH imbalances, and heavy metals in the effluent, and excessive particulate matter and gaseous pollutants in the air emissions.
2.  **Ineffective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** The Respondent's admission of "technical challenges" in consistently achieving NEQS from its existing ETP, coupled with the EPA's findings of inadequate treatment, confirms that the ETP, if operational, is not functioning effectively or is insufficient in capacity to treat the industrial wastewater to the required standards. The repeated issuance of EPOs and the persistent nature of the violations underscore this inadequacy.
3.  **Persistent Non-Compliance with EPOs:** The Respondent's failure to comply with multiple Environmental Protection Orders issued by the EPA over an extended period (since 2019) demonstrates a clear and persistent disregard for regulatory directives. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of ongoing efforts and financial constraints, these do not absolve it from its legal obligation to comply with environmental laws. The environmental degradation caused by the continuous discharge of untreated effluent into the Hadiara Drain and subsequently the River Ravi, along with air pollution, poses a serious threat to public health and ecological balance.
4.  **Environmental Damage and Public Nuisance:** The sustained violations have resulted in significant environmental degradation, including water pollution affecting aquatic life and potentially human health, and air pollution impacting local communities. This constitutes a public nuisance and a direct contravention of the fundamental right to a clean and healthy environment.
The Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the allegations against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited beyond reasonable doubt. The Respondent's defenses, while acknowledging efforts, do not negate the proven fact of non-compliance and the resulting environmental damage. The provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, are clear in mandating adherence to NEQS and empowering the EPA to take strict enforcement actions against polluters.
**Final Order and Penalty**
In light of the aforesaid findings and in exercise of the powers vested in this Tribunal under Sections 16, 17, and 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, the Respondent, is found guilty of violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by persistently discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and failing to comply with Environmental Protection Orders.
2.  **Imposition of Fine:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **17 January, 2023**.
