This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 47/2016, initiated by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit, alleging serious violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, established under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), is vested with the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints regarding contraventions of the provisions of PEPA, 1997, and the rules and regulations framed thereunder. The present complaint seeks a declaration that the Respondent has contravened environmental standards, an order for immediate cessation of such contraventions, and the imposition of penalties as prescribed by law.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited revolve around the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain, which eventually merges into natural water bodies, thereby causing significant water pollution. It was specifically alleged that on multiple inspections conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) officials, notably on 15.03.2016 and 28.04.2016, the Respondent's industrial unit was found to be operating without an effective wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) or that the existing plant was non-functional or severely inefficient. Samples of the discharged effluent were collected during these inspections and subsequently analyzed at the certified laboratory of the EPA. The laboratory reports, dated 25.03.2016 and 06.05.2016, revealed that various parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, as specified in S.R.O. 549(I)/2000. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent had failed to obtain the requisite No Objection Certificate (NOC) for the installation and operation of their industrial unit under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, or had violated the conditions of any existing NOC. A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on 12.05.2016, followed by a final notice on 01.06.2016, neither of which elicited a satisfactory response or demonstrative action towards compliance, leading to the filing of this complaint.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence. This included the detailed inspection reports prepared by the EPA field staff on 15.03.2016 and 28.04.2016, meticulously documenting the observations regarding the Respondent's effluent discharge practices and the apparent lack of functional treatment facilities. Crucially, the analytical reports from the EPA’s accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/Lab/W-123/2016 and EPA/Lab/W-187/2016, were tendered. These reports provided precise quantitative data confirming the exceedance of NEQS for multiple pollutants in the Respondent's effluent. The Complainant also submitted copies of the show-cause notice and the final notice issued to M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, along with proof of their dispatch and receipt, demonstrating
the Respondent’s consistent failure to address the environmental violations despite repeated official communications.
M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently contested the allegations. The Respondent's primary defense revolved around assertions that their industrial unit was equipped with a functional wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) and that due diligence was exercised to ensure compliance with environmental standards. They argued that the EPA's sampling
procedures were flawed, and the samples collected might not have been representative of their routine discharge. They further contended that the laboratory reports presented by the Complainant were questionable, lacking proper chain of custody documentation or being based on analyses conducted by a facility whose accreditation or methodology they disputed. The Respondent maintained that their WWTP was indeed operational, albeit possibly experiencing temporary technical glitches on the specific dates of inspection

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
