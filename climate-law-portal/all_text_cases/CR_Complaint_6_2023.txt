This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged under Section 17(1) read with Section 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleges serious violations of environmental laws and regulations, particularly concerning the discharge of industrial effluent.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility was engaged in the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system, which ultimately flows into a natural water body, without conforming to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Effluents. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. Persistent discharge of industrial wastewater exceeding the prescribed NEQS parameters for Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and several heavy metals, as evidenced by multiple inspections and laboratory analyses.
b. Failure to adequately operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), leading to the discharge of substandard effluent.
c. Non-compliance with the conditions stipulated in the Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate
(EIA/NOC) issued by the EPA, including but not limited to, the submission of regular self-monitoring reports and the implementation of a robust environmental management plan.
d. Failure to obtain necessary environmental approvals for expansions or modifications to its industrial processes that could impact effluent quality or quantity.
The Respondent, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that it had
invested significantly in a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was committed to operating it efficiently. The Respondent presented evidence of regular maintenance schedules, chemical purchases for the ETP, and internal monitoring reports which, they claimed, showed compliance or efforts towards compliance. The Respondent further argued that any instances of non-compliance were isolated, temporary malfunctions, or attributable to external factors

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
