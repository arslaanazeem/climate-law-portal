This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, seeks action against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards concerning the discharge of industrial effluent. The Complainant, representing the statutory body responsible for the enforcement of environmental protection laws in the Province of Punjab, brought this matter before the Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The core issue revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, operating a large-scale textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the natural water bodies without adequate treatment. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater with high levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and elevated pH levels, far exceeding the NEQS limits.
b. Failure to properly operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite previous notices and warnings issued by the EPA.
c. Non-compliance with the conditions of its Environmental Approval (if any) and failure to submit regular self-monitoring reports as required.
d. Causing pollution to the aquatic environment and posing a health risk to communities residing downstream due to the untreated discharge.
The Complainant asserted that these actions constituted a clear violation of Sections 11 and 16 of the PEPA, 1997, and warranted penal action by the Tribunal.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
1.  **Inspection Reports**: Detailed inspection reports dated 15.05.2018, 03.09.2018, and 20.03.2019, prepared by EPA field teams, documenting observations of effluent discharge points and the apparent inadequate functioning of the Respondent's ETP. The reports highlighted visual indicators of pollution, such as discolored water and foul odors.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports**: Certified laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15.05.2018, 03.09.2018, and 20.03.2019. These reports consistently showed exceedances of NEQS for BOD (averaging 150-200 mg/L against a limit of 80 mg/L), COD (averaging 400-550 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L), TSS (averaging 250-300 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L), and pH values outside the permissible range of 6-9.
3.  **Photographic and Video Evidence**: Photographs and short video clips depicting the discharge of effluent from the Respondent's premises into the public drain, demonstrating the visible characteristics of untreated wastewater.
4.  **Notices and Show Cause Notices**: Copies of formal notices and show cause notices issued by the EPA
to the Respondent on various dates, including 01.06.2018, 10.09.2018, and 25.03.2019, directing them to cease the discharge of untreated effluent, ensure compliance with NEQS, and provide an action plan. These notices clearly outlined the observed violations and warned of legal proceedings if remedial measures
were not taken.
**Respondent's Stance and Defense:**
Upon receiving the complaint and subsequent notices from the Tribunal, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, appeared through its legal counsel. The Respondent initially denied the persistent nature of the violations, asserting that their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was operational and they were making efforts to comply with the NEQS. They contended that occasional exceedances might occur due to operational fluctuations or external factors beyond their immediate control. They also argued that the samples collected by the EPA might not have been representative or that the laboratory analysis could be flawed. However, the Respondent failed to produce any credible counter-evidence, such as their own independent laboratory analysis reports demonstrating compliance, or detailed maintenance records of their ETP that would refute the Complainant's consistent findings. They requested additional time to upgrade their ETP and improve their monitoring mechanisms.
**Proceedings and Hearings:**
The Tribunal conducted several hearings where both parties presented their arguments and evidence. The Complainant's witnesses, including EPA officials involved in inspections and sample collection, were cross-examined. The laboratory analysts confirmed the validity and accuracy of their reports. The Respondent's representatives were given ample opportunity to present their defense, including calling witnesses or submitting documentary evidence. However, the Respondent primarily relied on oral submissions and generalized assurances of future compliance, without effectively challenging the factual veracity of the Complainant's inspection reports and laboratory analyses. The Tribunal carefully considered the voluminous evidence

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
