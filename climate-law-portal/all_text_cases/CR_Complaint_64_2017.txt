This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "the Company"), situated at Kala Shah Kaku Industrial Estate, Sheikhupura. The complaint, lodged on 15.05.2017, seeks the imposition of penalties and directions for compliance with environmental laws due to alleged persistent violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The EPA, as the primary regulatory body, initiated proceedings following multiple inspections and non-compliance by the Respondent, asserting its mandate to protect and conserve the environment.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the production of various chemicals including caustic soda, chlorine, and allied products, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into open drains, thereby polluting surface water bodies. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of excessive air emissions, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), from its stacks, exceeding the prescribed NEQS. It was also contended that the Respondent failed to manage its hazardous waste, particularly mercury-contaminated sludge and other chemical residues, in an environmentally sound manner, posing significant risks to public health and the ecosystem. The EPA specifically highlighted that despite repeated warnings, issuance of show cause notices, and opportunities for remedial action, the Respondent failed to implement effective pollution control measures, demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations. The complaint referenced inspection reports from 03.02.2017, 28.03.2017, and 20.04.2017, which consistently indicated NEQS exceedances for parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and mercury levels in wastewater, and for Particulate Matter (PM), SO2, and NOx in air emissions. These persistent violations were deemed to constitute an offense under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, warranting punitive action under Sections 16, 17, and 20 of the same Act.
The Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence, including copies of inspection reports dated 03.
02.2017, 28.03.2017, and 20.04.2017, along with detailed laboratory analysis reports substantiating the exceedances of NEQS for both effluent discharge and air emissions. The Complainant also submitted copies of the show cause notices issued to the Respondent, their unsatisfactory replies, and subsequent reminders, all
indicating the Respondent's persistent non-compliance and failure to take effective corrective measures despite ample opportunity. The Complainant emphasized that the continuous discharge of untreated effluent and excessive air emissions posed severe environmental degradation and health risks to the surrounding communities, justifying the stringent application of PEPA, 1997.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, primarily asserting that it had invested significantly in pollution control technologies and was operating its industrial processes in compliance with environmental standards. The Respondent contended that any observed exceedances were either temporary operational anomalies, attributable to unforeseen technical glitches, or were based on sampling and analysis methodologies that were flawed or unrepresentative. They argued that their Environmental Management Plan (EMP) was regularly updated and implemented, and that they had engaged reputable environmental consultants to monitor their emissions and discharges. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
