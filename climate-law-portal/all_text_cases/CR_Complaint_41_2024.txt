This complaint, bearing No. 41/2024, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, located within the territorial jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, having heard the parties and considered the presented evidence, proceeds to render its judgment.
The Complainant contended that the Respondent company was operating its industrial facility without adherence to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and air emissions, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). Specifically, the allegations included: (a) persistent discharge of improperly treated industrial wastewater into the public sewerage system/receiving waters, exceeding the permissible NEQS limits for various parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals; (b) emission of air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM10), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), from its boiler stacks and other process units, in excess of the stipulated NEQS; (c) failure to install and/or effectively operate a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to mitigate pollution; (d) non-compliance with previous directions and show-cause notices issued by the EPA for rectifying these environmental transgressions; and (e) operating without a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its industrial operations, or failing to renew it as required by law. The Complainant asserted that these actions constituted a grave threat to public health and the environment, causing pollution to water bodies, air quality degradation, and potential harm to ecosystems.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA teams on various dates between January 2023 and March 2024, detailing observations of effluent discharge points and stack emissions. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory were submitted, showing consistent exceedances of NEQS for effluent and air samples collected from the Respondent's premises. For instance, reports dated 15.03.2023, 10.08.2023, and 20.01.2024 indicated COD levels up to 350 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L) and TSS up to 280 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L) in the wastewater. Air emission reports for stack No. 2 on 25.02.2024 showed PM10 levels of 220 mg/Nm³ against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/Nm³. Copies of show-cause notices dated 01.04.2023 and 20.09.2023, followed by a final notice dated 05.02.2024, issued to the Respondent, along with their unsatisfactory replies, were also placed on record. The Complainant also produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Scientist, who conducted several inspections and sampling, confirming the veracity of the reports and the continued non-compliance. The Respondent, in its defense, primarily argued that it was in the process of upgrading its ETP and installing new APCDs, and that any exceedances were temporary during this transition phase. It presented some purchase orders for new equipment and a timeline for their installation. However, the Respondent failed to provide any independent third-party verified monitoring reports demonstrating compliance during the relevant period or to convincingly refute the EPA's laboratory findings. The Respondent's representative, Mr. Bilal Hassan, Manager Operations, during cross-examination, admitted to some operational challenges but maintained that the company was committed
to environmental compliance. However, the Tribunal notes that mere commitment and the intention to upgrade, without concrete, verifiable evidence of current compliance or significant progress within a reasonable timeframe, cannot absolve an industrial entity from its legal obligations to adhere to prescribed environmental standards. The purchase orders and proposed timeline, while indicative of future intent, do not retroactively negate the documented and consistent violations established by the Complainant's evidence over an extended period.
Having carefully considered the submissions of both parties and thoroughly reviewed the documentary and testimonial evidence, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof. The EPA's inspection reports, corroborated by the accredited laboratory analysis reports, unequivocally demonstrate persistent and significant exceedances of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both industrial effluents and air emissions by M/s Packages Limited. The Respondent's failure to effectively operate pollution control devices, its non-compliance with prior EPA directives, and its operation without a valid NOC or renewed NOC further solidify the case against it. The defense presented by the Respondent, while acknowledging operational challenges, failed to provide satisfactory evidence of compliance during the period of alleged violations or to sufficiently counter the robust evidence presented by the Complainant. The Tribunal is convinced that the Respondent has indeed violated Sections 11, 12, 13, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules and regulations framed thereunder, particularly the National Environmental Quality Standards (Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industry) Rules, 2001, and the Environmental Quality Standards (Effluent and Air Emissions) Rules, 2000.

In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby holds M/s Packages Limited (Respondent) responsible for the violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards as detailed above.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
