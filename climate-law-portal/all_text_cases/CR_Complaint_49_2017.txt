The present complaint, bearing No. 49/2017, has been filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), invoking the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The EPA seeks redress for alleged violations by the Respondent pertaining to the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions into the environment, contrary to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of PEPA, 1997. The Tribunal, being duly constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is empowered to adjudicate upon such environmental complaints and impose penalties as deemed appropriate.
The gravamen of the complaint, as articulated by the Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, centres on the Respondent's alleged failure to adequately treat its industrial effluent and control its atmospheric emissions. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that during an inspection conducted on 15.03.2017, at the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore, it was observed that the Respondent was discharging untreated industrial wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies, without conforming to the NEQS. Furthermore, the complaint detailed that the Respondent's boiler stack emissions were visibly dark and exceeded permissible limits for particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, indicating a lack of proper air pollution control devices or their inadequate operation. A sampling of effluent was conducted on the aforementioned date, and subsequent laboratory analysis revealed parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The Complainant further asserted that despite multiple notices issued to the Respondent, including a show-cause notice dated 05.04.2017, and a final warning dated 25.04.2017, requiring the installation and operationalization of an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), the Respondent failed to take concrete and satisfactory remedial measures, thereby continuing to pollute the environment. The complaint sought a direction for the Respondent to comply with environmental laws and the imposition of a penalty under PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included the inspection report dated 15.03.2017, prepared by the EPA inspection team, detailing the observations of non-compliance. Crucially, the Complainant submitted the laboratory analysis report (Ref. No. EPA/Lab/2017/03-125) dated 28.03.2017, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for various parameters in the effluent sample collected from the Respondent's premises. Photographic evidence depicting the discharge of discoloured effluent and visible emissions from the stacks was also tendered. Copies of the show-cause notice dated 05.04.2017, the final warning dated 25.04.
2017, and the detailed correspondence exchanged between the parties, all demonstrating the Complainant's persistent efforts to secure compliance from the Respondent.

They contended that they are a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship and have already initiated measures to upgrade their effluent treatment and air pollution control systems. They argued that the installation of advanced ETP and APCDs is a time-consuming and capital-intensive process, and they were in the midst of implementing these upgrades at the time of the inspections. The Respondent further submitted that the initial samples might not have been fully representative, and that subsequent internal monitoring indicated improvements. They also pointed out that they had engaged environmental consultants to advise on best practices and were actively pursuing a long-term compliance strategy. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
