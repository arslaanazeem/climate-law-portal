This judgment addresses Complaint No. 47/2023, filed on May 15, 2023, by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent industrial entity operating in the vicinity of Lahore. The complaint seeks redress for alleged violations of environmental laws, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent into public waterways and land, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing risks to public health. The Tribunal, constituted under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), has carefully considered the submissions and evidence presented by both parties to ascertain the extent of environmental non-compliance and determine appropriate remedial action.
The Complainant alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has consistently failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, it was averred that despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, the Respondent continued to discharge industrial wastewater containing hazardous pollutants, including but not limited to, high levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and various heavy metals, far exceeding the permissible limits. The complaint highlighted that the Respondent's existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was either non-functional, improperly maintained, or inadequate to treat the volume and type of waste generated by its chemical manufacturing processes. These discharges, according to the Complainant, were directly impacting the quality of groundwater, agricultural land, and local drainage systems, leading to soil contamination, adverse effects on aquatic life, and potential health hazards for communities residing downstream and adjacent to the discharge points. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent's actions constituted a clear violation of Sections 11 and 12 of PEPA, 1997, warranting stringent penalties and remedial orders.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple environmental inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between January 2023 and April 2023. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on three separate occasions (January 23, 2023, March 10, 2023, and April 18, 2023) were submitted. These reports consistently demonstrated exceedances of NEQS for BOD (averaging 350 mg/l against a standard of 80 mg/l), COD (averaging 900 mg/l against a standard of 200 mg/l), and TSS (averaging 450 mg/l against a standard of 200 mg/l), among other parameters. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the discolored and foul-smelling discharge entering open drains was also presented. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on February 15, 2023, and April 25, 2023, along with reminders and a final warning letter dated May 05
, 2023, were part of the submitted evidence, all highlighting the Respondent's persistent non-compliance and failure to take effective corrective measures.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. It was contended that the company is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship. The Respondent argued that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was operational and regularly maintained, and any exceedances in NEQS were either isolated incidents attributable to unforeseen operational upsets or due to sampling methodologies that may not have accurately represented the overall discharge quality. The Respondent further submitted that it had invested significantly in upgrading its ETP technology and was in the process of implementing further improvements to enhance its treatment capacity and efficiency. It sought leniency, emphasizing its economic contribution to the region and the potential adverse impact of severe penalties on its operations and workforce. The Respondent also presented some internal maintenance records for its ETP and stated that it had engaged environmental consultants to review its processes, but concrete evidence of sustained compliance or a robust action plan for immediate rectification was largely absent from its submissions.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. It noted the consistency of the Complainant's laboratory reports, which showed clear and repeated violations of NEQS across multiple parameters over several months. The photographic and videographic evidence further corroborated the claims of untreated effluent discharge. The issuance of multiple warnings and notices by the EPA, culminating in a final warning, clearly demonstrated that the Respondent had ample opportunity to rectify the situation but failed to do so effectively. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of being a responsible corporate entity and its stated intentions to upgrade its ETP, the documentary evidence of persistent non-compliance outweighs these assertions. The internal maintenance records provided by the Respondent lacked the specificity and independent verification required to counter the objective findings of the EPA's laboratory analyses. The argument regarding sampling methodologies was not substantiated with any credible counter-analysis or expert opinion. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has indeed violated Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby causing environmental pollution and posing risks to public health and natural resources.
In light of the compelling evidence and the persistent nature of the violations, the Tribunal finds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited guilty of environmental non-compliance.
**Therefore, the Tribunal issues the following order and penalty:**
1.  **Immediate Cessation of Non-Compliant Discharge:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
2.  **Upgradation and Functionalization of ETP:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment, submit a comprehensive action plan to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for the complete upgradation, repair, and functionalization of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure consistent
compliance with all applicable NEQS for industrial effluent discharge. This plan must include a detailed timeline, budget, and verifiable milestones for implementation, to be approved by the EPA. The upgraded ETP must be fully operational and demonstrate consistent compliance with NEQS within **one hundred and eighty (180) days** from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Independent Monitoring and Reporting:** For a period of two (2) years following the full functionalization of the upgraded ETP, the Respondent shall submit monthly independent laboratory analysis reports of its treated effluent to the EPA. These reports must be certified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, confirming consistent compliance with NEQS.
4.  **Financial Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for the proven violations of Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
5.  **Daily Penalty for Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, particularly the immediate cessation of non-compliant discharge (Point 1) and the submission/implementation of the ETP upgradation plan (Point 2) within the stipulated timeframes, shall result in a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day of continued non-compliance, until full compliance is achieved, without prejudice to other legal actions.
6.  **Environmental Restoration Costs:** The Tribunal reserves the right to order the Respondent to bear the costs of any environmental remediation or restoration efforts deemed necessary by the EPA to mitigate the damage caused by past discharges, upon assessment by relevant authorities.
7.  **Consequence of Persistent Failure:** Should the Respondent fail to demonstrate consistent compliance with NEQS even after the stipulated upgradation, or persistently fail to adhere to the terms of this order, the Tribunal may consider more stringent measures, including but not limited to, the suspension or revocation of its operating license and/or closure of the polluting industrial unit, as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
8.  **Compliance Review:** The EPA is directed to conduct regular inspections and submit a compliance report to this Tribunal every ninety (90) days for a period of one year, detailing the Respondent's progress and adherence to this order.
This judgment is pronounced this **25th day of October, 2023.**

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
