This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and regulations, particularly concerning the discharge of industrial effluent, and aims to secure compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as mandated by the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Tribunal, vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters related to environmental pollution and non-compliance, heard the parties extensively over several sessions.
The Complainant, through its duly authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent has been operating its textile manufacturing facility without adequately treating its industrial wastewater, resulting in the discharge of untreated or partially treated effluent into the surrounding environment, including nearby drains and water bodies. Specifically, the allegations included consistent violations of the NEQS for industrial effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and other hazardous chemicals associated with textile dyeing and processing. The EPA further contended that despite repeated communications, issuance of show-cause notices dated 15.03.2019 and 01.07.2019, and opportunities for remedial action, the Respondent failed to install a functional and effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or to adequately upgrade its existing facilities to meet the prescribed standards. The Complainant sought a directive for the Respondent to immediately cease the discharge of sub-standard effluent, install/upgrade a compliant ETP, and pay environmental compensation and penalties as provided under the law.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA teams on various dates, including 10.02.2019, 25.06.2019, and 12.11.2019. Each report was accompanied by laboratory analysis certificates from the EPA-accredited laboratory, demonstrating that the Respondent's effluent samples consistently exceeded the NEQS limits for multiple parameters. For instance, the analysis report dated 15.02.2019 showed BOD levels of 350 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), COD levels of 900 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and TSS of 450 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). Subsequent reports indicated similar or only marginally improved figures, confirming a pattern of non-compliance. The Complainant also submitted copies of the show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, which largely consisted of promises to take action without concrete results. Furthermore, photographic evidence depicting the discharge points and the apparent color and turbidity of the effluent was presented.
In response, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, represented by its legal counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. The Respondent argued that it had initiated steps to upgrade its ETP and was committed to environmental protection. They presented a project proposal for a new ETP, dated 15.01.2020, along with a timeline for its installation, claiming that the delay was due to technical complexities and financial constraints. The Respondent also attempted to challenge the methodology of sampling, suggesting that the samples might not be representative or that the EPA's laboratory procedures were flawed. However, no credible expert report or counter-analysis from an independent accredited laboratory was produced to contradict the EPA's findings. The Respondent's defense primarily focused on seeking more time to comply, rather than refuting the factual basis of the pollution levels.
The relevant legal framework governing this matter primarily includes the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Municipal and Industrial Effluents (Revised 2000), as amended from time to time.
*   **Section 11 of PEPA, 1997** prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration, or level that is in excess of the NEQS.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
