This judgment addresses Complaint No. 36/2024, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "
the EPA"), against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint alleges that the Respondent has been operating its industrial facility in violation of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), and its allied rules, specifically regarding the discharge of untreated industrial effluent into public waterways and exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The EPA submitted detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis results confirming the presence of pollutants beyond permissible limits, and photographic evidence to substantiate its claims. Despite multiple notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent allegedly failed to take adequate measures to rectify the violations, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem.
The Respondent, in its reply, denied the allegations, claiming to have an operational effluent treatment plant (ETP) and asserting compliance with environmental regulations. However, during the proceedings, the Tribunal meticulously considered the expert reports and the evidence presented by the EPA, including a cross-verification inspection conducted by an independent panel of experts. This independent assessment conclusively established that the Respondent's ETP was either non-functional, inadequate, or not being operated effectively, resulting in the continuous discharge of untreated or partially treated effluent that consistently failed to meet the prescribed NEQS.
Having heard the learned counsel for both parties, perused the entire record, and meticulously evaluated the evidence adduced, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the Respondent's culpability beyond reasonable doubt. The Respondent has indeed contravened Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging environmental pollutants in excess of the NEQS, and has failed to comply with the directions issued by the EPA under Section 16 of the Act. Such persistent non-compliance demonstrates a disregard for environmental protection laws and the health of the community.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings and the gravity of the environmental violations, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is declared to be in flagrant violation of Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Imposition of Fine:** A penalty of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for the continuous violation of environmental standards and persistent non-compliance with EPA directives. This fine shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
