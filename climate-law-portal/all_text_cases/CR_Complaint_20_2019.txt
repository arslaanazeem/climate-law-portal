This judgment addresses Complaint No. 20/2019, instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and regulations, alleging significant violations by the Respondent, thereby causing environmental degradation and public nuisance.
The Complainant's case, as articulated through the complaint and subsequent submissions, posits that the Respondent industrial unit has been operating in persistent non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and air emissions, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA 1997") and the rules framed thereunder. Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to be either non-functional or operating inefficiently, leading to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the public sewerage system, ultimately polluting surface and groundwater sources. Furthermore, the Complainant contended that the Respondent's stack emissions from its boiler operations exceeded the permissible NEQS limits, contributing to ambient air pollution. The EPA further alleged that despite repeated issuance of Show Cause Notices, warning letters, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures, demonstrating a disregard for environmental protection laws and the health and well-being of the surrounding community. This persistent non
-compliance, the Complainant asserted, necessitated the intervention of this Tribunal to impose appropriate penalties and ensure future adherence to environmental standards.
In response, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. The Respondent contended that it is a responsible corporate entity with a commitment to environmental stewardship. While acknowledging certain operational challenges and occasional deviations from NEQS, particularly concerning the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the Respondent submitted that it had undertaken significant efforts and invested substantially in upgrading its environmental infrastructure. They presented evidence of recent repairs, maintenance schedules, and procurement orders for new equipment for the ETP and air emission control systems. The Respondent further argued that the EPA's monitoring reports might not fully reflect the current operational status or the remedial actions taken, and requested additional time to demonstrate full compliance, citing the complexity of industrial processes and the time required for effective technological upgrades. They also pointed out their contribution to the local economy and employment, urging the Tribunal to adopt a pragmatic approach that encourages compliance rather than imposing punitive measures that could hinder their operations.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses of effluent and emission samples, show-cause notices, warning letters, and the Respondent's replies and submissions. It was observed that the EPA had provided a consistent series of inspection reports, dating back several months prior to the filing of the complaint, which consistently indicated exceedances of NEQS for various parameters in both industrial effluents and stack emissions. The laboratory reports, duly attested, corroborated these findings, showing levels of BOD, COD, TSS, and specific heavy metals in the discharged wastewater, and particulate matter and SOx in air emissions, well
**above the permissible limits.**
**Analysis and Findings of the Tribunal:**
After meticulous review of the documentary evidence, the arguments advanced by learned counsel for both parties, and considering the relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, this Tribunal makes the following findings:
1.  **Established Non-Compliance:** The consistent series of inspection reports and laboratory analyses presented by the EPA, dating back several months prior to the complaint, unequivocally establish a clear pattern of non-compliance by M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited. The Respondent's industrial effluents and air emissions repeatedly exceeded the NEQS, indicating either a non-functional, inefficient, or inadequately maintained Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air emission control systems.
2.  **Failure of Corrective Action:** Despite receiving multiple Show Cause Notices and warning letters, the Respondent's measures to achieve compliance were demonstrably insufficient or unduly delayed, leading to persistent environmental degradation. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's recent claims of investment and upgrades, these actions appear to have been initiated primarily in response to the EPA's enforcement actions and the filing of this complaint, rather than proactive compliance.
3.  **Statutory Obligation:** The Respondent, as a corporate entity operating an industrial unit, bears a fundamental statutory obligation under PEPA 1997 to ensure its operations do not cause environmental pollution or violate the NEQS. Economic contributions, while valuable, do not exempt an entity from its environmental responsibilities.
4.  **Jurisdiction and Powers:** This Tribunal is duly constituted and empowered under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, to hear and decide complaints of environmental pollution and to impose penalties as prescribed under Section 17 of the Act.
The Tribunal finds that the Complainant, the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, has successfully discharged its burden of proof, establishing beyond reasonable doubt that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has committed serious and persistent violations of Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluents and emitting air pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
