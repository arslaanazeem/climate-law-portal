**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15.03.2022, seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to enforce environmental laws and regulations against the Respondent for alleged persistent violations, which the Complainant contends are causing significant environmental degradation and posing risks to public health. The matter has undergone extensive hearings, presentation of evidence, and legal arguments from both sides.
The Complainant's allegations primarily revolve around several key areas of environmental non-compliance. Firstly, it is contended that the Respondent's manufacturing processes, particularly the operation of its glass melting furnaces, emit pollutants into the atmosphere, including particulate matter, oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulphur dioxide (SOx), exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial air emissions. Secondly, the Complainant alleges improper management and discharge of industrial effluent. It is claimed that wastewater generated from various stages of the glass production, including cooling and washing processes, is either inadequately treated or discharged directly into the municipal sewerage system or nearby water bodies without conforming to the NEQS for industrial effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as pH, Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and temperature. Thirdly, the complaint highlights the Respondent's alleged failure to effectively manage hazardous waste generated during its operations, such as spent refractories, rejected glass, and sludge from wastewater treatment, claiming these are often disposed of improperly without following prescribed procedures, posing risks of soil and groundwater contamination. Lastly, the Complainant asserts that the Respondent has failed to obtain or renew necessary No Objection Certificates (NOCs) or environmental approvals for certain operational expansions and continues to operate without full compliance with the conditions stipulated in previously issued Environmental Approval/EIA reports.
In support of its complaint, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials between September 2021 and February 2022. These reports detailed observations of visible smoke plumes from the Respondent's chimneys, a pungent odour in the vicinity of the plant, and evidence of industrial wastewater discharge. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of air and water samples collected during these inspections. These reports, prepared by the EPA-accredited environmental laboratory, indicated that the levels of particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), NOx, and SOx in the air emissions consistently exceeded the NEQS by significant margins. Similarly, the analysis of effluent samples revealed elevated levels of TSS, COD, and a pH outside the permissible range, confirming non-compliance with NEQS for liquid effluents. The Complainant also produced photographs and video footage depicting the discharge points and the nature of the emissions. Furthermore, an expert witness from the EPA, Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director (Technical), testified regarding the methodology of sample collection, analysis, and the implications of the observed pollution levels. Documentary evidence included show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, reminders for compliance, and the Respondent's unsatisfactory responses or failure to implement remedial measures within stipulated timelines.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations. Through its counsel and witness testimony from its Plant Manager, Mr. Raza Ali, the Respondent argued that it operates a state-of-the-art facility equipped with modern pollution control devices, including bag filters for particulate matter control and an effluent treatment plant (ETP) for wastewater. The Respondent submitted its own internal monitoring reports, which, contrary to EPA's findings, purported to show
compliance with the NEQS. The Respondent contended that the EPA's sampling methodology was flawed, alleging that samples might not have been representative or that the chain of custody was not strictly maintained, thereby rendering the laboratory analysis unreliable. They also argued that some emissions might have been intermittent or due to start-up/shut-down procedures, which are not indicative of regular operational non-compliance. Regarding hazardous waste, the Respondent asserted that it engages licensed contractors for the disposal of specified waste materials and maintains proper records thereof, denying any improper disposal. Furthermore, they claimed
that the alleged failure to obtain or renew certain environmental approvals and No Objection Certificates (NOCs) was either due to administrative delays on the part of the authorities or that applications for renewal were pending and being processed. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
