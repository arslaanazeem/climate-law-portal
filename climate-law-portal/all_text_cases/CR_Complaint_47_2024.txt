This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Ghani Glass") concerning alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint, registered on 15.03.2024, seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial orders against the Respondent for causing environmental pollution through the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emission of pollutants exceeding the prescribed standards.
The Complainant alleges that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, has persistently failed to comply with environmental regulations. Specifically, it is contended that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has been discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drains without adequate treatment, and emitting air pollutants from its stacks that exceed the NEQS. The EPA asserts that these activities constitute a violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the NEQS. The Complainant further maintains that despite multiple inspection visits, issuance of show-cause notices dated 15.01.2024 and 05.02.2024, and subsequent final notices, the Respondent has failed to take effective measures to control the pollution to acceptable levels. The EPA claims that the Respondent's actions are causing detrimental effects on public health and the environment, including contamination of water bodies and deterioration of air quality in the vicinity of the factory.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented several pieces of evidence. These included inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on various dates, specifically 10.01.2024, 28.01.2024, and 20.02.2024, detailing observations of inadequately treated effluent discharge and visible emissions. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory were submitted, showing that samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 10.01.2024 and 20.02.2024 consistently exceeded NEQS for parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended
Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals such as Lead (Pb) and Chromium (Cr). Furthermore, air emission monitoring reports dated 10.01.2024 and 20.02.2024 indicated levels of Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Particulate Matter (PM) exceeding the NEQS, confirming the allegations of air pollution. The Complainant also submitted copies of the show-cause notices and final notices issued to the Respondent, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, which failed to demonstrate concrete steps towards achieving compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, despite being duly served with the complaint and notices, appeared through its authorized representative and counsel. In its written reply and oral submissions, the Respondent generally denied the persistent nature of the violations, asserting that it was a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection. The Respondent contended that it had already initiated steps towards upgrading its existing effluent treatment plant (ETP) and installing advanced air pollution control devices (APCDs). It was argued that the procurement and installation of such sophisticated machinery required significant time, financial investment, and technical expertise, and that the process was actively underway. The Respondent requested a lenient view from the Tribunal, seeking a reasonable timeframe to complete the upgrades and achieve full compliance, emphasizing that any drastic measures like immediate closure or heavy penalties would severely impact its operations, production capacity, and the livelihood of its employees. The Respondent also questioned the methodology of some of the EPA's sampling and analysis, though without providing independent verifiable counter-evidence.
This Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence placed on record. The Complainant, through its detailed inspection reports, repeated issuance of notices, and particularly the accredited laboratory analysis reports, has presented compelling and irrefutable evidence demonstrating persistent non-compliance with the NEQS for both industrial effluent discharge and air emissions. The consistency of the violations across multiple samples taken on different dates leaves no doubt that the Respondent has been operating in breach of environmental regulations. The repeated nature of the violations, despite numerous warnings, indicates a systemic failure on the part of
the part of the Respondent to adhere to its statutory environmental obligations. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's submission regarding ongoing efforts to upgrade its facilities, these efforts, as presented, have clearly not been sufficient or timely enough to prevent continuous breaches of the NEQS over an extended period. The mere initiation of steps, without achieving actual compliance, does not absolve an industrial unit from its responsibility under PEPA, 1997. The health and environmental risks posed by the discharge of untreated effluent and excessive air emissions are significant and cannot be overlooked or indefinitely tolerated. The Respondent's questioning of sampling methodology, without presenting credible independent counter-evidence, holds no weight against the consistent findings of an accredited laboratory.
Therefore, based on the overwhelming and uncontroverted evidence presented by the Complainant, this Tribunal finds M/s Ghani Glass Limited in clear and persistent violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards. Such persistent non-compliance, despite repeated warnings, warrants a firm response to ensure environmental protection, deter future violations, and uphold the sanctity of environmental laws.
In light of the aforesaid findings and pursuant to the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 and Section 18 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby issues the following final order:
**ORDER:**
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited, the Respondent, is hereby declared to be in persistent violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for consistently discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is imposed on M/s Ghani Glass Limited for the persistent environmental violations. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
