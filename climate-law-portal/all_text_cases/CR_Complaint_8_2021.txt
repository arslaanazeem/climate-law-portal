This judgment addresses Complaint No. 8/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in Kot Lakhpat Industrial Area, Lahore. The complaint, lodged on May 10, 2021, seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address alleged contraventions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into water bodies, thereby causing environmental degradation and public health hazards.
The Complainant's primary allegations against the Respondent revolve around their consistent failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge. It was alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited operates without an adequately functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or, if one exists, it is not being operated effectively to treat the industrial wastewater generated from its dyeing and processing units. The complaint specifically highlighted that the Respondent has been discharging effluent containing high levels of chemical oxygen demand (COD), biological oxygen demand (BOD), total suspended solids (TSS), and other hazardous parameters far exceeding the permissible limits set under the NEQS. Furthermore, the Complainant contended that despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take substantive corrective measures, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental laws and regulations.
In support of its complaint, the EPA presented a series of documents and witness testimonies. Key evidence included inspection reports dated February 15, 2021, and April 02, 2021, conducted by authorized EPA officials, which documented the visual
observation of untreated effluent discharge and analytical reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points. These reports consistently showed parameters such as COD, BOD, TSS, pH, and oil & grease significantly exceeding the NEQS limits. Furthermore, the Complainant presented copies of various show-cause notices and final notices issued to M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, demanding compliance, all of which, according to the EPA, were met with inadequate or no effective response.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. In its written statement and subsequent arguments, the Respondent contended that it operates a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant and is committed to environmental compliance. They argued that any reported non-compliance might be isolated incidents or due to temporary operational issues, which were promptly addressed. The Respondent challenged the accuracy and methodology of the EPA's sampling and analysis, suggesting that proper chain of custody protocols might not have been strictly followed, thereby rendering the sample results unreliable. They also presented evidence of investments made in upgrading their ETP and claimed to be in the process of achieving full compliance. The Respondent further argued that the imposition of severe penalties would have a detrimental impact on their business operations and employment, urging the Tribunal to consider their ongoing efforts and economic contributions.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the evidence presented by both parties. It was noted that the inspection reports and laboratory analyses provided by the EPA were conducted by accredited laboratories and signed by authorized personnel, establishing a strong prima facie case of non-compliance. The consistent exceedance of multiple NEQS parameters over several months, as evidenced by the EPA's reports, could not be dismissed as isolated incidents. While the Respondent claimed to have an ETP, the analytical results unequivocally demonstrated its ineffectiveness in treating the industrial effluent to the prescribed standards. The Tribunal found the Respondent's arguments regarding the unreliability of EPA's sampling and analysis to be unsubstantiated, as no concrete evidence was provided to rebut the standard procedures followed by the regulatory body. The Tribunal also took into consideration the repeated warnings issued by the EPA, indicating a persistent failure on the part of the Respondent to take timely and adequate corrective measures.
The Tribunal, having considered all material on record, the arguments of the learned counsel for both parties, and the relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 16, and 17, finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been discharging industrial effluent in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby violating Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997. The persistent nature of these violations, despite repeated notices, demonstrates a clear disregard for environmental protection laws and the health and well-being of the public and the environment.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is declared to be in violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Imposition of Fine:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for the environmental damage caused and persistent non-compliance. This fine shall be deposited into the Government Treasury within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
3.  **Mandatory Upgradation/Operationalization of ETP:** The Respondent is directed to immediately ensure the full operationalization and, if necessary, upgradation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently meet all National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluent discharge. The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including timelines for any necessary upgrades, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **fifteen (15) days** of this order.
4.  **Submission of Compliance Reports:** The Respondent shall submit monthly self-monitoring reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of **one (1) year**, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS. The first report shall be due within **forty-five (45) days** of this order.
5.  **EPA Oversight:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is directed to conduct regular, unannounced inspections and sampling of the Respondent's effluent discharge points for a period of one year to ensure strict adherence to NEQS.
6.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of the fine or the directives regarding the ETP and compliance reports, shall result in further legal action, including but not limited to, the imposition of additional daily penalties as provided under Section 17 of the PEPA, 1997, and potential closure of the industrial unit as per law.
No order as to costs.
