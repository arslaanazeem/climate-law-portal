This judgment addresses Complaint No. 38/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), seeking redress for alleged violations of environmental standards and regulations. The primary objective of the proceedings is to ensure compliance with environmental laws, prevent pollution, and impose appropriate penalties on entities found to be in contravention of the applicable legal framework. The Tribunal, being a specialized forum, is tasked with the crucial responsibility of upholding environmental justice and safeguarding the ecological integrity of the province.
The Complainant, the Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, brought forth a series of grave allegations against M/s Nishat Mills Limited. It was contended that the Respondent, operating an industrial facility, was engaged in activities that led to the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under PEPA, 1997. Specifically, the complaint detailed that during routine inspections conducted on various dates, including 15.03.2021 and 28.06.2021, samples of the Respondent's industrial wastewater and stack emissions were collected. Subsequent laboratory analyses, conducted by the EPA's accredited laboratory, revealed significant deviations from the NEQS for parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH in the effluent, and Particulate Matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) in the air emissions. The Complainant further alleged that despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take adequate measures to mitigate the pollution, thereby continuing to pose a substantial threat to public health and the environment, particularly to the aquatic life and groundwater resources in the vicinity, and to the ambient air quality. The complaint also highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to maintain and operate its existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) effectively, or to install necessary upgrades to achieve compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA officials, photographic evidence documenting the discharge points and emission sources, and, most critically, the laboratory analysis reports bearing official seals and signatures, which unequivocally indicated the exceedance of NEQS. The Complainant also produced copies of the show-cause notices issued to
the Respondent, which were duly served, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, or in some instances, lack of a substantive response, all of which culminated in the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal after exhausting preliminary administrative remedies. The Complainant emphasized that the persistent nature of the violations, despite regulatory interventions, underscored the Respondent's deliberate disregard for environmental protection laws and the health and
health and the environment.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
