This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint pertains to alleged violations of environmental standards by M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit operating in the industrial area of Lahore. The EPA seeks appropriate remedies, including penalties and directions for compliance, against the Respondent for its alleged failure to manage industrial effluent in accordance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant's primary contention is that the Respondent has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system, which eventually contaminates surface water bodies, thereby causing environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life. Specifically, the EPA alleges that multiple site inspections conducted at the Respondent's premises on 15.03.2020, 02.06.2020, and 18.08.2020 revealed significant non-compliance. During these inspections, effluent samples were collected from the Respondent's discharge points. Subsequent laboratory analyses of these samples indicated that parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH consistently exceeded the permissible limits stipulated under the NEQS for industrial effluents. The Complainant further asserts that despite the issuance of a Show Cause Notice dated 25.03.2020 and a Final Notice dated 09.07.2020, requiring the Respondent to install or upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and ensure compliance with NEQS, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, thus necessitating the present complaint.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of documents, including detailed site inspection reports prepared by EPA officials, chain of custody forms for the collected effluent samples, and comprehensive laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory. These reports consistently demonstrated values for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH significantly higher than the NEQS, indicating insufficient treatment of industrial wastewater. The Complainant also submitted copies of the Show Cause Notice and Final Notice issued to the Respondent, along with proof of their dispatch and receipt, establishing that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations and given opportunities to rectify the situation. The Respondent, in its defense, primarily
contended that while acknowledging some initial challenges, it had been actively engaged in efforts to improve its effluent treatment processes. The Respondent claimed that the non-compliance noted in the initial inspections was due to unforeseen operational issues and that it had subsequently initiated the procurement and installation of advanced machinery for its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). It further argued that the time frame provided in the notices for full compliance was insufficient given the complexity and financial outlay required for such an upgrade. The Respondent submitted a project plan outlining its proposed ETP upgrade, along with purchase orders for new equipment, as evidence of its commitment to environmental compliance. It also requested additional time to complete the upgrades and achieve full adherence to the NEQS, promising regular updates on its progress.
This Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the documentary evidence placed on record. The core of the Complainant's case rests on the consistent findings of non-compliance as evidenced by multiple site inspection reports and corresponding laboratory analyses. These reports, prepared by certified EPA officials and laboratories, present a clear and unambiguous picture: the Respondent's industrial effluent consistently exceeded the permissible limits for key parameters under the NEQS. The chain of custody forms and laboratory reports appear robust and free from material defects, thus establishing the veracity of the sample collection and analysis process.
Conversely, while the Respondent has presented a plan for future upgrades and purchase orders, these documents do not negate the fact of past and ongoing non-compliance at the time the complaint was filed and during the period covered by the EPA's inspections. The plea for more time, while understandable from a business perspective, does not absolve the Respondent of its statutory obligation to comply with environmental standards. The Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, places a strict liability on industries to ensure their operations do not cause pollution in excess of prescribed standards. The issuance of a Show Cause Notice and a Final Notice provided ample opportunity for the Respondent to take corrective actions, yet the evidence suggests that effective measures were not implemented in a timely manner. The continuous discharge of inadequately treated effluent into the public sewerage system poses a serious threat to public health and the environment, which this Tribunal cannot overlook.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal is satisfied that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, the Respondent, has violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly by discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby causing environmental pollution.
Therefore, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation**: The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby declared to be in contravention of Section 11 and Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Effluents.
2.  **Penalty**: In exercise of the powers conferred under Section 19(2) read with Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, a monetary penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is imposed on the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, for the established violations. This penalty shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Remedial Directions**: The Respondent is directed to:
    a.  Immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent.
    b.  Expeditiously complete the installation and/or upgrade of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged effluent consistently meets the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
    c.  Submit a comprehensive compliance report, including a detailed timeline for the completion of ETP upgrades and a plan for continuous monitoring, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order.
    d.  Provide quarterly progress reports to the EPA, Lahore, until full compliance with NEQS is achieved and maintained for a period of one year.
    e.  Install real-time monitoring equipment at its discharge points, as may be prescribed by the EPA, within one hundred and eighty (180) days from the date of this order.
4.  **Continuing Contravention**: It is further ordered that, in case of continuing contravention after the expiry of the deadlines stipulated in these directions, the Respondent shall be liable to a further fine of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for every day during which the contravention continues, without prejudice to any other legal action that the Environmental Protection Agency may initiate.
5.  **Costs**: The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)**, payable to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab.
This order shall be effective immediately.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **05 February, 2023**.
