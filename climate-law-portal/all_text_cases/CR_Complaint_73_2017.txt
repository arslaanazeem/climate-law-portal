**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 73/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, seeking redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, constituted under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 19
97), has carefully considered the pleadings, evidence, and arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant, through the Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (the Respondent) was operating its industrial facility in violation of various provisions of PEPA, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, the allegations pertained to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into a public sewer/water body, exceeding prescribed limits for various parameters, and improper management of hazardous waste, thereby causing environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and ecosystems. The Complainant submitted inspection reports, laboratory analysis results of effluent samples, and notices issued to the Respondent, all indicating persistent non-compliance.
The Respondent, represented by its counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. They contended that they had installed an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and were making continuous efforts to upgrade their environmental management systems. They further argued that some alleged violations might have been intermittent or due to operational upsets, and that they were committed to achieving full compliance with environmental regulations. They presented evidence of investments made in environmental infrastructure and ongoing initiatives.
Having heard the arguments, examined the documentary evidence, including the inspection reports, laboratory test results, and the Respondent's compliance efforts, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that the Respondent has, at various times, discharged effluent exceeding the NEQS and failed to adequately manage its waste streams in accordance with the prescribed rules and regulations. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's efforts towards environmental improvement, these efforts have not consistently resulted in full compliance with the mandatory environmental standards set forth under PEPA, 1997. The persistent nature of some violations, as evidenced by multiple inspection reports over a period, necessitates a clear directive and penalty to ensure future adherence to environmental laws.
The Tribunal emphasizes that environmental protection is a fundamental right and a statutory obligation. Industrial units bear the primary responsibility for ensuring that their operations do not degrade the environment.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (the Respondent) is hereby directed to immediately cease all operations that lead to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent and to ensure that all discharges strictly comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at all times.
2.  **Upgradation of ETP:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed plan for the upgradation and optimization of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and waste management system to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment. This plan must include timelines for implementation and demonstrate how continuous compliance with NEQS and other environmental regulations will be achieved. The EPA shall review and approve this plan.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
