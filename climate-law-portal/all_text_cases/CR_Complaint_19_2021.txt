**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 19/2021 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a prominent textile manufacturing entity. The complaint, lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), pertains to alleged violations of environmental laws and standards concerning the discharge of industrial effluent. The Tribunal was constituted to ensure the enforcement of environmental regulations and to adjudicate disputes arising from environmental pollution.
The Complainant, representing the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, presented a series of allegations against M/s Nishat Mills Limited. It was contended that the Respondent's textile manufacturing unit, located within the jurisdiction of Lahore, was regularly discharging industrial effluent into the surrounding environment without adequate treatment. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that the quality of the discharged effluent consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for several critical parameters, including but not limited to Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and various heavy metals. The EPA asserted that despite repeated communications, warnings, and show-cause notices, the Respondent failed to take effective measures to comply with the NEQS and the conditions stipulated in their environmental approval. This alleged non-compliance was stated to be causing significant pollution to nearby water bodies and agricultural land, thereby posing a serious threat to public health, aquatic life, and the ecological balance of the area. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if present, was either non-functional, improperly operated, or inadequate to treat the volume and quality of effluent generated by their operations.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on various dates between 2019 and 2021, detailing observations of effluent discharge and ETP operational status. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on three separate occasions (15.03.2021, 28.05.2021, and 10.08.2021). These reports, conducted by an accredited laboratory, consistently indicated that the analyzed samples significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH levels, and in some instances, for specific heavy metals. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the visible discharge of colored and odorous effluent was also submitted. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices, warning letters, and a final Environmental Protection Order (EPO
) issued under Section 16 of the Act, which the Respondent allegedly failed to comply with, leading to the initiation of the present complaint before this Tribunal.
In response to the allegations, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied non-compliance. It was contended that the Respondent operates a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and is committed to environmental stewardship. The Respondent argued that any exceedances noted in the EPA's reports were either isolated incidents, attributable to transient operational fluctuations, or potentially due to flawed sampling methodologies or analytical errors by the Complainant's chosen laboratory. They presented internal monitoring reports and certificates of compliance from their own consultants, asserting that their treated effluent generally meets the NEQS. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed that they had actively engaged with the EPA to address concerns and had invested significantly in upgrading their ETP infrastructure and operational protocols. They requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, asserting that they are in substantial compliance with environmental regulations.
The Tribunal meticulously considered the arguments and evidence presented by both parties. It examined the inspection reports, the laboratory analysis reports, photographic evidence, and the correspondence exchanged between the EPA and the Respondent. The consistent nature of the violations recorded in the independent laboratory reports submitted by the Complainant, showing repeated exceedances of critical parameters over an extended period, weighed heavily in the Tribunal's assessment. The Tribunal noted that while the Respondent claimed to have a functional ETP and provided some internal reports, these reports lacked the independent accreditation and consistency observed in the Complainant's evidence. Furthermore, the Respondent's failure to provide satisfactory explanations for the repeated violations documented by the EPA, despite receiving multiple warnings and show-cause notices, undermined their defense. The Tribunal found the Respondent's arguments regarding sampling or analytical errors to be unsubstantiated by concrete evidence.
The Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, mandates strict adherence to the National Environmental Quality Standards. Section 12 of the Act prohibits the discharge of any effluent or waste that contravenes the NEQS. Section 16 empowers the Agency to issue Environmental Protection Orders to prevent or abate pollution, and non-compliance with such orders constitutes a serious breach. The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the multiple laboratory reports from an accredited source, clearly and consistently demonstrates that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has repeatedly discharged industrial effluent exceeding the NEQS for several key parameters. The Tribunal concludes that the Respondent has failed to operate its ETP effectively or adequately to ensure compliance with the prescribed environmental standards, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing risks to public health and the ecosystem. The repeated nature of the violations and the apparent disregard for the Environmental Protection Order issued by the EPA cannot be overlooked.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal holds M/s Nishat Mills Limited guilty of violating Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and of non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the Environmental Protection Order issued by the Environmental Protection Agency.
Therefore, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Immediate Compliance:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is directed to forthwith cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **ETP Upgrade/Maintenance:** The Respondent shall ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully functional, adequately maintained, and technologically capable of treating all generated industrial effluent to consistently meet or exceed the NEQS. A detailed action plan for any necessary upgrades or operational improvements, along with a timeline for implementation, shall be submitted to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
