**JUDGMENT**

This complaint was filed on 06 September, 2017 before this Tribunal, which exercises jurisdiction over environmental offences committed within the province of Punjab under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The matter pertains to violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by Askari Cement Limited, a Cement facility located at Rawalpindi. The Environmental Protection Order (EPO) bearing No. EPA/EPO/2017/029 was issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and the requisite compliance period has since expired without compliance being achieved.

**I. COMPLAINANT'S ALLEGATIONS**

The Complainant alleged that stack emission tests conducted at the Respondent's plant reveal that PM, SO2 and NOx levels grossly exceed the permissible NEQS limits, and the Respondent has failed to install or maintain adequate air pollution control equipment despite repeated directions by the Punjab EPA.

Specifically, the Complainant's allegations are as follows:

(a) The Respondent's facility has been found discharging/emitting pollutants in excess of NEQS limits in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997;

(b) The Respondent has failed to comply with Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2017/029 issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 within the stipulated compliance period;

(c) The Respondent has not taken adequate remedial and preventive measures despite multiple EPA inspection reports documenting the violations.

**II. EVIDENCE ON RECORD**

**1. EPA Inspection Reports**
   Inspection reports dated 08/11/2017 and 28/02/2017 prepared by authorized Environmental Officers of the Punjab EPA confirming NEQS violations at the Respondent's facility.

**2. Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2017/029**
   EPO issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 directing the Respondent to cease violations and achieve NEQS compliance within the specified period.

**3. Stack Emission Test Report**
   Certified stack emission test report (Ref. No. STACK/2017/0029) confirming exceedances of NEQS limits for PM, SO2 and NOx at the Respondent's production stack.

**4. Photographic Evidence**
   Photographs and video footage recorded during EPA inspection confirming the observed violations.

**5. Non-Compliance Report**
   Report confirming the Respondent's failure to achieve compliance within the period stipulated in EPO No. EPA/EPO/2017/029.

**III. RESPONDENT'S DEFENSE**

The Respondent appeared through its counsel Mr. Farooq Ahmad Khan who contended that the evidence submitted by the Complainant consisted primarily of photocopies of inspection reports and laboratory analyses, which are inadmissible in evidence under the law declared in 2023 CLD 383 (Punjab ET). It was further argued that the Respondent had taken all reasonable steps to achieve environmental compliance.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, Askari Cement Limited, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, Askari Cement Limited, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 600,000/- (Rupees Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 120,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order, including ensuring full operationalization of the Air Pollution Control Device (APCD), and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months commencing from the date of this order, incorporating certified laboratory test results from a NEQS-certified laboratory.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply with the directions in paragraphs 4 and 5 above within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for a period of one (1) year and to report findings to this Tribunal. The EPA is authorized to seal the facility in the event of continued non-compliance.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear the costs of this proceedings including the costs of laboratory analysis and EPA inspection, assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
