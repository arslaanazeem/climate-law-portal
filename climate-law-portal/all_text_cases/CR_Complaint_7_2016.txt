This judgment addresses Complaint No. 7/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint was instituted under the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging grave environmental violations, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent into public waterways without adequate treatment, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment. The Tribunal has diligently examined the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just and equitable decision.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent, a large-scale textile manufacturing facility, was consistently discharging industrial effluent containing hazardous chemicals, dyes, and other pollutants directly into the Lahore Branch Canal and subsequently into the River Ravi, without meeting the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). It was specifically alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if present, was either non-functional, inadequate, or deliberately bypassed, leading to the release of untreated or partially treated wastewater. The EPA further claimed that despite repeated warnings, issuance of notices, and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO), the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, thereby demonstrating a willful disregard for environmental laws and regulations. The Complainant sought stringent penalties and an order compelling the Respondent to comply with environmental standards.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15.03.2015, 22.07.2015, and 01.12.2015. These reports consistently documented observations of dark-colored, foul-smelling effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises directly into the public drainage system. Crucially, the EPA submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected during these inspections. These reports indicated significant exceedances of NEQS for several critical parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and the presence of heavy metals such as Chromium and Lead, far beyond permissible limits. For instance, the report dated 25.07.2015 showed COD levels at 750 mg/L (NEQS 150 mg/L), BOD5 at 300 mg/L (NEQS 80 mg/L), and TSS at 350 mg/L (NEQS 200 mg/L). Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices (e.g., dated 05.05.2015) and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO No. 123/2015 dated 20.08.2015) issued to the Respondent were placed on record, demonstrating the EPA's efforts to secure compliance prior to filing the complaint. The Assistant Director, EPA, Mr. Ahmed Khan, appeared as a witness, reiterating the findings of the inspection teams and the non-cooperative attitude of
the Respondent despite repeated attempts to engage them.**
**The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations.** While acknowledging the operation of a textile manufacturing unit at the specified location, they contended that they maintained a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) designed to treat industrial wastewater in accordance with national standards. It was argued that any occasional exceedances in NEQS, if proven, were isolated incidents attributable to unforeseen operational fluctuations, technical glitches, or external factors beyond their control, rather than a deliberate disregard for environmental regulations. The Respondent further questioned the accuracy and methodology of the EPA's sampling and laboratory analysis, suggesting potential procedural irregularities that could have compromised the integrity of the samples
or chain of custody. They further contended that they had invested significantly in their environmental infrastructure, including regular maintenance and upgrades of their ETP, and that any alleged exceedances were not reflective of a deliberate policy of non-compliance but rather isolated incidents. To substantiate their claims, the Respondent presented maintenance logs for their ETP and a few internal monitoring reports which, they argued, demonstrated their commitment to environmental stewardship.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,200,000/- (Rupees Two Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 440,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
