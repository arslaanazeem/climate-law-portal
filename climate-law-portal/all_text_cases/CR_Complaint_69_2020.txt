This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), located at Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. The complaint, registered on 15.10.2020, seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal has jurisdiction under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate upon such matters and to impose penalties and issue directions as deemed appropriate for the protection of the environment.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the surrounding environment, specifically into a local drain leading to River Ravi. Furthermore, the complaint detailed concerns regarding air emissions from the Respondent's facility, particularly from its boiler stacks and process units, which were suspected to exceed prescribed NEQS limits. The EPA asserted that despite repeated warnings, notices under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent failed to implement effective pollution control measures. Specific allegations included the discharge of effluent with high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH outside the permissible range, along with excessive particulate matter and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) in air emissions, posing a significant threat to public health and the local ecosystem.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted on 22.07.2020, 05.09.2020, and 28.09.2020. These reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis results of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points and air emission samples from its stacks. The analysis, conducted by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently showed exceedances of the NEQS for industrial effluents and air emissions. For instance, the effluent sample collected on 05.09.2020 indicated a COD level of 650 mg/L against the NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, BOD of 280 mg/L against 80 mg/L, and TSS of 320 mg/L against 200 mg/L, with a pH of 4.5. Air emission analysis from the boiler stack on the same date revealed particulate matter concentration of 600 mg/Nm3 against the NEQS limit of 400 mg/Nm3. The Complainant also submitted copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 15.08.2020 and 01.10.2020, and the Respondent's replies, which the EPA deemed unsatisfactory. An expert witness from the EPA's technical department testified about the methodology of sample collection and analysis, confirming the accuracy of the results and the severity of the violations.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations, arguing that it has invested significantly in environmental protection measures, including an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices. The Respondent submitted its own internal monitoring reports and a report
from a private environmental consultant, which purportedly showed compliance with NEQS on certain dates. The Respondent contended that the EPA's sampling methodology was flawed, and the samples might not have been representative of their regular discharge or emissions. They claimed that their ETP and APC systems were fully operational and regularly maintained, and any exceedances were isolated incidents or due to unforeseen operational disruptions, which were promptly rectified. Furthermore, the Respondent argued that imposing a heavy penalty would severely impact its financial viability and employment, requesting leniency and further opportunity to demonstrate compliance.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent, and has meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence and expert testimonies. While the Respondent presented internal reports and a private consultant's report claiming compliance, these reports were not supported by independent, accredited laboratory analyses conducted at the time of the alleged violations, nor did they comprehensively address the specific dates and parameters highlighted in the EPA's inspection reports. The EPA's evidence, comprising detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis results from an accredited laboratory, and expert testimony on sampling and analysis protocols, provides a consistent and compelling narrative of sustained non-compliance. The repeated exceedances of NEQS for critical parameters such as COD, BOD, TSS, pH, particulate matter, and SO2, as evidenced by the EPA's samples taken on multiple occasions, cannot be dismissed as isolated incidents. The pH level of 4.5, for instance, is a clear indicator of highly acidic discharge, posing immediate environmental hazards. The Tribunal finds the methodology employed by the EPA for sample collection and analysis to be sound and reliable, and the results to be credible. The show-cause notices issued by the EPA and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies further underscore the Respondent's failure to take adequate and timely corrective actions despite repeated opportunities.
Based on the overwhelming evidence presented by the Complainant, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has indeed violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Section 12 thereof, and has failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluents and air emissions, thereby causing
significant environmental degradation and posing a serious threat to public health and the local ecosystem. The Tribunal holds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited responsible for these grave violations.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is found guilty of violating Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and failing to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and air emissions.
2.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent and the emission of pollutants exceeding NEQS limits.
3.  **Environmental Improvement Plan:** The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order, submit a comprehensive Environmental Improvement Plan (EIP) to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore. This EIP must detail specific measures, timelines, and financial commitments for upgrading its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices to ensure consistent compliance with NEQS. The EIP shall be subject to review and approval by the EPA.
4.  **Implementation and Monitoring:** The Respondent shall implement the approved EIP within the stipulated timelines and shall submit monthly compliance reports, including independent laboratory analysis results of its effluent and air emissions, to the EPA for a period of one year, or until consistent compliance is demonstrated to the satisfaction of the EPA.
5.  **Penalty:** For the proven violations and sustained non-compliance, a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, payable to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
6.  **Continuing Offence:** Should the Respondent fail to comply with the directions contained herein, particularly regarding the cessation of violations and implementation of the EIP, it shall be liable to a further penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** for each day the failure or contravention continues, in addition to any other legal action the EPA may deem appropriate under PEPA, 1997.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only).

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **03 September, 2023**.
