**JUDGMENT**
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks enforcement against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Tribunal, duly constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, and having jurisdiction over environmental matters within Punjab, has carefully considered the submissions and evidence presented by both parties.
The Complainant, through its representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various paper and packaging products, was discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the River Ravi without adequate treatment, thereby contravening the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. Specifically, it was averred that routine inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed consistent exceedances of NEQS parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. The Complainant contended that these discharges posed a significant threat to public health and the environment, causing water pollution and ecological damage. Notices were reportedly issued to the Respondent prior to the filing of this complaint, demanding compliance, but without satisfactory response.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated 15.03.2023, 28.03.2023, and 10.04.2023. These reports detailed observations of the Respondent's effluent discharge point and noted visual signs of pollution. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2023/04/01, EPA/LAB/2023/04/15, and EPA/LAB/2023/04/28. These reports indicated that samples collected from the Respondent's final discharge point consistently showed BOD levels ranging from 150-220 mg/L (against NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), COD levels from 350-500 mg/L (against NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and TSS levels from 250-380 mg/L (against NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). The pH levels were also found to be outside the permissible range of 6-9. The Complainant also produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director EPA, who personally supervised the sampling process, and Mr. Bilal Hussain, EPA Chemist, who confirmed the accuracy and methodology of the laboratory analyses. Photographs and video evidence depicting the nature of the discharge were also submitted.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, vehemently denied the allegations. Through its legal counsel and the testimony of Mr. Farhan Ali, Plant Manager, the Respondent asserted that it operates a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and is committed to environmental compliance. They argued that the samples taken by the EPA might not have been representative, or that the sampling methodology itself was flawed. The Respondent presented maintenance records for their ETP, attempting to demonstrate regular upkeep. They also submitted their internal monitoring reports, which, contrary to EPA's findings, showed compliance with NEQS. The Respondent
submitted its internal monitoring reports, which, contrary to EPA's findings, showed compliance with NEQS. The Respondent further contested the reliability of the EPA's sampling and analysis methodology, suggesting possible procedural irregularities or contamination during sample collection and transportation. It was argued that the Respondent, being a reputable industrial entity, has invested significantly in its environmental infrastructure, including a sophisticated ETP, and maintains a strong commitment to
environmental responsibility. They further submitted that the EPA’s sampling and analysis procedures lacked the necessary transparency and that the chain of custody for the samples was not adequately established, thereby casting doubt on the veracity of the laboratory reports presented by the Complainant. The Respondent also requested an independent third-party sampling and analysis to verify the claims, which request was considered by the Tribunal but ultimately deemed unnecessary given the robust evidence

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
