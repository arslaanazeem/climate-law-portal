This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") located in Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15th May 2023, alleges serious violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent, emission of hazardous air pollutants, and improper management of solid and hazardous waste, all detrimental to public health and the environment. The Tribunal initiated proceedings upon receiving the complaint, ensuring due process was afforded to both parties through multiple hearings, submission of written statements, and presentation of evidence.
The Complainant alleged that M/s Packages Limited, a large manufacturing facility, has been operating in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated/Improperly Treated Effluent:** That the Respondent was discharging industrial wastewater containing high levels
of pollutants (e.g., BOD, COD, TSS, heavy metals) directly into the municipal sewerage system/water bodies without adequate treatment, thereby polluting water resources and posing risks to aquatic life and human health.
2.  **Emission of Hazardous Air Pollutants:** That the Respondent's industrial processes were emitting smoke, particulate matter, and other hazardous air pollutants (e.g
., sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter (PM2.5/PM10), and volatile organic compounds (VOCs)) into the atmosphere, exceeding permissible limits and contributing to air pollution, smog formation, and respiratory ailments among nearby communities.
3.  **Improper Management of Solid and Hazardous Waste:** That the Respondent was failing to properly segregate, store, treat, and dispose of its industrial solid waste and hazardous waste, leading to land contamination, groundwater pollution, and risks of exposure to hazardous substances for workers and the public.
The Tribunal initiated proceedings upon receiving the complaint, ensuring due process was afforded to both parties through multiple hearings, submission of written statements, and presentation of evidence. The Complainant presented detailed inspection reports, laboratory analyses of effluent and emission samples, photographic evidence, and expert testimonies, all indicating consistent non-compliance by the Respondent with the NEQS and the provisions of PEPA, 1997. Specific evidence included reports from EPA-accredited laboratories showing BOD, COD, and TSS levels in discharged effluent significantly above the prescribed limits, and stack emission reports indicating exceedances for PM and NOx.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its counsel, vehemently denied the extent of the alleged violations, though admitting to certain operational challenges. In their written statement and subsequent submissions, they contended that they have invested substantially in environmental management systems and pollution control technologies. They argued that any instances of non-compliance were isolated, temporary, or attributable to unforeseen operational malfunctions, and that they were actively undertaking measures to upgrade their treatment facilities and ensure compliance with NEQS. They also presented evidence of past environmental audits and certifications, asserting their commitment to environmental stewardship and corporate social responsibility. They further submitted a proposed action plan outlining steps for improvement.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses, expert testimonies, and the Respondent's explanations and proposed plan. While acknowledging the Respondent's claims of investment in environmental measures, the overwhelming evidence substantiated the Complainant's allegations. The samples collected by the EPA consistently showed levels of pollutants in the discharged effluent and stack emissions significantly exceeding the NEQS. Furthermore, the waste management practices observed during inspections were found to be inadequate, particularly concerning the segregation, storage, and disposal of hazardous waste. The Tribunal found that the Respondent had failed to implement and consistently operate effective pollution control measures as required by law, leading to continuous environmental degradation and public health risks. The operational challenges cited by the Respondent do not absolve them of their legal responsibility to comply with environmental standards. The Tribunal found that the violations were systemic and ongoing, necessitating stringent action to ensure future compliance.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
