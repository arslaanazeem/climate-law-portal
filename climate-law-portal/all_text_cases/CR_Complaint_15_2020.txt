This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15.03.2020, pertains to alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), as amended by the Punjab Environmental Protection (Amendment) Act, 2012, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge. The Tribunal, established under PEPA, 1997, possesses the requisite jurisdiction to adjudicate upon environmental complaints and impose penalties for non-compliance with environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant's allegations center on the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluent discharge and air emissions. Specifically, it was alleged that during a routine inspection conducted by EPA officials on 05.02.2020, the Respondent's industrial facility was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into the River Ravi. Samples of the effluent were collected from the discharge point and subjected to laboratory analysis. The analysis reports, appended to the complaint, indicated levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and Oil & Grease significantly exceeding the NEQS. Furthermore, the complaint also highlighted concerns regarding emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks, suggesting a lack of proper air pollution control measures. A show-cause notice, dated 10.02.2020, was issued to the Respondent, demanding an explanation for these violations and requiring immediate remedial actions. Despite the notice, the Complainant contended that satisfactory compliance or a credible action plan was not furnished by the Respondent, necessitating the present complaint before this Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included the detailed inspection report dated 05.02.2020, prepared by the EPA inspection team, which meticulously documented the observations at the Respondent's facility, including the absence of a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or inadequate operation thereof, and visible signs of pollution. Crucially, the Complainant submitted the laboratory analysis report (Ref. No. EPA/Lab/2020/035, dated 18.02.2020) from the EPA's accredited laboratory, which quantified the pollutants in the collected effluent samples. This report categorically showed BOD levels at 180 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD at 450 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), TSS at 220 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), and Oil & Grease at 40 mg
/L (NEQS: 10 mg/L). The inspection report and the laboratory analysis, coupled with the show-cause notice and the Respondent's alleged inadequate response, formed the bedrock of the Complainant's case, asserting a clear violation of Sections 11 and 12 of PEPA, 1997, which mandate adherence to NEQS for effluent discharge
and air emissions.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently contested the allegations. While acknowledging its industrial operations and the presence of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the Respondent contended that it was fully committed to environmental compliance. It was argued that the reported exceedances, if any, might be attributable to a temporary operational anomaly or an isolated incident, rather than a systemic failure. The Respondent submitted a written reply to the show-cause notice, outlining ongoing efforts to upgrade its ETP and install advanced air pollution control devices. A proposed timeline for these upgrades was also furnished, requesting the Tribunal's indulgence and time to achieve full and sustained compliance. Furthermore, the Respondent questioned the methodology of sample collection and analysis, hinting at potential procedural lapses, though no concrete evidence was adduced to discredit the EPA's accredited laboratory report.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
