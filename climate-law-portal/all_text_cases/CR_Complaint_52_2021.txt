This complaint, bearing No. 52/2021, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, on 15th March 2021, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, duly constituted under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), assumed jurisdiction to adjudicate the matter concerning the Respondent's industrial operations and their environmental impact within the territorial limits of Punjab.
The gravamen of the complaint lies in the allegations that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the surrounding water bodies, specifically the Hudiara Drain, and emitting pollutants into the ambient air, in excess of the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Complainant detailed several instances of non-compliance, including: (a) discharge of wastewater with levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding NEQS; (b) emission of Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), Sulfur Oxides (SOx), and Particulate Matter (PM10) beyond permissible limits; (c) improper handling and disposal of hazardous waste, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination; and (d) failure to submit regular self-monitoring reports as required by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). It was further alleged that despite repeated warnings, show-cause notices, and directives issued by the EPA on 10th November 2020 and 2nd February 2021, the Respondent failed to implement adequate pollution control measures or rectify the violations, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a body of evidence including:
1.
laboratory analysis reports dated 18th February 2021 and 5th March 2021, confirming the levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), Sulfur Oxides (SOx), and Particulate Matter (PM10) in the Respondent's effluent and
emissions exceeding the NEQS.
2.  Copies of show-cause notices dated 10th November 2020 and 2nd February 2021, and the Respondent's failure to adequately respond or comply with the directives contained therein.
3.  Inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on 15th January 2021 and 28th February 2021, documenting observed environmental violations at the Respondent's industrial premises.
4.  Photographic and videographic evidence illustrating the discharge of discolored effluent into the Hudiara Drain and visible air emissions from the Respondent's stacks.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, despite being duly served with notice of these proceedings on 25th March 2021, failed to file a written statement in response to the complaint within the stipulated time. Subsequently, several opportunities were afforded to the Respondent to present its defense, including adjournments granted on 15th April 2021, 10th May 2021, and 7th June 2021. However, the Respondent consistently failed to appear before the Tribunal or provide any satisfactory explanation for its alleged non-compliance. Consequently, the Tribunal proceeded to hear the matter ex-parte against the Respondent on 15th July 2021, relying solely on the evidence and arguments presented by the Complainant.
Having meticulously reviewed the complaint, the documentary evidence, and the submissions made by the Complainant, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been in persistent violation of various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The unchallenged laboratory reports, inspection findings, and the Respondent's failure to respond to official directives or appear before this Tribunal, collectively affirm the allegations of unauthorized discharge of untreated industrial effluent, excessive air emissions, and improper hazardous waste management. Such conduct constitutes a grave threat to the environment and public health, warranting immediate and stringent action.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested under Sections 16, 17, and 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby issues the following final order against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby declared to be in persistent violation of Sections 11, 12, 13, and 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) in respect of its effluent discharge, air emissions, and hazardous waste management.
2.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into any water body, including the Hudiara Drain, and to ensure that all emissions into the ambient air comply strictly with the prescribed NEQS.
3.  **Installation/Upgradation of Treatment Facilities:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, install, commission, or upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to effectively treat all industrial effluent and air emissions to meet the NEQS.
4.  **Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this order, develop and implement a comprehensive Hazardous Waste Management Plan, ensuring proper segregation, storage, treatment, and disposal of all hazardous waste in accordance with applicable environmental regulations and guidelines.
5.  **Submission of Self-Monitoring Reports:** The Respondent shall, commencing from the first month after the ninety-day compliance period, submit monthly self-monitoring reports to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, detailing the quality of its effluent discharge, air emissions, and hazardous waste management practices, duly certified by an EPA-approved laboratory.
6.  **Environmental Audit:** The Respondent shall, at its own cost, arrange for an independent environmental audit by an EPA-approved consultant within **six (6) months** from the date of this order, and submit the audit report to the EPA and this Tribunal.
7.  **Penalty:** For the persistent violations established in this complaint, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby imposed an environmental penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)**. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
8.  **Daily Penalty for Non-Compliance:** In the event of continued non-compliance with any part of this order after the expiry of the specified timelines, the Respondent shall be liable to a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day of non-compliance, until full compliance is achieved. The EPA is authorized to monitor and enforce this daily penalty.
9.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)**, to be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days**.
10. **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with the terms of this order may result in further legal action, including but not limited to, sealing of the industrial unit, revocation of environmental approvals, and initiation of criminal proceedings under the relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
The Registrar of the Tribunal is directed to ensure that a copy of this order is immediately dispatched to both the Complainant and the Respondent.
