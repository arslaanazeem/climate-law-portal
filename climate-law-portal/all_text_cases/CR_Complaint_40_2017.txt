**JUDGMENT**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 40/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant, representing the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, initiated this complaint on the grounds that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited was discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain without adequate treatment, causing environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life. Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections and subsequent effluent sample analyses revealed parameters exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The EPA had, on several occasions prior to the filing of this complaint, issued show-cause notices and warning letters to the Respondent, directing them to comply with environmental standards and install/upgrade their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), but without satisfactory response or sustained compliance. The core allegation is the persistent failure of the Respondent to treat its industrial wastewater to meet NEQS before discharge, thereby contravening the provisions of the Act.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, effluent sample collection forms, and laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory. These reports, dated between January 2016 and August 2017, consistently indicated that the discharged effluent from M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited contained levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding the NEQS. For instance, a report dated 15.03.2017 showed COD at 350 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), BOD5 at 180 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), and TSS at 250 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L). Furthermore, the Complainant submitted copies of show-cause notice No. EPA/HO/LHR/2016/543 dated 25.04.2016 and subsequent reminder No. EPA/HO/LHR/2017/121 dated 15.02.2017, demonstrating that the Respondent had been duly informed of their non-compliance and given opportunities to rectify the situation. The Assistant Director, EPA, also appeared as a witness, reiterating the agency's findings and the Respondent's protracted non-compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel and authorized representative, contested the allegations. They argued that they had indeed installed an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and were making continuous efforts to optimize its performance. They presented some internal laboratory reports and invoices for chemicals purchased for their ETP, aiming to demonstrate their commitment to environmental compliance. They also contended that the EPA's sampling methodology might not always be representative, and that occasional fluctuations in effluent quality are inherent in textile processing. They requested leniency, citing the significant investment already made in their ETP and their contribution to the national economy. However, they failed to produce any credible, independent laboratory reports from an accredited third-party showing consistent compliance with NEQS during the period in question.
This Tribunal has carefully considered the evidence presented by both parties in light of the relevant legal provisions. The core legal framework governing this complaint is the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, Section 11 of the Act prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste water or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Section 12 mandates that no proponent of a project shall commence construction or operation unless an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) is filed with the Agency and approval is granted. While the current complaint primarily focuses on Section 11, the spirit of Section 12 underscores the importance of
preventing environmental degradation at the very outset of any industrial activity and ensuring that industrial operations are conducted in an environmentally responsible manner. The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the consistent laboratory analysis reports spanning over a significant period (January 2016 to August 2017), unequivocally demonstrates that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been discharging industrial effluent with parameters far exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The parameters like Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) were consistently found to be substantially higher than permissible limits, indicating a clear and persistent failure to adequately treat wastewater before discharge. The show-cause notices and reminders issued by the EPA further substantiate that the Respondent was not only aware of its non-compliance but also failed to take effective and sustained corrective measures despite repeated opportunities.
While the Respondent claimed to have installed an ETP and made efforts towards compliance, the absence of credible, independent third-party laboratory reports demonstrating consistent adherence to NEQS during the period of alleged violations significantly weakens their defense. Internal reports, without external validation, cannot outweigh the consistent findings of the EPA's accredited laboratory. The argument regarding occasional fluctuations or sampling methodology also fails to explain the persistent and significant exceedances observed over multiple inspections. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent's actions constitute a clear violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain poses a serious threat to public health, aquatic ecosystems, and the overall environment, warranting stringent action.
**ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, and having established the persistent non-compliance and violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:
1.  **Penalty:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 2,500,000/- (Rupees Two Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** for the persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards and Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
