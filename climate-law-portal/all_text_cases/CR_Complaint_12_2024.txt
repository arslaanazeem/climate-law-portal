This judgment addresses the environmental complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in the industrial area of Lahore. The complaint, filed on 15th January 2024, seeks the Tribunal's intervention and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into public drains and excessive air emissions. This Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the PEPA, 1997, is vested with the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental standards.
The Complainant, represented by Legal Officer Mr. Adnan Ali, asserted that the Respondent's textile manufacturing operations have consistently failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial liquid effluents and air emissions. Specifically, the allegations include:
1.  Discharge of industrial effluent with parameters (Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and colour) significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS, directly into the public sewerage system/drain, thereby polluting the aquatic environment and posing risks to public health.
2.  Failure to operate and maintain an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite repeated warnings and directives from the EPA.
3.  Emission of air pollutants (particulate matter, oxides of nitrogen, sulfur dioxide) from its boiler and process stacks in excess of the NEQS for industrial air emissions, particularly due to inadequate or non-functional air pollution control devices (APCDs).
4.  Non-compliance with the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2000, by failing to submit regular environmental monitoring reports as required.
5.  Disregard of Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/LHR/2023/589 dated 20th November 2023, which directed the Respondent to rectify the aforementioned violations within 30 days.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant adduced the following evidence:
1.  **Inspection Report No. EPA/LHR/Insp/2023/112 dated 15th October 2023:** This report detailed observations of dark-coloured effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises directly into the municipal drain, indicating insufficient treatment. It also noted visible black smoke emanating from the factory stacks.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/2023/456 dated 25th October 2023:** This report presented the results of effluent samples collected on 15th October 2023. The analysis revealed BOD levels of 350 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD of 900 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), TSS of 450 mg/L (NEQS:
200 mg/L), pH of 5.5 (NEQS: 6-9), and a highly turbid and dark blue colour, indicating significant deviation from the prescribed standards.
3.  **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/Air/2023/187 dated 28th October 2023:** This report presented the results of air emissions monitoring conducted on 15th October 2023, indicating particulate matter (PM) emissions of 750 mg/Nm³ (NEQS: 250 mg/Nm³) and Sulfur Dioxide (SO2) emissions of 1200 mg/Nm³ (NEQS: 600 mg/Nm³) from the Respondent's boiler stack, confirming the Complainant's assertions regarding excessive air pollution.

**Environmental Protection Order (EPO) No. EPA/LHR/2023/589 dated 20th November 2023:** This order, duly served upon the Respondent, explicitly directed the Respondent to cease the discharge of untreated effluent, control air emissions within NEQS, and ensure the proper functioning of its ETP and APCDs within 30 days. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
