**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 18/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "Respondent"). The complaint was instituted on March 15, 2022, alleging violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, presided over by Mr. Justice (R) Nasir Saeed Sheikh, heard the arguments from both sides and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, an industrial entity operating in Lahore, has been discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and subsequently into the Hudiara Drain without adequate treatment, thereby violating the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for liquid effluent. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted on various dates, including December 10, 2021, January 20, 2022, and March 05, 2022, the Complainant's inspection teams observed the discharge of effluent from the Respondent's premises. Samples of the discharged effluent were collected and subsequently analyzed at the certified laboratory of the Environmental Protection Department, Government of Punjab. The laboratory reports, annexed with the complaint, consistently indicated that several parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeded the permissible limits stipulated in the NEQS. Furthermore, the Complainant asserted that a show-cause notice dated February 15, 2022, was issued to the Respondent, calling upon them to explain the violations and take remedial measures. Despite this, the Respondent allegedly failed to take effective steps to comply with the NEQS, necessitating the filing of the instant complaint for legal action and imposition of penalties under PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of documentary and oral evidence. The primary documentary evidence included three detailed inspection reports (Exh. C-1, C-3, C-5) prepared by the EPA inspection teams on December 10, 2021, January 20, 2022, and March 05, 2022, respectively, which meticulously documented the observations of effluent discharge. Crucially, corresponding laboratory analysis reports (Exh. C-2, C-4, C-6) from the Environmental Protection Department's certified laboratory were submitted, unequivocally demonstrating that the collected effluent samples exceeded the NEQS limits for COD, BOD, TSS, and pH. Photographs (Exh. C-7) depicting the discharge point and the visibly polluted effluent were also tendered. Additionally, copies of the show-cause notice dated February 15, 20
22 (Exh. C-8), and the subsequent reminder notices (Exh. C-9, C-10) were also placed on record. The Complainant's authorized representative, Mr. Adnan Khan, appeared and reiterated the contents of the complaint, emphasizing the persistent nature of the violations and the Respondent's failure to adequately address them despite repeated warnings.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
