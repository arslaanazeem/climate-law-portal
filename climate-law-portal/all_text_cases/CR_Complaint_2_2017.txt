This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15th March 2017, seeks the intervention of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging severe violations of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent. This Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, possesses the statutory mandate to adjudicate environmental disputes and impose penalties for non-compliance with environmental regulations.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were causing significant environmental degradation, specifically through the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The core allegations included:
a) Discharge of industrial wastewater containing high levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and heavy metals, directly into the public sewerage system/drainage channels, without adequate treatment, thereby violating NEQS for industrial effluents.
b) Emission of particulate matter (PM), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Sulphur Oxides (SOx) from its glass melting furnaces and other processes, exceeding the NEQS for industrial air emissions, contributing to ambient air pollution.
c) Failure to install and/or properly operate effective pollution control devices, specifically an adequately designed and functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate air pollution control equipment (e.
g., scrubbers, bag filters) for its emissions sources, leading to persistent non-compliance.
The Complainant contended that despite repeated notices and opportunities provided to the Respondent to rectify these breaches, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures, thereby necessitating the filing of the instant complaint before this Tribunal.
**Respondent's Defense:**
The Respondent, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it operates in full compliance with all applicable environmental laws and NEQS. It was contended that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has invested substantially in modern production technologies and pollution control measures, including an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control equipment, which are regularly maintained. The Respondent submitted that any occasional exceedances of NEQS, if proven, were minor, temporary, or attributable to unforeseen operational upsets, and that they were actively working towards continuous improvement. The Respondent further argued that the EPA's sampling and testing methodologies might be flawed or not representative of their overall operations and requested the dismissal of the complaint.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
