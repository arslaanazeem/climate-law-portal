### JUDGMENT
The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), through its Assistant Director, filed the present complaint on September 15, 2023, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial unit engaged in glass manufacturing, situated in Lahore. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging grave environmental violations and non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of environmental law. The Tribunal, having duly constituted and seized of the matter, proceeded to hear the parties and examine the evidence presented.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial operations were in flagrant disregard of environmental regulations. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. **Operating without a valid Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA):** The Respondent allegedly commenced and continued significant expansion projects and operational activities without obtaining the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval or No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and the EIA/IEE Regulations, 2000.
b. **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent:** It was contended that the Respondent was directly discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the public sewerage system/drain, which eventually leads to irrigation channels, in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS for liquid effluent. Multiple inspections and laboratory analyses reportedly confirmed the presence of various pollutants including heavy metals, high Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) far exceeding the prescribed limits.
c. **Emission of Air Pollutants beyond Prescribed Limits:** The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's glass manufacturing process, particularly from its furnaces and stacks, was emitting particulate matter, Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and other hazardous air pollutants significantly above the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, thereby contributing to ambient air pollution and posing health risks to nearby communities.
d. **Improper Management and Disposal of Solid and Hazardous Waste:** The complaint further highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to properly manage and dispose of industrial solid waste, including cullet, refractories, and other process waste, some of which was categorized as hazardous. It was claimed that waste was being indiscriminately dumped on open land within and outside the factory premises, contrary to Section 13 and 14 of PEPA, 1997, and relevant waste management rules.
e. **Non-Compliance with Previous Notices:** The Complainant submitted that despite numerous warning letters, show-cause notices (e.g., dated June 10, 2023, and July 25, 2023), and directions issued by the EPA for rectifying the environmental deficiencies, the Respondent failed to take substantive corrective measures, demonstrating a persistent pattern of non-compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence, which included:
i. **Inspection Reports:** Detailed reports prepared by EPA technical teams on various dates (e.g., May 20, 2023, and August 05, 2023) documenting the observed violations, operational status, and lack of environmental controls.
ii. **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certificates of analysis from accredited laboratories confirming the exceedance of NEQS for liquid effluent and gaseous
emissions.
iii. **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual records depicting the discharge points, smoke emissions from stacks, and improper waste dumping sites within and around the factory premises.
iv. **Copies of Warning Letters and Show-Cause Notices:** Documentation proving that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations and given opportunities to rectify them, which they failed to avail.
The Respondent, despite
being duly served with notices and afforded ample opportunity to present its defense, either failed to appear or presented a defense that was found to be unsubstantiated and lacking in credible evidence to rebut the serious allegations leveled by the Complainant. The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the comprehensive documentary and visual evidence submitted by the Environmental Protection Agency.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
