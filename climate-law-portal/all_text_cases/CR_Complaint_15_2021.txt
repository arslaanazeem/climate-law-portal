This judgment addresses Complaint No. 15/2021 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The essence of the complaint revolves around the Respondent's failure to adequately treat industrial effluent and control air emissions, thereby causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and the environment, in contravention of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA, 1997") and its allied rules and regulations.
The Complainant's allegations primarily stem from multiple inspections conducted at the Respondent's premises. It was alleged that on various dates, specifically an initial inspection on 1st March 2021, and a follow-up inspection on 15th April 2021, EPA officials observed the discharge of industrial effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into public waterways without adequate treatment. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly its boiler and process stacks, were emitting smoke and particulate matter that appeared to exceed permissible limits, contributing to air pollution in the surrounding residential and commercial areas. Samples of the discharged effluent were collected during these inspections, and laboratory analyses subsequently revealed that key parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels were consistently above the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. Similarly, observations indicated potential exceedances of NEQS for air emissions, specifically particulate matter. A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on 20th April 2021, detailing these violations and requiring remedial action, followed by an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 10th May 2021, directing immediate cessation of non-compliant discharges and emissions, and requiring the submission of a compliance plan. The Complainant asserted that the Respondent failed to fully
comply with the directives contained in the show-cause notice and the EPO, necessitating the filing of this complaint before the Tribunal for enforcement and imposition of penalties as stipulated under the PEPA, 1997.**

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
