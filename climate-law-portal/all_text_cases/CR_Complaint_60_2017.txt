This complaint, bearing No. 60/2017, was filed before this Tribunal on 15th May 2017 by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, acting on behalf of the Provincial Government. The complaint seeks punitive action, including the imposition of penalties and directions for environmental compliance, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is duly established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental laws.
The allegations leveled by the Complainant against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited are grave and pertain primarily to uncontrolled discharge of industrial effluent and emissions of air pollutants beyond permissible limits. Specifically, the EPA alleged that during routine inspections conducted on 10th February 2017 and 28th March 2017, the Respondent's textile dyeing and finishing unit was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater directly into the Lahore Branch Canal and emitting untreated smoke and particulate matter from its boiler stacks. Laboratory analysis of samples collected during these inspections revealed significant exceedances of NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, chromium, and lead in the effluent. The measured COD levels were reported to be 550 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, and TSS at 300 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L. Furthermore, air emission monitoring indicated particulate matter (PM10) concentrations of 750 µg/m³ against a NEQS limit of 500 µg/m³ for industrial units. The Complainant asserted that these violations posed a serious threat to public health, aquatic life, and the general environment, causing nuisance and degrading natural resources. A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on 15th April 2017, requiring an explanation and immediate remedial measures, but the response received was deemed unsatisfactory, leading to the filing of the present complaint. The Complainant contended that the Respondent had failed to install or properly operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and adequate air pollution control devices, despite repeated prior warnings and directives.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included the official inspection reports dated 10th February 2017 and 28th March 2017, prepared by EPA's field teams, detailing the observations of pollution discharge points and air emission sources. Crucially, the Complainant submitted certified laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited environmental laboratory, confirming the NEQS violations for both wastewater effluent and air emissions. These reports meticulously listed the parameters analyzed, the measured values, and the corresponding NEQS limits, clearly illustrating the extent of the exceedances. Photographic and videographic evidence, capturing the visible pollution plumes from the stacks and the discharge of colored effluent into the canal, was also tendered. The Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, appeared as a witness, reiterating the findings of the inspections and reports, and was subjected to cross-examination. He affirmed the scientific validity of the sampling and analysis methodologies employed. The Complainant also produced copies of the show-cause notice dated 15th April 2017 and the Respondent's reply,
which was subsequently found to be unsatisfactory by the Complainant, leading to the filing of the present complaint.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its learned counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and vehemently contested the allegations. In its written statement and subsequent arguments, the Respondent largely denied the persistent non-compliance, asserting that it had an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and
adequate air pollution control devices in place. The Respondent's counsel argued that any exceedances noted by the EPA were either temporary operational aberrations, possibly due to maintenance work or unforeseen technical glitches, or attributable to flaws in the EPA's sampling and analysis methodology. They contended that the ETP, though perhaps not always operating at peak efficiency, was nevertheless functional and designed to meet NEQS. The Respondent further submitted that they had invested significantly in environmental infrastructure and were committed to compliance. They presented internal maintenance logs and some operational records of their ETP, attempting to demonstrate regular operation. The counsel also raised questions regarding the chain of custody for the samples collected by the EPA and the calibration status of the EPA's monitoring equipment, suggesting that the accuracy of the laboratory reports could not be relied upon entirely. It was also argued that the show-cause notice period was insufficient for a comprehensive response

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
