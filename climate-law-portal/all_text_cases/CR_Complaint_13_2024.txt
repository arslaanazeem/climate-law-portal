**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Nishat Mills"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal, having heard the parties and examined the evidence on record, proceeds to deliver its findings and final order.
The Complainant alleges that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies. Specifically, the complaint details multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15th January 2024, 28th February 2024, and 10th March 2024. During these inspections, effluent samples were collected from the Respondent's discharge points. Subsequent laboratory analyses, conducted by the EPA-accredited laboratory, allegedly revealed significant exceedances in critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, consistently violating the NEQS for industrial effluents. The Complainant further asserts that despite repeated verbal warnings, issuance of show-cause notices dated 20th January 2024 and 5th March 2024, and opportunities to rectify the non-compliance, the Respondent failed to take effective measures to treat its industrial wastewater to meet the NEQS, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and aquatic ecosystems. The Complainant sought a direction from the Tribunal for the Respondent to immediately comply with NEQS, install or upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), and impose a suitable penalty under the relevant provisions of PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA field teams, photographs of the Respondent's discharge points and effluent streams, and certified laboratory analysis reports corresponding to the samples collected on the aforementioned dates. These lab reports clearly indicated the measured values of BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, juxtaposed against the permissible NEQS limits, showing consistent and significant deviations. Copies of the show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, along with proof of their delivery, were also submitted. The Complainant further produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Officer, who participated in the inspections and sample collection, attesting to the proper chain of custody and methodology employed.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, Mr. Zahid Ali, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. The Respondent contended that it operates a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and is committed to environmental protection. It was argued that any occasional exceedances might be due to operational fluctuations, which are promptly addressed. The Respondent challenged the validity and methodology of the EPA's sample collection and laboratory analysis, suggesting potential contamination or procedural irregularities. While admitting to receiving the show-cause notices, the Respondent claimed to have initiated steps to optimize its ETP and was in the process of implementing further upgrades to enhance its treatment efficiency. As evidence, the Respondent presented copies of invoices for recent purchases of ETP chemicals and minor equipment, along with a schematic diagram of their ETP and a letter of intent from an engineering firm for a potential ETP upgrade. However, no independent laboratory reports demonstrating consistent compliance with NEQS were presented by the Respondent. The Production Manager, Mr. Sohail Abbas, appeared as a witness for the Respondent, testifying about the challenges of managing industrial wastewater and their ongoing efforts towards compliance.
The present complaint falls under the purview of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The
primary objective of PEPA, 1997 is to provide for the protection, conservation, rehabilitation and improvement of the environment, for the prevention and control of pollution, and promotion of sustainable development. Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 strictly prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount, concentration, or level which is in
excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, or, where applicable, the standards established by the Federal Agency under clause (g) of sub-section (1) of Section 6. Furthermore, Section 17 of PEPA, 1997 empowers the Tribunal to impose penalties, including fines and imprisonment, for violations of the Act and its rules or regulations.
Having carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the evidence placed on record, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proving consistent non-compliance by the Respondent with the NEQS. The EPA's evidence, comprising multiple detailed inspection reports, photographic evidence, and crucially, certified laboratory analysis reports from an accredited laboratory, paints a clear picture of persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent. The consistency of the exceedances across various parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, pH) and dates strongly corroborates the EPA's allegations. The testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, the Environmental Officer, further solidified the procedural integrity of the sample collection and analysis.
While the Respondent contended operational fluctuations and challenged the EPA's methodology, it failed to present any concrete, independent, and consistent laboratory reports to counter the EPA's findings or demonstrate its own compliance with NEQS during the period in question. The invoices for ETP chemicals and the letter of intent for a potential upgrade, though indicative of some effort, do not negate the established fact of past and ongoing violations. These documents merely suggest an intent to improve, rather than proof of actual compliance. The Respondent's admission of receiving show-cause notices and its subsequent failure to provide satisfactory evidence of rectification further weakens its defense. The Tribunal notes that while managing industrial wastewater presents challenges, the responsibility to comply with statutory environmental standards remains absolute and non-negotiable for all industrial units. The environmental degradation caused by the discharge of untreated effluent directly impacts public health and the ecological balance, which the PEPA, 1997 seeks to protect. Therefore, based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has indeed violated Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by consistently discharging industrial effluent in excess of the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards.
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following final order and penalty:

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
