### JUDGMENT
The present complaint, bearing No. 37/2016, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura District. The Complainant sought the intervention of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by the Respondent. The Tribunal took cognizance of the matter, and notices were duly issued to the Respondent, who subsequently appeared through their authorized representatives and legal counsel.
The Complainant contended that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, has been operating in blatant disregard of environmental regulations. Specifically, the allegations included:
a.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent:** The Respondent was accused of regularly discharging industrial wastewater containing hazardous pollutants directly into the nearby public drainage system and subsequently into the River Ravi, without adequate treatment, thereby exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents. Parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and concentrations of heavy metals (e.g., Mercury, Lead, Chromium) were consistently found to be beyond permissible limits.
b.  **Excessive Air Emissions:** The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial processes, particularly from its boilers and chemical reactors, were emitting gaseous pollutants and particulate matter into the atmosphere far exceeding the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Suspended Particulate Matter (SPM) were highlighted as key pollutants.
c.  **Improper Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent was also charged with the improper handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous waste generated from its chemical processes, posing significant risks to soil and groundwater contamination, as well as to public health.
d.  **Failure to Install/Maintain Pollution Control Devices:** It was alleged that
the Respondent had either failed to install necessary pollution control equipment, such as an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) of adequate capacity and efficiency, air scrubbers, or particulate matter control devices, or had failed to properly maintain existing ones, rendering them ineffective in bringing emissions and effluents within NEQS limits. The Complainant further submitted that despite repeated warnings, inspections, and issuance of Environmental Protection Orders (
Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent had consistently failed to take effective remedial measures to comply with the NEQS. The Complainant, therefore, sought stringent penalties, including fines and directives for immediate compliance, under Sections 17(1) and 17(2) of PEPA, 199

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
