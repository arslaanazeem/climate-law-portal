This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint, registered as No. 17/2022, seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address persistent environmental violations by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial emissions and wastewater exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The EPA, acting under the mandate of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), has brought forth these proceedings to ensure compliance with environmental laws and to protect public health and the environment from the adverse impacts of industrial pollution.
The Complainant's primary allegations center on the Respondent's alleged failure to adequately control its industrial emissions and wastewater discharge. Specifically, it is contended that the Respondent's glass manufacturing operations, particularly its furnaces and associated processes, consistently release particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulfur dioxide (SO2) into the ambient air at levels significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial emissions. Furthermore, the complaint asserts that the wastewater generated from cooling, washing, and other processes within the facility is discharged into the municipal sewerage system without proper treatment, containing high levels of biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), chemical oxygen demand (COD), total suspended solids (TSS), and heavy metals, all in contravention of the NEQS for industrial effluents. The Complainant detailed several site inspections conducted on various dates, including June 15, 2021, September 28, 2021, and February 10, 2022, which consistently revealed these violations. Despite multiple warning notices, show-cause notices, and opportunities for compliance issued by the EPA under Sections 8 and 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent allegedly failed to take effective remedial measures, thereby necessitating the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal. The EPA claims that these continuous violations pose a significant threat to the air quality of the surrounding residential areas and contaminate water resources, impacting human health and local ecosystems.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams, documenting observed non-compliance during the aforementioned site visits. Crucially, the EPA submitted laboratory analysis reports of stack emissions and wastewater samples collected from the Respondent's facility. These reports, prepared by accredited environmental laboratories, unequivocally indicated that the levels of PM, NOx, and SO2 in air emissions, and BOD, COD, TSS, and certain heavy metals in wastewater, substantially exceeded the NEQS. Photographs and video footage taken during inspections were also submitted, illustrating visible smoke plumes and untreated effluent discharge points. Copies of all correspondence, including warning notices dated July 10, 20
21, and October 25, 2021, and show-cause notices dated March 5, 2022, and April 1, 2022, were also presented, demonstrating the EPA's consistent efforts to secure voluntary compliance before resorting to legal action. The Complainant emphasized that despite these repeated opportunities, the Respondent had failed to demonstrate satisfactory
compliance or provide a credible plan for remediation.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
