This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Nishat Mills"), a textile manufacturing unit, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint, registered as No. 24/2024, was formally lodged with this Tribunal on January 15, 2024, seeking appropriate action, including penalties and remedial measures, against the Respondent for its purported non-compliance with environmental laws. The Tribunal has jurisdiction to hear and decide this matter under Section 19 of PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Nishat Mills Limited are manifold. Firstly, it is alleged that the Respondent has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewer system and nearby agricultural land, thereby causing severe water pollution and posing significant risks to public health and the environment. This discharge is asserted to contain pollutants far exceeding the permissible limits prescribed by the NEQS for industrial effluents. Secondly, the Complainant contends that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly from its dyeing, finishing, and power generation units, are emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen oxides, into the atmosphere without adequate air pollution control devices, resulting in air quality degradation in the surrounding area. Thirdly, it is claimed that Nishat Mills has failed to install and maintain a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Treatment Plant (ATP) commensurate with the scale and nature of its operations, despite repeated warnings and notices from the EPA. Fourthly, the complaint highlights the Respondent's alleged failure to obtain the necessary Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE) or No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) for certain expansions or operational changes, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997. Finally, the Complainant alleges a consistent pattern of non-compliance and disregard for environmental regulations, evidenced by the Respondent's failure to respond adequately to show-cause notices and directives issued by the EPA.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive body of evidence. Key among these were the inspection reports dated October 20, 2023, and December 10, 2023, conducted by EPA officials, which documented the visual observation of dark-colored effluent discharge and visible air emissions. These reports were accompanied by photographs and video footage illustrating the alleged environmental degradation. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected on October 20, 2023, and December 10, 2023, from the Respondent's discharge points. These reports, prepared by an accredited laboratory, unequivocally
demonstrated that the parameters for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (specifically chromium and lead) in the effluent samples significantly exceeded the maximum permissible limits set by the NEQS. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports, though less conclusive in direct attribution, indicated elevated levels of particulate matter and sulfur dioxide in the vicinity of the Respondent's facility. Furthermore, the Complainant submitted copies of multiple show-cause notices issued to Nishat Mills between May 2023 and December 2023, along with a final warning letter dated December 28, 2023, all of which elicited either no response or unsatisfactory replies from the Respondent. The Complainant also presented expert testimony from an environmental engineer, who elaborated on the adverse impacts of untreated industrial discharge and emissions on aquatic life, soil fertility, and human respiratory health.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, vehemently denied the extent of the alleged violations and challenged the methodology and findings of the Complainant's inspection and laboratory reports. Through its legal counsel, the Respondent argued that the effluent samples might not have been collected in accordance with prescribed protocols, potentially leading to skewed results. It was contended that Nishat Mills operates a primary treatment facility for its industrial wastewater and has initiated plans for the installation of a comprehensive secondary Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the design and procurement for which are purportedly underway. The Respondent submitted certain internal project proposals and preliminary design documents as evidence of its commitment to environmental compliance. Regarding air emissions, Nishat Mills asserted that its power generation units are equipped with basic pollution control measures and that regular maintenance is carried out. They further claimed that any localized air quality issues could be attributed to multiple industrial sources in the area, not solely their operations. The Respondent also argued that the expansion projects referred to by the Complainant were minor modifications not requiring fresh EIA/IEE approvals under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and that previous NOCs covered existing operations. Finally, the Respondent pleaded for leniency, citing its significant contribution to the national economy, employment generation, and ongoing efforts to modernize its facilities, requesting additional time to bring its operations into full compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, expert testimonies, and oral arguments presented by both parties. After careful consideration, the Tribunal finds the Complainant's evidence regarding the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent to be overwhelmingly conclusive. The laboratory analysis reports, being from an accredited facility and corroborating visual observations from multiple inspection reports, leave no doubt that the Respondent's effluent discharge consistently exceeds the NEQS limits for several critical parameters. The Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology was unsubstantiated by any credible counter-evidence. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of initiating plans for an ETP, the mere existence of project proposals does not absolve it from its current and past non-compliance. The PEPA, 1997, mandates compliance, not merely intentions. The argument regarding minor modifications not requiring fresh EIA/IEE approvals was also found to be weak, as the nature and scale of the alleged expansions, as described by the Complainant, would reasonably trigger the requirement for updated environmental assessments.
Regarding air emissions, while direct causality can be complex in an industrial zone, the Complainant's inspection reports, coupled with the Respondent's admission of "basic" pollution control measures, suggest a lack of robust systems commensurate with the scale of its operations. The Tribunal notes that the Respondent failed to provide specific data or certifications regarding the efficiency of its existing air pollution control devices. The consistent failure to respond adequately to EPA notices further underscores a pattern of disregard for regulatory directives.
Therefore, this Tribunal concludes that M/s Nishat Mills Limited is in clear violation of Sections 11, 12, 13, and 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, as alleged by the Complainant. The environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's actions poses a serious threat to public health and the environment, necessitating immediate and stringent remedial measures.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Penalty:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby imposed a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** for its persistent violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
2.  **Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** The Respondent shall immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewer system or any other water body/land. M/s Nishat Mills Limited is directed to install a fully functional and compliant secondary Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) within **one hundred and twenty (120) days** from the date of this order. The ETP must be capable of treating all industrial effluent to meet the NEQS for industrial discharges. The Respondent shall submit weekly progress reports to the EPA, Punjab, regarding the installation and commissioning of the ETP.
3.  **Air Treatment Plant (ATP) / Air Pollution Control Devices:** The Respondent shall upgrade its existing air pollution control devices and/or install a suitable Air Treatment Plant (ATP) or other appropriate measures for its dyeing, finishing, and power generation units to ensure that all air emissions comply with the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. This measure shall be completed within **one hundred and eighty (180) days** from the date of this order. The Respondent shall submit a detailed plan for this upgrade/installation to the EPA, Punjab, within thirty (30) days.
4.  **Environmental Approvals:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited shall immediately apply for and obtain all necessary Environmental Approvals (EIA/IEE) or No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) for any expansions, modifications, or operational changes that have been undertaken without due process, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997. Proof of application shall be submitted to the EPA, Punjab, within thirty (30) days.
5.  **Compliance Monitoring:** The Respondent shall install continuous online monitoring systems for its effluent discharge and significant air emission points, providing real-time data access to the EPA, Punjab, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order.
6.  **Environmental Audit:** The Respondent shall arrange for an independent environmental audit of its entire facility within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this order, conducted by an EPA-approved third-party consultant. The audit report, along with an action plan for addressing any identified deficiencies, shall be submitted to the EPA, Punjab, and this Tribunal.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 500,000/- (Rupees Five Hundred Thousand only)**, to be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days.
Failure to comply with any part of this order within the stipulated timelines shall result in further punitive action, including but not limited to, daily fines, closure of the industrial unit, and initiation of criminal proceedings under the relevant provisions of PEPA,

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
