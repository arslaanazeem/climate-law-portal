This judgment addresses Complaint No. 15/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking adjudication on alleged violations of environmental laws. The Tribunal, presided over by Mr. Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal, has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties in this matter concerning industrial pollution.
The Complainant's primary allegations against the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a manufacturing unit engaged in the production of various chemicals, include the consistent discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants far in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, the EPA alleged that despite repeated warnings and notices, the Respondent continued to release untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into the public drainage system, impacting agricultural land and local water bodies. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if operational, was either inefficient or improperly maintained, leading to high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH deviations in their discharge. Additionally, the complaint touched upon concerns regarding air emissions from the Respondent's stacks, suggesting a potential exceedance of NEQS for particulate matter and gaseous pollutants, although the primary focus of the complaint remained on water pollution. The EPA asserted that these actions constituted a clear contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the NEQS notified thereunder, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, dating from early 2015 to late 2016, detailing visits by EPA officials to the Respondent's premises. Crucially, these reports were accompanied by authenticated laboratory analysis results of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates, including 12.03.2015, 28.08.2015, 05.02.20
16, and 19.09.2016. These reports consistently demonstrated that the analyzed samples contained levels of COD, BOD, TSS, and pH that significantly exceeded the permissible limits set by the NEQS. Furthermore, the Complainant submitted photographs depicting the state of the effluent discharge and the affected areas, alongside expert testimonies from environmental scientists confirming the adverse impacts of such pollution on the surrounding ecosystem and public health.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, categorically denied the persistent and willful violations alleged by the EPA. They argued that they are a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship and have invested substantially in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). The Respondent contended that their ETP is fully functional and designed to meet NEQS, and any occasional exceedances might be attributed to unforeseen operational upsets, power fluctuations, or the complex nature of raw materials and production processes. They also raised questions regarding the sampling methodology employed by the EPA, suggesting potential procedural irregularities or contamination during sample collection and transportation, which could have skewed the laboratory results. The Respondent further submitted that they had, on several occasions, provided explanations and compliance reports to the EPA detailing their efforts to improve environmental performance and that they had engaged consultants to optimize their ETP. They requested that the Tribunal consider their ongoing efforts and not impose a harsh penalty for what they termed as isolated incidents, rather than a pattern of deliberate non-compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the evidence presented by both sides. The Complainant's inspection reports and, more critically, the authenticated laboratory analysis results from multiple independent tests conducted over a prolonged period, provide compelling and consistent evidence of the Respondent's failure to comply with the NEQS. The repetitive nature of the violations, as evidenced by samples taken on different dates, strongly indicates that the exceedances were not merely isolated incidents or operational upsets but rather a systemic issue with the Respondent's wastewater management system or the efficacy of their ETP. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of investment in an ETP and commitment to environmental compliance, the objective scientific data unequivocally demonstrates a persistent breach of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Respondent's arguments regarding sampling methodology were found to be unsubstantiated, as the EPA presented standard operating procedures for sample collection and analysis, which appeared to have been followed diligently. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to adequately treat its industrial effluent,
thereby consistently discharging pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, in direct contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The persistent nature of these violations, as evidenced by multiple tests over an extended period, demonstrates a clear pattern of non-compliance, despite the Respondent's claims of environmental commitment. The environmental and public health risks associated with such untreated discharge are significant and cannot be overlooked.
**ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Sections 17 and 20 thereof, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is found guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by consistently discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** The Respondent is hereby directed to pay a penalty of PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only) for the persistent environmental violations. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent shall immediately undertake all necessary measures to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the efficient operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for all parameters, including but not limited to COD, BOD, TSS, and pH.
4.  **Compliance Plan and Timeline:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including a timeline for the completion of ETP upgrades and operational improvements, to the Complainant (EPA) within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment. The EPA shall review and approve this plan.
5.  **Regular Monitoring:** The Respondent shall arrange for independent third-party laboratory analysis of its effluent discharge on a monthly basis for a period of one (1) year, commencing from the approval of the compliance plan, and submit these reports to the EPA. The EPA shall also conduct its own unannounced inspections and sampling to verify compliance.
6.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of the penalty or the implementation of remedial measures within the stipulated timelines, shall result in further legal action, which may include enhanced penalties, closure of the industrial unit, or other measures as deemed appropriate under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
7.  **Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
