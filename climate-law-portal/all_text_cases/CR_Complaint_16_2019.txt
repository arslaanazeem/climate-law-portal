IN THE PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE
COMPLAINT NO. 16/2019
Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore
...Complainant
VERSUS
M/s Packages Limited
...Respondent
Presided by: Justice (R) Nasir Saeed Sheikh
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 16/2019, instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial unit engaged in paper and packaging manufacturing. The complaint, filed under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleges significant violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with statutory requirements, thereby posing a threat to public health and the environment. The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions of both parties, examined the documentary evidence, and heard the arguments presented by their respective counsels.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Packages Limited revolve around the persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates between January 2019 and April 2019, samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points consistently showed levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS. Furthermore, the Complainant contended that the Respondent's air emissions, particularly from its boiler stacks, contained particulate matter (PM10) and Sulfur Dioxide (SO2) in excess of the NEQS, indicating a failure to adequately operate or maintain pollution control equipment. The EPA also alleged that the Respondent had failed to submit regular environmental monitoring reports as required under its environmental approval conditions and had not taken adequate measures despite receiving multiple warning notices and show-cause notices from the EPA. The Complainant sought an order from this Tribunal directing the Respondent to cease all polluting activities, comply with NEQS, install and effectively operate necessary pollution control devices, and impose a substantial penalty as provided under PEPA, 1997.
In support of its complaint, the EPA presented a series of inspection reports, dated 15.01.2019, 28.02.2019,
and 12.04.2019, which included detailed laboratory analysis reports confirming the non-compliance of both effluent and air emissions with the prescribed NEQS for various parameters. Furthermore, copies of the warning notices and show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, along with the Respondent's inadequate or delayed responses, were submitted to demonstrate a pattern of disregard for environmental regulations.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship. The Respondent contended that it has invested significantly in state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plants (ETPs) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), which are regularly maintained and operated. It was argued that any exceedances noted by the EPA were either isolated incidents, attributable to transient operational fluctuations, or resulted from flawed sampling and analysis methodologies employed by the Complainant. The Respondent specifically challenged the accuracy and chain of custody of the samples collected by the EPA, suggesting potential contamination or procedural irregularities that could compromise the validity of the laboratory results. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed to have submitted all required environmental monitoring reports and presented its own internal monitoring data, which, it asserted, demonstrated compliance with NEQS. The Respondent requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, arguing that the EPA had failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and that imposing a penalty would be unjust given its substantial efforts and investments in environmental compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties and heard the detailed arguments of their respective counsels. While acknowledging the Respondent's assertion of significant investment in pollution control infrastructure, the Tribunal noted the consistent nature of the non-compliance indicated in the EPA's laboratory reports, across multiple dates and parameters. The EPA's inspection reports and laboratory analyses, conducted by accredited laboratories, provide a strong prima facie case of persistent violations. The Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology, while noted, was not substantiated with concrete evidence of procedural lapses that would invalidate the entire sampling process. Moreover, the lack of timely and adequate responses to repeated warning and show-cause notices issued by the EPA further undermines the Respondent's claim of full compliance and proactive environmental management. The Tribunal finds that the internal monitoring reports presented by the Respondent, while indicative of some efforts, do not fully reconcile with the independent findings of the EPA, particularly concerning the critical parameters of BOD, COD, TSS, and PM10.
Based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal concludes that the Complainant, EPA, has successfully established that M/s Packages Limited has been in persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants. The Respondent's failure to consistently operate and maintain its pollution control equipment effectively, and its
disregard for the repeated warnings issued by the EPA, constitute a clear breach of its environmental obligations under PEPA, 1997. Such persistent non-compliance poses an undeniable risk to public health, aquatic ecosystems, and the overall environmental quality of the surrounding areas. The Tribunal finds the Respondent’s arguments regarding flawed sampling and internal monitoring insufficient to rebut the robust evidence presented by the Complainant.
Therefore, in light of the foregoing findings and pursuant to the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal hereby issues the following final order and penalty:
**ORDER:**
1.  **CEASE AND DESIST:** M/s Packages Limited is hereby directed to immediately cease all discharges of industrial effluent and emissions of air pollutants that exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at its facility.
2.  **COMPLIANCE WITH NEQS:** The Respondent shall ensure full and continuous compliance with all applicable NEQS for both effluent discharge and air emissions forthwith.
3.  **UPGRADE/OPTIMIZE POLLUTION CONTROL:** The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment, submit a detailed action plan to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, outlining specific measures, including any necessary upgrades, optimization, or enhanced maintenance protocols for its Effluent Treatment Plants (ETPs) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), to ensure consistent compliance with NEQS. This plan shall include a clear timeline for implementation, which must not exceed six (6) months.
4.  **REGULAR MONITORING AND REPORTING:** The Respondent shall submit monthly environmental monitoring reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of two (2) years, commencing from the date of submission of the action plan. These reports shall detail the quality of its effluent discharge and air emissions against NEQS parameters.
5.  **EPA OVERSIGHT:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is authorized to conduct unannounced inspections and collect samples from the Respondent's facility at any time to verify compliance with this order.
**PENALTY:**
Pursuant to Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and considering the persistent nature and gravity of the violations, the Tribunal imposes a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million Only)** on M/s Packages Limited. The said penalty shall be deposited into the Environmental Protection Fund within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment. Failure to pay the penalty within the stipulated time may result in further legal action, including but not limited to, the recovery of the amount as arrears of land revenue, and an additional daily fine as per law.
The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings.
