**COMPLAINT NO. 48/2024**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**

**Presided by: Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, and vested with the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints pertaining to environmental violations, has carefully considered the submissions and evidence presented by both parties.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system/open drains, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. Specifically, the complaint detailed multiple instances of non-compliance, including the failure to adequately operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), leading to the discharge of untreated or partially treated wastewater. The EPA further contended that despite numerous warnings, show-cause notices, and opportunities provided for rectification, the Respondent continued its environmentally detrimental practices, demonstrating a blatant disregard for environmental laws and regulations. The allegations primarily focused on the violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste pollutant in excess of the NEQS.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, specifically mentioning inspections on 15.01.2024, 08.02.2024, and 01.03.2024. Crucially, the evidence comprised laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on these dates, which unequivocally indicated parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels far exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the discharge of dark-colored, foul-smelling effluent directly into the public drainage system was also presented. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited on 20.01.2024 and 15.02.2024, along with the Respondent's evasive or unsatisfactory replies, were placed on record. The Complainant also produced an affidavit from the Assistant Director, EPA, attesting to the authenticity of the reports and the persistent non-compliance. In response, the Respondent, while acknowledging the existence of an ETP, largely failed to provide concrete evidence of its consistent and effective operation. Their defense primarily revolved around claims of ongoing upgrade work and unforeseen operational difficulties, without providing verifiable data or third-party audit reports to counter the EPA's laboratory findings.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The crucial provision is Section 11, which unequivocally states, "No person shall discharge or emit or allow the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards." Section 16 of the Act empowers the Environmental Tribunal to hear complaints of violations of the Act and
and to impose penalties as prescribed therein. Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, outlines the penalties for contravention of the provisions of the Act, stipulating that whoever contravenes or fails to comply with any provision of this Act or the rules or regulations made thereunder shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years or with fine which may extend to one million rupees or with both, and in case of a continuing contravention, with an additional fine which may extend to twenty thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues.
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent. The Complainant has successfully established, through a preponderance of evidence, that the Respondent has been in consistent violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997. The laboratory analysis reports are conclusive, demonstrating pollutant levels (COD, BOD, TSS, pH) significantly exceeding the NEQS on multiple occasions. These scientific findings are further corroborated by the inspection reports, photographic and videographic evidence, which paint a clear picture of environmental non-compliance. The repeated issuance of show-cause notices and the Respondent's failure to provide a satisfactory and verifiable response or to take effective corrective measures further underscore their persistent disregard for environmental regulations.
Conversely, the Respondent's defense, based on vague assertions of ongoing upgrade work and operational difficulties, lacks any concrete documentary proof or independent verification. Mere claims without supporting data, such as ETP operational logs, maintenance records, or third-party audit reports, cannot outweigh the objective scientific evidence presented by the EPA. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant effectively, leading to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the public domain, thereby causing demonstrable environmental pollution and posing a significant risk to public health and aquatic ecosystems.
**FINDINGS:**
Based on the careful consideration of the evidence and arguments, this Tribunal finds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**ORDER:**
In light of the above findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (the Respondent) shall immediately cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not conform to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system or open drains.
2.  The Respondent shall ensure the full and effective operation and maintenance of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) at all times, ensuring that all discharged effluent meets the prescribed NEQS.
3.  The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive compliance report to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order, detailing the measures taken to achieve and maintain NEQS compliance, along with verifiable evidence such as ETP operational logs, maintenance records, and independent laboratory analysis reports of its treated effluent.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
