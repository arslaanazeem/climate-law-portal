**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 57/2018 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions, which pose a significant threat to public health and the environment. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA 1997), is seized with the jurisdiction to hear and decide matters pertaining to environmental contraventions within its territorial limits.
The Complainant, through its duly authorized representative, brought forth serious allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited. It was contended that the Respondent company has been operating without adequately treating its industrial wastewater before discharging it into the public sewerage system, which eventually leads to natural water bodies. Specific allegations included the consistent exceedance of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, as determined by multiple inspections and laboratory analyses. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if functional, was either insufficient in capacity, improperly maintained, or deliberately bypassed. The EPA also pointed out issues with air emissions, particularly from the Respondent's boiler operations, which were suspected of lacking proper filtration or emission control mechanisms, contributing to ambient air pollution. The Respondent was also accused of failing to obtain or renew necessary Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA/IEE) for certain operations or expansions, as required under Section 12 of PEPA 1997 and its associated regulations. Several show-cause notices and warnings issued by the EPA, demanding compliance and submission of an environmental management plan, had allegedly gone unheeded or were met with inadequate responses, necessitating the filing of this formal complaint before the Tribunal on 15.03.2018.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports dated 12.12.2017, 25.01.2018, and 05.03.2018, compiled by EPA field inspectors, highlighting observations of untreated effluent discharge points and visible air pollution. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/123/2017, EPA/LAB/156/2018, and EPA/LAB/178/2018, which conclusively showed that samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points consistently violated the prescribed NEQS for
...various critical parameters, often exceeding the permissible limits by significant margins. The Complainant also provided photographic evidence of dark smoke emanating from the Respondent's chimneys and presented expert testimony regarding the potential health impacts of such unchecked emissions. Copies of the unheeded show-cause notices, final warnings, and the original complaint document were also submitted, demonstrating the EPA's consistent efforts to secure compliance before resorting to legal action.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. They contended that they operate a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and have invested substantial resources in environmental compliance. They argued that any occasional exceedances were minor, temporary, and attributable to operational fluctuations or sampling anomalies, rather than systemic failure. They also claimed that their air emissions were within permissible limits, and they regularly maintain their boilers and filtration systems. Regarding the Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA/IEE), the Respondent asserted that they possessed all necessary permits or were in the process of renewing them, and that the EPA had not provided sufficient guidance or time for certain compliance aspects. They further highlighted their significant contribution to the national economy and employment, implying that harsh penalties could adversely impact their operations and the wider industrial sector. The Respondent requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, or at least issue directives for a more practical and phased compliance approach, rather than imposing immediate punitive measures.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. It noted that the laboratory analysis reports provided by the EPA were from an accredited laboratory and consistently pointed to violations of NEQS over several months. The Respondent's claims of operational fluctuations or sampling anomalies were not supported by any credible counter-evidence, such as their own independent laboratory reports or detailed operational logs demonstrating compliance. The photographic evidence of air emissions, coupled with the lack of documented, up-to-date environmental approvals, further strengthened the Complainant's case. The Tribunal found that while the Respondent's economic contribution is acknowledged, it cannot supersede the fundamental right of the public to a clean and healthy environment, as enshrined in the Constitution of Pakistan and protected by PEPA 1997. The repeated issuance of show-cause notices and
...final warnings by the EPA clearly indicated that the Respondent had ample opportunity to rectify the situation but failed to do so effectively.
The Tribunal concludes that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been in persistent violation of environmental laws and standards. The evidence overwhelmingly demonstrates that the Respondent's industrial effluent consistently exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby polluting water bodies, and that its air emissions contribute to ambient air pollution. Furthermore, the failure to secure or renew necessary environmental approvals for its operations constitutes a direct contravention of Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Respondent's arguments, while acknowledging some efforts, do not sufficiently negate the documented and consistent breaches of environmental regulations. The Tribunal is duty-bound to uphold the principles of environmental protection and ensure compliance with the law.
**ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby issues the following order:
1.  **GUILTY VERDICT:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is found guilty of violating Section 11 (Prohibition of discharge of untreated effluent), Section 12 (Prohibition of certain projects without approval), and Section 13 (Prohibition of emission of air pollutants) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed thereunder.
2.  **PENALTY:** A cumulative fine of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, to be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **REMEDIAL MEASURES AND COMPLIANCE PLAN:**
    *   The Respondent shall immediately upgrade and fully operationalize its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged industrial effluent consistently meets the NEQS for all parameters, including but not limited to COD, BOD, TSS, and pH.
    *   The Respondent shall install and maintain appropriate air pollution control devices (e.g., scrubbers, bag filters) for its boiler operations and any other significant emission sources, ensuring that all air emissions comply with the prescribed NEQS.
    *   The Respondent shall forthwith apply for and obtain all necessary Environmental Approvals (EIA/IEE/NOCs) for its existing operations and any planned expansions, as required under Section 12 of PEPA 1997.
    *   The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP) detailing the steps and timelines for achieving full compliance with all environmental laws and standards, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment. The EMP must include a schedule for regular monitoring and reporting of effluent and air emission quality.
4.  **MONITORING AND REPORTING:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is directed to conduct quarterly inspections and independent laboratory analyses of the Respondent's effluent and air emissions for a period of two (2) years, and submit compliance reports to this Tribunal every six (6) months.
5.  **CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, particularly the remedial measures and submission of the EMP, within the stipulated timelines, shall result in a further daily fine of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)** for each day of default, and may lead to the sealing of the Respondent's operations or other coercive measures as provided under PEPA 1997.
6.  **COSTS:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **12 May, 2022**.
