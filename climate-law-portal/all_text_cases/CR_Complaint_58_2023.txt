**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "the Company"), a chemical manufacturing facility located at Kala Shah Kaku Industrial Estate, District Sheikhupura. The complaint, registered under Section 17 read with Section 31 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to secure compliance with environmental laws and standards and to impose penalties for alleged violations.
**Allegations**
The Complainant's primary allegations against the Respondent are multifaceted and center on persistent non-compliance with national environmental laws and standards. Specifically, the EPA contended that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been:
1.  **Discharging Untreated/Partially Treated Effluent:** The Respondent's industrial operations, which involve the production of various chemicals, generate significant quantities of wastewater. It is alleged that this effluent is being discharged into the public sewer system or directly into nearby water bodies without adequate treatment, resulting in concentrations of pollutants far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Exceeding Air Emission Standards:** The Company's manufacturing processes, particularly those involving combustion and chemical reactions, are alleged to be emitting gaseous pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), into the atmosphere in quantities that contravene the NEQS for industrial emissions.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste:** The Respondent is accused of failing to properly segregate, store, treat, and dispose of hazardous waste generated during its operations, posing risks to public health and the environment.
4.  **Failure to Obtain/Renew Environmental Approvals:** The Complainant asserted that the Respondent has either failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, or has allowed existing approvals to lapse without renewal, thereby operating without legal environmental sanction.
5.  **Non-Compliance with Previous Notices and Directions:** The EPA stated that despite repeated inspections, issuance of warning notices, and directions for corrective measures, the Respondent has demonstrated a consistent pattern of non-compliance, failing to implement the necessary environmental controls and upgrades.
6.  **Violation of Environmental Laws:** The cumulative effect of these actions constitutes a direct contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of pollutants in excess of the NEQS, and other relevant provisions of the Act and its associated rules and regulations.
**Evidence**
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, presented the following documentary and oral evidence:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports, dated from January 2022 to September 2023, conducted by EPA officials, detailing observations of environmental non-compliance at the Respondent's facility. These reports consistently highlighted the presence of untreated effluent discharge and visible air emissions.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Analytical reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on effluent and ambient air samples collected during various inspections. These reports consistently showed exceedances of NEQS for several critical parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals in wastewater, and
particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) in air emissions, consistently exceeding the NEQS.
3.  **Show Cause Notices and Warning Letters:** Copies of numerous show cause notices and warning letters issued to the Respondent by the EPA, detailing specific environmental violations and directing remedial actions, along with irrefutable evidence of their proper delivery and receipt by the Respondent. These documents underscore a history of non-compliance despite official warnings.
4.  **Correspondence:** Records of official correspondence between the EPA and the Respondent, indicating the Respondent's inadequate responses, failure to provide satisfactory explanations, or demonstrate concrete steps towards compliance following the issuance of notices.
5.  **Photographic and Video Evidence:** Visual documentation, including photographs and video recordings, capturing instances of alleged pollution, such as discolored industrial discharge flowing into public drains, visible plumes of smoke/emissions
from stacks, and improperly stored waste materials within the facility premises, which corroborated the written inspection reports and laboratory findings.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
