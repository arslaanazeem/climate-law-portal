This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 39/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") on 15th March 2017. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent in the operation of its glass manufacturing facility located at 12-KM, Sheikhupura Road, Lahore. The matter came up for regular hearing, and both parties were afforded ample opportunity to present their respective cases, adduce evidence, and submit arguments.
The Complainant's case rests on the following allegations: Firstly, that the Respondent's glass manufacturing unit has been operating without a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) or Environmental Approval from the EPA, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, for a new or modified industrial undertaking. Secondly, specific complaints of air pollution were received from the local community and subsequently verified by EPA field inspections, indicating the emission of particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Thirdly, the Respondent was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the sewerage system/open drain, which eventually flows into nearby agricultural lands and water bodies, causing water pollution and posing a threat to human health and biodiversity. Laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA revealed concentrations of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and heavy metals in the effluent far exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Fourthly, the Respondent failed to properly manage and dispose of hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing process, including broken glass, refractory materials, and chemical sludge, in accordance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003. Multiple notices were issued by the EPA to the Respondent, including an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 20th December 2016, requiring immediate remedial measures and submission of a compliance plan, but these directives were largely ignored or inadequately addressed by the Respondent.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included certified copies of inspection reports conducted on various dates (e.g., 25th October 2016, 10th January 2017), which detailed the observed environmental degradation and operational deficiencies. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for air emissions (particulate matter, SO2, NOx) and wastewater discharge (TSS, COD, BOD, heavy metals). Photographs and video recordings of the Respondent's facility, depicting visible plumes of smoke from chimneys and discolored effluent discharge, were also tendered. Copies of the various show-
cause notices and the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 20th December 2016 were also presented, demonstrating the Complainant's persistent efforts to secure compliance prior to the filing of the formal complaint.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
