This judgment addresses Complaint No. 29/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, situated at 2-Km Manga Raiwind Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"). The proceedings commenced with the filing of the complaint on [Date of Filing, e.g., 15.03.2016], asserting a persistent failure on the part of the respondent to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) regarding industrial discharges and emissions, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
The Complainant, through its duly authorized representative, alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, has consistently discharged untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into public drains and emitted pollutants into the atmosphere without employing appropriate pollution control measures. Specific allegations include:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Effluent:** The respondent has been discharging industrial wastewater containing high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and exhibiting pH levels outside the prescribed NEQS limits, directly into the surrounding agricultural land and local water bodies, contrary to Section 11 of the Act.
2.  **Air Emissions:** The respondent's industrial operations, particularly from its boiler house and process units, have been emitting smoke and particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) exceeding the NEQS for industrial air emissions, in violation of Section 11 of the Act.
3.  **Lack of Environmental Management Systems:** Despite repeated notices and opportunities, the respondent has failed to install and operate an efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices, or to implement a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP) as required under the Act and its associated rules.
4.  **Failure to Comply with Notices:** The EPA issued several warning notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) to the respondent, specifically Notice No. EPA/Lahore/Enf-2015/1123 dated 25.11.2015 and EPO No. EPA/Lahore/Enf-2016/01 dated 05.01.2016, directing them to comply with NEQS and submit a compliance plan. The respondent allegedly failed to adequately respond to these directives or demonstrate satisfactory progress towards compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams on various dates, specifically detailing observations of effluent discharge points and smoke stacks. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from EPA-approved laboratories were submitted, comparing samples of the respondent's industrial effluent and air emissions against the NEQS. These reports consistently indicated exceedances of permissible limits for parameters such
as high Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels outside the prescribed limits in the effluent, and elevated concentrations of particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) in the air emissions. These reports, coupled with photographic and videographic evidence, painted a clear picture of systematic and
systematic and persistent disregard for environmental regulations and public health.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, submitting a written statement that broadly challenged the factual accuracy of the EPA's reports and the methodology of sampling. They contended that they were fully cognizant of their environmental responsibilities and had, in fact, initiated steps towards upgrading their existing pollution control

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
