**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL**
**LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 36/2018**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 36/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, located at Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. The complaint, lodged on October 15, 2018, alleges persistent violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA 1997), particularly concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions of air pollutants. The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions from both parties, examined the evidence presented, and reviewed the applicable legal framework to arrive at a just determination.
The Complainant initiated these proceedings by alleging that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has consistently failed to comply with environmental laws and standards. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations resulted in the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the public sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies, thereby causing severe water pollution. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent's manufacturing processes, particularly those involving the use of chemicals, generated significant air emissions containing hazardous gases and particulate matter exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS. Prior to filing the complaint, the EPA had issued multiple warning notices and environmental protection orders (EPOs) to the Respondent, including a show-cause notice dated July 20, 2018, and a final EPO dated September 5, 2018, directing immediate remedial measures and compliance. Despite these directives, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent failed to take effective steps to mitigate pollution, necessitating the present action before this Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant adduced a range of compelling evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on various dates between April 2018 and September 2018, which consistently noted deficiencies in the Respondent's effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. Crucially, the Complainant presented laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on May 10, 2018, July 15, 2018, and September 2, 2018. These reports, prepared by accredited laboratories, conclusively demonstrated that parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeded the NEQS limits for industrial effluent discharge. Similarly,
laboratory analysis reports of air emission samples taken from the Respondent's stacks on August 1, 2018, and September 10, 2018, indicated levels of particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) that were substantially above the prescribed NEQS for industrial air emissions.
In response, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. They argued that the company had invested considerable resources in upgrading its environmental infrastructure, including the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. They presented evidence of partial compliance efforts, such as purchase orders for new equipment, installation schedules, and internal monitoring reports which, they claimed, showed improving trends. The Respondent contended that some of the reported exceedances were isolated incidents or attributable to technical glitches during the commissioning phase of new equipment, rather than a willful disregard for environmental laws. Furthermore, they emphasized their significant contribution to the national economy and employment, urging the Tribunal to adopt a pragmatic approach that would allow them to continue operations while fully achieving compliance. They requested a reasonable timeframe to complete ongoing upgrades and achieve full NEQS compliance, rather than facing severe penalties that might jeopardize their operations.
The Tribunal has meticulously weighed the arguments and evidence presented by both parties. While acknowledging the Respondent's claims of efforts towards environmental compliance, the overwhelming evidence adduced by the Complainant, particularly the consistent and independently verified laboratory analysis reports of effluent and air emission samples, conclusively proves a pattern of non-compliance with the NEQS. The inspection reports further corroborate the deficiencies in the Respondent's pollution control infrastructure. The warning notices and EPOs issued by the EPA prior to the complaint clearly demonstrate that the Respondent was afforded ample opportunity to rectify the situation but failed to do so effectively within the stipulated timeframes. The argument that exceedances were isolated or due to technical glitches is not sufficiently supported by evidence to negate the persistent nature of the violations documented over several months. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has indeed discharged untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and released air pollutants exceeding permissible limits, thereby violating Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. The environmental degradation caused by such persistent violations poses a significant threat to public health and the ecosystem, necessitating a firm response from this Tribunal.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
Based on the foregoing analysis, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Finding of Responsibility:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is found responsible for persistent violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions.
2.  **Immediate Compliance Directive:** The Respondent is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and to ensure that all air emissions comply with the NEQS forthwith.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
