**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 41/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products situated in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, which have a detrimental impact on the local environment and public health. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20(1) of PEPA, 1997, is empowered to hear and adjudicate upon complaints pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance with National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant's primary contention is that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically its glass melting furnaces and associated processes, have consistently failed to comply with the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions. It is alleged that the Respondent's facility discharges particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and oxides of sulfur (SOx) into the ambient air at levels significantly exceeding the permissible limits. Furthermore, the Complainant asserts that the Respondent has engaged in improper management and disposal of solid waste, including broken glass cullet, refractory waste, and other industrial by-products, leading to unauthorized dumping and potential soil and groundwater contamination. A specific allegation also pertains to the operation of certain expansion units within the facility without obtaining the requisite No Objection Certificate (NOC) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval from the EPA, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997 and the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000. The Complainant also highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to maintain
proper environmental records, including self-monitoring reports, waste manifests, and operational data of pollution control devices, which are essential for demonstrating continuous compliance and transparency. The Complainant submitted several inspection reports, sampling analyses conducted by EPA-accredited laboratories, and photographic evidence to substantiate these claims, indicating a pattern of non-compliance over several months leading up to the filing of this complaint.
The Respondent
, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied all allegations, asserting that it operates its facility in full compliance with environmental laws and regulations. The Respondent contended that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has invested significantly in modern pollution control technologies, including state-of-the-art electrostatic precipitators and bag filters for particulate matter control, and that these systems are regularly maintained and monitored. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
