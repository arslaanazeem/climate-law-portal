This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint alleges various violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just and equitable decision in the interest of environmental protection and public health.
The gravamen of the complaint, lodged on October 18, 2022, is that the Respondent has been operating its textile processing and manufacturing facility in a manner detrimental to the environment, specifically by discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drains, which eventually flows into the River Ravi. The EPA's allegations include:
a. Violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, for discharging effluent containing pollutants in excess of the NEQS.
b. Failure to install and effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating industrial wastewater to meet NEQS.
c. Operating without a valid No-Objection Certificate (NOC) for environmental approval, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, particularly concerning the expansion or modification of its industrial processes that impact effluent discharge.
d. Non-compliance with previous Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued by the EPA for remedial actions.
The Complainant contended that these actions constitute a continuous offense, posing a significant threat to aquatic life, soil quality, and human health in the surrounding areas.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documents and expert testimonies. Key among these were:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports dated July 15, 2022, and September 28, 2022, detailing observations of dark-colored effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Analytical reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2022/07-15/005 and EPA/LAB/2022/09-28/012, confirming that samples of the discharged effluent exceeded the NEQS limits for several parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. For instance, the COD levels were found to be 850 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, and TSS at 400 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L.
3.  **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual records depicting the discharge points and the nature of the effluent.
4.  **Official Correspondence:** Copies of show-cause notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) previously issued to the Respondent, along with their acknowledgments, demonstrating the Respondent's awareness of the violations and the EPA's consistent efforts to secure compliance.
The Complainant also presented the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, an Environmental Analyst from EPA, who elaborated on the sampling methodology and the interpretation of the laboratory results.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel and Production Manager, Mr. Bilal Hassan, vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. Their defense rested on the following points:
1.  **Partial Compliance:** The Respondent asserted that it had initiated the process of upgrading its existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and had invested significantly in new technology to meet NEQS. They presented purchase orders for new filtration
equipment and presented a timeline for the completion of the ETP upgrade, projecting full operational capability by March 31, 2023. They argued that the samples taken by the EPA were either not representative of the overall discharge or were collected during a transitional period when the ETP was undergoing modifications and not operating at its optimal capacity. Furthermore, the Respondent submitted that they had actively engaged
...actively engaged with the EPA authorities to discuss the challenges faced during the upgrade and had sought an extension for full compliance, which they believed was being considered. They also presented evidence of regular monitoring of their internal treatment processes, arguing that occasional exceedances were inevitable during a complex upgrade project, but that their overall commitment to environmental stewardship remained unwavering. They emphasized their significant contribution to the national economy and employment, urging the Tribunal to adopt a pragmatic approach that allows for a reasonable transition period rather than imposing immediate, punitive measures that could jeopardize their operations.
The Complainant, in rebuttal, maintained that while acknowledging the Respondent's claims of ongoing upgrades, the continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent, as evidenced by multiple inspections and laboratory reports, constituted a clear and present violation of PEPA, 1997, and NEQS.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
