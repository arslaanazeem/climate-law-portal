This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 38/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a prominent textile manufacturing facility. The complaint alleges significant environmental non-compliance by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby posing a threat to public health and the environment. The matter came before this Tribunal for adjudication in accordance with the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997).
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Nishat Mills Limited, located within the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating its textile dyeing and finishing units without adequate pollution control measures. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent was discharging industrial wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually drains into the local water bodies, without proper treatment. Furthermore, the Complainant contended that the Respondent's boiler emissions and process stack emissions were not in conformity with the NEQS for air quality. These violations were first observed during a routine inspection conducted on May 15, 2018, followed by a detailed sampling and analysis on June 01, 2018. A Show Cause Notice bearing No. EPA/LHR/2018/1123 was issued to the Respondent on June 20, 2018, requiring them to explain their position and take corrective measures. Despite subsequent reminders and follow-up inspections, the Complainant maintained that the Respondent failed to adequately address the non-compliance issues, necessitating the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal under Section 21 of the PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included a detailed inspection report dated May 15, 2018, highlighting observations such as discolored effluent discharge, visible smoke from stacks, and the absence of a functional effluent treatment plant (ETP) or air pollution control devices. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory, dated June 15, 2018, which conclusively showed that samples of the Respondent's industrial effluent exceeded the NEQS limits for several parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Similarly, the analysis of air emission samples collected on June 01, 2018, indicated exceedances for Particulate Matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx). Copies of the Show Cause Notice, subsequent reminders, and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies were also placed on record. The Complainant's witnesses, including the Assistant Director (Technical) and the Laboratory Analyst, provided sworn testimonies confirming the inspection findings and the accuracy of the lab reports.
In response, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, represented by its legal counsel, denied the allegations. The Respondent contended that they were in the process of upgrading their ETP and installing new air pollution control equipment. They argued that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was flawed and that the laboratory analysis results were unreliable. The Respondent further submitted that they had invested significant capital in environmental compliance initiatives and were committed to sustainable operations. However, beyond general statements and future commitments, the Respondent failed to produce concrete evidence of a fully functional ETP or air pollution control systems that were operational and compliant with NEQS at the time of the alleged violations and subsequent inspections
.
**Discussion and Findings:**
This Tribunal has carefully considered the pleadings, the evidence presented by both parties, and the arguments advanced by their respective counsels. The core of the complaint revolves around the alleged non-compliance of M/s Nishat Mills Limited with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions, in violation of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997).
The Complainant, the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), presented a compelling case supported by concrete evidence. The inspection report clearly highlighted the visual indicators of pollution, such as discolored effluent and visible smoke. More critically, the laboratory analysis reports from a certified EPA laboratory provided scientific and quantifiable proof that the Respondent's industrial effluent and air emissions consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for multiple parameters. These reports, coupled with the sworn testimonies of EPA officials, establish a strong prima facie case of environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's operations. The issuance of a Show Cause Notice and subsequent reminders, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, further demonstrates a pattern of non-compliance and a failure to take timely corrective actions.
On the other hand, the Respondent's defense largely consisted of general denials, assertions of future commitments, and unsubstantiated claims regarding the alleged flaws in the EPA's sampling methodology and the unreliability of the laboratory analysis. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's stated intent to upgrade their pollution control infrastructure and their commitment to sustainable operations, such intentions or ongoing processes do not absolve them of their legal obligations to comply with environmental standards at all times. Crucially, the Respondent failed to produce any credible counter-evidence, such as independent laboratory reports or technical assessments, to refute the EPA's findings or to demonstrate that their existing or "in-progress" pollution control systems were indeed compliant with NEQS at the time of the alleged violations. Vague assertions of flawed methodology, without specific technical rebuttal or independent verification, cannot outweigh the evidentiary value of certified laboratory reports submitted by the regulatory authority.
The PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS are designed to protect public health and the environment from the adverse impacts of industrial activities. Non-compliance, especially by large industrial units, poses a significant threat to water bodies, air quality, and the overall ecological balance, directly affecting the well-being of surrounding communities. The evidence unequivocally demonstrates that M/s Nishat Mills Limited was in violation of these crucial environmental standards. The Respondent's failure to provide effective pollution control measures has led to the discharge of pollutants into the environment, necessitating intervention by this Tribunal.
Therefore, based on the preponderance of evidence, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully proven the allegations of environmental non-compliance against M/s Nishat Mills Limited. The Respondent has failed to operate its facility in accordance with the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**Final Order and Penalty:**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 21 read with Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby declared to be in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards and the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and non-compliant air emissions.
2.  **Cessation of Non-Compliance:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or non-compliant industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the NEQS from its facility.
3.  **Installation/Upgradation of Pollution Control Systems:** The Respondent shall, within a period of ninety (90) days from the date of this order, install, upgrade, and operationalize a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) that are capable of consistently achieving and maintaining compliance with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
