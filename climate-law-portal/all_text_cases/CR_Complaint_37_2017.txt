This judgment addresses Complaint No. 37/2017, initiated by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint was filed under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), read with other enabling provisions, alleging persistent violations of environmental standards. The Tribunal, duly constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, possesses the requisite jurisdiction to adjudicate upon the matters presented herein.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically its textile dyeing and finishing processes, were discharging untreated or inadequately treated effluent into public drains and ultimately into the River Ravi, thereby causing significant water pollution. It was alleged that the Respondent's effluent discharge consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS") for industrial effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. The EPA contended that despite repeated warnings, issuance of environmental protection orders (EPOs), and show-cause notices under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent failed to take effective measures to bring its discharge within the prescribed limits, thus posing a serious threat to public health, aquatic life, and the general environment. The complaint further highlighted the Respondent's failure to install and operate an efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or to adequately maintain its existing facility, leading to continuous non-compliance.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of documents and expert testimonies. These included:
1.  **Inspection Reports**: Multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between January 2016 and August 2017, detailing observations of effluent discharge points and operational status of the Respondent's ETP. These reports consistently noted deficiencies in the ETP's performance.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports**: Chemical analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on samples collected during the aforementioned inspections. These reports, specifically samples collected on 15.03.2017 and 21.07.2017, unequivocally showed BOD levels of 150 mg/L and 180 mg/L respectively (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), COD levels of 450 mg/L and 520 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and TSS levels of 300 mg/L and 350 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). pH levels were also found to be outside the permissible range on several occasions.
3.  **Show-Cause Notices and EPOs**: Copies of show-cause notices issued on 05.04.2017 and 01.08.2017, and an Environmental Protection Order issued on 15.05.2017, directing the Respondent to cease discharge of non-compliant effluent and implement corrective measures within a stipulated timeframe. The Complainant argued that the Respondent failed to comply with these directives.
4.  **Witness Testimony**: The Assistant Director, EPA, appeared before the Tribunal and reiterated the contents of the complaint, affirming the veracity of the inspection and laboratory reports.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, contested the allegations. Its defense primarily rested on the following points:
1.  **Technical Challenges**: It was argued that textile dyeing processes inherently produce complex wastewater, and despite best efforts, achieving 100% compliance with NEQS at all times is technically challenging, especially for older industrial setups.
2.  **ETP Upgradation Efforts**: The Respondent claimed to have initiated an upgradation project for its Effluent Treatment Plant in late 2016, which was still underway during the period of alleged violations. They presented invoices
and progress reports to demonstrate its commitment.
3.  **Sampling Irregularities**: The Respondent contended that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was not always in strict adherence to established protocols, potentially leading to inaccurate results. They suggested that samples might not have been representative of the overall discharge or that the chain of custody was not adequately maintained.
4.  **Partial Compliance**: While acknowledging some instances
of non-compliance, the Respondent asserted that its ETP was generally operational and that efforts were consistently made to meet the NEQS. They contended that the EPA's reports might have selectively highlighted non-compliant samples and failed to acknowledge periods or parameters where compliance was achieved. They also submitted some internal monitoring reports, albeit not independently verified, to support their claim of intermittent compliance.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
