This judgment addresses Complaint No. 45/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a prominent textile manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged on October 10, 2023, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), read with the Punjab Environmental Protection (Environmental Tribunals) Rules, 2012. The essence of the complaint pertains to alleged persistent violations of environmental quality standards and non-compliance with mandatory environmental regulations, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
The gravamen of the complaint lies in the allegations that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has consistently failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and gaseous emissions, specifically regarding the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater and the emission of air pollutants from its industrial operations. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated inspections, issuance of environmental protection orders, and show-cause notices, the Respondent has not taken adequate measures to control pollution effectively. Specifically, the EPA alleged that the wastewater discharged from the Respondent's dyeing and finishing units exceeded permissible limits for several key parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Furthermore, concerns were raised regarding uncontrolled emissions of particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) from the Respondent's boiler and generator stacks, contributing to ambient air pollution in the vicinity. The complaint detailed inspections conducted on June 15, 2023, August 02, 2023, and September 18, 2023, which consistently revealed non-compliance, leading to the issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of the Act on September 25, 2023, which the Respondent allegedly disregarded.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports from the aforementioned dates, prepared by EPA field teams, meticulously documenting the observed environmental non-compliances. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of wastewater samples collected from the Respondent's effluent discharge points on June 15, 2023, and August 02, 2023. These reports,
which unequivocally confirmed that the discharged wastewater parameters consistently exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, thereby corroborating the Complainant's assertions. Additionally, photographic and videographic evidence depicting the state of the Respondent’s treatment facilities and discharge points, along with copies of the previously issued Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and show-cause notices, were tendered to further bolster the Complainant's case.
In response, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. The Respondent contended that it had invested significantly in upgrading its effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. It was argued that any observed exceedances were either temporary aberrations, attributable to unforeseen operational challenges, or that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was flawed and not representative of their overall operations. The Respondent also submitted a purported action plan outlining future improvements and sought additional time to achieve full compliance, emphasizing its commitment to environmental stewardship and its substantial contribution to the national economy. They further asserted that the initial EPO was issued without adequate consideration of their ongoing efforts.
The Tribunal meticulously considered the arguments advanced by both parties and thoroughly examined the voluminous record placed before it. Oral testimonies were heard from EPA officials and representatives of the Respondent, and cross-examination of relevant witnesses was conducted. Particular attention was paid to the consistency of the EPA’s inspection findings and the scientific validity of the laboratory analysis reports, which bore the stamps of accredited laboratories and followed standard analytical procedures. The Tribunal noted that the alleged "temporary aberrations" had been observed consistently over several months, undermining the Respondent's defense. Furthermore, the action plan submitted by the Respondent, while indicative of future intent, did not negate the present and past violations clearly established by the evidence.
Upon careful evaluation, the Tribunal finds the evidence presented by the Complainant to be overwhelming and compelling. The repeated violations documented across multiple inspections and substantiated by scientific analysis
unequivocally demonstrate a consistent pattern of non-compliance by M/s Nishat Mills Limited with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the directives issued through Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs). The Respondent's arguments regarding temporary aberrations and flawed sampling methodologies are not supported by the evidence, which shows persistent exceedances over a considerable period. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
