### JUDGMENT
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 21/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, with its facility located at [Fictional Address], Sheikhupura Road, Lahore. The complaint was initiated under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized with the jurisdiction to adjudicate upon this matter. The proceedings commenced following the submission of the complaint by the EPA, subsequent to a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted at the Respondent's industrial premises.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically the emissions from its glass melting furnaces and the management of solid waste, were in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other statutory requirements. The primary allegations were:
1.
2. Failure to install and properly operate adequate air pollution control devices, resulting in the emission of particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) far exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
3. Discharge of industrial wastewater containing heavy metals, high pH, and other pollutants into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into open drains, without adequate treatment, thereby violating the NEQS for industrial effluents.
4. Improper storage and disposal of hazardous waste, including spent refractories, waste oil, and chemical sludges, posing a significant risk of soil and groundwater contamination, and lacking a proper hazardous waste management plan as required by law.
5. Operating without a valid Environmental Approval (NOC) for certain expanded operations or failing to comply with the conditions stipulated in previously issued NOCs.
The Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated [Fictional Dates], conducted by EPA officials, which detailed the observations of non-compliance. These reports were supported by laboratory analysis certificates of stack emissions, ambient air quality, and effluent samples collected from the Respondent's premises. The analysis consistently indicated exceedances of the NEQS for various parameters, particularly for particulate matter in stack emissions (e.g., PM10 and PM2.5), SO2, NOx, and for parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) in the wastewater discharge. Photographic evidence depicting visible emissions from stacks and improper waste storage was also submitted.
In response, the Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection. The Respondent submitted that it had invested substantial capital in installing state-of-the-art machinery and pollution control equipment, including bag filters and an effluent treatment plant. It was argued that any exceedances noted by the Complainant were either isolated incidents, attributable to transient operational issues, or based on flawed sampling and analytical methodologies employed by the EPA. The Respondent also presented internal environmental audit reports and records of its efforts to upgrade its facilities, along with a schedule for further improvements. It was further contended that the Respondent had obtained necessary NOCs for its operations and was in the process of renewing them or seeking approvals for expansion projects. The Respondent sought dismissal of the complaint, asserting that it was in substantial compliance with environmental laws.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including numerous documents, expert testimonies, and cross-examinations. The Tribunal also appointed an independent technical expert to conduct a site visit and submit a report on the Respondent's environmental compliance status. The expert's report largely corroborated the findings of the EPA, highlighting deficiencies in the operation and maintenance of the existing pollution control devices, particularly the inadequacy of the bag filters to consistently meet particulate matter NEQS and the intermittent functioning of the wastewater treatment plant. The report also confirmed the absence of a comprehensive hazardous waste management system.
After careful consideration of the pleadings, evidence, and arguments advanced by the learned counsels, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, on a preponderance of evidence, that the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, has been in continuous violation of several provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) framed thereunder. Specifically, the evidence overwhelmingly demonstrates:
1.  Persistent exceedance of NEQS for gaseous emissions, particularly particulate matter, from the glass melting furnaces.
2.  Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent in contravention of NEQS.
3.  Failure to implement an effective and legally compliant solid and
hazardous waste management plan, leading to improper storage and disposal of industrial and hazardous waste, thereby posing a significant threat to soil, groundwater, and public health. The Tribunal also notes the Respondent's failure to consistently operate with valid Environmental Approvals (NOCs) for its operations or to comply with the conditions stipulated therein.
The Respondent's arguments regarding isolated incidents or flawed methodologies were not found convincing in the face of consistent EPA reports, laboratory analyses, and the independent expert's corroborative findings. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of investment in pollution control, the evidence clearly indicates that these measures have been insufficient or inadequately maintained and operated to achieve compliance with the NEQS. The environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's operations is a matter of serious concern, necessitating immediate and stringent corrective actions.
**ORDER**
In view of the foregoing findings and the serious nature of the environmental violations established, this Tribunal, exercising its powers under Section 17 and Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited is hereby found guilty of violating Sections 11, 12, 13, and 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) Rules, 2000, and Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment. In addition, the Respondent shall pay a further penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)** for each day the contravention continues beyond ninety (90) days

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
