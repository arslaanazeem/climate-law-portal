**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint, bearing No. 18/2017, was lodged before this Tribunal on 15.05.2017, seeking redress for environmental pollution caused by the Respondent's industrial operations. The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction over the matter as per Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate environmental disputes and enforce compliance with environmental laws.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial unit located in the vicinity of Lahore was operating in blatant disregard of environmental safeguards. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent**: The Respondent was accused of discharging industrial wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system and/or nearby water bodies without adequate treatment, resulting in the exceedance of NEQS for industrial effluents. This discharge was alleged to contain high levels of pollutants, including but not limited to, Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and heavy metals, posing a significant threat to aquatic life, soil quality, and human health.
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding NEQS**: The Complainant further alleged that the Respondent's manufacturing processes, particularly the glass melting furnaces, were emitting smoke and particulate matter into the atmosphere in quantities far exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, leading to air pollution and adverse impacts on ambient air quality in the surrounding residential and agricultural areas.
3.  **Improper Waste Management**: The Respondent was also cited for improper handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste generated from its operations, in contravention of environmental regulations.
4.  **Failure to Obtain Environmental Approval**: It was asserted that the Respondent had either failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval for its operations or was operating in deviation from the conditions stipulated in such approvals, if granted.
5.  **Non-Compliance with Show-Cause Notices**: The Complainant submitted that despite repeated inspections, issuance of show-cause notices, and opportunities for rectification, the Respondent had failed to take adequate measures to mitigate the environmental damage and bring its operations into compliance.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between 2016 and 2017, specifically on 12.09.2016, 03.01.2017, and 18.04.2017. These reports detailed observations of dark smoke emissions, visible discharge of colored effluent, and improper waste heaps. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 03.01.2017 and 18.04.2017, and air emission samples taken from the Respondent's stacks on 12.09.2016. These reports, prepared by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently indicated parameters such as TSS, COD, BOD, and particulate matter significantly exceeding the NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the alleged pollution incidents was also presented. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 15.10.2016 and 25.0
1.2017 and 08.03.2017, unequivocally demanding the Respondent to take immediate corrective actions and to submit an environmental management plan.
The Respondent, represented by its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that M/s Ghani Glass Limited is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship. It was argued that the Respondent operates a functional Effluent
Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Systems (APCS) which are regularly maintained and fully operational, ensuring that all discharges and emissions are well within the NEQS. They presented their own internal monitoring reports and certificates from third-party environmental consultants, purportedly demonstrating compliance. Furthermore, the Respondent argued that the Complainant's sampling methodology might have been flawed, or that the samples were not representative of their routine operations. They also asserted that they possess a valid Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE) and have been diligently adhering to its conditions, and that any alleged issues were minor operational discrepancies that had been promptly addressed. Regarding the show-cause notices, the Respondent claimed to have responded to them, outlining their remedial actions and commitment to environmental protection.
This Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the entire record, including the documentary evidence, laboratory reports, and photographic/videographic evidence.
**Tribunal's Analysis and Findings:**
Upon a thorough examination of the evidence, the Tribunal finds the Complainant's submissions to be cogent and substantiated. The inspection reports consistently detail observations of environmental degradation directly attributable to the Respondent's operations. More critically, the laboratory analysis reports, generated by the EPA's accredited laboratory, provide undeniable scientific proof of significant exceedances of NEQS for both industrial effluent and air emissions. The consistency of these exceedances across multiple sampling dates undermines the Respondent's general assertions of compliance and raises serious doubts about the efficacy and regular operation of their claimed ETP and APCS. The Tribunal notes that while the Respondent presented internal monitoring reports, these lacked the independent verification and credibility associated with the Complainant's official laboratory findings. The burden of proof lay with the Complainant to establish the violations, which they have successfully discharged through robust scientific evidence.
The Respondent's claims of operating a functional ETP and APCS are not sufficiently supported by credible, independently verifiable evidence to counter the specific and repeated findings of the EPA's laboratory tests. The argument regarding flawed sampling methodology was unsubstantiated by any specific evidence or expert testimony. Furthermore, the Tribunal observes that despite the issuance of multiple show-cause notices, the Respondent failed to provide satisfactory evidence of concrete and sustained corrective measures to bring their operations into compliance with NEQS. The responses to the show-cause notices, while acknowledging receipt, largely comprised assurances rather than demonstrable proof of rectification. The photographic and videographic evidence further corroborates the visual observations of pollution, such as dark smoke and colored effluent discharge, which are indicative of operational deficiencies.
Concerning the allegation of failure to obtain environmental approval or deviation from its conditions, the Respondent failed to produce a valid, up-to-date Environmental Approval document that covered the scope of their current operations, or adequate proof of compliance with its stipulated conditions, especially regarding the discharge and emission parameters.
Therefore, based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has indeed violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, specifically with regard to:
1.  Discharge of untreated/improperly treated industrial effluent exceeding NEQS.
2.  Air emissions exceeding NEQS.
3.  Improper management and disposal of industrial waste.
4.  Failure to ensure full compliance with environmental approval conditions and/or operating without adequate environmental safeguards.
5.  Non-compliance with directives issued through show-cause notices.
These violations constitute an offense under Section 17(1) of the PEPA, 1997, which is punishable under Section 17(2) of the Act. The persistent nature of the violations, as evidenced by repeated inspections and analyses, demonstrates a serious disregard for environmental protection laws and the health and well-being of the surrounding community.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt**: The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is found guilty of violating the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, as detailed above.
2.  **Cessation of Pollution**: The Respondent shall immediately cease all activities that result in the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the NEQS.
3.  **Upgradation of Pollution Control Systems**: The Respondent is directed to forthwith undertake comprehensive upgradation and modernization of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Systems (APCS) to ensure that all industrial discharges and emissions consistently meet the prescribed NEQS. A detailed plan for such upgradation, along with a realistic timeline, shall be submitted to the Complainant (EPA) within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.
4.  **Waste Management Plan**: The Respondent shall develop and implement a robust Environmental Management Plan (EMP) specifically addressing the proper handling, storage, and disposal of all hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste generated from its operations. This EMP shall also be submitted to the Complainant within **thirty (30) days**.
5.  **Environmental Approval Compliance**: The Respondent shall ensure full compliance with all conditions of any existing Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE) or, if operating without a valid approval for its current scope of operations, shall immediately apply for and obtain the requisite approval from the Complainant as per the applicable regulations.
6.  **Monitoring and Reporting**: The Respondent shall establish a rigorous internal environmental monitoring program for its effluent, air emissions, and waste management practices. Quarterly monitoring reports, duly certified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, shall be submitted to the Complainant for a period of two years following the full implementation of the upgraded systems.
7.  **Penalty**: For the aforesaid violations, and in exercise of the powers conferred under Section 21(3) read with Section 17(2) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal imposes a monetary penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** on M/s Ghani Glass Limited. This penalty shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment.
8.  **Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**: In the event of continued non-compliance with any part of this order after the expiry of the stipulated timelines, the Respondent shall be liable to a further daily fine of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day the non-compliance continues, until full compliance is achieved. The Complainant shall be at liberty to initiate further proceedings for the recovery of such daily fines.
9.  **Compliance Report**: The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive compliance report to this Tribunal, through the Complainant, within **ninety (90) days** detailing the actions taken to implement this order, supported by documentary evidence.
This judgment is pronounced on this **10th day of October, 2019**.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
