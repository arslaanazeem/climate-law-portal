**Judgment**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), regarding alleged violations of environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant, representing the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, initiated this complaint under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), read with other enabling provisions of law. The complaint details a series of alleged non-compliances by the Respondent, a prominent textile manufacturing unit, with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other statutory obligations related to environmental protection. The Tribunal was constituted to adjudicate disputes and impose penalties for environmental violations, ensuring the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, and the prevention and control of pollution.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were generating and discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system and nearby water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental pollution. Specifically, it was contended that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed consistent exceedances of the NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, in the effluent discharged by the Respondent. The Complainant further asserted that despite repeated notices, warnings, and opportunities to rectify the situation, the Respondent failed to install a fully functional and compliant Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or to effectively operate its existing ETP to meet the prescribed standards, thereby continuing to violate Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2017, 28.04.2017, and 12.06.2017, prepared by EPA field teams, detailing the observations of non-compliance at the Respondent's premises. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, corresponding to effluent samples collected during these inspections. These reports consistently demonstrated that the Respondent's discharged effluent contained pollutants far exceeding the permissible NEQS limits. Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 20.03.2017 and 05.05.2017, along with subsequent reminders, were also tendered, evidencing the Complainant's
persistent efforts to secure compliance and the Respondent's continued failure to adequately respond or demonstrate effective remedial measures.
In response to the complaint, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its authorized representative, appeared before the Tribunal and contested the allegations. The Respondent primarily contended that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection and has already invested significantly in an Effluent Treatment Plant (ET
P) and has already invested significantly in an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). They asserted that the ETP was operational and designed to meet the NEQS, but acknowledged that occasional technical glitches or operational challenges might have led to temporary deviations. The Respondent further argued that the EPA's sampling methodology or laboratory analysis might not have accurately reflected their overall environmental performance, or that the specific samples cited were not representative of their continuous discharge. They emphasized their ongoing efforts to upgrade and maintain the ETP, presenting documents related to maintenance contracts and improvement plans. They requested that the Tribunal take a lenient view, considering their substantial investments and commitment to environmental compliance, and provide them with a further opportunity to demonstrate full compliance rather than imposing a harsh penalty.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The inspection reports and the corresponding laboratory analysis reports submitted by the Complainant consistently demonstrated that the Respondent's effluent samples, collected on multiple occasions, exceeded the prescribed NEQS limits for critical parameters. The Complainant's detailed record of show-cause notices and reminders further established that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations and provided ample opportunities to rectify the situation, yet failed to achieve consistent compliance. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent’s claims of investment in an ETP and general commitment to environmental responsibility, the onus under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, is to *not discharge* effluent that is non-compliant with NEQS. The mere existence of an ETP, or claims of operational challenges, does not absolve an industrial unit from the strict liability of meeting environmental standards. The evidence overwhelmingly points to a persistent failure on the part of the Respondent to operate its ETP effectively and consistently to meet the NEQS. The arguments regarding sampling methodology or representativeness were not sufficiently substantiated to discredit the official laboratory reports, which are presumed to be accurate unless proven otherwise. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has indeed violated Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
