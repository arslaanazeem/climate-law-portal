This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection
Agency, Lahore against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, the Respondent herein, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant, through its Assistant Director, presented compelling evidence establishing that the Respondent’s industrial operations were discharging untreated industrial effluent into public drains and emitting air pollutants exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The complaint meticulously detailed multiple inspections carried out by EPA officials, collection of effluent and emission samples, and subsequent laboratory analyses. These analyses consistently confirmed the exceedance of NEQS for various parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and particulate matter, among others. It was further submitted that despite repeated notices and warnings issued to the Respondent to take remedial measures, no adequate or timely steps were taken within the stipulated timeframes, necessitating the filing of the instant complaint before this Tribunal.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, acknowledged certain operational challenges but contended that significant investments had been made and were ongoing towards environmental compliance. It was argued that an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was under construction/upgradation and that measures were being implemented to improve air pollution control devices. The Respondent sought a lenient view, citing economic factors and a commitment to achieving full compliance in due course.
The Tribunal, having meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented by both parties, including inspection reports,
laboratory analyses, notices issued by the EPA, and the Respondent's written submissions, finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof. The evidence conclusively demonstrates that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has, for an extended period, been in violation of Sections 11, 12, and other relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. The repeated exceedances of NEQS for effluent discharge and air emissions, as confirmed by multiple independent laboratory reports, coupled with the Respondent's failure to take timely and effective remedial action despite numerous warnings, cannot be overlooked.
While the Tribunal takes note of the Respondent's ongoing efforts and investments towards environmental compliance, including the construction/upgradation of an Effluent Treatment Plant and improvements to air pollution control devices, these measures regrettably do not absolve the Respondent of past non-compliance or the continuing environmental harm caused. The commitment to future compliance, while commendable, must be balanced against the documented history of violations and the public interest in a clean and healthy environment. The Tribunal emphasizes that environmental protection is not merely a long-term goal but a continuous obligation, and industries must proactively ensure compliance rather than waiting for enforcement actions.
Therefore, after careful deliberation and in exercise of the powers vested in this Tribunal under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Finding of Violation:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is found guilty of violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, by discharging untreated industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of prescribed limits.
2.  **Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty of PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand only) to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Compliance Directive:** The Respondent shall ensure full and complete compliance with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards for both effluent discharge and air emissions within a strict period of ninety (90) days from the date of this judgment.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** During the compliance period specified in clause 3, the Respondent shall submit monthly progress reports, duly verified by a third-party environmental consultant approved by the EPA, to the Complainant and this Tribunal, detailing the measures taken and the progress achieved towards full compliance. The EPA shall also conduct unannounced inspections and independent sampling during this period.
5.  **Further Action:** Failure to achieve full compliance within the stipulated ninety (90) days or to submit the required reports shall result in further stringent legal action, including but not limited to, daily penalties as per Section 17(2) of PEPA, 1997, and sealing of the industrial unit, without further notice.
6.  **Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
