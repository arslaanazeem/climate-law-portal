This judgment pertains to a complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") on 15th May 2017. The Complainant, acting under the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, was operating in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which ultimately converges into natural water bodies. The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction over the matter as stipulated by Section 17 of the Act, aiming to ensure compliance with environmental laws and to penalize entities found in violation thereof. The proceedings involved multiple hearings, submission of documentary evidence, and examination of witnesses from both sides, culminating in the final arguments presented before this Tribunal.
The Complainant's primary allegation was that the Respondent was consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on 10th March 2017 and 25th April 2017, samples of effluent discharged from the Respondent's premises were collected. Subsequent laboratory analysis of these samples revealed significantly high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values outside the prescribed range. The EPA asserted that despite prior warnings and issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 20th January 2017, directing the Respondent to install and operationalize an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or upgrade existing facilities to meet NEQS, the Respondent failed to comply. This persistent non-compliance, according to the Complainant, constituted a grave violation of Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration or level that is in excess of the NEQS. The Complainant sought imposition of penalties and directions for immediate remedial measures.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of documents and witness testimonies. Key evidence included:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Detailed reports prepared by EPA inspection teams dated 10th March 2017 and 25th April 2017, documenting the site observations, sampling procedures, and findings regarding the Respondent's effluent discharge points.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2017/03-123 and EPA/LAB/2017/04-210, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for COD, BOD, TSS, and pH in the collected effluent samples. The reports clearly indicated concentrations several times higher than the stipulated limits.
3.  **Environmental Protection Order (EPO):** A copy of the EPO No. EPA/EPO/2017/01-005 issued to M/s Nishat Mills Limited on 20th January 2017, detailing the violations observed and mandating specific corrective actions within a defined timeframe.
4.  **Testimony of Mr. Adnan Khan:** The Assistant Director, EPA, who led the inspection teams and oversaw the sampling process, testified regarding the standard operating procedures followed for sample collection, preservation, and transportation, affirming the integrity of the evidence.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
