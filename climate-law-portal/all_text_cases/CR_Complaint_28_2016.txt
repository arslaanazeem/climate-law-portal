**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "the Company"), a textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15th March 2016, seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address alleged violations of environmental laws, specifically the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), pertaining to the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants into the environment. The EPA contends that the Respondent's industrial operations have been causing significant environmental degradation, posing a threat to public health and the surrounding ecosystem, despite repeated warnings and notices.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited revolve around the persistent discharge of industrial effluent directly into the Lahore Branch Canal and subsequently into agricultural lands, without adequate treatment. It was specifically alleged that the effluent discharged by the Respondent's textile dyeing and finishing processes consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various critical parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and concentrations of heavy metals such as Chromium and Lead. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of air pollution emanating from the Respondent's boiler operations, citing excessive emissions of particulate matter (PM), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), which were reported to be beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial emissions. The Complainant submitted that these violations constituted a direct contravention of Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the NEQS, or where no NEQS is prescribed, in
such amount, concentration or level as the Provincial Agency may prescribe.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
