This complaint, bearing No. 27/2024, was instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), read with other enabling provisions of the law. The complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial directions against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant's case is predicated on the assertion that the Respondent, a textile manufacturing unit, has been operating in a manner that contravenes the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and gaseous emissions, thereby causing environmental pollution. Specifically, the allegations include:
a. Discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants far in excess of the prescribed NEQS into the public sewerage system/drain, impacting water quality and aquatic life.
b. Emission of hazardous air pollutants from its boilers and other industrial processes, including particulate matter, nitrogen oxides (NOx), and sulfur dioxide (SO2), exceeding the NEQS, thereby contributing to ambient air pollution and posing risks to public health.
c. Failure to install, operate, and maintain an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and
adequate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), as mandated by law and environmental regulations.
d. Failure to obtain and/or renew the Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its operations, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and its associated regulations.
Despite repeated notices issued by the Complainant and summons from this Tribunal, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, failed to appear or file a written statement in response to the allegations. The Tribunal, therefore, proceeded ex-parte against the Respondent on 15.05.2024, after satisfying itself that due process had been followed in serving notices.
The Complainant presented documentary evidence, including analysis reports from accredited laboratories (e.g., Punjab Environmental Quality Control
Control Laboratory) confirming the exceedance of NEQS for various parameters in the discharged effluent and emitted gases. Inspection reports detailing the operational deficiencies of the Respondent's facility, photographic and videographic evidence showcasing the discharge of untreated effluent and visible emissions, and the affidavit of the Assistant Director, EPA, attesting to the Respondent's non-compliance and failure to respond, were also submitted. The Complainant's counsel reiterated that despite ample opportunities, the Respondent chose not to contest the allegations, leaving the evidence unchallenged.
Having meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by the Complainant and considering the Respondent's deliberate absence and failure to defend itself, this Tribunal finds the allegations leveled against M/s Nishat Mills Limited to be substantiated. The documentary evidence, particularly the laboratory analysis reports, unequivocally demonstrates that the Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent and emitting gaseous pollutants in flagrant violation of the NEQS, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing risks to public health. Furthermore, the Respondent's failure to install and maintain adequate pollution control devices and to obtain/renew the requisite environmental approvals constitutes a clear breach of its statutory obligations under PEPA, 1997, and its associated regulations. The Respondent's conduct reflects a blatant disregard for environmental laws and the well-being of the community.
Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, stipulates that whoever contravenes or fails to comply with any provision of this Act or the rules or regulations or orders made or directions issued thereunder shall be guilty of an offence and shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with an additional fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues. The gravity and persistent nature of the environmental violations established in this case warrant the imposition of a substantial penalty and stringent remedial directions.
**NOW, THEREFORE,** in light of the foregoing findings and the uncontroverted evidence, this Tribunal hereby passes the following final order:
1.  The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is found guilty of violating Sections 11, 12, and 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and gaseous emissions, and other relevant environmental regulations.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
