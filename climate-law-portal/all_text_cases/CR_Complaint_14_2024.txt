**JUDGMENT**
The present complaint, bearing No. 14/2024, was filed before this Tribunal on 15th February 2024, by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit operating in the vicinity of Lahore. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal, having heard the parties, examined the evidence, and considered the relevant legal framework, proceeds to render its judgment.
**Allegations**
The Complainant contended that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been operating its industrial facility in contravention of environmental laws and standards. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated/Improperly Treated Effluent:** That the
Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent directly into a nearby drain/water body without adequate treatment, resulting in pollution levels that consistently exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various critical parameters, including but not limited to Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals. This persistent non-compliance constitutes a clear violation of
Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with Rule 13 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000, which specifically prohibits the discharge of any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Failure to Obtain/Comply with Environmental Approval:** That the Respondent has either failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC) for its operations, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, or has not adhered to the conditions stipulated in any such approval, thereby operating without due environmental clearance or in violation of its terms.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
