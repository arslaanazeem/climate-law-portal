This judgment addresses the complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint, filed on 15th October, 2021, under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleges severe violations of environmental laws, specifically pertaining to the discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial operations were generating significant quantities of wastewater containing hazardous pollutants. Despite repeated warnings and the issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO), the Respondent continued to discharge untreated or improperly treated effluent directly into public drains, which ultimately merge into the River Ravi. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  Discharge of effluent with pH levels outside the prescribed range (6.5-8.5), high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) and Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), and the presence of heavy metals (e.g., chromium, lead) and dyes, all significantly exceeding the NEQS.
2.  Failure to install or properly operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite repeated directives and the environmental damage caused by the persistent discharge of untreated wastewater into public water bodies.
3.  Specific instances of non-compliance, as evidenced by analytical reports of effluent
samples collected on multiple occasions, specifically on 20th August, 2021, 10th September, 2021, and 5th October, 2021. These reports, prepared by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently demonstrated parameters far exceeding the NEQS, posing a severe threat to aquatic life, human health, and the overall ecological balance of the River Ravi.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 4,500,000/- (Rupees Four Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 900,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
