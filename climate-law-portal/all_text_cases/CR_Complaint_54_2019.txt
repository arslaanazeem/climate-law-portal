The present complaint, bearing No. 54/2019, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, on 15th July 2019, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, located at 12-KM, Sheikhupura Road, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The Complainant sought intervention from this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by the Respondent, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and public nuisance. This Tribunal was duly constituted to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and enforcement of environmental laws.
The genesis of the complaint lies in a series of inspections conducted by the Complainant's officials at the Respondent's industrial premises. The Complainant, through its learned counsel, Mr. Zahid Ali, averred that the Respondent, a prominent manufacturer of glass products, was operating its facility without adequate pollution control measures, particularly concerning air emissions and wastewater discharge. It was specifically alleged that the Respondent's industrial processes, including glass melting furnaces, were emitting particulate matter, Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Sulphur Oxides (SOx) significantly above the prescribed NEQS. Furthermore, the wastewater generated from its washing and cooling processes, purportedly containing elevated levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and heavy metals, was being discharged without proper treatment into the municipal sewerage system, eventually contaminating natural water bodies. The Complainant stated that multiple warning notices, including an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 12th April 2019, were issued to the Respondent, directing immediate compliance and installation of appropriate abatement technologies. Despite these directives, the Respondent allegedly failed to take effective remedial measures, necessitating the filing of the present complaint seeking imposition of penalties and directions for compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant produced a comprehensive set of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports dated 15th February 2019, 28th March 2019, and 10th June 2019, prepared by environmental inspectors, highlighting the visible plumes of smoke and suspected untreated effluent discharge. Crucially, laboratory
analysis reports of samples collected from the Respondent's premises on various dates, which unequivocally demonstrated that the levels of particulate matter (PM), NOx, and SOx in the air emissions, and TSS, COD, and heavy metals in the wastewater discharge, consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS by significant margins. Expert testimonies from environmental engineers further corroborated these findings, explaining the adverse impacts of such pollutants on human health and the ecosystem.
Despite being served with notices and provided ample opportunity to present its defense, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its learned counsel, Mr. Ahmed Khan, primarily contended that it had initiated steps towards upgrading its pollution control infrastructure and that the alleged violations were either intermittent or exaggerated. However, the Respondent failed to produce any concrete evidence, such as installation certificates, operational data of treatment plants, or recent compliance reports, to rebut the comprehensive evidence presented by the Complainant. The explanations offered were largely unsubstantiated and did not address the core allegations supported by scientific data.
Having meticulously considered the pleadings, documentary evidence, and oral arguments presented by both parties, this Tribunal finds the evidence adduced by the Complainant to be cogent, credible, and overwhelmingly establishing the persistent non-compliance by the Respondent with the NEQS and the directives issued through the Environmental Protection Order. The repeated breaches of environmental standards, despite prior warnings, demonstrate a clear disregard for environmental laws and the health and well-being of the surrounding community. The Respondent's failure to install and effectively operate adequate pollution control measures constitutes a direct violation of Sections 11, 12, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
Under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal is empowered to impose a penalty on any person who contravenes or fails to comply with the provisions of the Act, rules, or regulations made thereunder, or any order or direction issued by the Agency or the Tribunal. The gravity of the environmental degradation, the persistent nature of the violations, and the Respondent's lack of effective remedial action warrant a stringent response.
**THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED AS FOLLOWS:**
1.  **PENALTY:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited, the Respondent herein, is found guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards and the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Accordingly, a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million Only)** is imposed upon the Respondent. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
