**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 11/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of this Tribunal. The complaint, lodged on May 10, 2019, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "the Act"), alleging persistent violations of environmental standards pertaining to the discharge of industrial effluent.
The core of the Complainant's allegations is that the Respondent has been consistently discharging industrial effluent from its manufacturing facility located at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Lahore, into the public sewerage system and ultimately into natural water bodies, without adequate treatment, thereby exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under the Act. Specifically, the Complainant asserted that a routine inspection conducted by EPA officials on March 15, 2019, revealed the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was either non-functional or operating inefficiently. Samples of the discharged effluent were collected during this inspection, following due protocol, and subsequently submitted to the EPA's accredited laboratory for analysis. The laboratory report, dated March 20, 2019, allegedly confirmed alarming levels of various pollutants, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and certain heavy metals, all significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents. Following these findings, a Show Cause Notice bearing No. EPA/LH/ENV/2019/345 was issued to the Respondent on March 25, 2019, demanding an explanation and immediate remedial action. The Complainant contends that the Respondent's reply to the Show Cause Notice was unsatisfactory and failed to demonstrate effective measures to bring the discharge within NEQS limits, thus necessitating this formal complaint before the Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant submitted a comprehensive dossier of evidence, including the official inspection report of March 15, 2019, the duly attested laboratory analysis report dated March 20, 2019, clearly indicating the exceedance of NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and heavy metals, and copies of the Show Cause Notice and the Respondent’s
unsatisfactory reply. The Tribunal, taking cognizance of the complaint, issued notices to the Respondent, who subsequently appeared through their legal counsel.
The Respondent, in their written statement and subsequent arguments, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. They contended that their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was fully functional and regularly maintained, operating in accordance with design specifications. They further argued that the single inspection on March 15, 2019, and the subsequent laboratory report, could not be considered conclusive proof of "persistent violations." The Respondent submitted their own internal monitoring reports, maintenance logs for the ETP, and a report from a private environmental consultant affirming the general operational efficiency of their ETP. They also raised questions regarding the chain of custody for the samples collected by EPA and the calibration status of the EPA's laboratory equipment on the specific date of analysis, though no concrete evidence was presented to discredit the EPA's established protocols.
During the course of the proceedings, the Tribunal heard detailed arguments from both sides. The Complainant reiterated the findings of the EPA's inspection and the irrefutable evidence provided by the accredited laboratory report, emphasizing the severe exceedance of NEQS for critical parameters. The Respondent's counsel, while acknowledging the EPA's report, sought to portray it as an isolated incident, possibly due to a temporary malfunction, which they claimed had since been rectified. They presented evidence of recent upgrades to their ETP and commitments to implement stricter internal monitoring protocols.
After careful consideration of the documentary evidence, the oral submissions, and the legal framework established by the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal finds the Complainant's allegations to be substantially proven. The laboratory report of March 20, 2019, prepared by an accredited laboratory following due process, stands as compelling evidence of the discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards on the date of inspection. While the Respondent presented internal documents, these did not sufficiently rebut the specific, damning findings of the EPA's official analysis. The mere assertion of a 'temporary malfunction' without concrete, verifiable evidence or a subsequent satisfactory compliance report from an independent body is insufficient to negate the established violation. **V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
