This judgment addresses Complaint No. 11/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant's primary allegation is that the Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, resulting in the exceedance of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, thereby contravening the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). Specifically, the Complainant asserted that during a routine inspection conducted on March 15, 2023, EPA officials visited the Respondent's premises and collected samples of treated effluent being discharged from their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) outfall. Subsequent laboratory analysis of these samples, conducted by the EPA's certified laboratory, revealed parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS. A show-cause notice, dated April 10, 2023, was issued to the Respondent, demanding an explanation for the non-compliance and requiring immediate corrective actions. The Complainant further contended that despite the issuance of the notice and subsequent follow-up communications, the Respondent failed to demonstrate sustained compliance or provide a satisfactory action plan, necessitating the initiation of formal proceedings before this Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented the comprehensive inspection report dated March 15, 2023, prepared by the inspection team, detailing the observations made at the Respondent's facility, including the operational status of their ETP. Crucially, the Complainant submitted the official laboratory analysis report (Ref: EPA/Lab/2023/04-012, dated March 25, 2023), which categorically listed the measured values of the effluent parameters against the corresponding NEQS limits, clearly indicating multiple exceedances. The show-cause notice issued to the Respondent, along with the proof of its dispatch and receipt, was also placed on record. The Complainant further produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Inspector, who conducted the inspection and collected the samples, confirming the chain of custody and the procedures followed.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. While acknowledging the inspection and sample collection, the Respondent contended that the reported exceedances were an isolated incident, possibly due to a temporary operational glitch in their ETP, which was immediately rectified upon detection. They argued that their facility has a functional E
TP and is committed to environmental compliance. They submitted that immediately after receiving the show-cause notice, they initiated a thorough internal review of their ETP operations. They presented evidence of maintenance records for the period subsequent to the inspection, demonstrating remedial actions taken to optimize the ETP's performance. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed to have conducted their own independent laboratory analysis of effluent samples collected *after*
the initial EPA inspection, which, according to their submitted reports, demonstrated compliance with the NEQS. They argued that these post-inspection results indicated that the reported exceedances were transient and not reflective of their general operational standards. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
