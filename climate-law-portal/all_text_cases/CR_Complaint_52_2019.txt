This judgment addresses Complaint No. 52/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking redress for alleged violations of environmental laws. The Tribunal’s jurisdiction is invoked under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental standards.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial enterprise engaged in manufacturing activities, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the River Ravi, thereby causing significant environmental pollution. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted on various dates, particularly on 15.03.2019 and 22.05.2019, samples of the Respondent's industrial effluent were collected. Subsequent laboratory analyses of these samples by the EPA's accredited laboratory revealed that several parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeded the permissible limits stipulated by the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. The Complainant further contended that despite repeated verbal and written warnings, including a formal Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued on 10.06.2019 under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent failed to take adequate measures to bring its effluent discharge within the prescribed NEQS, thus posing a continuous threat to public health and the aquatic ecosystem. The complaint sought the imposition of penalties as per law and a directive for immediate compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented documentary evidence including the inspection reports dated 15.03.2019 and 22.05.2019, detailed laboratory analysis reports of the collected effluent samples clearly indicating exceedances of NEQS, copies of the warning notices and the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued to the Respondent, and photographic evidence depicting the discharge point. The Complainant also produced an expert witness, a Senior Environmental Analyst from EPA, who testified regarding the sampling methodology, analytical procedures, and the interpretation of results, confirming the non-compliance. On the other hand, the Respondent, represented by its legal counsel and technical team, vehemently denied the allegations. The Respondent presented its own internal monitoring data, which it claimed showed compliance or near-compliance with NEQS. It was argued that the Respondent had invested substantially in an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was in the process of upgrading it. The Respondent challenged the Complainant's sampling methodology, alleging that samples might not have been representative or that the laboratory analysis could be flawed. Affidavits from the Respondent's plant manager and environmental officer were submitted, outlining efforts to improve environmental performance. The Respondent also presented receipts for the purchase of chemicals and equipment for the ETP as evidence of ongoing efforts.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, or where applicable, the standards established under sub-section (1) of Section 12. Section 16 empowers the Agency to issue an Environmental Protection Order to a person contravening any provision of the Act or its rules, requiring them to take measures to mitigate or prevent pollution. Crucially, Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, prescribes penalties for contravention or failure to comply with any provision of the Act or the rules
or orders made thereunder. Section 20 grants the Tribunal the power to impose penalties and issue directives, including orders to cease pollution, install pollution control devices, or pay compensation for environmental damage. The Tribunal also considers the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as the legally binding benchmark for compliance under PEPA, 1997.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary and testimonial evidence on record. The Complainant's evidence, particularly the laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, stands as strong prima facie proof of non-compliance. The reports consistently indicate that the Respondent's effluent parameters, including BOD5, COD, TSS, and pH, significantly exceeded the NEQS on multiple occasions. The testimony of the Senior Environmental Analyst further corroborated the scientific validity of the sampling and analysis procedures. The issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, prior to the filing of this complaint, demonstrates that the EPA had provided the Respondent with an opportunity to rectify the situation, which, based on subsequent samples, was not adequately utilized.
While the Respondent presented internal monitoring data and receipts for ETP upgrades, these do not sufficiently rebut the specific findings of non-compliance on the dates of EPA's inspections. Internal monitoring data, while useful for operational control, lacks the independent verification and legal weight of samples collected and analyzed by the regulatory authority's accredited laboratory. Furthermore, the fact that the Respondent is in the process of upgrading its ETP, while commendable, does not absolve it of its past or ongoing responsibility to comply with NEQS. Environmental compliance is a continuous obligation, not merely an aspirational goal. The Respondent's general allegations regarding flawed sampling methodology were not substantiated with concrete evidence that would cast reasonable doubt on the EPA's procedures.
Therefore, based on the totality of the evidence, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Packages Limited was discharging industrial effluent in contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby causing environmental pollution.
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in
**it under Section 20 read with Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:**
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
1.  **Declaration of Non-Compliance:** It is hereby declared that M/s Packages Limited (the Respondent) has contravened Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Imposition of Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is hereby ordered to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million Only)** for the established violations of environmental laws. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Directive for Immediate Compliance:** The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and to ensure that all effluent discharged from its premises strictly conforms to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at all times.
4.  **Installation/Upgrade of Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** The Respondent shall, within a period of **four (4) months** from the date of this judgment, complete all necessary upgrades, modifications, or installation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure consistent compliance with NEQS. The Respondent shall submit a detailed action plan for achieving this compliance to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within fifteen (15) days.
5.  **Submission of Monitoring Reports:** During the four-month compliance period, the Respondent shall submit weekly self-monitoring reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, demonstrating compliance with NEQS. After the compliance period, monthly reports shall be submitted for a period of one year.
6.  **Inspection and Verification:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is authorized and directed to conduct unannounced inspections and collect samples from the Respondent's premises to verify compliance with this order.
7.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance with this Order:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the timely payment of the penalty or achievement of NEQS within the stipulated period, shall render the Respondent liable to further legal action, including but not limited to, the imposition of a daily penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** for each day of continued non-compliance, and/or closure of operations as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
8.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 150,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Fifty Thousand Only)**, to be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days.
This judgment is pronounced on this **25th day of October, 2023**.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **06 January, 2023**.
