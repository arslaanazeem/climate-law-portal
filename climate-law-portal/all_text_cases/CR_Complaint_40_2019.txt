**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the environmental complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), an industrial unit operating at [Hypothetical Address, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore]. The complaint, bearing No. 40/2019, was filed under Section 19 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged contraventions. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 11 of the Act, is seized of jurisdiction to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and its abatement.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, initiated these proceedings alleging that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit, was operating in flagrant disregard of environmental laws and regulations. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent had failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval for its establishment and subsequent expansions, as mandated by Section 12 of the Act and the Review of IEE/EIA Regulations, 2000. Furthermore, the Complainant contended that the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into a nearby drain/canal, and emitting air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby violating Section 11 of the Act. The EPA had, on multiple occasions, inspected the Respondent's premises, issued show-cause notices, and conducted sampling of effluent and emissions, all of which reportedly confirmed the persistent non-compliance and the absence of effective pollution control measures. The cumulative effect of these alleged violations was stated to be severe environmental degradation, adverse impacts on aquatic life, soil contamination, and potential health risks to the surrounding communities.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, starting from [Hypothetical Date, e.g., 15.03.2018] up to [Hypothetical Date, e.g., 20.08.2019], detailing observations of untreated effluent discharge and lack of proper air emission control devices. Laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected on [Hypothetical Date, e.g., 01.05.2018] and [Hypothetical Date, e.g., 10.07.2019] were submitted, unequivocally demonstrating that parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS. Similarly, reports concerning
air emissions, particularly from the Respondent's boiler and process stacks, submitted on [Hypothetical Date, e.g., 25.06.2019], indicated levels of Particulate Matter (PM), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx) that were substantially higher than the NEQS. These findings collectively painted a grim picture of persistent and widespread environmental non-compliance.
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, primarily arguing that it had initiated steps towards upgrading its pollution control infrastructure and was in the process of applying for the requisite environmental approvals. It was contended that the samples collected by the EPA were not representative or that the testing methodologies were flawed. Furthermore, the Respondent pleaded financial constraints and the complexity of installing advanced treatment systems as mitigating factors, requesting leniency and additional time to comply. Affidavits and some documentary evidence, including purchase orders for preliminary equipment and consultation agreements, were presented to demonstrate intent to comply.
The Complainant, however, countered that the Respondent had been given ample opportunities over several years to rectify its violations, yet concrete and effective measures remained largely unimplemented. The presented 'intent to comply' was deemed insufficient in the face of ongoing, demonstrable environmental damage. The Tribunal carefully considered the arguments from both sides, meticulously reviewing the extensive
documentary evidence and the arguments advanced by both sides.
The Tribunal notes that the Complainant's evidence, comprising multiple detailed inspection reports and scientific laboratory analyses of effluent and air emissions, consistently points to a pattern of non-compliance over an extended period. The samples were collected and analyzed by accredited laboratories, and the results unequivocally demonstrate that the Respondent's discharge and emissions parameters significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The absence of a valid EIA/IEE approval, as mandated by Section 12 of the Act, is also a clear violation that the Respondent has not effectively refuted.
While the Respondent's counsel presented arguments regarding steps towards future compliance and financial constraints, these do not negate the established facts of past and ongoing violations. The intent to comply, without concrete and operational pollution control measures in place, cannot absolve the Respondent of its responsibility under the Act. Environmental protection laws are designed to prevent pollution, not merely to encourage future compliance after damage has occurred. Financial constraints, while regrettable, cannot be accepted as a justification for causing environmental degradation, especially for an industrial unit of the Respondent's scale. The law places a strict liability on industrial units to operate in an environmentally sound manner.
The Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof, establishing beyond reasonable doubt that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been operating in persistent contravention of Sections 11 and 12 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and has consistently violated the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both effluent discharge and air emissions. The cumulative evidence overwhelmingly demonstrates a severe and continuing breach of environmental laws, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is found guilty of violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent is immediately directed to cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into any drain/canal and to stop emitting air pollutants exceeding the NEQS.
3.  **Installation of Pollution Control Measures:** The Respondent shall, within **one hundred and twenty (120) days** from the date of this order, install, commission, and ensure the effective operation of a comprehensive Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) that meet the prescribed NEQS.
4.  **Environmental Approvals:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, apply for and diligently pursue the obtaining of the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval, as mandated by Section 12 of the Act and the Review of IEE/EIA Regulations, 2000.
5.  **Compliance Reports:** The Respondent shall submit monthly progress reports to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, detailing the steps taken towards compliance with this order, verified by independent third-party environmental consultants where appropriate, until full compliance is achieved. The first report shall be due thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
6.  **Penalty:** For the established and prolonged violations of environmental laws, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby imposed a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)**. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
7.  **Default Penalty:** In case of failure to comply with any part of this order, particularly with regard to the installation and operationalization of pollution control measures and obtaining environmental approvals within the stipulated timeframes, the Respondent shall be liable to a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day the contravention continues, until full compliance is achieved. The EPA is authorized to take all necessary legal steps for the recovery of these penalties.
This Tribunal underscores the critical importance of industrial units adhering to environmental laws to safeguard public health and preserve the ecological integrity of our nation.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
