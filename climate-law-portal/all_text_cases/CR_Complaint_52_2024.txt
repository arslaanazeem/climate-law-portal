This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging multiple violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and its associated rules and regulations. The Tribunal was constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters concerning environmental pollution and to enforce environmental standards. The present proceedings commenced upon the formal submission of Complaint No. 52/2024, seeking redress for persistent environmental non-compliance by the Respondent, a textile manufacturing unit operating within the jurisdiction of Lahore.
The Complainant brought forth several serious allegations against the Respondent. Primarily, it was alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited was discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies without adequate treatment, consistently exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. The Complainant further asserted that the Respondent's industrial operations were releasing uncontrolled air emissions, particularly particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), from its boiler stacks and dyeing units, which also surpassed the prescribed NEQS for air quality. It was also contended that the Respondent had failed to obtain a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) for certain expansions and operational changes implemented since 2022, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997. Additionally, the complaint highlighted improper management of hazardous waste, including sludge from the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and chemical residues, which were allegedly being disposed of indiscriminately without adherence to the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003. The Complainant specified that despite repeated inspection visits, issuance of environmental protection orders (EPOs) dated 15.01.2024 and 28.02.2024, and subsequent show-cause notices, the Respondent had failed to implement effective corrective measures, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental laws and public health.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This
included detailed laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on multiple occasions, clearly demonstrating consistent exceedances of NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
