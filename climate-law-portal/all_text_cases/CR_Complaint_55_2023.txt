**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 55/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products, operating its facility at Sunder Industrial Estate, Raiwind Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA, 1997"), and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter "NEQS"), specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluents and gaseous emissions. The matter was brought before this Tribunal on 15th May 2023, following a series of inspections and notices issued by the Complainant. The Tribunal’s jurisdiction is invoked under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate environmental disputes and impose penalties for non-compliance.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial facility was found to be in persistent non-compliance with the prescribed environmental standards. Specifically, the allegations included:
a.   **Discharge of Untreated or Inadequately Treated Effluents:** On multiple inspection visits, particularly on 10th February 2023 and 15th March 2023, samples of industrial wastewater discharged from the Respondent's premises into the common drainage system were collected. Laboratory analysis of these samples, conducted by the EPA's accredited laboratory, revealed parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents.
b.   **Exceedance of Gaseous Emission Standards:** Monitoring of stack emissions from the Respondent's furnace on 15th March 2023 indicated levels of particulate matter (PM), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) surpassing the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. The Complainant alleged that the existing air pollution control devices were either inadequate or improperly maintained.
c.   **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste:** The Complainant also highlighted concerns regarding the storage and disposal of potentially hazardous solid waste generated during the glass manufacturing process, alleging a lack of proper segregation, temporary storage, and disposal mechanisms in line with environmental regulations.
d.   **Failure to Comply with Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs):** The Complainant submitted that despite numerous verbal warnings, formal show cause notices issued on 20th February 2023 and 25th March 2023, and a subsequent Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, on 5th April 2023, directing the Respondent to rectify the violations within a stipulated timeframe, the Respondent failed to achieve satisfactory compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA field teams, complete with photographic evidence documenting the state of effluent discharge points and stack emissions. Crucially, the Complainant submitted certified copies of laboratory analysis reports for both wastewater and gaseous emission samples, clearly indicating the exceedance of NEQS values. These reports were supported by the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director (Monitoring) at EPA, Lahore, who conducted the inspections and sampling, and Ms. Sara Ali, Chief Analyst at the EPA Laboratory, who verified the analytical
protocols and the accuracy of the results. The chain of custody for all samples was meticulously maintained, ensuring the integrity of the evidence presented.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, represented by its legal counsel, Mr. Zahid Hussain, vehemently contested the allegations of persistent non-compliance. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
