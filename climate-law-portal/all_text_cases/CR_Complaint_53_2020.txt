This judgment addresses Complaint No. 53/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS").
The Complainant alleges that the Respondent has consistently failed to comply with the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents, discharging wastewater containing pollutants in excess of permissible limits into the public sewerage system/open drains, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. Specifically, the complaint details multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials, which revealed an inadequately functioning Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) at the Respondent’s premises. Furthermore, it is alleged that the Respondent has been operating without obtaining the requisite Environmental Approval (Initial Environmental Examination/Environmental Impact Assessment) for its operations, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, or has failed to adhere to the conditions stipulated in any such approval. The EPA asserts that despite repeated warnings, show-cause notices, and opportunities to rectify the deficiencies, the Respondent has remained non-compliant, necessitating recourse to this Honourable Tribunal for punitive and remedial action.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, dating from January 2019 to August 2020, detailing observations of effluent discharge and the operational status of the Respondent's ETP. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected on various dates (e.g., February 15, 2019; July 10, 2019; March 05, 2020; and August 22, 2020) were submitted. These reports consistently indicated concentrations of parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH exceeding the NEQS. The Complainant also produced copies of show-cause notices issued under Section 16(1) of PEPA, 1997, dated March 20, 2019, and April 15, 2020, along with the Respondent's replies, which the Complainant deemed unsatisfactory and indicative of persistent non-compliance. The EPA further presented evidence of the Respondent's failure to provide a satisfactory Environmental Management Plan (EMP) or proof of a valid environmental approval.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. The Respondent contended that it operates a functional ETP and has invested significant capital in its maintenance and upgrading. It was argued that any exceedances in NEQS were either isolated incidents, attributable to technical glitches, or sampling inconsistencies, rather than a systemic failure. The Respondent also presented documents purportedly showing efforts towards ETP improvement and a commitment to environmental stewardship. They claimed to have applied for environmental approval or were in the process of updating it, asserting that the complexity of industrial processes sometimes leads to temporary operational challenges. The Respondent requested a lenient view, highlighting its contribution to the national economy and employment.
This Tribunal relies on the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly:
*   **Section 11 (Prohibition of certain discharges or emissions):** Which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the NEQS.
*   **Section 12 (Initial Environmental Examination and Environmental Impact Assessment):** Which mandates that no proponent of a project shall commence construction or operation unless an IEE or EIA, as required, has been filed with the Agency and approval granted.
*   **Section
16 (Penalties): Which prescribes penalties for contravention of Sections 11 and 12, including fines and directions for remedial measures.
**Analysis and Findings:**
This Tribunal has thoroughly examined the documentary evidence and heard the arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent. The Complainant, through its inspection reports and, more significantly, the consistent laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected on multiple dates (February 15, 2019; July 10, 2019; March 05, 2020; and August 22, 2020), has credibly demonstrated that the Respondent's industrial effluents consistently exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for parameters such as COD, BOD, TSS, and pH. These repeated violations, spanning over a significant period, cannot be dismissed as isolated incidents or mere technical glitches. The consistency of the exceedances strongly indicates a systemic failure in the Respondent's effluent treatment process.
Furthermore, the Complainant presented clear evidence of issuing show-cause notices dated March 20, 2019, and April 15, 2020, providing the Respondent ample opportunity to rectify its deficiencies. The Respondent's replies were deemed unsatisfactory, indicating a persistent lack of effective corrective action.
Regarding the requirement for Environmental Approval under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent failed to provide conclusive proof of obtaining or adhering to the conditions of a valid Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for its operations. The mere assertion of having applied for or being in the process of updating an approval does not absolve the Respondent of its statutory obligation to operate with a valid and compliant environmental approval.
The Respondent's arguments regarding isolated incidents, technical glitches, or sampling inconsistencies are unconvincing given the documented history of persistent violations. The evidence presented by the EPA, particularly the consistent laboratory reports and the failure to provide satisfactory proof of environmental approval, unequivocally establishes the Respondent’s non-compliance with Section 11 and Section 12 of the PEPA, 1997. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has indeed discharged industrial effluents exceeding the NEQS and has failed to operate with the requisite environmental approval, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a risk to public health, contrary to the spirit and letter of environmental laws.
In light of the foregoing analysis and findings, this Tribunal hereby finds M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, the Respondent, guilty of violating Section 11 (Prohibition of certain discharges or emissions) and Section 12 (Initial Environmental Examination and Environmental Impact Assessment) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
1.  **Financial Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** for the proven violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
2.  **Remedial Measures and Compliance Directives:**
    a.  **ETP Upgrade and Optimization:** The Respondent shall immediately undertake all necessary measures to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the efficient operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for all relevant parameters. A detailed action plan for ETP improvement, including a timeline for implementation, shall be submitted to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, within **forty-five (45) days** from the date of this judgment.
    b.  **Environmental Approval:** The Respondent shall obtain the requisite Environmental Approval (IEE/EIA) for its operations, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and submit proof thereof to the EPA, Lahore, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment. If an application is pending, the Respondent shall vigorously pursue its completion and approval within the stipulated timeframe.
    c.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** For a period of one (1) year, commencing from the date of this judgment, the Respondent shall conduct monthly self-monitoring of its effluent discharge quality through an EPA-accredited laboratory and submit the analytical reports to the EPA, Lahore, by the 10th of each succeeding month. The EPA, Lahore, shall also conduct unannounced inspections and sampling to verify compliance.
    d.  **Environmental Management Plan (EMP):** The Respondent shall develop and submit a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP) detailing measures for waste reduction, resource efficiency, and continuous environmental compliance to the EPA, Lahore, within **sixty (60) days** of this judgment.
3.  **Default Clause:** In the event of continued non-compliance with any of the directives outlined in paragraph 2 above, or failure to pay the imposed penalty within the stipulated time, the Respondent shall be liable to an additional daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day of default, until compliance is achieved, without prejudice to any other legal action that the EPA may initiate under PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
