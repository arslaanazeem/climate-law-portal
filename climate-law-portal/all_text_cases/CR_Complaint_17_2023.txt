This Tribunal was seized with Complaint No. 17/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products with a manufacturing facility located within the territorial jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint was instituted under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited stemmed from a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted by EPA officials
, which revealed consistent exceedances of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for air emissions, particularly regarding Particulate Matter (PM) and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), emanating from its manufacturing processes. Furthermore, the inspections highlighted deficiencies in the Respondent's wastewater treatment mechanisms, leading to the discharge of effluents exceeding prescribed NEQS limits for various parameters, including Total Suspended Solids (TSS) and
Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD). The complaint also cited a failure to submit regular environmental monitoring reports as required under their Environmental Approval conditions, demonstrating a lack of compliance with reporting obligations and a potential disregard for environmental management protocols.
In response to the complaint, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. They contended that they had invested significantly in state-of-the-art pollution control equipment, including electrostatic precipitators and advanced wastewater treatment plants, and were operating in substantial compliance with environmental regulations. They further argued that any observed exceedances were isolated incidents, perhaps due to temporary operational fluctuations or errors in sampling/analysis, and did not reflect a systemic failure. The Respondent also presented evidence of past environmental approvals and certifications, asserting their commitment to environmental stewardship. They requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, or in the alternative, to allow them a reasonable period to rectify any minor deficiencies.
The Tribunal carefully considered the pleadings of both parties, numerous documentary evidences including inspection reports, laboratory analysis results, environmental monitoring data, and the Respondent's compliance records. Oral arguments were heard over several sessions, and expert opinions were also taken into account regarding the technical aspects of the emissions and effluent standards.
Upon a thorough review of the material on record, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant, EPA, has successfully discharged its onus by presenting compelling evidence demonstrating consistent violations of the NEQS by the Respondent. The inspection reports and analytical results, particularly from multiple independent sampling events, clearly indicate that the Respondent’s air emissions for PM and NOx, and wastewater effluents for TSS and BOD, have repeatedly exceeded the prescribed limits. The arguments put forth by the Respondent, while acknowledging their efforts, failed to adequately explain or justify the persistent nature of these exceedances. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
