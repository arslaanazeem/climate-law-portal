This judgment addresses Complaint No. 18/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and its allied rules and regulations. The complaint pertains to the Respondent's industrial operations located in Lahore, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants, causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. The Tribunal has meticulously examined the pleadings, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just determination.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, has been operating in blatant disregard of environmental laws. Specifically, it was averred that the Respondent's facility was discharging industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drain without adequate treatment, resulting in its characteristics exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Municipal and Industrial Effluents. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial processes were emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter and various gases, beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS for Industrial Gaseous Emissions. The Complainant submitted that these violations were first identified
during a routine inspection conducted on [Date], followed by subsequent detailed environmental monitoring reports [reference specific EPA Lab Report No. and Date] which unequivocally confirmed the discharge of untreated effluent exceeding NEQS parameters for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, and air emissions containing particulate matter (PM10/PM2.5) and SOx/NOx above permissible limits. The Complainant further submitted copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on [Date 1] and [Date 2], requiring them to cease the violations and submit a comprehensive compliance plan. Despite repeated reminders and opportunities provided to rectify the situation, including a personal hearing on [Date 3], the Respondent allegedly failed to take effective remedial measures, thereby necessitating the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal.
The Respondent, through its counsel, while acknowledging the operational nature of its industrial unit, generally denied the allegations of continuous non-compliance. It was contended that the Respondent had initiated steps towards upgrading its environmental control infrastructure, including the planning for an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices, and requested further time for implementation. However, the Respondent failed to provide concrete evidence of substantial progress or a viable, time-bound action plan that would satisfy the requirements of PEPA, 1997 and NEQS.
The Tribunal, having meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, including the inspection reports, laboratory analysis results, photographic evidence, and
the detailed statements of both parties, finds that the Complainant has presented overwhelming and credible evidence to substantiate the allegations of environmental non-compliance against the Respondent. The environmental monitoring reports, specifically [reference specific EPA Lab Report No. and Date], unequivocally demonstrate that the Respondent's industrial effluent consistently exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, and its air emissions, particularly particulate matter (PM10/PM2.5), SOx, and NOx, are beyond permissible limits. These findings are further corroborated by photographic evidence and the inspection reports, which clearly indicate the discharge of untreated effluent into the public sewerage system/drain and visible emissions from the Respondent's stacks.
The Respondent's contention that it had "initiated steps" towards upgrading its environmental control infrastructure, including the planning for an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices, without providing any concrete evidence of substantial progress, detailed timelines, or financial commitments, is insufficient to rebut the clear and present evidence of ongoing violations. The mere intention to comply, without demonstrable action, does not absolve the Respondent of its statutory obligations under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its allied rules and regulations, particularly the NEQS. The Respondent has had ample opportunity, through show-cause notices and personal hearings, to rectify the situation, yet has failed to do so effectively.
The continuous discharge of untreated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants above permissible limits constitute a clear violation of Sections 11, 12, and 13 of PEPA, 1997, read with the NEQS. Such actions not only cause significant environmental degradation but also pose a serious threat to public health and the quality of life of the surrounding communities. The Tribunal is mandated to ensure strict compliance with environmental laws to protect and preserve the environment.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
