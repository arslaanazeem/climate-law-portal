**Introduction**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 63/2016, lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint was filed under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, ultimately impacting a nearby canal.
**Allegations**
The Complainant, through its complaint and subsequent submissions, primarily alleged that the Respondent was operating its industrial facility without an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or, alternatively, failing to operate its existing ETP in a manner that would ensure compliance with the NEQS for industrial effluents. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated/Sub-Standard Effluent:** The Respondent was allegedly discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS for
various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and oil and grease, among others.
2.  **Ineffective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** The Respondent's ETP was either non-functional, improperly maintained, or designed inadequately to meet the required treatment standards, leading to the discharge of hazardous pollutants.
3.  **Repeated Non-Compliance:** Despite multiple notices, warnings, and opportunities provided by the EPA for rectification, the Respondent allegedly failed to take effective measures to bring its operations into compliance with environmental laws.
4.  **Environmental Degradation:** The discharge of such effluent was causing significant environmental degradation, polluting the public sewerage system, and ultimately contaminating the nearby canal, thereby posing a threat to aquatic life and human health.
**Respondent's Stance**
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its representatives, generally denied the persistent nature of the violations. While acknowledging the existence of an ETP, they contended that it was operational and that any instances of non-compliance were isolated incidents due to technical glitches, power outages, or maintenance activities, rather than a systemic failure to treat effluent. They submitted that they were committed to environmental protection and had invested significantly in their ETP. They requested leniency and further time to upgrade their existing facilities if any deficiencies were indeed found.
**Proceedings and Evidence**
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence and heard arguments from both sides. The Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, effluent sampling reports, and laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA's certified laboratories from 2015 to 2016. These reports consistently indicated that the Respondent's discharged effluent exceeded the NEQS for various critical parameters on multiple occasions. Notices of non-compliance issued to the Respondent by the EPA were also placed on record, demonstrating a pattern of repeated warnings and the Respondent's failure to adequately respond.
The Respondent, in its defense, submitted some internal monitoring reports and invoices related to ETP maintenance. However, these submissions were largely insufficient to counter the objective and consistent data presented by the EPA's official laboratory reports. The Tribunal also noted the Respondent's failure to provide a comprehensive action plan or concrete evidence of significant upgrades to its ETP during the period of alleged violations, despite having ample opportunity to do so.
**Findings of the Tribunal**
Upon careful consideration of the evidence and the submissions of both parties, this Tribunal finds as follows:
1.  The Complainant has successfully established through credible and consistent laboratory reports that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, was indeed discharging industrial effluent that consistently violated the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) during the period in question.
2.  The Respondent's explanations regarding isolated technical glitches or maintenance issues are not persuasive enough to explain the recurring nature and extent of the NEQS violations documented by the EPA. The evidence points towards either an inadequately designed ETP or a severe failure in its operation and maintenance.
3.  The Respondent failed to take timely and effective corrective measures despite repeated notices and warnings from the Environmental Protection Agency.
4.  The persistent discharge of sub-standard effluent constitutes a clear contravention of Section 12 and Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and poses a significant threat to the environment and public health.
**Final Order and Penalty**
In light of the foregoing findings, and in exercise of the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17(2) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Compliance with NEQS:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to forthwith ensure that its industrial effluent discharge strictly complies with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents.
2.  **ETP Upgrade/Maintenance:** The Respondent shall immediately take all necessary steps to upgrade, repair, or replace its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and ensure its continuous and effective operation to achieve full compliance with NEQS. The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance action plan, including timelines, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Penalty:** For the persistent violations of environmental laws and NEQS, a penalty of **Rupees Seven Hundred and Fifty Thousand (Rs. 750,000/-)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
