The present complaint, bearing No. 19/2024, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial enterprise located in Lahore, under various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The jurisdiction of this Tribunal has been duly invoked under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate upon alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The matter at hand concerns the Respondent's compliance with National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other statutory obligations pertaining to industrial operations and pollution control.
The gravamen of the complaint lies in the alleged persistent failure of M/s Packages Limited to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emissions into the environment. The Complainant contends that despite repeated notices and opportunities for rectification, the Respondent has continued to discharge untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the municipal sewerage system, and to emit air pollutants from its boiler stacks that exceed the prescribed NEQS. Furthermore, the complaint alleges non-compliance with the self-monitoring and reporting requirements mandated by the EPA. Specifically, the allegations include:
a. Discharge of industrial effluent exceeding NEQS for parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH value.
b. Emission of air pollutants, particularly particulate matter (PM) and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), from its industrial processes, exceeding the NEQS for industrial emissions.
c. Failure to submit comprehensive and accurate self-monitoring reports as required under the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2001.
d. Non-compliance with the directions issued by the EPA through various environmental protection orders and notices, indicating a disregard for environmental regulations.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a robust body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.01.2024, 28.02.2024, and 12.03.2024, conducted by EPA teams at the Respondent's facility. Crucially, these reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis results of effluent and emission samples collected during these inspections. The analytical reports, prepared by the accredited EPA laboratory, consistently indicated that the discharged effluent parameters (e.g., COD levels of 450 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, BOD5 levels of 180 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L, and TSS of 220 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L) significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS. Similarly, air emission samples showed particulate matter levels of 850 mg/Nm3 against a NEQS limit of 6
00 mg/Nm3, and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx) also exceeding permissible limits. The Complainant further submitted copies of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued to the Respondent on 05.02.2024 and 20.03.2024, directing immediate corrective measures and imposing certain conditions, which, according to the Complainant, were largely unheeded. The non-submission of accurate self-monitoring reports was corroborated by the absence of credible data from the Respondent's side to counter the EPA's findings.
In response to the complaint, M/s Packages Limited, through its authorized representative and legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal. The Respondent primarily contested the validity of the sampling procedures and the accuracy of the laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA. It was argued that the samples might not have been representative of the facility's overall operations and that the EPA's methodologies were flawed. The Respondent further contended that it has invested substantially in environmental management systems and pollution control technologies, including an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, and that any non-compliance was either temporary, due to operational upsets, or was being actively addressed through ongoing upgrades and maintenance. The Respondent's counsel requested additional time to bring the facility into full compliance, emphasizing the company's significant contribution to the national economy and employment. However, the Respondent failed to produce compelling counter-evidence, such as independent laboratory reports or detailed records of ETP/APCD maintenance and performance, that could directly refute the specific findings of the EPA's inspection reports and analytical results. No verifiable self-monitoring reports demonstrating compliance were presented for the periods in question.
This Tribunal, having carefully considered the pleadings, the documentary evidence, and the arguments presented by both sides, finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof. The consistent and repeated findings of non-compliance, as evidenced by multiple inspection reports and accredited laboratory analyses, strongly corroborate the allegations. The Respondent's general assertions regarding its environmental investments, while noted, do not specifically rebut the concrete evidence of effluent and emission exceedances on the dates of inspection. The failure to produce independent verification or comprehensive self-monitoring data further weakens the Respondent's defense. The fact that Environmental Protection Orders were issued and allegedly not complied with underscores a persistent pattern of non-adherence to environmental standards. The Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules framed thereunder, particularly Sections 11, 12, and 13, unequivocally mandate adherence to NEQS for industrial discharges and emissions. Section 14 further obliges industries to submit self-monitoring reports. The Respondent, as a prominent industrial entity, has a statutory and moral obligation to operate in an environmentally responsible manner. The repeated violations pose a significant threat to public health and the environment.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Finding of Guilt:** M/s Packages Limited (the Respondent) is found guilty of violating Sections 11, 12, 13, and 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent and emissions, as well as failing to comply with self-monitoring and reporting requirements.
2.  **Immediate Compliance:** The Respondent shall immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants that exceed the prescribed NEQS.
3.  **Upgradation of Facilities:** The Respondent shall, within a period of **six (06) months** from the date of this judgment, undertake all necessary measures to upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to ensure full and consistent compliance with all applicable NEQS.
4.  **Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including a timeline for the upgrades and measures to achieve NEQS, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** of the date of this judgment. The EPA shall review and approve this plan.
5.  **Self-Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall forthwith implement a robust self-monitoring program, ensuring accurate and regular sampling and analysis of its effluent and emissions, and shall submit comprehensive self-monitoring reports to the EPA on a monthly basis, as required by the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2001.
6.  **Environmental Audit:** The Respondent shall arrange for an independent environmental audit of its facilities within **three (03) months** from the date of this judgment, the report of which shall be submitted to the EPA and this Tribunal.
7.  **Financial Penalty:** For the violations established, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency within **thirty (30) days** of the date of this judgment.
8.  **Continuing Contravention Fine:** In the event of continued non-compliance with NEQS or any other direction contained herein after the expiry of the six-month period mentioned in clause 3, the Respondent shall be liable to a further penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for each day the contravention continues, until full compliance is achieved.
9.  **Monitoring by EPA:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is directed to rigorously monitor the Respondent's compliance with this order and to submit quarterly progress reports to this Tribunal.
10. **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
The complaint is accordingly allowed.
Given under my hand and seal of the Tribunal

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
