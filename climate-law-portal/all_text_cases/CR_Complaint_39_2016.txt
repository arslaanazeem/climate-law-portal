This judgment addresses Complaint No. 39/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") concerning alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint was initiated on 15th March 2016, following a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted by the EPA, revealing the Respondent's alleged non-compliance with environmental regulations pertaining to industrial effluent discharge. The Tribunal, having heard arguments from both parties and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented, proceeds to render its decision.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, a prominent textile manufacturing unit operating in Lahore, was discharging industrial effluent directly into a public drain, which subsequently flows into the River Ravi, without adequate treatment. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if operational, was either inefficient or improperly managed, leading to the discharge of effluent exceeding the prescribed NEQS parameters. The EPA contended that multiple inspections and subsequent laboratory analyses confirmed significant exceedances of critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. The Complainant further stated that despite several warning notices and opportunities provided to the Respondent to rectify the situation, including notices issued on 10th January 2016 and 15th February 2016, no satisfactory remedial measures were implemented, compelling the EPA to file the present complaint under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, seeking punitive action and directives for compliance.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included:
1.  **Inspection Report dated 05th January 2016:** Documenting the initial site visit by EPA officials, observations of direct discharge, and the collection of effluent samples from the Respondent's discharge point.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/2016/0123 dated 20th January 2016:** Issued by the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming that the collected samples grossly exceeded NEQS for industrial effluents, with BOD levels reported at 280 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD at 650 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), TSS at 320 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), and pH at 10.5 (NEQS: 6-9).
3.  **Follow-up Inspection Report dated 01st March 2016:** Indicating continued non-compliance and documenting the absence of visible improvements in the quality of discharged effluent.
4.  **Notices and Show Cause Letters:** Copies of official communications issued to the Respondent, including a show-cause notice dated 10th January 2016 and a final warning dated 15th February 2016, to which the Respondent's replies were deemed unsatisfactory.
5.  **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual documentation of the discharge point and the state of the effluent.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was argued that the Respondent
was fully compliant with all environmental regulations and possessed a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) that was consistently operational. They argued that the EPA's sampling methodology was flawed, potentially leading to unrepresentative samples, and questioned the accuracy of the laboratory analyses, suggesting possible contamination during sample collection or transit. The Respondent further contended that their internal monitoring records, although not from an
accredited third-party laboratory, consistently showed their effluent parameters to be within the NEQS. They submitted these internal reports as evidence of their ongoing commitment to environmental compliance. Furthermore, the Respondent alleged that the EPA had failed to adhere to proper chain-of-custody procedures for sample collection and transportation, thereby compromising the integrity and admissibility of the laboratory results presented by the Complainant. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
