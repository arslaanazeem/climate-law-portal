This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), seeks redress against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, a prominent industrial enterprise engaged in glass manufacturing, situated in the industrial zone of Lahore. The gravamen of the complaint is the alleged persistent non-compliance by the Respondent with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and
other environmental regulations, leading to significant air and water pollution from its industrial processes and discharge of untreated wastewater. The Complainant asserts that despite numerous notices, warnings, and opportunities for rectification issued by the EPA over the past two years, the Respondent has failed to implement adequate pollution control measures, thereby posing a continuous threat to public health and the surrounding environment. Evidence presented includes reports from continuous emissions monitoring systems (CEMS) and laboratory analyses of wastewater samples, which consistently show exceedances of permissible limits for various parameters, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, and chemical oxygen demand (COD).
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, while acknowledging certain operational challenges, contended that it has made substantial investments in environmental compliance, including the installation of electrostatic precipitators and a wastewater treatment plant. They argued that achieving absolute compliance amidst peak production demands is a complex and ongoing process, and that they are committed to further improvements. The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, heard expert testimonies from both sides, and considered the arguments advanced by the learned counsels. A site inspection was also conducted by a technical committee appointed by the Tribunal, whose report corroborated the EPA's findings regarding intermittent non-compliance, particularly during periods of high production, and and highlighted deficiencies in the maintenance and operational efficiency of existing pollution control equipment.
Having thoroughly examined the record, the Tribunal finds that the the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof, establishing a clear pattern of non-compliance with the NEQS as stipulated under Schedule I and III of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000. While appreciating the Respondent's efforts and investments in environmental protection, the Tribunal cannot overlook the persistent exceedances of environmental standards, which demonstrably impact the local ecosystem and human health. The primary objective of PEPA, 1997, is to protect, conserve, rehabilitate, and improve the environment, and to prevent and control pollution. The Respondent, as a significant industrial entity, bears a statutory and moral obligation to operate within the prescribed environmental parameters. Therefore, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Ghani Glass Limited is liable for violating environmental regulations.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
