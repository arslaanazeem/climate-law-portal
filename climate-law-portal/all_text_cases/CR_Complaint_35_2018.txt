This complaint, bearing No. 35/2018, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in manufacturing packaging materials and allied products, located in Lahore. The complaint was instituted under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), read with the relevant rules and regulations, alleging persistent violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other environmental laws, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing potential health risks to the surrounding community. The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction to adjudicate upon the matter, aiming to ensure compliance with environmental safeguards and impose appropriate penalties for proven contraventions.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent had repeatedly failed to comply with the prescribed environmental standards despite numerous warnings and notices. Specifically, the allegations centered on three primary areas:
Firstly, the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies. It was alleged that the Respondent's wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) was either non-functional, inefficient, or improperly maintained, leading to the discharge of effluent exceeding the NEQS parameters for Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels.
Secondly, the emission of air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), from the Respondent's boiler stacks and other process vents, exceeding the NEQS for industrial air emissions. Regular monitoring by the Complainant indicated consistent breaches of these standards.
Thirdly, the improper management and disposal of hazardous waste generated during the manufacturing processes. The Complainant alleged that the Respondent lacked a proper hazardous waste storage facility and
was disposing of such waste in an environmentally unsound manner, causing potential contamination of soil and groundwater.
In response to the complaint, the Respondent, while acknowledging receipt of various notices from the EPA, contended that it had taken measures to address the environmental concerns. They submitted that significant investments had been made in upgrading their existing wastewater treatment facilities and installing emission control equipment. They further asserted that they were in the process
of implementing a comprehensive environmental management system (EMS) and had engaged environmental consultants to conduct regular audits and suggest further improvements. They argued that any exceedances of NEQS were either temporary aberrations due to operational challenges or were within acceptable margins of error, and that they were committed to achieving full compliance. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
