**ORDER (DISMISSAL)**

This complaint has been filed under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) against Orient Power Company (Pvt) Limited alleging violations of National Environmental Quality Standards.

**PREMATURE FILING — EPO COMPLIANCE PERIOD NOT EXPIRED**

An examination of the record reveals that the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) in this matter was issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and the compliance period stipulated therein had not expired at the time this complaint was filed before the Tribunal. The law declared in 2023 CLD 383 (Punjab ET) establishes that a complaint filed before the expiry of the EPO compliance period is premature and not maintainable. This Tribunal has no jurisdiction to entertain a complaint that does not disclose a completed cause of action. The present complaint is accordingly liable to be dismissed at the threshold.

**ORDER**

For the foregoing reasons, this complaint is hereby **DISMISSED**. The dismissal is, however, without prejudice to the right of the Punjab EPA to re-inspect the Respondent's facility and, if violations are established upon fresh inspection with proper documentation, to re-file the complaint before this Tribunal with original documents and after expiry of the EPO compliance period.

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct a fresh inspection of the Respondent's premises within thirty (30) days and to take such further action as the law permits.

No order as to costs.
