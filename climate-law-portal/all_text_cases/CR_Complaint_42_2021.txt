**Judgment**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 42/2021, filed by the
Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (the Respondent). The complaint alleges that the Respondent, operating its glass manufacturing facility at [Plausible Location, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore], has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drains, thereby contaminating the environment and violating the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA), and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
Upon receiving the complaint, notices were duly issued to the Respondent. The Respondent appeared through its authorized representative and counsel, submitting a written reply denying the allegations and asserting compliance with environmental regulations. Several hearings were conducted, during which both parties presented their arguments and submitted documentary evidence. The EPA presented inspection reports and laboratory analysis results of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's premises on various dates, which consistently indicated exceedances of multiple NEQS parameters, particularly for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and certain heavy metals. The Respondent, in its defense, contended that it possesses an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and that any reported exceedances were either temporary aberrations or attributable to faulty sampling procedures.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence, including the detailed technical reports submitted by the EPA's accredited laboratories and the Respondent's self-monitoring data. It was observed that while the Respondent does have an ETP, its operational efficiency and consistent performance appear to be inadequate to meet the prescribed NEQS. The consistent pattern of non-compliance, as evidenced by the EPA's multiple sampling reports over a period, outweighs the Respondent's general assertions of compliance. The primary objective of PEPA, 1997, is to provide for the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, and for the prevention and control of pollution. Section 16 of PEPA explicitly prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent, waste, air pollutant, or noise in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the NEQS. The Respondent's actions fall squarely within the ambit of this prohibition.
Based on the overwhelming and consistent evidence presented by the Complainant, the Tribunal finds that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards for its industrial effluent discharge, thereby violating Section 16 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
