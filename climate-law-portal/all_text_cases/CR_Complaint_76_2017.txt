This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated at Kala Shah Kaku Industrial Estate, Sheikhupura. The complaint, registered as No. 76/2017, seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws, specifically the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), and its allied rules and regulations, regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). This Tribunal exercises jurisdiction over environmental matters as conferred by Section 17 of the PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant's allegations are primarily rooted in several inspections conducted at the Respondent's industrial facility. It is alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent manufacturer of various chemicals, has consistently failed to comply with the NEQS for industrial effluent discharge and, on several occasions, has been found to be emitting air pollutants beyond permissible limits. Specifically, the EPA asserted that during inspections carried out on 12.04.2017, 28.06.2017, and 15.09.2017, samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points revealed concentrations of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeding the NEQS. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent had not effectively operated its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or Air Pollution Control Devices, leading to environmental degradation. It was also contended that despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures, thereby demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations and posing a continuous threat to public health and the surrounding ecosystem. The Complainant sought the imposition of penalties as prescribed under the PEPA, 1997, and directions for immediate compliance with environmental standards.
In support of its claims, the Complainant presented a robust body of evidence. This included detailed inspection
reports, duly signed by EPA officials, which documented the conditions observed during the visits. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from accredited environmental laboratories, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH levels in the effluent samples collected on the specified dates. These reports were supported by chain of custody forms, ensuring the integrity of the samples. Furthermore, the Com
plainant also presented copies of the show-cause notices and final warnings issued to the Respondent, demonstrating that the Respondent was duly informed of its non-compliance and provided opportunities to rectify the situation, yet failed to do so effectively.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently contested the allegations. While acknowledging that minor operational fluctuations might occasionally lead to temporary deviations from NEQS, the Respondent maintained that it has consistently invested substantial resources in establishing, maintaining, and upgrading its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs). It was argued that the sampling procedures adopted by the EPA were, at times, inconsistent or not truly representative of the Respondent's overall environmental performance. The Respondent also contended that some reported exceedances were marginal and could be attributed to factors beyond its immediate control, such as variations in raw material quality or power supply interruptions affecting the optimal functioning of the ETP. The counsel for the Respondent emphasized the company's significant contribution to the national economy and employment generation and assured the Tribunal of its unwavering commitment to environmental protection and continuous improvement in its compliance efforts. They requested the Tribunal to consider their ongoing efforts and to impose a lenient penalty, if any.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
