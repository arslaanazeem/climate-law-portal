**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 11/2024 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint pertains to alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent in its industrial operations.
The Complainant, representing the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, lodged the present complaint before this Tribunal on 15th February 2024, seeking appropriate action against the Respondent for continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment, thereby contravening the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and provisions of the PEPA, 1997. The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit operating in Lahore, was summoned to appear and respond to the allegations.
It is alleged by the Complainant that the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing various pollutants, including high levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values outside the permissible range, directly into the nearby water bodies and public drains without adequate treatment. The EPA's repeated inspections,
conducted on 20th November 2023 and 10th January 2024, revealed consistent non-compliance with the NEQS. Samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points and analyzed by accredited laboratories confirmed the presence of pollutants, including high levels of BOD, COD, TSS, and pH values outside the permissible range, significantly exceeding the prescribed limits. Consequently, a series of show-cause notices were issued to the Respondent on 5th December 2023 and 15th January 2024, directing them to cease the discharge of untreated effluent and to install/upgrade their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to meet the NEQS.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and filed a written reply on 1st March 2024. In their reply, the Respondent contended that they have an operational ETP and are making continuous efforts to comply with environmental standards. They further submitted that the alleged violations were either sporadic, due to unforeseen technical glitches in the ETP, or that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was flawed. They requested further time to upgrade their ETP and presented a proposed compliance schedule outlining their commitment to achieving full compliance within a reasonable timeframe.
During the proceedings, the Complainant presented detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis certificates, and photographic evidence substantiating the allegations of non-compliance. Expert testimony from an environmental engineer affirmed the inadequacy of the Respondent's existing ETP to treat the industrial effluent to the NEQS. The Complainant also highlighted that despite repeated warnings, notices, and opportunities for rectification, the Respondent had failed to demonstrate substantial and sustained improvement in their effluent quality over a prolonged period, indicating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations.
The Tribunal carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary and expert evidence placed on record. It is an undisputed fact that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited operates an industrial facility generating significant quantities of industrial effluent. The laboratory reports, which were not effectively rebutted by the Respondent's generalized assertions, consistently show that the discharged effluent contains parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, pH) significantly exceeding the NEQS. The Respondent's assertion of having an operational ETP is not sufficient if the ETP consistently fails to achieve the desired treatment levels as per the NEQS. While the Respondent expressed a willingness to upgrade their ETP, their past conduct indicates a persistent failure to comply
with environmental standards, causing discernible harm to the environment and potentially to public health. The burden is on the industrial unit to ensure that its operations do not degrade the environment, and mere intentions or belated promises of future compliance, while welcome, do not absolve it of its present and past transgressions. The consistent findings from multiple inspections and laboratory analyses, spanning several months, leave no doubt that the effluent discharged by the Respondent's facility has been in continuous violation of the NEQS. The Respondent's arguments regarding sporadic glitches or flawed sampling methodology were not supported by concrete evidence that could undermine the credibility of the EPA's findings, which included certified laboratory reports. This Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to exercise due diligence in managing its industrial waste and has, through its omissions, contravened the provisions of Section 12, 13, and 19 of the PEPA, 1997, read with the relevant NEQS.
In light of the overwhelming evidence presented by the Complainant and the Respondent's failure to adequately rebut the specific allegations of non-compliance, this Tribunal is satisfied that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is responsible for the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent in violation of the prescribed environmental standards.
**ORDER**
In view of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:
1.  **Guilty Verdict:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, the Respondent, is found guilty of contravening the provisions of Section 12, 13, and 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), for the persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent.
2.  **Financial Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Compliance Directive - Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** The Respondent shall, with immediate effect, cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment. Furthermore, the Respondent is directed to immediately undertake and complete the necessary upgrades or installation of a fully functional and compliant Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating all industrial effluent to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) in all parameters, within a strict period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment.
4.  **Compliance Plan Submission:** Within fifteen (15) days from the date of this judgment, the Respondent shall submit a detailed, time-bound compliance plan, including technical specifications of the proposed ETP upgrades/installation, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for review

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **14 May, 2023**.
