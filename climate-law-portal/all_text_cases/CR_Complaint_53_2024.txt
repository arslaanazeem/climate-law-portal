**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 53/2024**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging contraventions of environmental laws and regulations. The complaint, lodged on February 15, 2024, asserts that the Respondent, a chemical manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and subsequently into the Ravi River, causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life. The Tribunal’s jurisdiction is invoked under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997").
The Complainant's allegations are primarily rooted in a series of inspections conducted by the Environmental Protection Department (EPD) officials at the Respondent's premises. It is alleged that during inspections carried out on December 10, 2023, and January 20, 2024, it was observed that the Respondent’s Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was either non-functional or operating below its designed capacity, leading to the direct discharge of industrial wastewater without proper treatment. Specifically, the Complainant alleges that samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on both occasions, when subjected to laboratory analysis at the EPA-approved laboratory, consistently showed parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (e.g., lead, chromium) to be significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Effluents, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000, and S.R.O. 549(I)/2000 dated June 5, 2000. Furthermore, the complaint highlights the Respondent's failure to provide satisfactory evidence of compliance despite multiple warning notices and opportunities afforded by the EPA to rectify the deficiencies, thereby constituting a persistent violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge of effluent or waste into the environment in excess of the NEQS. The Complainant also detailed a history of non-compliance, including previous show-cause notices issued to the Respondent regarding similar violations.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence. This included certified copies of the inspection reports dated December 10, 2023, and January 20, 2024, prepared by the inspection team, detailing the observations of the ETP's operational status and the sampling procedures. Crucially, the Complainant submitted two detailed laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2023/12-005 and EPA/LAB/2024/01-012, which unequivocally confirmed the exceedance of NEQS for various parameters in the collected effluent samples. Photographic evidence showing the untreated effluent discharge points and the visible pollution downstream was also tendered. Additionally, an affidavit from Mr. Muhammad Asif, Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, was presented, affirming the veracity of the complaint and the procedural steps taken. The Respondent, in its defense, primarily argued that it had initiated an upgrade project for its ETP, citing technical challenges and delays in procurement of specialized equipment as reasons for the temporary non-compliance. The Respondent submitted a project plan for the ETP upgrade and invoices for some equipment, along with an undertaking to complete the upgrade within six months. However, the Respondent did not dispute the laboratory results presented by the Complainant, nor did it provide independent laboratory analysis to counter the Complainant's findings regarding the specific dates of alleged violations.
The relevant legal provisions governing this matter are primarily found in the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, explicitly prohibits any person from discharging or emitting any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the NEQS. Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, empowers the Agency to issue Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) to prevent or control pollution. Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, provides for penalties for contravention
of the provisions of the Act, including imprisonment and/or fines, with further daily penalties for continuing contravention. Specifically, Section 17(1) states that whoever contravenes or fails to comply with any provision of this Act or any order or rule made thereunder shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years or with fine which may extend to one million rupees, or with both. Furthermore, Section 17(2) provides that where the contravention is a continuing one, a further fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues shall also be imposed. The Tribunal also considers the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Environmental Tribunals) Rules, 2012, which outline the procedures for the functioning of these Tribunals.
Upon careful consideration of the pleadings, evidence presented, and arguments advanced by both parties, this Tribunal finds the Complainant's case to be well-substantiated.
The Complainant's case to be well-substantiated. The undisputed laboratory analysis reports unequivocally demonstrate that the effluent discharged by the Respondent on multiple occasions significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various critical parameters, including COD, BOD5, TSS, pH, and heavy metals. This consistent exceedance, coupled with the observations from the inspection reports regarding the ETP's non-functional or underperforming status, leaves no doubt that the Respondent has been in persistent contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's submission of a project plan for an ETP upgrade and invoices for some equipment, and their undertaking to complete the upgrade within six months, this does not absolve them of liability for the proven past and ongoing violations. The responsibility to comply with environmental standards is continuous, and a mere intention or ongoing project, without immediate effective measures to mitigate pollution, cannot be accepted as a valid defense against direct evidence of non-compliance. The Respondent's failure to dispute the laboratory findings further strengthens the Complainant's assertions. The environmental degradation caused by the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and subsequently into the Ravi River is a serious matter, impacting public health and aquatic ecosystems, and cannot be overlooked.
Therefore, this Tribunal, having found the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, guilty of contravening Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, hereby issues the following final order and penalty:
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
1.  **Cessation of Discharge:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is hereby ordered to immediately cease the discharge of any effluent or waste that does not meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its associated regulations.
2.  **ETP Functionality:** The Respondent shall ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully functional and operates at its designed capacity to effectively treat all industrial effluent to meet the NEQS before discharge.
3.  **Compliance Plan Submission:** The Respondent is directed to submit a detailed and time-bound compliance action plan, approved by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment, outlining specific measures, timelines, and responsible personnel for achieving and maintaining full compliance with NEQS. This plan must include provisions for regular monitoring and reporting to the EPA.
4.  **Penalty:**
    a.  **Initial Fine:** A fine of **PKR 800,000/- (Rupees Eight Hundred Thousand only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for the contraventions established. This fine shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment.
    b.  **Continuing Contravention Fine:** Furthermore, for the continuing contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, a further fine of **PKR 75,000/- (Rupees Seventy-Five Thousand only)** per day shall be imposed on the Respondent from the date of this judgment until such time as the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, certifies that the Respondent has achieved full compliance with the NEQS.
5.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports, including certified laboratory analysis results of their treated effluent, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of one year from the date of achieving full compliance, to ensure sustained adherence to NEQS.
6.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)**, payable to the Complainant.
Failure to comply with any part of this order shall constitute a further contravention of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and may result in additional legal action, including enhanced penalties and coercive measures, as per the law.
Pronounced in open Tribunal this **20th day of May, 2024**.
