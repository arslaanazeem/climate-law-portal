**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 53/2022 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and directions for compliance due to alleged violations of environmental laws and standards concerning industrial discharges and emissions. The Tribunal, having heard arguments from both parties, examined the evidence presented, and deliberated on the relevant legal framework, hereby renders its decision.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, initiated these proceedings against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit operating in Lahore. The core of the complaint revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and air emissions, as well as operating without the requisite No Objection Certificates (NOCs) or Environmental Approvals from the EPA. The Complainant asserts that the Respondent's operations are causing environmental pollution, posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem, thereby contravening the fundamental principles enshrined in PEPA, 1997.
The EPA's allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited include, but are not limited to, the following:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated/Partially Treated Industrial Effluent:** The Respondent is accused of discharging industrial wastewater into public drains/sewerage systems without adequate treatment, resulting in effluent parameters exceeding the NEQS, specifically concerning Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical
Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and other heavy metals.
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding NEQS:** The Respondent's industrial processes, particularly from its boilers and generators, are alleged to be releasing air pollutants such as Particulate Matter (PM), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) that surpass the prescribed NE
QS.
3.  **Operating Without Valid Environmental Approvals/NOCs:** The Respondent is alleged to be operating certain industrial processes or the entire facility without obtaining the mandatory Environmental Approvals or No Objection Certificates (NOCs) from the EPA, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and its associated regulations.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
