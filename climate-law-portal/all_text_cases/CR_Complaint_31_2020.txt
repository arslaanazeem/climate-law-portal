**J U D G M E N T**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 31/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint seeks redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and its associated rules and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants.
The Complainant's allegations, as detailed in the complaint dated October 15, 2020, primarily revolve around the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent and gaseous emissions. It is contended that the Respondent has, over a sustained period, been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually pollutes surface water bodies. Furthermore, the complaint asserts that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly emissions from its boiler and other manufacturing processes, consistently exceed the prescribed NEQS for air pollutants. Specific instances cited include an inspection conducted on August 20, 2020, where samples of effluent and air emissions were collected. The subsequent laboratory analysis, according to the Complainant, revealed significant deviations from the NEQS, including elevated levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and various heavy metals in the effluent, and concentrations of SOx, NOx, and Particulate Matter (PM) above permissible limits in the air emissions. The Complainant further alleges that despite several warning letters and show-cause notices issued by the EPA, including one dated September 10, 2020, the Respondent failed to take adequate remedial measures or provide a satisfactory compliance plan, thereby demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental laws and regulations. The EPA seeks imposition of penalties as per PEPA, 1997, and a directive for immediate compliance.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant submitted a comprehensive set of documents including: the inspection report dated August 20, 2020, detailing the observations made by the EPA inspection team; laboratory analysis reports from an accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for both effluent and air emissions; copies of the show-cause notice dated September 10, 2020, and subsequent reminders; and photographic evidence depicting the discharge point and emission sources. The Assistant Director, EPA, also appeared as a witness, reiterating the contents of the complaint and the validity of the evidence presented. It was emphasized that the EPA had provided ample opportunity for the Respondent to rectify the situation before resorting to legal action.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its counsel and Environmental Manager, contested the allegations. While acknowledging its industrial operations and the generation of effluent and emissions, the Respondent argued that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship. It was submitted that the company has invested substantially in environmental control technologies, including an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. The Respondent challenged the veracity of the EPA's sampling methodology and laboratory analysis reports, suggesting potential flaws in the collection or preservation of samples. They presented their own internal monitoring reports, which, according to them, showed compliance or only minor, temporary deviations from NEQS. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed that it had initiated an upgrade project for its ETP and installed new bag filters for its boiler exhaust, with an estimated completion timeline of six months from the date of the complaint, demonstrating a proactive approach to environmental compliance. They presented a letter of intent with a reputable vendor for the upgrade and an internal memo outlining the project plan. The Respondent's Environmental Manager testified that any past non-compliance was an isolated incident or due to transient operational upsets, which were promptly addressed. They requested the Tribunal to consider their ongoing efforts and not impose punitive measures that could hinder their
industrial operations or their commitment to environmental improvements. They requested the Tribunal to consider their ongoing efforts and not impose punitive measures that could hinder their ability to complete the planned upgrades.
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses, show-cause notices, and the Respondent's internal documents and testimony. It notes that the Complainant's evidence, comprising official inspection reports and laboratory analyses conducted by an accredited laboratory, provides a robust prima facie case for the alleged violations. The laboratory reports clearly indicate exceedances of NEQS for various parameters in both effluent and air emissions, substantiating the EPA's claims.
While the Respondent has challenged the sampling methodology, no concrete evidence or expert testimony was adduced to convincingly demonstrate any procedural flaws that would invalidate the EPA's official samples and their subsequent analysis. General assertions of potential flaws are insufficient to rebut the presumption of regularity attached to official acts performed by a statutory body like the EPA. Furthermore, the Respondent's internal monitoring reports, while showing some data, cannot supersede independent third-party analyses, especially when contradictory. The very fact that the Respondent is undertaking significant upgrades to its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and installing new air pollution control devices, as acknowledged by them, implicitly suggests an awareness of the inadequacy of existing systems to meet the NEQS, even if they characterize past non-compliance as "isolated" or "transient."
The Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Section 11, strictly prohibits the discharge of effluent or emission of air pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards. Section 20 of the Act prescribes penalties for contravention of Section 11, including a fine not exceeding one million rupees and, in the case of a continuing contravention, an additional fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues. This Tribunal is vested with the authority under the Act to ensure compliance with environmental laws and to impose penalties where violations are established.
Based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Packages Limited has been in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluent and air emissions, as stipulated under Section 1
1 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the accredited laboratory reports, overwhelmingly demonstrates that the Respondent's industrial operations have consistently resulted in the discharge of effluent and emission of air pollutants exceeding the permissible limits set by the National Environmental Quality Standards.
While the Tribunal takes note of the Respondent's stated commitment to environmental stewardship and their ongoing efforts to upgrade their Effluent Treatment Plant and install new air pollution control devices, these actions, though commendable for future compliance, do not absolve them of liability for past and present violations. The duty to comply with NEQS is continuous and immediate. The Respondent's proactive measures, however, indicate a willingness to rectify the situation, which the Tribunal has considered in determining the appropriate penalty and remedial directives.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Packages Limited is found to be in violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Penalty:** A penalty of **Rupees Seven Hundred and Fifty Thousand (PKR 750,000/-)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the established contraventions. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Immediate Compliance Directive:** The Respondent shall ensure immediate and continuous compliance with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluent discharge and air emissions.
4.  **Completion of Upgrades:** The Respondent shall complete the stated upgrades to its Effluent Treatment Plant and the installation of new air pollution control devices within the six-month timeline as represented to the Tribunal, commencing from the date of this judgment.
5.  **Submission of Compliance Plan and Monitoring Reports:**
    *   Within thirty (30) days of this judgment, the Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan to the EPA, outlining the steps, timelines, and resources dedicated to achieving and maintaining NEQS.
    *   The Respondent shall submit monthly progress reports to the EPA regarding the upgrade project and quarterly self-monitoring reports, certified by an accredited laboratory, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS for both effluent and air emissions, for a period of two years following the completion of the upgrades.
    *   The EPA shall conduct unannounced inspections and independent monitoring to verify compliance.
6.  **Future Non-Compliance:** Any future non-compliance with the NEQS or failure to adhere to the directives of this order shall result in further stringent penalties, including the daily additional fine as stipulated in Section 20 of the PEPA, 1997, and other legal consequences.
7.  **Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.
