**JUDGMENT**
**Introduction**
1.  This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 20/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in industrial operations located at Kalashah Kaku Industrial Estate, Sheikhupura. The complaint, lodged under Section 17 read with Sections 11 and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial measures for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the prescribed Pakistan Environmental Quality Standards (PEQS).
**Allegations**
2.  The Complainant alleges that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, thereby violating the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the Pakistan Environmental Quality Standards (PEQS) for Industrial and Municipal Effluents as notified vide S.R.O. 549(I)/2000 dated 05.06.2000, as amended from time to time.
3.  Specifically, the allegations stem from a routine inspection conducted by an EPA team on **October 15, 2020**, at the Respondent's premises. During this inspection, samples of the industrial effluent being discharged from the Respondent's facility were collected from the final discharge point. These samples were subsequently analyzed at the EPA's accredited laboratory.
4.  The laboratory analysis reports, annexed with the complaint, indicated that several key parameters of the discharged effluent were significantly above the permissible PEQS limits. These parameters included, but were not limited to:
    *   Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD)
    *   Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5)
    *   Total Suspended Solids (TSS)
    *   pH value (found to be outside the acceptable range)
5.  The Complainant contended that such non-compliance constitutes a direct violation of Section 11 of the Act, which prohibits the discharge
of any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount or concentration that exceeds the Pakistan Environmental Quality Standards (PEQS). Furthermore, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent's actions fall under the purview of Section 16 of the Act, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any pollutant in excess of the PEQS, and therefore warrants the imposition of penalties as prescribed under Section 1
7 of the Act.

The Respondent appeared through its authorized representative and counsel and filed a written reply denying the allegations in general terms but failed to provide substantive evidence or laboratory reports to refute the specific findings of the EPA's analysis reports. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
