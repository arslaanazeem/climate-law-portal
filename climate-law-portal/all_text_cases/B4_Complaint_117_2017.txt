**CONSENT ORDER**

This complaint was filed under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) alleging violations of National Environmental Quality Standards by the Respondent.

Upon hearing learned counsel for the parties and after examining the Consent Compliance Plan (CCP) jointly submitted by the Complainant and the Respondent through its counsel Mr. Rana Asif Saeed, this Tribunal is satisfied that the parties have reached a mutually acceptable arrangement for remediation of the environmental violations.

**TERMS OF CONSENT ORDER**

**1. Acknowledgment**

The Respondent, Sabroso Foods (Pvt) Limited, acknowledges that its facility has been operating in partial non-compliance with the applicable National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and undertakes to achieve full compliance within the period stipulated herein.

**2. Reduced Penalty**

In view of the Respondent's cooperation and voluntary undertaking, this Tribunal imposes a reduced penalty of **PKR 350,000/- (Rupees Three Hundred Fifty Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, representing a 50% reduction from the maximum applicable penalty, payable within twenty-one (21) days.

**3. PSDF Contribution**

The Respondent shall deposit **PKR 70,000/-** into the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Timeline**

The Respondent shall achieve full NEQS compliance within **90 days** of this order. A detailed compliance schedule is attached as Annex-I to this order.

**5. Third-Party Audit**

The Respondent shall engage a NEQS-certified third-party laboratory to conduct a compliance audit at the end of the compliance period and submit the audit report to both the Punjab EPA and this Tribunal.

**6. Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA shall conduct quarterly inspections of the Respondent's facility for a period of two (2) years and report findings to this Tribunal.

**7. Breach of Consent Order**

In the event of breach of any term of this Consent Order, the Complainant is at liberty to apply for revival of the original complaint proceedings, and the Respondent shall be liable for the full penalty of **PKR 700,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred Thousand only)** plus daily fines under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**8. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
