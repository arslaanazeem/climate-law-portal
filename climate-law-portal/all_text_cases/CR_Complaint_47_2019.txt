This judgment addresses Complaint No. 47/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of the Act. The Tribunal, being the statutory forum for adjudicating environmental disputes and ensuring compliance with environmental standards, is seized of the matter to determine the extent of the Respondent's environmental responsibility and to issue appropriate directions.
The Complainant's case rests on the assertion that the Respondent, in the course of its industrial operations, has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants into the environment, causing significant environmental degradation and posing potential health risks to the surrounding community. Specifically, it is alleged that during a routine inspection conducted by EPA officials on 15.08.2019, samples of industrial effluent discharged from the Respondent's premises and ambient air samples from its vicinity were collected. Subsequent laboratory analyses of these samples revealed that several key parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and certain heavy metals in the effluent, significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS for industrial discharges. Similarly, the analysis of air samples indicated particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) levels beyond the permissible NEQS for industrial emissions. The Complainant further alleges that despite issuance of a Show Cause Notice dated 05.09.2019, and subsequent
warnings and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures, necessitating the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently contested the allegations. In its written reply and during arguments, it was contended that the company operates a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, which are regularly maintained and monitored. The Respondent asserted its commitment to environmental stewardship as a responsible corporate entity and argued that the samples collected by the EPA might not be truly representative of its routine operations, or that external factors could have influenced the readings. Internal monitoring reports, maintenance logs, and certifications for its environmental management systems were submitted to demonstrate proactive efforts towards environmental compliance. The Respondent further maintained that any detected exceedance, if accurately measured, was an isolated incident and not indicative of a systemic failure to comply with environmental regulations.
This Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant's laboratory reports, duly attested and prepared by accredited environmental laboratories, provided scientific data indicating significant exceedances of NEQS for both effluent discharge and air emissions on the specific date of inspection, 15.08.2019. While the Respondent presented evidence of an operational ETP and air pollution control systems, it failed to conclusively refute the specific analytical results of the samples collected by the EPA on the said date. The internal monitoring
reports, although indicative of general compliance, did not specifically address or contradict the findings of the EPA for the critical date in question. The Tribunal finds that the burden of proof, in this instance, rests with the Respondent to demonstrate continuous compliance, especially in light of scientifically substantiated violations. The argument that the exceedance was an isolated incident, while plausible, does not absolve the Respondent of responsibility for the violation proven on 15.08.2019, particularly considering the prior show cause notice and warnings issued by the EPA. The Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, emphasizes a strict liability approach for environmental pollution, where the mere fact of discharge or emission exceeding prescribed standards constitutes a violation, irrespective of intent or isolated nature. Environmental protection demands continuous vigilance and adherence to prescribed standards. While the Respondent's efforts in establishing treatment facilities are commendable, their effectiveness must be consistently demonstrated. The Tribunal, therefore, concludes that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Packages Limited was in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards on 15.08.2019, regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants, contrary to Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, read with the relevant NEQS.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 21 and Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Packages Limited (the Respondent) is hereby declared to have violated the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge and industrial air emissions on 15.08.2019, as established by the evidence presented by the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore.
2.  **Imposition of Penalty:** A monetary penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million Only)** is imposed on the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, for the aforesaid violations. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
