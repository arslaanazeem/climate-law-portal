This judgment addresses Complaint No. 3/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), concerning alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the submissions, evidence, and arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant, through its Assistant Director, initiated this complaint alleging that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, was operating in violation of environmental laws. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually found its way into natural water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental pollution. The Complainant further contended that the effluent discharged by the Respondent consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS limits for various parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Additionally, the complaint highlighted the Respondent's failure to obtain a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its industrial operations from the EPA, as mandated by Section 12 of the PEPA, 1997, despite repeated notices and opportunities to comply. The Complainant sought a direction from the Tribunal for the Respondent to cease its polluting activities, install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), obtain the requisite NOC, and pay environmental penalties as provided under the law.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant submitted a series of inspection reports, dated 15.01.2020, 03.03.2020, and 28.05.2020, conducted by EPA officials at the Respondent's premises. These reports detailed observations of effluent discharge points and the collection of samples. Crucially, the Complainant presented laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory, corresponding to the collected samples. These reports consistently indicated that the discharged effluent contained levels of BOD, COD, and TSS significantly above the NEQS limits. For instance, the report dated 20.01.2020 showed BOD at 150 mg/L (NEQS 80 mg/L), COD at 400 mg/L (NEQS 150 mg/L), and TSS at 250 mg/L (NEQS 200 mg/L). Photographs depicting the colour and condition of the discharged effluent were also submitted. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 20.02.2020 and 10.04.2020, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, were placed on record. The Complainant also produced evidence showing that the Respondent's application for an NOC was incomplete and had not been granted final approval due to
continuous non-compliance with the NEQS and the absence of a functional and effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) at its premises, a prerequisite for NOC issuance.
In response, the Respondent, through its counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it had installed an ETP and was making sincere efforts to comply with environmental regulations. It was contended that any exceedances in NEQS parameters
were isolated incidents or resulted from temporary operational issues, which had since been rectified. The Respondent asserted that its ETP, though perhaps requiring periodic maintenance and upgrades, was generally functional and designed to meet the NEQS. It was further submitted that the Respondent had actively engaged with the EPA regarding its NOC application and was committed to fulfilling all regulatory requirements. The Respondent requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, or in the alternative, to grant a reasonable timeframe for full compliance, without imposing harsh penalties, given its purported efforts and economic contributions.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
