This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 10/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "
EPA") against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint specifically details non-compliance with effluent discharge standards and inadequate waste management practices at the Respondent's industrial facility located in Lahore, causing environmental degradation and public nuisance.
Upon receipt of the complaint, the Tribunal issued notices to the Respondent, who appeared through its authorized representative and legal counsel. Multiple hearings were conducted, during which both parties presented their arguments, submitted documentary evidence, including inspection reports by the EPA, laboratory analysis results of effluent samples, and the Respondent's compliance submissions and mitigation plans. The Tribunal also considered expert testimony regarding the nature and extent of the alleged environmental damage.
The core of the EPA's contention was that despite repeated warnings and notices, the Respondent failed to consistently adhere to the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluent and solid waste management, as mandated by Section 11 of the Act. The EPA presented compelling evidence, including analytical reports from accredited laboratories, which consistently showed parameters exceeding the permissible limits. The Respondent, while acknowledging certain operational challenges, argued that it had taken steps towards compliance and invested in treatment facilities, seeking leniency and time for full compliance.
After careful consideration of the pleadings, evidence on record, and arguments advanced by both sides, this Tribunal finds that the Environmental Protection Agency has successfully established that M/s Packages Limited has indeed been in violation of Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and improper solid waste disposal, thereby contravening the National Environmental Quality Standards. The evidence clearly indicates a pattern of non-compliance that has contributed to environmental pollution.
The Tribunal emphasizes that environmental protection is a fundamental right and a collective responsibility. Industrial entities have a statutory obligation to operate in a manner that does not harm the environment or public health. While acknowledging the Respondent's efforts to improve, the persistent breaches of NEQS cannot be overlooked. The gravity of the environmental impact, coupled with the Respondent's failure to achieve consistent compliance within reasonable timelines despite previous directives, necessitates a firm response from this Tribunal to uphold the sanctity of environmental laws and deter future violations.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
