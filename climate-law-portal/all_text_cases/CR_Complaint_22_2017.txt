**COMPLAINT NO. 22/2017**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi**
The genesis of this complaint, bearing No. 22/2017, lies in a formal complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, on October 16, 2017. The complaint was filed against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent chemical manufacturing facility situated in the industrial zone of Sheikhupura, Punjab. The core issue revolves around the alleged persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the surrounding environment, causing significant pollution and posing a threat to public health and ecological integrity, in contravention of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS").
The Complainant's case rests on the following allegations: The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is engaged in the large-scale production of various chemicals, a process that inherently generates substantial quantities of industrial wastewater. Despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, the Respondent has consistently failed to install an effective effluent treatment plant (ETP) or to operate its existing treatment facilities to the required standards. Specifically, the EPA conducted multiple inspections of the Respondent's premises. An inspection on August 15, 2017, revealed the direct discharge of dark-colored, foul-smelling effluent into an open drain leading to agricultural fields and a local stream. Samples collected during this inspection, along with subsequent sampling on September 05, 2017, were analyzed at the EPA's accredited laboratory. The laboratory reports, annexed to the complaint, indicated that critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (e.g., Chromium, Lead) significantly exceeded the permissible limits stipulated by the NEQS. The Complainant further alleged that this continuous non-compliance has resulted in severe soil contamination, degradation of water quality in local irrigation channels, adverse impacts on aquatic life, and potential health hazards for the nearby communities reliant on these water sources. The EPA had issued a show-cause notice on September 20, 2017,
to which the Respondent provided an unsatisfactory reply, failing to demonstrate compliance or a credible plan for remediation. Consequently, the EPA initiated legal proceedings before this Tribunal.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently contested the allegations. Their primary defense centered on the assertion that an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was indeed operational at their facility. They argued that any instances of non-compliance were isolated, attributable to unforeseen technical malfunctions, power outages, or operational challenges, rather than a deliberate disregard for environmental regulations. The Respondent further questioned the scientific validity of the samples collected by the EPA, suggesting potential flaws in the sampling methodology, chain of custody, or laboratory analysis that could have led to inaccurate results. They also presented arguments regarding the significant financial investment already made in their existing ETP and pleaded for more time to implement further upgrades, citing economic constraints.
This Tribunal meticulously reviewed the comprehensive evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant submitted detailed inspection reports, photographic evidence clearly depicting the discharge of untreated effluent, and a series of accredited laboratory analysis reports from August and September 2017, which consistently demonstrated critical environmental parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, pH, heavy metals) far exceeding the NEQS. The testimonies of the EPA's sampling officers and laboratory analysts, who were subjected to rigorous cross-examination, remained consistent and credible, affirming the proper procedures followed in sample collection, preservation, and analysis. The chain of custody was also meticulously documented and presented.
Conversely, the Respondent, despite claiming to have an operational ETP, failed to produce concrete evidence of its effective functioning during the periods of alleged non-compliance. No credible operational logs, maintenance records, or independent third-party audit reports were presented that could substantiate their claims of temporary malfunctions or demonstrate consistent compliance. The general assertions of technical glitches and financial difficulties, while noted, do not absolve a manufacturing facility of its statutory obligation to comply with environmental laws and protect public health and the environment. The continuous nature of the alleged discharge, as evidenced by multiple inspections and samples, strongly contravenes the Respondent's assertion of isolated incidents.
**FINDINGS:**
After careful consideration of all the evidence, arguments, and legal provisions, this Tribunal finds that:
1.  The Complainant, the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been in persistent violation of Section 11(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with Rule 13 of the Environmental Quality Standards (Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industry) Rules, 2000, by consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment.
2.  The effluent discharged by the Respondent significantly exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various critical parameters, posing a clear and present danger to public health, agricultural land
and aquatic ecosystems, thereby contravening the spirit and letter of PEPA, 1997.
3.  The Respondent failed to provide satisfactory evidence of an effectively operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or a credible plan for achieving compliance despite repeated warnings and opportunities given by the Complainant.
4.  The defense presented by the Respondent, citing isolated incidents or financial constraints, is not sufficient to negate the documented, consistent pattern of non-compliance and the grave environmental harm caused.
**ORDER:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders that:
1.  M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, the Respondent, is found GUILTY of violating Section 11(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with Rule 13 of the Environmental Quality Standards (Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industry) Rules, 2000.
2.  The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment.
3.  The Respondent shall forthwith take all necessary measures to ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) operates effectively and consistently, bringing all discharged effluent parameters within the permissible limits of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at all times.
4.  The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance action plan, including timelines for any required upgrades, modifications, or installation of new treatment facilities to its ETP, to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
5.  The EPA shall conduct regular and frequent monitoring, at least twice a month for the next six months, to ensure strict compliance with NEQS and shall submit detailed compliance reports to this Tribunal every two months.
6.  The Respondent shall bear all costs associated with the monitoring activities conducted by the EPA, as directed above, for a period of one year from the date of this order.
7.  Failure to comply with these directions shall result in further legal action, including enhanced penalties and coercive measures as provided under PEPA, 1997, and other applicable laws.
**PENALTY:**
Under the powers vested in this Tribunal by Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and considering the gravity, persistent nature, and documented adverse impacts of the violations, the Tribunal imposes the following penalty:
1.  A fine of PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand only) is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, payable to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
2.  In the event of continued non-compliance with the directions outlined in the 'ORDER' section beyond the stipulated timelines, an additional daily fine of PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only) shall be imposed for each day of continued contravention, until full compliance is achieved, without prejudice to any other legal action that may be taken.
This order shall be enforced immediately.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **17 March, 2023**.
