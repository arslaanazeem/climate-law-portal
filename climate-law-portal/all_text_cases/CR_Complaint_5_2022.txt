This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "Respondent"), an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal (hereinafter referred to as "the Tribunal") exercises jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial measures for the Respondent's alleged non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) concerning industrial effluent discharge.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial unit, located at [Specific Address, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore], was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Lahore Branch Canal, a designated water body, thereby causing significant environmental pollution. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  Repeated discharge of industrial effluent with parameters exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for pH, Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and Oil & Grease.
2.  Failure to install and/or operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite multiple notices and warnings issued by the EPA.
3.  Non-compliance with the conditions stipulated in the Environmental Approval granted under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, which mandated the continuous operation of an ETP to meet NEQS.
4.  Causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to aquatic life, agricultural land, and public health due to the discharge of hazardous pollutants.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
1.  **Site Inspection Reports:** Three detailed site inspection reports dated 12.10.2021, 28.12.2021, and 15.02.2022, prepared by EPA officials, which consistently noted the discharge of colored and odorous effluent from the Respondent’s premises into the canal, and the apparent non-functional or inadequately functioning ETP.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory for effluent samples collected on 12.10.2021, 28.12.2021, and 15.02.2022. These reports unequivocally indicated that the effluent parameters (BOD5, COD, TSS, and pH) significantly exceeded the NEQS limits on all three occasions. For instance, BOD5 was found to be 250 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD 600 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), and TSS 350 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L) in the sample collected on 15.02.2022.
3.  **Correspondence:** Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 20.10.2021 and 05.01.2022, warning letters, and minutes of meetings held between EPA officials and the Respondent's management, highlighting repeated non-compliance and the Respondent's failure to take corrective actions within stipulated timelines.
4.  **Testimony:** The Assistant Director, EPA, and the environmental chemist who conducted the laboratory analyses provided sworn testimonies, affirming the authenticity of the reports and the persistent nature of the violations.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
