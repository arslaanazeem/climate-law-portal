**JUDGMENT**

This complaint was filed on 17 October, 2022 before this Tribunal, which exercises jurisdiction over environmental offences committed within the province of Punjab under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The matter pertains to violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by Surgimed Hospital (Pvt) Limited, a Hospitals (Biomedical Waste) facility located at Faisalabad. This is a repeat violation by the Respondent, having previously been found in breach of environmental standards, which aggravates the present offence. The Environmental Protection Order (EPO) bearing No. EPA/EPO/2022/091 was issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and the requisite compliance period has since expired without compliance being achieved.

**I. COMPLAINANT'S ALLEGATIONS**

The Complainant alleged that EPA inspection of the Respondent's healthcare facility revealed that hospital waste including sharps, infectious materials and pharmaceutical waste are not being segregated at source, are not stored in designated containers, and are not being treated through authorized disposal methods as mandated by the Punjab Hospital Waste Management Rules, 2014.

Specifically, the Complainant's allegations are as follows:

(a) The Respondent's facility has been found discharging/emitting pollutants in excess of NEQS limits in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997;

(b) The Respondent has failed to comply with Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2022/091 issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 within the stipulated compliance period;

(c) The Respondent has not taken adequate remedial and preventive measures despite multiple EPA inspection reports documenting the violations.

**II. EVIDENCE ON RECORD**

**1. EPA Inspection Reports**
   Inspection reports dated 23/10/2022 and 15/03/2022 prepared by authorized Environmental Officers of the Punjab EPA confirming NEQS violations at the Respondent's facility.

**2. Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2022/091**
   EPO issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 directing the Respondent to cease violations and achieve NEQS compliance within the specified period.

**3. Hospital Waste Inspection Report**
   Inspection report confirming non-segregation of biomedical waste, absence of designated storage facilities, and improper disposal methods in violation of Rules 5, 7, 8 and 11 of the Punjab Hospital Waste Management Rules, 2014.

**4. Photographic Evidence**
   Photographs and video footage recorded during EPA inspection confirming the observed violations.

**5. Non-Compliance Report**
   Report confirming the Respondent's failure to achieve compliance within the period stipulated in EPO No. EPA/EPO/2022/091.

**EVIDENCE OF VIOLATION**

  As detailed in the inspection reports and supporting documentation listed above.

**III. RESPONDENT'S DEFENSE**

Learned counsel for the Respondent, Mr. Rana Asif Saeed, argued that the pollution levels measured by the EPA were the result of a temporary operational disruption and that the Respondent had been in routine compliance with NEQS standards as evidenced by its own third-party laboratory reports. The sampling methodology was also challenged as not conforming to prescribed protocols.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, Surgimed Hospital (Pvt) Limited, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, Surgimed Hospital (Pvt) Limited, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,900,000/- (Rupees Two Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 580,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order, including ensuring full operationalization of the Effluent Treatment Plant / pollution control system, and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months commencing from the date of this order, incorporating certified laboratory test results from a NEQS-certified laboratory.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply with the directions in paragraphs 4 and 5 above within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for a period of one (1) year and to report findings to this Tribunal. The EPA is authorized to seal the facility in the event of continued non-compliance.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear the costs of this proceedings including the costs of laboratory analysis and EPA inspection, assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
