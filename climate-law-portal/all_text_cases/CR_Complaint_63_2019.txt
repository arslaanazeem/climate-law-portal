**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 63/2019 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Nishat Mills"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, particularly concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and air emissions, thereby causing significant pollution and environmental degradation.
The Complainant's case, as articulated in the complaint and subsequent submissions, primarily rests on the following allegations:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent**: The Respondent's textile processing unit has been repeatedly found discharging industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies without adequate treatment, thereby exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and color.
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding Standards**: The operation of boilers and generators by the Respondent for power generation and process heating has allegedly resulted in the emission of air pollutants, including Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Particulate Matter (PM), in concentrations exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial air emissions.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste**: The Respondent has allegedly failed to adequately manage and dispose of hazardous waste generated during the textile dyeing and finishing processes, including chemical sludge and spent dyes, in an environmentally sound manner, posing risks to soil and groundwater contamination.
4.  **Failure to Comply with Environmental Improvement Notices**: Despite repeated inspections, issuance of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs), and notices for improvement by the EPA, the Respondent has allegedly failed to take effective corrective measures to bring its operations into compliance with environmental laws.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence, which included:
*   **Inspection Reports**: Multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA field teams on various dates between January 2018 and May 2019, documenting observations of dark-colored effluent discharge, visible smoke emissions, and inadequate waste storage facilities at the Respondent's premises.
*   **Laboratory Analysis Reports**: Test reports from EPA-certified laboratories of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15.03.2018, 22.08.2018, and 07.02.2019, consistently showing exceedances of NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and color. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports from stack emissions on 10.04.2018 and 18.09.2018 indicated higher than permissible levels of SO2 and PM.
*   **Photographic and Videographic Evidence**: Visual records depicting the discharge of colored effluent, plumes of smoke from chimneys, and improperly stored chemical drums.
*   **Copies of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs)**: Issued under Section 16
of PEPA, 1997, on 01.05.2018 and 12.11.2018, directing the Respondent to cease the discharge of untreated effluent, control air emissions, and submit an environmental management plan within specified timelines. These EPOs were followed by reminder notices and warnings of legal action due to continued non-compliance.
**Respondent's Stance and Defence:**
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its learned counsel, vigorously contested the allegations. While acknowledging the challenges inherent in textile processing, the Respondent argued that it has consistently endeavored to comply with environmental regulations. It was submitted that the Respondent has already initiated steps towards environmental improvement, including preliminary work for the installation of an upgraded Effluent Treatment Plant

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
