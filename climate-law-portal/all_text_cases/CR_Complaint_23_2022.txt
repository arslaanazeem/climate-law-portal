This judgment addresses Complaint No. 23/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Punjab Environmental Tribunal is vested with the jurisdiction to adjudicate such matters under Section 20 of the PEPA, 1997, read with other enabling provisions.
The Complainant, through its filed complaint dated 15.08.2022, contended that the Respondent unit, engaged in textile processing, has been operating in contravention of established environmental laws and standards. Specifically, it was alleged that during a routine inspection conducted on 01.07.2022 by a team of EPA officials, the Respondent unit was found to be discharging untreated industrial effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into the River Ravi, without adequate treatment. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's air emissions from its boiler stack were also observed to be exceeding permissible limits, indicating a lack of proper air pollution control devices or their inefficient operation. The complaint highlighted that the Respondent had not installed a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) of appropriate capacity, nor had it effectively managed its air emissions, despite previous warnings and advisories issued by the EPA. A show-cause notice dated 15.07.2022 was issued to the Respondent, to which the reply received was deemed unsatisfactory, failing to provide concrete evidence of compliance or a viable action plan. The Complainant sought the imposition of penalties as prescribed under PEPA, 1997, and directions for immediate remedial actions to prevent further environmental degradation.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive inspection report dated 01.07.2022,
which included photographic evidence of the discharge, laboratory analysis reports of the effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge point showing parameters far exceeding the NEQS, and observations regarding the visible air emissions. The analysis reports, specifically, indicated elevated levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals, all beyond the prescribed limits. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
