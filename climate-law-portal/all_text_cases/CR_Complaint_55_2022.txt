**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 55/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint alleges contraventions of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent in its industrial operations. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), has jurisdiction to adjudicate upon this matter.
The Complainant, representing the provincial environmental regulatory body, lodged this complaint on 15th March 2022, following a series of inspections and monitoring activities at the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The core of the complaint revolves around the alleged persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Complainant seeks the imposition of penalties, an order for immediate compliance with environmental standards, and the recovery of costs associated with the proceedings.
The allegations against M/s Nishat Mills Limited are multifaceted. Firstly, it is contended that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically the dyeing and finishing processes, generate significant volumes of wastewater which, upon discharge into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the River Ravi, consistently fail to meet the NEQS for industrial and municipal effluent. The Complainant particularized breaches concerning parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and color. Secondly, the complaint posits that the Respondent's boiler house and other combustion sources emit particulate matter (PM) and other gaseous pollutants (e.g., NOx, SOx) into the ambient air at levels exceeding the NEQS for industrial emissions. These alleged violations, according to the Complainant, pose a direct threat to public health, aquatic ecosystems, and the overall environmental quality of the region. The EPA had issued multiple Show Cause Notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) to the Respondent prior to filing this complaint, which the Respondent allegedly failed to comply with effectively.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included:
1.  **Site Inspection Reports**: Dated 15th January 2022, 10th February 2022, and 1st March 2022, detailing observations of dark-colored effluent, visible smoke emissions, and the absence of fully functional pollution control measures during the inspections.
2.  **Effluent Sample Collection Reports**: Documenting the methodology of collecting wastewater samples from the Respondent's discharge points on 15th January 2022 and 1st March 2022, in accordance with standard protocols.
3.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports**: Issued by the EPA-accredited Environmental Monitoring and Analytical Laboratory, Lahore, bearing reference numbers EMAL/2022/01/017 and EMAL/2022/03/005. These reports unequivocally indicated that the collected effluent samples exceeded NEQS limits for BOD (average 450 mg/L against standard 80 mg/L), COD (average 1200 mg/L against standard 150 mg/L), TSS (average 350 mg/L against standard 200 mg/L), and pH (average 11.5 against standard 6-9). The reports also noted significant color presence.
4.  **Air Emission Monitoring Reports**: Conducted on 10th February 2022, showing PM emissions
(average 250 mg/Nm3 against the prescribed NEQS of 150 mg/Nm3) and elevated levels of Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) that also exceeded their respective NEQS limits.
5.  **Copies of Show Cause Notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs)**: Issued by the EPA to the Respondent on various
dates from September 2021 to February 2022, detailing the specific violations and directing remedial actions, which the Complainant asserts were largely disregarded or inadequately addressed.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
