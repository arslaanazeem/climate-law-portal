This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 55/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. The complaint, lodged on 15th December 2016, sought the intervention of this Tribunal to address persistent environmental non-compliance by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and inadequate management of hazardous waste, in contravention of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, EPA, alleged that the Respondent's chemical manufacturing operations were causing significant environmental degradation through the continuous discharge of industrial wastewater containing pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS parameters into the nearby irrigation channels and ultimately into the River Ravi. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent had failed to adequately manage and dispose of hazardous solid waste generated by its processes, leading to environmental contamination and posing risks to public health. The Complainant detailed several instances of non-compliance, including failure to operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively, if at all, and a disregard for notices issued by the EPA directing the Respondent to rectify these violations. Specific parameters cited as being in excess of NEQS included Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and various heavy metals. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated warnings and opportunities to comply, the Respondent had demonstrated a persistent pattern of non-adherence to environmental regulations, necessitating the Tribunal's intervention to impose penalties and ensure compliance.
In support of its claim, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated between January 2016 and November 2016, prepared by EPA officials, which consistently noted deficiencies in the Respondent's waste management practices and the apparent non-operation or ineffective operation of its ETP. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates (e.g., 22nd March 2016, 17th July 2016, 05th October 2016). These reports, conducted by EPA-accredited laboratories, unequivocally indicated that the discharged wastewater contained pollutants, particularly BOD, COD, and TSS, at concentrations several times higher than the NEQS limits. For instance, the report of 05th October 2016 showed COD levels at 750 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, and TSS at 320 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L. Photographic evidence depicting the colour and turbidity of the discharged effluent and improperly stored waste drums was also submitted. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices and final warnings issued to the Respondent by the EPA, detailing the violations and demanding corrective action, were placed on record, demonstrating the Complainant's attempts to secure voluntary compliance prior to filing the complaint. The Complainant
further sought an order from this Tribunal directing the Respondent to immediately cease the discharge of untreated effluent, properly manage its hazardous waste, and impose appropriate penalties as prescribed under Section 17 of PEPA 1997 for the violations committed.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, appeared through its learned counsel and submitted a written reply denying the allegations of persistent non-compliance

The Respondent contended that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was fully operational and regularly maintained, asserting that any instances of non-compliance cited by the Complainant were isolated, minor deviations or attributable to temporary operational issues, rather than a systematic failure. The Respondent challenged the veracity of the EPA's sampling procedures and laboratory analyses

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
