This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. The complaint, registered on 15th February 2020, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge. The Tribunal, established to ensure the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, assumed jurisdiction to adjudicate upon the matter concerning the Respondent's alleged persistent environmental transgressions.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing hazardous chemicals and pollutants directly into the nearby water bodies and land without adequate treatment, thereby violating the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as stipulated under the PEPA, 1997. Specific allegations included:
1.  Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent exceeding NEQS limits for parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and specific heavy metals (e.g., Mercury, Lead, Chromium).
2.  Failure to install and maintain a fully functional and efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating the diverse and complex waste streams generated by its chemical manufacturing processes.
3.  Non-compliance with multiple Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and show-cause notices issued by the EPA on various dates, specifically on 12th July 2018, 5th March 2019, and 18th November 2019, which directed the Respondent to rectify the environmental violations and submit compliance reports.
4.  Causing significant environmental degradation to the surrounding agricultural land, groundwater resources, and surface water bodies, posing a direct threat to public health and biodiversity in the vicinity.
5.  Operating the industrial facility without a valid Environmental Management Plan (EMP) or consistently failing to adhere to the commitments made in its Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) reports, if any.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on 10th June 2018, 28th February 2019, and 10th January 202
2020. These reports consistently highlighted the Respondent's failure to comply with NEQS, supported by laboratory analysis reports confirming effluent parameters far exceeding permissible limits for BOD, COD, TSS, pH, and heavy metals. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the discharge points and affected areas was also submitted, along with affidavits from local residents attesting to the environmental and health impacts suffered due to the Respondent's operations.
In response, the Respondent, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. The Respondent contended that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was functional and that it was making continuous efforts to upgrade its environmental management systems. It was argued that some of the sampling methodologies employed by the EPA were flawed or that external factors contributed to the alleged pollution. The Respondent also presented records of its investments in environmental sustainability initiatives and challenged the extent of the environmental damage attributed solely to its operations.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the submissions from both parties, including the documentary evidence, inspection reports, laboratory analyses, and the arguments presented by their respective counsels during the hearings held on various dates between March 2020 and February 2024. Expert testimonies were also considered, and cross-examination of witnesses was conducted to ascertain the veracity of the claims. The Tribunal carefully considered the provisions of PEPA, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 12, and 16, which mandate adherence to NEQS, proper treatment of industrial waste, and the powers of the EPA to issue Environmental Protection Orders.
Upon thorough deliberation, this Tribunal finds the evidence presented by the Complainant to be overwhelmingly compelling and consistent. The repeated inspection reports, corroborated by independent laboratory analyses demonstrating persistent exceedances of NEQS, leave no room for doubt regarding the Respondent's non-compliance. The Respondent's failure to effectively address the violations despite multiple Environmental Protection Orders and show-cause notices issued by the EPA indicates a pattern of disregard for environmental regulations and a lack of earnest commitment to environmental protection. The arguments put forth by the Respondent regarding flawed methodologies or external factors were not substantiated with sufficient credible evidence to rebut the Complainant's robust case. The environmental degradation documented, including harm to water bodies, land, and the potential threat to public health, is a direct consequence of the Respondent's operations and its failure to treat industrial effluent adequately.
Therefore, having carefully weighed the evidence and arguments, this Tribunal finds the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, guilty of the environmental violations as alleged by the Complainant.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 4,500,000/- (Rupees Four Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 900,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
