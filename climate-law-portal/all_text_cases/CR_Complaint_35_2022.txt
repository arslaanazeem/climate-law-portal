This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint alleges contraventions of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent in the course of its industrial operations. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, has jurisdiction to hear and decide such complaints.
The Complainant, through its duly authorized representative, Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, presented a series of allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, a prominent manufacturer of glass products located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The core allegations revolved around:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated/Partially Treated Effluent**: The Respondent was accused of discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain without adequate treatment, resulting in concentrations of pollutants significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial and municipal effluents. Specific parameters cited included Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (T
SS), and heavy metals such as lead and chromium. Samples collected on various dates, specifically on 15.03.2022 and 02.05.2022, by EPA officials and subsequently analyzed by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently showed non-compliance with NEQS.
2.  **Emission of Air Pollutants**: The Respondent was further alleged to be emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), from its industrial processes, particularly glass melting furnaces, without adequate air pollution control devices, thereby contributing to ambient air quality degradation in violation of NEQS for ambient air and industrial emissions.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste**: The complaint also highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to properly manage and dispose of hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing processes, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination and posing risks to public health and safety.
4.  **Failure to Obtain Environmental Approval**: It was also contended that the Respondent had expanded its operations or initiated certain new processes without obtaining the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval or No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the EPA, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and the EIA/IEE Regulations, 2000.
The Complainant presented documentary evidence including laboratory analysis reports, site inspection reports, photographs, and notices of violation issued to the Respondent. Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, also testified, affirming the findings of the EPA's investigations and the persistent non-compliance by the Respondent despite repeated warnings and opportunities to rectify the deficiencies.
The Respondent, represented by its legal counsel, Mr. Zahid Ali Khan, submitted a written statement denying the severity of the allegations and claiming to have undertaken measures to comply with environmental standards. The Respondent contended that its effluent treatment plant was operational, albeit facing occasional technical issues, and that efforts were underway to upgrade air pollution control equipment. It was further argued that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was flawed and that the laboratory results were not entirely reflective of the Respondent’s overall compliance efforts. However, no independent corroborating evidence, such as third-party audit reports or alternative laboratory analyses, was presented by the Respondent to substantiate its claims.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both sides. The laboratory reports submitted by the Complainant were found to be credible, having been conducted by an accredited laboratory following standard procedures. The site inspection reports and photographic evidence clearly depicted the discharge of discolored effluent and the presence of industrial emissions without proper abatement. The Tribunal noted the Respondent's admission, albeit qualified, of operational challenges with its treatment facilities, which corroborated the Complainant's allegations of untreated/partially treated effluent discharge. Furthermore, the Respondent failed to produce any valid EIA approval or NOC for its recent expansions, confirming the violation of Section 12 of PEPA, 1997.
Upon careful consideration of the documentary evidence, the testimonies, and the arguments advanced by both parties, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the contraventions of environmental laws and standards by M/s Ghani Glass Limited. The Respondent has failed to demonstrate adequate compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) regarding effluent discharge and air emissions, and has also violated the procedural requirements of PEPA, 1997, by expanding operations without the necessary environmental approvals. Such persistent non-compliance poses a significant threat to public health and the environment.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and in exercise of the powers conferred upon this Tribunal under Sections 16, 17, and 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Cessation of Non-Compliance**: M/s Ghani Glass Limited is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or partially treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain and to ensure that all emissions from its industrial processes comply fully with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under PEPA, 1997.
2.  **Installation/Upgradation of Treatment Facilities**: The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, install and/or upgrade its effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) to achieve full compliance with NEQS. The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including timelines and technical specifications, to the EPA within **thirty (30) days** of this order for approval.
3.  **Obtain Environmental Approval**: The Respondent shall, within **one hundred and twenty (120) days** from the date of this order, initiate and complete the process for obtaining the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval or No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its current operations and any recent expansions, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting**: The Respondent shall implement a robust internal environmental monitoring program and submit quarterly compliance reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party environmental consultant, to the EPA for a period of two years.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
