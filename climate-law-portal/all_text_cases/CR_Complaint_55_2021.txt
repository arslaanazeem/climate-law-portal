PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL
LAHORE
**COMPLAINT NO. 55/2021**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Packages Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company duly incorporated under the Companies Act, 2017, and operating an industrial facility at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Lahore. The complaint, bearing No. 55/2021, was instituted on 10th November 2021, alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluents and emission of air pollutants. The Tribunal, having heard the arguments of both parties and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented, proceeds to deliver its judgment.
The Complainant's allegations against M/s Packages Limited primarily revolve around the Respondent's failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial liquid effluents and gaseous emissions, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"). Specifically, it was alleged that during a routine inspection conducted on 15th September 2021, EPA officials observed visible signs of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater being discharged from the Respondent's premises into a municipal drain, which eventually connects to the River Ravi. Samples of the effluent and air emissions from the Respondent's stacks were collected. Subsequent laboratory analysis reports, dated 20th September 2021, revealed that the discharged effluent contained levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH significantly exceeding the NEQS. Furthermore, the analysis of air emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks indicated particulate matter (PM) and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) concentrations above the permissible limits set by the NEQS. The Complainant also asserted that the Respondent had previously received a warning notice on 1st June 2021 regarding similar environmental non-compliance issues, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and a failure to implement effective pollution control measures despite prior intimation.
In support of its complaint, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence. This included the Inspection Report dated 15th September 2021, prepared by Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director, EPA, detailing the observations of visible pollution and the sampling procedure. Crucially, the Complainant submitted the official laboratory analysis report from an EPA-accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for both liquid effluents and air emissions. The report provided specific values for BOD (350 mg/L against NEQS of
80 mg/L), COD (700 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and TSS (450 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L). The pH was recorded at 4.5, falling outside the permissible range of 6-9. Similarly, the air emission analysis showed particulate matter concentration of 250 mg/Nm³ against the NEQS of 100 mg/Nm³ and Sulphur Dioxide levels of 850 ppm against the NEQS of 600 ppm. The Complainant further appended copies of the prior warning notice issued on 1st June 2021, and the subsequent reminder, illustrating a history of non-compliance and the Respondent’s failure to take corrective action.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its counsel, contested the allegations, arguing that it had invested significantly in pollution control equipment, including an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air scrubbers. They contended that the observed exceedances might have been due to a temporary malfunction or an isolated incident, and that their facility generally operates in compliance with environmental standards. The Respondent also questioned the methodology of sample collection and analysis, suggesting potential procedural irregularities. However, no concrete evidence or independent laboratory reports were presented by the Respondent to refute the findings of the EPA-accredited laboratory. The Respondent also failed to provide documentary proof of recent maintenance records for their pollution control devices or a robust environmental management plan showing proactive measures.
Upon careful consideration of the documentary evidence and the arguments advanced by both parties, the Tribunal finds the Complainant's case to be well-substantiated. The detailed inspection report, coupled with the conclusive laboratory analysis reports from an accredited institution, provides clear and compelling evidence of the Respondent’s non-compliance with the NEQS for both industrial effluents and air emissions. The specific values obtained from the laboratory analysis, significantly exceeding the prescribed limits, leave no room for doubt regarding the environmental damage being caused. The Respondent's general assertions of investment in pollution control, without providing specific evidence to counter the EPA's findings for the dates in question, are insufficient to rebut the strong prima facie case established by the Complainant. Furthermore, the prior warning notice serves to highlight
the Respondent's persistent failure to rectify the environmental non-compliance, despite having been duly notified. The Respondent's defence, resting on general claims of investment and questioning sampling methodology without presenting concrete evidence to support these assertions, lacks credibility and fails to dislodge the robust evidence presented by the Complainant.
Therefore, this Tribunal concludes that M/s Packages Limited is in clear violation of Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and gaseous emissions, as notified under the Act. The discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater and the emission of air pollutants beyond permissible limits constitute a serious environmental offence, posing a significant threat to public health and the ecological balance of the River Ravi and surrounding areas. Such persistent non-compliance, especially after prior warnings, demonstrates a dereliction of environmental responsibility on the part of the Respondent.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is found guilty of violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluents and emitting air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Imposition of Fine:** A penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the aforementioned violations. This fine shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Corrective Measures and Compliance Plan:**
    *   The Respondent shall immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants exceeding the NEQS.
    *   Within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment, the Respondent shall upgrade, repair, or replace its existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (air scrubbers, filters, etc.) to ensure full and continuous compliance with all applicable NEQS for both liquid effluents and gaseous emissions.
    *   Within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment, the Respondent shall submit a detailed Environmental Compliance Plan (ECP) to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab. The ECP must outline specific timelines, technical specifications of proposed upgrades, and a robust monitoring mechanism to ensure sustained compliance.
    *   The Respondent shall install continuous online monitoring systems for key parameters of both effluent discharge and stack emissions, with data accessible in real-time by the EPA, within ninety (90) days from the date of this judgment.
4.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall conduct quarterly third-party environmental audits by an EPA-accredited consultant for a period of two (2) years, submitting the reports to the EPA within fifteen (15) days of their completion.
5.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the timely payment of the fine or implementation of corrective measures, shall result in further legal action, including but not limited to, the imposition of additional daily fines as per Section 17 of the Act, sealing of the industrial unit, or any other action deemed appropriate by this Tribunal.
6.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
