This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint, instituted on October 26, 2020, seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just and equitable decision.
The EPA, through its Assistant Director, Lahore, brought forth several allegations against the Respondent. It was contended that M/s Packages Limited, operating a large-scale paper and packaging manufacturing facility, had consistently failed to comply with the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents and air emissions. Specifically, the complaint alleged that routine monitoring and surprise inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including July 15, 2020, August 28, 2020, and September 10, 2020, revealed discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into the municipal sewerage system, containing parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (e.g., Lead, Chromium) significantly exceeding the permissible limits set under the NEQS. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial stacks emitted particulate matter (PM10), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) above the NEQS, indicating insufficient air pollution control measures. The complaint also highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to maintain proper records of hazardous waste generation, treatment, and disposal, as mandated by the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and the Punjab Environmental Quality Standards for Industrial and Municipal Effluents, 2016. The EPA asserted that despite repeated notices of violation issued on August 05, 2020, and September 20, 2020, the Respondent had not taken adequate remedial actions, thereby posing a continuous threat to public health and the environment.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams, documenting site observations, operational deficiencies, and non-compliant practices. Crucially, the EPA submitted laboratory analysis reports from its accredited environmental laboratory, bearing dates July 18, 2020, August 30, 2020, and September 12, 2020, which presented irrefutable data indicating the exceedance of NEQS for various parameters in both wastewater effluents and air emissions. For instance, effluent samples showed COD levels ranging from 350-450 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, and TSS levels of 250-300 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L. Air emission reports indicated PM10 levels of 200-250 µg/m³ against a NEQS limit of 150 µg/m³. Photographic evidence depicting the discharge points and visible emissions was also tendered. The Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, appeared as a witness, reiterating the findings of the inspection reports and the agency's efforts to secure compliance from the Respondent. Copies of the show-cause notices and the Respondent's inadequate replies were also placed on record.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages
Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. While acknowledging the presence of an industrial facility and the generation of effluents and emissions, the Respondent contended that it operates within the framework of environmental regulations and possesses a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Systems (APCS). The Respondent argued that the EPA's sampling methodology might have been flawed

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 4,500,000/- (Rupees Four Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 900,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
