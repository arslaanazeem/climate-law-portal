This judgment addresses Complaint No. 17/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and directions for compliance with environmental laws.
The Complainant's case, as articulated in the complaint and subsequent submissions, posits that the Respondent, a chemical manufacturing facility located in Lahore, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Lahore Canal, and emitting air pollutants in excess of the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, the allegations include:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Effluent:** It is alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations generate a significant volume of wastewater containing high levels of chemical oxygen demand (COD), biological oxygen demand (BOD), total suspended solids (TSS), and heavy metals such as mercury and chromium, which are being discharged without meeting the NEQS for industrial effluent.
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding Standards:** The Complainant asserts that the Respondent's industrial stack emissions contain sulphur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and particulate matter (PM10) in concentrations significantly higher than the limits stipulated by the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste:** The Respondent is further accused of inadequate storage and disposal of hazardous solid waste generated during its manufacturing processes, posing a risk to soil and groundwater contamination.
4.  **Non-compliance with Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs):** The EPA states that despite multiple inspection visits, issuance of show-cause notices, and two Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) dated 15.03.2021 and 05.05.2021, requiring the Respondent to install and operationalize an effective effluent treatment plant (ETP), upgrade its air pollution control devices, and submit a hazardous waste management plan, the Respondent has failed to achieve compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive body of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams on various dates, specifically 01.03.2021, 15.04.2021, and 20.06.2021,
which documented the observed non-compliance. Crucially, the Complainant also submitted comprehensive laboratory analysis reports from accredited environmental laboratories, confirming that the Respondent's effluent and air emissions consistently exceeded the permissible limits for the aforementioned parameters as per NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the direct discharge of industrial wastewater into the Lahore Canal and visible plumes of emissions from the Respondent's stacks were also presented.
The Respondent, despite being afforded ample opportunity to present its defense and rebut the serious allegations leveled against it, offered a general denial without producing any credible evidence to counter the Complainant's detailed submissions. While the Respondent's counsel argued that the facility was in the process of upgrading its environmental controls, no concrete timelines, progress reports, or verifiable technical plans were submitted to substantiate these claims. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,000,000/- (Rupees Three Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 600,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
