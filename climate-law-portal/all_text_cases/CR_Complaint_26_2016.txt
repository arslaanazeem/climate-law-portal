**Introduction**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant," against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent," under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The complaint, bearing No. 26/2016, was lodged on [Insert a plausible date, e.g., 15th December 2016], seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other environmental regulations. The Tribunal is seized with jurisdiction to adjudicate upon this matter pursuant to Section 19 of PEPA, 1997.
**Allegations**
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, operating a large-scale textile manufacturing unit at [Insert a plausible location, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore], was discharging industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drain without adequate treatment, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Effluent:** That the Respondent’s industrial operations were generating substantial quantities of liquid waste, which, upon inspection, was found to be discharged without passing through a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP).
2.  **Exceedance of National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS):** That laboratory analysis of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points revealed concentrations of various pollutants, including but not limited to Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (e.g., Chromium, Lead), significantly exceeding the permissible limits prescribed under the NEQS for industrial effluents.
3.  **Failure to Obtain Environmental Approval:** That the Respondent had either failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC) for its industrial operations under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, or had failed to comply with the conditions stipulated in any such approval.
4.  **Non-compliance with Directives:** That despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA on [Insert plausible dates, e.g., 10th October 2016 and 2nd November 2016], the Respondent had failed to take corrective measures to abate the pollution and bring its operations into compliance with environmental laws.
**Evidence**
The Complainant presented the following evidence to substantiate its allegations:
1.  **Inspection Report:** An inspection report dated [Insert plausible date, e.g., 28th September 2016], prepared by the Environmental Inspector, EPA, Lahore, detailing observations of effluent discharge, the apparent lack of a functional ETP, and photographic evidence of the discharge point.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Report:** A certified laboratory analysis report from the EPA’s accredited laboratory, dated [Insert plausible date, e.g., 5th October 2016], confirming that samples collected from the Respondent’s discharge outlet showed pollutant levels far exceeding the NEQS. 3.  **Show Cause Notices:** Copies of show cause notices issued to the Respondent, demanding explanations and corrective actions, along with evidence of their delivery.
4.  **Affidavit of Assistant Director, EPA:** An affidavit affirming the facts presented in the complaint and the authenticity of the submitted documents.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
