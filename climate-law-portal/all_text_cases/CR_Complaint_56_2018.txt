This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 56/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of this Tribunal. The complaint, lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleges grave violations of national environmental quality standards and other related environmental regulations by the Respondent. The Tribunal, duly constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, and possessing the requisite jurisdiction to adjudicate upon environmental offences, proceeded with the matter following due process.
The Complainant's allegations primarily revolve around the Respondent's alleged failure to manage and treat its industrial effluent in conformity with the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, it was asserted that during an inspection conducted by EPA officials on 15th March 2018, and a subsequent follow-up inspection on 22nd May 2018, the Respondent's industrial facility located at [Hypothetical Address, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore] was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into the River Ravi. Samples collected during these inspections, upon laboratory analysis by the EPA's accredited laboratory,
confirmed the presence of pollutants exceeding the NEQS parameters for various critical indicators, including but not limited to Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals. These findings were duly documented in inspection reports and laboratory analysis certificates, which were submitted as evidence by the Complainant.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, was duly served with notices and afforded ample opportunity to present its defense. In its initial response, the Respondent contended that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was functional and designed to meet the NEQS. However, during the course of the proceedings, the Respondent failed to provide credible independent laboratory reports, operational logs, or any other documentary evidence to substantiate its claim of consistent compliance. Furthermore, the Respondent could not effectively rebut the specific findings of the EPA's laboratory analyses or challenge the methodology employed by the EPA officials during inspections and sampling.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by the Complainant, including the detailed inspection reports, photographic evidence of discharge points, and the comprehensive laboratory analyses. The consistency and thoroughness of the EPA's evidence, coupled with the Respondent's inability to provide a cogent and verifiable defense, led the Tribunal to conclude that the allegations of environmental non-compliance were well-founded. The discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually contaminates the River Ravi, constitutes a serious environmental offense with far-reaching implications for public health, aquatic ecosystems, and the overall environmental integrity of the region. Such actions are in direct contravention of the fundamental principles enshrined in the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, which aims to protect, conserve, rehabilitate, and improve the environment.
Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, unequivocally prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration, or level that is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards. The evidence presented by the EPA clearly establishes that M/s Packages Limited has violated this mandatory provision. The gravity of the violation is further amplified by the Respondent's stature as a large industrial entity, which is expected to operate with the highest standards of environmental stewardship.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
Having carefully considered all the evidence, arguments, and legal provisions, this Tribunal hereby finds M/s Packages Limited guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
It is therefore ordered as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Non-Compliance:** M/s Packages Limited

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
