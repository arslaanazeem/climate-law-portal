**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 2/2016 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged on 15th January 2016, sought penal action against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS").
The complaint, lodged on 15th January 2016, sought penal action against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS").
The Complainant contended that the Respondent's industrial operations were
discharging untreated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, far exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Inspections conducted by EPA officials on multiple occasions, specifically on 2nd November 2015 and 28th December 2015, revealed a consistent pattern of non-compliance. Laboratory analyses of samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points and stack emissions confirmed the presence of various hazardous substances beyond permissible limits, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment. The Complainant further asserted that the Respondent had failed to install and operate adequate effluent treatment plants (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) despite repeated notices and warnings issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, thereby demonstrating a willful disregard for environmental regulations.
In response, the Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, arguing that it had undertaken substantial measures to comply with environmental standards. The Respondent submitted that it had invested heavily in modernizing its production processes and was in the process of upgrading its existing ETP and APCDs. It was contended that any exceedance of NEQS was temporary, attributable to start-up issues with new equipment or unforeseen operational disruptions, and not indicative of a deliberate or systemic failure to comply. The Respondent also questioned the methodology and chain of custody of the samples collected by the EPA, suggesting that the analytical reports might not accurately reflect the true state of affairs. Furthermore, the Respondent pleaded for leniency, emphasizing its role as a significant employer and contributor to the national economy, and assured the Tribunal of its commitment to achieving full compliance within a reasonable timeframe.
The Tribunal carefully considered the arguments presented by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence placed on record. This included the inspection reports, sampling protocols, laboratory analysis certificates, notices issued by EPA, and the Respondent's replies and technical reports. Expert testimony was also heard regarding the efficacy of the Respondent's existing pollution control measures.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
