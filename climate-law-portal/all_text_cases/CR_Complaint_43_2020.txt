This judgment addresses Complaint No. 43/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Tribunal was constituted under Section 20 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and its enforcement. The present complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of the PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant's allegations, as detailed in the complaint filed on October 20, 2020, primarily revolve around the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically its glass manufacturing facility located in the vicinity of Lahore. It is contended that the Respondent has been consistently discharging gaseous emissions from its industrial chimneys and stacks that exceed the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, particularly concerning particulate matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2). The complaint further alleges that despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to install or adequately operate appropriate pollution control devices, thereby continuing to contribute significantly to ambient air pollution in the area. Additionally, the Complainant raised concerns regarding the inadequate management and disposal of industrial solid waste, including cullet and refractory waste, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including July 15, 2020, August 28, 2020, and September 25, 2020. These reports detailed observations of dark, dense smoke emanating from the Respondent's stacks, indicative of high particulate matter emissions. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of stack emissions samples collected by accredited EPA laboratories on August 1, 2020, and September 10, 2020. These reports unequivocally indicated that the concentrations of PM, NOx, and SO2 significantly exceeded the limits stipulated in the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. For instance, the PM levels were found to be consistently over 300 mg/Nm3 against a standard of 150 mg/Nm3, and NOx levels were recorded at approximately 1200 mg/Nm3 against a standard of 800 mg/Nm3. The Complainant also produced copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on August 15, 2020, and September 30, 2020, demanding compliance and explanation, to which the Respondent's replies were deemed unsatisfactory by the EPA. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting visible emissions was also tendered.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations. Through its legal counsel, the Respondent argued that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection and has invested substantially in modern manufacturing technology. The Respondent contended that the emission exceedances, if any, were transient and attributable to specific operational upsets or start-up/shut-down phases, rather than systemic non-compliance. It was further argued that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was flawed or not representative of normal operating conditions. The Respondent presented its own internal environmental audit reports and maintenance logs for its pollution control equipment, primarily electrostatic precipitators (ESPs) and scrubbers, asserting that these devices were regularly maintained and operational. An expert witness, Dr. Amir Sohail, an environmental engineer, testified on behalf of the Respondent, suggesting possible inaccuracies in EPA's testing protocols and the complexities of monitoring emissions from glass furnaces. The Respondent also submitted an Environmental Management Plan (EMP) outlining future measures to upgrade its air pollution control systems, including a proposal for installing a new baghouse filter system, indicating a proactive approach to compliance. Regarding solid waste, the Respondent presented records of contracts with authorized waste handlers for the disposal of hazardous waste and emphasized efforts to recycle cullet.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, prohibits any person from discharging or emitting any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level that is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), or from contravening any other standard established under this Act. Section 16
of the PEPA, 1997, empowers the Tribunal to hear and adjudicate complaints regarding contraventions of the Act and the rules and regulations made thereunder. Section 17 grants the EPA officials powers to enter, inspect, take samples, and conduct tests. Crucially, Section 21 of the Act prescribes penalties for contravention of the provisions of the Act, including imprisonment and fines,
which may extend to one million rupees and with a further fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which the contravention continues. The Tribunal is also empowered to direct the defaulting party to take specific measures to prevent or abate pollution, restore the environment, and pay compensation for environmental damage.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. **FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
