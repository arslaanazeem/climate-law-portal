This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 10/2016, initiated by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint, filed on 15th March 2016, seeks penal action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant’s primary allegations against M/s Packages Limited revolve around consistent non-compliance with the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules and regulations. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were generating and discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/receiving water bodies, containing pollutants such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals, all in concentrations significantly exceeding the NEQS. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of excessive emissions of air pollutants, including Particulate Matter (PM), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), from the Respondent's stacks, thereby contributing to ambient air pollution. The Complainant also asserted that the Respondent had failed to maintain and operate its effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) efficiently, despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA. The persistent violations, according to the Complainant, posed a significant threat to public health and the environment, necessitating urgent intervention by this Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 12th January 2015, 20th May 2015, 18th September 2015, and 5th February 2016, detailing observations of operational deficiencies in the Respondent's ETP and APCDs. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, corresponding to samples of industrial effluent and stack emissions collected during these inspections. These reports consistently indicated that the pollutant parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, pH, heavy metals in effluent; PM, SO2, NOx in air emissions) significantly exceeded the NEQS. Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 10th March 2015, 30th June 2015, and 15th October 2015, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, were also tendered. An expert witness from the EPA's technical wing testified regarding the methodology of sampling and analysis, confirming the accuracy of the lab reports and the severity of the violations.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting its commitment to environmental
compliance and corporate social responsibility. Through their learned counsel, they contended that M/s Packages Limited has invested substantial resources in establishing and maintaining a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and various Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), which are regularly monitored and operated by trained personnel. They argued that any alleged exceedances of NEQS were either isolated incidents, attributable to transient
operational anomalies, such as power fluctuations or temporary equipment maintenance, which were promptly rectified. They further challenged the sampling methodology employed by the EPA, suggesting potential inconsistencies in sample collection, preservation, or transportation that could have compromised the integrity and representativeness of the laboratory results. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
