This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit, situated in the industrial area of Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15th October, 2019, alleges persistent violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by the Respondent, thereby causing pollution and degradation of the environment. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), has jurisdiction to hear and adjudicate upon such matters.
The Complainant's allegations primarily revolve around the Respondent's failure to comply with environmental standards and legal requirements. Specifically, it was alleged
that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited was consistently discharging industrial effluent containing hazardous substances and pollutants beyond the permissible limits prescribed by the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint highlighted several specific instances, supported by inspection reports and laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA, demonstrating exceedances for parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and various heavy metals. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent had failed to install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite repeated warnings and notices, thereby contravening Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge of effluent or waste causing pollution in excess of NEQS. The Complainant also asserted that the Respondent had failed to obtain a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its operations or, alternatively, had failed to adhere to the conditions of any existing NOC, a requirement under Section 12 of the PEPA, 1997.
The Tribunal, having carefully considered the complaint, the evidence presented by the Complainant, including detailed inspection reports, laboratory test results of effluent samples collected on multiple dates, and the failure of the Respondent to present a convincing defence or demonstrate compliance, finds the allegations to be substantiated. The consistent exceedance of NEQS, as evidenced by the analytical reports, unequivocally establishes the Respondent's violation of environmental laws. The Tribunal notes that the Respondent was provided ample opportunity to rectify its non-compliance and present its case, but failed to do so effectively, leaving the evidence presented by the EPA unchallenged in its material aspects. The persistent discharge of untreated industrial waste into the environment constitutes a serious breach of environmental protection laws and poses a significant threat to public health and the ecosystem.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Liability:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is found liable for violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and for failing to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Daily Penalty for Non-Compliance:** In addition to the above, the Respondent shall pay a further penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day of continued non-compliance with the directions outlined in paragraphs 4, 5, and 6 below, commencing from the expiry of the respective compliance periods.
4.  **Installation and Operation of ETP:** The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent. Within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, the Respondent shall install, upgrade, and fully operationalize an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating its industrial wastewater to meet the prescribed NEQS.
5.  **Obtaining NOC/Permits:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this order, obtain all necessary No Objection Certificates (NOCs) and environmental permits required under the PEPA, 1997, and its rules, or demonstrate full compliance with the conditions of any existing NOCs.
6.  **Environmental Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit quarterly environmental monitoring reports, duly certified by an EPA-approved laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, for a period of two years, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS and other environmental regulations. The first report shall be due three months after the operationalization of the ETP.
7.  **Environmental Performance Guarantee:** The Respondent shall furnish an environmental performance guarantee in the amount of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** of this order, which may be forfeited in case of future non-compliance.
8.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 200,000/- (Rupees Two Hundred Thousand only)**, payable to the Complainant within thirty (30) days.
Failure to comply with any part of this order may result in further legal action, including but not limited to, closure of the industrial unit under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997.
