This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, exercising its jurisdiction conferred under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, has carefully considered the matter brought before it to ensure the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, the prevention and control of pollution, and the promotion of sustainable development.
The Complainant alleged that M/s Ghani Glass Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating in gross violation of environmental laws. Specifically, the allegations included: (a) persistent discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial liquid effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH; (b) emission of air pollutants, including Particulate Matter (PM), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), from its stacks, which regularly surpass the NEQS for industrial air emissions; (c) failure to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), leading to the aforementioned exceedances; (d) failure to submit regular and accurate self-monitoring reports as required under the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2001; and (e) disregard for multiple notices and warnings issued by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for corrective measures, culminating in the filing of this complaint after the Respondent failed to demonstrate satisfactory compliance. The Complainant further contended that these violations posed a significant threat to public health and the surrounding environment, including water bodies and ambient air quality.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included certified copies of multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between October 2023 and February 2024, detailing observations of malfunctioning or inadequate pollution control systems. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent and stack emission samples collected during these inspections by accredited laboratories. These reports consistently demonstrated that the Respondent's discharges and emissions significantly exceeded the NEQS. For instance, an effluent sample collected on 15.11.2023 showed COD levels of 450 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L, and TSS levels of 300 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L. Similarly, stack emission analysis from 20.12.2023 indicated PM levels of 800 mg/Nm3 against a standard of 600 mg/Nm3. The Complainant also produced copies of show-cause notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued to the Respondent under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, which largely consisted of promises of future action without concrete proof of compliance. The Complainant's representative, Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, also appeared before the Tribunal and affirmed the veracity of the documentary evidence and the agency's persistent efforts to bring the Respondent into compliance. The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its counsel, largely denied the allegations, attributing any exceedances to temporary operational glitches or outdated monitoring equipment used by the EPA. However, the Respondent failed to present any credible independent laboratory reports demonstrating compliance, nor did they provide detailed records of maintenance or upgrades
of their pollution control systems. The Respondent's counsel merely submitted an undertaking stating that measures would be taken to comply with NEQS in the future, without providing any timeline or concrete action plan.
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the voluminous record, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses, show-cause notices, EPOs, and the replies thereto. It is evident that the Complainant has presented a robust and consistent body of evidence, meticulously documented and scientifically supported by accredited laboratory reports. The consistency of the exceedances over several months, as demonstrated by multiple samples, clearly indicates a systemic failure on the part of the Respondent to effectively manage its environmental responsibilities. The Respondent's defense, consisting primarily of general denials and vague promises, is unsubstantiated and lacks any credible evidence to rebut the specific and detailed allegations. The Tribunal finds the claim of 'temporary operational glitches' or 'outdated monitoring equipment' to be self-serving and unsupported by any proof, especially given the Respondent's failure to present its own independent monitoring data or maintenance records for its pollution control devices. Therefore, the Tribunal is satisfied that the Complainant has discharged its burden of proof beyond reasonable doubt, establishing that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has been operating in persistent violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the NEQS. Furthermore, the Respondent's failure to maintain and operate its pollution control devices effectively constitutes a violation of the general duty to prevent pollution and protect the environment, as enshrined in PEPA, 1997. The consistent disregard for EPA notices and EPOs also points to a deliberate non-compliance rather than an isolated incident.
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested under Section 20 read
with Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal hereby finds the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, GUILTY of persistent violations of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and associated rules and regulations, including the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**ACCORDINGLY, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED AS FOLLOWS:**
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is directed to forthwith cease all discharges of industrial effluent and emissions of air pollutants that exceed the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Upgradation/Maintenance of Pollution Control Systems:** The Respondent shall immediately take all necessary measures to upgrade, repair, and ensure the effective operation and continuous maintenance of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to achieve full compliance with NEQS within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order.
3.  **Submission of Compliance Plan:** Within **thirty (30) days** of this order, the Respondent shall submit to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, a detailed, time-bound action plan outlining the specific steps, technical specifications, and financial commitments for achieving and sustaining compliance with NEQS. This plan shall be subject to review and approval by the EPA.
4.  **Regular Self-Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall strictly comply with the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2001, and submit accurate, certified self-monitoring reports of its effluent and emissions to the EPA on a monthly basis, commencing from the first month following the date of this order. These reports must include independent laboratory analyses from EPA-approved laboratories.
5.  **Environmental Audit:** The Respondent shall arrange for an independent environmental audit of its operations, including the functionality of its ETP and APCDs, by an EPA-accredited environmental consultant within **six (6) months** from the date of this order. The audit report shall be submitted to the EPA and the Tribunal.
6.  **Penalty:** For the persistent and grave violations established, M/s Ghani Glass Limited is hereby imposed a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million Only)**. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
7.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:**
    *   Failure to comply with any part of this order, particularly the timelines stipulated in clauses 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5, shall result in the imposition of an additional daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for each day of continued non-compliance, without prejudice to other actions.
    *   In the event of continued or deliberate non-compliance, the Tribunal may consider further stringent actions, including but not limited to, directing the closure of the industrial unit under Section 16(b) of PEPA, 1997, and initiating proceedings for contempt of court.
8.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
The Registrar is directed to ensure compliance with this order and to report any instances of non-compliance to the Tribunal for further necessary action.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
