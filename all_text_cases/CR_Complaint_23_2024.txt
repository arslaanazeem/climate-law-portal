**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 23/2024, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, located at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant initiated these proceedings on February 15, 2024, asserting that the Respondent has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants exceeding the prescribed NEQS into the municipal sewerage system, and emitting untreated gaseous pollutants into the ambient air, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. The complaint further alleges inadequate management of hazardous waste and failure to install and operate appropriate pollution control devices despite repeated warnings and notices from the EPA.
The allegations against M/s Packages Limited primarily stem from several inspections conducted by the EPA. Specifically, it is alleged that during an inspection on November 10, 2023, EPA officials observed the direct discharge of untreated industrial wastewater from the Respondent's premises. Subsequent sampling and laboratory analysis of the effluent, conducted by the EPA's accredited laboratory on November 15, 2023 (Report No. EPA/Lab/LHR/2023/11-045), revealed levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (Chromium, Lead) significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluent discharge into inland waters/municipal sewers. For instance, BOD was recorded at 450 mg/l against a standard of 80 mg/l, COD at 1200 mg/l against 150 mg/l, and TSS at 300 mg/l against 200 mg/l. Furthermore, air
emissions monitoring conducted on December 5, 2023 (Report No. EPA/Air/LHR/2023/12-012) indicated concentrations of particulate matter (PM10) and sulfur dioxide (SO2) from the Respondent's boiler stacks exceeding the NEQS. PM10 was found to be 250 µg/m³ against a standard of 150 µg/m³, and SO2 at 1800 µg/m³ against 1000 µg/m³. These findings were communicated to the Respondent through a notice under Section 16 of the Act on December 20, 2023, followed by a formal show-cause notice on January 10, 2024, demanding an explanation and a comprehensive compliance plan.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, appeared through its authorized representative and legal counsel. While acknowledging the findings of the EPA's inspections and laboratory analyses, the Respondent contended that they have an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP
), which they assert is generally operational. However, they conceded that the plant may have experienced temporary operational inefficiencies or technical glitches during the periods of EPA inspection, leading to the reported exceedances. They further submitted that they are a responsible corporate entity, employing a large workforce and contributing significantly to the national economy, and that they are committed to environmental stewardship. They presented a preliminary plan for upgrading their existing ETP and installing additional air pollution control equipment, requesting a reasonable timeframe for implementation and seeking a lenient view from the Tribunal, asserting that the violations were not intentional or malicious.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions of both parties and the documentary evidence on record. The Complainant has presented clear, scientific evidence in the form of laboratory reports from an accredited EPA laboratory, unequivocally demonstrating that the Respondent's industrial effluent and air emissions, on multiple occasions, significantly exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards. The levels of BOD, COD, TSS, heavy metals (Chromium, Lead) in the wastewater, and particulate matter (PM10) and sulfur dioxide (SO2) in air emissions, are alarming and indicative of substantial environmental pollution.
While the Respondent acknowledges these findings and attributes them to "temporary operational inefficiencies," this explanation does not absolve them of their legal obligations under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the NEQS. The NEQS are non-negotiable standards designed to protect public health and the environment, and any deviation, regardless of intent, constitutes a violation. The onus was on the Respondent to ensure continuous compliance. The fact that repeat inspections and analyses consistently revealed exceedances suggests a systemic issue, either with the design, capacity, or consistent operation and maintenance of their existing pollution control systems. Furthermore, the Respondent's preliminary plan for upgrades, while a positive step, does not negate the past and ongoing non-compliance that necessitated this complaint. Their status as a large employer and economic contributor, while noted, cannot be used as a shield against environmental accountability. Environmental laws apply equally to all entities, irrespective of their size or economic impact. The Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the violations as alleged.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 4,900,000/- (Rupees Four Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 980,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **18 April, 2023**.
