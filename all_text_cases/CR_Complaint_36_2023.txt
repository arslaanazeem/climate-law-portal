The present complaint, bearing No. 36/2023, has been filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in manufacturing packaging materials and related products. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of the PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant's allegations against the Respondent primarily revolve around persistent non-compliance with environmental standards. Specifically, it is alleged that the Respondent is discharging industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into the Hudiara Drain, without adequate treatment, resulting in the effluent parameters exceeding the prescribed NEQS for Municipal and Industrial Effluents. The Complainant asserts that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed a consistent pattern of non-compliance. Furthermore, the complaint alleges that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly emissions from its boiler stacks and other process units, are contributing to air pollution, with certain parameters of air emissions exceeding the NEQS for Industrial Gaseous Emissions. It is also contended that the Respondent has failed to adequately manage and dispose of its industrial solid waste and hazardous waste in an environmentally sound manner, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination. The Complainant submitted that despite repeated warnings, issuance of show cause notices dated 10.04.2023 and 25.05.2023, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent has failed to implement effective pollution control measures, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports dated 15.03.2023, 20.04.2023, and 05.06.2023, prepared by EPA field teams, which documented observations of discolored and foul-smelling effluent discharge, visible smoke plumes, and improper waste
disposal practices. These reports were corroborated by photographic and videographic evidence capturing the alleged violations in real-time. Crucially, the Complainant also submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent and air emission samples collected from the Respondent's premises on various dates, specifically 16.03.2023, 21.04.2023, and 0
05.06.2023. These reports unequivocally indicated that the levels of various pollutants, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals in the effluent, and particulate matter (PM), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) in air emissions, consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
