This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), pertains to alleged violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The Tribunal has jurisdiction under Section 21 of the Act to adjudicate upon complaints regarding contraventions of the Act and the rules and regulations made thereunder. The proceedings commenced upon receipt of the complaint, and notices were duly issued to the Respondent, who subsequently appeared through their authorized representative and counsel.
The Complainant contended that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which eventually found its way into natural water bodies, causing significant environmental degradation and posing risks to public health. Specifically, the allegations included:
a.  Discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Municipal and Industrial Effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH.
b.  Failure to operate and maintain an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) commensurate with the volume and characteristics of the effluent generated by its manufacturing processes.
c.  Non-compliance with previous directions and notices issued by the EPA concerning the improvement of their pollution control measures.
d.  Operating in a manner detrimental to the environment and public health, in contravention of Section 11 of the Act.
To substantiate its claims, the
Complainant submitted detailed inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, along with corresponding laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points. These laboratory reports, duly attested by the authorized chemists, consistently indicated that the discharged effluent contained parameters significantly exceeding the permissible NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. Furthermore, the Complainant presented copies of multiple Environmental Improvement Notices (
EINs) and final show-cause notices previously issued to the Respondent, demonstrating a pattern of non-compliance and a failure to adequately address the pollution concerns despite repeated warnings.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its authorized representative and counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. They contended that they had made significant investments in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and were committed to operating it effectively. Their primary arguments included:
a.  Challenges to the sampling methodology employed by EPA officials, suggesting that samples might have been collected from non-representative points or that the chain of custody was not strictly maintained, thus questioning the validity of the laboratory results.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
