**J U D G M E N T**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity located in Lahore, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, established under Section 20 of the PEPA, 1997, is vested with the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints regarding environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental laws. The instant complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial directions against the Respondent for its alleged failure to comply with environmental standards.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent, operating a large-scale manufacturing facility involving paper, board, and packaging production, was found to be discharging industrial effluent and emitting gaseous pollutants in excess of the NEQS. Specifically, the allegations, primarily stemming from an inspection conducted by EPA officials on 15th September 2022, include:
1.  Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system/water body, with laboratory analysis revealing levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents.
2.  Emission of particulate matter (PM10 and PM2.5) and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) from its industrial stacks, particularly those associated with the boiler and process units, beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
3.  Improper management and disposal of hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste generated during the manufacturing process, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination.
4.  Failure to regularly submit environmental self-monitoring reports as required under the Environmental Protection (Self-Monitoring and Reporting) Rules, 2001, indicating a lack of transparency and commitment to environmental compliance.
5.  Operating without a valid Environmental Approval (NOC) for certain process expansions or modifications, as mandated by Section 12 of the PEPA, 1997, and the Environmental Impact Assessment Regulations, 2000.
A Show Cause Notice, bearing reference No. EPA/LHR/AD/2022/543, was issued to the Respondent on 25th September 2022, to which the Respondent submitted a reply deemed unsatisfactory by the Complainant, thus necessitating the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included:
1.  **Inspection Report dated 15th September 2022**: Prepared by a team of EPA technical experts, detailing observations of effluent discharge points, stack emissions, and waste accumulation areas.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/LAB/2022/987 dated 20th September 2022**: Issued by the accredited EPA laboratory, confirming the presence of pollutants in the effluent samples (e.g., BOD at 120 mg/L against NEQS of 80 mg/L, COD at 350 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L, TSS at 250 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L, and pH outside the 6-9 range). The report also indicated gaseous emission levels (e.g., PM10 at 200 µg/m³ against NEQS of 150 µg/m³, SO2 at 400 µg/m³ against NEQS of
350 µg/m³).
3.  **Photographic and Video Evidence**: Documenting visible pollution, improper waste storage, and non-compliant practices observed during the inspection.
4.  **Correspondence Record**: Including the Show Cause Notice and the Respondent's unsatisfactory reply, demonstrating a prior attempt at resolution and the Respondent's failure to adequately address the concerns raised by the
Complainant. The EPA also submitted copies of relevant NEQS notifications and the PEPA, 1997, to establish the legal framework for the alleged violations.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
