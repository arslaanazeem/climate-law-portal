This judgment addresses Complaint No. 3/2018, instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent industrial entity located in the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint, filed on 15.02.2018, alleges significant violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions exceeding prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal commenced proceedings in accordance with the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), aiming to adjudicate the environmental liabilities of the Respondent and ensure compliance with the nation's environmental protection framework. Throughout the proceedings, both parties were afforded ample opportunity to present their respective cases, adduce evidence, and make legal submissions before this Tribunal.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, operating a chemical manufacturing facility, has consistently failed to comply with the NEQS for industrial effluent discharge and gaseous emissions. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted on 10.11.2017 and 05.01.2018, the EPA monitoring teams found the Respondent's effluent discharge into the nearby canal to contain levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding the permissible limits. Furthermore, air emission samples from the Respondent's stacks showed elevated concentrations of Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) and Particulate Matter (PM10), indicating inadequate treatment and filtration systems. The Complainant asserted that these persistent violations posed a serious threat to aquatic life, contaminated agricultural land irrigated by the canal water, and adversely affected the health of local communities residing in the vicinity. A show cause notice was issued to the Respondent on 20.01.2018, demanding immediate remedial action and explanation for non-compliance, to which the Respondent's reply was deemed unsatisfactory, leading to the filing of the present complaint. The Complainant sought not only a penalty but also directions for the Respondent to install and operate appropriate pollution control devices.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports dated 10.11.2017 and 05.01.2018, prepared by EPA field teams, clearly documenting the observations of non-compliance. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA-accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/Lab/Eff/11-17/023 and EPA/Lab/Em/01-18/005, were submitted, which conclusively showed the effluent parameters and air emission levels to be in excess of the NEQS. The reports provided precise numerical values for COD, BOD, TSS, pH, SO2, and PM10, highlighting the extent of the deviation. The Complainant also produced copies of the show cause notice issued to M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited on 20.01.2018, along with the Respondent's inadequate reply dated 29.01.2018. The testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Officer, EPA, who led both inspection teams, corroborated the documentary evidence, affirming the findings and the Respondent's continued non-compliance despite prior warnings. The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, in its defence, initially denied the allegations, arguing that its existing effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices were functional and regularly maintained. They submitted maintenance records and internal monitoring reports, which, however, lacked independent verification. Later, during the proceedings, the Respondent admitted to certain operational challenges leading to sporadic non-compliance but asserted that corrective measures were being implemented, including the procurement of an upgraded filtration system and a new wastewater treatment module. They presented a project plan for the upgrade, requesting time for its implementation.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). Specifically, Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level which is in excess of the NEQS. Section 16 mandates Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for new projects and significant modifications, although not directly punitive in this case, it underscores the preventive aspects of environmental law. Section 20 empowers the Environmental Tribunal to hear complaints and pass orders, including the imposition of penalties. Section 23 of PEPA, 1997, prescribes penalties for violations of Section 11, stating that whoever contravenes the provisions of Section 11 shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with a further fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues. Furthermore, the National Environmental Quality Standards
(NEQS) are notified by the Federal Government under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and are legally binding standards that all industries must adhere to. The primary objective of these legal provisions is to prevent pollution, protect public health, and conserve the environment.
Having meticulously considered the pleadings, evidence, and legal submissions presented by both parties, this Tribunal finds the Complainant has overwhelmingly discharged its burden of proof. The inspection reports dated 10.11.2017 and 05.01.2018, coupled with the conclusive laboratory analysis reports (EPA/Lab/Eff/11-17/023 and EPA/Lab/Em/01-18/005), unequivocally demonstrate that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited was, on the dates specified, discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS for COD, BOD, TSS, pH, SO2, and PM10. The numerical values provided in the laboratory reports leave no room for doubt regarding the severity and extent of the violations. The testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Officer, further solidified these findings, confirming the authenticity of the inspection process and the persistent nature of the non-compliance.
While the Respondent initially denied the allegations, its subsequent admission of "certain operational challenges leading to sporadic non-compliance" serves as an implicit acknowledgment of the violations. The submission of maintenance records and internal monitoring reports by the Respondent, without independent verification, does not sufficiently rebut the strong, independently verified evidence presented by the Complainant. Furthermore, the Respondent's proposal for an upgrade project, while commendable as a future step, does not absolve it of liability for past and present contraventions of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997. The law requires continuous compliance, not merely a future promise to comply. The show cause notice issued on 20.01.2018 and the Respondent's unsatisfactory reply further underscore the Respondent's failure to take timely and effective remedial action.
Therefore, this Tribunal concludes that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has contravened the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging untreated industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards on multiple occasions, specifically on 10.11.2017 and 05.01.2018, and has failed to demonstrate continuous compliance thereafter. Such persistent violations pose a significant threat to the environment and public health, warranting immediate and stringent action by this Tribunal.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is found to be in contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Imposition of Penalty:** Pursuant to Section 23(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby directed to pay a cumulative fine of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)**. This penalty comprises a fine of PKR 1,000,000/- for the initial contravention and an additional fine of PKR 500,000/- for the continuing nature of the contravention as evidenced by the second inspection and the Respondent's own admission. The said fine shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Remedial Directions for Effluent Treatment:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, shall forthwith take all necessary measures to ensure that its industrial effluent discharge strictly adheres to the National Environmental Quality Standards. To this end, the Respondent is directed to:
    *   Immediately upgrade its existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or install a new, fully functional ETP capable of treating all industrial wastewater to meet NEQS, within a period of **six (6) months** from the date of this order.
    *   Submit a detailed implementation plan for the ETP upgrade, including timelines and milestones, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order for its review and approval.
    *   Ensure continuous operation and maintenance of the ETP to guarantee consistent compliance.
4.  **Remedial Directions for Air Emissions:** The Respondent shall forthwith take all necessary measures to ensure that its air emissions strictly adhere to the National Environmental Quality Standards. To this end, the Respondent is directed to:
    *   Immediately upgrade its existing air pollution control devices or install new, efficient systems capable of treating gaseous emissions to meet NEQS, within a period of **six (6) months** from the date of this order.
    *   Submit a detailed implementation plan for the air pollution control upgrade, including timelines and milestones, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order for its review and approval.
    *   Ensure continuous operation and maintenance of the air pollution control systems to guarantee consistent compliance.
5.  **Monitoring and Reporting:**
    *   The Respondent shall conduct independent third-party monitoring of its effluent discharge and air emissions on a monthly basis and submit the certified laboratory reports to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, for a period of **two (2) years** following the completion of the upgrades.
    *   The Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, is directed to conduct quarterly inspections of M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for a period of **two (2) years** to verify compliance with this order and the NEQS.
6.  **Continuing Contravention Clause:** In the event of any further contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, after the expiry of the six-month period for upgrades, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited shall be liable to a further fine of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** for every day during which such contravention continues, in addition to any other legal action deemed appropriate by the EPA or this Tribunal.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
8.  **Compliance Report:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, shall submit a compliance report to this Tribunal on the progress of the Respondent's implementation of this order every three months until full compliance is achieved and verified.
