This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 74/2017, initiated by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), invoking the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17(2) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act").
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, operating a textile manufacturing unit, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment, specifically into the local drainage system connected to agricultural lands and a nearby canal, thereby violating the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under Section 12 of the Act and read with S.R.O. 549(I)/2000. It was contended that despite repeated notices and the issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of the Act, requiring the Respondent to install or upgrade its effluent treatment plant and comply with NEQS, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures. Samples of the effluent collected from the Respondent's premises on various dates, including 15.03.2017 and 28.06.
2017, by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) laboratories, consistently revealed parameters exceeding the prescribed NEQS, particularly concerning Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. These repeated violations, it was argued, demonstrated a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and posed a significant threat to public health and the ecological balance of the affected area.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its counsel, contested the allegations. While acknowledging the operation of a textile unit, it was contended that the Respondent had made substantial investments in an effluent treatment plant (ETP) and was continuously striving to achieve compliance with NEQS. The Respondent questioned the methodology of sample collection and analysis, suggesting potential procedural irregularities or contamination. It was further argued that the Respondent was a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship and had taken various measures to minimize its environmental footprint, including exploring advanced treatment technologies. The Respondent sought dismissal of the complaint, asserting that it was not in willful violation of the Act and was actively working towards full compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented by both parties, including the complaint, notices, EPOs, effluent analysis reports, and the Respondent's replies and technical reports. Oral testimonies were also heard from EPA officials involved in sample collection and analysis, as well as representatives from the Respondent's management and technical staff. The core issue before the Tribunal was to ascertain whether the Respondent was indeed discharging effluent in violation of NEQS and whether it had taken adequate and timely remedial measures as mandated by the Act and the EPOs issued thereunder.
Upon careful consideration of the evidence and arguments, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, through a series of consistent and corroborated laboratory reports, that the effluent discharged by the Respondent's textile manufacturing unit on multiple occasions, including the dates specified, failed to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards as prescribed under Section 12 of the Act. The repeated nature of the violations, despite the issuance of notices and an Environmental Protection Order, indicates a failure on the part of the Respondent to implement effective and sustainable measures to treat its industrial discharge to the required standards. While the Respondent's efforts towards environmental compliance are noted, these efforts have demonstrably not yet resulted in consistent adherence to NEQS, thereby causing environmental degradation. The procedural objections raised by the Respondent regarding sample collection were not substantiated with credible counter-evidence sufficient to invalidate the EPA's findings. The Tribunal therefore concludes that the Respondent is in violation of Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby declared to be in violation of Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Mandatory Compliance Order:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is directed to forthwith upgrade, improve, and/or operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged effluent consistently meets the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) in letter and spirit.
3.  **Timeframe for Compliance:** The Respondent shall achieve full and consistent compliance with NEQS within a period of **six (6) months** from the date of this judgment. The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including timelines and milestones for ETP upgrade/improvement, to the Complainant within **thirty (30) days** of this judgment.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** During the compliance period, the Respondent shall provide monthly progress reports to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), detailing the measures taken and the results of self-monitoring of its effluent. The EPA shall conduct unannounced inspections and collect samples for independent analysis at least once every month during this period to verify compliance.
5.  **Imposition of Penalty:** In accordance with Section 17(3) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million Only)** is hereby imposed upon M/s Nishat Mills Limited for the violations established. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment.
6.  **Continuing Offence Clause:** It is further ordered that if the Respondent fails to achieve full compliance with NEQS within the stipulated six (6) months, or if subsequent monitoring reveals continued non-compliance, the Respondent shall be liable to a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for each day the violation continues, in addition to any other legal action the EPA may deem fit under the Act.
7.  **Cost of Proceedings:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)**, to be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days.
8.  **Disposal of Complaint:** The complaint stands disposed of in the above terms.
