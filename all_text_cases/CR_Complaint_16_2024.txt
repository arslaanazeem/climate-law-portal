**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 16/2024**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ghani Glass Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal**
This Tribunal was seized of the present complaint, filed on 15.01.2024, by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated at 15-KM, Sheikhupura Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), specifically pertaining to industrial emissions and effluent discharge.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, has been operating in a manner detrimental to the environment and public health. It was alleged that the Respondent consistently discharges untreated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drains, leading to water pollution, and emits air pollutants from its industrial processes without adequate treatment, thereby exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, the complaint detailed allegations of: (a) discharge of liquid effluent with pH levels, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) far exceeding the NEQS limits; (b) emission of particulate matter (PM) and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) from its stacks beyond the permissible NEQS, particularly from its glass melting furnaces; and (c) improper storage and disposal of hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste, creating potential for soil and groundwater contamination. The Complainant further stated that multiple warning notices, including a Show Cause Notice dated 05.12.2023, had been issued to the Respondent, requiring corrective measures, but these had gone unheeded, necessitating the filing of this formal complaint before the Tribunal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documents and witness testimonies. Key among these were the inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on 15.10.2023 and 28.11.2023, detailing observations of dark plumes from stacks and visible discharge of coloured effluent. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA Laboratory, dated 20.10.2023 and 02.12.2023, were submitted. These reports confirmed that samples collected from the Respondent's effluent discharge point showed pH levels of 4.5 (NEQS limit 6-9), COD of 450 mg/L (NEQS limit 150 mg/L), BOD5 of 220 mg/L (NEQS limit 80 mg/L), and TSS of 300 mg/L (NEQS limit 200 mg/L). Similarly, stack emission analyses revealed PM levels of 650 mg/Nm³ (NEQS limit 500 mg/Nm³) and NOx levels of 1200 mg/Nm³ (NEQS limit 800 mg/Nm³ for industrial furnaces). Photographic evidence depicting the colour of the effluent and the smoke plumes was also submitted. Mr. Usman Ali, Assistant Director (Technical) EPA, appeared as a witness, confirming the inspection findings and the authenticity of the laboratory reports.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its Legal Counsel, denied the allegations, asserting that it operates with due regard for environmental laws. The Respondent contended that it had installed an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (scrubbers/bag filters) and was continuously striving to improve its environmental performance. They presented a report from a private consultant firm,
asserting that their emissions and effluent parameters were generally within permissible limits, or that efforts were underway to bring them into compliance. They also submitted invoices for the purchase of chemicals used in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and maintenance records for their air pollution control equipment, arguing these demonstrated a commitment to environmental protection. Mr. Asif Mehmood, General Manager Operations for M/s Ghani Glass Limited, appeared as a witness, reiterating the company's commitment and explaining the operational challenges faced by the industry. He contended that the EPA's sampling methods might have been flawed or that the reported exceedances were temporary aberrations rather than consistent violations.
The Tribunal carefully considered the arguments put forth by both parties and meticulously examined the evidence presented. It is evident that the Complainant has provided concrete and verifiable evidence, including certified laboratory reports from the EPA's own laboratory, which is mandated and equipped for such analyses under the PEPA, 1997. These reports consistently indicate significant exceedances of the NEQS for various parameters in both liquid effluent and air emissions. The visual observations documented in the inspection reports and corroborated by photographic evidence further strengthen the Complainant's case. The testimony of Mr. Usman Ali, Assistant Director (Technical) EPA, lent credibility to these findings.
Conversely, while the Respondent asserted a general commitment to environmental compliance and presented a report from a private consultant, the said report lacked the specific detail, methodology, and independent verification required to counter the definitive findings of the EPA's certified laboratory. The Respondent's explanation of temporary aberrations or flawed sampling methods remained unsubstantiated by specific counter-evidence. The invoices for
chemicals used in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and maintenance records for their air pollution control equipment, while indicative of some effort, do not negate the objective scientific data presented by the Complainant. These records do not demonstrate that the ETP and air pollution control devices were operating effectively or optimally at the time of the alleged violations to bring the emissions and effluent parameters within the prescribed NEQS. The testimony of Mr. Asif Mehmood, General Manager Operations, focused on general commitment and operational challenges but failed to provide specific technical data or independent analysis to refute the EPA's findings.
Based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has been operating in violation of Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed thereunder. The consistent exceedances of NEQS for pH, COD, BOD5, TSS in effluent, and PM, NOx in air emissions, as confirmed by certified laboratory reports, constitute a clear breach of environmental regulations. The improper storage and disposal of solid waste further exacerbates the environmental impact. The Respondent's failure to take effective corrective measures despite repeated notices from the EPA demonstrates a disregard for environmental protection laws.
Therefore, this Tribunal, in exercise of the powers vested under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, hereby issues the following final order:
**ORDER:**
1.  The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is found **guilty** of violating the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial emissions and effluent discharge.
2.  The Respondent shall immediately take all necessary steps to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the efficient operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (scrubbers/bag filters) to consistently meet all parameters of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for liquid effluent and air emissions.
3.  The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this order, submit to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) a comprehensive compliance report, including:
    *   Detailed plans for the upgrade and optimization of its ETP and air pollution control devices.
    *   Latest laboratory analysis reports from an EPA-certified independent laboratory confirming compliance with NEQS for all relevant parameters of effluent and air emissions.
    *   A robust waste management plan for hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste, ensuring proper storage, treatment, and disposal in an environmentally sound manner.
4.  The Respondent shall, for a period of one year, submit quarterly monitoring reports, verified by an EPA-certified independent laboratory, to the EPA, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS.
5.  **PENALTY:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ghani Glass Limited for the violations established. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
6.  In case of continued non-compliance with any part of this order after the expiry of the sixty (60) days compliance period mentioned in paragraph 3, the Respondent shall be liable to a further daily penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)** for each day the violation continues, until full compliance is achieved, in addition to any other legal action deemed appropriate by the Tribunal under the PEPA, 1997.
7.  The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, shall conduct periodic inspections and monitoring to ensure strict compliance with this order and shall submit compliance reports to this Tribunal as and when required.
8.  Costs of this complaint are awarded to the Complainant.
Pronounced in open court this **28th day of May, 2024**.
