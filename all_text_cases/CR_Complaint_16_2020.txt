This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, located within the territorial jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint, registered as No. 16/2020, was lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), alleging various contraventions of environmental laws and standards, thereby causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and the environment. This Tribunal was seized of the matter to adjudicate upon the environmental violations and to ensure compliance with the prevailing environmental regime.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited are multifaceted. It is averred that the Respondent unit has consistently been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and/or adjacent water bodies, thereby exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under the Act. Specifically, the complaint detailed multiple instances of non-compliance, citing chemical oxygen demand (COD), biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), total suspended solids (TSS), and pH levels far surpassing the permissible limits. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), despite its existence, was either not fully operational, improperly maintained, or insufficient to treat the volume and type of waste generated. The Complainant also asserted that the Respondent had failed to manage its hazardous waste streams in an environmentally sound manner, lacking proper storage, segregation, and disposal mechanisms. Repeated notices and warnings issued by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to the Respondent, directing them to rectify these deficiencies and comply with environmental laws, were allegedly disregarded, necessitating the filing of the instant complaint before this Tribunal. The cumulative effect of these alleged actions was stated to be environmental degradation, contamination of water resources, and potential adverse impacts on human health and aquatic life.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a robust array of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on various dates between 2019 and 2020, detailing observations of the Respondent's premises and ETP operations. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from EPA-accredited laboratories were submitted, correlating with the inspection dates, which consistently demonstrated that effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points exceeded the NEQS for several critical parameters. Specific reports dated 15.03.2019, 22.08.2019, and 10.01.2020 were highlighted, showing significant exceedances. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices, environmental protection orders (EPOs), and warning letters
issued to the Respondent were also placed on record, demonstrating a persistent pattern of non-compliance despite formal communications. These documents collectively painted a picture of recurrent environmental violations and a failure on the Respondent's part to address the concerns raised by the regulatory authority over an extended period.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently contested the allegations. While acknowledging the existence of an industrial unit and an Effluent Treatment Plant, it was argued that the ETP was fully functional and maintained as per environmental standards. The Respondent asserted that any occasional
exceedances of NEQS were minor and transient, attributable to unforeseen operational fluctuations or external factors beyond their immediate control, rather than a systemic failure of their ETP. It was further contended that the sampling methodology adopted by the EPA officials was not always in strict adherence to prescribed protocols, potentially leading to skewed results. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
