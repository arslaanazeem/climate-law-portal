**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 21/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, alleging severe violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, constituted under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to reach a just and equitable decision.
The Complainant, representing the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, initiated these proceedings on October 25, 2021, asserting that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore, has been operating in blatant disregard of environmental regulations. Specifically, the allegations centered on the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into public sewers and ultimately into natural water bodies, thereby causing significant water pollution and environmental degradation. The EPA, as the primary regulatory body, sought a declaration of the Respondent's non-compliance, imposition of penalties, and directives for immediate remedial action to protect public health and the environment.
The core allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited were multifaceted. Firstly, it was contended that the Respondent's industrial operations consistently discharged effluent exceeding the permissible National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters, including but not limited to Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and several heavy metals. Secondly, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if present, was either non-functional, improperly maintained, or insufficient to treat the volume and characteristics of the effluent generated. Thirdly, the complaint highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to obtain or adhere to the conditions of an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval, as mandated by Section 12 of the Act. Lastly, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent's activities constituted a public nuisance and posed a direct threat to the ecosystem and the health of communities residing downstream from the discharge points.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between April 2021 and September 2021. These reports consistently documented observations of dark-colored, foul-smelling effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected by EPA teams on May 10, 2021, July 15, 2021, and September 02, 2021. These reports, prepared by accredited laboratories, unequivocally indicated that the effluent parameters, particularly BOD, COD, and TSS, were several times higher than the prescribed NEQS. Photographic evidence depicting the discharge points and the visibly polluted receiving environment was also tendered. Furthermore, notices of violation and show-cause notices issued to the Respondent prior to the filing of
the complaint were also placed on record, demonstrating that the Respondent had been given ample opportunity to rectify the violations but failed to do so.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was argued that the company operates a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and consistently strives to comply with environmental regulations. The Respondent contended that the samples collected by the EPA might not have been representative of their routine discharge, or that the analytical methods employed were potentially flawed. They also presented internal monitoring reports, asserting that their effluent parameters were generally within permissible limits. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed to be in the process of applying for or updating necessary environmental approvals, asserting that any alleged non-compliance was minor, temporary, and promptly addressed. They also questioned the severity of the alleged environmental impact, suggesting it was exaggerated.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary and oral evidence presented by both parties. It noted that the Complainant's laboratory analysis reports, originating from accredited laboratories and conducted on multiple dates, consistently demonstrated significant exceedances of the NEQS across various critical parameters. These findings were corroborated by the visual observations and photographic evidence of pollution. The repeated issuance of notices of violation by the EPA prior to the complaint further underscored a pattern of persistent non-compliance that the Respondent failed to adequately address. While the Respondent presented internal monitoring reports, these lacked independent verification and were directly contradicted by the official EPA findings, which the Tribunal found more credible and reliable. The Respondent's arguments regarding flawed sampling or analysis by the EPA were not substantiated with any convincing counter-evidence. The Tribunal found that the presence of an ETP, even if claimed, was clearly insufficient or improperly operated, as evidenced by the persistent discharge of effluent far exceeding the prescribed standards. The failure to secure or maintain an EIA/IEE approval, as mandated by law for an industrial unit of this scale, was also a serious and undisputed omission.
Based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been and continues to be in flagrant violation of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 12, and 16, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. The discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent has caused significant water pollution, posing a direct threat to the environment and public health. The Respondent's disregard for environmental laws, despite repeated warnings, necessitates strict remedial measures and penalties.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following final order and penalty:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby declared to be in violation of Sections 11, 12, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Immediate Cessation of Discharge:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, shall immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into public sewers, natural water bodies, or any other receiving environment that does not meet the prescribed NEQS.
3.  **ETP Upgrade and Compliance:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days** from the date of this judgment, upgrade and fully operationalize its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged effluent consistently meets the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). **FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
