**Introduction**
1.  This complaint, bearing No. 31/2024, has been filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, hereinafter referred to as the "Complainant," against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, situated at 22-KM Ferozepur Road, Lahore, hereinafter referred to as the "Respondent." The complaint seeks the intervention of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations concerning industrial emissions, effluent discharge, and hazardous waste management by the Respondent's glass manufacturing facility. The Complainant contends that the Respondent's operations are causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem, necessitating immediate remedial action and imposition of penalties as prescribed by law.
**Allegations**
2.  The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited are multifaceted, stemming from multiple inspections and monitoring activities conducted by the EPA. Specifically, the Complainant alleges:
    a.  **Air Pollution:** The Respondent's glass melting furnaces and associated processes are emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulfur dioxide (SO2), significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions. It is alleged that the Respondent has failed to install adequate air pollution control devices or maintain existing ones effectively.
    b.  **Water Pollution:** The industrial wastewater discharged from the Respondent's facility, particularly from raw material washing, cullet processing, and cooling operations, is being released without adequate treatment. Laboratory analyses of samples collected on various dates (e.g., November 15, 2023, and January 10, 2024) consistently indicate high levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), elevated pH, and the presence of heavy metals such as lead and chromium, all in excess of the NEQS for industrial effluents.
    c.  **Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent is allegedly failing to manage and dispose of hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing process, including furnace refractory waste, dust collector sludge, and broken glass cullet contaminated with process chemicals, in an environmentally sound manner
d. **Non-compliance with Environmental Approval Conditions:** The Respondent has allegedly failed to adhere to the conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval (if any), or has commenced/expanded operations without obtaining the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and its associated regulations. This systemic failure to
obtain/update environmental approvals, a further penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)** shall be levied for each day of continued non-compliance until full compliance is achieved. The EPA is authorized to initiate further proceedings for the recovery of such daily fines.
    g.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings,

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,800,000/- (Rupees Three Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 760,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
