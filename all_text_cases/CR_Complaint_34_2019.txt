This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated at 20-KM, Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint, lodged on May 15, 2019, alleges persistent violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), causing significant air and land pollution in the vicinity of the Respondent's industrial operations. The core issue before this Tribunal is to ascertain whether the Respondent has indeed contravened environmental laws and, if so, to issue appropriate directions and penalties.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited are multifaceted. Firstly, it is contended that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly its glass melting furnaces, are emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SOx), far in excess of the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous
emissions. Secondly, the complaint highlights the alleged improper disposal of solid waste, including broken glass cullet and refractory materials, which are reportedly left exposed, leading to land degradation and potential contamination of groundwater. Furthermore, it is contended that the Respondent has failed to implement adequate measures for the control of fugitive emissions from its raw material handling and storage areas, contributing significantly to ambient air pollution. The EPA also asserts that
the Respondent has, despite repeated warnings and issuance of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, failed to take effective remedial measures to abate the pollution. The Complainant further highlights the absence of a robust Environmental Management System (EMS) within the Respondent's operations, leading to a continuous cycle of non-compliance and environmental degradation, thereby posing a direct threat to public health and the local ecosystem.
In response, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it operates with due regard for environmental regulations. The Respondent contended that it has invested significantly in modern pollution control technologies, including bag filter systems for particulate matter control and improved waste handling procedures. It was argued that occasional exceedances, if any, were transient and attributable to operational exigencies or external factors beyond their control, and that they are actively working towards achieving full compliance. The Respondent also presented data purporting to show compliance with certain parameters and outlined future plans for technological upgrades and environmental stewardship.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the documentary evidence presented by both parties, including environmental monitoring reports submitted by the EPA, the Respondent's operational data, and expert opinions. Site inspections were also conducted to verify the ground realities. After careful consideration of the arguments advanced and the evidence on record, it became unequivocally clear that while the Respondent has made some efforts, these have been insufficient to consistently meet the NEQS. The monitoring reports consistently indicated exceedances of PM, NOx, and SOx emissions, particularly from the glass melting furnaces. Furthermore, the evidence regarding the improper disposal of solid waste and the lack of effective fugitive emission controls was substantiated during site visits and corroborated by photographic evidence.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
