This complaint, filed under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, seeks action against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Complainant asserts that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, has consistently failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and gaseous emissions, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health.
The Complainant asserts that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, has consistently failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and gaseous emissions, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health.
The Complainant presented a series of monitoring reports, laboratory analyses, and inspection findings, spanning several years, which unequivocally demonstrated that the Respondent's discharge of industrial effluents into the nearby water bodies and its gaseous emissions from stacks consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS parameters for various pollutants, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and particulate matter. Despite repeated notices, warnings, and opportunities to rectify the deficiencies, the Respondent allegedly failed to implement adequate treatment measures or upgrade its pollution control infrastructure.
The Respondent, through its counsel, initially contested the accuracy of the monitoring reports and asserted that it had undertaken efforts to comply with environmental standards. However, during the course of proceedings, the Respondent largely failed to present compelling counter-evidence or demonstrate substantial progress in addressing the identified violations. Expert testimony presented by the EPA further corroborated the findings of non-compliance and highlighted the potential long-term ecological damage and health risks associated with the Respondent's operations.
This Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, heard arguments from both sides, and considered the reports of technical experts. It was established that the Respondent had been issued multiple environmental protection orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of the Act, which were subsequently disregarded or inadequately addressed, leading to the filing of this complaint under Section 17(1).
Upon careful consideration of the entire record, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has indeed violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, specifically Section 12 thereof, by discharging effluents and emissions exceeding the NEQS, and has failed to comply with the directions issued by the EPA. The persistent non-compliance demonstrates a serious dereliction of environmental responsibility and a disregard for the well-being of the surrounding community and ecosystem.
In light of the proven violations and the continuous nature of the environmental harm, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17(2) and Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is directed to immediately cease all discharges of industrial effluents and gaseous emissions that exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Implementation of Treatment Plan:** The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order, submit a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP) and a detailed proposal for upgrading its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to achieve full compliance with NEQS. This plan shall be subject to review and approval by the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab.
3.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for its persistent violations of environmental laws and regulations. This amount shall be paid into the Environmental Protection Fund within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
4.  **Daily Fine:** Should the Respondent fail to achieve NEQS compliance within six (6) months from the approval of its EMP, a further daily fine of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)**
shall be imposed for each day of continued non-compliance thereafter until full compliance is achieved and certified by the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab. This daily fine shall be in addition to any other penalties or actions that may be deemed appropriate by this Tribunal or the EPA under the Act.
5.  **Compliance Monitoring and Verification:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, is directed to conduct regular and unannounced inspections of the Respondent's premises, collect samples of effluents and emissions, and submit quarterly compliance reports to this Tribunal for a period of two (2) years or until sustained compliance is demonstrated to the satisfaction of the Tribunal.
6.  **Respondent's Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly progress reports, including certified laboratory analysis results of its treated effluents and emissions, to the EPA and a copy to this Tribunal, commencing from the date of submission of the EMP until full compliance is verified.
7.  **Further Legal Action:** In the event of continued non-compliance with any of the directions contained in this order, the Tribunal reserves the right to initiate further punitive measures, including but not limited to, the sealing of the industrial unit, revocation of environmental approvals, and/or the initiation of criminal proceedings against the management of M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited under the relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
8.  **Costs:** The costs of these proceedings shall be borne by the Respondent.
Let a copy of this order be sent to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, and M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for strict compliance.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
