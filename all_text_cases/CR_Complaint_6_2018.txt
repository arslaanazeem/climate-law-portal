Presided by: Shazib Saeed
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, under Section 21 read with Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeks the prosecution and imposition of penalties against M/s Nishat Mills Limited for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, duly constituted under the Act, holds jurisdiction to adjudicate upon such matters and enforce compliance with environmental regulations. The proceedings herein have been conducted in accordance with the prescribed rules and principles of natural justice, providing both parties ample opportunity to present their respective cases.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, was found to be discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system without adequate treatment. Specifically, it was averred that an inspection conducted by EPA officials on 15th January 2018 revealed the discharge of highly colored and foul-smelling effluent. Samples of this effluent were collected from the discharge point leading into the metropolitan sewerage network. Subsequent laboratory analysis of these samples, conducted by the EPA Environmental Laboratory, indicated that the discharged effluent exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for several key parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and Chromium. The Complainant contended that this continuous discharge constituted a clear violation of Section 11 of the Act, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in excess of the NEQS. It was further asserted that despite repeated notices and directives from the EPA, including a Show Cause Notice dated 25th January 2018, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures to bring its effluent discharge within the permissible limits.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the Inspection Report dated 15th January 2018, prepared by EPA officials, detailing the observations made during the site
visit, along with photographic evidence of the colored effluent being discharged. The laboratory analysis report, dated 20th January 2018, duly signed by the authorized chemist of the EPA Environmental Laboratory, was also submitted. This report unequivocally confirmed that the levels of BOD (250 mg/L against NEQS of 80 mg/L), COD (600 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L), TSS (300 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and Chromium (1.5 mg/L against NEQS of 1.0 mg/L) in the collected samples significantly exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards. The Complainant further placed on record copies of the Show Cause Notice dated 25th January 2018, issued under Section 16 of the Act, requiring the Respondent to explain why an Environmental Protection Order should not be issued and penalties imposed. It was highlighted that despite receiving this notice, the Respondent failed to provide a satisfactory response or demonstrate any concrete steps towards rectifying the violations within the stipulated time. The Complainant, therefore, sought the imposition of the maximum penalty as prescribed under Section 17 of the Act, along with an order for the Respondent to forthwith cease the discharge of untreated effluent and install an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure compliance with NEQS.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its authorized representative and legal counsel, denied the allegations. It was contended that the sampling conducted by the EPA officials was not representative and that the facility generally operates in compliance with environmental standards. The Respondent asserted that they have an existing primary treatment system in place and that any observed exceedance of NEQS might have been an isolated incident, possibly due to a temporary malfunction or an unusual surge in production, which was immediately addressed. They questioned the accuracy of the laboratory analysis report, suggesting potential flaws in the sample collection, preservation, or analytical methodology, though no independent laboratory report was submitted to counter the EPA's findings. The Respondent further argued that they are a responsible corporate entity with a strong commitment to environmental stewardship and have invested substantially in upgrading their processes to minimize environmental impact. They requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, or in the alternative, to issue a directive for joint re-sampling and analysis by an independent third-party laboratory.
The Tribunal meticulously considered the arguments advanced by both parties and thoroughly examined the documentary evidence presented. The Inspection Report, corroborated by photographic evidence, clearly established the physical observation of highly colored and foul-smelling effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises directly into the public sewerage system. More critically, the laboratory analysis report from the EPA Environmental Laboratory, a designated and accredited facility, provided objective scientific evidence of the NEQS violations across multiple key parameters. The Respondent's general denial of the accuracy of the sampling and analysis, without presenting any credible counter-evidence or an independent expert report, fails to rebut the strong prima facie
evidence presented by the Complainant. The Respondent's unsubstantiated claims regarding the non-representative nature of the samples or alleged flaws in the analytical methodology, without producing any concrete evidence to support these assertions, are unconvincing and cannot outweigh the credible and documented findings of the EPA. The EPA Environmental Laboratory is an authorized body, and its reports carry significant weight, particularly when unchallenged by independent, scientifically sound counter-reports. The Tribunal finds no reason to doubt the integrity or accuracy of the sampling and analysis conducted by the EPA officials. The argument of an "isolated incident" or "temporary malfunction" is also not supported by any evidence of immediate corrective action or subsequent compliance, especially in light of the repeated notices issued by the EPA.
Therefore, based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal is satisfied that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has indeed violated the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system. The Respondent's failure to take effective remedial measures despite receiving official notices further aggravates the nature of the contravention.
In view of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers conferred upon it under Sections 16, 17, and 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal hereby proceeds to pass the following order:
**I. PENALTY:**
M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby found guilty of contravening Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and is ordered to pay a penalty of PKR 750,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand Only) into the Government Treasury within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
**II. ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION ORDER (EPO):**
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is directed to:
a) Immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent that exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system or any other water body.
b) Within sixty (60) days from the date of this order, submit to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, a comprehensive plan for the installation/upgradation of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of consistently treating its industrial effluent to meet all prescribed NEQS parameters. The plan must include a detailed timeline for its implementation.
c) Ensure the full installation and operationalization of the approved ETP within six (6) months from the date of approval of the plan by the EPA. During this period, the Respondent shall adopt interim measures, to the satisfaction of the EPA, to minimize pollution.
d) Submit monthly monitoring reports of its treated effluent, duly certified by an EPA-approved laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency for a period of one year, commencing from the date of operationalization of the ETP, to ensure sustained compliance.
**III. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE:**
Failure to comply

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
