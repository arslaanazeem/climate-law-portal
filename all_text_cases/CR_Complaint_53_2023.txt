**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed
by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging contravention of various provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
**1. Background of the Complaint:**
The Complainant, through its inspection team, conducted a routine monitoring visit to the Respondent's industrial unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore on 15th
February, 2023. During the inspection, it was observed that the Respondent's industrial unit was discharging untreated industrial effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system, containing pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters, including pH, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS). Furthermore, the unit appeared to lack an adequately functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or was operating it inefficiently. The inspection team also noted potential air emissions without proper air pollution control measures. A show-cause notice was subsequently issued to the Respondent, seeking an explanation for these violations, but the response received was deemed unsatisfactory by the Complainant, leading to the filing of this complaint.
**2. Allegations and Evidence:**
The Complainant alleged violations of Section 12, 13, and 16 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with various NEQS parameters specified in SRO 779(I)/93 dated 24th August 1993, as amended. The primary evidence presented included the inspection report dated 15.02.2023, photographic evidence, and laboratory analysis reports of the effluent samples collected, which unequivocally confirmed the exceedance of NEQS limits.
**3. Respondent's Defense:**
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, appeared through its counsel and submitted a written reply, primarily contending that they had an operational ETP and were making efforts to comply. However, they failed to produce concrete evidence of consistent compliance or effective operation of their ETP during the period in question, nor did they adequately rebut the laboratory findings presented by the Complainant.
**4. Findings of the Tribunal:**
After careful consideration of the complaint, the evidence presented by the Complainant, and the Respondent's submissions, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the allegations of environmental pollution against M/s Nishat Mills Limited. The laboratory reports conclusively demonstrate that the effluent discharged by the Respondent exceeded the prescribed NEQS for multiple parameters, constituting a clear violation of Section 12 of the Act. The Respondent's failure to maintain and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant, leading to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent, is a serious environmental offense. The Tribunal also notes the Respondent's inability to provide satisfactory evidence of compliance or effective remedial measures taken subsequent to the inspection.
**5. Order and Penalty:**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 19(2) and 20 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following final order:
*   **a. Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is found guilty of violating Section 12 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997,

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **21 May, 2023**.
