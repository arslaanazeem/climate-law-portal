The present complaint, bearing No. 30/2024, has been lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of glass products. The Complainant seeks the intervention of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), alleging persistent violations of environmental standards and regulations by the Respondent, which pose a significant threat to public health and the environment within the vicinity of its operational facility located in District Sheikhupura.
The Complainant's case rests on the assertion that the Respondent has been operating its industrial processes without due regard for the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other statutory requirements. Specifically, the following allegations were leveled against M/s Ghani Glass Limited:
1.  **Exceedance of Air Emission Standards**: It is alleged that the Respondent's industrial stacks are continuously discharging gaseous emissions containing Particulate Matter (PM), Sulfur Oxides (SOx), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) in concentrations significantly exceeding the NEQS limits for industrial air emissions. This contributes to ambient air pollution, affecting the health of local residents and the surrounding ecosystem.
2.  **Uncontrolled Discharge of Untreated/Partially Treated Effluents**: The Complainant contends that the Respondent is discharging industrial wastewater containing elevated levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and exhibiting an improper pH value, directly into the public drainage system and subsequently into local water bodies, without adequate treatment. This practice allegedly contaminates water resources and endangers aquatic life and human health.
3.  **Inadequate Management of Hazardous Waste**: The Complainant further alleged that the Respondent failed to properly segregate, store, and dispose of hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing processes, leading to environmental contamination and potential exposure risks.
4.  **Failure to Implement Approved Environmental Management Plan (EMP)**: Despite previous communications and directives from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), the Respondent has allegedly failed to effectively implement an approved Environmental Management Plan (EMP) designed to mitigate its environmental footprint.
To substantiate the allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple site inspection reports conducted by EPA teams on various dates between January 2023 and February 2024, detailing observations of visible smoke plumes, effluent discharge points, and waste management practices. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of air emission samples collected from the Respondent's stacks and liquid effluent samples collected from its discharge points. These reports, prepared by accredited laboratories, conclusively demonstrated that the concentrations of PM, SOx, and NOx in air emissions consistently exceeded the NEQS by an average of 150-200%. Similarly, the wastewater analysis revealed TSS levels up to 300 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L), COD levels exceeding 400 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and BOD levels above 100 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), with pH values often outside the permissible range of 6-9. Notices of non-compliance issued by the EPA to the Respondent, along with their acknowledgment and subsequent unsatisfactory responses, were also tendered as evidence. Furthermore, photographic and videographic evidence depicting the alleged violations, including the discharge of discolored effluents and improperly stored waste, was presented.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its legal counsel,
vehemently denied the persistent nature and magnitude of the alleged violations. While acknowledging receipt of some non-compliance notices, the Respondent contended that any exceedances were either sporadic, attributable to specific operational adjustments, or within the margin of error for industrial processes. The Respondent's counsel further raised concerns regarding the chain of custody for some samples and the calibration status of certain testing equipment used by the EPA, attempting
to cast doubt on the reliability of the Complainant's laboratory analysis reports. The Respondent asserted that it had, in fact, undertaken significant measures to upgrade its pollution control infrastructure, including the installation of a new electrostatic precipitator for air emissions and an activated sludge treatment plant for wastewater, albeit these projects were still in various stages of commissioning and optimization. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
