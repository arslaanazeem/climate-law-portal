**JUDGMENT**
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, on 15th July 2020, under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeks redress against M/s
Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. Specifically, the complaint details that during inspections conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) on various dates between January and June 2020, the Respondent's industrial unit was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/water bodies, exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for several parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS), as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Rules, 1997. The complaint further alleged that the Respondent failed to install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and neglected to submit regular environmental monitoring reports, in contravention of its Environmental Approval (NOC) conditions and Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Act.
The Complainant sought a direction from this Tribunal for the Respondent to immediately cease the discharge of untreated effluent, install and operate an effective ETP in compliance with NEQS, pay an environmental penalty as per Section 17(1) of the Act, and compensate for environmental damage caused.
The Respondent, through its counsel, contested the complaint, generally denying the extent of the alleged violations and asserting that it had taken steps to improve its environmental performance. However, the evidence presented by the Complainant, including detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis results from accredited laboratories, and photographic evidence, convincingly demonstrated persistent non-compliance with the NEQS and the provisions of the Act. Despite several notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures within the stipulated timeframes.
Having heard the arguments from both sides, meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, and considered the relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules framed thereunder, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established the Respondent's continuous violation of Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Act, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards. The persistent discharge of untreated industrial effluent constitutes a serious environmental offense, posing a significant threat to public health and the ecosystem.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
