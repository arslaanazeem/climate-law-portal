**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, alleging violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its associated rules and regulations concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions. The Tribunal was seized with the matter to determine the extent of environmental damage, the responsibility of the respondent, and to impose appropriate penalties and directives for compliance.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, brought forth specific allegations against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, located in Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. It was contended that the respondent company, engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, was consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies without adequate treatment. Furthermore, it was alleged that the respondent's industrial operations resulted in significant air emissions, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), which also failed to comply with the prescribed NEQS, thereby contributing to air pollution in the vicinity. The EPA asserted that despite multiple notices and warnings issued to the respondent, including Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) dated 15.05.2019 and 22.08.2019, the company failed to take effective measures to control and mitigate the pollution, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental laws and public health. The Complainant sought the imposition of penalties as stipulated under the PEPA, 1997, and directives for the immediate cessation of non-compliant discharges and emissions.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a robust array of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA field teams on various dates, specifically 01.03.2019, 10.07.2019, and 15.11.2019, which documented the operational status of the respondent’s Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, noting their inadequacy or non-functionality. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the respondent's discharge points on 05.03.2019, 12.07.2019, and 18.11.2019, conducted by EPA-certified laboratories. These reports consistently showed parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly above the NEQS. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports from 07.03.2019 and 14.11.2019, measuring stack emissions, indicated elevated levels of particulate matter and gaseous pollutants. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the untreated discharge and visible emissions from the respondent's premises was also presented. The Complainant further provided copies of the aforementioned Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and the respondent’s responses, or lack thereof, demonstrating a history of non-compliance. The Assistant Director, EPA, also testified in person, detailing the agency's efforts to ensure compliance and the respondent's repeated failures.
In response, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. The Respondent argued that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection and has invested substantially in pollution control infrastructure. They presented evidence of partial upgrades to their ETP and installation of new scrubbers for air emissions, along with invoices and project completion certificates for these improvements. The Respondent contended that some fluctuations in effluent and air quality parameters were unavoidable due to the complex nature of chemical manufacturing processes and occasional operational disruptions, which were being promptly addressed. They also questioned the methodology and timing of the EPA's sampling, suggesting that samples might have been taken during periods of unavoidable plant upset or that the EPA's sampling points were not representative. Furthermore, the Respondent submitted internal monitoring reports, albeit less frequent than required, indicating efforts towards compliance and a commitment to achieving NEQS. A representative from the company's technical department testified, outlining the challenges faced in meeting stringent environmental standards continuously and detailing ongoing efforts to optimize their pollution control systems.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
