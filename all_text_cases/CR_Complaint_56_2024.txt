This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, alleging severe violations of environmental laws, specifically regarding the discharge of untreated industrial effluent. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, presided over by Mr. Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi, has thoroughly examined the submissions and evidence presented by both parties to determine the extent of environmental non-compliance and to issue a just and appropriate order.
The Complainant, the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, brought forth a series of grave allegations against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of Lahore. It was primarily contended that the Respondent company has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system, which eventually leads to the River Ravi, without adequate treatment. The specific allegations included: failure to install or effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating industrial wastewater to NEQS parameters; persistent violation of NEQS for several critical parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH; causing significant water pollution detrimental to aquatic life, public health, and agricultural use of water downstream; and a general disregard for environmental regulations despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA. The Complainant further asserted that the Respondent's actions constitute a direct contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as PEPA, 1997).
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple laboratory analysis reports
...multiple laboratory analysis reports, spanning over a period of two years, consistently indicating pollutant levels (including Biological Oxygen Demand, Chemical Oxygen Demand, Total Suspended Solids, pH, and specific heavy metals) significantly above the prescribed NEQS. These reports were further corroborated by detailed inspection reports from EPA field teams, photographic and video evidence depicting the direct discharge of discolored and foul-smelling effluent into the public sewerage system, which ultimately converges with the River Ravi. The Complainant also presented copies of numerous show-cause notices and warning letters issued to
...numerous show-cause notices and warning letters issued to the Respondent over the past two years, highlighting the persistent nature of the violations and the Respondent's failure to take adequate corrective measures despite repeated official communications. Expert testimony from environmental engineers was also presented, explaining the detrimental impact of such untreated effluent on the receiving water bodies and ecosystems.
In response to these grave accusations, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the extent of the alleged non-compliance. The Respondent contended that while minor deviations from NEQS might have occurred on isolated occasions, these were not persistent or intentional. They argued that an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was indeed operational at their facility, which they claimed was designed to meet NEQS. The Respondent further submitted that any observed non-compliance could be attributed to technical glitches, fluctuations in production processes, or the inherent challenges of treating complex industrial wastewater. They also questioned the methodology and calibration of the EPA's sampling and testing equipment, suggesting that the laboratory reports might not accurately reflect the true quality of their discharged effluent. Furthermore, the Respondent pleaded for leniency, citing their significant contribution to the national economy, employment generation, and ongoing efforts to upgrade their environmental management systems, including plans for a more advanced ETP. They requested additional time to implement further improvements and avoid any punitive action that might severely impact their operations.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the extensive documentary and technical evidence placed on record. Upon a thorough review, the Tribunal finds the evidence presented by the Environmental Protection Agency to be overwhelming, consistent, and credible. The multiple laboratory analysis reports, spanning a considerable period and indicating consistent exceedances of NEQS for critical parameters, leave no room for doubt regarding the persistent nature of the violations. The Respondent's claims of isolated incidents or technical glitches are contradicted by the sheer volume and consistency of the EPA's data. The photographic and video evidence further corroborates the direct discharge of inadequately treated effluent. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's economic contributions, these cannot be prioritized over the fundamental right to a clean and healthy environment, enshrined in the Constitution of Pakistan. The Respondent's assertions regarding the operational ETP are not supported by the analytical results, which clearly demonstrate its inadequacy or ineffective operation in consistently meeting the NEQS. The repeated issuance of show-cause notices and warnings by the EPA underscores the Respondent's continued disregard for environmental regulations despite ample opportunity to rectify the situation.
Therefore, after careful deliberation and evaluation of all presented facts and evidence, the Tribunal is unequivocally convinced that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has indeed been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent, thereby causing significant environmental pollution in direct contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The persistent violation of National Environmental Quality Standards, particularly for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, has been established beyond reasonable doubt. The Tribunal finds the Respondent liable for causing environmental degradation and failing to comply with its statutory obligations under environmental law.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby found to be in persistent violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and has failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for the discharge of industrial effluent.
2.  **Mandatory Installation/Upgradation of ETP:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is directed to immediately undertake the necessary measures to install, upgrade, and fully operationalize an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) that is capable of consistently treating its industrial wastewater to meet all prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) before discharge. This upgradation/installation shall be completed within a strict period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order.
3.  **Submission of Action Plan:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed action plan, including timelines and technical specifications for the ETP upgradation/installation, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **fifteen (15) days** of this order for review and approval.
4.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** Upon completion and operationalization of the upgraded ETP, the Respondent shall submit monthly self-monitoring reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of one year, demonstrating consistent compliance with NEQS. The EPA shall also conduct unannounced inspections and sampling to verify compliance.
5.  **Environmental Penalty:** A penalty of **Rupees Ten Million (PKR 10,000,000/-)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for the persistent violations of environmental laws and regulations. This amount shall be deposited into the Environmental Protection Fund within **thirty (30) days** of the date of this order.
6.  **Daily Penalties for Non-Compliance:** In the event of non-compliance with the directives outlined in paragraphs 2, 3, and 4 of this order, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited shall be liable to a further penalty of **Rupees One Hundred Thousand (PKR 100,000/-)** per day for each day the non-compliance continues, starting from the expiry of the respective deadlines.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, to be determined by the Registrar of the Tribunal.
8.  **Closure Threat:** Failure to comply with the mandatory installation/upgradation of the ETP within the stipulated ninety (90) days, or persistent non-compliance thereafter, may lead to further stringent actions, including but not limited to, the sealing of the industrial unit or specific polluting sections thereof, until full compliance is achieved.
This order is issued to ensure the protection of the environment and public health, and to uphold the sanctity of environmental laws in the Province of Punjab.
