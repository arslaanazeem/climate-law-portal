This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") on October 12, 2017. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"), primarily concerning air emissions and wastewater discharge from the Respondent's industrial facility located in Lahore. The core issue before this Tribunal is to determine whether the Respondent has engaged in activities causing environmental pollution in contravention of the applicable laws and standards, and if so, to impose appropriate penalties and remedial measures.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, has been operating in persistent violation of environmental laws and standards. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Exceedance of Air Emission Standards:** That the Respondent's industrial facility consistently discharged air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), in concentrations exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. It was contended that despite repeated warnings and notices, the Respondent failed to install or adequately maintain appropriate air pollution control devices, particularly for its furnace stacks.
2.  **Improper Wastewater Discharge:** That the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system, eventually leading to contamination of natural water bodies. The Complainant asserted that the discharged effluent contained parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) far beyond the NEQS limits for industrial liquid effluents.
3.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Approval Conditions:** That the Respondent had failed to adhere to the specific conditions stipulated in the Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA approval letter No. EPA/EIA/2010/XYZ dated March 15, 2010) granted by the EPA, particularly regarding regular environmental monitoring, submission of compliance reports, and maintenance of pollution control equipment.
4.  **Failure to Respond to Notices:** That the Respondent failed to provide satisfactory responses or take effective remedial actions despite multiple Show Cause Notices (e.g., No. EPA/Enf/2016/ABC dated May 20, 2016, and No. EPA/Enf/2017/DEF dated August 10, 2017) issued by the EPA, thereby demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant
presented a series of environmental monitoring reports, laboratory analysis certificates of air emissions and wastewater samples collected from the Respondent's facility on various dates between 2016 and 2017. These reports, prepared by accredited laboratories, consistently indicated levels of particulate matter, NOx, SO2, COD, BOD, and TSS exceeding the NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting visible plumes from stacks and
discolored wastewater discharge were also submitted. Expert testimonies from environmental engineers further corroborated the technical findings, explaining the adverse impacts of such emissions and discharges on public health and the environment, including air quality degradation and water contamination. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
