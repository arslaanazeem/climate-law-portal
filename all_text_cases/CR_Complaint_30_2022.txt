**JUDGMENT**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 30/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, alleging violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and directives for compliance, aiming to protect the environment from industrial pollution.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, filed the present complaint on 15.08.2022, asserting that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/water bodies and emitting air pollutants without proper control mechanisms. The allegations stem from a series of inspections conducted by EPA officials on 05.07.2022 and 25.07.2022. During these inspections, samples of the industrial effluent and air emissions were collected from various discharge points and stacks of the Respondent's facility located at Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. Subsequent laboratory analysis reports, appended with the complaint, indicated that the levels of several parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (specifically Chromium and Lead) in the effluent, significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents. Similarly, air emission samples showed levels of Particulate Matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) exceeding the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent had failed to obtain the requisite No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) for its expansion projects and was operating without a fully functional and efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and adequate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), despite repeated verbal and written warnings, including show-cause notices issued on 15.07.2022 and 02.08.2022, to which the Respondent's response was deemed unsatisfactory and non-committal.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant produced the original inspection reports dated 05.07.2022 and 25.07.2022, duly signed by the inspecting officers. These reports detailed the observations made on-site, including visible signs of pollution, the absence of a fully operational ETP, and the direct discharge of effluent. Crucially, the Complainant submitted the official laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/Lab/W-1234/2022 and EPA/Lab/A-
5678/2022, respectively. These reports unequivocally confirmed that the levels of pollutants in both effluent and air emissions from the Respondent's facility were in gross violation of the NEQS, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its learned counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and filed a written statement vehemently denying the
** allegations leveled against it. The Respondent contended that the inspections conducted by the EPA officials were flawed, arguing that proper procedures for sample collection, sealing, and chain of custody were not strictly adhered to, thereby casting doubt on the veracity and reliability of the laboratory analysis reports. It was further submitted that the samples were not collected in the presence of the Respondent's authorized representative, nor were counter-samples provided for

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
