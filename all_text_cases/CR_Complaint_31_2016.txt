**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 31/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the
EPA), against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent").
The Complainant's primary contention, as detailed in the complaint, is that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically its textile processing activities, are in violation of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), and the National Environmental
Quality Standards (NEQS) promulgated thereunder. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial effluent discharge consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life.
Notices were duly issued to the Respondent, who appeared through its authorized representative and counsel. The Respondent, in its written statement and subsequent arguments, generally denied the allegations, asserting that it had an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was making efforts to comply with environmental regulations. However, the Complainant presented documentary evidence, including multiple laboratory analysis reports from independent accredited laboratories, which consistently demonstrated the Respondent's effluent discharge to be in excess of the NEQS over a prolonged period. The Tribunal also took into consideration the observations made during site visits, if any, and the reports submitted by the EPA.
After hearing both parties, examining the evidence on record, and considering the relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules and regulations framed thereunder, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has, on multiple occasions, discharged industrial effluent in contravention of the NEQS, thereby violating Section 12 and Section 16 of the PEPA, 1997. The Respondent's failure to maintain its ETP to the required standards and to ensure compliance with NEQS is evident from the consistent exceedances documented by the EPA.
The environmental degradation caused by non-compliant industrial discharges is a serious matter that directly impacts public health, ecosystems, and sustainable development. The intent of PEPA, 1997, is to protect, conserve, rehabilitate, and improve the environment, for the prevention and control of pollution, and for the promotion of sustainable development. Non-compliance, especially by large industrial units, undermines these fundamental objectives.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers conferred upon this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 199

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
