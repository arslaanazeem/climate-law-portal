**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**

**Presided by: Lubna Ali**
This Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), has convened to adjudicate upon the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant") against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint, registered on 15th October 2020, pertains to alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent in the operation of its chemical manufacturing facility located in Lahore. The proceedings have been conducted in accordance with the prescribed legal framework to ensure a fair and impartial hearing for all parties involved.
The Complainant's allegations are primarily centered on the Respondent's persistent failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge and gaseous emissions, as stipulated under PEPA, 1997. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's facility has been continuously discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the public sewerage system/water bodies, containing pollutants such as high Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals, far exceeding the permissible NEQS limits. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of uncontrolled air emissions from the Respondent's stacks, releasing particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) into the ambient air, thereby contributing to air pollution and posing a risk to public health and the environment. The Complainant also asserted that despite multiple warning notices, show-cause notices, and opportunities for compliance issued by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) since early 2019, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, demonstrating a willful disregard for environmental protection laws and the conditions of its Environmental Approval.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between January 2019 and September 2020, detailing observations of non-compliant operational practices. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from EPA-accredited laboratories, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for effluent discharge and air emissions based on samples collected during these inspections. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the discharge points and visible emissions were also tendered. Copies of all warning notices, show-cause notices, and minutes of compliance meetings held with the Respondent were placed on record, along with the Respondent's responses (or lack thereof). The Assistant Director, EPA, also provided oral testimony, reiterating the agency's efforts to secure compliance and the Respondent's consistent failure. In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel and representatives, denied the allegations, arguing that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was flawed, and that the analytical results were not representative of their overall operations. They presented internal monitoring reports, which, however, lacked independent verification, and claimed to have initiated steps towards upgrading their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices, albeit without providing concrete evidence of full operational compliance or significant improvement at the time of the inspections. They also cited operational challenges and financial constraints as contributing factors to delays in achieving full compliance, requesting further time to implement necessary upgrades.
The legal framework governing this matter is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, mandates that no person shall commence construction or operation of a project likely to cause adverse environmental effects without filing an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and obtaining approval from the EPA. More pertinently, Section 11 prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent, waste, or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Section 16 empowers the EPA to issue environmental protection orders, and Section 17 prescribes penalties for contravention of the Act, Rules, or Regulations made thereunder, including fines and daily penalties for continuing contraventions. The National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Liquid Effluents (S.R.O. 549(I)/2000) and Gaseous Emissions (S.R.O. 549(I)/2000) are directly applicable here, setting the permissible limits for various pollutants. The Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2019, also provide the mechanism for the utilization of penalties imposed under the Act. This Tribunal's powers to impose penalties and issue directions for environmental compliance are derived from
Section 17 and 20 of PEPA, 1997, read with the relevant rules and regulations.
**FINDINGS AND ANALYSIS OF THE TRIBUNAL:**
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the voluminous record, including the documentary evidence and oral testimonies presented by both parties. It is evident that the Complainant, through the Environmental Protection Agency, has presented a compelling and substantiated
case against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited. The series of inspection reports, coupled with the independently verified laboratory analysis reports from EPA-accredited laboratories, unequivocally demonstrate that the Respondent's industrial effluent discharge and gaseous emissions consistently exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) over a prolonged period. The photographic and videographic evidence further corroborated these observations.
The Tribunal finds the Respondent's arguments regarding flawed sampling methodology to be unsubstantiated. The EPA followed standard sampling protocols, and the laboratory reports bear the hallmarks of independent verification. The internal monitoring reports presented by the Respondent, while noted, cannot override the independently verified data presented by the Complainant, particularly given their lack of external accreditation or verification.
While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of operational challenges and financial constraints, these do not absolve them of their legal obligation to comply with environmental laws. Environmental protection is a non-negotiable aspect of industrial operations, and the health and well-being of the public and the environment cannot be compromised due to commercial difficulties. The Respondent had ample opportunity, through multiple warning and show-cause notices, to rectify the situation, yet failed to demonstrate effective and sustained compliance. Their initiation of steps towards upgrading ETP and APC devices, while a positive development, came late and did not prevent the sustained violations documented.
Therefore, based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has indeed violated Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging effluents and emissions exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards, and has further contravened the conditions of its Environmental Approval. This persistent non-compliance warrants a decisive response from this Tribunal.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
Having carefully considered all aspects of the case, the Tribunal hereby finds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, the Respondent, guilty of violating the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, specifically Section 11 thereof, by consistently failing to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluent discharge and gaseous emissions.
Therefore, this Tribunal makes the following order:
1.  **Penalty Imposition:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for the contraventions established. This amount shall be deposited into the Punjab Environmental Protection Fund within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
2.  **Remedial Action and Compliance Plan:** The Respondent is directed to immediately implement and fully operationalize all necessary upgrades to its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices to ensure full and sustained compliance with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Respondent shall submit a detailed, time-bound compliance action plan, duly vetted by an independent environmental consultant, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly progress reports to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, detailing the steps taken, analytical results of effluent and emission monitoring (from an EPA-accredited laboratory), and the overall status of compliance, for a period of one (1) year or until sustained compliance is demonstrated to the satisfaction of the EPA. The EPA is authorized to conduct unannounced inspections and sampling to verify compliance.
4.  **Daily Penalty for Continued Non-Compliance:** In the event of continued non-compliance with NEQS after ninety (90) days from the date of this order, or failure to adhere to the approved compliance plan, a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** shall be levied against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for each day of such default, until full compliance is achieved.
5.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
