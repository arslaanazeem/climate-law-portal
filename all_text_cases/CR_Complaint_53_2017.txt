The present complaint, bearing No. 53/2017, was filed before this Tribunal by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, on 20.06.2017, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. The complaint seeks inter alia, a declaration that the Respondent is operating in violation of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), particularly Section 11 thereof, and various National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby causing environmental degradation and posing risks to public health. The EPA invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 20 read with Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, seeking appropriate relief, including penalties and remedial measures.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent, engaged in the large-scale production of various industrial chemicals including caustic soda and chlorine, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public drainage system, which eventually merges into the River Ravi. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent's air emissions from its various stacks contain pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The specific allegations included:
a.  Discharge of industrial effluent with pH levels outside the permissible range, high Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and elevated concentrations of heavy metals such as mercury, lead, and chromium, in contravention of S.R.O. 549(I)/2000 dated 05.06.2000.
b.  Emission of particulate matter, sulphur dioxide (SO2), and chlorine gas (Cl2) from its stacks beyond the limits stipulated by NEQS, thereby contributing to air pollution in the surrounding residential and agricultural areas.
c.  Failure to install and effectively operate a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and adequate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) commensurate with the scale and nature of its operations.
d.  Non-compliance with the conditions laid down in the Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA) granted by the EPA for its operations.
e.  Repeated disregard for warnings, show-cause notices, and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued by the EPA under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, compelling the Complainant to approach this Tribunal for enforcement.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2016, 28.0
8.2016, and 10.01.2017, detailing observed non-compliance and sampling results. Crucially, it submitted laboratory analysis reports from accredited laboratories, confirming the presence of pollutants in the Respondent's effluent and emissions far exceeding the NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the discharge points and visible air pollution were also submitted. Furthermore, copies of the
warnings, show-cause notices, and Environmental Protection Orders previously issued to the Respondent, demonstrating a pattern of persistent non-compliance despite repeated attempts by the EPA to secure voluntary adherence to environmental laws.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
