**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 18/2016**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi**
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, on 15.03.2016, under Section 20 read with Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), seeks to address the alleged persistent environmental pollution caused by M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited. The Complainant, acting in its statutory capacity to enforce environmental laws and protect public health and the environment, brought this action against the Respondent, a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura, for its alleged failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of the PEPA, 1997. The Tribunal, vested with exclusive jurisdiction to hear and decide environmental complaints, has meticulously examined the record, heard arguments from both sides, and considered all material evidence presented.
The Complainant alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited was continuously discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the surrounding environment, specifically into a nearby drain which eventually merges with river Ravi, thereby causing severe water pollution. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of air pollution through the emission of noxious gases and particulate matter from the Respondent's industrial processes, exceeding the prescribed NEQS. It was also contended that the Respondent had failed to properly manage its hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste, leading to unscientific disposal practices and land degradation. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated inspections, issuance of environmental protection orders (EPOs), and show-cause notices by the EPA, the Respondent had failed to take adequate measures to control pollution and achieve compliance with environmental laws, thus posing a significant threat to public health, aquatic life, and the general ecosystem. Specific dates of inspections, including 15.10.2015 and 20.01.2016, were cited, where effluent and air samples were collected, and subsequent laboratory analyses consistently revealed parameters exceeding the NEQS.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA officials, clearly documenting the state of the Respondent's effluent treatment plant (ETP), air
emission control systems, and solid waste management practices. Crucially, it included certified laboratory analysis reports of effluent and air samples, consistently demonstrating levels of pollutants, such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, lead, mercury, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and particulate matter (PM10), significantly exceeding the NEQS. Furthermore, copies of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, and show-cause notices, along with the Respondent's inadequate responses or failures to comply, were presented as documentary evidence. Photographic evidence depicting improper waste disposal sites and visible emissions from stacks was also submitted.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel and representatives, denied the persistent and willful non-compliance. While acknowledging certain operational challenges inherent in chemical manufacturing, the Respondent contended that it had made substantial investments and efforts towards upgrading its environmental infrastructure. They claimed to be in the process of installing advanced effluent treatment technologies and improving air pollution control measures, including the retrofitting of scrubbers and bag filters. The Respondent presented some internal reports and invoices related to the purchase of environmental equipment and claimed that improvements were ongoing, asserting that the most recent readings, though not fully compliant, showed a trend towards reduction in pollutants. They also argued that some of the samples cited by the Complainant might not be fully representative of their overall discharge/emissions or that external factors contributed to the pollution. However, they failed to provide independent third-party verified monitoring data or a clear, time-bound action plan approved by the EPA demonstrating full commitment to achieving and sustaining NEQS compliance.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the voluminous record. It is evident from the comprehensive evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the consistent laboratory analysis reports from multiple dates, that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been in persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules and regulations framed thereunder. The repeated issuance of EPOs and show-cause notices by the EPA, followed by the Respondent's failure to achieve compliance, underscores a pattern of neglect and insufficient action. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's assertion of ongoing efforts, these efforts have demonstrably not been adequate or timely enough to bring the facility into compliance, nor have they mitigated the significant environmental harm caused. The Respondent's defense lacked credible, independently verifiable data to counter the Complainant's specific allegations and evidence of non-compliance. The continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent into public waterways and the emission of pollutants into the atmosphere pose an undeniable and severe threat to public health, the ecosystem of River Ravi, and the general environment, which the PEPA, 1997, explicitly aims to protect. The gravity of the violations, coupled with the Respondent's prolonged failure to heed regulatory directives, necessitates a firm and decisive intervention by this Tribunal.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal holds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited responsible for continuous and persistent violations of Section 11, Section 13, and Section 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
Therefore, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Pollution and Compliance:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby directed to immediately cease all discharges of effluent and emissions of gases and particulate matter that exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and to ensure proper management of all hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste.
2.  **Upgradation and Installation:** The Respondent shall, within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, upgrade its existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and install/retrofit all necessary air pollution control devices (APCDs), including but not limited to scrubbers, bag filters, and continuous emission monitoring systems, to ensure full and sustained compliance with all applicable NEQS.
3.  **Environmental Management Plan:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed, time-bound Environmental Management Plan (EMP), including an independent third-party verified monitoring protocol, to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order, for approval and continuous oversight.
4.  **Financial Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 10,000,000/- (Rupees Ten Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for the persistent violations and environmental damage caused. This amount shall be deposited into the Punjab Environmental Protection Fund within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
5.  **Daily Penalty for Non-Compliance:** Should the Respondent fail to achieve full compliance with the NEQS and the directives contained in paragraph 2 above within the stipulated ninety (90) days, a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** shall be levied for each day of continued non-compliance thereafter, until full compliance is demonstrably achieved and verified by the EPA. This daily penalty shall also be deposited into the Punjab Environmental Protection Fund.
6.  **Closure Threat:** Failure to comply with the terms of this order, particularly concerning the achievement of NEQS within the specified timeframe, may result in further stringent actions, including but not limited to the sealing or temporary closure of the industrial unit under Section 16 of the PEPA, 1997, without further notice.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **04 October, 2022**.
