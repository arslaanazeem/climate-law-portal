This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing facility located in Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15.03.2022, seeks remedial action and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The core purpose of the Tribunal's proceedings is to ensure compliance with environmental laws, protect public health, and preserve the ecological integrity of the region, pursuant to its mandate under the PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant, through its Assistant Director, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, was engaged in industrial operations that resulted in the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which ultimately leads to the River Ravi, and emission of air pollutants exceeding prescribed standards. Specifically, the allegations included:
a) Discharge of industrial effluent with parameters exceeding the NEQS for various pollutants, including but not limited to Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. This was detected during an unannounced inspection conducted on 01.02.2022.
b) Operation of a boiler and other combustion sources without adequate air pollution control measures, leading to the emission of smoke and particulate matter exceeding the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, as observed during the same inspection.
c) Failure to maintain proper records of environmental monitoring and treatment plant operations, as required by the terms of their No Objection Certificate (NOC) and relevant environmental regulations.
d) Non-compliance with the directives issued by the EPA in an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 15.02.2022, which required the Respondent to rectify the identified violations within seven days.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
i.  An inspection report dated 01.02.2022, prepared by a team of EPA officials, detailing the observations of non-compliant effluent discharge points and visible air emissions. The report included photographic evidence of dark smoke emanating from the Respondent's chimneys and discoloured effluent.
ii. Laboratory analysis report No. EPA/LAB/007/2022 dated 08.02.2022, from the EPA's accredited laboratory. This report confirmed that samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge point on 01.02.2022 showed COD levels of 550 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), BOD5 levels of 280 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), and TSS levels of 300 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L).
iii. A copy of the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 15.02.2022, issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, directing the Respondent to cease discharge of non-compliant effluent, install/upgrade air pollution control devices, and submit a compliance report within seven days.
iv. Subsequent correspondence including a reminder notice dated 28.02.2022, indicating continued non-compliance and the Respondent's failure to provide satisfactory evidence of rectification within the stipulated period.
The Respondent, through its legal counsel, Mr. Ali Raza, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that M/s Packages Limited is a responsible corporate entity with a robust environmental management system in place. They contended that while momentary technical glitches in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) might have occurred, these were promptly addressed. The Respondent presented internal audit reports and maintenance logs purporting to show regular upkeep of their environmental infrastructure. They argued that the EPA's inspection and sampling might have captured an atypical operational moment and questioned the representativeness of the samples. Furthermore, they submitted that they have invested substantial capital in upgrading their environmental control systems and are committed to achieving and maintaining full compliance with NEQS. They requested the Tribunal to consider their proactive measures and grant them a reasonable timeframe to demonstrate complete compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence and arguments presented by both parties. The Complainant's inspection report, photographic evidence, and particularly the laboratory analysis report (ii) from an accredited EPA laboratory, constitute strong prima facie evidence of violations. The exceedance of NEQS for COD, BOD5, and TSS was significant, indicating a substantial failure in the Respondent's effluent treatment processes. Similarly, the visible air emissions observed during the inspection point towards inadequate air pollution control. The Respondent's claims of momentary glitches, while plausible in theory, were not substantiated by independent verifiable data or counter-expert reports. Their internal audit reports and maintenance logs, without independent verification, do not sufficiently refute the specific findings of the EPA's accredited laboratory and inspection team.
Furthermore, the Respondent's failure to comply with the directives contained in the EPO dated 15.02.2022 within the specified seven days, and their subsequent non-response to the reminder notice, demonstrates a lack of timely action and a disregard for regulatory directives. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's stated commitment to environmental stewardship and their ongoing investments, these do not absolve them from the proven instances of non-compliance. Environmental protection laws are designed to ensure continuous adherence to standards, not merely aspirational goals. The objective evidence presented by the EPA clearly establishes that the Respondent was operating in violation of the prescribed environmental standards and failed to take adequate remedial measures despite being formally directed to do so.
**FINDINGS:**
Based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, has contravened:
1.  **Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997**, by discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system with parameters exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997**, by emitting air pollutants without adequate air pollution control measures, resulting
in emissions exceeding the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
3.  **Section 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997**, by failing to comply with the directives issued in the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 15.02.2022 within the stipulated timeframe.
**ORDER:**
In light of the aforementioned findings and pursuant to the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered that:
1.  The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, shall immediately cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the public sewerage system or any other water body.
2.  The Respondent shall forthwith take all necessary measures to upgrade, repair, or install appropriate air pollution control devices (APCDs) and effluent treatment plant (ETP) infrastructure to ensure continuous compliance with the NEQS for both industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluent discharge.
3.  The Respondent shall, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order, submit to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, a comprehensive compliance plan detailing the specific actions, timelines, and technical specifications for achieving and maintaining full compliance with NEQS for all its operations. This plan shall include a schedule for the installation/upgrade of necessary equipment, a robust monitoring protocol, and a commitment to regular reporting.
4.  The Respondent shall, for a period of six (6) months following the submission of the compliance plan, submit monthly monitoring reports, duly certified by an independent accredited laboratory, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS for both effluent and emissions. These reports shall be submitted to the EPA, Lahore, with a copy to this Tribunal.
5.  The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is directed to conduct regular and unannounced inspections of the Respondent's facility to verify compliance with this Order and the NEQS. The EPA shall submit quarterly compliance reports to this Tribunal for a period of one year.
**PENALTY:**
Considering the gravity of the environmental violations, the proven exceedance of NEQS, and the Respondent's failure to comply with the Environmental Protection Order, thereby demonstrating a disregard for regulatory directives, the Tribunal, exercising its powers under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, hereby imposes the following penalties:
1.  A fine of **PKR 750,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred and Fifty Thousand Only)** is imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the contraventions established under Sections 11, 12, and 16 of the PEPA, 1997. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
2.  In the event of continued non-compliance with any part of this Order beyond the specified timelines, the Respondent shall be liable to an additional fine of **PKR 20,000/- (Rupees Twenty Thousand Only)** for every day during which such contravention or failure continues, until full compliance is achieved, as per Section 17(1) of the PEPA, 1997.
This judgment shall take immediate effect.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
