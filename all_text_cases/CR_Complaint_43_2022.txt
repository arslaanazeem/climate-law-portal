**Presided by: Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal**
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), read with Section 20 thereof, seeks enforcement against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for alleged violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with statutory directives. The proceedings commenced on 15th May 2022, following the submission of a detailed complaint outlining the Respondent's persistent failure to adhere to environmental regulations governing industrial operations. The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction to adjudicate upon the matter, aiming to ensure compliance with environmental laws and protect public health and the environment from degradation caused by industrial pollution.
The Complainant asserted that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies, thereby contaminating water resources. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial processes were releasing air emissions containing hazardous pollutants significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and Punjab Environmental Quality Standards (PEQS). Specific allegations included the absence of a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) of requisite capacity, or their consistent non-operation, leading to direct discharge of hazardous substances. The complaint further detailed that multiple show-cause notices and environmental improvement notices, dating back to October 2021, had been issued to the Respondent by the EPA, directing them to comply with environmental standards and submit an environmental management plan, but these notices were consistently ignored or met with unsatisfactory responses, demonstrating a wilful disregard for environmental compliance. The Complainant sought a declaration of the Respondent's culpability, imposition of a substantial fine, and an order for immediate remedial measures.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams on various dates, specifically 10th October 2021, 5th January 2022, and 18th March 2022, which documented observations of direct discharge channels, visible plumes of smoke, and the absence of operational ETP/APCDs. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 10th October 2021 and 18th March 2022 were submitted, demonstrating concentrations of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (e.g., lead, chromium) significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS/PEQS. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports from the vicinity of the plant, conducted on 5th January 2022, indicated levels of particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) far beyond permissible limits. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the state of the discharge and emissions was also presented. Copies of the aforementioned show-cause notices, environmental improvement notices, and the Respondent's non-committal or non-responsive replies were placed on record. The Respondent, despite being duly served, failed to file a written statement within the stipulated time and did not present any substantive evidence to rebut the Complainant's claims or demonstrate compliance with environmental standards. Their counsel appeared on few dates but failed to produce any credible defence or documentation supporting their adherence to environmental regulations.
The Tribunal's jurisdiction and the legal framework for this complaint are primarily derived from the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 12 of the Act prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) established by the Pakistan Environmental Protection Council. Section 16 mandates that no person shall operate a motor vehicle, or an industrial unit or other source of noise, emission or waste which is causing air pollution or noise pollution in excess of the NEQS. Section 17 empowers the Tribunal to take cognizance of complaints and impose penalties. Specifically, Section 17(1) allows any person to file a complaint before the Tribunal against any person for contravention of any provision of the Act or the rules or regulations made thereunder. Section 20 provides for penalties, stating that whoever contravenes or fails to comply with the provisions of Section 12 or Section 16 or any order issued thereunder, shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with a further fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues. Furthermore, the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and Punjab Environmental Quality Standards (PEQS) for municipal and industrial effluent, and industrial gaseous emissions, promulgated under the Act
, establish the permissible limits for various pollutants, which the Respondent is alleged to have consistently exceeded. The legislative intent behind these provisions is unequivocally to prevent environmental degradation, safeguard public health, and ensure sustainable industrial practices.
Having meticulously reviewed the complaint, the documentary evidence, and the legal submissions, the Tribunal notes with concern the Respondent's persistent non-compliance and utter disregard for environmental regulations. The Complainant has presented a robust body of evidence, including detailed inspection reports, irrefutable laboratory analysis reports of effluent and air samples, and photographic/videographic evidence, all unequivocally demonstrating that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been discharging untreated/inadequately treated industrial effluent and emitting hazardous air pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS/PEQS. The consistent issuance of show-cause and improvement notices by the EPA, and the Respondent's failure to respond adequately or take corrective action, further underscore a deliberate and willful violation of environmental laws.
The Respondent, despite ample opportunity, failed to file a written statement, rebut the specific allegations, or provide any evidence whatsoever to demonstrate compliance. Their counsel's inability to present a credible defence leaves the Complainant's assertions unchallenged and proven on record. The evidence on record clearly establishes a pattern of gross negligence and a flagrant breach of Sections 12 and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's actions poses a significant threat to public health, local ecosystems, and water resources, necessitating immediate and stringent intervention by this Tribunal.
**FINDINGS:**
Based on the uncontroverted evidence and the Respondent's failure to present a defence, the Tribunal finds that:
1.  M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has consistently discharged untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and local water bodies, in contravention of Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and in violation of the NEQS/PEQS for industrial effluent.
2.  M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has emitted air pollutants significantly exceeding the NEQS/PEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, thereby causing air pollution in contravention of Section 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
3.  The Respondent has willfully disregarded multiple show-cause notices and environmental improvement notices issued by the Environmental Protection Agency.
4.  The Respondent has failed to install and/or consistently operate a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) of requisite capacity.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,900,000/- (Rupees Three Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 780,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **15 February, 2023**.
