**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 54/2017 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of this Tribunal. The complaint, lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations concerning industrial effluent discharge and non-compliance with National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, vested with powers under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997, to hear and adjudicate environmental complaints, has meticulously examined the submissions and evidence presented by both parties.
The Complainant's primary allegations against the Respondent revolve around repeated and persistent contraventions of environmental standards. Specifically, it was alleged that M/s Packages Limited, operating a large-scale manufacturing facility, has been discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the River Ravi without adequate treatment, thereby exceeding the prescribed NEQS for various parameters. The Complainant detailed several instances of non-compliance, including:
(a) Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater containing levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (e.g., Chromium, Lead) significantly above the NEQS, as stipulated in the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000, and the NEQS Rules.
(b) Failure to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), leading to consistent violations.
(c) Non-compliance with the conditions stipulated in the Environmental Approval (EA) granted to the Respondent under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, particularly regarding the regular submission of self-monitoring reports and adherence to discharge parameters.
(d) Disregard for prior notices and directions issued by the EPA for rectification of the environmental non-compliance.
(e) Causing environmental pollution detrimental to public health and the ecosystem, in contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the NEQS.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2016, 22.08.2016, and 05.01.2017, conducted by EPA officials, which documented the operational deficiencies of the Respondent's ETP and the visible signs of pollution. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates (e.g., 2
0.03.2016, 28.08.2016, and 10.01.2017), which consistently revealed pollutant levels significantly exceeding the NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and heavy metals. These reports, prepared by accredited laboratories, served as prima facie evidence of the Respondent's environmental transgressions. Furthermore, the Complainant provided copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 01.04.2016 and 15.0
9.2016, and final warnings, which were allegedly disregarded.

The Respondent contended that it is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship, as evidenced by its past investments in pollution control technologies. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
