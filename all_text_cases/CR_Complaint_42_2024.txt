This judgment addresses Complaint No. 42/2024, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") concerning alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal has taken cognizance of the matter under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), specifically Section 21 thereof, which vests this Tribunal with the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance. The present complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for its continuous discharge of untreated industrial effluent into the environment, thereby contravening the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and causing significant environmental degradation.
The Complainant's allegations center on the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility located at [Specific Address, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore]. It is contended that the Respondent has consistently failed to install and/or operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) in accordance with environmental regulations. Specifically, the EPA alleges that repeated inspections conducted by its field teams revealed the direct discharge of industrial wastewater, containing various pollutants, into public drains and ultimately into the River Ravi, without adequate treatment. The Complainant further asserts that the chemical and physical parameters of the discharged effluent, as evidenced by laboratory analyses, significantly exceed the prescribed NEQS for industrial discharges. Despite multiple notices, show-cause notices, and opportunities for compliance issued by the EPA to the Respondent over the past several months, the Respondent has allegedly demonstrated a persistent disregard for environmental laws and has failed to take concrete, effective measures to mitigate the pollution caused by its operations. The Complainant sought an order from this Tribunal directing the Respondent to cease its polluting activities, install a functional ETP, and impose a substantial penalty for the violations committed.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included a series of inspection reports dated 15.01.2024, 28.02.2024, and 12.03.2024, prepared by EPA officials, detailing the observations of untreated effluent discharge from the Respondent's premises. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from an EPA-accredited laboratory, dated 20.01.2024 and 05.03.2024, pertaining to effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points. These reports unequivocally indicated that parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels were substantially above the NEQS limits. For instance, the BOD levels were reported at 450 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L, and COD at 1100 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the direct flow of colored and odorous effluent from the Respondent's facility into the public drainage system was also presented. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 25.01.2024 and 08.03.2024, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies or lack thereof, were placed on record, demonstrating a pattern of non-compliance and unheeded warnings.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its counsel, contested the allegations. While acknowledging the existence of a manufacturing facility and the generation of industrial wastewater, the Respondent argued that it had initiated steps towards environmental compliance. It was contended that an ETP was "under construction" or "in the process of upgradation" and that the current discharge was temporary or due

They maintained that significant financial investment had already been made towards the establishment of a state-of-the-art ETP, and that the process was genuinely nearing completion. **Tribunal's Deliberation and Findings:**
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the documentary and forensic evidence placed on record. While the Respondent acknowledges the generation of industrial wastewater and the ongoing efforts towards ETP installation, their defense regarding the "under construction" or "upgradation" status of the ETP, and the temporary nature of the discharge, does not absolve them from their statutory obligations under PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS. The law mandates compliance with environmental standards, and the continuous discharge of untreated effluent, irrespective of the reasons cited for delay, constitutes a clear violation.
The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the multiple inspection reports and the accredited laboratory analysis reports, provides irrefutable proof of non-compliance. The significantly elevated levels of BOD, COD, TSS, and abnormal pH values consistently found in the Respondent's effluent samples demonstrate a severe breach of the NEQS. The photographic and videographic evidence further corroborates the direct discharge of polluting substances into public drains, ultimately impacting the River Ravi. The Respondent's contention that the EPA's sampling methodology might not be representative or that other sources might contribute to pollution is speculative and unsubstantiated by any credible counter-evidence.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
