This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking redressal for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Tribunal, having heard arguments from both sides and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented, proceeds to deliver its findings and final order.
The EPA, through its Assistant Director, initiated these proceedings on 10.08.2022, asserting that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically its glass manufacturing unit located at 12-KM, Sheikhupura Road, Lahore, were in continuous contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluents. The allegations primarily focus on two critical aspects: firstly, the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into municipal drains, eventually flowing into public water bodies, with pollutant levels exceeding the prescribed NEQS; and secondly, the emission of particulate matter, nitrogen oxides (NOx), and sulfur oxides (SOx) from its stacks beyond permissible limits, thereby contributing to air pollution in the vicinity. The EPA contended that despite multiple inspections, issuance of show-cause notices dated 05.05.2022 and 25.07.2022, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental protection laws and public health.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA field teams on 01.04.2022, 20.06.2022, and 01.08.2022, which documented observations of dark plume emissions from the Respondent's stacks and direct discharge of colored effluent. Crucially, the EPA submitted laboratory analysis reports from samples collected on these dates, duly attested by the designated environmental laboratory. These reports unequivocally indicated that the levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), and pH in the liquid effluent consistently exceeded the NEQS. Similarly, the air emission analyses revealed particulate matter concentrations significantly higher than the stipulated limits. Photographs and video footage depicting the alleged violations were also submitted. Furthermore, copies of the show-cause notices and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, which largely acknowledged the issues but failed to demonstrate concrete steps towards compliance, formed part of the evidence. The Complainant also presented expert testimony from an environmental engineer, elucidating the adverse impacts of such pollution on human health and the ecosystem.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. Through its legal counsel and technical representatives, the Respondent argued that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship. They presented evidence of significant investments made in pollution control technologies, including the installation of an Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for air emissions and an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) for wastewater. The Respondent's defense revolved around the assertion that any exceedances recorded by the EPA were either transient, attributable to temporary operational malfunctions, or due to sampling discrepancies. They submitted their own internal monitoring reports, which, while occasionally showing spikes, generally claimed compliance with NEQS. The Respondent's technical expert testified that the ETP and ESP were fully functional and regularly maintained. They further contended that the process of upgrading and optimizing pollution control equipment is ongoing and requires time. They also highlighted their efforts in tree plantation and corporate social responsibility initiatives as proof of their commitment to the environment.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) notified thereunder. Specifically, Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level in excess of the NEQS. Section 12 mandates that no proponent of a project shall commence construction or operation unless an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental
Impact Assessment (EIA) has been filed with the Agency and approval granted. While these sections primarily address new projects, the underlying principle of ensuring environmental compatibility and adherence to NEQS is universally applicable and forms the bedrock for regulating existing industrial operations. Section 17 of the PEPA, 1997, prescribes penalties for contravention of Section 11 and other provisions, stipulating fines
, imprisonment, or both. Specifically, Section 17(1) states that whoever contravenes or fails to comply with the provisions of Section 11 (among others) shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with an additional fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
