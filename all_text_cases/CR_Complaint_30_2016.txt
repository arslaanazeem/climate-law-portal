This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, under Section 19
of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), alleges that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, located at Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura, has been discharging untreated industrial effluent containing hazardous substances into the environment, specifically into the local water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental pollution and adversely affecting public health and aquatic life. The complaint further asserts that the Respondent has failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge, despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the Complainant.
The Tribunal took cognizance of the complaint on 15.03.2016. Notices were issued to the Respondent, who appeared through their legal counsel and filed a written statement denying the allegations. During the course of the proceedings, the Complainant presented documentary evidence, including multiple inspection reports, laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points, and correspondence exchanged between the EPA and the Respondent. These reports consistently indicated that the discharged effluent exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals. The Complainant also produced photographs and expert testimonies substantiating the environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's activities.
The Respondent, in their defense, contended that they had installed an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and were making efforts to comply with the NEQS. However, they failed to provide convincing evidence of consistent compliance or effective operation of their ETP during the relevant period. The Tribunal also noted that the Respondent had been granted ample opportunities to rectify the deficiencies but had largely failed to do so, demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations and the well-being of the surrounding community.
After careful consideration of the evidence presented by both parties, the arguments advanced by their respective counsels, and the relevant provisions of the Act, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has violated Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging environmental pollutants in excess of the NEQS, thereby causing pollution of the environment. The persistent non-compliance and the documented evidence of pollution leave no doubt regarding the Respondent's culpability.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby:
1.  **DECLARES** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited GUILTY of violating Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
2.  **IMPOSES** a monetary penalty of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** upon M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, payable to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **DIRECTS** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and to ensure full and continuous compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at all times.
4.  **ORDERS** the Respondent to submit a comprehensive and actionable plan for the upgrade, optimization, and effective operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within sixty (60) days. **FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
