This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks an adjudication and imposition of penalties against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged persistent violations of environmental standards and regulations. The complaint was formally lodged with this Tribunal on October 27, 2020, following a series of inspections and notices issued to the Respondent. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, possesses the requisite jurisdiction to hear and decide matters pertaining to environmental pollution and contraventions of environmental laws within the province of Punjab.
The Complainant has alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions, specifically concerning particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and oxides of sulphur (SOx). It is further alleged that the Respondent has failed to effectively operate and maintain its installed air pollution control devices, resulting in the continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated emissions into the ambient air, thereby causing air pollution in the surrounding areas. The EPA claims to have conducted multiple inspections of the Respondent's premises on various dates, including July 15, 2020, August 10, 2020, and September 05, 2020. During these inspections, visible emissions were observed emanating from the Respondent's stacks, and subsequent laboratory analyses of samples collected by the EPA revealed that the concentrations of PM, NOx, and SOx consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS limits. The Complainant asserts that prior to filing this complaint, several warning notices and environmental protection orders were issued to the Respondent under Sections 16 and 17 of PEPA, 1997, directing them to rectify the non-compliance and install/upgrade appropriate pollution control technology. Despite these directives, the Respondent allegedly failed to take adequate corrective measures, leading to the continued environmental degradation and public nuisance.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant submitted a comprehensive set of documentary evidence, including detailed inspection reports dated July 15, 2020, August 10, 2020, and September 05, 2020, prepared by EPA officials. These reports documented observations of dark, opaque smoke emanating from
the Respondent's stacks, photographs capturing these visible emissions, and laboratory analysis reports from accredited laboratories confirming the exceedance of NEQS for PM, NOx, and SOx. Copies of warning notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued to the Respondent, along with proof of their service, were also appended to demonstrate the Respondent's persistent non-compliance despite repeated official directives. An affidavit from
the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, Mr. Tariq Mehmood, was also submitted, attesting to the authenticity of the documents and the factual accuracy of the allegations made in the complaint.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
