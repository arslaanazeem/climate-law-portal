**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, alleging violations of environmental laws concerning the discharge of industrial effluent.
The complainant, representing the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, instituted this complaint under Section 17 read with Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), contending that the respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, located in Kala Shah Kaku Industrial Area, District Sheikhupura, was operating its industrial facility without adequately treating its industrial wastewater and continuously discharging effluent that did not conform to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint highlighted the respondent's alleged failure to install or effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of bringing the discharged effluent within the prescribed NEQS limits, thereby causing significant environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and aquatic ecosystems. The Tribunal took cognizance of the matter, and notices were duly issued to the respondent.
The complainant asserted that despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, the respondent persistently failed to comply with environmental regulations. Specifically, it was alleged that during various inspections conducted by EPA officials, the effluent being discharged from the respondent's facility into the nearby drain consistently showed parameters exceeding the NEQS for several critical pollutants, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. The complainant submitted that these violations constituted a direct breach of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in excess of the NEQS, and Section 12, which mandates the installation of treatment facilities. The EPA sought an order from the Tribunal directing the respondent to cease such discharges, ensure full compliance with NEQS, and impose an appropriate penalty as provided under the law.
To substantiate its claims, the complainant presented a series of inspection reports, laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the respondent's discharge points on various dates, including 15.03.2016, 22.04.2016, and 07.06.2016. These reports, duly attested by EPA's accredited laboratories, consistently indicated that the sampled effluent contained pollutants far exceeding the permissible NEQS limits. For instance, the analysis reports showed BOD levels ranging from 350-450 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), COD levels between 800-1000 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and TSS levels of 300-400 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). Furthermore, copies of warning notices and show-cause notices issued to M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited on 28.03.2016 and 15.05.2016, along with their acknowledgements, were submitted as evidence of the respondent
's consistent failure to rectify the situation despite being duly informed.
The respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its authorized representative and legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and contested the complaint. While acknowledging the operation of an industrial facility at the specified location, the respondent broadly denied the allegations of non-compliance with NEQS and persistent discharge of untreated effluent. The respondent contended that it had
invested significantly in environmental protection measures, including the installation of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) several years ago. The respondent asserted that the ETP was generally operational and that any instances of non-compliance were isolated, temporary, and attributable to unforeseen operational issues, power fluctuations, or routine maintenance activities, rather than a deliberate or continuous failure to comply.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
