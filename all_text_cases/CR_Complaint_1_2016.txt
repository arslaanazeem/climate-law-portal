This judgment addresses Complaint No. 1/2016, lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. The complaint, filed on 10.02.2016, seeks redress under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental quality standards by the Respondent, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the ecological integrity of the surrounding area. The Tribunal is seized of this matter to determine the liability of the Respondent and to issue appropriate orders for environmental protection and restitution.
The Complainant's primary contention is that the Respondent has been continuously discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, in blatant disregard of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under PEPA, 1997. Specifically, the EPA alleges that multiple inspections and subsequent laboratory analyses of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points revealed concentrations of various pollutants, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (such as lead and chromium), far exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS. The Complainant further asserts that despite repeated notices, warnings, and the issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, dated 15.10.2015, directing the Respondent to install and/or upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and ensure compliance, the Respondent has failed to take effective remedial measures. This alleged non-compliance, according to the Complainant, constitutes a grave violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and warrants punitive action, including the imposition of a substantial penalty and directives for immediate compliance.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA field teams on various dates, specifically 05.08.2015, 20.10.2015, and 25.01.2016, which documented the visual observation of untreated effluent discharge and the operational status of the Respondent's ETP, noted to be either non-functional or inadequately maintained during
these visits. Crucially, the Complainant submitted comprehensive laboratory analysis reports from accredited environmental laboratories, specifically dated 08.08.2015, 22.10.2015, and 28.01.2016, which unequivocally demonstrated that the Respondent's effluent samples consistently exceeded the NEQS for COD, BOD5, TSS,
pH, and heavy metals. The EPA also presented copies of the show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 15.10.2015, and subsequent reminders, all of which underscore the persistent nature of the violations and the Respondent's apparent failure to heed regulatory directives.
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
