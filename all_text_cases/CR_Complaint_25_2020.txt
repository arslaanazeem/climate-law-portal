**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 25/2020 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, with its industrial facility located at 10-KM, Raiwind Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The complainant, through its authorized representative, initiated these proceedings following a series of inspections and monitoring activities at the Respondent's premises. It is alleged that the Respondent has repeatedly failed to comply with the environmental standards prescribed under the law, thereby causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and the environment. Specifically, the allegations are as follows:
1.  **Discharge of Pollutants**: The Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain without adequate treatment, where the parameters of the discharged effluent consistently exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, particularly concerning Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and pH levels.
2.  **Air Emissions**: The Respondent's industrial processes, particularly the operation of its glass melting furnaces, are emitting gaseous pollutants (Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Particulate Matter (PM)) into the ambient air, the concentrations of which have been found to be in excess of the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
3.  **Ineffective Pollution Control Devices**: The pollution control devices installed by the Respondent, including the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) such as electrostatic precipitators/bag filters, are either inadequately designed, improperly maintained, or not being operated efficiently, leading to the aforementioned exceedances.
4.  **Failure to Comply with Directives**: Despite multiple show-cause notices, environmental protection orders (EPOs), and opportunities for compliance provided by the EPA, the Respondent has failed to take effective remedial measures to bring its operations into compliance with the NEQS and the conditions of its Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC).
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
*   **Inspection Reports**: Copies of inspection reports dated 15.03.2019, 22.07.2019, and 10.12.2019, detailing observations of the Respondent's industrial operations, the condition of its ETP and APCDs, and the apparent lack of effective pollution control.
*   **Laboratory Analysis Reports**: Certified laboratory analysis reports from the EPA-accredited laboratory, corresponding to samples collected from the Respondent's effluent discharge points and stack emissions on the aforementioned inspection dates. These reports consistently showed parameters significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS. For instance, TSS levels in effluent were reported as high as 350 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), BOD5 at 180 mg/L (against NEQS of 80 mg/L), and PM in stack emissions at 450 mg/Nm3 (against NEQS of 200 mg/Nm3).
*   **Show-Cause Notices and Environmental Protection Orders**: Copies of show-cause notices issued on 05.04.2019 and 05.08.2019, and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, on 15.01.2020, directing the Respondent to cease discharge of untreated effluent and control air emissions.
*   **Witness Testimony**: The testimony of Mr. Asif Khan, Environmental Inspector, EPA, who conducted the inspections and collected samples, confirming the findings in the inspection and lab reports.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to
environmental compliance and corporate social responsibility. Through its legal counsel, the Respondent submitted that it has installed state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) at its facility, which are regularly maintained and operated. It was contended that any observed exceedances were either isolated incidents, temporary operational fluctuations, or attributable to external factors beyond their
control. They further submitted that they have been actively collaborating with the EPA to understand the alleged issues and have committed resources towards continuous improvement. It was argued that the laboratory analysis reports presented by the Complainant might not accurately reflect the overall operational efficiency, as samples taken at specific times may not be representative of continuous compliance. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
