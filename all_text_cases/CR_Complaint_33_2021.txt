This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint was instituted on October 15, 2021, seeking enforcement of environmental laws, imposition of penalties, and remedial measures against the Respondent for polluting the environment.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, located in the industrial area of Lahore, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/drain, which eventually leads to a natural water body, thereby causing significant water pollution. The specific allegations included:
a) Discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the permissible limits of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and colour.
b) Failure to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), despite repeated directives and warnings from the EPA.
c) Non-compliance with the environmental management plan submitted by the Respondent.
d) Causing degradation of the aquatic environment and posing a threat to public health and safety in the surrounding areas.
The Complainant asserted that these actions constituted a direct contravention of Section 11 of the Act, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in excess of the NEQS.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports, dated June 10, 2021, August 25, 2021, and September 28, 2021, conducted by EPA officials, clearly indicating the discharge of coloured and foul-smelling effluent from the Respondent's premises.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Analytical reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, corresponding to the samples collected during the aforementioned inspections. These reports consistently showed that the effluent parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, and colour) significantly exceeded the NEQS limits. For instance, the report dated September 29, 2021, revealed BOD levels of 350 mg/L (NEQS limit: 80 mg/L), COD of 800 mg/L (NEQS limit: 150 mg/L), and TSS of 200 mg/L (NEQS limit:
200 mg/L), pH levels fluctuating outside the permissible range of 6-9, and the presence of significant visible colour, all in contravention of the NEQS.
3.  **Show Cause Notices:** Copies of show cause notices issued to the Respondent on July 15, 2021, and September 10, 2021, clearly outlining the violations and directing immediate corrective actions, which were evidently not complied with effectively.
4.  **Photographic and Video Evidence:** Photographic and video evidence captured during inspections, depicting the discharge of visibly polluted effluent from the
Respondent's premises into the public drainage system.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its learned counsel, contested the allegations. The Respondent primarily argued that it had an operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was making continuous efforts to comply with environmental standards. They claimed that any occasional exceedances were due to operational fluctuations or technical issues, which were being addressed. The Respondent also submitted:
1.  **ETP Operational Records:** Records purporting to show the operation and maintenance of their ETP.
2.  **Compliance Plan:** A revised environmental management plan outlining steps to achieve full compliance, including proposed upgrades to the ETP.
3.  **Previous Efforts:** Evidence of past investments in environmental infrastructure.
However, the Respondent failed to provide consistent laboratory reports from an independent accredited laboratory demonstrating sustained compliance with NEQS, nor did they present compelling evidence to counter the specific findings of the EPA's inspection and analysis reports. The Respondent’s counsel requested leniency, emphasizing the economic contribution of the industrial unit and the employment it generates.
**Tribunal's Analysis and Findings:**
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the documentary and visual evidence placed on record.
It is evident from the multiple inspection reports and the consistent laboratory analysis reports provided by the EPA that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been repeatedly discharging industrial effluent that significantly exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for several critical parameters, including BOD, COD, TSS, pH, and colour. The photographic and video evidence further corroborates the visual observation of polluted discharge. The show cause notices issued by the EPA clearly demonstrate that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations and given opportunities to rectify the situation, yet effective compliance was not achieved.
While the Respondent claims to have an operational ETP and is making efforts towards compliance, the objective evidence, particularly the analytical reports, overwhelmingly indicates a persistent failure to meet the NEQS. The operational records of the ETP submitted by the Respondent do not sufficiently negate the findings of non-compliance, especially in the absence of independent verification or consistent self-monitoring data demonstrating adherence to standards. The argument of "operational fluctuations" does not justify continuous and significant exceedances of the prescribed limits, which are designed to protect the environment and public health.
The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant to ensure compliance with the NEQS and has consequently violated Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and eventually into natural water bodies constitutes a serious environmental offense, posing a direct threat to aquatic life and public health. The Tribunal is mindful of the economic significance of industrial units, but this cannot be a justification for continuous environmental degradation. Environmental protection is a fundamental right and a mandatory obligation under the Act.
**ORDER:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby holds M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited responsible for violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
Therefore, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Penalty Imposition:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby directed to pay a cumulative environmental penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order. This penalty is imposed for the persistent violations and non-compliance with environmental laws.
2.  **Remedial Measures and Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order, submit a comprehensive and time-bound action plan, duly approved by the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, for the immediate upgrade, optimization, and effective operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure consistent compliance with all parameters of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). This plan shall include:
    *   A detailed technical assessment of the existing ETP.
    *   Specific proposed upgrades and modifications.
    *   A timeline for implementation, not exceeding six (6) months.
    *   A robust self-monitoring mechanism with regular submission of independent laboratory reports to the EPA.
3.  **Prohibition of Non-Compliant Discharge:** The Respondent is strictly prohibited from discharging any industrial effluent that does not meet the NEQS into the public sewerage system or any natural water body. The EPA is authorized to take immediate action, including sealing of the industrial unit or relevant sections thereof, in case of any future non-compliance.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports, including analytical results from an EPA-accredited independent laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, for a period of two (2) years, or until continuous compliance is demonstrated to the satisfaction of the EPA.
5.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
Failure to comply with any part of this order may result in further legal action, including enhanced penalties and coercive measures as provided under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
