This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 41/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a prominent textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions into the environment, causing pollution and detriment to public health and ecological balance. The Tribunal has meticulously examined the pleadings, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just and equitable decision.
The Complainant, the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, initiated these proceedings on 15.03.2021, alleging that M/s Nishat Mills Limited, the Respondent, was operating its textile manufacturing unit without adequate environmental controls. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. Discharge of industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/natural water bodies without prior treatment to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), causing severe water pollution.
b. Emission of air pollutants, including particulate matter and hazardous gases, from its industrial processes, exceeding the prescribed NEQS for air quality.
c. Failure to obtain the requisite Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) under Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), for its industrial operations or subsequent expansions.
d. Non-compliance with previous environmental improvement notices issued by the EPA, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations.
e. Generation and improper disposal of hazardous waste, posing risks to soil and groundwater contamination.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documentary and testimonial evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.01.2021, 28.02.2021, and 05.03.2021, conducted by EPA officials, which documented the visual observation of untreated wastewater discharge and visible air emissions. Laboratory analysis reports, specifically Report No. EPA/LAB/2021/0078 dated 08.03.2021, were submitted, indicating that samples of effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points significantly exceeded NEQS parameters for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports showed exceedances for Particulate Matter (PM2.5 and PM10) and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx). Copies of show-cause notices issued on 10.02.2021 and 01.03.2021, and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies thereto, were also presented. Furthermore, an affidavit from Mr. Sarmad Khan, Environmental Officer, EPA, detailed the history of non-compliance and the Respondent's failure to install a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) despite repeated warnings.
In response, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, the Respondent, contested the allegations through its written statement filed on 10.04.2021. The Respondent admitted to operating a textile manufacturing unit but denied any deliberate or persistent violation of environmental laws. It argued that it had initiated the process of upgrading its existing ETP and installing new APCDs, but the process was delayed due to unforeseen circumstances, including supply chain disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and technical complexities. The Respondent submitted a project plan for the installation of an advanced ETP and APCDs, along with a timeline for completion, indicating a commitment to environmental compliance. It also presented evidence of significant financial investment already made towards these upgrades. The Respondent contended that minor exceedances of NEQS, if any, were temporary and not indicative of systemic failure, and that its operations were generally in line with best available techniques. It further argued that its existing environmental management system
, was robust and continuously striving for improvement. It emphasized that it had already engaged environmental consultants to conduct a comprehensive environmental audit and propose corrective measures, and that the construction of new facilities was well underway. The Respondent also presented a detailed schedule for the completion of the upgraded ETP and APCDs by 31.12.2022, along with a commitment to regularly report progress to the EPA. It sought a lenient view from the Tribunal
, and therefore sought a lenient view from the Tribunal.
**Analysis and Findings of the Tribunal:**
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments, evidence, and legal submissions from both the Complainant and the Respondent. It is undisputed that M/s Nishat Mills Limited operates a significant textile manufacturing unit. The core of the complaint revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with environmental laws and regulations, particularly regarding the discharge of untreated effluent and emissions, and the lack of requisite environmental approvals.
The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the laboratory analysis reports (Report No. EPA/LAB/2021/0078 dated 08.03.2021), conclusively establishes that the industrial effluent discharged by the Respondent significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports demonstrated exceedances for Particulate Matter (PM2.5 and PM10) and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx). These are not "minor exceedances" as claimed by the Respondent, but rather significant deviations from the prescribed standards, indicating a clear failure to adequately treat industrial waste before discharge and emission. The visual observations documented in multiple inspection reports further corroborate these findings.
Furthermore, the Complainant's assertion regarding the Respondent's failure to obtain the mandatory Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) under Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), for its operations or expansions, remains largely uncontroverted. While the Respondent has initiated processes for upgrades, the statutory requirement for environmental approval precedes operation or expansion. The repeated issuance of show-cause notices and the affidavit of Mr. Sarmad Khan, Environmental Officer, EPA, detailing a history of non-compliance and the absence of functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) at the time of inspections, strongly support the Complainant's allegations of persistent disregard for environmental regulations.
The Respondent's defense, attributing delays to unforeseen circumstances such as the COVID-19 pandemic and technical complexities, while sympathetic, does not absolve it of its statutory obligations to comply with environmental laws. Environmental protection is a non-negotiable imperative, and industries are expected to integrate environmental compliance into their operational planning and budgeting. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's submission of a project plan, commitment to install advanced ETP and APCDs by 31.12.2022, and evidence of financial investment, these are corrective measures initiated *after* the violations were identified and complaints filed. These actions demonstrate a belated recognition of responsibility and a commitment to future compliance, which the Tribunal views as a mitigating factor in determining the quantum of penalty, but not as a basis for exoneration from past and ongoing violations.
Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 explicitly prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount, concentration, or level that causes pollution in excess of the NEQS. Section 12 mandates that no proponent of a project shall commence construction or operation unless an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) has been filed with and approved by the EPA. The evidence clearly demonstrates violations of both these fundamental provisions.
Therefore, based on the overwhelming evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the irrefutable laboratory analysis reports and the Respondent's admitted state of non-compliance at the time of inspections, the Tribunal finds that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has indeed committed violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**Final Order and Penalty:**
In light of the foregoing analysis and findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby issues the following final order:
1.  **Finding of Guilt:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited (the Respondent) is found guilty of violating Section 11 and Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, by discharging untreated industrial effluent, emitting air pollutants exceeding prescribed limits, and operating without the requisite environmental approvals.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is hereby directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 15,000,000/- (Rupees Fifteen Million only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment. This penalty reflects the gravity and duration of the environmental damage caused, while also acknowledging the Respondent's demonstrated commitment and investment towards future remediation.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
