**JUDGMENT**
**Introduction**
This complaint, instituted on October 26, 2023, by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, seeks redress against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Complainant contends that the Respondent's industrial operations are causing significant environmental pollution by discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of the prescribed standards, thereby posing a serious threat to public health and the environment. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal is vested with the jurisdiction to adjudicate such matters under Section 21 of the Act.
**Allegations**
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited are as follows:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Effluent:** It is alleged that the Respondent's manufacturing facility is discharging industrial wastewater directly into a public drain/sewer system without adequate treatment, or treatment that fails to meet the NEQS for industrial effluents. An inspection conducted by EPA officials on September 15, 2023, revealed visible discoloration and foul odor in the discharged effluent.
2.  **Exceedance of NEQS for Effluent:** Laboratory analysis of samples collected during the September 15, 2023, inspection showed parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeding the permissible limits set forth in the NEQS. Specifically, BOD was found to be 350 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD 800 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), and TSS 450 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L).
3.  **Air Emissions Exceeding NEQS:** The complaint further alleges that the Respondent's boiler stack emissions and other process emissions contain particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) in concentrations higher than the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. While specific measurements were not provided in the initial complaint, the EPA's monitoring activities indicated consistent non-compliance.
4.  **Improper Solid Waste Management:** The Respondent is accused of improper disposal of hazardous and non-hazardous solid
waste, including dye sludge and other manufacturing by-products, in an unscientific and environmentally unsound manner. It is alleged that waste is often dumped in open areas adjacent to the facility or mixed with municipal waste without proper segregation, treatment, or disposal, in violation of environmental regulations regarding hazardous waste management and posing risks of soil and groundwater contamination.
**Respondent's Defense**
Upon receipt of the
notice of this complaint, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, appeared before the Tribunal through its authorized representative and counsel. In its written statement, the Respondent generally denied the allegations, asserting that it operates an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and has installed air pollution control devices to comply with environmental standards. It contended that any exceedances noted by the EPA were either isolated incidents

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
