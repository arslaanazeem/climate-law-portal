**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 13/2018 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks adjudication of alleged environmental pollution and non-compliance with the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The proceedings commenced upon the filing of the complaint on 15.03.2018, followed by the issuance of notices to the Respondent, who subsequently appeared through counsel and submitted a written reply. The Tribunal heard arguments from both sides and meticulously examined the evidence presented.
The Complainant's primary allegations are that the Respondent has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into a public drain, which ultimately merges into a major water body, thereby causing severe water pollution. Specifically, the EPA contended that multiple inspections conducted at the Respondent's premises revealed a persistent failure to operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively, or at times, its complete bypass. The Complainant further alleged that laboratory analyses of effluent samples collected on various dates,
specifically on 05.02.2018, 18.02.2018, and 01.03.2018, consistently showed levels of various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) limits. These findings, documented in inspection reports and analytical certificates appended to the complaint, form the core of the Complainant's assertion that the Respondent is in continuous violation of Sections 11 and 16 of PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS for industrial effluents.
In response, the Respondent, through its counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was contended that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited operates a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) which is fully functional and designed to meet NEQS. The Respondent argued that any instances of non-compliance, if at all, were isolated incidents attributable to unforeseen operational glitches, power fluctuations, or maintenance activities, and not indicative of a deliberate or persistent failure to comply. Furthermore, the Respondent submitted evidence of substantial investments made in upgrading its ETP, regular maintenance logs, and internal monitoring reports which, it claimed, demonstrated its commitment to environmental protection. It was also argued that the sampling methodology adopted by the EPA on certain occasions was flawed or not conducted in the presence of the Respondent's authorized representatives, thereby rendering the sample analyses unreliable.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments and evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant has produced a series of inspection reports, photographic evidence, and certified laboratory analysis reports from multiple dates, all consistently pointing towards the discharge of effluent exceeding NEQS limits. The consistency and frequency of these violations, as documented by the EPA, undermine the Respondent's claim of isolated incidents or temporary glitches. While the Respondent has indeed shown proof of an ETP installation and some maintenance records, the efficacy of its operation at the time of the alleged violations remains questionable in light of the repeated adverse laboratory findings. The argument regarding sampling methodology was not sufficiently substantiated with
credible counter-evidence or expert testimony to cast doubt on the EPA's established protocols. The Tribunal notes that environmental violations, particularly those involving the discharge of untreated industrial waste into public waterways, pose a serious threat to public health and the ecosystem, necessitating strict enforcement of environmental laws. The repeated nature of the non-compliance, as evidenced by the consistent exceedances of NEQS parameters over several months, indicates a systemic failure on the part of the Respondent to effectively manage its effluent discharge, rather than mere isolated incidents.
In light of the overwhelming evidence presented by the Complainant, the Tribunal finds that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, has indeed been discharging industrial effluent in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards and has failed to operate its Effluent Treatment Plant effectively, thereby violating the provisions of Sections 11 and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Respondent's arguments, while acknowledging some efforts towards environmental compliance, do not sufficiently negate the specific findings of non-compliance documented by the EPA on the dates in question.
**ORDER**
For the foregoing reasons, the Tribunal holds M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, the Respondent herein, responsible for the environmental violations as alleged by the Complainant.
It is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is found guilty of violating Sections 11 and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluents.
2.  The Respondent is directed to ensure immediate and continuous compliance with all applicable provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, particularly with regard to the effective operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant and the quality of its discharged effluent.
3.  A penalty of **PKR 750,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand Only)** is hereby imposed on the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. This penalty shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
4.  In addition to the penalty, the Respondent is directed to submit a comprehensive compliance report, duly verified by an independent environmental consultant approved by the EPA, within sixty (60) days, detailing the measures taken to ensure sustained compliance with NEQS.
5.  Failure to pay the imposed penalty within the stipulated time, or continued non-compliance with the NEQS, shall render the Respondent liable to further penalties and legal action, including potential closure of operations, as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
6.  The costs of these proceedings are to be borne by the Respondent.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **13 April, 2023**.
