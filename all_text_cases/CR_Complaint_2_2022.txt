PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL
LAHORE
COMPLAINT NO. 2/2022
Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore
...Complainant
VERSUS
M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited
...Respondent
**Presided by:Muhammad Irfan
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 2/2022, initiated by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (the Respondent), a prominent industrial unit engaged in chemical manufacturing. The complaint was filed under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), specifically alleging persistent violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with regulatory directives concerning industrial effluent discharge. The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, and vested with the jurisdiction to hear and decide such complaints, has meticulously reviewed the facts and legal arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, located in the industrial zone, has been continuously discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the nearby Hudiara Drain, which subsequently flows into agricultural lands and water bodies. These discharges were asserted to contain pollutants significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, particularly in parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH levels, and the presence of heavy metals. The EPA contended that despite multiple inspections, issuance of show-cause notices, and directives to install and effectively operate a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the Respondent failed to take substantive corrective measures, thereby causing severe environmental degradation, posing a threat to public health, aquatic life, and agricultural productivity in the vicinity. The complaint further highlighted the Respondent's failure to obtain or adhere to the conditions of an Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE) for their operations, particularly concerning waste management and discharge.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included a series of inspection reports dated from January 2021 to March 2022, conducted by EPA officials, which consistently documented the discharge of foul-smelling, discolored effluent. Crucially, the Complainant submitted detailed laboratory analysis reports from samples collected on various dates (e.g., 15.02.2021, 01.07.2021, 10.11.2021, and 20.02.2022) by accredited laboratories, confirming that the effluent parameters, including COD (ranging from 500-800 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L), BOD5 (ranging from 250-400 mg/L against NEQS of 80 mg/L), TSS (ranging from 300-550 mg/L against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and pH (ranging from 4.0-5.5 or 9.5-11.0 against NEQS of 6.0-9.0), were significantly above the prescribed NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the direct discharge pipe and the polluted state of the Hudiara Drain were also submitted. Copies of show-cause notices issued on 05.03.2021 and 15.01.2022, along with the Respondent's evasive and non-committal replies, formed part of the evidence. Witness testimonies from EPA
officials involved in inspections and sample collection further corroborated the documentary evidence, affirming the persistent nature of the violations.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. They argued that they had initiated steps towards upgrading their effluent treatment facilities, citing technical challenges and delays in procurement and installation of advanced machinery. They contended that their operations were vital for the local economy and employment, and that any alleged exceedances were temporary aberrations rather than persistent violations. The Respondent also questioned the methodology of sample collection and analysis by the Complainant, suggesting potential procedural irregularities, though no concrete evidence to support these claims was presented. They further submitted that they were in the process of applying for an updated EIA/IEE.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions of both parties, examined the voluminous documentary evidence, and taken into account the legal framework of PEPA, 1997. It is evident from the consistent and repeated analytical reports, coupled with photographic and videographic evidence and corroborated by witness testimonies, that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been in continuous and flagrant violation of the NEQS for industrial effluents. The discrepancies between the discharged effluent parameters and the prescribed NEQS are significant and persistent, indicating either a complete lack of functional ETP or its grossly inadequate operation. The Respondent's arguments regarding technical challenges and ongoing efforts, while noted, do not absolve them of their legal
obligations under PEPA, 1997. Their claims of procedural irregularities in sample collection were unsubstantiated and did not provide any credible counter-evidence to refute the consistent findings of the accredited laboratories. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent's arguments, while acknowledging some efforts, do not absolve them of liability for the documented, widespread, and persistent environmental damage caused by their operations.
The Tribunal notes that the Respondent, as a prominent industrial entity, bears a significant responsibility to operate in an environmentally sound manner and to strictly adhere to the NEQS. The adverse impacts of their untreated effluent discharge on the Hudiara Drain, agricultural lands, and the health of local communities are severe and undeniable. Such conduct constitutes a clear and flagrant breach of Section 11(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, which prohibits the discharge of effluents exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Furthermore, the Respondent's failure to obtain or adhere to the conditions of an Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE) for their operations, particularly concerning waste management and discharge, is a direct violation of Section 12 of the Act. The persistent nature of these violations, despite repeated show-cause notices and directives from the EPA, demonstrates a serious disregard for environmental laws and the well-being of the surrounding environment and populace.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the overwhelming and irrefutable evidence presented by the Complainant and the Respondent's failure to provide a satisfactory legal or factual defense for their persistent non-compliance, this Tribunal hereby finds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited **GUILTY** of violating the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, specifically Section 11(1) and Section 12, as detailed in the complaint.
Accordingly, pursuant to the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the following orders and penalties are issued:
1.  **Immediate Cessation of Illegal Discharge:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent that does not conform to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the Hudiara Drain or any other water body.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
