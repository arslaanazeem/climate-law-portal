This complaint, bearing No. 24/2021, was instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Complainant sought appropriate legal action, including penalties and remedial measures, against the Respondent for causing environmental pollution.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, was operating its textile manufacturing facility without adequately treating its industrial effluent, leading to the discharge of pollutants into the environment in quantities exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) officials on various dates, including 15.03.2021 and 29.03.2021, the Respondent’s effluent discharge was found to be highly contaminated. The Complainant further alleged that despite repeated issuance of Environmental Improvement Notices and Show Cause Notices by the EPA, requiring the Respondent to install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and to comply with NEQS, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures. This persistent non-compliance, according to the Complainant, constituted a blatant disregard for environmental laws, posing a serious threat to public health and the surrounding ecosystem, particularly impacting local
water bodies, agricultural land, and the health of communities residing nearby.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, despite being duly served with notices and provided ample opportunity to present its defense, largely failed to submit a comprehensive and satisfactory response that could effectively rebut the allegations leveled by the Complainant. While the Respondent made general assertions of compliance, no concrete evidence, such as independent laboratory reports demonstrating NEQS compliance, or a detailed plan for the installation and operationalization of an effective ETP, was presented before this Tribunal. The record, including the inspection reports and laboratory analyses submitted by the EPA, consistently indicated the discharge of effluent exceeding the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. These reports, prepared by accredited laboratories, stand as credible evidence in support of the Complainant's assertions.
This Tribunal, having carefully considered the complaint, the documentary evidence presented by the Complainant, and the limited response from the Respondent, finds that the Respondent has indeed violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Section 11 (Prohibition of discharge or emission of effluents or waste) and Section 14 (Prohibition of import of hazardous waste), and has failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards. The persistent failure to treat industrial effluent to acceptable standards, despite repeated warnings and notices from the EPA, demonstrates a serious dereliction of environmental responsibility. Such conduct not only contravenes national environmental laws but also poses significant risks to public health and the ecological balance.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Violation:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent that exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Installation/Upgradation of ETP:** The Respondent shall, within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, install, commission, and ensure the full operationalization of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) that is capable of consistently treating its industrial effluent to meet all parameters of the NEQS. A detailed action plan for the same shall be submitted to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) within **fifteen (15) days** of this order.
3.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly self-monitoring reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, to the EPA for a period of one year, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS.
4.  **Environmental Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Nishat Mills Limited for the violations of PEPA, 1997 and NEQS. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** of this order.
5.  **Default Clause:** In case of failure to comply with any part of this order, particularly the installation and operationalization of the ETP within the stipulated timeframe, the Environmental Protection Agency is authorized to take further legal action, including but not limited to, sealing of the industrial unit or imposing further daily penalties as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997, without further recourse to this Tribunal.
6.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
This order shall be communicated to all concerned parties forthwith.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
