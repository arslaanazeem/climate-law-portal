This judgment addresses Complaint No. 52/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in manufacturing packaging materials and allied products, having its facility located at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Gulberg III, Lahore. The complaint, lodged on May 15, 2022, seeks punitive action and remediation for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS").
The Complainant's case rests on a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted at the Respondent's industrial premises. It is alleged that the Respondent has been operating in contravention of environmental laws by discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which eventually drains into natural water bodies, thereby causing water pollution. Furthermore, the Complainant asserts that the Respondent's industrial processes, particularly its boiler operations and manufacturing activities, emit air pollutants exceeding the prescribed NEQS limits, contributing to ambient air quality degradation. Specific instances cited include:
a.  An inspection conducted on February 20, 2022, which revealed visible signs of effluent discharge without adequate treatment. Samples of effluent were collected from the discharge point.
b.  Laboratory analysis of the collected effluent samples, conducted by the EPA-accredited laboratory on March 5, 2022, indicated levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents.
c.  Stack emission monitoring conducted on April 1,
2022, revealed particulate matter (PM) and oxides of nitrogen (NOx) emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks exceeding the NEQS limits, further substantiated by subsequent monitoring on April 20, 2022.
Despite notices issued by the EPA on March 10, 2022, and April 25, 2022, requiring the Respondent to take corrective measures and provide an action plan, the Complainant contends that the Respondent failed to adequately address the non-compliance within the stipulated timeframes, necessitating the filing of the present complaint. The Complainant sought an order for the Respondent to cease its polluting activities, install appropriate pollution control equipment, and impose a penalty as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions made by the Complainant and the documentary evidence provided, including inspection reports and laboratory analyses, which were duly authenticated. The Respondent, despite being properly served with notices of these proceedings and given ample opportunity to appear and defend the allegations, chose not to contest the complaint or present any counter-evidence to refute the findings of the EPA. This absence of rebuttal leads the Tribunal to accept the veracity of the Complainant's assertions and the evidence presented as uncontroverted.
Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, explicitly prohibits the discharge of any effluent or waste into the environment in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards. Similarly, Section 15 mandates compliance with NEQS for air emissions from industrial sources. The documented evidence unequivocally demonstrates a breach of these provisions by the Respondent. The consistent exceedance of NEQS for both industrial effluent and air emissions constitutes a clear violation of environmental laws, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
Therefore, based on the uncontroverted evidence and the Respondent's failure to appear and defend the allegations, this Tribunal finds M/s Packages Limited guilty of violating Sections 11 and 15 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the aforesaid findings, this Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Immediate Cessation of Non-Compliance:** The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and to ensure that all air emissions from its facility comply with the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards forthwith.
2.  **Installation and Upgradation of Pollution Control Systems:** The Respondent shall, within a period of **six (6) months** from the date of this order, install, upgrade, and fully operationalize an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) that are capable of consistently achieving the NEQS for all discharged effluents and emitted gases.
3.  **Submission of Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including timelines for the installation/upgradation of the ETP and APCDs, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** of this order.
4.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** Upon completion of the installation/upgradation, the Respondent shall conduct regular monitoring of its effluent and air emissions through an EPA-accredited laboratory and submit quarterly compliance reports to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of **two (2) years**.
5.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the aforementioned violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **sixty (60) days** of this order.
6.  **Daily Fine for Non-Compliance:** In the event of continued non-compliance with this order beyond the stipulated deadlines, the Respondent shall be liable to pay an additional fine of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** for each day of default, in accordance with Section 17(2) of PEPA, 1997.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)**.
This order shall be enforced strictly, and any further violations or failure to comply with these directives will result in more stringent actions as provided under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
