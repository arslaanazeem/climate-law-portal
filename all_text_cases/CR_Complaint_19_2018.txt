This judgment addresses Complaint No. 19/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, seeking redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint, lodged on October 25, 2018, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 19 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate upon environmental contraventions and impose penalties as prescribed by law. The Tribunal is seized with the responsibility to ensure adherence to environmental regulations, protect public health, and safeguard the ecological integrity of the province.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Ghani Glass Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating in persistent violation of environmental laws. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluents into the public sewerage system/open drains and emitting air pollutants from its stacks in excess of the prescribed NEQS. The complaint detailed multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials between April 2018 and September 2018. During these inspections, samples of industrial effluent and stack emissions were collected. Laboratory analysis reports, appended to the complaint, purportedly confirmed that parameters such as Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), pH, and specific heavy metals in the effluent, and Particulate Matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) in the air emissions, consistently exceeded the permissible limits set by the NEQS. The Complainant further stated that several warning letters,
environmental protection orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, and show-cause notices were issued to M/s Ghani Glass Limited, directing them to take immediate corrective measures and comply with the NEQS. Despite these repeated communications and directives, the Respondent allegedly failed to implement adequate pollution control measures, thereby continuing to discharge pollutants in contravention of the law. The Complainant sought a declaration that the Respondent had violated environmental laws, imposition of a penalty as provided under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, and a mandatory direction for the Respondent to install and operate appropriate pollution control equipment to meet the NEQS.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and contested the complaint. While acknowledging its industrial operations, the Respondent generally denied the persistent and willful violations as alleged. It was contended that the Respondent had made efforts to upgrade its pollution control systems and was in the process of installing advanced effluent treatment plants (ETPs) and air filtration systems. The Respondent further argued that some of the samples might have been collected without proper protocols or that the analytical reports were not entirely conclusive of persistent non-compliance. It was also submitted that the Respondent is a significant industrial contributor to the national economy and employs a large workforce, and that any punitive action should be balanced with economic considerations and
the substantial investments already made towards environmental compliance. They requested the Tribunal to take a holistic view, consider their ongoing efforts, and perhaps provide a structured timeline for full compliance rather than imposing immediate stringent penalties that could adversely affect their business operations and workforce.
**Tribunal's Deliberations and Findings:**
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the pleadings, the documentary evidence submitted by both parties, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses, warning letters, EPOs, and show-cause notices issued by the Complainant. The Tribunal also considered the arguments advanced by the learned counsels for the Complainant and the Respondent.
It is evident from the consistent and repeated nature of the laboratory analysis reports, spanning several months and multiple inspections, that the Respondent's industrial operations have indeed been discharging effluents and emitting air pollutants in excess of the NEQS. The Complainant's evidence, particularly the detailed analytical reports, provides a strong prima facie case of persistent non-compliance. While the Respondent has claimed ongoing efforts and investments, these claims, though noted, do not negate the documented violations that have occurred over a prolonged period, leading to potential environmental degradation and public health risks. The issuance of multiple warning letters and EPOs prior to the complaint further underscores the Respondent's failure to take timely and effective corrective actions despite being duly notified. The argument regarding economic considerations, while relevant in a broader policy context, cannot override the statutory mandate of this Tribunal to enforce environmental laws and protect the environment from pollution. The PEPA, 1997, and the NEQS are designed to ensure a clean and healthy environment, and continuous violations by industrial units cannot be overlooked under the guise of economic contribution. Therefore, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has violated the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**ORDER:**

1.  **Declaration of Violation:** It is hereby declared that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has been found in persistent violation of Section 12 and Section 15 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, having failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards regarding the discharge of industrial effluents and emission of air pollutants.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
