This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, alleging contraventions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Act, exercises jurisdiction to adjudicate matters concerning environmental pollution and impose penalties for violations. The present proceedings were initiated to address persistent environmental non-compliance by the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, whose operations are alleged to have caused significant environmental degradation.
The Complainant's case rests on the fundamental allegation that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has consistently failed to comply with environmental laws and regulations. Specifically, it is alleged that the Respondent has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into public drains/water bodies, thereby exceeding the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to, Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Furthermore, the Complainant alleges that the Respondent operates without a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its current operations or has failed to adhere to the conditions stipulated in any previously issued NOC. It is also contended that the Respondent has neglected to install and maintain appropriate effluent treatment plants (ETP) and air pollution control devices, or that existing facilities are either non-functional or insufficient to meet the NEQS. The complaint further highlights the Respondent's failure to submit regular environmental monitoring reports as required by law, indicating a systemic disregard for environmental compliance and regulatory oversight.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2016, 28.06.2016, and 12.09.2016, compiled by EPA officials, detailing observations of direct discharge of industrial effluent and visible signs of pollution. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the Punjab Environmental Laboratory (PEL) for effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates. These reports consistently showed parameters significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents, particularly high levels of BOD, COD, and TSS. Photographic evidence depicting the discharge points and the color/turbidity of the effluent was also presented. Witness statements from the inspecting officers corroborated the findings of the inspection reports and sampling procedures. Notices of violation and environmental protection orders previously issued to the Respondent, demonstrating a history of non-compliance and the EPA's repeated efforts to secure compliance, were also tendered as evidence.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its authorized representative, vehemently denied the allegations. While acknowledging the operation of a textile manufacturing unit, the Respondent contended that it had installed an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was making continuous efforts to ensure compliance with environmental standards. The Respondent argued that any exceedances noted by the Complainant were either isolated incidents, attributable to operational upsets, or resulted from faulty sampling and analysis procedures by the EPA. They submitted an internal monitoring report from a private laboratory, which purportedly showed compliance on certain dates, and asserted that they were in the process of upgrading their ETP. Financial constraints and the scale of their operations were cited as reasons for any perceived delays in achieving full compliance. The Respondent also presented a copy of an application for an NOC, claiming proactive engagement with the regulatory framework, though without producing a valid, currently effective NOC.
The legal framework governing this case is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of the Act prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level in excess of the NEQS. Section 12 mandates that no proponent of a project shall commence construction or operation unless he has filed an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) with the EPA and obtained an NOC. Section 16 grants the EPA the power to issue Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) for violations of the Act, and Section 17 prescribes penalties for such violations, including fines and imprisonment. The National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial and Municipal Eff
luents, as well as for Air Emissions, are crucial benchmarks against which compliance is measured. These standards are legally binding and form the bedrock of environmental protection efforts in the country.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the documentary and oral evidence presented. The Complainant's case is substantially supported by a series of consistent inspection reports
and corroborated by credible laboratory analysis reports from the Punjab Environmental Laboratory. These reports consistently demonstrate that the effluent discharged by M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited significantly exceeds the prescribed NEQS for multiple parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS). **V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
