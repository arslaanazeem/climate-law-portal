**ORDER (DISMISSAL)**

This complaint has been filed under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) against Saif Power Limited alleging violations of National Environmental Quality Standards.

**ADMISSIBILITY OF PHOTOCOPIED DOCUMENTS**

The Complainant has produced inspection reports and laboratory analyses in the form of photocopies. The law on this point is no longer res integra. In 2023 CLD 383, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal has authoritatively held that photocopies of inspection reports and laboratory results are inadmissible in evidence before this Tribunal in the absence of the original documents or a certificate of authenticity under the Qanun-e-Shahadat Order, 1984. The Complainant has neither produced original documents nor offered any explanation for their absence. The evidentiary foundation of this complaint accordingly collapses and the matter cannot proceed.

**ORDER**

For the foregoing reasons, this complaint is hereby **DISMISSED**. The dismissal is, however, without prejudice to the right of the Punjab EPA to re-inspect the Respondent's facility and, if violations are established upon fresh inspection with proper documentation, to re-file the complaint before this Tribunal with original documents and after expiry of the EPO compliance period.

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct a fresh inspection of the Respondent's premises within thirty (30) days and to take such further action as the law permits.

No order as to costs.
