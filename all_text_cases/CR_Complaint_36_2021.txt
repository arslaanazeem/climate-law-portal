This judgment addresses Complaint No. 36/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeking adjudication on alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations concerning the discharge of industrial effluents. The Tribunal, having duly considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties, proceeds to render its decision.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Packages Limited, a prominent industrial unit involved in manufacturing operations located in Lahore, was found to be discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/water body without adequate treatment, thereby failing to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. Specifically, it was contended that during routine inspections conducted by EPA officials, samples of the Respondent's treated effluent were collected and subsequently analyzed at the EPA's accredited laboratory. The laboratory reports, annexed with the complaint, purportedly revealed concentrations of various parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS), significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS limits. The Complainant asserted that these violations constituted a contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and were detrimental to the environment and public health, warranting punitive action and remedial measures. An Environmental Protection Order (EPO) bearing No. EPA/LHR/EPO/2021/45, dated April 15, 2021, was issued to the Respondent, directing immediate compliance, which the Complainant claimed was not fully adhered to.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of documents, including the official inspection report dated March 20, 2021, detailing the observations made at the Respondent's premises, particularly concerning the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) operations. Crucially, the laboratory analysis report from the Punjab Environmental Protection Department's laboratory, dated April 5, 2021, was submitted, indicating BOD levels of 150 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), COD levels of 400 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and TSS levels of 250 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). A subsequent follow-up inspection report dated May 10, 2021, and an accompanying lab report dated May 25, 2021, similarly showed persistent non-compliance, albeit with slight improvements, indicating BOD at 120 mg/L, COD at 300 mg/L, and TSS at 200 mg/L. The Complainant also produced
the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) bearing No. EPA/LHR/EPO/2021/45, dated April 15, 2021, which had directed the Respondent to ensure compliance within a stipulated period and to submit a comprehensive plan for ETP optimization. The Complainant further asserted that despite the issuance of the EPO and subsequent reminders, the Respondent had failed
to achieve full and sustained compliance with the NEQS and the specific directions outlined in the EPO, thereby necessitating the filing of the instant complaint before this Tribunal.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
