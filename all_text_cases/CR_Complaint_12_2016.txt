This judgment addresses Complaint No. 12/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS").
The EPA's allegations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited primarily revolve around the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies, and the emission of air pollutants from its industrial processes. Specifically, the complainant asserted that despite repeated warnings and issuance of several Show Cause Notices, the Respondent failed to install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and adequate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) in accordance with the prescribed NEQS. It was further alleged that routine monitoring and inspection by EPA officials revealed that the parameters of the discharged effluent, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and temperature, consistently exceeded the permissible limits stipulated under the NEQS. Similarly, stack emissions were found to be non-compliant with air quality standards. The EPA contended that these continuous violations posed a significant threat to public health and the environment, causing water and air pollution in the vicinity and beyond.
To substantiate its claims, the complainant presented a series of inspection reports, including those dated 15.03.2016, 28.05.2016, and 10.08.2016, conducted by the environmental inspectors of the EPA. These reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA Environmental Monitoring Laboratory, which provided scientific evidence of the non-compliance. For instance, the analysis report dated 20.03.2016, pertaining to an effluent sample collected on 15.03.2016, indicated BOD levels of 350 mg/L (against NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), COD levels of 800 mg/L (against NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), and TSS of 450 mg/L (against NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). Similar exceedances were noted in subsequent reports. Photographic evidence depicting the discharge of coloured effluent directly into the drain was also submitted. Furthermore, copies of the Show Cause Notices issued to the Respondent on 25.03.2016 and 15.06.2016, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, formed part of the evidence. The EPA also
presented legal arguments outlining the Respondent's statutory obligations under PEPA, 1997, particularly Section 11, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in excess of the NEQS. The complainant prayed for the imposition of penalties as prescribed under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, and for a mandatory injunction directing the Respondent to
install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and adequate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) in full compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and to pay penalties as provided under the law.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and submitted a written reply to the complaint. In its defense, the Respondent generally denied the allegations, stating that it had taken steps to improve its environmental performance and was in the process of upgrading its pollution control infrastructure. However, the Respondent failed to provide concrete evidence, such as recent compliance reports, operational ETP/APCD data, or verifiable progress on upgrades, that could effectively counter the specific and detailed evidence presented by the EPA. The Tribunal noted that the Respondent’s submissions largely consisted of general assurances rather than specific refutations of the scientific data and inspection findings. No independent expert reports were presented by the Respondent to challenge the EPA's laboratory analyses.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
