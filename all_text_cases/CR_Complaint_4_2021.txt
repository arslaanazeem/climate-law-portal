This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located at Raiwind Road, Lahore. The complaint, lodged on February 15, 2021, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), alleging persistent violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with statutory obligations. The Tribunal, having heard the learned counsel for both parties and meticulously reviewed the presented evidence, proceeds to render its decision.
The gravamen of the complaint is that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into a public drain, which ultimately merges with the River Ravi, thereby causing significant water pollution. Specifically, the EPA’s allegations include:
a. Failure to operate and maintain a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating industrial wastewater to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
b. Discharge of effluent samples showing parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels consistently exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS.
c. Non-compliance with the conditions stipulated in the No Objection Certificate (NOC) previously issued by the EPA, particularly concerning regular monitoring and submission of compliance reports.
d. Failure to respond adequately to multiple show-cause notices issued by the EPA, demanding corrective measures and submission of environmental management plans. The EPA further alleged that the respondent’s activities posed a significant threat to aquatic life, public health, and the overall ecological balance of the area.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence, including:
i. Multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between August 2020 and January 2021, detailing observations of direct discharge and the condition of the ETP (or lack thereof).
ii. Laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA Environmental Monitoring Laboratory, dated September 15, 2020, November 10, 2020, and January 20, 2021. These reports consistently demonstrated that the discharged effluent samples exceeded NEQS for BOD (averaging 150 mg/L against a limit of 80 mg/L), COD (averaging 450 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L), TSS (averaging 250 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L), and exhibited a pH range of 4.5-5.5 against the permissible range of 6-9.
iii. Copies of show-cause notices issued to M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited on October 1, 2020, and December 5, 2020, along with their unsatisfactory replies or lack thereof.
iv. Testimony from Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Analyst at EPA, who explained the sampling methodology, chain of custody, and the interpretation of laboratory results, affirming the non-compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its counsel, denied the allegations, contending that they had indeed installed an ETP and were making efforts to comply with environmental regulations. They submitted:
i. A copy of a purchase order for certain ETP components, dated March
10, 2020, asserting their commitment to environmental protection. They further contended that the installation of the ETP was a complex and time-consuming process, and they were in the final stages of commissioning and optimization. They challenged the frequency and methodology of EPA's sampling, suggesting that the samples might not be representative of their overall discharge, and claimed that recent internal monitoring showed improved effluent quality
. The Tribunal, having considered the submissions and evidence from both sides, finds the Respondent's defense to be largely unsubstantiated. The mere submission of a purchase order for ETP components, dated prior to the period of consistent violations documented by the EPA, does not absolve the Respondent of its responsibility to comply with environmental standards. While the installation and optimization of an ETP can be a complex process, the continuous and documented discharge of effluent significantly exceeding NEQS over several months, coupled with the failure to adequately respond to statutory notices, indicates a clear dereliction of duty and a lack of effective environmental management.
The Respondent's challenge to the EPA's sampling methodology and claims of internal monitoring showing improved quality were not supported by any credible, independently verified evidence, such as certified laboratory reports from the same periods or expert testimony to counter the Complainant's detailed presentation. In contrast, the Complainant's evidence, including multiple inspection reports, consistent laboratory analysis from a certified EPA laboratory, and expert testimony, provides a robust and compelling case of persistent environmental violations. The parameters consistently exceeding NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH levels, as detailed in the laboratory reports, unequivocally demonstrate the hazardous nature of the discharged effluent and its potential for severe environmental degradation.
Therefore, this Tribunal concludes that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has failed to comply with its obligations under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, specifically violating Section 11 (Prohibition of discharge or emission of pollution in excess of standards), Section 16 (Prohibition of import of hazardous waste), and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed thereunder. The persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into a public drain, ultimately affecting the River Ravi, constitutes a serious environmental offense with potential long-term adverse impacts on water quality, aquatic ecosystems, and public health.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Complaint Upheld:** The complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby **upheld**.
2.  **Cessation of Discharge:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is **immediately directed** to cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into any public drain or water body.
3.  **Operational ETP:** The Respondent shall ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully operational, optimized, and consistently capable of treating all industrial wastewater to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for all relevant parameters, including BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, **within a period of sixty (60) days** from the date of this order.
4.  **Compliance Plan and Reporting:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited shall submit a detailed ETP operational and maintenance plan, along with a compliance schedule, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, **within fifteen (15) days** of this order. Furthermore, the Respondent shall submit monthly effluent analysis reports from an EPA-certified laboratory to the EPA for a period of six (6) consecutive months, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS.
5.  **Financial Penalty:** For the persistent and grave violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby imposed a **penalty of Rupees One Million (PKR 1,000,000/-)**. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, **within thirty (30) days** of the date of this order.
6.  **Continuing Fine:** In addition to the above penalty, should M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited fail to achieve full compliance with NEQS as directed in paragraph 3 within the stipulated sixty (60) days, a further **additional fine of Rupees One Hundred Thousand (PKR 100,000/-) shall be imposed for each day** during which such contravention or failure continues, until full compliance is achieved and certified by the EPA.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **Rupees Fifty Thousand (PKR 50,000/-)**, to be paid to the Complainant (EPA) within thirty (30) days.
8.  **EPA Monitoring:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is authorized to conduct regular and unannounced inspections of the Respondent's premises and collect effluent samples to verify compliance with this order and the NEQS.
Failure to comply with any part of this order shall entail further legal action and more stringent penalties as provided under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
