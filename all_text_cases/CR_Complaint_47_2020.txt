This complaint was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, under Section 17 read with Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging contraventions of environmental laws by M/s Ghani Glass Limited, a manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The Tribunal's jurisdiction in this matter is derived from the provisions of PEPA, 1997, which empower it to hear and decide complaints regarding violations of environmental standards and regulations.
The Complainant's case rests on the following allegations: That M/s Ghani Glass Limited has been operating its glass manufacturing unit without adequate environmental controls, leading to significant pollution. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial emissions into the ambient air, particularly from its furnace stacks, consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Particulate Matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2). Furthermore, it was contended that the wastewater discharged from the Respondent's facility into the public drainage system contained levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) far exceeding the prescribed NEQS, thereby causing water pollution and posing a threat to public health and aquatic ecosystems. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to implement effective pollution control measures.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated 15.03.2019, 22.08.2019, and 10.01.2020, conducted by EPA officials. These reports detailed visual observations of dark, opaque plumes emanating from the Respondent's stacks and discolored effluent being discharged. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from an accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for air emissions (PM at 650 mg/Nm3 against a standard of 400 mg/Nm3; NOx at 1200 mg/Nm3 against a standard of 600 mg/Nm3) and wastewater parameters (COD at 450 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L; BOD at 200 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L; TSS at 300 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L). The Complainant also produced copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 01.04.2019 and 05.09.2019, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, which largely acknowledged the issues but failed to demonstrate concrete and timely corrective actions. An expert witness from the EPA's technical wing also testified, explaining the methodology of sampling and analysis, and the potential environmental and health impacts of the observed exceedances.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its learned counsel, contended that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection. It was argued that the facility has installed an Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for air pollution control and an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) for wastewater management. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
