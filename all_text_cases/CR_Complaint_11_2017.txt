This judgment addresses Complaint No. 11/2017 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located at [Hypothetical Address, e.g., 26-KM Ferozepur Road, Lahore]. The complaint alleges persistent violations of environmental laws and standards, particularly concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and air emissions, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. The Tribunal, constituted under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), has jurisdiction to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental standards.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, alleged that the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility was operating in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of PEPA, 1997. Specifically, the allegations included: (a) persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/natural water bodies, exceeding the prescribed NEQS for various parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals; (b) emission of air pollutants, including particulate matter and gaseous pollutants, beyond permissible limits, thereby contributing to air pollution in the vicinity; (c) failure to install and effectively operate an adequate Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs); and (d) operating without a valid Environmental Approval/No-Objection Certificate (NOC) for certain operational aspects or expansions, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997. The Complainant further contended that despite numerous warnings, show-cause notices, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent failed to take substantive corrective measures, necessitating the present complaint for enforcement and imposition of penalties.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a substantial body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2016, 28.07.2016, 10.01.2017, and 05.05.2017, conducted by EPA officials, which consistently noted the absence of a fully functional ETP and visible signs of pollution. Crucially, the Complainant submitted detailed laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, dated 20.03.2016, 02.08.2016, 14.01.2017, and 08.05.2017. These reports unequivocally demonstrated that the Respondent's discharged effluent samples consistently exceeded the NEQS limits for BOD (e.g., recorded values up to 400 mg/L against a limit of 80 mg/L), COD (up to 1200 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L), TSS (up to 350 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L), and also showed elevated levels of certain heavy metals. Photographic and videographic evidence captured during the inspections further corroborated the visual observations of effluent discharge and emissions. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued
on 25.04.2016, 15.09.2016, and 20.02.2017, along with the Respondent's evasive or inadequate responses, were also presented to demonstrate a pattern of non-compliance despite repeated official interventions.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, represented by its legal counsel, Mr. Zahid Ali, contested the allegations. While acknowledging certain operational challenges inherent in textile manufacturing, the Respondent primarily argued that they were actively engaged in upgrading their environmental infrastructure. They claimed to have initiated the process of installing an advanced Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control equipment, and that the construction was underway. They further contended that some of the samples taken by the EPA might not be representative or that their facility was undergoing temporary maintenance during the inspection periods. The Respondent also submitted an Environmental Management Plan (EMP) outlining future steps towards compliance and sought more time to achieve the prescribed standards, asserting their commitment to environmental protection. They denied operating without a valid Environmental Approval for their core operations, though admitted that certain expansions might require updated clearances.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed
and considered the voluminous record, including the inspection reports, laboratory analysis reports, photographic/videographic evidence, show-cause notices, and the responses thereto, alongside the arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant's evidence, particularly the consistent findings from multiple independent laboratory analyses conducted by an accredited EPA laboratory over an extended period (March 2016 to May 2017), overwhelmingly demonstrates that the Respondent's industrial effluent consistently exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals. The recorded values significantly surpassed the permissible limits, indicating a severe and persistent failure to treat industrial wastewater. The photographic and videographic evidence further corroborated the visual observations of untreated effluent discharge and visible air emissions. The repeated issuance of show-cause notices by the EPA and the Respondent's failure to demonstrate substantive compliance within the stipulated periods underscore a pattern of deliberate or negligent non-compliance.
The Respondent's defence, while acknowledging "operational challenges" and claiming to be "actively engaged in upgrading their environmental infrastructure," failed to provide concrete, verifiable evidence of effective measures taken to abate the pollution during the period of alleged contravention. While an Environmental Management Plan (EMP) outlining future steps is commendable, it does not absolve the Respondent of past and ongoing violations. The contention that samples might not be representative or that the facility was undergoing "temporary maintenance" was not substantiated with specific data or records that could invalidate the EPA's consistent findings. Furthermore, the admission that "certain expansions might require updated clearances" is a tacit acknowledgment of potential non-compliance with Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, which mandates environmental approval for new projects or significant modifications.
The Tribunal finds that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, has been operating in clear contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards. The persistent nature of these violations, as evidenced by the repeated inspections and laboratory analyses, demonstrates a failure to install and effectively operate adequate pollution control mechanisms. The Respondent has also, by its own admission regarding expansions, failed to fully comply with Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, which requires obtaining environmental approval for certain undertakings.
The environmental degradation caused by the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and unchecked air emissions poses a significant threat to public health, water quality, and the surrounding ecosystem, directly undermining the objectives of PEPA, 1997. The Tribunal cannot countenance such flagrant disregard for environmental laws and standards.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby finds M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited guilty of persistent environmental pollution and non-compliance with the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
Therefore, the Tribunal passes the following order:
1.  **Imposition of Fine**: M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby ordered to pay a cumulative fine of **PKR 1,000,000 (Rupees One Million Only)** for the contraventions of Section 11 and Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, as established by the evidence. This fine shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this decision.
2.  **Daily Penalty for Continuing Contravention**: In addition to the above, the Respondent shall pay an additional fine of **PKR 50,000 (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for each day the contravention of NEQS continues, effective from the date of this judgment, until such time as the Respondent fully complies with the NEQS for effluent discharge and air emissions.
3.  **Mandatory Installation and Operation of ETP/APCDs**: The Respondent is directed to immediately install and operationalize a fully functional, state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) that are capable of ensuring that all discharged effluent and air emissions consistently meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards. **FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **12 December, 2022**.
