This judgment concerns a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency
, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as 'the Complainant') against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as 'the Respondent') for alleged violations of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as 'the Act') and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint specifically details that the Respondent's industrial unit was discharging untreated or
inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into public drains/water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. The complaint further alleges that repeated inspections by the Complainant's team revealed consistent exceedances of the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards for various parameters, including but not limited to Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, as evidenced by laboratory reports appended to the complaint.
Despite multiple warning notices issued by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) under Section 16 of the Act, requiring the Respondent to take remedial measures and install an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the Respondent allegedly failed to comply, leading to the filing of the present complaint under Section 17 of the Act read with Section 20 thereof.
The Respondent, through its counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and contested the allegations, primarily arguing that they were in the process of upgrading their ETP and that any non-compliance was temporary and not willful. However, the evidence presented by the Complainant, including detailed inspection reports, photographic evidence, and comprehensive laboratory analysis reports from accredited laboratories, consistently demonstrated the ongoing discharge of sub-standard effluent over a prolonged period.
Having heard the arguments of both parties and meticulously reviewing the documentary evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been in persistent violation of Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent without conforming to the National Environmental Quality Standards. The Respondent's failure to install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant, despite repeated directives, constitutes a serious breach of environmental regulations and demonstrates a disregard for their environmental responsibilities. The gravity of the environmental damage caused and the prolonged nature of the non-compliance warrant a stern response under the Act, particularly Sections 17 and 20, which empower this Tribunal to impose penalties and issue directions for environmental protection.
**NOW, THEREFORE, this Tribunal orders as follows:**
1.  M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, the Respondent, is hereby found guilty of violating Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment.
3.  The Respondent shall, within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, install and commission a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating its industrial effluent to meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards. A compliance report, along with third-party validated laboratory test results, shall be submitted to the Complainant and this Tribunal upon completion.
4.  In default of compliance with paragraph 3, the Respondent's industrial operations shall be liable to be sealed by the Environmental Protection Agency without further notice from this Tribunal.
5.  A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for the persistent violations of environmental laws. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment, failing which it shall be recovered as arrears of land revenue.
6.  The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, shall conduct regular monitoring of the Respondent's premises to ensure strict compliance with this order and the NEQS, submitting quarterly compliance reports to this Tribunal for a period of one year.
7.  The complaint is accordingly disposed of. No order as to costs.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
