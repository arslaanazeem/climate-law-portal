This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), seeking adjudication on alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), has heard the parties and examined the evidence adduced to determine the merits of the complaint.
The Complainant, through its duly authorized representative, Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, instituted the present complaint on 1st February 2024, alleging that the Respondent, an industrial entity engaged in manufacturing operations, has been discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, it was alleged that during a routine inspection conducted on 15th January 2024, the EPA team observed the discharge of partially treated wastewater directly into a public drain connected to the River Ravi. Further, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent had failed to adequately operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite previous advisories, resulting in persistent non-compliance. The allegations included violations of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration, or level in excess of the NEQS, and non-adherence to the conditions stipulated in their Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA) granted under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997. The Complainant sought a direction for the Respondent to immediately comply with NEQS, implement necessary upgrades to its ETP, and impose penalties as prescribed by law.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive inspection report dated 15th January 2024, authored by the EPA inspection team. This report detailed observations of the Respondent's ETP, noting deficiencies in its operational parameters and the visible quality of the discharged effluent. Crucially, the Complainant submitted a laboratory analysis report from the EPA's accredited laboratory, reference No. EPA/LAB/2024/007, dated 20th January 2024. This report confirmed that samples of the effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge point on 15th January 2024 showed concentrations of several key parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and Phenols, significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents. For instance, the BOD was recorded at 120 mg/L against the NEQS limit of 80 mg/L, COD at 350 mg/L against 150 mg/L, and TSS at 250 mg/L against 200 mg/L. The Complainant also produced copies of a Show Cause Notice issued to the Respondent on 22nd January 2024, and the Respondent's unsatisfactory reply dated 29th January 2024, which failed to provide concrete evidence of corrective measures or refute the lab findings. Mr. Asif Mehmood, appearing as a witness for the Complainant, corroborated the inspection findings and the procedural steps taken by the EPA.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
