**Introduction**
1.  This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 27/2021, filed on 15.09.2021, by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant," against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, located at 12-KM, Sheikhupura Road, Lahore, hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent." The Complainant, acting under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address alleged environmental violations committed by the Respondent in the course of its industrial operations. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997, is vested with the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints regarding contraventions of the Act and the rules and regulations made thereunder.
**Allegations**
2.  The Complainant has leveled several serious allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, primarily revolving around non-compliance with environmental standards and statutory requirements. The core allegations are as follows:
    a.  **Operation without Environmental Approval:** That the Respondent commenced and is operating its industrial facility without obtaining the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval from the EPA, as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000.
    b.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent:** That the Respondent is regularly discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public drainage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies,
causing severe water pollution and posing a significant threat to aquatic ecosystems and public health, in direct contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Municipal and Industrial Effluents.
    c.  **Emission of Untreated Air Pollutants:** That the Respondent's industrial processes, particularly its furnaces and stacks
causing the emission of untreated air pollutants into the atmosphere, including particulate matter, nitrogen oxides (NOx), sulfur oxides (SOx), and other hazardous gases, exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Gaseous Emissions. This uncontrolled discharge of pollutants is contributing to air quality degradation, acid rain, and poses serious health risks to the local population, including respiratory illnesses and other chronic conditions

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
