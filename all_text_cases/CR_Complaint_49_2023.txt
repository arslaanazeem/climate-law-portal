**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 49/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit. The complaint was instituted before this Tribunal on October 12, 2023, seeking redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), is vested with the jurisdiction to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and impose penalties for contraventions of the Act and its associated rules and regulations. The core issue revolves around the Respondent's alleged discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the permissible limits set by the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"), thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were generating and discharging untreated or inadequately treated effluent into the surrounding environment, specifically into the local sewerage system which eventually merges with a natural water body. It was specifically averred that during a surprise inspection conducted on September 28, 2023, by a team of EPA officials, samples of the discharged effluent from the Respondent's premises were collected for laboratory analysis. The subsequent analysis, conducted at the certified laboratory of the EPA, revealed that critical parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH value significantly exceeded the NEQS prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000, and subsequent amendments. The Complainant further asserted that prior notices and warnings had been issued to the Respondent regarding their non-compliance, but these had failed to elicit the necessary remedial action, thereby necessitating the initiation of formal legal proceedings before this Tribunal under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997. The Complainant sought an order for the Respondent to cease the illegal discharge, implement an effective effluent treatment plant (ETP), and pay a penalty for the contraventions.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of documentary and testimonial evidence. This included the detailed Inspection Report dated September 28, 2023, prepared by the EPA inspection team, which meticulously documented the site conditions, the sampling methodology, and observations regarding the nature of the effluent. Crucially, the official Laboratory Analysis Report, bearing reference No. EPA/LAB/2023/11-007 and dated October 05, 2023, was submitted. This report explicitly detailed the analytical results, confirming that the BOD levels were found to be 120 mg/L (against NEQS of 80 mg/L), COD at 350 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), TSS at 250 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and pH at 10.5 (against NEQS range of 6-9). The Complainant also produced copies of show-cause notices previously issued to the Respondent, dated July 15, 2023, and August 20, 2023, demonstrating a pattern of non-compliance and the Complainant's efforts
to secure compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, was duly served with the complaint and notices for appearance before this Tribunal. Despite repeated opportunities afforded to them to present their defense, including filing a written statement and adducing evidence, the Respondent primarily relied on general denials and vague assertions of ongoing efforts to upgrade their treatment facilities. However, they failed to produce any credible documentary
or testimonial evidence to counter the specific allegations and the laboratory findings of the EPA. They did not present any independent laboratory analysis report to challenge the Complainant's findings, nor did they provide a detailed timeline or plan for the purported upgrade of their treatment facilities. The Tribunal granted several adjournments to the Respondent to enable them to produce such evidence, but no substantive material was ever presented. This failure to present a robust defense leaves the Complainant's evidence largely uncontroverted.
**Tribunal's Deliberation and Findings:**
The Tribunal has carefully considered the pleadings, the documentary evidence submitted by the Complainant, and the arguments advanced by their representative. The Inspection Report dated September 28, 2023, meticulously details the observations made at the Respondent's premises. The Laboratory Analysis Report (EPA/LAB/2023/11-007) is a critical piece of evidence. It is issued by a certified laboratory of the EPA, an expert body in environmental analysis. The results clearly indicate that the discharged effluent significantly exceeds the NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. These exceedances are not marginal but substantial, pointing towards a systemic failure in effluent management.
The prior show-cause notices issued on July 15, 2023, and August 20, 2023, establish that the Respondent was well aware of their non-compliance and had ample opportunity to rectify the situation before the formal complaint was filed. Their continued non-compliance despite these warnings demonstrates a disregard for environmental regulations and the potential harm caused. The Respondent's defense, consisting of general denials and unsubstantiated claims of ongoing efforts, is insufficient to rebut the specific, scientific, and documented evidence presented by the Complainant. No credible evidence was produced to contradict the EPA's findings or to demonstrate actual compliance or genuine progress towards it.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
