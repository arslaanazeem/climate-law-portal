**JUDGMENT**
This complaint, instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, on October 15, 2019, seeks redress against M/s Packages Limited, a prominent manufacturing facility located in Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal's mandate is to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution, ensuring the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, and for the prevention and control of pollution.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, submitted that the Respondent's industrial operations were generating and discharging pollutants into the environment in contravention of established environmental laws and standards. Specifically, the allegations included:
a.  **Discharge of Untreated or Partially Treated Industrial Effluent:** It was alleged that the Respondent's wastewater treatment plant (WWTP), if operational, was either inadequate or improperly maintained, leading to the discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the NEQS parameters for Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH into the municipal sewerage system, eventually impacting natural water bodies.
b.  **Air Emissions Exceeding Standards:** The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial processes, particularly from its boilers and other combustion sources, were emitting smoke, particulate matter, and other gaseous pollutants (such as NOx and SOx) into the ambient air at concentrations significantly higher than the NEQS for industrial emissions.
c.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste:** The complaint further highlighted concerns regarding the Respondent's handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous waste generated from its manufacturing processes, alleging non-compliance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and general environmental best practices.
d.  **Noise Pollution:** Incidental to the industrial operations, allegations of excessive noise levels impacting surrounding communities were also raised.
The Complainant submitted that despite repeated verbal warnings and formal notices issued on August 20, 2019, and September 25, 2019, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures, thereby necessitating the filing of this formal complaint before the Tribunal under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports prepared by EPA field teams on various dates (e.g., July 15, 2019; August 10, 2019; September 18, 2019), detailing observations of dark smoke plumes, visible effluent discharge, and general site conditions.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points on August 10, 2019, and September 18, 2019. These reports consistently indicated exceedances of NEQS for BOD (e.g., 250 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L), COD (e.g., 500 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), and TSS (e.g., 200 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L). Similar reports for air emissions showed particulate matter (PM10) levels exceeding NEQS.
3.  **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual documentation depicting plumes of smoke, discolored effluent, and inadequately stored waste materials within the Respondent's premises.
4.  **Testimony of EPA Officials:** Mr. Tariq Shah, Environmental Inspector, and Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director, testified before
the Tribunal, corroborating the documentary evidence.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, Mr. Zahid Ali, vehemently contested the allegations. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
