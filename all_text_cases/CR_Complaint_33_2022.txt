This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 33/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint was initiated following alleged contraventions of environmental laws and regulations within the jurisdiction of the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore. The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant's case rested on the assertion that the Respondent, an industrial entity engaged in manufacturing operations, was in persistent violation of environmental standards. Specifically, it was alleged that M/s Packages Limited was discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into public drains without adequate treatment. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of excessive air emissions from the Respondent's industrial stacks, particularly concerning particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, again exceeding prescribed NEQS limits. The Complainant also contended that the Respondent had failed to submit a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP) detailing measures for pollution control, despite repeated notices, and was operating certain units without a valid No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). These actions were deemed to be in direct contravention of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules and regulations, posing a significant threat to public health and the surrounding environment.
To substantiate its claims, the Environmental Protection Agency presented a substantial body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2022, 02.04.2022, and 20.04.2022, meticulously documenting observations of the Respondent's premises and operations. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15.03.2022 and 02.04.2022, which clearly indicated levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (specifically lead and chromium) significantly higher than the NEQS. Similarly, air quality monitoring reports from 20.04.2022 demonstrated particulate matter (PM10) and SO2 concentrations above the permissible limits. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the color and turbidity of the discharged effluent and visible stack emissions was also presented. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 25.03.2022 and 10.04.2022, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, were placed on record. The Complainant's witnesses, including the inspecting officer and the laboratory analyst, provided sworn testimonies affirming the authenticity and accuracy of the reports and observations. The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, in its defense, primarily argued that it had initiated measures to upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control
systems and was in the process of obtaining the necessary NOCs. They contended that environmental compliance was a continuous process and that significant investments had already been made towards achieving sustainable operations. The Respondent also questioned the methodology of some sample collections and the timing of certain inspections, suggesting that some alleged violations were momentary or had already been addressed. They further submitted that a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan was under preparation and would be submitted shortly, requesting the Tribunal to consider their proactive steps and grant them reasonable time to achieve full compliance rather than imposing immediate harsh penalties.
The Tribunal, after a thorough examination of the evidence, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses, photographic evidence, and the testimonies of the Complainant's witnesses, found the evidence presented by the Environmental Protection Agency to be compelling and largely uncontroverted by the Respondent's defense. While acknowledging the Respondent's claims of initiating upgrades and efforts towards compliance, these efforts were deemed insufficient to mitigate the established violations and the demonstrable harm to the environment. The laboratory reports unequivocally established that the discharged effluent and air emissions consistently exceeded the NEQS, posing a clear and present danger to public health and the ecosystem. The absence of valid NOCs for certain operational units and the failure to submit a timely and comprehensive EMP further underscored a pattern of non-compliance. The Tribunal noted that the repeated issuance of show-cause notices and the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies indicated a lack of urgency in addressing the grave environmental concerns. Therefore, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Packages Limited has indeed contravened various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 12, and 13, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industry) Rules, 2001. The Tribunal holds the Respondent liable for the environmental degradation caused by its operations.
**ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal hereby finds M/s Packages Limited **guilty** of violating environmental laws and regulations as detailed in the complaint. Accordingly, the Tribunal issues the following orders:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is hereby directed to immediately cease all discharges of industrial effluent and air emissions that exceed the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Upgrade and Installation:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, complete the upgrade of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged effluent consistently meets the NEQS for all parameters, including COD,

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
