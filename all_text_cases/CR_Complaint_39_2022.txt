Presided by: Muhammad Irfan
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 39/2022, brought forth by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited, a prominent industrial entity operating within the jurisdiction of this Tribunal. The complaint, filed under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), alleges various breaches of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, posing a potential threat to the environment and public health. The Tribunal, having heard both parties and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented, proceeds to render its decision.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Packages Limited, located in Lahore, was found to be in violation of several environmental standards and operational requirements during multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. Discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), particularly in relation to Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, contrary to Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
b. Failure to maintain and operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively, leading to the aforementioned NEQS exceedances.
c. Inadequate management and disposal of hazardous waste generated from its manufacturing processes, in contravention of the Pakistan Environmental Protection (Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries) Rules, 2001, and other allied regulations.
d. Non-compliance with certain conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval (NOC) issued by the EPA.
The Complainant contended that these violations constituted a serious breach of environmental laws, necessitating immediate remedial action and the imposition of penalties as prescribed by law.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documents and testimonies. Key among these were:
i. **Inspection Report dated 15.03.2022:** This report, prepared by a team of EPA inspectors, detailed observations of the Respondent's ETP operations and effluent discharge points, noting visible signs of inadequate treatment.
ii. **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/2022/0123 dated 22.03.2022:** This report, from the EPA's accredited laboratory, presented the analytical results of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge point on 15.03.2022. The report conclusively showed BOD levels at 120 mg/L (against NEQS 80 mg/L), COD at 300 mg/L (against NEQS 150 mg/L), TSS at 250 mg/L (against NEQS 200 mg/L), and pH at 4.5 (against NEQS 6-9).
iii. **Show Cause Notice dated 01.04.2022:** A copy of the show cause notice issued to the Respondent detailing the alleged violations and demanding an explanation.
iv. **Response from M/s Packages Limited dated 15.04.2022:** The Respondent's initial reply to the show cause notice, acknowledging certain operational challenges but outlining ongoing efforts to upgrade its ETP and improve environmental performance.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, while not outright denying the initial exceedances, presented its defence primarily focused on:
i. **Technical Explanation:** Arguing that the exceedances were temporary, attributable to a specific operational upset in their ETP due to unforeseen maintenance requirements at the time of inspection, which has since been rectified.
ii. **Proactive Measures:** Demonstrating significant investments made into upgrading its ETP, including the installation of advanced tertiary treatment units, post the initial inspection.
iii. **Subsequent Compliance Reports:** Submitting self-monitoring reports and independent laboratory analysis reports (e.g., from [Name of
*independent accredited laboratory*] demonstrating that post-upgrade effluent samples were well within the NEQS parameters.
iv. **Commitment to Environmental Stewardship:** Emphasizing its long-standing commitment to environmental responsibility and its willingness to cooperate with the EPA to ensure sustainable operations.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent, along with the documentary evidence. It is undeniable from the EPA's Inspection Report dated 15.03.2022 and the Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/2022/0123 dated 22.03.2022 that M/s Packages Limited was found to be in violation of NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH levels at the time of the inspection. These violations, as established by the Complainant's evidence, constitute a breach of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and other relevant environmental regulations. The Respondent's initial reply to the show cause notice also implicitly acknowledged operational challenges leading to these exceedances.
However, the Tribunal also takes note of the Respondent's subsequent actions and submissions. The Respondent has presented credible evidence, including independent laboratory reports, to demonstrate that significant investments have been made to upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant. These upgrades appear to have successfully brought their effluent discharge within the prescribed NEQS. The proactive measures taken by the Respondent post-inspection, and their reported return to compliance, are mitigating factors that the Tribunal deems crucial in determining the appropriate final order and penalty.
While the initial violations are serious and cannot be overlooked, the Tribunal also aims to foster an environment of compliance and encourage industries to take prompt and effective corrective actions. The Respondent's demonstration of rectification and commitment to ongoing environmental management weighs significantly in this decision. The Tribunal emphasizes that continuous compliance is not merely an aspiration but a legal obligation, and any future deviations will be viewed with extreme gravity.
**THEREFORE, THE TRIBUNAL, after careful deliberation, hereby orders as follows:**
1.  **Finding of Violation:** M/s Packages Limited is found to have been in violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as established by the EPA's inspection and laboratory reports dated 15.03.2022 and 22.03.2022, respectively.
2.  **Penalty Imposition:** In light of the established violations, but also considering the proactive and corrective measures undertaken by the Respondent, a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Continuous Compliance Directive:** The Respondent, M/
/s Packages Limited, is directed to ensure continuous compliance with all applicable provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), and all other relevant environmental laws, rules, and regulations. It shall maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and any other pollution control devices in optimal operational condition at all times to prevent any future exceedances.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting Requirements:** M/s Packages Limited shall submit quarterly self-monitoring reports, duly verified by an independent accredited laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, for a period of two (2) years from the date of this order. These reports shall specifically include analytical results of effluent discharge parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, pH, and any other relevant parameters) to demonstrate ongoing compliance with NEQS. The first report shall be due within ninety (90) days from the date of this order.
5.  **Consequences of Future Non-Compliance:** The Tribunal warns M/s Packages Limited that any future violations of environmental laws and regulations, particularly regarding effluent discharge and waste management, will be viewed with extreme gravity and may result in significantly harsher penalties, including but not limited to, higher fines, closure orders, or other stringent legal actions as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
6.  **EPA Oversight:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, is directed to closely monitor the compliance of M/s Packages Limited, particularly concerning the quarterly reports and the operational effectiveness of its ETP, and to take immediate appropriate action in case of any observed or reported non-compliance.
7.  **No Order as to Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.
This order is pronounced this **15th day of October, 2023**, in the presence of the representatives of both parties.
