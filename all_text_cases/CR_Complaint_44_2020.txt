This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 44/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, on 15th October 2020. The complaint alleges persistent contraventions of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and inadequate management of hazardous waste, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), has jurisdiction to adjudicate such matters and enforce environmental compliance.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore, has been operating without effective pollution control measures. Specifically, it was alleged that:
a. The Respondent was discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into the surrounding environment, including a nearby drain which ultimately flows into agricultural lands and water bodies.
b. Multiple inspections carried out by EPA officials revealed the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) of the Respondent to be either non-functional or operating inefficiently, leading to the discharge of untreated or partially treated wastewater.
c. Laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected on 10th August 2020 and 28th September 2020 showed parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels to be significantly above the prescribed NEQS limits.
d. The Respondent failed to adequately manage and dispose of its hazardous solid waste, including sludge from the ETP, which was reportedly being stored improperly or disposed of in an environmentally unsound manner.
e. Despite repeated notices, including a Show Cause Notice dated 20th August 2020 and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, on 5th October 2020, directing the Respondent to cease the polluting activities and ensure compliance, the Respondent failed to take effective corrective measures.
The Complainant presented the following documentary and testimonial evidence:
i. Inspection Reports dated 10th August 2020 and 28th September 2020, detailing observations of the ETP's operational status and the discharge points.
ii. Laboratory Analysis Reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2020/F-123 and EPA/LAB/2020
/F-345, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for various parameters.
iii. Copies of the Show Cause Notice dated 20th August 2020 and the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 5th October 2020, along with proof of their service to the Respondent.
iv. Correspondence exchanged between the EPA and the Respondent, demonstrating the latter's failure to adequately respond or implement corrective actions despite repeated warnings.
v. Testimonies from EPA officials involved in the inspections and sample collection, corroborating the documentary evidence.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, despite being duly served with the complaint and subsequent notices for appearance, chose not to file a detailed written statement or present a substantive defense before this Tribunal. On several hearing dates, their representatives appeared but largely failed to provide satisfactory explanations for the alleged violations or demonstrate tangible steps taken towards compliance. They vaguely contended that efforts were underway to upgrade their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and improve waste management practices, but no concrete evidence, progress reports, or timelines were presented to substantiate these claims. Their failure to effectively rebut the compelling evidence presented by the Complainant left the allegations largely uncontroverted.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the evidence presented by the Complainant, including the inspection reports, laboratory analysis reports, and the series of enforcement actions taken by the EPA. The consistency and corroborative nature of the evidence leave no doubt regarding the Respondent's persistent non-compliance with the environmental laws and standards. The laboratory reports unequivocally demonstrate that the effluent discharged by M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited far exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for critical parameters. The failure of the ETP to function efficiently, coupled with the inadequate management of hazardous waste, constitutes a clear violation of Sections 11, 12, 13, and 14 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules and regulations framed thereunder. The issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16, and the Respondent's subsequent failure to comply, further underscores the gravity of their environmental negligence.
Considering the uncontroverted evidence and the Respondent's failure to present a credible defense or demonstrate compliance, this Tribunal finds M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited guilty of the alleged contraventions of environmental laws and standards. The persistent discharge of untreated industrial effluent and the improper management of hazardous waste by the Respondent pose a serious and ongoing threat to public health, aquatic life, and agricultural productivity in the vicinity.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and in exercise of the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 and other enabling provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Penalty:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby imposed a cumulative fine of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million Only)** for the persistent violations of environmental laws and standards, to be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
2.  **Compliance with NEQS:** The Respondent shall immediately cease the discharge of untreated or partially treated effluent. They are directed to ensure that their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully operational, upgraded if necessary, and consistently meets all prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as stipulated under PEPA, 1997, and its regulations.
3.  **Submission of Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive Environmental Compliance and Improvement Plan to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order. This plan must detail specific measures, timelines, and financial commitments for achieving and maintaining full compliance with NEQS for effluent discharge and proper hazardous waste management.
4.  **Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent shall develop and implement an environmentally sound hazardous waste management plan, including proper collection, segregation, storage, transportation, and disposal of all hazardous waste, particularly ETP sludge, in accordance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and other relevant environmental regulations. This plan shall also be part of the overall compliance plan mentioned above.
5.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** For a period of two (2) years from the date of this order, the Respondent shall submit quarterly environmental monitoring reports, duly verified by an EPA-accredited laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, demonstrating continuous compliance with NEQS and proper waste management practices.
6.  **Environmental Performance Guarantee:** The Respondent shall furnish an Environmental Performance Guarantee in the form of a bank guarantee amounting to **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million Only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within forty-five (45) days from the date of this order. This guarantee shall be forfeited in case of any future non-compliance with the directives of this Tribunal or PEPA, 1997, within the aforementioned two-year period.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 200,000/- (Rupees Two Hundred Thousand Only)**, to be paid to the Complainant within thirty (30) days.
8.  **Enforcement:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, is directed to rigorously monitor the Respondent's compliance with this order and is authorized to take further legal action, including but not limited to, sealing of the industrial unit or imposition of further penalties under PEPA, 1997, should the Respondent fail to adhere to any part of this order.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
