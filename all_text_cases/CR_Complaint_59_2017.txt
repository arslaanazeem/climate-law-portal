**Judgment**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 59/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, alleging violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, presided over by Mr. Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi, has meticulously reviewed the presented facts, evidence, and legal arguments to arrive at a just and lawful determination. The Complainant, represented by learned counsel for EPA, and the Respondent, represented by their learned counsel, presented their respective cases before this Tribunal.
The Complainant asserted that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, an industrial unit engaged in chemical manufacturing, was consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies, specifically the Deg Nullah, without adhering to the prescribed NEQS. The allegations stemmed from multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials, which reportedly revealed significant exceedances of permissible limits for various pollutants. It was specifically alleged that the Respondent's effluent contained alarmingly high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH outside the acceptable range, posing a severe threat to public health and the aquatic ecosystem. The Complainant further contended that despite repeated notices and opportunities to rectify the situation, the Respondent failed to install or effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of bringing their discharges within the NEQS.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated 15.03.2017, 28.06.2017, and 12.09.2017, prepared by authorized officers of the EPA. These reports detailed the observations of on-site conditions, including the discharge points and the apparent lack of functional treatment facilities. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, corresponding to effluent samples collected during these inspections. The analysis results consistently indicated that the discharged effluent far exceeded the NEQS for industrial discharges: COD values were found to be between 450-600 mg/L (NEQS limit: 150 mg/L), BOD5 between 200-300 mg/L (NEQS limit: 80 mg/L), TSS between 250-350 mg/L (NEQS limit: 200 mg/L), and pH ranging from 4.5 to 5.5 (NEQS range: 6-9). Furthermore, copies of statutory notices issued under Section 16 of the Act, dated 05.04.2017 and 01.08.2017, demanding compliance and threatening legal action, were placed on record. The Complainant also produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director EPA, who personally conducted some
of the inspections and sampling, confirming the veracity of the inspection and laboratory reports. The Complainant further highlighted that despite the issuance of statutory notices, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, thereby perpetuating the environmental degradation.
In response, the Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was contended that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited operates with due diligence and has an
effective and operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) installed at its premises, designed to treat industrial effluent to meet the prescribed NEQS. Learned counsel for the Respondent argued that the EPA’s sampling methodology might have been flawed, or that the samples taken were not representative of their regular discharge. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
