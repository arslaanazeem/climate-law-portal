**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 22/2023 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of glass products. The complaint seeks a declaration of environmental contraventions by the Respondent and the imposition of penalties and remedial measures under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997").
The Complainant initiated this action following multiple inspections and monitoring activities at the Respondent's manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The core of the complaint revolves around persistent exceedances of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluents, particularly concerning particulate matter, oxides of nitrogen (NOx), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and certain heavy metals in wastewater discharge. The EPA asserts that despite repeated warnings and formal notices, the Respondent has failed to implement adequate pollution control measures, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing risks to public health and safety in the surrounding areas.
The Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence, including:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports dated 15.01.2023, 10.03.2023, and 25.04.2023, detailing observations of visible smoke plumes from the Respondent's stacks and discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into the municipal drainage system.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming exceedances of NEQS. Specifically, air emission samples collected on 15.01.2023 and 25.04.2023 showed particulate matter concentrations of 650 mg/Nm³ and 720 mg/Nm³ respectively, against the NEQS limit of 500 mg/Nm³ for industrial gaseous emissions. NOx levels were recorded at 950 mg/Nm³ and 1020 mg/Nm³ against a limit of 800 mg/Nm³. Wastewater samples collected on 10.03.2023
showed Total Suspended Solids (TSS) at 250 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) at 600 mg/L against a limit of 400 mg/L, and lead concentrations at 0.5 mg/L against a limit of 0.1 mg/L, all indicating severe non-compliance with NEQS for industrial liquid effluents.
3.  **Formal Notices and Warnings:** Copies of show-cause notices issued on 20.02.2023 and 15.04.2
023, informing the Respondent of the detected violations and requiring them to take corrective actions, along with subsequent reminders.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently contested the allegations, asserting its commitment to environmental stewardship. While not entirely denying the technical reports, the Respondent argued that the reported exceedances were either isolated incidents attributable to temporary operational malfunctions or were within acceptable parameters considering the complexity and scale of their manufacturing processes. The Respondent further submitted that it has continuously invested in upgrading its pollution control infrastructure, including plans for installing advanced electrostatic precipitators for air emissions and modernizing its wastewater treatment plant. It was contended that significant financial and technical resources have been allocated for these improvements, but such large-scale projects require considerable time for implementation and commissioning. The Respondent requested leniency and additional time to achieve full compliance, highlighting its economic contribution and employment generation in the region.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the comprehensive evidence presented by the Complainant and the arguments put forth by the Respondent. The laboratory analysis reports, certified by an accredited EPA laboratory, provide clear and irrefutable evidence of consistent and significant exceedances of the National Environmental Quality Standards for both gaseous emissions and liquid effluents. The repetitive nature of these violations, as documented across multiple inspection reports and confirmed by samples taken on different dates, indicates a systemic failure in the Respondent's existing pollution control mechanisms rather than isolated incidents or temporary glitches.
While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of ongoing efforts and investments in environmental upgrades, it is imperative to emphasize that compliance with NEQS is a mandatory legal requirement under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and not merely a target to be achieved at the Respondent's convenience. The persistent discharge of pollutants exceeding prescribed limits poses a serious threat to public health, local ecosystems, and the overall environmental integrity of the surrounding area. The arguments regarding economic contribution, while relevant in a broader context, cannot override the fundamental obligation to operate within environmental laws. Sections 11(1) and 11(2) of PEPA, 1997, unequivocally prohibit the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in excess of the NEQS. The evidence unequivocally establishes contravention of these provisions by the Respondent.
Therefore, having considered all material facts and legal arguments, the Tribunal finds M/s Ghani Glass Limited responsible for contravening the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Contravention:** It is hereby declared that M/s Ghani Glass Limited (the Respondent) has contravened Sections 11(1) and 11(2) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by persistently discharging industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluents exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** Pursuant to Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is imposed on M/s Ghani Glass Limited for the proven contraventions. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
