**Introduction**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 19/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in industrial operations in the vicinity of Lahore. The complaint was lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), alleging serious contraventions of environmental laws and regulations, particularly concerning the discharge of industrial effluents. The Tribunal, having heard the parties and considered the evidence presented, proceeds to render its decision.
**Allegations**
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial facility has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Hadiara Drain, which eventually merges with the River Ravi. This discharge, it was alleged, is in stark violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial and municipal effluents, as notified by the Government of Pakistan. Specifically, the EPA contended that multiple inspections conducted at the Respondent's premises revealed the absence of a functional and effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), or at best, an ETP that was either non-operational or grossly inefficient, leading to the release of pollutants far exceeding permissible limits. The Complainant further alleged that despite repeated notices, warnings, and opportunities for compliance provided by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take substantive measures to abate the pollution, thereby causing severe environmental degradation, posing risks to public health, and harming aquatic ecosystems. The core allegations centered on the Respondent's failure to comply with Section 11 of the Act, which prohibits the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS, and its general disregard for environmental protection obligations.
**Evidence**
The Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence to substantiate its claims. This included:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports, dated 15.01.2023, 10.03.2023, and 25.04.2023, prepared by EPA field teams, explicitly detailing observations of dark-colored, foul-smelling effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises into the Hadiara Drain. These reports also noted the dilapidated condition of the purported ETP.
2
.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Analytical reports from accredited laboratories, dated 20.01.2023, 15.03.2023, and 01.05.2023, confirming that samples of effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points consistently exceeded the NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels.
3.  **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Digital photographs and video recordings submitted by the EPA, depicting the discharge of discolored effluent, the dilapidated condition of the purported ETP, and the observable environmental impact on the Hadiara Drain.
4.  **Correspondence History:** A detailed record of notices, warning letters, and show-cause notices issued by the EPA to the Respondent since late 2022, along with the Respondent's often inadequate or delayed responses, demonstrating a clear pattern of non-compliance and disregard for regulatory directives.
**Respondent's Defense**
In response to the allegations, the Respondent, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied any deliberate non-compliance. It was contended that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection. The Respondent submitted that its Effluent Treatment Plant
(ETP) was indeed operational, albeit undergoing maintenance and upgrades during the periods of EPA inspections. They argued that occasional exceedances of NEQS were due to unforeseen operational glitches or fluctuations in production cycles, and not a systemic failure. The Respondent also questioned the methodology of sample collection by the EPA, suggesting that samples might not have been representative or that laboratory analyses could be flawed. Furthermore, they claimed to have invested substantial amounts in environmental management systems and were actively working towards achieving full compliance, requesting more time to complete the ongoing upgrades to their ETP. They presented some internal records of ETP operational logs and purchase orders for ETP spare parts, asserting their good faith efforts and commitment to environmental stewardship.
**Findings of the Tribunal**
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant's evidence, comprising detailed inspection reports, consistent laboratory analysis reports from accredited facilities, and corroborating photographic/videographic material, unequivocally demonstrated a persistent pattern of discharge of industrial effluent exceeding the NEQS. The laboratory reports consistently showed high levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and significant pH variations, indicating severe pollution. The dilapidated condition of the purported ETP, as noted in multiple inspection reports, further undermined the Respondent's claims of a fully functional and effective treatment system.
While the Respondent claimed ongoing maintenance and upgrades, the lack of substantial improvement in effluent quality over several months, despite repeated notices from the EPA, suggests either a deliberate delay or an inadequate commitment to environmental compliance. The internal records submitted by the Respondent regarding ETP operations and spare parts purchases were vague and did not sufficiently counter the robust scientific evidence of non-compliance presented by the EPA. The Tribunal finds the Respondent's arguments regarding sampling methodology or potential lab flaws unsubstantiated and lacking credible evidence. The consistent nature of the exceedances across multiple samples and dates points towards a systemic failure rather than isolated operational glitches.
Therefore, based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal finds that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has indeed violated Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 199

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
