**JUDGMENT**
This complaint, bearing No. 55/2020, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products situated in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint, lodged on 15th October, 2020, invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent non-compliance by the Respondent with various provisions of environmental law, particularly regarding the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Complainant seeks the imposition of penalties and directions for remedial measures against the Respondent for its alleged environmental transgressions.
The Complainant contended that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically the manufacturing of glass, were generating significant quantities of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater and gaseous emissions. It was specifically alleged that the Respondent was:
a. Discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (e.g., lead, cadmium) far exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents, in contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
b. Emitting air pollutants, including but not limited to Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), Sulphur Oxides (SOx), and Particulate Matter (PM10/PM2.5), from its industrial stacks beyond the permissible limits stipulated in the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
c. Failing
to install and operate appropriate pollution control devices, including a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) for wastewater and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) for gaseous emissions, thereby failing to implement environmental management measures mandated by law and industry best practices.
d. Failing to obtain the necessary No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) from the EPA for significant operational changes or expansions, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, read with the Environmental Impact Assessment Regulations, 2000.
The Complainant submitted multiple inspection reports, laboratory analysis results of effluent and emission samples collected from the Respondent's facility between January 2019 and September 2020. These reports consistently indicated that the levels of pollutants in the Respondent's discharges and emissions significantly exceeded the NEQS. Despite repeated warnings, show-cause notices, and opportunities for compliance provided by the EPA, the Respondent allegedly failed to take effective remedial actions, thus necessitating the present complaint before this Tribunal.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations. Through its counsel, the Respondent contended that it is a responsible corporate entity with a strong commitment to environmental stewardship. It asserted that it had invested substantially in modern manufacturing processes and pollution control technologies, including an operational ETP and APCDs. The Respondent argued that any reported exceedances were either isolated incidents due to temporary technical malfunctions, or were attributable to flawed sampling methodologies and analytical errors on the part of the Complainant's representatives. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed that it was actively engaged in upgrading its existing infrastructure and had submitted plans for further improvements to the EPA, demonstrating its good faith and intention to comply fully with environmental regulations. It also contended that the imposition of a heavy penalty would adversely affect its operations, employment, and contribution to the national economy.
The Tribunal carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties, examined the documentary evidence presented, including the inspection reports, laboratory test results, and correspondence exchanged between the EPA and the Respondent. Expert testimonies were also heard regarding the technical aspects of pollution control and the accuracy of the sampling and analysis procedures.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. **V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
