This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), invoking the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint seeks appropriate action, including imposition of penalties and directives for environmental compliance, against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations concerning industrial emissions and effluent discharge. The Tribunal is seized with the responsibility to ensure the enforcement of environmental standards and protection of public health and ecological integrity within its territorial jurisdiction.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, operating an industrial glass manufacturing unit located at [Specific Location, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore], has been consistently violating the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents and air emissions. Specifically, it was contended that the Respondent’s industrial unit was discharging untreated or inadequately treated wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system/Lahore Canal, leading to severe pollution. Laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA revealed that the discharged effluent exceeded the permissible limits for various parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Furthermore, the complaint asserted that the Respondent's industrial processes, particularly its furnace operations, were emitting particulate matter, Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) into the ambient air without adequate air pollution control devices (APCDs) or proper maintenance thereof, thereby contributing significantly to air pollution in the surrounding residential and industrial areas. The EPA claimed to have issued multiple inspection reports, show-cause notices, and environmental protection orders (EPOs) to the Respondent, yet full compliance remained elusive, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental safeguards.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of documents and expert testimonies. These included:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Dated 15.03.2022, 05.05.2022, and 20.07.2022, detailing observations of effluent discharge points, smoke emissions, and the operational status of the Respondent's wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) and air pollution control systems.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Official test reports from the EPA-certified laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2022/03-123, EPA/LAB/2022/05-245, and EPA/LAB/2022/07-389, confirming exceedances of NEQS for effluent parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, pH) and ambient air quality parameters (PM10, SO2, NOx) based on samples collected on respective dates.
3.  **Show-Cause Notices and EPOs:** Copies of formal notices issued to the Respondent on 25.03.2022 and 15.06.2022, highlighting violations and directing remedial actions, along with the Respondent’s inadequate responses.
4.  **Expert Testimony:** An environmental chemist from the EPA laboratory testified under oath, explaining the methodology of sample collection and analysis, and confirming the accuracy of the lab reports.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its legal counsel and technical representatives, contested the allegations. They submitted:
1.  **Environmental Management Plan (EMP):** A document outlining their commitment to environmental stewardship and planned upgrades.
2.  **Details of Existing Infrastructure:** Documentation regarding their operational WWTP and APCDs (e.g., bag filters, scrubbers), asserting that these were installed and maintained.
3.  **Upgrade Plans and Procurement Documents:** Evidence, including purchase orders and invoices for new, advanced treatment equipment and APCDs, arguing that significant investments were being made to enhance their environmental performance and achieve full compliance.
4.  **Internal Monitoring Data:** Limited internal monitoring data, which, however, often contradicted the EPA's findings or focused on parameters not primarily at issue.
The Tribunal carefully reviewed all submitted evidence, including the cross-examination of witnesses from both sides.
The legal framework governing this complaint primarily stems from the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The relevant provisions include:
1.  **Section 11 (Prohibition of certain discharges or emissions):** This section prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration, or
level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) or the standards established by the Agency.
2.  **Section 13 (National Environmental Quality Standards):** Empowers the Federal Agency to establish NEQS, which serve as the benchmark for permissible limits of pollutants.
3.  **Section 14 (Powers of the Agency):** Delineates the powers of
the Agency, including the power to issue Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 to prevent or control environmental pollution, and to take samples and conduct inspections.
4.  **Section 16 (Environmental Protection Order):** This section empowers the Federal Agency or Provincial Agency to issue an Environmental Protection Order to a person to prohibit or require specific actions to prevent or control pollution

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
