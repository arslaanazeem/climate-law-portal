**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 10/2024 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Sapphire Fibres"), concerning alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Complainant seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial measures against the Respondent for environmental degradation caused by the discharge of untreated industrial effluent.
The Complainant, through its authorized officer, initiated this complaint on 15th January 2024, asserting that the Respondent, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the prescribed NEQS into the public sewerage system/receiving water bodies. It was specifically alleged that despite repeated warnings, issuance of environmental protection orders, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent has failed to adequately treat its industrial waste, thereby causing significant environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life. The complaint detailed that routine inspections and subsequent laboratory analyses confirmed the persistent non-compliance, demonstrating a clear disregard for environmental regulations.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of documents and reports. These included copies of multiple inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, specifically mentioning inspections on 15th October 2023, 2nd November 2023, and 18th December 2023. These reports uniformly indicated the discharge of visibly colored and odorous effluent from the Respondent’s premises. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports corresponding to effluent samples collected during these inspections. The samples were collected in accordance with standard protocols and analyzed by the EPA's accredited laboratory. The results consistently showed parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels significantly exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial effluents. For instance, the report dated 20th October 2023 indicated BOD5 at 350 mg/L (NEQS limit: 80 mg/L), COD at 700 mg/L (NEQS limit: 150 mg/L), and TSS at 280 mg/L (NEQS limit: 200 mg/L). Similar exceedances were noted in subsequent reports. Furthermore, the Complainant provided copies of a Show Cause Notice issued to the Respondent on 26th December 2023, and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 5th January 2024, directing the Respondent to cease the discharge of untreated effluent and to ensure compliance with NEQS. The Respondent's reply to the Show Cause Notice, if any, was deemed unsatisfactory by the Complainant, failing to provide concrete evidence of effective remedial measures.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, contested the complaint, primarily arguing that their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was fully operational and designed to meet NEQS. They contended that the samples collected by the EPA might not have been representative, or that occasional fluctuations due to process variations could lead to temporary non-compliance. The Respondent also submitted a copy of their ETP operational logbook and claimed to have invested substantial amounts in upgrading their treatment facilities. However, no independent laboratory reports or third-party verification of their effluent quality were presented to counter the specific findings of the EPA's laboratory analyses. The Respondent did not produce any witness to testify regarding the efficacy or continuous operation of their ETP at the time of the EPA's inspections and sample collections.
The core legal provisions relevant to this complaint are enshrined in the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, explicitly prohibits any person from discharging or emitting any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration, or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, or where applicable, the standards established under sub-section (3) of Section 12. This section forms the bedrock of environmental pollution control in Pakistan.
Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, grants the Environmental Tribunal the power to impose penalties and direct remedial measures. Specifically, sub-section (1) states that "Where the Tribunal is satisfied that
an environmental pollution has occurred, it may direct the polluter to take such measures as it may specify to mitigate or abate the pollution, and may also impose a penalty." Sub-section (2) further elaborates that "Whoever contravenes the provisions of section 11, or an order issued under section 16(1), shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with an additional fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues."
Having meticulously reviewed the pleadings, documentary evidence, and arguments presented by both parties, this Tribunal finds the Complainant's case to be well-substantiated. The series of inspection reports, coupled with the consistent laboratory analysis reports provided by the EPA, unequivocally demonstrate that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been discharging industrial effluent with parameters significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards. The specific exceedances in BOD5, COD, and TSS, as detailed in the reports dated 20th October 2023 and subsequent analyses, are clear indicators of persistent non-compliance. The issuance of a Show Cause Notice and an Environmental Protection Order prior to the filing of this complaint further establishes that the Respondent was duly informed of its violations and provided opportunities to rectify the situation, which it evidently failed to do effectively.
On the other hand, the Respondent's defense primarily relied on general assertions regarding the functionality of its Effluent Treatment Plant and speculative claims about sample representativeness or temporary fluctuations. Crucially, the Respondent failed to produce any credible independent laboratory reports or expert testimony to counter the specific, scientific findings presented by the EPA. The operational logbook of the ETP, without corresponding verifiable effluent quality data, does not sufficiently negate the EPA's evidence of non-compliance. The absence of a witness to testify regarding the continuous and effective operation of their ETP further weakens their stance. This Tribunal gives due weight to the official reports and laboratory analyses conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency, which are presumed to be carried out in the regular course of official duty and with scientific rigor.
Based on the preponderance of evidence, this Tribunal is satisfied that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has indeed contravened the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby causing environmental pollution. The violations appear to be persistent and deliberate, despite prior warnings and directives from the EPA. Such actions not only undermine environmental protection efforts but also pose a significant risk to public health and the ecological balance of the receiving environment.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **GUILTY VERDICT:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is found guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **IMMEDIATE COMPLIANCE:** The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not conform to the National Environmental Quality Standards. The Respondent shall ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully operational, optimized, and consistently treating all industrial effluent to meet the NEQS at all times.
3.  **MONETARY PENALTY:** A penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for the contravention of environmental laws. This penalty shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
4.  **CONTINUING CONTRAVENTION PENALTY:** In addition to the lump sum penalty, should the Respondent continue to discharge non-compliant effluent after the date of this judgment, a further penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** shall be imposed for each day of continuing contravention, commencing from the 31st day after the date of this judgment. The EPA is authorized to monitor and levy this daily penalty.
5.  **REMEDIAL MEASURES AND REPORTING:**
    *   The Respondent shall submit a detailed action plan to the EPA within fifteen (15) days from the date of this judgment, outlining specific measures, timelines, and responsible personnel to ensure sustained compliance with NEQS.
    *   The Respondent shall engage an independent, EPA-accredited laboratory to conduct monthly analyses of its treated effluent for a period of six (6) months, commencing from the date of this judgment. The reports of these analyses shall be submitted directly to the EPA within five (5) days of their receipt.
    *   The Respondent shall grant full access to EPA officials for inspections and sampling without prior notice, to verify compliance with this order.
6.  **COSTS:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only), payable to the Complainant within thirty (30) days.
7.  **DEFAULT CLAUSE:** In case of failure to comply with any part of this order, the EPA is authorized to initiate further legal action, including but not limited to sealing of the industrial unit or prosecution under relevant provisions of PEPA, 1997, without further recourse to this Tribunal.
This judgment is pronounced on this **25th day of April, 2024**.
Any party aggrieved by this judgment may prefer an appeal to the High Court within thirty (30) days as per the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
