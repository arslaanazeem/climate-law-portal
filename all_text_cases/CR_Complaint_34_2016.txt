**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 34/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint was instituted on October 27, 2016, seeking the imposition of penalties and directions for compliance due to persistent discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions exceeding prescribed standards.
The Complainant, through its authorized officer, asserted that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent chemical manufacturing facility, was operating in a manner detrimental to the environment and public health. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent was regularly discharging industrial wastewater containing pollutants such as high Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals directly into the nearby water bodies and drainage systems without adequate treatment. Furthermore, the complaint detailed concerns regarding air emissions from the Respondent's stacks, which were suspected of exceeding NEQS for various parameters including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx). The Complainant maintained that these violations posed significant threats to aquatic life, soil quality, and the health of communities residing in the vicinity. Repeated inspections by EPA officials, commencing from early 2016, consistently revealed non-compliance despite prior warnings and show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, urging them to rectify the deficiencies in their effluent and emission treatment systems. The Complainant emphasized that the Respondent had failed to install or effectively operate an Environmental Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices commensurate with the scale and nature of their industrial operations, thereby demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations and their corporate responsibility.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant submitted a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated February 15, 2016, April 20, 2016, and July 12, 2016, detailing the observations made by EPA teams at the Respondent's premises. Crucially, these reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis certificates from the EPA's accredited laboratory, showing the parameters of effluent samples collected on those dates. For instance, the analysis of the sample collected on April 20, 2016, revealed COD levels of 450 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), BOD levels of 220 mg/L (against NEQS of 80 mg/L), and TSS of 300 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), alongside elevated levels of specific heavy metals. Similarly, stack emission monitoring reports indicated particulate matter exceeding NEQS. The Complainant also produced copies of Show Cause Notice No. EPA/Lahore/SCN/2016/112 dated May 10, 2016, and Final Notice No. EPA/Lahore/FN/2016/201 dated August 15, 2016, which had been duly served upon the Respondent, along with their unsatisfactory responses. Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director (Technical) EPA, appeared as a witness, providing sworn testimony corroborating the inspection findings and the validity of the laboratory reports.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through their legal counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. They argued that they were a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental protection and had invested significantly in their pollution control infrastructure. Their defense primarily revolved around technical challenges in achieving consistent compliance due to the complex nature of their chemical processes and fluctuations in raw material quality. They presented evidence of ongoing efforts to upgrade their existing effluent treatment plant (ETP), including procurement orders for new equipment and a timeline for its installation and commissioning. They also submitted internal monitoring reports, which, while showing some exceedances, also indicated periods of compliance or near-compliance. The Respondent's representative, Mr. Tariq Saeed, General Manager Operations, appeared before the Tribunal, explaining the steps taken and assuring the Tribunal of their commitment to achieving full compliance within a reasonable timeframe. They requested leniency, citing their contribution to the national economy and employment generation.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of the Act strictly prohibits the discharge or
emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) or other standards established by the Agency under this Act. Section 16 further empowers the Federal Government to establish NEQS for various environmental parameters. Any person contravening the provisions of Section 11 is liable to be proceeded against under Section 17 of the Act, which grants
the Environmental Tribunal the power to impose penalties, including fines and directions for compliance, on persons found to be in contravention of the Act's provisions. Section 17(1) stipulates that any person who contravenes or fails to comply with the provisions of Section 11 shall be punishable with a fine not exceeding one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with an additional fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues. The Tribunal is also empowered under Section 17(2) to direct the polluter to take such measures as may be necessary to mitigate or abate the pollution.
**FINDINGS OF THE TRIBUNAL**
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the extensive documentary evidence and oral testimonies presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent. It is unequivocally established from the evidence, particularly the multiple inspection reports and corresponding laboratory analysis certificates from the EPA’s accredited laboratory, that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited was indeed discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) on several occasions as alleged by the Complainant. The specific parameters, such as COD, BOD, TSS, heavy metals, and particulate matter, consistently exceeded the permissible limits, demonstrating a clear and persistent violation of Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997. The testimony of Mr. Asif Mehmood, Assistant Director (Technical) EPA, further corroborated these findings.
While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of technical challenges inherent in their chemical processes and their ongoing efforts to upgrade their pollution control infrastructure,

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
