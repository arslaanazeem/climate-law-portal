This judgment concerns the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks adjudication under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging contravention of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized of the matter to determine the liability of the Respondent and pass appropriate orders.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been discharging industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain without adequate treatment, thereby causing environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life. Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), though present, was either non-functional, inefficient, or bypassed, leading to the discharge of effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters. The complaint highlighted that despite repeated warnings and issuance of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent failed to bring its operations into compliance with the prescribed environmental standards. The Complainant sought imposition of penalties as per Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, and directions for immediate remedial measures.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence. This included inspection reports dated 15.11.2023, 20.12.2023, and 10.01.2024, prepared by EPA field teams, detailing observations of effluent discharge, the operational status of the ETP, and photographic evidence of the discharge points. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA Laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2023/1125, EPA/LAB/2023/1280, and EPA/LAB/2024/0150, were submitted. These reports consistently showed that samples of the Respondent's industrial effluent collected on the respective inspection dates significantly exceeded the NEQS limits for critical parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and several heavy metals like Chromium and Lead. Copies of show-cause notices issued on 25.11.2023 and 05.01.2024, and the subsequent Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 15.01.2024, directing the Respondent to cease discharge of untreated effluent and ensure compliance, were also placed on record. The Complainant also produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Environmental Inspector, who conducted the inspections and collected samples, affirming the veracity of the reports and observations.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, represented by its legal counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. They contended that they had invested significantly in an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and were committed to environmental protection. They argued that any instances of non-compliance might have been due to technical glitches, power fluctuations, or temporary operational issues, which they claimed were promptly addressed. The Respondent produced a copy of their ETP design specifications and maintenance logbooks, along with a recent purchase order for certain ETP spare parts. However, they failed to produce any independent laboratory analysis reports contradicting the EPA's findings or demonstrating consistent compliance with NEQS. The Respondent's production manager, Mr. Tariq Hussain, appeared as a witness, stating that the ETP was generally operational, but admitted to occasional challenges in maintaining optimal performance.
The legal framework governing this case primarily stems from the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act,
1997, particularly Section 11, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration, or level exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) or the standards established by the Federal Agency. Section 16 empowers the Federal Agency (and by extension, the EPA) to issue Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) to individuals or undertakings requiring them to take measures to prevent or abate pollution. Crucially, Section 17 prescribes penalties for contravention of the Act, Rules, Regulations, or EPOs, including imprisonment and/or fines, with additional daily fines for continuing contravention. Section 21 establishes the Environmental Tribunal for adjudication of such matters.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant's case is robust and well-supported by documentary and testimonial evidence. The inspection reports, coupled with the certified laboratory analysis reports, provide irrefutable proof that the Respondent's industrial effluent consistently exceeded the NEQS limits for multiple critical parameters over an extended period. The repeated issuance of show-cause notices and the subsequent EPO dated 15.01.2024 demonstrate that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations and given ample opportunity to rectify the situation. The testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan further corroborated the factual findings.
Conversely, the Respondent's defense, while acknowledging investment in an ETP and expressing commitment to environmental protection, failed to effectively counter the specific allegations of non-compliance. Their explanation of "technical glitches" or "temporary operational issues" is insufficient to justify the consistent and significant exceedances of NEQS as evidenced by the EPA's laboratory reports. The production of ETP design specifications and maintenance logbooks, without independent verification of their effectiveness in achieving NEQS compliance, holds little weight against the objective scientific data presented by the Complainant. Furthermore, the Respondent's failure to provide any independent laboratory analysis reports to contradict the EPA's findings weakens their position considerably. Mr.
Tariq Hussain's admission to occasional challenges in maintaining optimal performance further supports the inference that the ETP was not consistently operating at the required efficiency to meet NEQS.
Based on the overwhelming evidence presented by the Complainant and the inadequacy of the Respondent's defense, this Tribunal finds that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has contravened the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and has failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards. Furthermore, the Respondent has failed to comply with the Environmental Protection Order dated 15.01.2024, issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997. The persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent constitutes a serious environmental offense, posing significant risks to public health and the environment.
**ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 and Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Penalty:** The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is hereby found guilty of contravening the provisions of PEPA, 1997, and is directed to pay a monetary fine of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
2.  **Daily Fine for Continuing Contravention:** In addition to the above, the Respondent shall pay a further daily fine of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** for each day the contravention has continued since the issuance of the Environmental Protection Order dated 15.01.2024, up to the date of this judgment, and shall continue to pay the said daily fine until full compliance with NEQS is achieved and verified by the Complainant. The total amount of this daily fine shall be calculated by the Complainant and submitted to the Tribunal within seven (7) days for final endorsement.
3.  **Remedial Measures and Compliance:** The Respondent is directed to immediately take all necessary measures to ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully functional, optimized, and consistently meets all parameters of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge.
4.  **Submission of Compliance Report:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance report, including independent third-party laboratory analysis reports demonstrating consistent adherence to NEQS, to the Complainant and this Tribunal within sixty (60) days from the date of this order. The third-party laboratory must be accredited by the Pakistan National Accreditation Council (PNAC).
5.  **Monitoring and Verification:** The Complainant (EPA) is directed to conduct regular and unannounced inspections and sampling of the Respondent's effluent discharge for a period of six (6) months following the submission of the compliance report, to verify sustained compliance with NEQS. The cost of such monitoring shall be borne by the Respondent.
6.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of fines and implementation of remedial measures within the stipulated timeframes, shall result in further legal action, including but not limited to, sealing of the industrial unit or other coercive measures as provided under PEPA, 1997, and other applicable laws.
7.  **Case Closure:** This complaint stands disposed of in terms of the above order.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
