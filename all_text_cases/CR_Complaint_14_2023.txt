This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals, with its industrial unit located in Kala Shah Kaku, District Sheikhupura. The complaint, registered on March 15, 2023, seeks redressal for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment, causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and ecological integrity.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's chemical manufacturing facility was found to be discharging industrial wastewater containing pollutants in concentrations exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules. Specifically, it was averred that during a routine inspection conducted on January 25, 2023, by an EPA team, samples of the effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises into a nearby drain were collected. Subsequent laboratory analysis of these samples, conducted at the EPA's accredited laboratory, revealed parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels that significantly exceeded the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial effluent discharge. Furthermore, the complaint highlighted a persistent failure on the part of the Respondent to operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively or consistently, despite prior warnings and notices issued by the EPA. It was contended that such non-compliance constituted a clear violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and associated regulations, leading to environmental degradation and potential harm to aquatic life and agricultural lands reliant on the affected water body.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive inspection report dated January 25, 2023, detailing the observations made during the site visit, including photographic evidence of the discharge point and the visibly polluted water body. Crucially, the analytical report from the EPA laboratory, bearing reference No. EPA/Lab/2023/0078, dated February 15, 2023, was submitted. This report explicitly indicated that the COD level was 450 mg/L (NEQS limit: 150 mg/L), BOD5 was 200 mg/L (NEQS limit: 80 mg/L), TSS was 300 mg/L (NEQS limit: 200 mg/L), and the pH was 4.5 (NEQS range: 6-9). Additionally, copies of previous show-cause notices and warning letters issued to the Respondent on April 10, 2022, and September 20, 2022, regarding similar non-compliances were placed on record, demonstrating a pattern of repeated violations. The Assistant Director, EPA, also appeared as a witness, reiterating the findings of the inspection and analysis.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. It was argued that the company maintains an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and is committed to environmental protection. The Respondent contended that the sampling conducted by the EPA might not have been representative, possibly due to an isolated operational anomaly or a temporary breakdown of a specific ETP component. They presented their internal monitoring reports for the period of December 2022 to January 2023, which, according to them, showed compliance with most NEQS parameters. They further submitted an affidavit from their Chief Operating Officer detailing the operational procedures of their ETP and outlining recent investments made in upgrading the plant. During the proceedings, the Respondent also produced a purchase order for new filtration membranes and a maintenance log for the ETP, attempting to demonstrate ongoing efforts towards compliance. However, these internal reports did not directly contradict the EPA's specific findings for the samples taken on January 25, 2023, nor did they provide independent third-party verification.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of
the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), or such other standards as may be prescribed. Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, prescribes penalties for contravention of Section 11, stating that whoever contravenes the provisions of Section 11 shall be punishable with a fine not exceeding one million rupees and with imprisonment for a term not exceeding two years, or with both. In case of a continuing contravention, an additional fine not exceeding one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues shall be imposed. Furthermore, Section 20 empowers the Tribunal to issue directions requiring a person to take such measures as may be necessary to mitigate or prevent pollution.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments presented by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary and testimonial evidence on record. It is undisputed that the Respondent operates a chemical manufacturing facility that generates industrial effluent. The core issue revolves around whether this effluent was discharged in compliance with the NEQS on the date of inspection, i.e., January 25, 2023.
The Complainant's evidence, particularly the analytical report from the EPA's accredited laboratory (Ref. No. EPA/Lab/2023/0078, dated February 15, 2023), stands as a strong and credible piece of evidence. This report clearly demonstrates that key parameters of the effluent discharged by the Respondent – COD, BOD5, TSS, and pH – significantly exceeded the permissible limits set by the NEQS. The photographic evidence corroborated the visual observation of pollution at the discharge point. The prior warnings and show-cause notices issued by the EPA further underscore a pattern of non-compliance, indicating that the issue was not an isolated incident but a recurring problem that the Respondent had failed to adequately address despite official admonitions.
While the Respondent presented internal monitoring reports and an affidavit asserting commitment to environmental protection and detailing ETP operations and upgrades, these submissions do not directly refute the specific findings of the EPA's laboratory analysis for the samples collected on January 25, 2023. Internal reports, without independent verification, cannot override the findings of an official regulatory body's accredited laboratory, especially when direct evidence of exceedance is provided. The explanation of an "isolated operational anomaly or a temporary breakdown" remains unsubstantiated and does not absolve the Respondent of its responsibility to ensure continuous compliance with environmental standards. The very purpose of an ETP is to consistently treat effluent to meet NEQS, and any failure, whether temporary or persistent, constitutes a violation.
Based on the preponderance of evidence, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited was indeed discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in concentrations exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards on January 25, 2023, thereby violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Respondent's failure to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant has led to environmental degradation and poses a threat to public health and ecological integrity.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
Therefore, this Tribunal holds M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, the Respondent, liable for contravention of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
In light of the repeated nature of the violations, as evidenced by prior warnings, and the significant exceedance of NEQS parameters, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order and penalty:
1.  **Penalty:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby directed to pay a fine of **PKR 750,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand only)** to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
2.  **Immediate Compliance:** The Respondent is directed to immediately take all necessary measures to ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) operates effectively and consistently to treat all industrial effluent to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) before discharge.
3.  **Compliance Report:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance report, duly verified by an independent environmental consultant accredited by EPA, within sixty (60) days, demonstrating that the ETP is fully operational and consistently achieving NEQS for all parameters. This report must include at least three sets of weekly effluent analysis reports from an EPA-approved third-party laboratory.
4.  **Monitoring and Audit:** The Respondent shall allow and facilitate regular and unannounced inspections by the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, and shall bear the cost of any third-party environmental audit deemed necessary by the EPA to verify compliance for a period of one year.
5.  **Warning:** Failure to comply with this order, or any future contravention of environmental laws and standards, will result in more stringent penalties, including higher fines, closure of operations, and initiation of criminal proceedings as per the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
The complaint is accordingly allowed. No order as to costs.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
