**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located in Kot Lakhpat Industrial Area, Lahore. The complaint was instituted under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter "NEQS").
The Complainant's case, as articulated in the complaint and subsequent submissions, posits that the Respondent has been operating its textile dyeing and finishing processes in a manner detrimental to the environment and public health. Specifically, the EPA alleged that the Respondent:
a.  Was consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drain, with parameters significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial liquid effluents, thereby causing severe water pollution.
b.  Failed to install, or if installed, to properly operate and maintain an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of bringing its discharge within NEQS limits.
c. Failed to provide satisfactory information or take adequate remedial measures despite multiple notices issued by the EPA, demonstrating a persistent pattern of non-compliance.
The Tribunal notes that despite repeated opportunities provided by the Complainant to the Respondent to rectify the deficiencies and bring its operations into compliance with the NEQS, including the issuance of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and show-cause notices, the
Respondent continued its non-compliant operations. Despite being afforded ample opportunities to present its case, the Respondent failed to provide credible evidence of consistent compliance or a concrete action plan demonstrating a genuine commitment to rectify the persistent violations. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
