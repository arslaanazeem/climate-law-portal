This judgment addresses Complaint No. 26/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a prominent textile manufacturing facility located at [Hypothetical Address, e.g., Ferozepur Road, Lahore]. The complaint, lodged on March 15, 2020, brought forth serious allegations of environmental pollution and non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and provisions of the Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"). The core issue revolves around the Respondent's alleged continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into natural water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and public health risks.
The Complainant, through its complaint dated March 15, 2020, alleged that M/s Nishat Mills Limited was operating its textile dyeing and finishing units without an effective and functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or, alternatively, was failing to operate its existing ETP at optimal efficiency. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  Discharge of industrial effluent containing parameters (such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and various heavy metals) significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents, in violation of Section 11(a) of the Act.
2.  Failure to obtain or renew the Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its operations, as required under Section 12 of the Act and relevant EIA/IEE Regulations.
3.  Ignoring repeated warning notices and show-cause notices issued by the EPA regarding non
...compliance and pollution control measures.
The Complainant presented detailed laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on multiple occasions (e.g., May 2, 2019, August 18, 2019, and January 10, 2020). These reports consistently indicated that the discharged effluent contained levels of COD, BOD, TSS, and heavy metals significantly above the NEQS, thereby posing an imminent threat to aquatic life and human health. Furthermore, inspection reports prepared by EPA officials corroborated the absence of a properly functioning ETP or the inadequate operation thereof. Despite repeated communications, including a final show-cause notice dated February 10, 2020, the Respondent failed to demonstrate satisfactory compliance or present a credible plan for remediation.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, in its reply dated March 30, 2020, generally denied
the allegations of continuous non-compliance, stating that an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was indeed operational at its facility, albeit admitting to occasional operational challenges. The Respondent contended that the samples collected by the Complainant might not have been representative, or that the sampling methodology employed was flawed. M/s Nishat Mills Limited further claimed to be actively engaged in the process of upgrading its existing ETP to enhance its efficiency and was pursuing the renewal of its Environmental Approval, citing administrative delays as a primary reason for the hold-up. The Respondent requested additional time to achieve full compliance, emphasizing its significant contribution to the national economy and employment generation as mitigating factors.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant's laboratory analysis reports, which consistently indicated that the discharged effluent contained parameters significantly exceeding the NEQS on multiple occasions, were found to be credible, professionally conducted, and conclusive. These reports were further substantiated by the detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA officials, which corroborated the Complainant's assertions regarding the inadequate operation of the ETP. While the Respondent claimed to have an operational ETP, no credible evidence, such as ETP operational logs, maintenance records, or self-monitoring reports demonstrating consistent compliance with NEQS, was furnished to effectively counter the specific findings of the EPA. The Tribunal found the Respondent's argument of administrative delays for environmental approval renewal to be an insufficient justification for continued environmental degradation, particularly given the repeated warnings and show-cause notices issued by the EPA, which provided ample opportunity for rectification. The Tribunal acknowledged the Respondent's economic contributions but emphasized that such contributions cannot override the paramount importance of environmental protection and public health as enshrined in the Environmental Protection Act, 1997.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
