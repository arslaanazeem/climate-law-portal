**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 65/2017 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Act, exercises jurisdiction over environmental matters, including the adjudication of complaints regarding pollution and non-compliance with environmental standards.
The Complainant's case rests on the assertion that the Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, causing significant pollution and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem. Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed consistent exceedances of the NEQS for various parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, in the effluent discharged from the Respondent's facility. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent had failed to install or properly operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, thereby contravening the mandatory provisions of the Act.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated 15.03.2017, 28.05.2017, and 10.08.2017, detailing the observations of EPA officials regarding the Respondent's discharge practices. These reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis certificates from the EPA's approved environmental laboratory, which confirmed the NEQS exceedances for samples collected from the Respondent's final discharge point on each inspection date. For instance, the analysis of the sample collected on 28.05.2017 showed a BOD level of 280 mg/L against the NEQS limit of 80
mg/L, a COD level of 650 mg/L against the NEQS limit of 150 mg/L, and TSS of 320 mg/L against the NEQS limit of 200 mg/L. The Complainant further presented similar analysis reports for the samples collected on 15.03.2017 and 10.08.2017, all consistently showing significant exceedances across multiple parameters. Additionally, copies of warning notices and show-cause notices issued to the Respondent by the EPA, dated 01.04.2017 and 20.06.2017 respectively, were placed on record, indicating that the Respondent had been duly informed of the violations and given opportunities to rectify the situation, yet failed to do so effectively.
In response to the complaint, the Respondent, through its counsel, denied the allegations, asserting that it had installed an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was making continuous efforts to comply with environmental standards. It was contended that any reported exceedances were either isolated incidents or might have resulted from temporary operational issues. The Respondent also claimed that it was in the process of upgrading its ETP and sought further time to achieve full compliance with the NEQS. However, the Respondent failed to produce any concrete documentary evidence, such as recent independent laboratory test reports, ETP operational logs, or maintenance records, that could effectively counter the specific findings presented by the Complainant. No evidence was led to demonstrate that the effluent quality was consistently within NEQS limits at any point after
the initial notices were issued or during the period under complaint. The Respondent's assertion of upgrading its ETP remained unsubstantiated with verifiable progress reports or technical specifications that could demonstrate current or imminent compliance.
**Findings of the Tribunal:**
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence placed on record. The Complainant's case is strongly supported by consistent and repeated laboratory analysis reports from an authorized EPA laboratory, which unequivocally demonstrate persistent exceedances of the NEQS for critical parameters (BOD, COD, TSS, and pH) over several months. These reports are corroborated by the inspection reports detailing the observations of EPA officials. The issuance of warning and show-cause notices prior to the filing of the complaint further indicates that the Respondent was provided ample opportunity to rectify the situation, but failed to do so effectively.
Conversely, the Respondent's defense largely consisted of general denials and unsubstantiated claims of efforts towards compliance and ETP upgrades. No credible documentary evidence was presented to counter the specific findings of the EPA's laboratory analyses or to demonstrate that the ETP, if operational, was effective in treating the effluent to NEQS standards. The absence of independent third-party audit reports, ETP operational logs, or recent compliance certificates significantly weakens the Respondent's position. The Respondent's failure to produce any evidence demonstrating compliance even after being put on notice by the EPA, and subsequently by this Tribunal, strongly suggests a continuous pattern of non-compliance.
Based on the overwhelming and uncontroverted evidence presented by the Complainant, the Tribunal finds that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, has consistently discharged industrial effluent in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby contravening Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Respondent's failure to install or effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant capable of meeting NEQS, despite repeated warnings, constitutes a clear and continuous breach of its environmental obligations under the Act.
**Order and Penalty:**
In light of the aforesaid findings, and in exercise of the powers conferred upon this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal hereby passes the following order:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is found guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by continuously discharging industrial effluent in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Imposition of Fine:** The Respondent is directed to pay a fine of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** for the contravention of the Act.
3.  **Daily Penalty for Continuing Violation:** Furthermore, considering the continuous nature of the violation, the Respondent shall pay an additional fine of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for each day the contravention has continued, commencing from the date of filing of this complaint (i.e., 20.09.2017) until the date of this judgment. This amount shall be calculated by the Complainant and submitted to the Tribunal within fifteen (15) days for final approval and payment direction.
4.  **Direction for Compliance:** The Respondent is hereby directed to immediately take all necessary measures to ensure full compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for its industrial effluent discharge. This includes, but is not limited to, upgrading, installing, or effectively operating an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of consistently treating all discharged effluent to meet NEQS parameters.
5.  **Timeline for Compliance:** The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive compliance plan, including a detailed timeline for the upgrade/installation and operationalization of a fully compliant ETP, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment. The Respondent shall achieve full compliance with NEQS within a maximum period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment.
6.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall submit monthly progress reports, along with independent third-party laboratory analysis reports of its treated effluent, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, until consistent compliance is demonstrated for a continuous period of six months.
7.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of fines or the achievement of NEQS within the stipulated timeframe, shall result in further legal action, which may include the imposition of enhanced daily fines, closure of the industrial unit under Section 16 of the Act, and/or other coercive measures as deemed appropriate by the Tribunal.
8.  **Costs:** The costs of these proceedings shall be borne by the Respondent.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
