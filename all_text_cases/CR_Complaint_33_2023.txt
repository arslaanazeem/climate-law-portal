This judgment addresses Complaint No. 33/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited, located at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Gulberg-III, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint alleges serious violations of environmental laws and regulations by the Respondent, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and non-compliance with environmental standards, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Packages Limited include: firstly, the persistent discharge of industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system, and subsequently into natural water bodies, without adequate treatment, resulting in pollutant levels far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents; secondly, the failure to operate and maintain their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively, or at times, its complete non-operation, despite repeated directives and warnings from the EPA; thirdly, the non-compliance with the conditions stipulated in their Environmental Approval (if any, or the absence thereof), and failure to adopt cleaner production technologies; and fourthly, the improper management and disposal of hazardous waste generated from their manufacturing processes, contravening the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003. These actions, or inactions, were alleged to constitute a direct contravention of Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997).
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.05.2023, 02.06.2023, and 18.07.2023, conducted by EPA officials, which consistently noted the discharge of visibly polluted effluent and the non-functional status of the Respondent's ETP during several visits. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the Punjab Environmental Monitoring and Assessment Centre (PEMAC), dated 20.05.2023 and 25.07.2023, demonstrated that samples of the Respondent's discharged effluent contained Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) levels up to 450 mg/L (NEQS limit 150 mg/L), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD) up to 280 mg/L (NEQS limit 80 mg/L), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) up to 300 mg/L (NEQS limit 200 mg/L), besides elevated levels of heavy metals such as Chromium and Lead, all significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The Complainant also submitted copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 05.06.2023 and 01.08.2023, detailing
the observed violations and warning of impending legal action if corrective measures were not promptly taken. Despite these notices, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent failed to demonstrate any significant improvement or provide a satisfactory response, leading to the initiation of the present complaint before this Tribunal.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, submitting a written statement of defense. The Respondent contended that it is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship. They argued that their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully functional and regularly maintained, asserting that any instances of non-operation or exceeding NEQS limits were isolated incidents, possibly due to temporary technical malfunctions or sampling errors. The Respondent further claimed that they have invested substantially in environmental infrastructure and are actively pursuing upgrades to their ETP to meet stricter future environmental standards. Regarding hazardous waste, they stated that all such waste
is collected, stored, and disposed of through authorized and licensed contractors in strict accordance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003. They also challenged the accuracy and methodology of the EPA's sampling and laboratory analysis, suggesting potential contamination or procedural irregularities that might have led to skewed results. The Respondent emphasized that they operate a large-scale industrial facility, and occasional minor fluctuations in effluent quality are an inherent part of such operations, which they are committed to addressing. They requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, emphasizing their ongoing efforts and commitment to sustainable operations and their significant contribution to the national economy.
**Tribunal's Consideration and Findings:**
This Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the pleadings, documentary evidence, and arguments presented by both the Complainant and the Respondent. The core issue revolves around whether the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, has consistently violated environmental laws, particularly concerning effluent discharge and waste management, as alleged by the EPA.
The Complainant's evidence, including multiple inspection reports and laboratory analysis reports from the Punjab Environmental Monitoring and Assessment Centre (PEMAC), provides a strong and consistent prima facie case. The laboratory reports, in particular, repeatedly and demonstrably show effluent parameters (Chemical Oxygen Demand, Biological Oxygen Demand, Total Suspended Solids, and heavy metals like Chromium and Lead) significantly exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) limits on multiple occasions. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,200,000/- (Rupees Three Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 640,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
