**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), invoking the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint, instituted on 10th September 2020, seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties against the Respondent for alleged contraventions of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, a glass manufacturing facility located in Lahore, was operating in a manner detrimental to the environment and public health. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Exceedance of Air Emission Standards:** The Respondent's industrial processes, particularly the glass melting furnaces, were reportedly emitting pollutants such as Particulate Matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) in excess of the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
2.  **Improper Wastewater Discharge:** The industrial effluent generated from the Respondent's cooling and washing processes was being discharged without adequate treatment, failing to meet the NEQS for industrial liquid effluents, particularly concerning parameters like Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and pH.
3.  **Inadequate Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent was alleged to be improperly managing and disposing of hazardous solid waste, including refractory materials and cullet contaminated with certain chemicals, without adhering to the guidelines for hazardous waste management.
4.  **Failure to Submit Environmental Monitoring Reports:** The Complainant further contended that the Respondent had consistently failed to submit regular Self-Monitoring and Reporting Tool (SMART) reports as required under the Self-Monitoring and Reporting Rules, 2001, thereby hindering the EPA's oversight and compliance verification efforts.
5.  **Non-Compliance with NOC Conditions:** The Respondent was found to be in violation of several conditions stipulated in its No Objection Certificate (NOC) issued by the EPA, particularly those pertaining to the installation and maintenance of pollution control equipment and regular environmental audits.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, laboratory analyses, and official correspondence. The evidence included:

2020, which highlighted visible emissions, improper effluent discharge points, and accumulation of hazardous waste. This was corroborated by **laboratory analysis reports dated 25th May 2020 and 10th August 2020**, confirming that samples taken from the Respondent's stacks and discharge points exceeded NEQS for PM, NOx, SO2, TSS, COD, and pH

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
