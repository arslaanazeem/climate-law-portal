This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, on behalf of the Complainant, seeks remedial action and imposition of penalties against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Respondent, a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in Lahore, is engaged in a highly energy-intensive industrial process that involves significant air emissions and generation of industrial waste. The Tribunal was constituted under Section 20 of the PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate upon such matters of environmental non-compliance.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent consistently failed to comply with the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions, particularly concerning Particulate Matter (PM) and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), emanating from its glass melting furnaces. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent was engaged in improper management and disposal of hazardous industrial waste, specifically spent refractories and cullet contaminated with heavy metals, without adherence to the prescribed Hazardous Substances Rules, 2000. The Complainant detailed multiple instances of non-compliance, asserting that despite repeated inspections, issuance of show-cause notices, and directives for corrective measures, the Respondent persistently operated in contravention of environmental laws, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment. The complaint specifically referenced inspections conducted on March 15, 2022, and June 20, 2022, where air emission samples collected revealed exceedances of NEQS, and observations indicated inadequate hazardous waste storage protocols.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documents and expert testimonies. These included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA officials, photographic evidence depicting the alleged improper waste storage facilities and visible emissions, and laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory. These laboratory reports, dated March 25, 2022, and June 28, 2022, consistently indicated that the Respondent's stack emissions for Particulate Matter exceeded the NEQS of 200 mg/Nm³ (found at 350-420 mg/Nm³) and NOx emissions exceeded the NEQS of 600 mg/Nm³ (found at 750-900 mg/Nm³). The Complainant also submitted copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on April 10, 2022, and July 5, 2022, along with subsequent reminder letters, to which the Respondent's responses were deemed unsatisfactory or indicative of insufficient remedial action. An expert witness from the EPA testified regarding the potential environmental and health impacts of such persistent non-compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations. Through its legal counsel, the Respondent submitted a written statement asserting that it had installed and maintained state-of-the-art pollution control equipment, including Electrostatic Precipitators (ESPs) for particulate matter control and Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems for
NOx reduction. They argued that any exceedances noted by the Complainant were isolated incidents, possibly attributable to start-up/shut-down phases, calibration issues, or external factors beyond their immediate control. The Respondent contended that their internal monitoring data, though not fully presented in court, consistently showed compliance. They questioned the accuracy and methodology of the EPA's sampling and laboratory analysis, suggesting potential procedural
flaws in the EPA's sampling and analysis protocols. They further emphasized their significant investment in environmental protection technologies and their commitment to sustainable operations, presenting general evidence of their environmental management system. However, the Respondent failed to produce its internal monitoring data, which it claimed demonstrated compliance, despite being given opportunities to do so.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
