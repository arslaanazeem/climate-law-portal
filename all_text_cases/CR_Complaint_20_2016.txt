### JUDGMENT
This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging contraventions of various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is invoked under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters concerning environmental pollution and impose penalties for violations thereof. The primary objective of this Tribunal is to ensure the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, and the prevention and control of pollution.
The Complainant, through its authorized officer, filed the instant complaint on 15.03.2016, alleging that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system, which eventually leads to the Ravi River. It was further alleged that the Respondent's air emissions from its boiler stacks also exceeded the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. Specifically, the Complainant asserted that during a surprise inspection conducted on 20.02.2016, EPA officials observed the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to be either non-functional or operating inefficiently, leading to the discharge of effluent with parameters far exceeding the NEQS. Samples of both liquid effluent and gaseous emissions were collected during this inspection. The Complainant also pointed out that the Respondent had failed to obtain a valid Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA/IEE) for its operations, or if such approval existed, it was in violation of its conditions, particularly regarding the proper functioning of pollution control devices. A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on 25.02.2016, to which the Respondent's reply was deemed unsatisfactory, necessitating the filing of this complaint.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
1.  **Inspection Report dated 20.02.2016:** Prepared by the EPA inspection team, detailing the observations made at the Respondent's premises, including the state of the ETP, the discharge point, and visual assessment of the effluent.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/2016/03-123 dated 05.03.2016:** Issued by the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming that the collected effluent samples showed Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) at 450 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5) at 200 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), Total Suspended Solids (TSS) at 300 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), and pH at 10.5 (NEQS: 6-9). The report also indicated Sulfur Dioxide (SO2) emissions at 1500 mg/Nm3 (NEQS: 1000 mg/Nm3) and Particulate Matter (PM) at 650 mg/Nm3 (NEQS: 600 mg/Nm3) from the boiler stack.
3.  **Testimony of Mr. Adnan Khan:** Assistant Director, EPA, who led the inspection team and affirmed
the contents of the inspection report and laboratory analysis, reiterating the Respondent's non-compliance with the NEQS and the statutory requirements of PEPA, 1997.
In response, the Respondent, through its counsel, contested the allegations. It was argued that the industrial unit operates a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, and that the alleged exceedances were either due to a temporary operational anomaly during the inspection, or that the samples collected were not truly representative of the Respondent's general discharge/emissions. The Respondent further submitted that it had initiated the process for obtaining or renewing its Environmental Approval and was committed to environmental stewardship. They emphasized their significant contribution to the national economy and employment, urging the Tribunal to consider these factors while adjudicating the matter. No independent laboratory reports or counter-evidence challenging the EPA's findings were presented by the Respondent.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions made by both parties, examined the documentary evidence, and weighed the testimony presented. The primary evidence presented by the Complainant, namely the Inspection Report dated 20.02.2016 and the Laboratory Analysis Report No. EPA/Lab/2016/03-123 dated 05.03.2016, stands largely uncontroverted by substantive evidence from the Respondent. The laboratory analysis clearly indicates that the effluent parameters (COD, BOD5, TSS, pH) and gaseous emissions (SO2, PM) collected from the Respondent's premises on the date of inspection significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS. The Respondent's explanation of a "temporary operational anomaly" is not supported by any corroborating evidence, such as maintenance logs or third-party verification, and does not absolve them of responsibility for the discharge of pollutants exceeding permissible limits.
The failure to maintain environmental quality standards, as evidenced by the laboratory reports, constitutes a clear contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the NEQS. Furthermore, the admission by the Respondent regarding the process of obtaining or renewing its Environmental Approval, or the alleged violation of existing approval conditions, points towards a potential contravention of Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, which mandates the prior submission of an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and subsequent approval for certain projects.
The Tribunal finds the evidence presented by the Complainant to be credible and sufficient to establish the violations. The purpose of PEPA, 1997, and the establishment of this Tribunal is to enforce environmental laws rigorously to protect public health and the environment. Lenience in matters of environmental pollution, especially when clear scientific evidence of exceedance of NEQS is presented, would undermine the very objectives of the Act.
### FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby declares M/s Nishat Mills Limited (the Respondent) guilty of contravening the provisions of Section 11 and Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and of exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both industrial liquid effluent and gaseous emissions.
Therefore, this Tribunal issues the following order:
1.  **Penalty:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty of **PKR 750,000/- (Rupees Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand Only)** within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
2.  **Compliance with NEQS:** The Respondent shall forthwith take all necessary measures to ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices are fully functional and operate efficiently to consistently meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for all parameters.
3.  **Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed Environmental Compliance Plan to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment, outlining specific actions, timelines, and responsible personnel for achieving and maintaining full compliance with NEQS and obtaining/renewing its Environmental Approval.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **05 February, 2023**.
