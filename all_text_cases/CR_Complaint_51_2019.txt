**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 51/2019 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in Lahore. The complaint seeks a declaration that the Respondent is in violation of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"), along with consequential penalties and directions for compliance. The Tribunal has diligently heard arguments from both sides and meticulously reviewed the evidence presented to reach a just and equitable decision.
The Complainant, through its Assistant Director
, presented compelling evidence of repeated non-compliance by the Respondent with the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both air emissions and industrial effluent discharge. The Complainant detailed a series of inspections conducted at the Respondent's facility on various dates, including [mention specific dates if available, otherwise general], which consistently revealed exceedances of NEQS parameters. Laboratory analysis reports, appended to the complaint, specifically highlighted elevated levels of particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulphur dioxide (SOx) in air emissions, and excessive Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and abnormal pH levels in the discharged wastewater. Furthermore, the Complainant submitted copies of multiple show-cause notices and environmental protection orders issued to the Respondent, demonstrating a history of warnings and opportunities for compliance which, according to the EPA, were not adequately addressed. The EPA asserted that the Respondent's operations posed a significant threat to public health and the environment, necessitating immediate and stringent action under PEPA, 1997.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, through its legal counsel, acknowledged certain operational challenges but vehemently denied any deliberate or persistent non-compliance. The Respondent contended that it had invested considerable resources in upgrading its pollution control infrastructure, including the installation of modern air pollution control devices and an effluent treatment plant (ETP). It was argued that achieving absolute compliance with NEQS in a continuous manufacturing process is a complex and time-consuming endeavor, requiring ongoing adjustments and technological improvements. The Respondent also questioned the methodology and calibration of some of the EPA's monitoring equipment, suggesting potential inaccuracies in the reported data. Moreover, the Respondent emphasized its significant contribution to the national economy and employment, urging the Tribunal to adopt a pragmatic approach that balances environmental protection with industrial sustainability. It sought more time to achieve full compliance, outlining a proposed phased action plan.
The Tribunal, having carefully considered the arguments presented by both sides and having thoroughly reviewed the documentary evidence, including inspection reports, laboratory analyses, show-cause notices, and the Respondent's submissions, finds that the Complainant has successfully established a pattern of non-compliance with the NEQS. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's efforts and investments in pollution control, the evidence unequivocally demonstrates that the Respondent's facility has repeatedly failed to meet the mandatory environmental standards set forth under PEPA, 1997. The laboratory reports, conducted by accredited laboratories, consistently point towards exceedances, which cannot be dismissed merely as technical glitches or methodological errors. The repeated issuance of notices and orders by the EPA further substantiates the persistence of these violations. The Tribunal is of the considered view that environmental protection cannot be compromised for industrial convenience, and compliance with NEQS is a non-negotiable legal obligation. The public's right to a clean and healthy environment, enshrined in the Constitution, must take precedence.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby issues the following final order and penalty:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited is hereby declared to be in clear violation of Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) in respect of both air emissions and industrial effluent discharge.
2.  **Environmental Penalty (Fine):** The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment, into the designated account of the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab.
3.  **Compliance Directives:** The Respondent is directed to undertake the following remedial measures:
    *   **Air Emissions:** Immediately upgrade and install state-of-the-art air pollution control devices (e.g., bag filters, scrubbers) to ensure that all parameters of air emissions, particularly Particulate Matter, NOx, and SOx, are brought within the prescribed NEQS. A comprehensive compliance plan, including timelines for installation and commissioning, shall be submitted to the EPA within fifteen (15) days.
    *   **Effluent Discharge:** Ensure that the existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully operational, optimized, and, if necessary, upgraded to effectively treat all industrial wastewater such that the discharged effluent consistently meets all NEQS parameters, including BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. A detailed plan for ETP optimization or upgrade, with clear milestones, shall be submitted to the EPA within fifteen (15) days.
4.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall install continuous online monitoring systems for both air emissions and effluent discharge, providing real-time data access to the EPA. Monthly compliance reports, duly verified by an independent third-party environmental consultant approved by the EPA, shall be submitted to the EPA for a period of one year, commencing from the date of this judgment.
5.  **Cessation of Non-Compliant Operations:** In the event of persistent non-compliance with the NEQS after a period of ninety (90) days from the date of this judgment, the Environmental Protection Agency is authorized to take further stringent action, including the closure of the non-compliant sections of the facility, in accordance with the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
6.  **Costs:** The costs of this complaint shall also be borne by the Respondent.
This judgment shall be implemented forthwith. The EPA is directed to ensure strict compliance with this order and to report back to the Tribunal on the progress made by the Respondent within ninety (90) days.
