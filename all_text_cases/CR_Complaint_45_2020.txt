This judgment addresses Complaint No. 45/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging and paper products. The complaint, lodged on October 15, 2020, invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and regulations, particularly concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants.
The EPA's complaint detailed a series of alleged environmental contraventions by the Respondent's industrial facility located in Lahore. Specifically, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into surface waters, causing significant water pollution. Furthermore, it was alleged that
the Respondent's industrial operations were emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen oxides, in excess of the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for ambient air and industrial emissions. These emissions, it was contended, were contributing significantly to air quality degradation in the surrounding areas, causing smog, and posing serious health risks to the local population, in violation of Section 12 of PEPA, 1997.
The EPA supported its complaint with documentary evidence, including multiple inspection reports dated January 10, 2020, March 5, 2020, July 22, 2020, and September 15, 2020, which detailed the alleged non-compliance. These reports were accompanied by laboratory analysis results of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points and stack emissions, all indicating exceedances of the NEQS for various parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and specific air pollutants like PM2.5 and SO2. Copies of Show Cause Notices issued to the Respondent and subsequent unsatisfactory responses were also presented as evidence of the Respondent's failure to address the violations.
In response, M/s Packages Limited, through its counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. The Respondent asserted that it operates with due regard for environmental protection
and has made substantial investments in state-of-the-art Environmental Treatment Plants (ETP) for wastewater management and Air Pollution Control Systems (APCS) for mitigating air emissions. Their counsel argued that the Respondent conducts regular internal monitoring and audits to ensure compliance, and that any alleged exceedances were either isolated incidents, attributable to unforeseen operational fluctuations, or not reflective of their overall commitment to environmental

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
