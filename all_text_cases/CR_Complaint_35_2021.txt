This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 35/2021 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint seeks the intervention of this Tribunal for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. The Complainant, representing the provincial environmental regulatory body, has invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the PEPA, 1997, to address persistent environmental non-compliance by the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing.
The Complainant's allegations centre on the Respondent's continuous discharge of industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, resulting in the exceedance of prescribed NEQS. Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15.03.2021, 28.05.2021, and 12.08.2021, revealed the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to be either non-functional, improperly operated, or insufficient to treat the industrial wastewater to the required standards. Laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected during these inspections consistently indicated levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding the permissible NEQS limits. Furthermore, the presence of certain heavy metals in concentrations above the acceptable thresholds was also reported. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated issuance of show-cause notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of the PEPA, 1997, directing the Respondent to rectify the deficiencies and ensure compliance, M/s Nishat Mills Limited failed to take effective measures, thereby continuing to pollute the environment and posing a substantial risk to water bodies, soil quality, and public health in the vicinity.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams, duly signed by the inspecting officers. Crucially, a series of certified laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory were submitted, providing scientific data on the quality of the discharged effluent and unequivocally demonstrating the exceedance of NEQS parameters. Copies of all show-cause notices, warning letters, and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued to the Respondent, along with proof of their service, were placed on record, establishing the Complainant's diligent efforts to secure compliance through administrative means. The testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director, EPA, who conducted several inspections and oversaw the sampling process, further corroborated the documentary evidence. He deposed that the Respondent's ETP, when operational, was visibly inadequate, and during some visits, it appeared to be bypassed or running below its designed capacity.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations, arguing that it had an operational ETP and was making continuous efforts towards environmental compliance. They questioned the sampling methodology on certain occasions and the accuracy of some laboratory reports, though no independent counter-analysis
was presented to rebut the Complainant's scientific findings. The Respondent's counsel further submitted that significant investments had been made in the ETP over time and that operational challenges, rather than deliberate non-compliance, were the root cause of any occasional exceedances. They requested the Tribunal to consider their ongoing efforts and provide a reasonable timeframe for achieving full compliance, rather than imposing immediate stringent penalties.
The Tribunal meticulously examined the evidence presented by both parties. It noted the consistency in the Complainant's inspection reports and, more significantly, the undeniable scientific data contained in the certified laboratory analysis reports. These reports, emanating from an accredited EPA laboratory, consistently showed effluent parameters far exceeding the NEQS limits on multiple occasions, spanning several months. The Respondent's general denial and questioning of methodology, without producing any credible, independent counter-analysis or expert testimony to challenge the EPA's findings, failed to sufficiently discredit the robust evidence presented by the Complainant. The Tribunal also observed that the repeated issuance of show-cause notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of the PEPA, 1997, clearly demonstrated that the Respondent had been duly informed of the violations and provided ample opportunity to rectify the situation. The failure to comply with these administrative directives, as evidenced by subsequent inspections and sampling results, indicated a persistent and serious disregard for environmental regulations.
Having carefully considered all the material placed on record and the arguments advanced by the learned counsels, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has been in continuous violation of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection
Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. The Respondent's repeated failure to adhere to the prescribed standards, despite numerous warnings and administrative orders, constitutes a serious breach of environmental law, posing significant risks to public health and the ecological balance. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's assertion of having made investments in its ETP and facing operational challenges, these factors do not absolve it from the legal obligation to comply with environmental standards. The evidence overwhelmingly demonstrates that the measures taken thus far have been insufficient to achieve the required level of treatment, leading to continuous pollution.
Therefore, having established the Respondent's culpability, this Tribunal proceeds to impose penalties and issue directives in accordance with the powers vested in it under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
1.  M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby found guilty of continuously violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, as detailed in the body of this judgment.
2.  In light of the persistent and serious nature of the violations, and pursuant to Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, M/s Nishat Mills Limited is hereby directed to pay an environmental penalty (fine) of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand only)**. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  Furthermore, M/s Nishat Mills Limited is directed to undertake immediate and effective measures to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the consistent and proper functioning of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to achieve full compliance with all parameters of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Respondent shall submit a detailed action plan, including a comprehensive timeline for the completion of these upgrades, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within forty-five (45) days from the date of this order.
4.  The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports, duly verified by an independent environmental consultant approved by the EPA, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, for a period of twelve (12) months, commencing from the submission of the action plan, detailing the operational status of the ETP and the quality of the treated effluent.
5.  The Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, is directed to conduct regular, unannounced inspections and sampling of the Respondent's effluent for a period of two (2) years to ensure sustained compliance with the NEQS.
6.  Failure to comply with any part of this order, including the payment of the penalty or the implementation of the directed remedial measures within the stipulated timelines, shall render the Respondent liable to further penalties, including a daily fine of **PKR 75,000/-

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
