This judgment addresses Complaint No. 7/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing located in Sundar Industrial Estate, Lahore. The complaint was lodged under Section 17 read with Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Tribunal has duly heard both parties and examined the evidence presented to reach a just conclusion.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent was operating its textile manufacturing facility in flagrant disregard of environmental standards. Specifically, the allegations included:
a) Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, subsequently flowing into natural water bodies, thereby exceeding the National Environmental
Quality Standards (NEQS).
b) Emission of air pollutants, including particulate matter and noxious gases, from its stacks without proper air pollution control devices, thereby exceeding permissible limits.
c) Improper disposal of hazardous solid waste, leading to soil contamination and posing risks to public health and the environment.
The Tribunal, having meticulously reviewed the inspection reports, laboratory analyses of effluent and emission samples conducted by the EPA, and the Respondent's submissions, finds that the allegations leveled against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited are substantiated. The evidence unequivocally demonstrates that the Respondent has been operating in contravention of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both effluent discharge and air emissions. The Respondent's failure to install or effectively operate an adequate Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices has resulted in significant environmental degradation, posing a serious threat to public health and the local ecosystem. Such actions constitute a clear violation of Section 12, 13, and 15 of PEPA, 1997, read with the relevant SROs and Regulations. Therefore, the Respondent is liable to penalties as prescribed under Section 17(3) and Section 21 of PEPA, 1997.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is directed to immediately cease all activities that result in the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Installation/Upgradation of Treatment Facilities:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, install a fully functional and compliant Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to ensure that all discharges and emissions meet the prescribed NEQS. A detailed action plan for the installation/upgradation, including timelines, shall be submitted to the EPA within **thirty (30) days**.
3.  **Proper Waste Management:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days**, formulate and implement a comprehensive hazardous waste management plan, ensuring proper segregation, storage, and disposal of all industrial waste in accordance with the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and relevant rules and regulations.
4.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited for the continuous and severe violations of environmental laws. This amount shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.
5.  **Daily Fine:** In case of non-compliance with the directions in paragraphs 1, 2, and 3, a further daily fine of **PKR 75,000/- (Rupees Seventy-Five Thousand only)** shall be levied on the Respondent for each day the violation continues, until full compliance is achieved.
6.  **Environmental Monitoring:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is directed to conduct regular and unannounced inspections and monitoring of the Respondent's facility for a period of **two (2) years** to ensure sustained compliance with all environmental laws and this judgment. The cost of such monitoring, if any, shall be borne by the Respondent.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
