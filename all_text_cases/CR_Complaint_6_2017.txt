This judgment addresses Complaint No. 6/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing facility situated in Lahore. The complaint seeks adjudication and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluents and emission of gaseous pollutants, leading to environmental degradation and non-compliance with National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant, through its legal representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were in persistent violation of environmental protection laws. Specifically, it was asserted that:
a) The Respondent's industrial gaseous emissions, particularly from its boiler stacks and process vents, consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for Particulate Matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO
₂), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOₓ).
b) The industrial effluents discharged from the Respondent’s premises into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into natural water bodies failed to meet the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD₅), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals.
c) Despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent had failed to take adequate measures to rectify these violations, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental protection regulations. The Complainant submitted inspection reports, laboratory analysis results, and copies of show-cause notices as evidence of non-compliance.
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It was contended that M/s Packages Limited is a responsible corporate entity with a strong commitment to environmental stewardship. The Respondent argued that:
a) It had invested significantly in state-of-the-art environmental management systems, including an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), which were regularly maintained and upgraded.
b) Any occasional exceedances of NEQS were minor, temporary, and attributable to unforeseen operational upsets, which were promptly addressed.
c) The Respondent had been actively collaborating with the EPA, submitting self-monitoring reports, and undertaking voluntary measures to further improve its environmental performance.
d) The laboratory reports presented by the Complainant were disputed on grounds of sampling methodology and chain of custody, and the Respondent presented its own independent monitoring reports to demonstrate compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including the inspection reports, analytical data from EPA-approved laboratories, and the Respondent's self-monitoring records. It was observed that while the Respondent had indeed invested in environmental infrastructure, the evidence, particularly the consistent pattern of exceedances documented by the EPA over several monitoring periods, pointed towards recurring non-compliance. The Tribunal noted that the onus was on the Respondent to demonstrate continuous compliance with NEQS, and occasional operational upsets, if frequent, could not serve as a perpetual excuse for persistent violations. The Tribunal also took into consideration the previous show-cause notices issued by the EPA, which indicated that the Respondent was well aware of the violations and had been given ample opportunity to rectify the situation.
After careful consideration of the arguments, evidence, and relevant provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, has been in violation of environmental laws and regulations as alleged. The persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluents and emissions exceeding NEQS constitutes environmental pollution and is detrimental to public health and the environment.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Packages Limited is found guilty of violating Section 11 and Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), pertaining to the discharge of industrial effluents and emission of gaseous pollutants.
2.  **Cessation of Violation:** The Respondent shall immediately cease all activities that result in the discharge of industrial effluents and emission of gaseous pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
3.  **Compliance Plan:** The Respondent is directed to submit to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order, a comprehensive and time-bound Environmental Compliance Plan detailing specific measures, including technological upgrades, operational improvements, and monitoring protocols, to ensure continuous and sustained compliance with all applicable NEQS for both air emissions and effluent discharge. This plan must be approved by the EPA.
4.  **Technological Upgrades:** The Respondent shall undertake all necessary upgrades and improvements to its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) as identified in the approved Compliance Plan, and ensure their optimal functioning to achieve full compliance with NEQS.
5.  **Regular Monitoring & Reporting:** The Respondent shall conduct regular, independent third-party monitoring of its effluent discharge and gaseous emissions, at least on a monthly basis, and submit quarterly compliance reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency for a period of two (2) years.
6.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the persistent violations of environmental laws and regulations. This penalty shall be paid into the account of the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within sixty (60) days from the date of this order.
7.  **Default Clause:** In case of failure to submit the Compliance Plan or pay the penalty within the stipulated timeframes, or in case of continued non-compliance with NEQS following this order, the Environmental Protection Agency shall be at liberty to initiate further legal action, including but not limited to, the sealing of the industrial unit or imposing further daily penalties as provided under PEPA, 1997.
8.  **Costs:** There shall be no order as to costs.
