This judgment pertains to the complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in manufacturing packaging materials. The complaint, filed under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA, 1997"), alleges serious violations of environmental laws and standards, posing a threat to public health and the environment. The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the submissions, evidence, and arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just and equitable decision.
The Complainant's primary allegations against the Respondent revolve around persistent non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge and air emissions. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were discharging untreated or inadequately treated wastewater directly into the municipal sewerage system, eventually polluting surface water bodies. Laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA reportedly indicated that key parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels in the effluent consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS limits. Furthermore, the complaint detailed concerns regarding the emission of hazardous air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulphur dioxide (SO2), from the Respondent's stacks, exceeding the permissible NEQS for industrial air emissions. The Complainant also highlighted the Respondent's alleged failure to implement effective pollution control measures despite repeated warnings and notices, demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations and corporate environmental responsibility. The EPA sought punitive action, including fines and directives for immediate compliance, to prevent further environmental degradation.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a robust body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated between January 2022 and August 2022, detailing observations of effluent discharge points and stack emissions, alongside photographic and videographic evidence. Crucially, the Complainant submitted comprehensive laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming that samples collected from the Respondent's effluent discharge points and air emission stacks on various dates consistently showed levels of pollutants significantly exceeding the NEQS. For instance, one report dated March 15, 2022, indicated BOD levels of 150 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L, and COD levels of 450 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L. Air emission reports from May 20, 2022, showed PM levels of 600 mg/Nm³ against a NEQS limit of 400 mg/Nm³. Copies of show cause notices issued to the Respondent on April 10, 2022, and July 5,
2022, were also submitted, demonstrating the EPA's consistent efforts to secure voluntary compliance before resorting to legal action.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. It argued that it is a responsible corporate entity with a dedicated environmental management system and significant investments in pollution control infrastructure, including an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. The Respondent contended that any exceedances reported by the Complainant were isolated incidents, possibly attributable to temporary operational upsets or inaccuracies in the EPA's sampling and analysis methodologies. It presented internal monitoring data, albeit not independently verified by an accredited third-party laboratory, which it claimed demonstrated general compliance with NEQS. The Respondent further highlighted its various corporate social responsibility initiatives and environmental certifications, such as ISO 14001, as proof of its commitment to environmental protection. It requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, or in the alternative, to provide a reasonable timeframe for any necessary rectifications without imposing punitive fines that
would hinder its operational viability and capacity to invest further in environmental improvements.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
