**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 15/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial enterprise located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged on October 23, 2018, seeks redressal for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, duly constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial facility, engaged in various manufacturing processes including paper, board, and packaging materials, was operating in contravention of environmental laws. Specifically, it was asserted that the Respondent was:
a) Discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/drain, the parameters of which consistently exceeded the NEQS for various pollutants, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH.
b) Emitting air pollutants, particularly particulate matter (PM) and oxides of sulfur and nitrogen, from its boiler stacks and other process vents, which were found to be above the permissible NEQS limits.
c) Failing to effectively manage and dispose of its hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste in an environmentally sound manner.
d) Operating without a valid Environmental Approval/NOC for certain expansions or modifications, or failing to adhere to the conditions stipulated in existing environmental approvals.
The Complainant contended that these activities posed a significant threat to public health and the environment, causing pollution of water bodies
and degradation of the natural environment, thereby violating Sections 11, 12, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Complainant sought appropriate penal action under Section 17 of the PEPA, 1997, along with directives for the Respondent to bring its operations into compliance with the NEQS and other environmental regulations.
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it operates its facility with due regard for environmental protection and has made significant investments in pollution control infrastructure. It was contended that M/s Packages Limited maintains an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) designed to meet NEQS, and that any reported exceedances were either isolated incidents, attributable to operational upsets, or based on non-representative sampling by the Complainant. The Respondent further submitted that it regularly monitors its emissions and discharges, and strives for continuous improvement in its environmental performance, holding valid environmental approvals for its core operations and being in the process of obtaining necessary clearances for any recent expansions or modifications. The Respondent also presented evidence of its solid waste management practices, emphasizing recycling and disposal through authorized channels.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
