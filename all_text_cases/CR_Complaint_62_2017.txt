The present complaint, bearing No. 62/2017, was filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS") concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and inadequate waste management practices. The Tribunal was constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters relating to environmental contraventions.
The crux of the allegations leveled by the Complainant against the Respondent is the persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the Lahore Branch Canal, a public water body, thereby causing significant water pollution and posing a threat to aquatic life and human health. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that despite repeated warnings and notices, the Respondent failed to install a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating its industrial wastewater to meet the prescribed NEQS parameters for discharge. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of improper disposal of solid and hazardous waste generated during the textile manufacturing process, leading to localized soil contamination and aesthetic degradation. The EPA asserted that the Respondent's actions constituted a direct contravention of environmental laws, causing irreparable harm to the ecosystem and public interest.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, dating from January 2016 to April 2017, conducted by EPA officials at the Respondent's premises. These reports consistently highlighted the absence of an operational ETP and documented direct discharge points releasing dark-colored, foul-smelling effluent into the canal. Laboratory analysis reports, certified by the EPA's accredited laboratory, were submitted as Annexures 'C' to 'H', showing that samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points consistently exceeded the NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH
, heavy metals (such as Chromium and Lead), and Oil & Grease. The Complainant further appended copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, dated May 2016 and February 2017, demanding immediate compliance and outlining potential legal consequences, to which the Respondent's responses were deemed unsatisfactory and evasive.

the allegations, primarily arguing that the EPA's inspection reports and laboratory analyses were flawed, lacking proper methodology in sample collection, preservation, and testing, thus rendering the results unreliable. It was further contended that M/s Nishat Mills Limited is a responsible corporate entity with a strong commitment to environmental compliance, having already initiated significant investments towards upgrading its environmental infrastructure. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,400,000/- (Rupees Two Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 480,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
