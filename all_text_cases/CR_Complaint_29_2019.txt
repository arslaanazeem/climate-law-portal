This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 29/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, on 15th October 2019, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated at 22-KM Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remediation measures against the Respondent for alleged contraventions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS") for industrial effluent discharge, thereby causing environmental pollution and degradation. The Tribunal has diligently heard arguments from both parties and meticulously reviewed the submitted evidence to render a just and lawful decision.
It is alleged by the Complainant that the Respondent, a textile manufacturing unit, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, or with treatment that fails to meet the prescribed NEQS. The EPA conducted multiple inspections of the Respondent's premises. Specifically, an inspection on 12th August 2019 revealed that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was either non-functional or operating inefficiently, leading to the discharge of untreated or partially treated wastewater. Samples collected during this inspection, upon laboratory analysis, demonstrated significant exceedance of the NEQS parameters for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. The Complainant further asserts that despite repeated verbal and written warnings, including a Show Cause Notice dated 28th August 2019, followed by an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued on 25th September 2019, directing the Respondent to cease the illegal discharge and ensure compliance with NEQS, the Respondent failed to take effective corrective measures. The EPA contends that the Respondent's continued non-compliance poses a serious threat to public health and the environment, particularly contaminating surface and groundwater resources in the vicinity.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documents, including the inspection reports from 12th August 2019 and subsequent follow-up visits, duly signed by EPA officials. Crucially, the laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers and dates, clearly indicated the levels of pollutants in the Respondent's discharge, demonstrating significant deviations from the NEQS. Copies of the Show Cause Notice dated 28th August 2019 and the Environmental Protection Order dated 25th September 2019, along with proof of their dispatch and receipt by the Respondent, were also submitted. The Complainant also presented the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director EPA, who elaborated on the inspection findings, sampling procedures, and the Respondent's persistent non-compliance. On the other hand, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel and authorized representative Mr. Imran Ali, primarily argued that they had installed an ETP and were making continuous efforts to upgrade and optimize its functionality. They submitted some internal monitoring reports, which, however, did not consistently reflect compliance with NEQS. The Respondent also contended that the EPA's sampling methodology might have been flawed or that the samples might not have been representative of their typical discharge. They requested more time to achieve full compliance, citing the technical complexities and financial
outlays involved in achieving complete and consistent compliance with the stringent NEQS. They sought a grace period to further enhance their ETP capabilities and demonstrate sustained adherence to environmental standards.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions of both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary and testimonial evidence placed on record. It is evident from the comprehensive inspection reports and the undisputed laboratory analysis reports submitted by the Complainant that the Respondent's industrial effluent consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for key parameters, including COD, BOD, TSS, and pH, on multiple occasions. The Complainant's evidence, particularly the accredited laboratory reports, carries significant probative value and clearly establishes a prima facie case of environmental pollution and violation of PEPA, 1997.
While the Respondent's assertion of having installed an ETP and making efforts towards its optimization is noted, these efforts, as evidenced by their own internal monitoring reports and the EPA's findings, have not been sufficient to achieve and maintain compliance with the NEQS. The claim regarding flawed sampling methodology or unrepresentative samples was not substantiated with credible evidence to effectively rebut the scientific findings presented by the EPA. The issuance of a Show Cause Notice and a subsequent Environmental Protection Order demonstrates that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations and provided opportunities to rectify the situation, yet failed to do so effectively. The continued discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent poses an undeniable threat to the environment and public health, as rightly highlighted by the Complainant.
Therefore, the Tribunal finds that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, has contravened Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging environmental pollutants in excess of the NEQS, and has failed to comply with the Environmental Protection Order issued under Section 16 of the Act.
**NOW,
THEREFORE, in light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby **ORDERS** as follows:
1.  **CONVICTION:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is found **GUILTY** of contravening Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, and of failing to comply with the Environmental Protection Order issued under Section 16 of the said Act.
2.  **PENALTY:** Pursuant to Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** for the contraventions established.
3.  **CONTINUING PENALTY:** Furthermore, for any continued non-compliance from the date of this order until full compliance is achieved, the Respondent shall pay a daily penalty of **PKR 75,000/- (Rupees Seventy-Five Thousand Only)** for each day the contravention continues.
4.  **REMEDIATION AND COMPLIANCE ORDER:**
    *   The Respondent shall immediately cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards.
    *   The Respondent is directed to forthwith undertake all necessary measures to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the efficient and continuous operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to achieve and maintain consistent compliance with the NEQS for all relevant parameters (including COD, BOD, TSS, and pH).
    *   The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive compliance plan to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order, detailing the steps and timeline for achieving full compliance.
    *   The Respondent shall provide monthly progress reports, including independent laboratory analysis reports of its treated effluent, to the EPA for a period of six months, or until the EPA is satisfied with consistent compliance.
    *   The EPA is authorized to conduct regular inspections and collect samples to verify compliance.
5.  **FURTHER ACTION:** Failure to comply with any part of this order may result in further legal proceedings, including but not limited to, the sealing of the industrial unit under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
6.  **COSTS:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
7.  **DISPOSAL:** Complaint No. 29/2019 is hereby **DISPOSED OF** in the terms stated above.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
