This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit, for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint, registered on October 15, 2018, seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial measures against the Respondent for discharging industrial effluent exceeding prescribed standards and operating without proper environmental approvals.
The Complainant's allegations center on the Respondent's failure to adequately treat its industrial effluent before discharge, thereby causing environmental pollution. Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed consistent non-compliance with the NEQS for industrial effluents. On March 15, 2018, an inspection team from the EPA visited the Respondent's facility located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, and collected samples of the treated effluent being discharged into the nearby Hadiara Drain, which eventually flows into the River Ravi. Subsequent laboratory analysis, as per report No. EPA/LAB/2018/03-123 dated April 05, 2018, indicated that the effluent samples exceeded the NEQS for several critical parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. The report showed COD at 350 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), BOD5 at 180 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), TSS at 220 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), and pH at 9.5 (NEQS: 6-9). A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on April 10, 2018, demanding an explanation and immediate corrective action. The Respondent submitted a reply dated April 25, 2018, acknowledging some operational issues with its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) but assuring the EPA of corrective measures. However, a follow-up inspection on August 20, 2018, and subsequent sampling, with lab report No. EPA/LAB/2018/08-210 dated September 10, 2018, again revealed similar exceedances, demonstrating a persistent failure to comply. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent had failed to obtain a valid Environmental Approval (NOC) for its expanded operations and effluent discharge system, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant presented a robust body of evidence to substantiate its
allegations, including detailed inspection reports, photographs of the discharge points, and the aforementioned successive laboratory analyses confirming persistent exceedances of NEQS for COD, BOD5, TSS, and pH. The Complainant further submitted expert testimony from an environmental engineer highlighting the inadequacy of the Respondent's existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to handle the volume and nature of its industrial waste, particularly after alleged
expansion of operations without upgrading its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). The Complainant argued that the Respondent's actions constituted a clear violation of Sections 11, 12, and 16 of PEPA, 1997, which mandate compliance with NEQS, obtaining environmental approvals for new or expanded projects, and prohibit discharge of pollutants in excess of standards, respectively. The Complainant sought maximum penalties as provided under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, along with directives for immediate compliance and upgradation of the ETP to meet the prescribed standards.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. While acknowledging the initial exceedances reported in March 2018, the Respondent contended that these were due to temporary operational malfunctions in their ETP, exacerbated by unforeseen power fluctuations. They asserted that immediate remedial measures, including repairs and optimization of the ETP's performance, were undertaken promptly. Regarding the follow-up inspection in August 2018, the Respondent argued that the samples might not have been truly representative of their routine discharge or that the testing methodology could be subject to challenge. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed that it had initiated the process for obtaining environmental approval for its expanded operations well before the complaint was filed and was actively pursuing its issuance, presenting evidence of applications submitted to the EPA. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
