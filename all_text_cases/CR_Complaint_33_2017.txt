This judgment addresses Complaint No. 33/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint was lodged on the 15th of March, 2017, seeking a finding of guilt against the Respondent for causing environmental degradation and imposing appropriate penalties and remedial measures.
The Complainant contended that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in the industrial zone of Lahore, was operating without adequate environmental safeguards, leading to significant pollution. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into a nearby municipal drain, which eventually merged with the River Ravi, thereby contravening the NEQS for industrial effluents. Furthermore, the complaint detailed allegations of excessive air emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks, exceeding permissible limits for particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, contributing to ambient air pollution. The EPA's investigations also indicated non-compliance with noise level standards within and around the factory premises, affecting the health and well-being of workers and nearby residents. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent had failed to take satisfactory corrective actions, necessitating the initiation of formal proceedings before this Tribunal.
To substantiate the allegations, the Complainant submitted a comprehensive set of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 12.12.2016, 18.01.2017, and 05.03.2017, prepared by EPA field teams, detailing observations of effluent discharge points and stack emissions. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA-accredited laboratory, dated 20.01.2017 and 08.03.2017, were presented, which showed that samples of the Respondent's industrial effluent exceeded the NEQS parameters for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Similarly, air emission analyses indicated exceedances for particulate matter (PM10) and SO2. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the color and volume of the discharged effluent and the visible plume from stacks was also tendered. The Assistant Director, EPA, and two environmental inspectors testified, affirming the contents of their reports and the Respondent's persistent non-compliance. On its part, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel and production manager, vehemently denied the allegations. They presented evidence of their existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), arguing that these facilities were operational and generally compliant. They submitted operational logs for their ETP and APCDs, as well as internal monitoring reports. While acknowledging minor fluctuations, they attributed any deviations to unforeseen operational exigencies or variations in raw material quality, rather than deliberate non-compliance. They also highlighted their significant contribution to the national economy and employment, urging a lenient view.
The legal framework governing this matter is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, strictly prohibits any person from discharging or emitting any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Section 16 grants the Agency powers to issue environmental protection orders to prevent or control pollution. Section 17 prescribes penalties for contravention of the provisions of the Act, including imprisonment and fines. Specifically, Section 17(1) states that whoever contravenes or fails to comply with the provisions of Section 11 shall be punishable with a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, with a further fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which the contravention continues. This Tribunal derives its jurisdiction from Section 21 and 22 of PEPA, 1997, to hear and adjudicate complaints regarding environmental violations.
Upon careful consideration of the documentary and oral evidence presented by both parties, and after hearing the elaborate arguments of their respective counsels, this Tribunal has reached the following
**findings and order:**
**FINDINGS:**
1.  The evidence presented by the Complainant, particularly the multiple inspection reports and the comprehensive laboratory analysis reports from the EPA-accredited laboratory, unequivocally demonstrates that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen
Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. The laboratory reports also confirm the exceedance of NEQS for these parameters.
2.  The Tribunal further finds that the air emissions from the Respondent's boiler stacks, specifically particulate matter (PM10) and sulfur dioxide (SO2), also exceeded the permissible limits set by the NEQS, as evidenced by the EPA's laboratory analyses dated 20.01.2017 and 08.03.2017.
3.  The allegations regarding non-compliance with noise level standards within and around the factory premises have also been substantiated by the Complainant's evidence, indicating a failure to maintain ambient noise levels within NEQS limits.
4.  The Respondent's arguments regarding the operational status of their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), and attributing deviations to unforeseen operational exigencies or variations in raw material quality, are not sufficiently persuasive to negate the consistent and documented violations presented by the EPA. The internal monitoring reports submitted by the Respondent do not adequately counter the independent and accredited laboratory findings of the EPA.
5.  Despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent failed to take adequate and sustained corrective actions to bring its operations into consistent compliance with the NEQS and the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
6.  Therefore, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has contravened the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent, emitting air pollutants, and generating noise in amounts, concentrations, and levels in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards.
**ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the above findings, this Tribunal hereby finds the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
**It is hereby ordered as follows:**
1.  **Penalty:** Pursuant to Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is hereby ordered to pay a fine of **PKR 900,000/- (Rupees Nine Hundred Thousand only)** for the contravention of Section 11 of the Act.
2.  **Continuing Contravention Fine:** Furthermore, recognizing the persistent nature of the violations as established by the evidence, the Respondent shall pay a further fine of **PKR 75,000/- (Rupees Seventy-Five Thousand only) for every day** from the date of this order until full compliance with the NEQS for effluent discharge, air emissions, and noise levels is achieved and confirmed by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).
3.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent is directed to immediately undertake the following remedial actions:
    *   **Upgrade and Optimize ETP and APCDs:** The Respondent shall upgrade and optimize its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) to ensure consistent compliance with all relevant NEQS parameters for industrial effluents and air emissions. This must be completed within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
