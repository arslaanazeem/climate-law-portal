This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging violations of various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Complainant seeks the imposition of penalties and directions for compliance as provided under the relevant environmental statutes.
The Complainant submitted that the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, operating a textile processing unit at Ferozepur Road, Lahore, was engaged in industrial activities that generate significant quantities of wastewater and air emissions. During routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, specifically on 15.03.2019 and 22.04.2019, it was observed that the Respondent's industrial unit was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drain, which ultimately leads to a natural water body, without conforming to the NEQS. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's air emission stacks were emitting dark smoke and particulate matter exceeding the prescribed NEQS, and that the unit lacked a fully functional and efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs). It was also alleged that the Respondent had failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA/IEE) for its operations or for any significant modifications/expansions, and had not submitted regular Self-Monitoring and Reporting Tool (SMART) reports as required under the law. Show-cause notices were issued on 29.03.2019 and 03.05.2019, to which the Respondent's replies were found unsatisfactory, leading to the filing of this complaint under Section 17(2) of PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant presented documentary evidence including inspection reports dated 15.03.2019 and 22.04.2019, duly signed by EPA officials, which detailed the observations regarding the discharge of effluent and air emissions. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA-accredited laboratory, bearing reference numbers EPA/Lab/2019/123 and EPA/Lab/2019/145, were submitted. These reports indicated that samples of the Respondent's industrial effluent collected on the aforementioned dates consistently exceeded the NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to, Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Photographic evidence depicting the discharge of discolored effluent and visible dark smoke from stacks was also tendered. Copies of the show-cause notices and the Respondent's replies, which largely contained assurances of future compliance or denial without substantive proof, were also part of the record. The Respondent, in its defense, primarily contended that it was in the process of upgrading its ETP and installing new APCDs, and that any non-compliance was temporary and unintentional. It presented some purchase orders for equipment and a partial compliance plan, but failed to provide any certified laboratory reports demonstrating current compliance with NEQS or proof of a valid environmental approval for its operations. No credible evidence was presented to rebut the specific findings of the EPA'
laboratory analysis reports, photographic evidence, and inspection findings. The Respondent's reliance on mere assurances and a partial compliance plan, without concrete proof of current adherence to environmental standards, is insufficient to rebut the clear and convincing evidence presented by the Complainant.
**FINDINGS:**
Upon careful consideration of the evidence adduced by both parties, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, beyond reasonable doubt, that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been in persistent violation of various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. Specifically, the Respondent has:
1.  Discharged untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent exceeding NEQS into the public sewerage system/drain.
2.  Emitted dark smoke and particulate matter from its stacks exceeding the prescribed NEQS.
3.  Failed to install and operate a fully functional and efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs).
4.  Operated without obtaining the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA/IEE) for its operations or any significant modifications/expansions.
5.  Failed to submit regular Self-Monitoring and Reporting Tool (SMART) reports as required by law.
These violations constitute a direct contravention of Section 11, 12, and 13 of PEPA, 1997, read with the relevant NEQS. The Respondent's actions demonstrate a clear disregard for environmental regulations and pose a significant threat to public health and the environment.
**ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings and in exercise of the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17(2) and 17(3) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:
1.  **Immediate Compliance:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to immediately cease all activities that result in the discharge of untreated/inadequately treated effluent and the emission of pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Installation/

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
