### Judgment
**Introduction**
1.  This judgment concerns Complaint No. 67/2019 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint, lodged on October 25, 2019, seeks action against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter "NEQS") for industrial effluents. The Tribunal is constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, and possesses the requisite jurisdiction to adjudicate upon the present matter. The core of the dispute revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to adequately treat its industrial wastewater before discharge, thereby causing environmental degradation.
**Allegations**
2.  The Complainant, through its filed complaint and subsequent submissions, primarily alleged the following against the Respondent:
    *   **Discharge of Untreated/Improperly Treated Effluent:** That the Respondent was discharging industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drain without meeting the prescribed NEQS, particularly concerning parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH.
    *   **Failure to Operate/Maintain Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP):** That despite having an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), it was either non-functional, improperly maintained, or insufficient to treat the volume and characteristics of the effluent generated by the textile manufacturing processes, leading to consistent NEQS violations.
    *   **Non-compliance with Environmental Regulations:** That the Respondent had failed to comply with previous directions, notices, and warnings issued by the EPA for rectifying environmental non-compliance.
    *   **Threat to Public Health and Environment:** That the discharge of substandard effluent posed a significant threat to public health, aquatic life, and the general environment, contaminating water bodies and agricultural land downstream.
    *   **Failure to Submit Monitoring Reports:** That the Respondent had failed to regularly submit self-monitoring reports as required under the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industry Rules, 2001.
**Evidence**
3.  The Complainant presented the following evidence to substantiate its allegations:
    *   **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports dated July 10, 2019, August 28, 20
19, and October 15, 2019, detailing observations of effluent discharge, sampling procedures, and the condition of the Respondent's ETP. These reports consistently indicated non-compliance with NEQS.

parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
