**JUDGMENT**

This complaint was filed on 21 March, 2023 before this Tribunal, which exercises jurisdiction over environmental offences committed within the province of Punjab under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The matter pertains to violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) by Bestway Cement Limited, a Cement facility located at Chakwal. The Environmental Protection Order (EPO) bearing No. EPA/EPO/2023/097 was issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and the requisite compliance period has since expired without compliance being achieved.

**I. COMPLAINANT'S ALLEGATIONS**

The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's battery manufacturing facility generates lead-laden solid waste and acid effluent which are being disposed of without the mandatory hazardous waste disposal license, resulting in serious contamination of the surrounding environment and endangering the health of nearby communities.

Specifically, the Complainant's allegations are as follows:

(a) The Respondent's facility has been found discharging/emitting pollutants in excess of NEQS limits in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997;

(b) The Respondent has failed to comply with Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2023/097 issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 within the stipulated compliance period;

(c) The Respondent has not taken adequate remedial and preventive measures despite multiple EPA inspection reports documenting the violations.

**II. EVIDENCE ON RECORD**

**1. EPA Inspection Reports**
   Inspection reports dated 18/03/2023 and 12/07/2023 prepared by authorized Environmental Officers of the Punjab EPA confirming NEQS violations at the Respondent's facility.

**2. Environmental Protection Order No. EPA/EPO/2023/097**
   EPO issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 directing the Respondent to cease violations and achieve NEQS compliance within the specified period.

**3. Hazardous Waste Sample Analysis**
   Certified laboratory analysis confirming presence of hexavalent chromium (Cr VI) and total chromium (Cr) at concentrations exceeding permissible limits in waste material found at the Respondent's premises.

**4. Licence Verification Report**
   Confirmation from the Punjab EPA that the Respondent does not hold a valid licence for handling and disposal of hazardous waste as required under Section 14 of PEPA, 1997 and the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003.

**5. Photographic Evidence**
   Photographs and video footage recorded during EPA inspection confirming the observed violations.

**6. Non-Compliance Report**
   Report confirming the Respondent's failure to achieve compliance within the period stipulated in EPO No. EPA/EPO/2023/097.

**EVIDENCE OF VIOLATION**

  As detailed in the inspection reports and supporting documentation listed above.

**III. RESPONDENT'S DEFENSE**

The Respondent, through its learned counsel Mr. Asghar Ali Shah, contested the complaint on the grounds that the ETP was undergoing mandatory maintenance at the time of inspection and was fully functional both before and after the said period. It was submitted that the samples were not collected in the presence of the Respondent's representative and the chain of custody was not maintained, rendering the results inadmissible.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, Bestway Cement Limited, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, Bestway Cement Limited, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,500,000/- (Rupees Two Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 500,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order, including ensuring full operationalization of the Effluent Treatment Plant / pollution control system, and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months commencing from the date of this order, incorporating certified laboratory test results from a NEQS-certified laboratory.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply with the directions in paragraphs 4 and 5 above within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for a period of one (1) year and to report findings to this Tribunal. The EPA is authorized to seal the facility in the event of continued non-compliance.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear the costs of this proceedings including the costs of laboratory analysis and EPA inspection, assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
