**Judgment**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 55/2019, lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint, filed under the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks the enforcement of environmental standards and penalties for alleged non-compliance. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, has jurisdiction to hear and decide this matter.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were in persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. Specifically, it was brought to the Tribunal's attention that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater directly into the public drainage system, eventually contaminating surface and groundwater resources. The allegations included the failure of the Respondent to operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating their industrial discharge to meet the prescribed NEQS, despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent had not submitted regular self-monitoring reports as required, nor had they taken adequate measures to mitigate the environmental damage caused by their operations, thereby contravening the fundamental principles of environmental protection enshrined in the PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documentary and testimonial evidence. This included detailed inspection reports dated 12.03.2019, 28.05.2019, and 15.08.2019, prepared by EPA field teams, which consistently highlighted operational deficiencies in the Respondent's ETP and observed the discharge of discolored and foul-smelling effluent. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from EPA-accredited laboratories, attached to the inspection reports, indicated that samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates significantly exceeded the NEQS for several key parameters, including Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD),
Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals. These reports unequivocally demonstrated the Respondent's consistent non-compliance with the NEQS. Expert testimony from EPA officials further elucidated the detrimental environmental impact of such discharges on aquatic life, soil quality, and potential risks to human health.
The Respondent, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it possessed a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and was committed to environmental compliance. It was contended that any instances of non-compliance were isolated, temporary, and attributable to unforeseen operational glitches or maintenance activities, rather than a deliberate disregard for environmental regulations. The Respondent claimed to have invested substantial resources in upgrading its ETP and was actively working towards achieving full compliance. Furthermore, the Respondent questioned the sampling methodology and accuracy of the laboratory reports presented by the Complainant, suggesting procedural irregularities and a lack of due process in certain inspections. They requested additional time to further enhance their ETP capabilities and demonstrate compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. It noted the consistent pattern of violations documented in the EPA’s inspection and laboratory reports, spanning several months. The scientific data from accredited laboratories, indicating significant exceedances of NEQS for multiple parameters, stood as compelling and objective evidence. While acknowledging the Respondent's claims of efforts towards compliance and operational challenges, the Tribunal found these explanations insufficient to negate the documented, repeated, and substantial breaches of environmental standards. The burden was on the Respondent to demonstrate effective treatment and compliance, which they failed to do convincingly against the robust evidence presented by the EPA. The Tribunal emphasized that environmental protection is a non-negotiable obligation for industrial units, and temporary operational issues do not absolve them of their responsibility to prevent pollution. The provisions of PEPA, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 12, 16, and 17, mandate strict adherence to NEQS and empower the Tribunal to impose penalties for non-compliance. The Tribunal found no credible evidence of procedural irregularities in the EPA's sampling or analysis that would invalidate the findings.
**Findings:**
Based on the totality of the evidence and considering the arguments advanced by both sides, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been in persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards for industrial effluents, thereby contravening the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The Respondent's operations have demonstrably contributed to environmental degradation by discharging inadequately treated wastewater into the public drainage system, causing pollution and posing risks to the environment and public health.
**Order and Penalty:**
In light of the persistent and significant violations, and exercising the powers vested under Sections 16, 17, and 21 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order and penalty:
1.  **Immediate Cessation of Non-Compliant Discharge:** The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of any effluent that does not meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **ETP
Upgradation and Optimization:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, undertake all necessary measures to upgrade, optimize, and ensure the continuous and effective operation of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to consistently achieve the prescribed NEQS for all parameters. The ETP must be fully functional and capable of treating the entire volume of industrial effluent generated by the facility.
3.  **Submission of Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order, submit a comprehensive compliance plan to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), detailing the specific technical and operational measures it intends to implement to ensure consistent adherence to NEQS, along with a realistic timeline for completion of these measures.
4.  **Regular Self-Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent is directed to conduct regular self-monitoring of its effluent discharge at least twice a month through an EPA-accredited laboratory and submit these detailed analytical reports to the EPA on a monthly basis. The first such report shall be submitted within **forty-five (45) days** of this order.
5.  **Financial Penalty:** For the persistent non-compliance and environmental degradation caused, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is hereby imposed a financial penalty of **PKR 5,000,000 (Rupees Five Million only)**. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
6.  **Daily Penalty for Continued Non-Compliance:** Should the Respondent fail to achieve full compliance with the NEQS within the ninety (90) day period specified in point 2, or fail to adhere to any other directive contained herein, a further daily penalty of **PKR 50,000 (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** shall be imposed for each day of continued non-compliance, until full compliance is demonstrably achieved to the satisfaction of the EPA.
7.  **Right of Inspection:** The EPA shall retain the unfettered right to conduct unannounced inspections and collect samples from the Respondent's premises to verify compliance with this order and the PEPA, 1997.
8.  **Consequences of Failure to Comply:** Failure to strictly comply with this order, including the payment of the penalty and implementation of compliance measures, may result in further legal action, including but not limited to, the sealing of the industrial unit under Section 16 of the PEPA, 1997, and initiation of proceedings for contempt of Tribunal.
The complaint is accordingly **allowed**.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
