This complaint, bearing No. 49/2020, has been filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku Industrial Estate, Sheikhupura. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby causing significant environmental degradation and public health risks. The Tribunal's jurisdiction is invoked under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997").
The gravamen of the allegations levelled by the Complainant against the Respondent is that the latter has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing hazardous chemicals and pollutants directly into the adjacent drain, which eventually flows into the River Ravi, without adequate treatment. The EPA's investigations revealed that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if present, was either non-functional, improperly operated, or grossly inadequate to treat the industrial waste generated by its chemical processes. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that the discharged effluent exceeded the NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals such as Mercury, Lead, and Chromium. Furthermore, the complaint highlighted a failure on the part of the Respondent to comply with previous warning notices and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued by the EPA under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, requiring immediate remedial measures. The Complainant also alluded to complaints from local residents regarding foul odours and health issues attributable to the Respondent's operations.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple site inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, specifically 15.03.2020, 28.05.2020, and 10.08.2020, detailing observations of direct discharge of discoloured effluent and the dilapidated condition of the Respondent's ETP. Crucially, the EPA submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected during these inspections, duly sealed and analysed by the EPA's accredited laboratory.
These reports consistently indicated that the collected samples far exceeded the NEQS for COD, BOD, TSS, pH, and crucially, showed alarmingly high concentrations of heavy metals like Mercury, Lead, and Chromium. The Complainant further presented copies of the warning notices dated 01.04.2020 and 15.06.2020, along with the Environmental Protection
Order (EPO) dated 01.07.2020, which had been duly served upon the Respondent.
Despite repeated opportunities afforded to the Respondent to present its defence, including filing a written statement and leading evidence, the Respondent largely failed to effectively rebut the substantial evidence produced by the Complainant. While the Respondent's counsel appeared and made submissions, their arguments primarily focused on procedural aspects or claimed ongoing efforts to upgrade the ETP, without providing concrete proof of compliance or effective remediation during the period in question. No credible laboratory reports from an independent accredited lab were presented by the Respondent to counter the EPA's findings. The Respondent's assertion of financial constraints or technical difficulties, without demonstrable steps towards compliance, cannot absolve it of its statutory obligations under PEPA, 1997.
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the complaint, the evidence adduced by the Complainant, and the submissions made by both parties. It is abundantly clear from the site inspection reports, corroborated by the consistent and alarming results of the laboratory analysis reports, that the Respondent has been in flagrant violation of the NEQS for effluent discharge. The levels of COD, BOD, TSS, pH, and particularly heavy metals like Mercury, Lead, and Chromium, far exceed the permissible limits, posing severe threats to aquatic life, soil quality, and human health, especially given the discharge into the River Ravi. The failure to comply with the warning notices and the subsequent Environmental Protection Order further demonstrates a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and the authority of the EPA. The alleged complaints from local residents, though not independently verified by the Tribunal in this specific proceeding, lend further weight to the potential impact of the Respondent's non-compliance. The Respondent's arguments, while noted, fail to provide a satisfactory explanation or justification for the continuous and severe environmental pollution caused by its operations. The responsibility to operate an effective ETP and ensure compliance with NEQS rests squarely with the Respondent.
Therefore, based on the overwhelming evidence presented, this Tribunal finds the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, guilty of violating Sections 12, 13, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Effluents.
**ORDER AND PENALTY:**
Accordingly, the complaint is hereby allowed.
1.  The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the adjacent drain or any other water body. The Respondent shall ensure that all discharged effluent fully complies with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under PEPA, 1997.
2.  The Respondent is further directed to submit to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order, a comprehensive plan for the immediate upgrade, repair, or replacement of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure continuous and effective treatment of all industrial effluent. The plan shall include specific timelines for implementation

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,900,000/- (Rupees Two Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 580,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
