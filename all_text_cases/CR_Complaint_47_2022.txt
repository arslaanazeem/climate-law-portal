Presided by: Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Nishat Mills"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks the enforcement of environmental standards and the imposition of penalties for alleged violations concerning the discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions. The Tribunal is seized of the matter pursuant to its jurisdiction conferred by PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate on environmental contraventions and ensure compliance with national environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant's primary allegations against the Respondent are multifaceted and center on persistent non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under PEPA, 1997. Specifically, it is alleged that Nishat Mills has been:
a. Discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Lahore Canal/public drainage system, with parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and color consistently exceeding the permissible NEQS limits. This discharge poses a significant threat to aquatic life, soil quality, and human health in the downstream areas.
b. Failing to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), leading to the release of pollutants into the environment. Inspections revealed the ETP was either partially functional, bypassed, or operating below its designed efficiency.
c. Emitting air pollutants from its boiler and generator stacks, including but not limited to Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), Sulfur Oxides (SOx), and particulate matter, in concentrations that exceed the NEQS for industrial air emissions. These emissions contribute to air pollution and potential respiratory health issues for nearby communities.
d. Improperly managing and disposing of hazardous solid waste, including chemical sludge from the ETP, dye residues, and other industrial by-products, without adherence to prescribed safe disposal practices, thereby risking soil and groundwater contamination.
e. Failing to submit regular and accurate environmental monitoring reports to the EPA, despite repeated directives, thus hindering effective oversight and assessment of their environmental performance.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a substantial body
of documentary evidence, including multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2022, 28.05.2022, and 10.08.2022, laboratory analysis reports confirming the NEQS exceedances for effluent and air emissions, show-cause notices issued to Nishat Mills on various occasions, and correspondence detailing the Respondent's failure to adequately address the issues. Expert testimonies from EPA officials underscored the gravity and persistence of the environmental damage caused by the Respondent's operations. The Complainant emphasized that despite repeated warnings, opportunities for corrective action, and discussions, Nishat Mills had failed to demonstrate consistent and verifiable compliance with environmental regulations.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, initially denied the allegations, asserting that it possessed a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, which were regularly maintained. It was contended that any observed exceedances might be isolated incidents, attributed to operational fluctuations, or potentially due to sampling methodology discrepancies. The Respondent submitted some internal monitoring reports and maintenance logs, claiming to demonstrate efforts towards compliance. However, upon cross-examination and presentation of the EPA's comprehensive evidence, the Respondent's defense gradually shifted towards acknowledging certain operational challenges and committing to undertake further remedial measures. They requested leniency, citing their significant contribution to the national economy and employment.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. It carefully considered the inspection reports, laboratory analysis results from accredited EPA laboratories, and the consistent pattern of violations documented over several months. The EPA's evidence, being official records generated in the ordinary course of regulatory oversight, carried significant weight. The Tribunal found the Respondent's explanations regarding operational fluctuations and sampling discrepancies unconvincing, especially in light of the repeated nature and magnitude of the NEQS exceedances. The internal reports submitted by the Respondent were found to be insufficient to rebut the overwhelming evidence of non-compliance presented by the Complainant.
It is abundantly clear that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been in persistent violation of various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) thereunder. The discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent has demonstrably polluted the Lahore Canal and public drainage systems, posing severe threats to aquatic ecosystems and public health. The uncontrolled emission of air pollutants contributes directly to ambient air quality degradation. The improper management of hazardous waste further exacerbates environmental risks. Such continuous disregard for environmental laws cannot be overlooked, regardless of the Respondent's economic contributions. Environmental protection is a constitutional imperative and a fundamental right of every citizen.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby holds M/s Nishat Mills Limited liable for the persistent environmental violations as detailed in the complaint.
It is hereby ORDERED as follows:
1.  **Immediate Compliance:** M/s Nishat Mills Limited shall, with immediate effect, ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and all air pollution control devices are fully operational and maintained at all times, ensuring that all discharges and emissions strictly comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) as prescribed under PEPA, 1997.
2.  **ETP Upgrade/Optimization:** The Respondent shall submit a detailed plan for the upgrade, optimization, or replacement of its existing ETP, if necessary, to the EPA within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment. The implementation of this plan shall commence within sixty (60) days and be completed within six (6) months, under the strict supervision and approval of the EPA.
3.  **Air Pollution Control:** The Respondent shall install, upgrade, or optimize appropriate air pollution control equipment for all its stacks (boilers, generators, etc.) within three (3) months to ensure that all air emissions meet the prescribed NEQS. A compliance report, including stack monitoring results from an EPA-approved laboratory, shall be submitted monthly thereafter.
4.  **Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent shall develop and implement a comprehensive hazardous waste management plan, including proper segregation, storage, treatment, and disposal of all hazardous industrial by-products (sludge, dye residues, etc.), strictly in accordance with the provisions of PEPA, 1997, and relevant rules. This plan shall be submitted to the EPA for approval within forty-five (45) days.
5.  **Environmental Monitoring Reports:** The Respondent shall submit monthly environmental monitoring reports for both effluent and air emissions, duly verified by an EPA-approved laboratory, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for a period of two (2) years, or until continuous compliance is demonstrated to the satisfaction of the EPA.
6.  **Penalty Imposition:** For the persistent and grave violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the NEQS, a financial penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Nishat Mills Limited. This amount shall be deposited into the account of the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
7.  **Default Clause:** In the event of continued non-compliance with any of the directives contained in this order, the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is authorized to initiate further legal action, including but not limited to, sealing of the industrial unit or specific polluting sections thereof, and imposing further daily penalties as prescribed under PEPA, 1997.
8.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at PKR 500,000/- (Rupees Five Hundred Thousand only), to be paid to the Complainant within thirty (30) days.
The complaint is accordingly allowed.
