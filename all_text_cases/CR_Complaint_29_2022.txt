This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located within the
territorial jurisdiction of the EPA, Lahore. The complaint alleges that the Respondent has been operating its chemical manufacturing facility in violation of various provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (as amended) and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), primarily concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into public drains/water bodies, causing significant water pollution, and non-compliance with air emission standards. The EPA presented evidence including inspection reports, laboratory analyses of effluent and stack emissions, and photographic evidence, indicating consistent exceedances of prescribed NEQS limits and a lack of proper waste management protocols.
The Respondent, through its counsel, contested the allegations, arguing that it has invested substantially in its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, and is making continuous efforts to upgrade its environmental management systems. It was further submitted that occasional exceedances were due to operational fluctuations or challenges in meeting increasingly stringent standards, and that the Respondent is committed to achieving full compliance. The Tribunal heard arguments from both sides, examined the documentary evidence, including expert opinions and monitoring data submitted by both the EPA and the Respondent, and considered the testimonies of witnesses.
Upon careful perusal of the record and after hearing the learned counsel for the parties, this Tribunal finds that the EPA has successfully established, on a balance of probabilities, that the Respondent has indeed failed to consistently comply with the NEQS for industrial effluent discharge and certain air emission parameters, as stipulated under the Act. While acknowledging the Respondent's efforts towards environmental compliance, the persistent nature of the violations, as evidenced by multiple inspections and laboratory reports over a significant period, necessitates intervention to protect public health and the environment. The Tribunal notes that environmental degradation caused by industrial pollution has far-reaching consequences, affecting water quality, aquatic life, and the health of communities residing near the industrial facility. Therefore, strict adherence to environmental laws is paramount.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
