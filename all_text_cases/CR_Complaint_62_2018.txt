This judgment pertains to an environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing located in Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15th March 2018, seeks the enforcement of environmental laws and standards, imposition of penalties, and directions for remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate the matter and ensure compliance with environmental regulations.
The Complainant asserted that the
Complainant asserted that the Respondent unit was consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system, which eventually flowed into natural water bodies, causing severe environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. The Complainant submitted that multiple site inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 10th January 2018, 28th February 2018, and 12th March 2018, revealed significant non-compliance with the NEQS for industrial effluent. Specifically, laboratory analysis reports of samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points indicated levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and Oil & Grease far exceeding the prescribed limits. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Respondent lacked a fully functional and effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or failed to operate its existing ETP to achieve the required NEQS parameters. The Complainant also highlighted the Respondent's failure to obtain the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC) for its industrial operations under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, despite repeated reminders and show-cause notices issued by the EPA. The Complainant, therefore, sought stringent action, including the imposition of maximum penalties as per law, and directions for immediate remedial measures to ensure environmental compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its authorized representative, appeared before the Tribunal and contested the allegations. The Respondent's counsel argued that the unit had an operational ETP and was committed to environmental protection. While acknowledging some past instances of non-compliance, the Respondent contended that efforts were continuously being made to upgrade and maintain the ETP, and that any exceedances were either temporary or minor in nature. The Respondent further submitted that they were in the process of applying for the necessary environmental approvals and sought leniency, citing the significant investment already made in environmental management and the economic implications of any punitive action.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including the detailed inspection reports, photographic evidence, and comprehensive laboratory analysis reports submitted by the Complainant. The Respondent also submitted some internal monitoring reports and documentation related to ETP maintenance. After careful consideration of the arguments and the documentary evidence, the Tribunal found that the laboratory reports consistently demonstrated non-compliance with the NEQS over an extended period. The Respondent's explanations regarding temporary exceedances or ongoing upgrades were not sufficiently substantiated to negate the overwhelming evidence of persistent violations. The Tribunal noted that while the Respondent claimed to have an ETP, its effectiveness in achieving the NEQS was clearly deficient, as evidenced by the repeated failures in effluent quality. The failure to secure an Environmental Approval (NOC) as mandated by Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, was also a serious breach.
The Tribunal, therefore, concludes that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has committed grave violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards, thereby endangering the environment and public health.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following order and penalty:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby found guilty of violating Section 12 and Section 13 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Cessation of Non-Compliant Discharge:** The Respondent is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any industrial effluent that does not meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards.
3.  **Upgrade/Installation of ETP:** The Respondent shall, within a period of **six (6) months** from the date of this order

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
