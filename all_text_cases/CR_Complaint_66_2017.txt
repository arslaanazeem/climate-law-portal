**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 66/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. The complaint seeks punitive action and remediation measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, was operating its chemical manufacturing plant in a manner detrimental to the environment and public health. Specifically, the allegations included:
a) Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Lahore Branch Canal, a vital water body, thereby contaminating it and exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents.
b) Emission of hazardous air pollutants, including but not limited to sulfur dioxide (SO2) and particulate matter (PM), from its industrial processes, which were consistently found to be above the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial air emissions.
c) Failure to install and maintain an effective and fully operational Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices commensurate with the scale and nature of its operations.
d) Operating without a valid Environmental Approval (NOC) or having failed to comply with the conditions stipulated in any previously issued Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval.
e) Causing environmental degradation, nuisance, and potential health hazards to the local community residing in the vicinity of the plant.
These actions, the Complainant contended, constitute a direct contravention of the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the rules and regulations framed thereunder.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
i. **Inspection Reports:** Multiple field inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates between January 2017 and August 2017, detailing observations of dark-colored effluent discharge, visible smoke plumes, and foul odors emanating from the Respondent’s premises.
ii. **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, analyzing samples of effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points and ambient air samples collected from the plant's vicinity. These reports consistently showed concentrations of various parameters (e.g., BOD5, COD, TSS, pH, heavy metals in effluent; SO2, NOx, PM10 in air) significantly exceeding the NEQS limits. For instance, effluent BOD5 levels were reported as high as 450 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L, and air SO2 levels at 1200 µg/m³ against a NEQS limit of 600 µg/m³.
iii. **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual documentation depicting the alleged discharge of untreated effluent into the canal and visible emissions from the factory stacks.
iv. **Show Cause Notices and Correspondence:** Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent by the EPA, detailing the violations and requiring remedial action, along with the Respondent's inadequate or non-responsive replies.
v. **Witness Testimony:** Statements from EPA field officers confirming the observations and sampling procedures.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations. Their defense primarily rested on the following points:
i. **Technical Compliance:** Claim that the Respondent had an operational ETP and APC system, which was regularly maintained. They submitted internal monitoring reports, which, upon scrutiny, were found to be either incomplete, inconsistent, or not from accredited laboratories.
ii. **Sampling Methodology Dispute:** Questioned the methodology and chain of custody for the samples collected by the EPA, suggesting potential contamination or improper handling that could have skewed the results. However, no concrete evidence was provided to support this claim.
iii. **Force Majeure/Operational Issues:** Argued that any exceedances might have been due to unforeseen technical glitches or temporary operational upsets, which were promptly addressed. No specific incident reports or records were provided to substantiate this.
iv. **Commitment to Environmental Protection:** Asserted its commitment to environmental protection and social responsibility, citing investments in environmental management systems, though tangible proof of their effectiveness was lacking.
v. **Progressive Improvements:** Claimed to have initiated measures to upgrade its ETP and APC systems post-complaint, without admitting prior
non-compliance. These claims, however, were not supported by credible, verifiable data or independent third-party assessments.
**Analysis and Discussion:**
This Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant's evidence, particularly the certified laboratory analysis reports (Exhibit ii) and the consistent findings in multiple inspection reports (Exhibit i), provides a compelling and unequivocal demonstration
of the Respondent's non-compliance with environmental regulations. The Respondent's defense, while attempting to cast doubt on the Complainant's evidence or attribute non-compliance to temporary issues, largely failed to present credible counter-evidence or verifiable data. The internal monitoring reports submitted by the Respondent were not from accredited laboratories and lacked the necessary rigor to refute the EPA's certified analysis. Similarly, the claims regarding sampling methodology disputes and force majeure events were not substantiated with specific proof or incident reports, remaining mere assertions.
The consistent exceedances of NEQS for both effluent discharge and air emissions, as evidenced by multiple laboratory reports over several months, indicate a systemic failure on the part of the Respondent to adequately treat its industrial waste and control its emissions. The visual evidence further corroborates the EPA's findings. The Respondent's assertion of commitment to environmental protection and initiation of upgrades post-complaint, while commendable if true, does not absolve it of its past and ongoing violations, nor does it demonstrate present compliance. Environmental protection is a continuous responsibility, and proactive measures are required, not merely reactive ones after intervention by regulatory bodies.
The detrimental impact of discharging untreated effluent into the Lahore Branch Canal and emitting hazardous air pollutants is severe, posing significant risks to aquatic life, agricultural irrigation, public health, and the overall ecological balance. Such actions are in direct contravention of the fundamental principles enshrined in the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, which mandates the protection, conservation, rehabilitation, and improvement of the environment, and the prevention and control of pollution. The Respondent's failure to install and maintain effective treatment and control mechanisms, as required by law, demonstrates a disregard for its environmental obligations.
Therefore, this Tribunal finds the evidence presented by the Complainant to be overwhelming and conclusive, establishing beyond reasonable doubt that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been operating in persistent violation of the environmental laws and standards of Pakistan. The Respondent has failed to rebut the well-documented and scientifically supported allegations.
**FINDINGS:**
Based on the detailed analysis of the evidence and arguments presented, this Tribunal makes the following findings:
1.  The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the Lahore Branch Canal, exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, in violation of Section 12 and Section 13 of the PEPA, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industry) Rules, 2001.
2.  The Respondent has been emitting hazardous air pollutants from its industrial processes, exceeding the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial air emissions, in violation of Section 12 and Section 13 of the PEPA, 1997.
3.  The Respondent has failed to install, maintain, and operate effective and fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices commensurate with the scale and nature of its operations, as required under environmental laws and best industrial practices.
4.  The Respondent's operations have caused environmental degradation, nuisance, and potential health hazards to the local community residing in the vicinity of the plant.
**ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 22 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is hereby directed to immediately cease the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into any water body and to cease the emission of air pollutants exceeding the NEQS limits.
2.  **Installation/Upgradation of Treatment Facilities:** The Respondent shall, within a period of **six (06) months** from the date of this judgment, install, upgrade, and ensure the full operationalization of a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control (APC) devices that are capable of consistently meeting all relevant National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
3.  **Compliance Plan Submission:** Within **thirty (30) days** of this judgment, the Respondent shall submit a detailed, time-bound compliance plan to the Complainant (EPA), outlining the steps, technologies, and timelines for achieving full compliance with NEQS for both effluent and air emissions. This plan shall be subject to review and approval by the EPA.
4.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall conduct continuous self-monitoring of its effluent and air emissions, submit monthly compliance reports certified by an EPA-accredited laboratory to the Complainant, and allow unimpeded access to EPA officials for inspection and sampling.
5.  **Environmental Approval:** The Respondent shall ensure it possesses all necessary and valid environmental approvals (NOC/EIA/IEE) for its operations and shall comply with all conditions stipulated therein.
6.  **Financial Penalty:** For the past and ongoing violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the severe environmental degradation caused, a penalty of **PKR 10,000,000/- (Rupees Ten Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited. This amount shall be deposited with the Complainant (EPA) within **thirty (30) days** of the date of this judgment, to be utilized for environmental remediation and awareness programs.
7.  **Daily Penalty for Non-Compliance:** Should the Respondent fail to comply with any part of this order, particularly concerning the installation/upgradation and operationalization of treatment facilities within the stipulated six (06) months, a further penalty of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** per day shall be imposed for each day of continued non-compliance, until full compliance is achieved.
8.  **Third-Party Environmental Audit:** Upon the completion of the upgrades, and within seven (07) months of this judgment, the Respondent shall commission an independent environmental audit by an EPA-approved third-party consultant, at its own expense, to verify the effectiveness and compliance of its ETP and APC systems. The audit report shall be submitted to the EPA.
9.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.
This judgment shall take immediate effect. Any appeal against this order may be filed before the Supreme Court of Pakistan within the statutory period.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
