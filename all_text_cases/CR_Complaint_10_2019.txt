**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter "EPA" or "the Agency"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, particularly concerning the discharge of industrial effluents exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint, lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter "PEPA, 1997"), seeks appropriate legal action and imposition of penalties against the Respondent for causing environmental pollution. The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at a just and equitable decision.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemical products, was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into a public drain, which subsequently flows into larger water bodies. The specific allegations included:
1.  **Violation of National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS):** That the samples of effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points on multiple occasions demonstrated parameters (such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH value) significantly exceeding the limits stipulated in the NEQS for Municipal and Industrial Effluents.
2.  **Failure to Operate/Maintain Effluent Treatment Plant (ET
P) effectively, leading to the discharge of untreated or partially treated effluent.
3.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Management Plan (EMP) and Previous Directions:** That despite repeated warnings, show-cause notices, and directions issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take adequate measures to mitigate pollution and bring its operations into compliance with environmental standards. The EPA submitted inspection reports, laboratory analysis
reports, and copies of show-cause notices and final warnings issued to the Respondent, all of which consistently indicated non-compliance and a persistent failure to address the pollution issues. The Complainant emphasized that despite ample opportunity and regulatory intervention, the Respondent had not taken effective steps to bring its operations into compliance with the NEQS, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
