### JUDGMENT
The present complaint, bearing No. 13/2017, was lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, paper, and board products, located in Lahore. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards, specifically the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to
as "NEQS") for alleged discharges exceeding permissible limits, particularly concerning industrial effluent and air emissions from its manufacturing operations.
**Proceedings and Evidence:**
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the complaint, the detailed inspection reports submitted by the EPA, including laboratory analyses of effluent and emission samples collected from the Respondent's facility on various dates, and the notices issued to the Respondent prior to the lodging of this complaint. The Respondent was duly served and appeared through its authorized representatives and legal counsel. They filed a comprehensive reply, contesting the allegations and presenting their own data regarding environmental compliance, highlighting their existing effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air pollution control measures. Expert witnesses from both sides were examined, and extensive arguments were heard over several sittings of the Tribunal. The core of the EPA's case rested on the consistent exceedance of NEQS parameters, particularly Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) in the wastewater, and particulate matter (PM) and oxides of nitrogen (NOx) in air emissions, as evidenced by multiple monitoring reports. The Respondent, while acknowledging some historical challenges, contended that they had undertaken significant measures to upgrade their environmental infrastructure and were striving for full compliance, requesting leniency due to their substantial investments and economic contribution.
**Findings:**
Upon careful consideration of the documentary evidence, the expert testimonies, and the arguments advanced by both parties, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, on a balance of probabilities, that the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, has indeed been in violation of the provisions of PEPA, 1997, specifically Sections 11 and 12, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards. The laboratory reports consistently demonstrated that the treated effluent discharged from the Respondent's premises and their air emissions, on multiple occasions, exceeded the prescribed NEQS. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's efforts to improve their environmental management systems and invest in pollution control technologies, these measures have not, to date, consistently brought their operations into full and continuous compliance with the mandatory national standards. The environmental impact of such non-compliance, particularly on the receiving water bodies and ambient air quality, cannot be overlooked. The Respondent's responsibility to operate within the stipulated environmental parameters is absolute, irrespective of the scale of their operations or their contribution to the economy. The evidence presented clearly indicates a pattern of non-compliance that warrants corrective action and penalty.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
