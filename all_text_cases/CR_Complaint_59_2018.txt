This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 59/2018, instituted by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, a prominent chemical manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") for alleged violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with regulatory requirements. The proceedings commenced with the filing of the complaint on 05.07.2018, following a series of inspections and notices
issued to the respondent regarding their discharge of industrial effluent and air emissions. The Tribunal took cognizance of the matter, and notices were duly served upon M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, who subsequently filed their written statement denying the allegations and requesting dismissal of the complaint.
During the course of proceedings, numerous hearings were conducted, wherein both parties presented their arguments, submitted documentary evidence, and cross-examined
witnesses. The Complainant, through the EPA's technical experts and laboratory reports, presented overwhelming evidence demonstrating that the industrial effluent discharged by the Respondent consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals. Furthermore, monitoring reports indicated non-compliance with NEQS for air emissions, particularly regarding particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, emanating from the Respondent's industrial processes. The EPA also highlighted the Respondent's failure to install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate air pollution control devices despite repeated warnings and show-cause notices issued under Section 16 of the Act.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, while acknowledging the existence of an ETP, contended that the reported exceedances were either isolated incidents, attributable to unforeseen operational disruptions, or that the sampling methodologies employed by the EPA were flawed. They presented internal monitoring data and expert opinions to challenge the EPA's findings, arguing that they were committed to environmental protection and had invested significantly in upgrading their facilities. However, the Tribunal, after carefully examining the evidence, including independent expert assessments commissioned during the proceedings, found the Respondent's explanations to be insufficient and lacking corroborative proof. The consistent nature of the violations, as evidenced by multiple samples taken over an extended period by the EPA, pointed towards a systemic failure to comply with environmental standards rather than isolated incidents.
The Tribunal finds that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has indeed violated the provisions of Sections 11, 12, 13, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the Environmental Protection Agency (Review of IEE/EIA) Regulations, 2000. The Respondent's operations have caused, and continue to cause, environmental pollution in contravention of the law, posing a significant threat to public health and the environment in the vicinity.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is found guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
2.  **Pecuniary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is imposed upon M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited for the past violations. This amount shall be paid into the Environmental Protection Fund within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
