**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 29/2024 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), regarding alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint seeks remedial action, imposition of penalties, and compliance with environmental laws.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, has alleged that the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, a prominent manufacturer operating its facility at [hypothetical address, e.g., Sundar Industrial Estate, Lahore], has been consistently discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, the allegations include:
a. Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into public drains, with laboratory analysis reports indicating parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents.
b. Emission of particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) from its furnace and other industrial processes into the ambient air, beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial emissions.
c. Failure to operate and maintain an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), despite repeated verbal and written directives from the EPA.
d. Non-compliance with the terms and conditions of the No Objection Certificate (NOC) or Environmental Approval previously granted by the EPA, particularly concerning self-monitoring and reporting requirements.
e. Failure to respond adequately to show-cause notices issued by the Complainant, thereby demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations and a continuous threat to public health and the environment.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
i. **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports, dated 15.11.2023, 28.12.2023, and 10.02.2024, detailing observations of dark-colored effluent discharge, visible smoke emissions, and the apparent non-functional or under-performing status of the Respondent's ETP and APCDs.
ii. **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, corresponding to samples collected during the aforementioned inspections. These reports consistently showed:
    - Wastewater samples: COD levels ranging from 450-600 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), BOD levels from 200-300 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), TSS from 350-480 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L), and pH values outside the 6-9 range.
    - Air emission samples: Particulate matter concentrations exceeding 200 mg/Nm3 (NEQS: 150 mg/Nm3), and elevated levels of SO2 and NOx.
iii. **Show Cause Notices:** Copies of three show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 20.11.2023, 05.01.2024, and 15.02.2024, outlining the observed violations and demanding remedial action and explanation.
iv. **Correspondence:** Records of minimal and unsatisfactory responses from the Respondent, largely admitting the issues but failing to provide concrete plans or timelines for compliance, or challenging the EPA's findings without substantive evidence.
The Respondent, M/s Gh
ani Glass Limited, through its legal counsel, Mr. [Hypothetical Counsel Name], appeared before the Tribunal and presented its defense. The Respondent largely did not deny the factual existence of the discharges and emissions but contested the severity and attributed the non-compliance to various operational challenges, including:
i. **Technical Difficulties:** Claimed that the existing Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) were facing unforeseen technical malfunctions and required specialized parts and expertise for repair and upgrade, leading to temporary inefficiencies.
ii. **Economic Constraints:** Argued that the significant investment required for a complete overhaul or installation of new, state-of-the-art treatment facilities placed a substantial financial burden on the company, impacting its operational viability, especially in the current economic climate.
iii. **Ongoing Efforts:** Stated that the company was actively engaged in procuring necessary equipment and consulting with environmental engineering firms to devise a comprehensive plan for upgrading its environmental infrastructure. They requested additional time to implement these measures.
iv. **Challenging Methodology (Limited Scope):** While not outright rejecting all lab reports, counsel briefly questioned the representativeness of some samples, suggesting they might have been taken during peak load periods not reflective of average operations, though this was not supported by specific counter-evidence.
v. **Commitment to Compliance:** Expressed a strong commitment to eventually achieve full compliance with NEQS and requested the Tribunal to consider a phased approach to avoid immediate disruption to its manufacturing operations and employment.
**Analysis and Findings of the Tribunal:**
This Tribunal has carefully considered the pleadings, the evidence presented by the Complainant, and the arguments advanced by the Respondent.
The Complainant, the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, has presented a compelling body of evidence. The multiple inspection reports consistently document visible signs of environmental degradation emanating from the Respondent's facility. More crucially, the certified laboratory analysis reports provide irrefutable scientific evidence of repeated and significant exceedances of the National Environmental Quality Standards for both industrial effluent discharge and air emissions. The documented levels of COD, BOD, TSS, pH, Particulate Matter, SO2, and NOx are not merely marginal deviations but represent substantial violations that pose a clear and present danger to public health and the environment, including local aquatic life, soil quality, and ambient air quality for nearby communities.
Furthermore, the Complainant's submission of show-cause notices and subsequent correspondence clearly demonstrates that the Respondent was duly informed of the violations, provided opportunities to rectify the situation, and given avenues to present a satisfactory explanation or compliance plan. The Respondent's responses, as noted, have been largely unsatisfactory, failing to provide concrete, time-bound commitments or effective remedial actions. The repeated nature of the violations, despite warnings, indicates a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and the mandate of the EPA.
While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of technical difficulties and economic constraints, these do not absolve a commercial entity of its legal and moral obligation to comply with environmental laws. Operating an industrial facility comes with inherent responsibilities, including the investment required to mitigate its environmental footprint. The PEPA, 1997, is designed to protect the environment and public health, and economic considerations, while relevant in some contexts, cannot override the fundamental requirement for compliance with statutory environmental standards, particularly when violations are chronic and severe. The Respondent has had ample time since the initial inspections and notices to demonstrate meaningful progress, which it has failed to do. The vague assertions of "ongoing efforts" without concrete timelines or demonstrable progress are insufficient to counter the specific and scientific evidence presented by the EPA.
Based on the overwhelming evidence, the Tribunal finds that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has indeed violated Sections 11, 12, and 13 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards, and by failing to install and maintain appropriate treatment and control devices. The Respondent has also failed to comply with the terms of its environmental approval and lawful directions issued by the EPA.
**Final Order and Penalty:**
In light of the foregoing findings, and in exercise of the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 19(2) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Immediate Cessation of Violations:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited is hereby directed to immediately cease all discharges of industrial effluent and emissions of air pollutants that are in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Submission of Compliance Plan:** The Respondent shall, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment, submit a comprehensive and time-bound Environmental Compliance Plan to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore. This plan must detail:
    a.  Specific measures for the upgrade, repair, or installation of a fully functional and compliant Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of treating all industrial wastewater to meet NEQS.
    b.  Specific measures for the upgrade, repair, or installation of effective Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) capable of treating all industrial air emissions to meet NEQS.
    c.  Detailed timelines for the implementation of each measure, not exceeding **six (6) months** for full operational compliance.
    d.  Provisions for regular self-monitoring, reporting protocols, and submission of monthly compliance reports to the EPA.
3.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** Until full compliance is achieved, the Respondent shall arrange for independent, third-party laboratory testing of its effluent and emissions on a fortnightly basis and submit these reports directly to the EPA.
4.  **Penalty Imposition:** A penalty of **Rupees Five Hundred Thousand (PKR 500,000/-)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ghani Glass Limited for the violations proven before this Tribunal. This penalty shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency within **fifteen (15) days** from the date of this judgment.
5.  **Daily Fine for Continuing Violation:** In addition to the above penalty, M/s Ghani Glass Limited shall be liable to pay a further fine of **Rupees Ten Thousand (PKR 10,000/-)** for each day that the violations continue beyond the date of this judgment, until such time as the EPA certifies full compliance with NEQS, or until the approved compliance plan is demonstrably being implemented according to its stipulated timelines.
6.  **Cost of Proceedings:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, estimated at **Rupees Fifty Thousand (PKR 50,000/-)**, to be paid to the Complainant within fifteen (15) days.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
