**J U D G M E N T**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 13/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint seeks a declaration of environmental violations, imposition of penalties, and remedial directives under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997").
The Complainant initiated these proceedings following multiple inspections and monitoring activities conducted at the Respondent's industrial facility. The core of the allegations revolves around the Respondent's consistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewer system and ultimately into local water bodies, in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the specific requirements of PEPA, 1997. The EPA asserts that despite repeated warnings, show-cause notices, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent has failed to implement effective pollution control measures, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health.
The Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence to substantiate its claims. This included:
1.  **Inspection Reports (Form A & Form B):** Multiple inspection reports dated 15.03.2022, 22.05.2022, and 01.08.2022, detailing observations of the Respondent's effluent discharge points, the operational status of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), and the general environmental management practices at the site. These reports consistently noted an absence of a fully functional ETP capable of treating the volume and nature of industrial waste generated.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15.03.2022, 22.05.2022, and 01.08.2022. These reports consistently indicated that the discharged effluent exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to:
    *   Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD5): Averaging 320 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L.
    *   Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD): Averaging 750 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L.
    *   Total Suspended Solids (TSS): Averaging 280 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L.
    *   pH: Ranging from 4.5 to 5.2, significantly outside the NEQS range of 6.5-9.0.
    *   Colour: Highly visible and persistent, indicating inadequate removal of dyes.
3.  **Show-Cause Notices and Correspondence:** Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 05.04.2022 and 01.06.2022, outlining the violations and demanding corrective actions, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies or lack thereof.
4.  **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)/Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) Compliance Status:** Records indicating that while the Respondent had obtained approval for its EIA/IEE, it had failed to comply with the stipulated conditions, particularly regarding the operation and maintenance of an effective ETP.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations, asserting that it has an operational ETP and is committed to environmental compliance. The Respondent presented the following in its defense:
1.  **Photographs:** A set of photographs purportedly showing an ETP facility at their premises.
2.  **Internal Monitoring Reports:** Some internal laboratory reports claiming compliance, though these were not from an accredited third-party laboratory and lacked consistent data for all parameters.
3.  **Commitment Letter:** A letter stating their intention to upgrade the ETP and achieve full compliance within a reasonable timeframe.
However, the Respondent failed to provide any independent, verifiable evidence, such as third-party accredited laboratory reports, operational logs of their ETP, or proof of significant investment in ETP upgrades that would contradict the EPA's consistent findings of non-compliance. Their defense largely rested on
a general denial and a promise of future compliance, without providing any credible, independently verifiable data or concrete evidence to counter the detailed and consistent findings presented by the Complainant. The photographs presented were inconclusive as to the operational efficacy and capacity of the ETP, and the internal monitoring reports, lacking accreditation and comprehensive parameter analysis, could not stand against the certified laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited
laboratory.
Having carefully considered the documentary evidence presented by the Complainant, including the detailed inspection reports and the consistent findings of the certified laboratory analyses, and having evaluated the Respondent's defense, this Tribunal finds the Complainant's case to be overwhelmingly substantiated. The EPA's reports consistently demonstrate that the Respondent's industrial effluent parameters far exceed the NEQS, indicating a clear failure to adequately treat its wastewater before discharge. The Respondent's defense, consisting primarily of general denials, inconclusive photographs, and unaccredited internal reports, lacks the credibility and specificity required to refute the robust evidence provided by the EPA. The commitment letter, while acknowledging an intention to comply, does not absolve the Respondent of past and ongoing violations.
The consistent exceedances of critical parameters such as BOD5, COD, TSS, and the highly acidic pH levels, coupled with visible colour, confirm a persistent and severe violation of the NEQS, which are legally binding standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997. The Respondent's failure to operate and maintain a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of meeting these standards, despite repeated warnings and opportunities, constitutes a serious contravention of its environmental obligations and the conditions of its EIA/IEE approval. Such negligence not only pollutes public waterways but also poses significant risks to aquatic life, agricultural lands, and human health.
This Tribunal, therefore, concludes that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is in clear and persistent violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial and Municipal Effluents (SRO 549(I)/2000). The evidence unequivocally establishes the Respondent's culpability for causing environmental pollution through the discharge of untreated industrial effluent.
**ORDER:**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** It is hereby declared that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has continuously and persistently violated the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewer system and local water bodies.
2.  **Imposition of Penalty:** For the proven environmental contraventions, a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, to be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Remedial Directives:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is directed to:
    a.  **Immediate Cessation:** Immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewer system or any water body.
    b.  **ETP Upgrade/Installation:** Within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, ensure the full functional upgrade, rehabilitation, or installation of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) that is demonstrably capable of consistently treating all industrial effluent generated at its facility to meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
    c.  **Compliance Monitoring and Reporting:**
        i.  Submit a detailed plan for the ETP upgrade/installation, including timelines and technical specifications, to the EPA, Punjab, within fifteen (15) days of this judgment.
        ii.  Commence monthly submission of certified laboratory analysis reports, from an EPA-accredited third-party laboratory, confirming compliance with all NEQS parameters, starting from the date of this judgment and continuing for a period of one (1) year after the ETP upgrade/installation is completed and verified. The first such report shall be submitted within thirty (30) days.
        iii.  Allow EPA officials unrestricted access to the facility for inspections and sample collection to verify compliance.
    d.  **Environmental Restoration:** Undertake, in consultation with the EPA, any necessary measures for the restoration of the affected environment, if deemed feasible and necessary by the EPA.
4.  **Continuing Contravention Penalty:** In the event of non-compliance with any of the remedial directives outlined in paragraph 3 above, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited shall be liable to an additional penalty of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand Only)** for each day the contravention continues, until full compliance is achieved and verified by the EPA.
5.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order may result in further legal action, including but not limited to, the closure of the industrial unit under Section 16 of the PEPA, 1997, and other enforcement measures as deemed appropriate by this Tribunal.
6.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
