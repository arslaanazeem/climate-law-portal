**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 53/2021, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated at Kala Shah Kaku Industrial Estate, Sheikhupura. The complaint, lodged on October 15, 2021, seeks redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and safety. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), has jurisdiction to hear and decide the matter.
The Complainant, through its representative, presented a series of allegations asserting that the Respondent, a prominent chemical manufacturing facility, has consistently failed to comply with environmental standards. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted on July 14, 2021, and September 02, 2021, the EPA team observed the Respondent discharging untreated industrial effluent directly into a nearby drain, which eventually merges with the River Ravi. Samples of the effluent were collected and subsequently analyzed at the EPA's accredited laboratory. The laboratory reports, dated August 05, 2021, and September 20, 2021, revealed that critical parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and concentrations of heavy metals (Chromium and Lead) significantly exceeded the NEQS for industrial effluent discharge. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's air emissions from its boiler stack also exceeded the prescribed NEQS for particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, as evidenced by monitoring reports from September 02, 2021. The Complainant further stated that despite multiple warning notices issued on August 10, 2021, and September 25, 2021, directing the Respondent to install and operationalize an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and upgrade its air pollution control devices, the
Respondent failed to take substantive corrective measures, thus necessitating the filing of the present complaint. The Complainant sought appropriate penalties and directives for the Respondent to comply with environmental laws.
The Respondent, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it is a responsible corporate entity committed to environmental stewardship. While acknowledging certain operational challenges, the Respondent contended that it had initiated steps to upgrade its environmental
management systems and was in the process of implementing significant upgrades to its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices. It was argued that the procurement and installation of advanced environmental control technologies require a considerable timeframe, and the Respondent had already initiated the process, including engaging consultants and placing orders for new equipment. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
