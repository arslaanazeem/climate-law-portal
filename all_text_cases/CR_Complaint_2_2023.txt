This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Sheikhupura, Punjab. The complaint invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various contraventions of environmental laws and regulations. The Tribunal is seized with the responsibility to adjudicate upon the allegations of environmental pollution and non-compliance, ensuring the protection of the environment and public health in accordance with the law.
The Complainant lodged the complaint on February 15, 2023, alleging that the Respondent was operating its chemical manufacturing facility in blatant disregard of environmental standards and conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval. Specifically, the allegations included:
a.  **Discharge of Untreated/Partially Treated Effluent**: The Respondent was consistently discharging industrial effluent directly into the open drains and eventually into the River Ravi, without adequate treatment, causing severe water pollution. The effluent parameters, particularly Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (mercury, chromium), were repeatedly found to be in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents.
b.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste**: The Respondent was allegedly disposing of hazardous solid and liquid waste, including chemical sludge and spent catalysts, in an uncontrolled manner within the factory premises and adjacent open land, posing a significant risk of soil and groundwater contamination.
c.  **Failure to Maintain Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP)**: Despite having an ETP installed, the Complainant contended that it was either non-functional, improperly operated, or inadequately designed to treat the volume and nature of the industrial waste generated by the Respondent.
d.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Approval Conditions**: The Respondent had allegedly failed to adhere to several conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE approval no. EPA/Punjab/2010/1234 dated March 10, 2010), particularly those related to regular monitoring, submission of compliance reports, and proper waste management.
e.  **Repeated Violations and Non-Responsiveness**: The Complainant submitted that despite numerous warning notices, show-cause notices, and opportunities for rectification issued by the EPA since 2021, the Respondent had failed to take effective corrective measures, demonstrating a persistent pattern of non-compliance.
The Complainant presented the following evidence in support of its allegations:
a.  **Inspection Reports**: Copies of inspection reports dated July 12, 2022, September 28, 2022, and January 18, 2023, detailing on-site observations of effluent discharge, sludge disposal, and ETP operational status. These reports consistently noted visible signs of pollution and non-compliance.
b.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports**: Official laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming that samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates (e.g., July 12, 2022, and January 18, 2023) showed BOD levels exceeding 80 mg/L (against NEQS of 80 mg/L for discharge into sewerage), COD levels exceeding 400 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), TSS exceeding 250 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and detectable levels of mercury and chromium far above permissible limits.
c.  **Phot
ographic and Video Evidence**: Visual records taken during inspections, showing untreated effluent flowing into open drains, accumulated hazardous waste, and the ETP appearing to be non-operational or poorly maintained.
d.  **Warning and Show-Cause Notices**: Copies of official correspondence issued to the Respondent, including warning notices dated August 10, 2021, and November 5, 20
22, and a final show-cause notice dated January 25, 2023, all of which elicited either no response or unsatisfactory replies from the Respondent.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,800,000/- (Rupees Two Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 560,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
