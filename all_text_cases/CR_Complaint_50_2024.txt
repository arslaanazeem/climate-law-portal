**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 50/2024, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), located at Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The matter falls squarely within the jurisdiction of this Tribunal as established under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997, and its powers under Section 24 thereof.
The Complainant, through its duly authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of various chemical products, has been discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, thereby exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, the allegations include:
a. Continuous discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into nearby drains/water bodies, resulting in severe water pollution.
b. Failure to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) in accordance with the design specifications and environmental best practices.
c. Non-compliance with the conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) issued by the EPA.
d. Repeated violations despite prior warnings, notices, and opportunities to rectify the deficiencies, as evidenced by multiple inspections and laboratory analyses conducted by the EPA.
e. Causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and biodiversity in the surrounding areas.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documentary and oral evidence. This included inspection reports dated 15.01.2024, 05.02.2024, and 20.02.2024, conducted by EPA officials, which consistently noted the unsatisfactory operation of the Respondent's ETP and observed the discharge of discolored and foul-smelling effluent. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on samples collected on 15.01.2024 and 20.02.2024, demonstrated significant exceedances of NEQS for parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. The Complainant also submitted copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 25.01.2024 and 28.02.2024, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, which failed to provide concrete evidence of corrective measures or sustained compliance. The Complainant's witnesses, including the Assistant Director and a laboratory analyst, testified regarding the inspection findings and the accuracy of the lab reports.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel and representatives, contested the allegations. They argued that their ETP was operational and that all reasonable efforts were being made to comply with environmental regulations. They presented some internal maintenance records for the ETP and claimed that occasional fluctuations in effluent quality were unavoidable due to the complex nature of their chemical processes and
the inherent challenges in maintaining absolute consistency in effluent quality. They further contended that the EPA had not provided sufficient time for them to implement comprehensive upgrades to their ETP, which they claimed were underway. The Respondent's representatives also questioned the methodology of some of the EPA's inspections and sample collections, suggesting that they might not fully represent the overall operational efficiency of their plant. However, beyond general assertions and a few internal log entries, the Respondent failed to produce concrete, independently verifiable
evidence of sustained compliance with NEQS or effective measures taken to rectify the persistent deficiencies in their ETP's operation. Their assertions regarding unavoidable fluctuations and ongoing upgrades remained largely unsubstantiated by external audits, third-party verification, or demonstrable improvement in effluent quality subsequent to the EPA's notices. The Tribunal notes that while industrial processes can be complex, the obligation to comply with environmental standards is absolute and continuous, not merely aspirational or subject to indefinite grace periods for upgrades. The Respondent's failure to present compelling counter-evidence or to effectively challenge the scientific validity of the EPA's laboratory reports significantly weakens their defense.
Having meticulously reviewed the pleadings, documentary evidence, and oral testimonies, this Tribunal finds the Complainant's case to be well-founded and supported by credible and consistent evidence. The repeated inspection reports, corroborated by scientific laboratory analyses showing significant exceedances of NEQS for critical parameters, conclusively establish that the Respondent has been discharging industrial effluent into the environment without adequate treatment, in clear violation of Section 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards. The Tribunal is satisfied that the Respondent has failed to operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant effectively, leading to continuous environmental pollution and posing a demonstrable risk to public health and the ecosystem. The prior warnings and show-cause notices issued by the EPA, coupled with the Respondent's inadequate responses, further underscore a pattern of non-compliance and a lack of diligent effort to meet environmental obligations.
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby issues the following final order and penalty:

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
