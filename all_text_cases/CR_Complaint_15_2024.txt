This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated in Lahore, alleging violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint, bearing No. 15/2024, was lodged before this Tribunal seeking enforcement of environmental laws, imposition of penalties, and directions for compliance against the Respondent for its alleged role in causing environmental pollution through the discharge of industrial effluent. This Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, possesses the requisite jurisdiction to adjudicate upon such matters and enforce environmental protection measures.
The Complainant's case, as articulated in the complaint and subsequent submissions, primarily rested on the assertion that the Respondent, an industrial entity engaged in textile manufacturing, has consistently failed to treat its industrial effluent to the prescribed NEQS before discharging it into the environment. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15th January 2024, 5th February 2024, and 1st March 2024, samples of industrial effluent discharged from the Respondent's premises were collected. Subsequent laboratory analyses of these samples revealed a consistent exceedance of the NEQS for several critical parameters, including but not limited to, Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH value. The Complainant further contended that despite the issuance of multiple show-cause notices dated 20th January 2024 and 10th February 2024, directing the Respondent to rectify the non-compliance and upgrade its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, thereby demonstrating a disregard for environmental regulations and perpetuating environmental degradation. The Complainant sought a declaration that the Respondent is in violation of PEPA, 1997, and prayed for the imposition of maximum penalties as stipulated under the law, along with a mandatory order for the Respondent to bring its operations into full compliance with the NEQS.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of documentary and oral evidence. This included certified copies of the inspection reports prepared by the EPA field teams on the aforementioned dates, clearly documenting the sampling procedures and initial observations. Crucially, the Complainant submitted detailed laboratory analysis reports from the EPA-accredited laboratory, which provided scientific data conclusively demonstrating the exceedance of NEQS for the effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points. These reports, signed by qualified chemists
and duly attested, formed the cornerstone of the Complainant's evidentiary presentation. Furthermore, the Complainant produced sworn affidavits from EPA officials involved in the inspections, corroborating the procedural regularity of sample collection and the accuracy of the inspection reports. These officials were also available for cross-examination, reinforcing the credibility of the Complainant's assertions.
In response, the Respondent, M/s
Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel and authorized representatives, vehemently denied the allegations of consistent non-compliance. While acknowledging the presence of an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) at its facility, the Respondent contended that it was fully functional and designed to meet the NEQS. They argued that any instances of exceedance, if proven, were isolated occurrences attributable to unforeseen operational fluctuations, power outages

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
