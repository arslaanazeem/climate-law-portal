JUDGMENT
The present environmental complaint, bearing No. 59/2016, has been filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), invoking the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint seeks redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of industrial effluent failing to meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing located in Lahore, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the environment, causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 22.03.2016 and 15.06.2016, samples of the industrial effluent being discharged from the Respondent's premises were collected. These samples, upon laboratory analysis by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently showed parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels far exceeding the limits prescribed by the NEQS. It was further contended that despite repeated communications, issuance of show-cause notices dated 05.04.2016 and 25.07.2016, and opportunities afforded to the Respondent to rectify the deficiencies in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and bring their discharge within the NEQS, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures. The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's actions constituted a clear contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge
or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) or the standards established by the Agency under this Act.
Despite being served with notices and provided ample opportunity to respond and rectify the alleged violations, the Respondent failed to effectively rebut the evidence presented by the Complainant or demonstrate compliance with the NEQS. The Tribunal meticulously examined the inspection reports, laboratory analysis results, and the show-cause notices issued by the EPA. The evidence on record unequivocally establishes that the effluent discharged from the Respondent's industrial unit consistently exceeded the prescribed limits for BOD, COD, TSS, and pH, thereby causing environmental pollution and posing a risk to public health, in clear violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997.
This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its onus of proving the alleged environmental violations against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited. The Respondent's persistent failure to comply with environmental regulations, despite repeated warnings and opportunities for rectification, demonstrates a dereliction of their environmental responsibility.
**ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 read with Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  The Respondent, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, is found guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  The Respondent is hereby directed to pay a penalty of PKR 500,000/- (Rupees Five Hundred Thousand only) for the violations committed. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Government of Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  The Respondent is further directed to immediately take all necessary measures to upgrade and ensure the effective functioning of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to bring its industrial effluent discharge within the prescribed NEQS. The Respondent shall submit a comprehensive compliance report, duly verified by an independent environmental consultant approved by the EPA, within ninety (90) days, demonstrating full compliance with the NEQS. Failure to comply with this directive shall result in further legal action, including potential daily penalties and closure orders as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
4.  The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is authorized to conduct follow-up inspections to ensure strict compliance with this order and to initiate further proceedings in case of non-compliance.
5.  This judgment shall be effective immediately.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
