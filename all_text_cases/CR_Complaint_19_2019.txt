**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 19/2019, lodged by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore. The complaint seeks the intervention of this Tribunal to address alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS") concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent into the environment. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized with the jurisdiction to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and contraventions of environmental laws and standards.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, was consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the prescribed NEQS into public sewers and ultimately into natural water bodies without adequate treatment. Specifically, the complaint detailed multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15.03.2019, 28.05.2019, and 12.08.2019. During these inspections, effluent samples were collected from the Respondent's discharge points. Subsequent laboratory analyses of these samples revealed significant deviations from the NEQS, particularly concerning parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. For instance, the analysis reports indicated BOD levels ranging from 350 mg/L to 500 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L, COD levels from 900 mg/L to 1200 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L, and TSS levels from 250 mg/L to 400 mg/L against a limit of 200 mg/L. The Complainant further contended that the Respondent either lacked a functional and effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or failed to operate its existing ETP to the required standards. Despite repeated verbal warnings, written notices, and a formal show-cause notice issued by the EPA on 05.09.2019, the Respondent allegedly failed to take corrective measures, thereby continuing to pollute the environment and posing a serious threat to public
health and environmental ecosystems.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its authorized representative and legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and submitted a detailed written reply. The Respondent vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance and asserted that it operates a fully functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) designed to meet the NEQS. They contended that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was
flawed and did not accurately represent the quality of their treated effluent. They argued that external factors, such as issues with the municipal sewerage system or potential contamination during transport of samples, might have skewed the results. The Respondent presented operational logs and maintenance records for their ETP, asserting that it was regularly maintained and operated by qualified personnel. They also submitted a report from an independent environmental consultant, which, while acknowledging some historical challenges, indicated that recent upgrades had been implemented to improve effluent quality. The Respondent requested the Tribunal to order fresh, joint sampling and analysis to ascertain the true state of their effluent discharge. They further emphasized their significant investment in environmental infrastructure and their commitment to sustainable practices, asserting that any exceedances were inadvertent and not a result of deliberate non-compliance.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant, the EPA, submitted comprehensive inspection reports, laboratory analysis certificates from accredited laboratories, and copies of the show-cause notice and subsequent correspondence. The consistency of the analytical results across multiple dates (15.03.2019, 28.05.2019, 12.08.2019) showing significant exceedances of NEQS for BOD, COD, and TSS, strongly supports the EPA's contention of persistent non-compliance. The EPA's sampling methodology, as described in their reports, appeared to follow standard protocols.
While the Respondent presented arguments regarding flawed sampling methodology and external factors, they failed to provide concrete evidence to substantiate these claims effectively. The independent consultant's report, while mentioning recent upgrades, did not definitively refute the EPA's findings from the specific dates in question. Furthermore, the operational logs and maintenance records, while indicating routine upkeep, did not negate the chemical analysis results which consistently demonstrated effluent quality far below the prescribed standards. The argument of "inadvertent" exceedances is difficult to reconcile with the magnitude and persistence of the violations reported over several months.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
