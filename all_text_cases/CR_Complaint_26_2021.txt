This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a manufacturing entity engaged in the production of glass products, situated at Industrial Estate, Raiwind Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of various provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal has heard arguments from both sides, reviewed the submitted evidence, and considered the legal framework governing environmental protection in Pakistan.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent has been operating its industrial facility in flagrant disregard of environmental laws and standards. Specifically, the allegations centered around:
1.  **Air Pollution:** The Respondent's glass melting furnaces were alleged to be operating without adequate air pollution control devices, resulting in the emission of particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulfur dioxide (SO2) significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. Repeated observations indicated dense plumes of smoke emanating from the factory chimneys, impacting air quality in the surrounding residential and agricultural areas.
2.  **Water Pollution:** The Respondent was accused of discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater directly into the public drainage system, which eventually merges into local water bodies. Analysis of wastewater samples revealed elevated levels of Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and heavy metals (such as lead and chromium), far exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluents. This discharge posed a severe threat to aquatic life, soil quality, and public health.
3.  **Hazardous Waste Management:** The Respondent reportedly failed to implement proper management protocols for hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing process, including contaminated cullet, refractory waste, and sludge from its rudimentary wastewater treatment plant. There was no evidence of safe storage, treatment, or disposal practices for these materials, leading to potential soil and groundwater contamination.
4.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Approval Conditions:** The Respondent was alleged to be operating in violation of the conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval (NOC) issued by the EPA, particularly concerning the installation and continuous operation of pollution control technologies and regular monitoring.
5.  **Repeated Violations and Non-Responsiveness:** The EPA further alleged that despite numerous inspection visits, issuance of show-cause notices dated 10.02.2021 and 05.04.2021, and warning letters, the Respondent consistently failed to take effective remedial measures or submit a satisfactory compliance plan, demonstrating a persistent pattern of non-compliance.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA technical teams on 15.03.2021, 28.05.2021, and 12.07.2021, documenting observed violations, including visual evidence of excessive smoke emissions and discharge of colored effluent.
2.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for both gaseous emissions and industrial effluents, based on samples collected during the aforementioned inspections. These reports clearly indicated PM levels up to 750 mg/Nm3 (against NEQS of 200 mg/Nm3) and COD levels up to 600 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L).
3.  **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual records depicting dense smoke plumes from chimneys and direct discharge of effluent into open drains, corroborating the observations made in the inspection reports.
4.  **Copies of Notices and Correspondence:** Records of show-cause notices, warning letters, and subsequent reminders issued to the Respondent, along with the Respondent's
response, which consistently lacked concrete action plans or verifiable commitments.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 4,200,000/- (Rupees Four Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 840,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
