This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant") against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial enterprise operating within the jurisdiction of Lahore. The complaint, lodged on 15th March 2022, invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant contended that the Respondent's industrial operations, specifically relating to its paper and packaging manufacturing units, were causing significant environmental pollution. The primary allegations included the discharge of industrial effluent into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the River Ravi without adequate treatment, resulting in parameters exceeding the NEQS for industrial and municipal effluents. Furthermore, the complaint detailed excessive emissions of air pollutants, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx), from the Respondent’s boiler stacks, which were also reportedly beyond the prescribed NEQS. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated notices, warnings, and an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent had failed to implement effective pollution control measures or consistently comply with the environmental standards, thereby posing a risk to public health and the environment.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 10th October 2021, 28th December 2021, and 20th February 2022, detailing observations of dark-colored effluent discharge and visible smoke emissions. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory. The effluent samples collected on these dates consistently showed levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values significantly exceeding the NEQS. For instance, the report dated 28th December 2021 indicated BOD levels of 350 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L) and COD levels of 700 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L). Similarly, air quality monitoring reports from the same periods revealed particulate matter (PM10) concentrations of 650 µg/m³ (against a NEQS limit of 500 µg/m³) and SO2 concentrations of 1500 µg/m³ (against a NEQS limit of 600 µg/m³) from the Respondent's main stack. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
