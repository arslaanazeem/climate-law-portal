This Tribunal was seized with a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), situated in Sheikhupura, on 15th May 2023. The complaint invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging grave violations of environmental laws and standards, thereby causing pollution detrimental to public health and the environment.
The gravamen of the complaint was that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in chemical manufacturing, was consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the open drains, which eventually merged into local water bodies, including the River Ravi. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that multiple inspections conducted by EPA officials revealed that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was either non-functional or operating inefficiently, leading to the discharge of effluent with parameters far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under PEPA, 1997. The allegations detailed exceedances in Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH levels, and the presence of heavy metals such as Chromium and Lead, all in violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration, or level that contravenes the NEQS. Furthermore, the complaint highlighted the Respondent's consistent failure to heed repeated warnings, show-cause notices, and directives issued by the EPA to rectify the situation, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental compliance.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports dated 1
st October 2022, 15th December 2022, and 20th March 2023, comprehensively detailing the non-compliance and the specific parameters exceeding NEQS. These reports were corroborated by independent laboratory analysis certificates from accredited institutions, unequivocally demonstrating the presence of pollutants in concentrations far above permissible limits. Furthermore, the Complainant submitted copies of numerous
show-cause notices, environmental protection orders, and final directives issued to the Respondent, all of which unequivocally demanded immediate remedial action and compliance with NEQS. These documents clearly established a pattern of persistent non-compliance and a blatant disregard for environmental regulations, despite ample opportunity provided to the Respondent to rectify its deficiencies.
Upon service of the complaint, the Respondent was duly summoned to appear before this Tribunal and file its written statement. Despite multiple opportunities afforded, the Respondent failed to submit a comprehensive and satisfactory reply to the grave allegations leveled against it. While representatives of M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited did appear on certain dates, their submissions primarily consisted of vague assurances of future compliance and general denials, unsupported by any credible evidence or technical reports that could rebut the specific findings of the EPA. No independent laboratory reports were presented by the Respondent to counter the Complainant's data, nor was any evidence adduced to demonstrate the efficient and compliant operation of their ETP during the periods in question. The Respondent's failure to adequately defend itself against the detailed and well-substantiated allegations further strengthened the Complainant's case.
After carefully considering the complaint, the voluminous documentary evidence presented by the Complainant, and the arguments advanced by both parties, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof. The evidence unequivocally establishes that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been consistently discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards, in clear violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The repeated failures to comply with directives and rectify the operational deficiencies of its Effluent Treatment Plant demonstrate a deliberate and persistent pattern of environmental non-compliance, causing demonstrable harm to the local environment and posing a significant threat to public health, particularly to the aquatic ecosystem of the River Ravi and communities reliant on these water bodies.
The Tribunal notes with serious concern the Respondent's protracted non-compliance, which has persisted despite numerous warnings and enforcement actions by the Environmental Protection Agency. Such conduct not only undermines the objectives of PEPA, 1997, but also sets a dangerous precedent for other industrial units. Environmental protection is a fundamental right, and industries bear a statutory and moral obligation to operate in an environmentally sound manner. The Respondent's actions are a clear breach of this obligation.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
