This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a corporate entity engaged in manufacturing paper, board, and packaging materials. The complaint, lodged under Section 17 read with Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks the imposition of penalties and remedial orders for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal's jurisdiction is invoked to adjudicate upon matters concerning environmental pollution and non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
The Complainant's case rests on the following allegations:
1.  That the Respondent's industrial facility located in Lahore was inspected by EPA officials on multiple occasions, specifically on 15.03.2017, 22.06.2017, and 10.10.2017.
2.  During these inspections, it was observed that the Respondent was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain, which eventually flows into the River Ravi.
3.  Samples of the industrial effluent were collected during each inspection and sent to the EPA's accredited laboratory for analysis. The laboratory reports, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/17-03/214, EPA/LAB/17-06/501, and EPA/LAB/17-10/892, consistently indicated that several parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeded the permissible limits prescribed by the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents.
4.  Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's air emissions from its boiler stacks, particularly particulate matter (PM), were also found to be in excess of the NEQS during the inspection on 10.10.2017, as evidenced by report no. EPA/LAB/17-10/893.
5.  Despite repeated issuance of show-cause notices (e.g., No. AD(L)/EPA/2017/1123 dated 25.03.2017, No. AD(L)/EPA/2017/1876 dated 05.07.2017, No. AD(L)/EPA/2017/3015 dated 20.10.2017) and opportunities for compliance and representation, the Respondent failed to take effective measures to control the pollution to acceptable standards.
6.  The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's continued non-compliance constitutes a contravention of Sections 11, 12, and 13 of PEPA, 1997, causing environmental degradation and public health hazards.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
