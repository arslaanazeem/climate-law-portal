This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging contraventions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The complaint seeks the imposition of penalties and directions for compliance with environmental laws.
The Complainant, through its authorized officer, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial entity engaged in manufacturing various paper and packaging products, has been operating its facility located at Shahrah-e-Roomi, Lahore, without adequate pollution control measures, leading to significant environmental degradation. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's industrial effluent discharged into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into the River Ravi, consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Furthermore, the Complainant contended that the Respondent's stack emissions from its boiler operations also failed to meet the NEQS for particulate matter and oxides of nitrogen (NOx). The Complainant asserted that despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA following multiple inspections and sample analyses, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial actions to bring its operations into compliance. The complaint particularized that a final show-cause notice dated 15.01.2020 was issued, to which the Respondent's reply was found unsatisfactory, necessitating the filing of the present complaint under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997, read with Section 20 thereof.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included inspection reports dated 12.08.2019, 23.09.2019, and 05.11.2019, detailing observations of untreated effluent discharge and visible smoke emissions. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA Provincial Environmental Laboratory, Lahore, pertaining to effluent samples collected on 12.08.2019 and 05.11.2019, which indicated COD levels of 450 mg/L and 480 mg/L respectively (against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L), BOD levels of 220 mg/L and 240 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 80 mg/L), and TSS levels of 350 mg/L and 380 mg/L (against a NEQS limit of 200 mg/L). Similarly, stack emission monitoring reports dated 23.09.2019 showed particulate matter concentrations of 1200 mg/Nm³ (against a NEQS limit of 600
mg/Nm³). The Complainant also presented copies of the show-cause notices, the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, and other official correspondence, all demonstrating a consistent pattern of non-compliance and the Respondent's failure to adequately address the environmental violations despite ample opportunity.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that
it is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship. Their counsel contended that the Respondent has already invested substantial resources in establishing an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) devices. They argued that any reported exceedances were either isolated incidents, attributable to operational fluctuations, or that the sampling and analysis methodologies employed by the EPA were not entirely reflective of their overall compliance efforts. Furthermore, the Respondent submitted that they are actively engaged in a continuous improvement process, exploring advanced technologies to further enhance their environmental performance and are in the process of upgrading their existing ETP and APC systems. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
