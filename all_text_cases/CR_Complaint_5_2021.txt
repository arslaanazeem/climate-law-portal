**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**

**Presided by: Justice (R) Syed Iftikhar Hussain Shah**
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, under Section 16 read with Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), seeks redress against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The EPA, as the primary regulatory body for environmental protection in the province, has brought this action to ensure compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the provisions of the Act, safeguarding public health and the environment from industrial pollution.
The Complainant's allegations against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited primarily revolve around the discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the permissible limits set by the NEQS and operating without requisite environmental approvals. Specifically, it is alleged that the Respondent has been operating its chemical manufacturing unit at Sundar Industrial Estate, Lahore, without a valid Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval, as mandated by Section 12 of the Act and the Punjab Environmental Protection (Environmental Impact Assessment) Regulations, 2000. Furthermore, the EPA contends that inspections conducted on various dates, including 10.03.2021 and 25.04.2021, revealed that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if present, was
either non-functional, improperly maintained, or insufficient to treat the industrial effluent to the prescribed NEQS. Laboratory analyses of effluent samples collected during these inspections consistently showed parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH values exceeding the established NEQS, posing a significant threat to the receiving water bodies and the surrounding environment. Despite repeated notices and opportunities for compliance, including a show-cause notice dated 05.05.2021, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures or provide satisfactory explanations for its persistent non-compliance.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, was duly served with notice of this complaint and afforded ample opportunity to present its defense. Initially, representatives of the Respondent appeared and sought adjournments, citing various reasons. However, despite these opportunities, the Respondent failed to submit a comprehensive written reply to the allegations, nor did it produce any credible evidence to controvert the findings of the EPA's inspection reports and laboratory analyses. During subsequent hearings, the Respondent's representation became sporadic, and ultimately, it failed to appear or adequately defend itself against the serious charges leveled by the Complainant. The Tribunal, after granting several adjournments in the interest of justice, proceeded with the matter ex-parte against the Respondent on 15.09.2021, relying on the evidence presented by the Complainant.
Having carefully considered the complaint, the documentary evidence placed on record by the Complainant, including inspection reports, effluent analysis reports, show-cause notices, and photographic evidence, and in the absence of any rebuttal from the Respondent, this Tribunal finds the allegations of the Complainant to be substantiated. The consistent failure of the Respondent to operate with requisite environmental approvals, particularly an approved EIA/IEE, and its documented discharge of industrial effluent in violation of the NEQS, constitute clear breaches of Section 12 and Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, respectively. The Respondent's disregard for environmental regulations not only undermines the objectives of the Act but also poses an imminent threat to public health and ecological integrity.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Compliance with Environmental Approvals:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is directed to immediately cease all operations that require an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval under Section 12 of the Act, until such time as the requisite approval has been obtained from the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
