This judgment addresses Complaint No. 45/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The complaint seeks redress against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in an industrial area of Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), for alleged violations of environmental laws and standards, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and excessive air emissions, thereby causing environmental pollution and public nuisance.
The Complainant asserted that the Respondent's industrial operations were in persistent contravention of the provisions of PEPA, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, it was alleged that following numerous public complaints regarding foul odour and contamination of local water bodies, the EPA conducted multiple inspections of the Respondent's premises. These inspections, carried out on 15th May 2019, 2nd July 2019, and 18th August 2019, revealed that the Respondent was discharging industrial effluent directly into a municipal drain without adequate treatment
, and that laboratory analyses of samples collected on these dates consistently showed pollutant levels significantly exceeding the NEQS for various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Furthermore, monitoring of air emissions indicated higher than permissible levels of particulate matter and other noxious gases, contributing to the persistent foul odour reported by residents.
Despite repeated notices issued by the EPA on 25th May 2019, 10th July 2019, and 20th August 2019, directing the Respondent to install and operationalize an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) and to cease all non-compliant discharges and emissions, the Respondent failed to demonstrate satisfactory compliance. The Respondent, in its written replies dated 5th June 2019 and 15th July 2019, acknowledged the issues but cited operational challenges and financial constraints, promising future compliance without providing a concrete action plan or a definitive
timeline for achieving compliance. The Tribunal notes that while the Respondent acknowledged the environmental issues, their responses lacked the requisite urgency and commitment to rectify the severe environmental damage being caused.
The Tribunal, having meticulously reviewed the complaint, the evidence presented by the Complainant, including inspection reports, laboratory analyses, and copies of notices issued, as well as the Respondent's written replies, finds that the Complainant has successfully established a prima facie case of persistent environmental pollution by the Respondent. The documented evidence overwhelmingly demonstrates that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited has been operating in flagrant disregard of the National Environmental Quality Standards and the mandatory provisions of PEPA, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 12, and 13, which prohibit the discharge of effluent or emissions in excess of NEQS without proper treatment and require the installation of appropriate pollution control equipment. The repeated failures to comply with EPA directives, despite multiple opportunities, underscore a clear pattern of non-compliance.
The environmental degradation caused by the discharge of untreated industrial effluent into municipal drains and the emission of excessive air pollutants poses a significant threat to public health and the local ecosystem. The reported foul odour and contamination of water bodies are direct consequences of the Respondent's actions, infringing upon the fundamental right of citizens to a clean and healthy environment.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Non-Compliant Activities:** M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is immediately directed to cease the discharge of any untreated industrial effluent and to halt emissions exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) from its premises.
2.  **Installation/Upgradation of Pollution Control Systems:** The Respondent is hereby mandated to install and operationalize a fully functional and NEQS-compliant Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) within a strict period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment. The design and operational parameters of these systems shall be submitted to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, for approval within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment, and regular progress reports shall be furnished to the EPA.
3.  **Monetary Penalty:** For the persistent violations of environmental laws and standards, M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited is hereby imposed a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)**. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.
4.  **Daily Fine for Non-Compliance:** Should the Respondent fail to comply with the directions contained in clauses 1 and 2 of this order within the stipulated timeframes, a further daily fine of **PKR 50,000/- (Rupees Fifty Thousand only)** shall be levied for each day of continued non-compliance until full compliance is achieved.
5.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** The Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, shall conduct fortnightly inspections of the Respondent's premises to monitor compliance with this order and shall submit compliance reports to this Tribunal every month until full compliance is certified.
6.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
