This judgment addresses Complaint No. 17/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of various chemicals. The complaint was lodged under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging contraventions of environmental laws and standards. This Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized with the jurisdiction to adjudicate upon complaints pertaining to environmental pollution and impose penalties as prescribed by law.
The Complainant's allegations center on the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge and air emissions. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted on March 15, 2020, and April 22, 2020, the Respondent's industrial unit was found to be discharging untreated or inadequately treated wastewater directly into the nearby water body, and emitting gaseous pollutants from its stacks without proper abatement, thereby exceeding the permissible limits set forth in the NEQS. The Complainant further asserted that the Respondent had failed to maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) in operational condition, or that the existing systems were insufficient to meet the NEQS. A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on May 05, 2020, to which the reply was deemed unsatisfactory, leading to the filing of the present complaint seeking remedial action and imposition of penalties.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant submitted detailed inspection reports dated March 15, 2020, and April 22, 2020, prepared by the field inspection teams of the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). These reports included observations of the Respondent's premises, photographs depicting the alleged discharge points and emissions, and most critically, laboratory analysis reports of samples collected from the Respondent's effluent discharge and stack emissions. The laboratory reports, issued by the EPA's accredited laboratory, explicitly indicated that parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH in the wastewater samples, and Sulfur Dioxide (SO2) and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) in the air emission samples, significantly exceeded the NEQS. The Complainant also produced copies of the show-cause notice and the Respondent's reply, which, according to the Complainant, largely admitted to operational challenges but failed to demonstrate immediate compliance or a concrete plan for achieving it within a reasonable timeframe.
The Respondent, represented by its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of willful non-compliance. While acknowledging the findings of the EPA's inspection reports and laboratory analyses, the Respondent contended that the reported exceedances were temporary and attributable to unforeseen operational disruptions and maintenance activities that were underway during the inspection periods. The Respondent submitted documentary evidence, including purchase orders for upgrade components for its ETP and APCDs, and internal maintenance logs, to demonstrate its commitment to environmental compliance. It was further argued that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is a responsible corporate entity with a long history of operations, and has invested heavily in environmental infrastructure. The Respondent assured the Tribunal of its readiness to undertake all necessary measures to bring its operations into full compliance with the NEQS and requested a lenient view, emphasizing the economic impact of punitive measures on its operations and workforce, especially in the prevailing economic climate.
The legal framework governing this complaint is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, is central to the allegations, prohibiting the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration, or level which is in excess of the NEQS. Section 12 mandates the submission of an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for new projects and significant expansions, and
Section 16 empowers the EPA to grant environmental approvals, subject to such conditions as it may deem fit. Crucially, Section 17 of PEPA, 1997, outlines the powers of the Agency to issue Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and initiate legal proceedings against persons contravening the provisions of the Act or the NEQS. Sub-section (2) of
Section 17 specifies the penalties for contravention of an EPO, while Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, provides for general penalties for contravention of any provision of the Act or rules or regulations made thereunder, including the NEQS. This section empowers the Tribunal to impose a fine which may extend to one million rupees, and in case of a continuing contravention, an additional fine which may extend to one hundred thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues. Furthermore, Section 20 of the Act grants this Tribunal the power to issue directions, including those for the cessation of pollution, restoration of the environment, and payment of compensation.
Having heard the learned counsel for both parties and meticulously examined the documentary evidence placed on record, this Tribunal finds the

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
