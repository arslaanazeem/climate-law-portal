This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") concerning alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The complaint, bearing No. 51/2024, was lodged on February 15, 2024, following a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted by the EPA at the Respondent's textile manufacturing facility located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The Tribunal has diligently heard the arguments presented by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary and material evidence placed on record to arrive at a just and lawful determination of the matter.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were in persistent violation of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Industrial Effluent**: The Respondent was accused of regularly discharging industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system and ultimately into local water bodies without adequate treatment, resulting in pollutant concentrations significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (Chromium, Lead).
2.  **Air Emissions Exceeding NEQS**: It was further alleged that the Respondent's boiler stacks and other process vents were emitting air pollutants, primarily Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), and Particulate Matter (PM), in concentrations substantially higher than the NEQS, contributing to ambient air quality degradation.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste**: The Complainant asserted that the Respondent failed to properly segregate, store, and dispose of hazardous waste generated from its dyeing, printing, and finishing processes, including chemical sludge, spent dyes, and contaminated packaging, in accordance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and other relevant waste management regulations.
4.  **Failure to Install/Maintain Pollution Control Devices**: Despite prior warnings and notices, the Respondent allegedly failed to install a fully functional and adequately sized Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and appropriate Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), or to maintain existing ones in an operational state to meet the NEQS.
5.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Approvals**: The Respondent had allegedly failed to comply with the conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval (if any) or, alternatively, had failed to obtain the requisite environmental approvals for significant expansions or operational changes.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated October 20, 2023, December 05, 2023, and January 25, 2024, detailing observations of effluent discharge points, smoke emissions, and waste storage practices. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from an EPA-accredited laboratory, confirming the exceedance of NEQS for effluent samples collected on November 10, 2023, and January 15, 2024, showing BOD levels of
250 mg/L (against NEQS of 80 mg/L), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) levels of 600 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), Total Suspended Solids (TSS) of 350 mg/L (against NEQS of 200 mg/L), and detectable levels of Chromium and Lead significantly above permissible limits. /Nm3 (against NEQS of 150 mg/Nm3). The Complainant also furnished photographic and videographic evidence depicting visible emissions, improper waste storage, and direct discharge points. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 3,000,000/- (Rupees Three Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 600,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
