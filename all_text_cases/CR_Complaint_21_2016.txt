**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 21/2016 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of glass products, located in Lahore. The Complainant seeks the Tribunal's intervention under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging serious environmental violations by the Respondent, causing pollution and potential harm to public health and the environment. The Tribunal, having jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, proceeded to hear the matter after due issuance of notices to both parties.
The Complainant's allegations against M/s Ghani Glass Limited are multi-faceted. Firstly, it is contended that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly the glass melting furnaces and associated processes, are emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulfur dioxide (SO2), in concentrations far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) established under PEPA, 1997. Secondly, the Complainant alleges that the Respondent is discharging industrial wastewater, containing elevated levels of parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and exhibiting abnormal pH levels, directly into the municipal sewerage system or nearby water bodies without adequate treatment, thereby violating NEQS for effluent discharge. Thirdly, it is claimed that the Respondent failed to obtain the requisite Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval for its operations, or if obtained, has not adhered to the conditions stipulated therein. Lastly, the Complainant asserts that despite repeated inspections, issuance of show-cause notices, and directives for compliance, the Respondent has demonstrated a persistent pattern of non-compliance, failing to install or adequately operate pollution control devices, submit self-monitoring reports, or implement effective environmental management measures.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a robust body of evidence. This included detailed inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on various dates, specifically highlighting observations of visible emissions from chimneys and direct discharge of untreated effluent. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports (e.g
., those dated [Date 1] and [Date 2]), which conclusively demonstrated that both ambient air quality and industrial effluent samples collected from the Respondent's premises contained pollutants significantly above the permissible NEQS limits. Photographs and video evidence depicting visible smoke plumes and discolored wastewater discharges were also submitted. Furthermore, copies of numerous show-cause notices, environmental protection orders (EPOs), and warning letters issued to the Respondent since [Year] were placed on record, indicating a history of non-compliance and the Respondent's failure to adequately respond to regulatory directives. Expert testimonies from environmental engineers corroborated the technical findings and highlighted the potential adverse impacts on human health and local ecosystems.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting its commitment to environmental stewardship. Through its legal counsel and presented witnesses, the Respondent contended that it had invested substantial resources in upgrading its pollution control infrastructure. It was argued that the emissions and discharges, when they occurred, were either within acceptable limits or were temporary deviations due to operational upsets, which were promptly rectified. The Respondent claimed to have installed state-of-the-art bag filters and scrubbers for air emissions and a primary wastewater treatment plant for its effluent. It further submitted that it was in the process of obtaining or updating its EIA/IEE approval and had submitted relevant documentation to the EPA. The Respondent also questioned the methodology and calibration of the EPA's sampling equipment and the chain of custody for the samples, suggesting that the laboratory results might not be entirely accurate or representative. It presented its own internal monitoring reports, which, it claimed, showed compliance with most NEQS parameters, and argued that any discrepancies were minor or attributable to external factors beyond its control.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties, including the inspection reports, laboratory analysis reports, photographic evidence, and the Respondent's explanations and counter-arguments. After careful consideration of the documentary evidence and testimonies, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully discharged its burden of proof. The consistent and repeated violations documented through multiple inspections and independent laboratory analyses spanning several years clearly demonstrate a persistent pattern of non-compliance by the Respondent. The Respondent's claims of substantial investment in pollution control were not adequately substantiated with verifiable operational data or independent third-party audit reports demonstrating consistent compliance. Furthermore, the internal monitoring reports submitted by the Respondent were found to be inconsistent with the EPA's findings and lacked independent verification. The Tribunal rejects the Respondent's technical challenges to the EPA's sampling and analysis methodology, finding the EPA's procedures to be standard and reliable. The failure to obtain or adhere to EIA/IEE conditions, coupled with the documented exceedances of NEQS for both air emissions and effluent discharge, constitutes a clear violation of Sections 11, 12, 13, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) Rules. The persistent nature of the violations, despite repeated notices and directives, underscores the gravity of the Respondent's environmental negligence and its potential adverse impact on public health and the environment.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby declares M/s Ghani Glass Limited, the Respondent, guilty of violating Sections 11, 12, 13, and 16 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) Rules.
Accordingly, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent shall immediately cease all activities causing the emission of air pollutants and discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent exceeding NEQS limits.
2.  **Installation and Upgrade of Pollution Control Systems:** The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, install, upgrade, and fully operationalize appropriate and effective air pollution control devices (including but not limited to advanced scrubbers and particulate matter control systems) and a comprehensive wastewater treatment plant capable of consistently treating all industrial effluent to meet the prescribed NEQS parameters before discharge.
3.  **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Compliance:** The Respondent shall, within **sixty (60) days**, submit a comprehensive Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) report for its entire operations to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for approval, or if an approval exists, demonstrate strict adherence to all conditions stipulated therein.
4.  **Self-Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall establish a robust self-monitoring mechanism, including continuous emission monitoring systems (CEMS) for key air pollutants and regular effluent quality monitoring, and shall submit monthly compliance reports, duly verified by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, to the Complainant for a period of two years.
5.  **Environmental Management Plan:** The Respondent shall develop and implement an Environmental Management Plan (EMP) within **forty-five (45) days**, detailing measures for waste management, energy efficiency, and emergency response, to be submitted to the EPA for review.
6.  **Monetary Penalty:** Pursuant to Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ghani Glass Limited for the persistent and grave environmental violations. This amount shall be deposited with the Registrar of this Tribunal within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 500,000/- (Rupees Five Hundred Thousand only)**, to be paid to the Complainant within **thirty (30) days**.
8.  **Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Failure to comply with any part of this order within the stipulated timelines shall result in further punitive action, including but not limited to enhanced penalties, closure of operations, and initiation of criminal proceedings as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant (EPA) is directed to monitor the Respondent's compliance with this order and submit quarterly progress reports to this Tribunal for the next two years.
