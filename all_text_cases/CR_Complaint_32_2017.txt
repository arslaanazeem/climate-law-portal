This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited, a textile manufacturing unit located in an industrial area of Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules and regulations. The Tribunal was constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate matters relating to environmental pollution and provide expeditious remedies. The Complainant, as the regulatory authority, brought this action in furtherance of its mandate to protect, conserve, rehabilitate, and improve the environment.
The Complainant alleged that M/s Sapphire Fibres Limited was operating its textile dyeing and finishing unit without adequately treating its industrial effluent, thereby discharging hazardous waste into the public sewerage system and ultimately into the river Ravi, in blatant disregard of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Discharge of Untreated Effluent**: The Respondent was found to be discharging industrial wastewater with parameters (Biological Oxygen Demand - BOD, Chemical Oxygen Demand - COD, Total Suspended Solids - TSS, pH, and colour) far exceeding the prescribed NEQS, as revealed by multiple laboratory analyses.
2.  **Failure to Install/Operate an Effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP)**: Despite previous warnings and notices, the Respondent had either failed to install a functional ETP or the installed ETP was not operating efficiently to meet the NEQS.
3.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Management Plan (EMP)**: The Respondent had failed to adhere to the environmental management protocols and mitigation measures outlined in its previously approved Initial Environmental Examination (IEE)/Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or, in the absence thereof, general best environmental practices.
4.  **Failure to Respond to Notices**: The Complainant issued a Show Cause Notice on 15.03.2017, followed by a Final Notice on 10.04.2017, requiring the
Respondent to explain its position and take corrective measures. However, the Respondent failed to provide any satisfactory response or demonstrate compliance within the stipulated timeframes, leading to the filing of the instant complaint before this Tribunal.**
**Proceedings before the Tribunal:**
Upon filing of the complaint, notices were issued to the Respondent. Despite being duly served and afforded multiple opportunities to appear and present its defense, the Respondent either
failed to appear or appeared through counsel but failed to submit a cogent defense or satisfactory explanation regarding the serious allegations leveled against it. The Complainant, through its authorized representative, presented documentary evidence including copies of the Show Cause Notice, Final Notice, multiple laboratory analysis reports detailing the exceedance of NEQS parameters, and photographic evidence of the alleged discharge. These documents were duly submitted and placed on record. Despite opportunities granted to the Respondent to cross-examine the Complainant's witnesses or present its own evidence, the Respondent largely remained unresponsive or failed to produce any credible evidence to rebut

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
