**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 29/2018**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ghani Glass Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Ali Baqar Najafi**
**Date of Decision: December 15, 2023**
This judgment addresses a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturer of glass products, located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint was initiated following repeated inspections and monitoring activities conducted by the EPA, which allegedly revealed persistent violations of environmental standards. The Tribunal exercises its jurisdiction under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), to adjudicate matters pertaining to environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental laws and regulations. The core issue before this Tribunal is to determine whether the Respondent has contravened the provisions of PEPA, 1997, and its allied rules, and if so, to impose appropriate penalties and remedial measures.
The Complainant's allegations, as detailed in the complaint and supporting documents, primarily concern the Respondent's failure to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluent discharge. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's glass manufacturing facility was emitting particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen oxides (NOx) into the ambient air from its furnace stacks at levels significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS. Furthermore, the complaint asserted that the industrial effluent discharged from the Respondent's plant into the public sewerage system, and ultimately into nearby water bodies, contained parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) far in excess of the permissible limits. The EPA contended that despite multiple warning notices, show-cause notices, and opportunities for rectification, the Respondent had failed to install or adequately operate requisite pollution control devices, particularly an effective Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) or equivalent system for air emissions and a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) for wastewater. The cumulative effect of these alleged violations, according to the Complainant, was significant environmental degradation, posing a threat to public health and the local ecosystem.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated 15.03.2017, 22.06.2017, and 05.10.2017, prepared by EPA technical teams. These reports included detailed observations, photographic evidence, and laboratory analysis results of air emissions samples collected from the Respondent's main furnace stacks and effluent samples taken from their discharge points. The laboratory reports, issued by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently indicated exceedances of NEQS for particulate matter (PM), SO2, and NOx in air emissions, and for COD, BOD, and TSS in the wastewater. For instance, the report of 05.10.2017 showed PM levels at 450 mg/Nm3 against a NEQS limit of 300 mg/Nm3, and COD levels at 350 mg/L against a NEQS limit of 150 mg/L. The Complainant also submitted copies of show-cause notice No. EPA/LHR/CC/102-17/456 dated 10.11.2017, issued to the Respondent, and their unsatisfactory reply thereto, further demonstrating the Respondent's alleged non-compliance. On its part, the Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. They argued that their facility operated with state-of-the-art technology and had installed advanced pollution control systems. They presented internal monitoring reports and certificates from a private environmental consultant firm, purporting to show compliance with NEQS. They contended that the EPA's sampling methodology might have been flawed or that the samples were not representative of their routine operations. They also claimed that they were in the process of upgrading their ETP and ESP systems, demonstrating their commitment to environmental protection. However, no concrete evidence of completed upgrades or consistent compliance over the period in question was provided.
The legal framework governing this case is primarily the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Specifically, Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the NEQS. Section 12 mandates that no industrial unit shall commence operation without obtaining an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) approval or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) approval, depending on the scale and nature of the project. While the Respondent had initial approvals, the complaint pertains to their ongoing operational non-compliance. Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, empowers the Tribunal to impose penalties, including fines and directions for remedial measures, for violations of the Act. The National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluents, notified under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, serve as the benchmark against which the Respondent's performance is to be measured.
Upon careful consideration of the pleadings, the documentary evidence presented by both parties, and the arguments advanced by their respective counsels, this Tribunal finds
that the Complainant has successfully established, through a preponderance of credible evidence, that the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, has indeed been in persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluent discharge, as mandated by Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997). The series of inspection reports, supported by detailed laboratory analysis results from the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently demonstrate exceedances of permissible limits for particulate matter, SO2, and NOx in air emissions, and for COD, BOD, and TSS in wastewater.
The Respondent's defense, primarily relying on internal monitoring reports and certificates from a private consultant, failed to provide concrete, independently verifiable evidence to effectively rebut the compelling findings of the EPA. Their claims of being in the process of upgrading systems, while indicative of a potential future commitment, do not absolve them of the established non-compliance during the period in question. The Tribunal notes the absence of any satisfactory explanation or proof of immediate, effective measures taken by the Respondent to rectify the violations despite repeated warnings and show-cause notices issued by the EPA. This persistent non-compliance poses a significant risk to public health and the environment, necessitating a firm and decisive intervention by this Tribunal.
Therefore, after careful deliberation and in light of the findings, this Tribunal hereby issues the following final order and penalty:
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** The Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, is hereby found guilty of contravening Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by failing to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and liquid effluent discharge.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** A fine of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million Only)** is imposed on the Respondent for the persistent violations established in this judgment. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
3.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent is directed to immediately undertake and complete the following remedial measures:
    *   **Air Pollution Control:** Install, upgrade, and fully operationalize an effective Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) or equivalent air pollution control system for its furnace stacks to ensure that all gaseous emissions, including particulate matter, SO2, and NOx, consistently meet the prescribed NEQS.
    *   **Wastewater Treatment:** Install, upgrade, and fully operationalize a robust Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all industrial effluent discharged from its facility consistently meets the prescribed NEQS for parameters such as COD, BOD, and TSS.
4.  **Timeline for Compliance:** The Respondent shall complete the installation/upgrade and operationalization of the aforementioned pollution control devices (ESP and ETP) within a strict period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order.
5.  **Monitoring and Reporting:** Upon completion of the remedial measures, the Respondent shall:
    *   Submit a detailed compliance report, duly certified by an independent environmental consultant approved by the EPA, within one hundred (100) days from the date of this order, confirming the operational status and effectiveness of the upgraded systems.
    *   Conduct monthly third-party monitoring of its air emissions and effluent discharge, using an EPA-accredited laboratory, for a period of six (6) months thereafter, and submit these detailed monitoring reports to the EPA within the first week of each subsequent month.
6.  **Continuing Contravention Penalty:** In the event of failure to comply with the directives in paragraphs 3 and 4 within the stipulated ninety (90) days, the Respondent shall be liable to an additional daily fine of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand Only)** for each day of continued non-compliance until full compliance is achieved, in addition to any other penalties as provided under PEPA, 1997, including potential closure of the industrial unit.
7.  **Costs:** The Respondent shall bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 200,000/- (Rupees Two Hundred Thousand Only)**, to be paid to the Complainant (EPA) within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.
The EPA is directed to closely monitor the Respondent's compliance with this order and to report any further instances of non-compliance to this Tribunal for appropriate action.
