**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging contraventions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The Tribunal has meticulously examined the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties to arrive at this decision concerning the environmental compliance of the Respondent's industrial operations.
The Complainant initiated these proceedings on 10th May 2021, asserting that the Respondent's industrial unit, engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, was operating in a manner detrimental to the environment and in clear violation of established environmental laws. Specifically, the allegations included the discharge of industrial effluent containing pollutants in concentrations exceeding the prescribed NEQS, and the emission of air pollutants, particularly particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, also beyond permissible limits. The EPA contended that despite repeated notices and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent had failed to install, upgrade, or effectively operate appropriate pollution control measures, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a risk to public health and the ecosystem in the vicinity of its operations. The Complainant further highlighted that routine inspections conducted by EPA officials on 15th March 2021 and 22nd April 2021 revealed consistent non-compliance, necessitating the filing of this complaint for enforcement and imposition of penalties as per PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant submitted a series of documents and presented witness testimony. Key among the evidence were the inspection reports dated 15th March 2021 and 22nd April 2021, prepared by EPA field teams, which meticulously documented observations
...observations, including photographic evidence, and detailed reports of effluent and air emission samples collected from the Respondent's premises. Laboratory analysis reports, appended to these inspection documents, unequivocally indicated that the discharged industrial effluent consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to, Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. Similarly, the ambient air quality monitoring revealed concentrations of particulate matter (PM10/PM2.
.5) and sulfur dioxide (SO2) significantly above the permissible limits set by NEQS. The Complainant also emphasized the Respondent's failure to obtain a valid Environmental Approval (NOC/EIA) for its operations or to renew it, as required under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997 and its associated regulations, further compounding the violations.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
