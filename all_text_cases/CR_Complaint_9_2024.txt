**J U D G M E N T**
The instant complaint, bearing No. 9/2024, has been filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, acting on behalf of the Provincial Agency established under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing unit operating in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and standards, along with the imposition of penalties for alleged violations. This Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, is seized of the matter to adjudicate upon the environmental transgressions and ensure compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other relevant environmental regulations.
The Complainant has primarily alleged that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been consistently discharging industrial effluent and emitting air pollutants from its manufacturing facility in contravention of the NEQS, thereby causing environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and the ecosystem. Specifically, the allegations include:
a.   Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into the municipal sewerage system/open drains, exceeding the permissible limits for various parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, as prescribed by the NEQS.
b.   Emission of air pollutants, including Particulate Matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), from its boiler stacks and other process emissions, exceeding the NEQS standards for industrial emissions.
c.   Failure to install and/or adequately operate appropriate effluent treatment plants (ETP) and air pollution control devices (APCDs) despite repeated warnings and notices from the EPA.
d.   Non-compliance with Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued by the EPA under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, requiring the Respondent to adopt remedial measures and submit compliance reports.
e.   Creating a public nuisance and contributing to localized environmental pollution, impacting the quality of water bodies and ambient air in the vicinity of its operations.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence, which included:
i.   Multiple inspection reports prepared by EPA officials, detailing site visits conducted on various dates between October 2023 and March 2024, which consistently highlighted operational deficiencies in the Respondent's environmental management systems.
ii.  Laboratory analysis reports from the certified EPA laboratory, dated November 15, 2023, January 20, 2024, and March 10, 2024, confirming that samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points consistently exceeded NEQS for BOD, COD, and TSS. For instance, the report dated January 20, 2024, showed BOD levels at 120 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), COD at 350 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), and TSS at 250 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L).
iii. Air quality monitoring reports, also from the EPA laboratory, dated December 10, 2023, and February 28, 2024, indicating that stack emissions from the Respondent's boiler units exceeded NEQS for PM (e.g., 600 mg/Nm³ against NEQS of 400 mg/Nm³) and SO2.
iv. Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on November 01, 2023, and February 05, 2024, clearly outlining the violations and demanding immediate corrective actions.
v. Copies of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997,
...requiring the Respondent to adopt remedial measures and submit compliance reports, which the Respondent failed to fully adhere to.
vi. Correspondence between the EPA and the Respondent, including reminders and final notices, indicating the Respondent's awareness of the violations and the legal consequences of non-compliance.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,600,000/- (Rupees One Million Six Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 320,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
