This judgment addresses Complaint No. 50/2023 filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited, a prominent manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged on October 10, 2023, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 26 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), seeking redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning air emissions and discharge of industrial effluent. The Tribunal is seized with the responsibility to determine whether the Respondent has contravened the provisions of the PEPA, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), and if so, to impose appropriate penalties and remedial measures.

**BACKGROUND AND ALLEGATIONS:**
The complaint lodged by the Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, detailed multiple instances of alleged environmental non-compliance
, primarily stemming from routine monitoring and inspection reports conducted by EPA officials between June 2023 and September 2023. These reports indicated that M/s Packages Limited was allegedly exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both industrial effluent discharge and gaseous emissions from its manufacturing processes. Specifically, the allegations included:
    (i) Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system, with samples collected on July 15, 2023, and August 28, 2023, showing levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) significantly above the NEQS limits.
    (ii) Emission of particulate matter (PM), Sulfur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) from its industrial stacks exceeding the NEQS, as evidenced by air quality monitoring reports dated June 20, 2023, and September 5, 2023.
    (iii) Failure to install and/or properly operate necessary pollution control equipment, particularly an effective effluent treatment plant and air scrubbers/filters, despite previous warnings and notices

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
