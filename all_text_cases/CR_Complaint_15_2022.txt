This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 15/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The Complainant invoked the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 19 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging violations of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent's industrial facility located at Kot Lakhpat, Lahore. The Tribunal is constituted under Section 20 of the PEPA, 1997, and is empowered to hear and decide complaints regarding environmental pollution.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial facility, engaged in manufacturing activities, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/drain, which eventually merges into the Lahore Canal, without adhering to the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). Specifically, it was alleged that multiple inspections conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) officials revealed consistent exceedances of NEQS parameters for Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH in the effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points. Furthermore, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's air emission sources, particularly from its boiler stacks, were emitting pollutants beyond the permissible NEQS limits, contributing to ambient air quality degradation. A show-cause notice was issued to the Respondent on 10.08.2022, followed by a final warning, neither of which, according to the Complainant, resulted in satisfactory compliance or effective remedial measures, thereby necessitating the filing of this complaint for enforcement and imposition of penalties.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports dated 05.07.2022, 20.08.2022, and 15.09.2022, prepared by EPA officials, detailing the observations of non-compliance. These reports were accompanied by analytical laboratory reports from the Punjab Environmental Quality Laboratory (PEQL), an accredited facility, confirming the exceedances of NEQS for COD (ranging from 300 mg/L to 450 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), BOD (ranging from 180 mg/L to 280 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L), and TSS (ranging from 250 mg/L to 350 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L) in the wastewater samples. Additionally, PEQL reports for air emissions indicated particulate matter (PM) levels exceeding the NEQS from specific stacks. The Complainant also produced photographs and video evidence depicting the nature of the discharge and the visible emissions. Witness statements from Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director EPA, and Ms. Sara Ali,
Environmental Analyst, were also recorded, corroborating the inspection findings and laboratory results.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting that it operates a state-of-the-art industrial facility with a functional Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and measures in place to control air emissions. The Respondent contended that the samples collected by the Com
plainant were not representative, or that the sampling methodology employed did not adhere to established protocols, thus rendering the analytical results unreliable. It was further argued that their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was fully operational and regularly maintained, and that any alleged exceedances were isolated incidents, possibly due to temporary operational fluctuations, which were promptly rectified. The Respondent presented its own internal monitoring reports, duly verified by an independent third-party environmental consultant, demonstrating compliance with NEQS for various parameters over a sustained period, particularly prior to and subsequent to the dates of the Complainant's inspections. Regarding air emissions, the Respondent asserted that its boilers were equipped with appropriate air pollution control devices and operated within prescribed limits, and that any visible emissions were within permissible opacity standards. The Respondent also highlighted its significant investments in environmental management systems and corporate social responsibility initiatives, arguing against any deliberate intention to pollute. They requested the Tribunal to dismiss the complaint, or in the alternative, to allow them a reasonable period to address any minor discrepancies.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. The Complainant's evidence, comprising multiple inspection reports, certified laboratory analyses from the Punjab Environmental Quality Laboratory (PEQL), photographic and video evidence, and corroborated witness statements, established a prima facie case of consistent non-compliance with NEQS for both effluent discharge and air emissions. The PEQL reports, being from an accredited government laboratory, carry significant evidentiary weight. The repeated nature of the exceedances across different inspection dates undermines the Respondent's argument of "isolated incidents" or "temporary operational fluctuations." While the Respondent presented internal monitoring reports, these were not independently verified by a regulatory authority during the relevant periods of alleged violations, nor did they sufficiently rebut the specific findings of the EPA's inspections and subsequent laboratory analyses. The burden of proof, initially on the Complainant, was discharged through the compelling evidence presented. Subsequently, the burden shifted to the Respondent to demonstrate compliance, which it failed to do effectively in the face of the Complainant's robust evidence.
The Tribunal finds that the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, has violated Sections 11 and 12 of the PEPA, 1997, by discharging industrial effluent containing pollutants in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards into the public sewerage system/drain, and by emitting air pollutants from its industrial facility in excess of the prescribed limits, thereby contributing to environmental degradation. The persistent nature of these violations, despite the issuance of a show-cause notice and a final warning, indicates a failure to implement effective remedial measures in a timely manner.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Liability:** M/s Packages Limited is found liable for violating Sections 11 and 12 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards.
2.  **Cessation of Non-Compliance:** The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, is forthwith directed to ensure strict and immediate compliance with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both industrial effluent discharge and air emissions from its facility located at Kot Lakhpat, Lahore.
3.  **Submission of Compliance Action Plan:** The Respondent shall, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order, submit a detailed and time-bound Compliance Action Plan to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, outlining the specific measures, upgrades, and investments it intends to undertake to achieve and maintain full compliance with NEQS. This plan shall include, but not be limited to, detailed proposals for the upgrade and optimization of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and air pollution control devices, along with a schedule for their implementation.
4.  **Regular Monitoring and Reporting:** The Respondent shall implement a robust self-monitoring program, including regular sampling and analysis of its effluent and air emissions by an EPA-approved third-party laboratory, and submit quarterly compliance reports to the Environmental Protection Agency for a period of two (2) years.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,700,000/- (Rupees One Million Seven Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 340,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **15 June, 2023**.
