**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 42/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Kala Shah Kaku, Sheikhupura. The complaint was initiated under Section 21 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), read with other enabling provisions, seeking appropriate legal action and penalties for alleged violations of environmental laws and National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, having jurisdiction over environmental matters in the province of Punjab, duly admitted the complaint and proceeded with the trial.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, was engaged in the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into a public drain, which subsequently merges with the River Ravi, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to human health and aquatic life. Specifically, it was alleged that during routine inspections conducted by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) officials on various dates, particularly on 15.03.2018 and 28.03.2018, it was observed that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) was either non-functional, bypassed, or operating inefficiently. Samples of the discharged effluent were collected from the outfall point of the Respondent's facility. Subsequent laboratory analyses of these samples revealed that several critical parameters, including but not limited to Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and concentrations of heavy metals such as Chromium and Lead, were found to be significantly exceeding the permissible limits prescribed under the NEQS for industrial effluents. The Complainant further contended that despite repeated notices and warnings issued to the Respondent, including Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) dated 05.04.2018 and 20.04.2018, requiring immediate compliance with environmental standards and proper operation of their ETP, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations. This non-compliance, it was asserted, constituted a violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and warranted the imposition of penalties as stipulated under Section 17 of the Act.
To substantiate the allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive set of evidence. This included:
1.  **Inspection Reports:** Detailed reports prepared by EPA field teams documenting the site visits on 15.03.2018 and 28.03.2018, noting the operational status of the ETP and the discharge points.
2.  **Sampling Protocols and Chain of Custody Forms:** Documents confirming the proper collection of effluent samples in accordance with established environmental sampling procedures.
3.  **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified reports from the EPA Environmental Laboratory, Lahore, bearing reference numbers EPA/LAB/2018/03/178 and EPA/LAB/2018/03/291, respectively, which clearly indicated the exceedance of NEQS for BOD (e.g., 250 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L), COD (e.g., 600 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), TSS (e.g., 350 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), and elevated levels of Chromium and Lead.
4.  **Notices and Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs):** Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 25.03.2018, followed by formal EPOs dated 05.04.2018 and 20.04.2018, directing the Respondent to cease the discharge of untreated effluent and to ensure compliance with NEQS within specified timelines.
5.  **Correspondence:** Records of communication between the EPA and the Respondent, demonstrating the Respondent's failure to provide satisfactory responses or implement corrective actions.
The Respondent, in their defense, generally denied the allegations, claiming that they possessed a functional ETP and were committed to environmental compliance. They contended that any observed exceedance could be attributed to temporary operational glitches, heavy rainfall events diluting the samples, or potential errors in the sampling and analysis methodology employed by the EPA. They further submitted an internal report claiming that their ETP was operational during the relevant period, although this report lacked independent verification and specific data correlating to the dates of EPA's sampling.
The Tribunal carefully considered the arguments and evidence presented by both parties. The Respondent's defense, primarily relying on general denials and an unsubstantiated internal report, failed to provide specific, verifiable data or independent expert analysis to counter the detailed and scientifically rigorous evidence presented by the Complainant. The claims of "temporary operational glitches" or "heavy rainfall events" were not supported by any contemporaneous records or evidence. Furthermore, the Complainant's meticulous documentation, including inspection reports, properly maintained chain of custody forms, and certified laboratory analysis reports from an accredited EPA laboratory, established a strong and credible case. The consistency of the exceedances across multiple parameters and sampling dates strongly indicates a systemic failure in the Respondent's effluent treatment process, rather than isolated incidents. The Respondent's failure to comply with the directives contained in the EPOs also demonstrated a persistent disregard for environmental regulations and the authority of the EPA.
Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, explicitly prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste in an amount, concentration, or level that exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The laboratory reports unequivocally confirm that the Respondent's discharged effluent significantly surpassed the permissible limits for several critical parameters, thereby constituting a clear and undeniable violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997. Section 17 of the Act prescribes penalties for such violations, including fines and other punitive measures.
**FINDINGS:**
Based on the foregoing, the Tribunal finds that the Complainant, through cogent and admissible evidence, has successfully proven the allegations against the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited. The Respondent has failed to provide a satisfactory defense or credible evidence to refute the charges. It is established that the Respondent was, at the relevant times, discharging industrial effluent into a public drain that significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards, causing environmental pollution and failing to comply with Environmental Protection Orders.
**ORDER:**
In view of the gravity of the environmental violations, the persistent non-compliance, and the potential harm posed to the environment and public health, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Conviction:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is found guilty of violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
2.  **Penalty:** A fine of **PKR 10,000,000/- (Rupees Ten Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited under Section 17(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Compliance Directives:**
    a.  The Respondent shall immediately cease the discharge of any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and shall ensure that all discharges from its facility strictly comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) at all times.
    b.  The Respondent shall, within sixty (60) days from the date of this judgment, submit a comprehensive plan for the upgrade, rehabilitation, or replacement of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, ensuring its robust and continuous operation to meet NEQS. The implementation of this plan shall be completed within a timeframe approved by the EPA

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
