This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), under Section 17 read with Sections 11 and 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks enforcement against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent") for alleged violations of environmental standards and non-compliance with statutory obligations. The complaint was instituted on 12.04.2016, alleging persistent environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's industrial operations.
The Complainant's primary contention is that the Respondent, a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating in blatant disregard of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions and proper waste management practices. Specifically, the allegations include:
1.  **Exceedance of NEQS for Gaseous Emissions:** The Respondent's industrial furnaces and boilers are alleged to be emitting particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx) significantly above the permissible limits prescribed under the NEQS, thereby contributing to air pollution in the vicinity.
2.  **Improper Disposal of Industrial Solid Waste:** The Respondent is accused of failing to adequately manage and dispose of its industrial solid waste, including cullet, refractory waste, and other manufacturing by-products, leading to environmental contamination and potential health hazards.
3.  **Failure to Install/Maintain Pollution Control Devices:** Despite repeated warnings and directives, the Respondent allegedly failed to install and/or maintain effective air pollution control devices, such as electrostatic precipitators or bag filters, necessary to mitigate the hazardous emissions from its stacks.
4.  **Non-Compliance with Environmental Management Plan (EMP):** The Respondent is said to have failed to adhere to the commitments made in its approved Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and subsequent Environmental Management Plan, particularly concerning regular environmental monitoring and reporting.
5.  **Operation in Violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997:** The cumulative effect of these actions is alleged to constitute a direct violation of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, which prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level in excess of the NEQS.
In support of its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports conducted by EPA officials on various dates, including 15.01.2016
, 28.02.2016, and 05.04.2016. These reports meticulously documented visual observations of dark smoke emissions, improper waste stockpiling, and the absence of functional pollution control equipment. Crucially, the Complainant also submitted laboratory analysis reports of ambient air samples and stack emissions collected on 18.03.2
016, which unequivocally indicated exceedances of NEQS for PM, SO2, and NOx, thereby substantiating the allegations of air pollution.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
