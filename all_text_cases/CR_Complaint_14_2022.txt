This judgment addresses Complaint No. 14/2022, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is invoked under Section 22 of the Act, empowering it to hear and decide complaints regarding environmental contraventions.
The Complainant's primary allegations against M/s Packages Limited revolve around persistent non-compliance with environmental standards, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent and the emission of air pollutants. It is contended that the Respondent's manufacturing processes, which involve significant water usage and generate various by-products, result in the release of pollutants into the environment exceeding the prescribed NEQS. The EPA asserts that despite repeated warnings and notices, the Respondent has failed to install, operate, or maintain an effective Environmental Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control (APC) systems capable of bringing their discharges and emissions within the permissible limits. The complaint particularly highlights the discharge of effluent containing high levels of Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and other hazardous parameters into the
Lahore Canal, thereby severely impacting aquatic life and posing significant health risks to local communities dependent on these water resources. Furthermore, the complaint details recurring instances of black smoke emissions and noxious fumes from the Respondent's industrial stacks, indicative of inadequate air pollution control measures and a direct contravention of NEQS for ambient air quality.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by the Complainant, which included multiple
environmental monitoring reports, laboratory analyses of effluent and air samples, photographic and videographic evidence of pollution incidents, and copies of show-cause notices and warning letters previously issued to the Respondent by the EPA. These documents consistently demonstrated that the Respondent's discharges and emissions exceeded the prescribed NEQS parameters over an extended period.
Despite being afforded ample opportunity to present its defense, the Respondent largely failed to adequately controvert the substantive allegations or provide convincing evidence of effective compliance measures. While the Respondent claimed to be in the process of upgrading its pollution control infrastructure, the evidence clearly indicated a persistent and ongoing failure to meet the statutory environmental standards, causing discernible harm to the environment and public health.
Having carefully considered the documentary evidence, the expert testimonies, and the arguments advanced by both parties, this Tribunal finds the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, guilty of contravening the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Sections 11, 12, and 13, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent and air emissions. The persistent nature of the violations and the demonstrable environmental degradation caused by the Respondent's operations warrant immediate and stringent action.
**Therefore, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:**
1.  **Declaration of Violation:** M/s Packages Limited is declared to be in violation of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for its industrial effluent discharge and air emissions.
2.  **Imposition of Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Packages Limited for the aforementioned environmental violations. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
