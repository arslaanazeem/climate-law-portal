**PUNJAB ENVIRONMENTAL TRIBUNAL, LAHORE**
**COMPLAINT NO. 56/2023**
**Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore**
...Complainant
**VERSUS**
**M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited**
...Respondent
**Presided by: Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 56/2023, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a company engaged in the manufacturing of various chemical products located in the industrial zone of Lahore. The complaint seeks redress for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and its associated rules and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants beyond prescribed standards. The Tribunal took cognizance of the matter on [Fictional Date, e.g., 15th May 2023], and after several hearings, arguments were concluded on [Fictional Date, e.g., 20th November 2023].
The Complainant's case, as articulated in the complaint and subsequent submissions, posits that the Respondent has been consistently operating in contravention of environmental laws. Specifically, it is alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been:
1.  Discharging industrial effluent directly into the public sewerage system/drainage channels without adequate treatment, resulting in pollutant concentrations far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for liquid effluent.
2.  Emitting gaseous pollutants, including particulate matter and sulfur dioxide, from its stacks and processes, which similarly violate the NEQS for gaseous emissions, thereby contributing to air pollution in the surrounding residential and industrial areas.
3.  Failing to maintain and operate its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) effectively, or in some instances, allegedly bypassing them altogether.
4.  Neglecting to submit regular self-monitoring reports as required under the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2001 (SMART Rules).
5.  Operating without a valid Environmental Approval (EIA/IEE) for certain expansions or modifications, or failing to comply with the conditions stipulated in any existing approval.
These alleged violations, the Complainant contends,
pose a significant threat to public health and the environment, particularly impacting aquatic life in the drainage channels, contaminating groundwater, and causing respiratory issues among residents due to poor air quality. The EPA submits that despite repeated warnings, issuance of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent has failed to take adequate
steps for compliance, necessitating the present complaint for enforcement and imposition of penalties as stipulated under the Act.
The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, or alternatively, presented mitigating circumstances. They argued that significant investments have been made in their pollution control infrastructure, and they are continuously striving to improve their environmental performance. They contended that some alleged violations might be sporadic or due to technical glitches rather than a deliberate bypass. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed that the financial burden of immediate full compliance with all NEQS, particularly for legacy facilities, is substantial and requested additional time and a more phased approach for upgrades. They also asserted that they submit reports, albeit sometimes with delays, and are in discussions with the EPA regarding the scope and conditions of their environmental approvals for recent modifications.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented by both parties, including inspection reports, laboratory analysis results of effluent and emission samples collected by the EPA on various dates, previous Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued to the Respondent, and the Respondent's compliance history. Expert opinions on the efficacy of the existing treatment plants and the interpretation of monitoring data were also considered. The findings from multiple independent analyses consistently revealed that the Respondent's discharged effluent and emitted gases frequently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for several critical parameters, including BOD, COD, TSS, heavy metals in liquid effluent, and particulate matter, SO2, and NOx in gaseous emissions. The evidence strongly suggested that the Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) were either inadequately designed, poorly maintained, or not consistently operated at their optimal efficiency. The Respondent's explanations regarding technical challenges and ongoing efforts, while noted, did not sufficiently counter the overwhelming evidence of persistent non-compliance. The Tribunal also observed a pattern of delayed or incomplete submission of self-monitoring reports, hindering the EPA's oversight capabilities.
Upon careful consideration of the pleadings, evidence, and arguments presented, this Tribunal finds that the Complainant has successfully established, through cogent and compelling evidence, that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has indeed been in persistent and significant violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for both liquid effluent discharge and gaseous emissions, as mandated by the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its associated rules and regulations. The evidence clearly indicates the inadequacy of the existing pollution control measures and a failure to adhere to the conditions of their environmental approvals and previous EPOs. The Respondent's arguments regarding technical difficulties or ongoing efforts, while noted, do not absolve them of their legal obligations to comply with environmental laws and standards, especially given the repeated notices and opportunities for compliance provided by the EPA over an extended period. The continued discharge of untreated or inadequately treated pollutants poses a direct and demonstrable threat to public health and the environment.
Therefore, in light of the proven violations and considering the severe environmental and public health risks posed, this Tribunal, exercising its powers under Sections 17, 19, and 20 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, hereby issues the following:
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
1.  **Financial Penalty:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby directed to pay a financial penalty of **PKR 7,500,000/- (Rupees Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** for the proven violations of environmental laws and standards.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,100,000/- (Rupees One Million One Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 220,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **12 May, 2023**.
