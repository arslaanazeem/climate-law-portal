**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 13/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity located in Lahore. The complaint was formally lodged with this Tribunal on July 14, 2016, alleging serious violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) in relation to the discharge of industrial effluent.
The genesis of this complaint lies in persistent concerns regarding the Respondent's environmental compliance. The Complainant's primary contention was that M/s Packages Limited was operating its industrial facility without adequately treating its industrial wastewater, resulting in the discharge of effluent that consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for various pollutants. Specifically, the Complainant alleged that the Respondent's effluent, discharged into the municipal sewerage system which eventually feeds into the Hudiara Drain, contained levels of Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and certain heavy metals far beyond permissible limits. These discharges were asserted to be causing significant environmental degradation, polluting water bodies, impacting aquatic life, and posing a tangible threat to public health and safety in the surrounding areas. The Complainant further maintained that despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent had failed to take adequate measures to rectify the situation or install and operate an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) capable of achieving compliance with the NEQS.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a robust body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports prepared by EPA officials on various dates, notably April 12, 2016, and June 08, 2016, which documented observations of discharge points and collection of effluent samples. Crucially, the Complainant submitted detailed laboratory analysis reports from the EPA’s accredited laboratory, corresponding to the samples collected during these inspections. These reports consistently demonstrated that the Respondent's industrial effluent exhibited values significantly higher than the NEQS for parameters such as BOD (e.g., 250 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L), COD (e.g., 600 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), and TSS (e.g., 300 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), alongside elevated levels of certain heavy metals. The Complainant also presented copies of Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) issued to the Respondent prior to the filing of the
complaint, emphasizing the Respondent’s persistent failure to comply despite these formal directives.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and contested the allegations. While acknowledging its industrial operations and the generation of wastewater, the Respondent primarily argued that it had taken, and continued to take, substantial measures to ensure environmental compliance. It was contended that an Eff
luent Treatment Plant (ETP) was indeed operational at its facility, and that it had invested significantly in upgrading and maintaining this plant to meet environmental standards. The Respondent asserted that any occasional exceedances of NEQS were either isolated incidents, attributable to specific operational challenges, or potentially resulted from flaws in the EPA's sampling and analysis methodology. Furthermore, the Respondent highlighted its commitment to corporate social responsibility and environmental stewardship, detailing ongoing efforts to improve its waste management practices, including plans for further enhancements to its ETP technology and capacity. It also provided documentation, such as internal reports and invoices related to ETP maintenance and chemical purchases, to support its claims of proactive environmental management. The Respondent sought to emphasize its good faith efforts and requested the Tribunal to consider these mitigating circumstances, arguing against the imposition of stringent penalties.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence and heard detailed arguments from both sides. It carefully considered the inspection reports and laboratory analyses presented by the Complainant, which consistently indicated non-compliance over a significant period. While acknowledging the Respondent's claims of having an operational ETP and making efforts towards compliance, the Tribunal found the evidence of persistent NEQS violations, as demonstrated by the EPA's accredited laboratory reports, to be compelling and largely unrebutted on their factual merits. The Respondent's explanations regarding isolated incidents or methodological flaws, while noted, did not sufficiently negate the consistent pattern of exceedances across multiple parameters and sampling dates. The Tribunal observed that the burden of proving compliance with NEQS rests squarely on the industrial unit discharging effluent, and mere possession of an ETP does not absolve an entity if the discharged effluent fails to meet the prescribed standards.
The Tribunal therefore concludes that M/s Packages Limited has failed to consistently operate its industrial facility in a manner that ensures compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent, thereby violating Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. The persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent poses a clear and present danger to the environment and public health, contravening the fundamental principles of environmental protection enshrined in PEPA, 1997. The repeated issuance of notices and Environmental Protection Orders by the EPA, prior to the filing of this complaint, further underscores the Respondent's failure to take timely and effective corrective action.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby issues the following Final Order and Penalty against M/s Packages Limited:
1.  **Violation Declared:** M/s Packages Limited is found to be in violation of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, for discharging industrial effluent that consistently exceeds the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  **Immediate Compliance Directive:** The Respondent is hereby directed to immediately take all necessary measures to ensure that its industrial effluent meets the prescribed NEQS for all parameters, including but not limited to BOD, COD, TSS, and heavy metals, before discharge into any sewerage system or water body.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,200,000/- (Rupees One Million Two Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 240,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
