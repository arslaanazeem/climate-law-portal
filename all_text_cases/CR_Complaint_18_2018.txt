This judgment addresses the environmental complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "the Mill"), situated in Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint, registered on 15.03.2018, seeks enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"). The core issue revolves around the Respondent's alleged failure to comply with prescribed environmental standards for industrial effluent and air emissions, thereby causing pollution and posing a threat to public health and the environment.
The Complainant, through its authorized officer, alleged that the Respondent, an industrial unit engaged in textile manufacturing, had been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies, without conforming to the NEQS for industrial and municipal effluent. Furthermore, it was alleged that the Mill's boiler stacks were emitting air pollutants beyond the permissible limits set by the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. The complaint specifically referenced multiple site inspections conducted by EPA officials on 10.12.2017, 25.01.2018, and 05.03.2018. During these inspections, samples of effluent and stack emissions were collected from designated discharge points and emission sources. Subsequent laboratory analyses, conducted by the EPA's accredited laboratory, allegedly revealed significant exceedances of parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH in the effluent, and Particulate Matter (PM) and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx) in the air emissions. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated verbal warnings and a formal Environmental Protection Order (EPO) issued under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, dated 20.02.2018, the Respondent failed to take adequate corrective measures to bring its operations into compliance with the NEQS, thereby committing an offence punishable under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence
, including detailed inspection reports from the aforementioned dates, accompanied by certified laboratory analysis reports for effluent and stack emission samples. These reports consistently demonstrated significant exceedances of the NEQS for various parameters, unequivocally establishing non-compliance. The Complainant also submitted the Environmental Protection Order (EPO) dated 20.02.2018, which served as a formal directive to the Respondent to rectify the
violations within the stipulated period, failing which legal proceedings would be initiated. The Complainant further contended that despite the issuance of the EPO, the Respondent failed to demonstrate any significant improvement in its environmental performance, thereby necessitating the present complaint for enforcement and penalty.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Having considered the evidence on record, this Tribunal finds that the EPA inspection was conducted by authorized officers, samples were collected following prescribed protocols, and the laboratory results are from a certified laboratory. The chain of custody documentation is complete and the Respondent's challenge to the sampling methodology is without merit. The NEQS exceedances are established beyond any reasonable doubt.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
