This judgment is rendered in Complaint No. 41/2016, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Complainant seeks the imposition of penalties and environmental restoration measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Tribunal is seized with jurisdiction to adjudicate this matter under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent, a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating in contravention of various provisions of PEPA, 1997, and its allied rules and regulations. Specifically, the allegations included:
1.  **Exceedance of National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Gaseous Emissions:** It was alleged that the Respondent’s industrial operations, particularly emissions from its glass melting furnaces, consistently exceeded the prescribed NEQS for particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), as stipulated in the NEQS for Industrial Gaseous Emissions.
2.  **Discharge of Untreated or Partially Treated Industrial Effluents:** The Complainant asserted that the Respondent was discharging industrial wastewater containing elevated levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Lead (Pb), and Chromium (Cr) into the municipal sewerage system, or directly into nearby water bodies, without adequate treatment, thereby violating the NEQS for Industrial Effluents.
3.  **Improper Management of Hazardous Waste:** The EPA alleged that the Respondent failed to properly segregate, store, and dispose of hazardous waste generated during its manufacturing processes, such as spent refractories, cullet contaminated with heavy metals, and sludge from its wastewater treatment plant (if any), in accordance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and other relevant guidelines.
4.  **Failure to Comply with Environmental Approval Conditions:** It was further alleged that the Respondent had either not obtained the requisite Environmental Approval (NOC) under Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, or, if obtained, had failed to adhere to the conditions stipulated therein, particularly regarding the installation and operational efficiency of pollution control devices and regular environmental monitoring.
5.  **Non-Submission of Self-Monitoring and Environmental Audit Reports:** The Complainant claimed that the Respondent had been remiss in submitting periodic self-monitoring reports and environmental audit reports, which are mandatory requirements for industrial units under the environmental regulations.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 4,400,000/- (Rupees Four Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 880,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
