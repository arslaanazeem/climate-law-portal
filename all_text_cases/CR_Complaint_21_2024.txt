**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), concerning alleged violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal has jurisdiction to hear and determine this complaint under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA 1997"). The proceedings commenced on 15.03.2024, and after several hearings, the arguments from both sides were concluded, and the matter reserved for judgment.
The Complainant, through its authorized officer, alleged that the Respondent, a textile manufacturing unit located in Lahore, was discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent directly into the Lahore Canal/public sewerage system, thereby contaminating water bodies and posing a significant threat to public health and the environment. Specifically, it was contended that the Respondent's effluent discharge consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial liquid effluents, particularly concerning parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and color. Furthermore, the complaint included allegations of inadequate air pollution control measures, leading to excessive stack emissions from the Respondent's boiler house, and improper management of hazardous solid waste generated during the dyeing and finishing processes. The Complainant asserted that despite repeated notices and opportunities for compliance, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures, necessitating the present action for enforcement and imposition of penalties under PEPA 1997.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a series of inspection reports, dating from October 2023 to February 2024, conducted by EPA field teams at the Respondent's premises. These reports consistently documented observations of dark-colored effluent being discharged from the Respondent's facility. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected during these inspections, duly certified by the EPA's accredited laboratory. These reports indicated significant exceedances of NEQS for BOD (e.g., recorded values of 350-480 mg/L against a standard of 80 mg/L), COD (e.g., 900-1200 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), TSS (e.g., 300-450 mg/L against a standard of 200 mg/L), and highly fluctuating pH levels (e.g., 4.5 and 10.5 against a standard range of 6-9). Photographic and videographic evidence showing the visible pollution from the discharge point was also submitted. Additionally, the Complainant
also presented evidence detailing the Respondent's historical non-compliance, including previous warning letters and show-cause notices issued by the EPA, which the Respondent allegedly failed to adequately address.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal has carefully examined the inspection report, laboratory analysis results, and the photographic evidence produced by the Complainant. The measured pollutant concentrations significantly exceed the NEQS limits and the Respondent has failed to rebut this evidence with credible contrary data. The defense regarding ETP maintenance does not excuse ongoing pollution discharge and cannot serve as a justification for non-compliance with mandatory environmental standards.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,800,000/- (Rupees One Million Eight Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 360,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
