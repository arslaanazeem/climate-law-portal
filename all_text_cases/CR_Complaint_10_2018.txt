This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), seeks to address alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations by M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The Complainant asserts that the Respondent, a prominent manufacturing entity, has been discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS"), thereby causing environmental degradation and public nuisance. The Tribunal's jurisdiction is invoked to ensure compliance with environmental safeguards and to impose penalties for non-compliance as stipulated by law.
The Complainant's primary allegations stem from a series of inspections conducted at the Respondent's manufacturing facility located in Kot Lakhpat, Lahore. It is contended that despite repeated warnings and notices, the Respondent has consistently failed to effectively operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), leading to the discharge of untreated or inadequately treated wastewater into the municipal sewerage system, which eventually flows into natural water bodies. Specifically, the allegations include:
a.  **Discharge of Pollutants Exceeding NEQS:** On multiple occasions, particularly during inspections on June 15, 2017, and September 28, 2017, samples of industrial effluent collected from the Respondent's discharge points revealed concentrations of various parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH, significantly exceeding the limits prescribed by the NEQS.
b.  **Ineffective ETP Operation:** The ETP installed by the Respondent was found to be either non-operational or operating below its designed efficiency, failing to treat the effluent to the required standards.
c.  **Failure to Respond to Notices:** The EPA issued a show-cause notice dated July 10, 2017, followed by a final warning notice dated October 15, 2017, demanding immediate compliance and submission of a detailed action plan. The Respondent's responses were deemed unsatisfactory, lacking concrete steps for sustained compliance.
d.  **Public Nuisance and Environmental Damage:** The continued discharge of substandard effluent is alleged to contribute to water pollution, adversely affecting aquatic life, soil quality, and posing potential health risks to communities downstream.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented the following evidence:
i.  **Inspection Reports:** Detailed inspection reports dated June 15, 201
7, and September 28, 2017, detailing the observations of non-compliance and the failure of the ETP.
ii. **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Certified laboratory reports from accredited facilities confirming that effluent samples collected on the aforementioned dates significantly exceeded NEQS for COD, BOD5, Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH. These reports were issued by the
Punjab Environmental Quality Standards Monitoring Laboratory, an accredited facility, unequivocally demonstrating non-compliance with the NEQS.
iii. **Notices and Correspondence:** Copies of the show-cause notice dated July 10, 2017, and the final warning notice dated October 15, 2017, along with the Respondent's unsatisfactory replies, evidencing a failure to address the violations adequately within the stipulated timeframes.
iv. **Expert Testimony:** The testimony of EPA officials involved in the inspections and sample collection, corroborating the findings of the reports and detailing the Respondent's operational deficiencies.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, asserting its commitment to environmental stewardship and compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. It was contended that the Respondent has invested substantially in a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and consistently endeavors to operate it efficiently. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
