This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, seeks enforcement of environmental laws against M/s Ghani Glass Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent glass manufacturing facility located in Lahore. The complaint alleges persistent violations of environmental standards, specifically concerning air emissions and wastewater discharge, thereby posing a significant threat to public health and the environment, and contravening the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Tribunal has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant's allegations stem from a series of inspections and monitoring activities conducted at the Respondent's facility. It is contended that the Respondent's industrial operations, particularly its glass melting furnaces and associated processes, have been consistently releasing air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), oxides of nitrogen (NOx), and sulfur dioxide (SO2), into the atmosphere in concentrations exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial gaseous emissions. Furthermore, the Complainant alleges that the wastewater generated from the Respondent's manufacturing processes, including cooling water and process water, is being discharged without adequate treatment, resulting in elevated levels of Total Suspended Solids (TSS), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), and other parameters beyond the prescribed NEQS limits for industrial effluents. The Complainant asserts that despite multiple verbal warnings, written notices, and the issuance of an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997, the Respondent failed to take effective remedial measures to bring its operations into compliance. The complaint, therefore, seeks appropriate penalties and directives for immediate compliance with environmental regulations.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a detailed record comprising multiple inspection reports dated between January 2015 and June 2016. These reports included photographic evidence of visible emissions from chimneys and untreated effluent discharge. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports from accredited environmental laboratories, which consistently showed exceedances of NEQS for both air emissions (specifically PM10 and NOx) and wastewater parameters (TSS and COD) during sampling events conducted on 10.03.2015, 22.09.2015, and 05.04.2016. Copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent on 15.05.2015 and 01.11.2015, along with the final EPO dated 20.06.2016, were also placed on record, demonstrating the Complainant's efforts to secure voluntary compliance prior to filing the instant complaint. The Complainant also produced the responses received from the Respondent to these notices, which were largely deemed unsatisfactory or indicative of insufficient action.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, vehemently denied the persistent nature of the alleged violations. While acknowledging certain historical challenges in meeting all environmental standards, the Respondent argued that significant investments have been made in upgrading its pollution control infrastructure. The Respondent provided evidence of the installation of a new Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for air emission control in December 2015 and an upgraded Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) in March 2016. They submitted internal monitoring reports and a third-party audit report dated July 2016, which, according to them, indicated substantial improvements and compliance with NEQS post-upgradation. The Respondent contended that any exceedances noted by the EPA were either isolated incidents, attributable to transient operational issues, or occurred prior to the completion of their remedial measures. They further argued that they have a robust environmental management system in place and are committed to sustainable operations, seeking to demonstrate their good faith through ongoing efforts and financial commitments towards environmental protection.
The pertinent legal provisions governing this matter are primarily derived from the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, explicitly prohibits any person
from discharging or emitting any effluent or waste or air pollutant or noise in an amount, concentration or level which is in excess of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) or, where applicable, the standards established by the Agency under clause (g) of sub-section (1) of Section 6. Furthermore, Section 16 empowers the Agency to issue an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) to
any person causing or about to cause pollution, requiring them to take such measures as may be specified in the order to mitigate or prevent pollution. Failure to comply with an EPO constitutes a further contravention of the Act, attracting penalties under Section 17 of PEPA, 1997. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
