### JUDGMENT
The Punjab Environmental Tribunal, Lahore, having duly considered the complaint, the evidence adduced by the parties, and the arguments presented, hereby renders its judgment in Complaint No. 12/2019.
This complaint, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant"), on behalf of the Provincial Government, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a chemical manufacturing facility located in Lahore, invokes the jurisdiction of this Tribunal under Section 22 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"). The Complainant seeks remedial action and penalties against the Respondent for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, particularly regarding the discharge of untreated industrial effluent.
The Complainant's case rests upon a series of allegations asserting that the Respondent has been consistently operating in contravention of environmental standards. Specifically, it is alleged that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been discharging industrial effluent directly into the local sewerage system and ultimately into nearby water bodies without adequate treatment. The Complainant asserts that this effluent contains pollutants in concentrations far exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its provincial adaptation, the PEPA, 1997. Furthermore, it is alleged that the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP), if present, is either non-functional, improperly maintained, or insufficient to treat the volume and type of waste generated. The Complainant also highlighted the Respondent's failure to comply with previous warning notices and a lack of commitment to implement effective pollution control measures, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health and aquatic life.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a robust body of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated between 2018 and 2019, prepared by EPA officials, detailing observations of direct discharge, foul odor, and visible signs of pollution originating from the Respondent's premises. Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates (e.g., 15.03.2018, 22.08.2018, 10.04.2019). These reports consistently showed parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH levels, and heavy metal concentrations (e.g., Chromium, Lead) significantly exceeding the prescribed NEQS limits. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting the dark, odorous discharge and its impact on the receiving environment was also presented. Furthermore, copies of show-cause notices issued to the Respondent, along with records of non-compliance and inadequate responses, formed part of the evidence. The Complainant also produced the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan, Assistant Director EPA, who conducted several inspections and personally oversaw the collection of samples, affirming the veracity of the reports.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, through its legal counsel, denied the allegations. While acknowledging the operation of a chemical manufacturing facility, the Respondent contended that it maintains an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and is committed to environmental compliance. The Respondent argued that any exceedance of NEQS might be isolated incidents, or attributed to external factors beyond its control, or
attributed to temporary operational glitches which have since been rectified, or that the sampling and analysis methodology employed by the Complainant was flawed or not representative of the Respondent's overall operational discharge. They further contended that significant investments have been made in their Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and that efforts are continuously underway to optimize its performance and ensure adherence to environmental standards. The Respondent also questioned the severity of the alleged environmental impact, suggesting that the Complainant's claims were exaggerated and that the facility's operations were largely compliant.
### **Tribunal's Deliberation and Findings:**
The Tribunal has meticulously reviewed the voluminous evidence presented by both parties, including the detailed inspection reports, laboratory analysis results, photographic and videographic evidence, and the testimony of Mr. Ahmed Khan. Upon careful examination, the Tribunal finds the Complainant's case to be overwhelmingly substantiated. The consistent and repeated exceedances of critical National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), pH, and heavy metals (Chromium, Lead) across multiple sampling dates unequivocally demonstrate a persistent failure on the part of the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, to adequately treat its industrial effluent.
The Respondent's defense, while acknowledging the presence of an ETP, failed to provide sufficient credible counter-evidence to refute the specific findings of the Complainant's laboratory reports or to demonstrate that its ETP was consistently operational and effective in bringing the discharge within prescribed limits. The argument regarding flawed sampling methodology was not supported by any expert testimony or alternative, independently verified analysis reports. Furthermore, the Tribunal notes with grave concern the Respondent's documented history of non-compliance with previous warning notices and show-cause notices, which indicates a systemic rather than an isolated problem. The visible signs of pollution and the documented impact on the receiving environment,
further corroborate the Complainant's assertions. The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to operate its facility in an environmentally responsible manner and has, through its persistent discharge of untreated or inadequately treated effluent, caused significant environmental degradation and posed a clear threat to public health and aquatic ecosystems. This constitutes a clear violation of Sections 11(1), 12, and 16 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, read with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents, which mandate adherence to prescribed standards and prohibit the discharge of pollutants in excess of such standards. The Tribunal therefore concludes that the Complainant has successfully established its case against the Respondent beyond reasonable doubt.
### **ORDER**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is found liable for violating the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, particularly Sections 11(1), 12, and 16, by consistently discharging industrial effluent exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS).
2.  The Respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, undertake a comprehensive upgrade, repair, and operational optimization of its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to ensure that all discharged effluent consistently meets the prescribed NEQS for industrial effluents.
3.  The Respondent shall submit a detailed compliance plan, including timelines, technical specifications for the ETP upgrade, and a schedule for regular internal monitoring, to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment, for approval and continuous oversight.
4.  The Respondent shall institute a robust internal monitoring mechanism and submit monthly effluent quality monitoring reports, duly certified by an EPA-approved independent laboratory, to the Complainant for a period of **one (1) year** following the completion of the ETP upgrade, demonstrating continuous compliance.
5.  Failure to comply with any part of this order shall attract further legal action, including but not limited to, the imposition of additional penalties and the sealing of the industrial unit as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.
### **PENALTY**
For the persistent and grave violations and the environmental damage caused, and in exercise of the powers vested under Section 17 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, the Tribunal imposes the following penalty:
1.  A fine of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited.
2.  This fine shall be paid to the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.
3.  In addition to the above fine, the Respondent shall be liable to pay an environmental compensation charge of **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)** for each day of non-compliance with the remedial orders specified above (specifically, the ETP upgrade and consistent NEQS adherence), commencing from the expiry of the ninety (90) day period for ETP upgrade, until full compliance is achieved and verified by the EPA.
4.  In the event of non-payment of the imposed fine within the stipulated period, the amount shall be recovered as arrears of land revenue, and further legal proceedings, including the sealing of the industrial unit, may be initiated without further notice, as per the provisions of PEPA, 1997.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,900,000/- (Rupees One Million Nine Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 380,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
