**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 41/2017, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"). The complaint was initiated under the provisions of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging contravention of environmental laws and standards by the Respondent. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, has the jurisdiction to hear and decide complaints concerning environmental pollution and violations of the Act and its associated rules and regulations.
The Complainant alleged that M/s Packages Limited, an industrial unit involved in the manufacturing of packaging materials, was discharging industrial effluent without adequate treatment into the public sewerage system and ultimately into surface water bodies, thereby causing significant environmental degradation and posing a threat to public health. Specifically, it was alleged that the Respondent's discharged effluent consistently exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for various parameters, in blatant disregard of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997. The Complainant further contended that despite repeated warnings and notices issued by the EPA, the Respondent failed to take effective measures to control the pollution emanating from its premises, thus necessitating the filing of the present complaint seeking enforcement of environmental laws and imposition of penalties.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented several pieces of evidence. Key among these were multiple inspection reports compiled by EPA officials on various dates between January 2017 and August 2017, detailing observations of effluent discharge and the operational status of the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). Crucially, the Complainant submitted laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on 15th March 2017, 22nd May 2017, and 10th August 2017. These reports, prepared by the EPA's accredited laboratory, consistently indicated that parameters such as Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD5), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels in the Respondent's effluent significantly exceeded the prescribed NEQS. For instance, the report dated 15th March 2017 showed BOD5 at 180 mg/L (NEQS limit: 80 mg/L), COD at 450 mg/L (NEQS limit: 150 mg/L), and TSS at 220 mg/L (NEQS limit: 200 mg/L). The Complainant also produced copies of show-cause notices issued to
the Respondent on 20th April 2017, 1st June 2017, and 15th August 2017, demanding explanations and remedial actions, which, according to the Complainant, were either inadequately addressed or ignored.
In response, the Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. While acknowledging the operation of an industrial unit and the generation of industrial effluent, the Respondent asserted that it had installed and was operating an Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) to treat its wastewater in accordance with environmental regulations. The Respondent contended that any exceedances noted in the EPA's reports were either isolated incidents, attributable to operational anomalies, or based on sampling methodologies that were not entirely representative. The Respondent further submitted that it had invested significant resources in upgrading its ETP technology and implementing best available techniques to minimize its environmental footprint.
It presented internal records of ETP maintenance logs and invoices for chemical purchases and equipment upgrades, arguing these demonstrated a commitment to environmental compliance. Furthermore, the Respondent questioned the validity of some of the EPA's sampling procedures, suggesting potential contamination or non-adherence to standard protocols, though no concrete evidence was brought forward to substantiate these claims beyond general assertions. The Respondent prayed for the dismissal of the complaint, asserting that it was a responsible corporate citizen striving for full compliance.
The Tribunal has carefully considered the arguments advanced by both parties and meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented. The core of the Complainant's case rests on the consistent laboratory analysis reports showing significant exceedances of NEQS for critical parameters such as BOD5, COD, and TSS. These reports, generated by an accredited EPA laboratory on multiple dates, provide strong and objective evidence of non-compliance. While the Respondent claimed operational anomalies or issues with sampling methodology, it failed to provide any credible counter-evidence, such as its own independent laboratory analysis reports for the same periods, or detailed technical explanations for the alleged anomalies that would convincingly discredit the EPA's findings. The mere assertion of investing in ETP upgrades, while commendable, does not absolve the Respondent of past or ongoing violations demonstrated by the EPA's concrete data. The show-cause notices and inspection reports further corroborate the EPA's diligent efforts to bring the Respondent into compliance prior to filing this complaint, indicating a pattern of disregard for regulatory directives.
Section 11 of the PEPA, 1997, unequivocally prohibits the discharge of any effluent or waste that exceeds the NEQS, and Section 17 of the Act prescribes penalties for such contraventions. The evidence on record, particularly the repeated and significant exceedances documented in the laboratory reports, leaves no doubt that the Respondent was in contravention of Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, by consistently discharging industrial effluent that did not meet the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards. The Tribunal finds the Complainant has successfully established its case against M/s Packages Limited.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal holds M/s Packages Limited liable for violating Section 11 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
**ORDER:**
1.  **Penalty Imposed:** M/s Packages Limited is hereby directed to pay a monetary penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** for the contraventions established. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
2.  **Remedial Measures:** The Respondent is directed to immediately ensure that its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) is fully operational, optimized, and consistently treating its industrial effluent to meet the National Environmental Quality Standards

---

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,300,000/- (Rupees One Million Three Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 260,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **24 June, 2022**.
