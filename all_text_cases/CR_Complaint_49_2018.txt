**JUDGMENT**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 49/2018, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent industrial entity engaged in the manufacturing of packaging materials, paper, and board. The complaint was instituted on 12th March 2018, alleging persistent non-compliance with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), thereby causing environmental degradation and public nuisance. The Tribunal was seized of the matter to adjudicate upon the alleged violations and determine appropriate remedial measures and penalties under the law.
The Complainant, through its learned counsel, contended that the Respondent's industrial operations at its facility located in Lahore were generating significant quantities of industrial effluent and air emissions which were being discharged into the environment without adequate treatment, in direct contravention of the prescribed NEQS. Specifically, the allegations included:
a. Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial wastewater into public sewers/drains, with parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH consistently exceeding the limits specified in the NEQS for Industrial Effluents.
b. Emissions of hazardous air pollutants, including Particulate Matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), from the Respondent's boilers and other industrial processes, exceeding the NEQS for Gaseous Emissions from Industrial Sources.
c. Failure to operate and maintain a functional and efficient Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) commensurate with the scale and nature of its industrial activities.
d. Non-compliance with previous environmental improvement notices issued by the EPA, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental regulations.
e. Causing environmental pollution detrimental to public health and the surrounding ecosystem, including contamination of water bodies and deterioration of ambient air quality in the vicinity.
To substantiate its claims, the Complainant presented a series of documentary and analytical evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15th January 2017, 28th April 2017, and 10th February 2018, detailing observations of effluent discharge points and emission sources, along with photographic evidence. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on samples collected on 15th January 2017, 28th April 2017, and 10th February 201
8, consistently showed parameters exceeding NEQS for various pollutants, including BOD, COD, TSS, pH in wastewater, and PM, SO2, NOx in air emissions. These reports, duly authenticated, formed the cornerstone of the Complainant's assertion that the Respondent was in continuous breach of environmental standards.
The Respondent, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations, contending that M/s Packages Limited is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship. They argued that the facility operates a state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and utilizes modern Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs), which are regularly maintained and upgraded. The Respondent's counsel submitted that any occasional exceedances noted in the EPA's reports might be attributed to:
a. Fluctuations in industrial processes beyond immediate control, or
b. Methodological discrepancies in sampling and analysis by the EPA, or
c. Isolated incidents that have since been rectified and do not represent a persistent pattern.
They presented their own internal monitoring reports and certificates from third-party environmental consultants, purporting to show compliance with NEQS for significant periods. Furthermore, the Respondent claimed to have invested substantial capital in environmental infrastructure and training of personnel,
demonstrating its commitment to sustainable operations. The Respondent also submitted that it had engaged in constructive dialogue with the EPA on previous occasions to address any concerns and was actively working towards further improvements. They requested the Tribunal to consider their overall environmental performance and the economic implications of any stringent penalties, asserting that their operations contribute significantly to the national economy and employment.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

The evidence presented by the Punjab EPA is comprehensive, consisting of original inspection reports signed by authorized officers, certified laboratory analyses, and photographic documentation of pollution discharge. This Tribunal finds that the Complainant has discharged its evidentiary burden and the pollution violations are proven. The Respondent's argument that samples were not collected in its presence is not supported by the record and is accordingly rejected.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

This Tribunal is vested with jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997 to try environmental offences committed within its territorial limits. Having found the Respondent guilty of violating NEQS standards, the Tribunal is empowered under Section 17(1) to impose financial penalties, direct remedial measures, and order contribution to the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund (PSDF) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
