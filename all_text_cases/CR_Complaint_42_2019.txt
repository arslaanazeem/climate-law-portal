**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 42/2019, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent manufacturing entity located in Lahore. The complaint, lodged on October 23, 2019, seeks punitive action and remedial measures against the Respondent for alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997) and its allied rules and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and emissions of air pollutants exceeding the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, has carefully considered the submissions, evidence, and legal arguments presented by both parties.
The Complainant alleged that the Respondent's industrial facility was involved in several environmental transgressions. Specifically, it was asserted that:
a) The Respondent was discharging industrial effluent directly into the municipal sewerage system and subsequently into natural waterways without adequate treatment, resulting in pollution levels exceeding the NEQS for industrial effluent.
b) The Respondent's boilers and other process units were emitting air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM), Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), and Nitrogen Oxides (NOx), into the atmosphere at concentrations significantly higher than the NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions.
c) The Respondent had failed to install, operate, and maintain an effective Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) and Air Pollution Control Devices (APCDs) as required by environmental laws and previous directives from the EPA.
d) The Respondent had not obtained the requisite Environmental Approval/No Objection Certificate (NOC) for its expanded operations or specific processes, particularly regarding its paper and packaging manufacturing units, in defiance of Section 12 of PEPA, 1997.
e) Despite repeated notices and warnings issued by the EPA, the Respondent had failed to take corrective measures, demonstrating a persistent disregard for environmental protection laws.
To substantiate its allegations, the Complainant presented a comprehensive dossier of evidence. This included:
i. **Inspection Reports:** Multiple inspection reports, dated June 12, 2019, August 05, 2019, and September 20, 2019, prepared by EPA field teams. These reports documented observations of visible untreated effluent discharge and black smoke emissions from the Respondent's premises.
ii. **Laboratory Analysis Reports:** Analytical reports from the EPA's accredited laboratory, based on effluent samples collected on June 12, 2019, and September 20, 2019, and air emission samples collected on August 05, 2019. These reports conclusively indicated that the discharged effluent had Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) levels of 450 mg/L (NEQS: 150 mg/L), Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) levels of 280 mg/L (NEQS: 80 mg/L), and Total Suspended Solids (TSS) of 320 mg/L (NEQS: 200 mg/L). Similarly, air emission analyses showed particulate matter concentrations of 650 mg/Nm³ (NEQS: 400 mg/Nm³) and SO2 levels of 2,100 mg/Nm³ (NEQS: 1,700 mg/Nm³).
iii. **Show Cause Notices:** Copies of Show Cause Notices issued to the Respondent on July 01, 2019, and September 25, 2019, detailing the violations and requiring immediate compliance.
iv. **Correspondence:** Official correspondence between the EPA and the Respondent, including the Respondent's replies, which largely acknowledged the issues but cited ongoing efforts without presenting concrete evidence of compliance within the stipulated timeframes.
v. **Photographic and Videographic Evidence:** Visual documentation, including photographs and short video clips, depicting the visible pollution emanating from the Respondent's facility, corroborating the inspection reports.
The Respondent, through its
legal counsel, vehemently contested some allegations while acknowledging certain operational challenges. The Respondent submitted that:
a) While there might have been intermittent instances of effluent discharge exceeding NEQS due to unforeseen operational glitches or maintenance activities, the Respondent was genuinely committed to environmental compliance and had initiated steps towards upgrading its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP). They presented a proposed timeline for the ETP upgrade, suggesting completion
by June 30, 2024.
b) Regarding air emissions, the Respondent contended that it had installed modern air pollution control devices (APCDs) and was in the process of optimizing their efficiency. They attributed occasional spikes in emissions to specific process adjustments or fuel quality issues, asserting that these were not indicative of continuous non-compliance. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 200,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
