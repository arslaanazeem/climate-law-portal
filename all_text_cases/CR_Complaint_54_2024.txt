**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a prominent textile manufacturing facility located in Lahore. The complaint seeks enforcement of environmental laws and regulations, specifically the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997"), alleging various violations pertaining to industrial effluent discharge. The Tribunal, constituted under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, holds the jurisdiction to hear and adjudicate upon such complaints, impose penalties, and issue necessary directions for environmental protection.
The Complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that the Respondent's industrial operations were in contravention of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and the conditions stipulated in its Environmental Approval. Specifically, the allegations included:
a.  Discharge of untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/drain, exceeding the prescribed NEQS for various parameters.
b.  Failure to operate and maintain its Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) effectively, leading to consistent non-compliance with environmental standards.
c.  Failure to provide complete and accurate self-monitoring reports as required by the Self-Monitoring and Reporting by Industries Rules, 2001.
d.  Violation of the conditions set forth in the Environmental Approval
granted to the Respondent.
The Complainant presented documentary evidence including multiple laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on various dates, which consistently indicated parameters such as Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels exceeding the permissible NEQS limits. Furthermore, inspection reports prepared by EPA officials detailed observations of the
inadequacy of the Respondent's Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) operations and the visible discharge of polluted water.
The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, through its legal counsel, vehemently denied the allegations. They contended that they operate a functional ETP and are committed to environmental compliance. They argued that the samples might not have been collected in accordance with prescribed procedures, or that the analytical reports were flawed. The Respondent further submitted that they had already initiated measures to upgrade their ETP and improve operational efficiency, and requested additional time to achieve full compliance, citing the significant investment and technical complexities involved. They presented some internal operational records and a timeline for proposed upgrades as part of their defense.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the evidence presented by both parties. While acknowledging the Respondent's stated commitment to environmental protection and their ongoing efforts to upgrade the ETP, the consistent and repeated nature of the violations, as evidenced by multiple laboratory reports from different dates, could not be overlooked. The Complainant's evidence, particularly the independent laboratory analyses showing consistent exceedance of NEQS for critical parameters, was compelling and largely unrebutted by substantive counter-evidence regarding sample integrity or analytical accuracy. The inspection reports further corroborated the operational deficiencies of the ETP. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

Upon examination of all evidence produced before this Tribunal, it is evident that the Respondent has been operating its facility in continuous violation of applicable NEQS standards. The defense arguments do not address the substance of the documented pollution. This Tribunal holds that strict liability applies under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, and proof of intent or negligence is not required for the imposition of penalty.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 imposes strict liability upon any person who discharges or emits, or allows the discharge or emission of, any effluent, waste, air pollutant or noise in excess of NEQS. The Respondent's industrial activity falls squarely within this prohibition. Proof of intent is not required; the act of pollution itself attracts penal consequences under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
