**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses Complaint No. 42/2020, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA" or "the Complainant"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), a textile manufacturing unit located at Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations, specifically concerning the discharge of untreated industrial effluent and air emissions, which pose a significant threat to public health and the environment. The Tribunal, having heard arguments from both sides and meticulously reviewed the submitted evidence, proceeds to render its decision.

The Complainant, through various inspection reports, laboratory analyses of effluent samples collected from the Respondent’s discharge points, and air emission monitoring data, demonstrated consistent and significant exceedances of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). These reports, spanning multiple visits, indicated high levels of biological oxygen demand (BOD), chemical oxygen demand (COD), total suspended solids (TSS), and other hazardous parameters in the industrial effluent, along with elevated particulate matter and gaseous pollutants in air emissions. Such violations are in direct contravention of Sections 11, 12, and 15 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997).
The Respondent, while acknowledging certain operational challenges and asserting ongoing efforts to upgrade its pollution control infrastructure, failed to provide conclusive evidence of sustained compliance with the NEQS during the period under review. Their submissions largely focused on future plans rather than demonstrating current adherence to environmental standards or mitigating the documented past violations. The Tribunal notes that while proactive measures are commendable, they do not absolve the Respondent of liability for past and present non-compliance that has demonstrably impacted the environment. The persistent nature of the violations, as evidenced by the Complainant's reports, underscores a clear failure to adequately manage environmental discharges.
Considering the gravity of the environmental pollution caused, the potential harm to public health and the ecosystem, and the Respondent's statutory obligations under PEPA, 1997, the Tribunal finds M/s Nishat Mills Limited to be in violation of environmental laws and regulations.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
