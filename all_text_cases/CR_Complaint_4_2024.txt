### JUDGMENT
This judgment pertains to the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging multiple violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 199
7. The complaint primarily alleges the discharge of untreated industrial effluent into public drains/water bodies and the emission of pollutants exceeding the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS), thereby causing environmental degradation and public health hazards in violation of Sections 11, 12, and 16 of the Act.
The Complainant, through its inspections and subsequent analytical reports of samples collected from the Respondent's premises, presented compelling evidence demonstrating persistent non-compliance. Despite the issuance of multiple show-cause notices and opportunities for rectification, the Respondent failed to implement adequate measures to control pollution effectively and bring its operations into conformity with the NEQS. The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence, including laboratory analysis reports, inspection reports, and the correspondence exchanged between the parties, alongside the arguments presented by the learned counsels.
Upon careful consideration of the facts, evidence, and legal submissions, this Tribunal finds that the Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, has indeed committed the alleged violations of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the rules and regulations framed thereunder. The continuous discharge of untreated effluent and the emission of pollutants beyond permissible limits constitute a clear breach of statutory obligations and pose a serious threat to the environment and the health of the surrounding community.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Tribunal hereby orders as follows:
1.  **Cessation of Violations:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is directed to immediately cease the discharge of any untreated industrial effluent into public drains or water bodies and to ensure that all emissions from its facility comply strictly with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) forthwith.
2.  **Monetary Penalty:** A penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand Only)** is imposed on the Respondent for the proven violations of PEPA, 1997. This amount shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within thirty (30) days from the date of this judgment.
3.  **Environmental Management Plan (EMP):** The Respondent is directed to submit a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP), including a detailed timeline for the installation/upgradation of necessary effluent treatment plants (ETP) and air pollution control devices, to the Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, within sixty (60) days. The EMP must demonstrate a clear path to achieving full compliance with all environmental laws and standards.
4.  **Regular Monitoring & Reporting:** The Respondent shall allow regular and unhindered access to EPA officials for monitoring and inspection purposes and shall submit quarterly compliance reports to the Complainant for a period of two years, detailing the progress made in achieving and maintaining environmental standards.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
