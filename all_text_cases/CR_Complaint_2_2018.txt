This judgment pertains to a complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ghani Glass Limited, seeking redress for alleged violations of environmental laws and regulations. The Tribunal is seized of this matter under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (PEPA, 1997), specifically Section 21 which empowers this Tribunal to hear and adjudicate upon complaints relating to environmental pollution and non-compliance with environmental standards. The core issue before this Tribunal is to determine whether the respondent, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, has indeed contravened the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) and other statutory obligations, thereby causing environmental degradation.
The complainant, through its authorized representative, alleged that M/s Ghani Glass Limited, an industrial unit engaged in the manufacturing of glass products located in the industrial zone of Lahore, has been operating in a manner that causes significant air pollution and improper waste management, in blatant disregard of environmental safeguards. Specifically, the allegations included: (a) persistent exceedance of NEQS for gaseous emissions, particularly Particulate Matter (PM), Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx), and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2) from its furnace stacks; (b) inadequate treatment and disposal of industrial wastewater, leading to potential contamination of surface and groundwater; and (c) improper storage and disposal of hazardous waste, including cullet contaminated with heavy metals and refractory waste, in open areas within and around its premises, posing risks to public health and the environment. These alleged violations were brought to the respondent's attention through multiple notices issued by the EPA, which reportedly failed to elicit satisfactory corrective action.
To substantiate its claims, the complainant presented a comprehensive array of evidence. This included multiple inspection reports dated 15.05.2017, 03.08.2017, and 12.11.2017, conducted by EPA officials, detailing observed non-compliance. Crucially, laboratory analysis reports from EPA-accredited laboratories, based on samples collected from the respondent's emission stacks and wastewater discharge points on various dates, consistently showed levels of PM, NOx, SO2, and certain heavy metals in wastewater far exceeding the NEQS. Photographic and videographic evidence depicting visible emissions, open dumping of industrial waste, and inadequate effluent treatment facilities was also submitted. The sworn testimonies of EPA inspectors, who conducted the site visits and collected samples, further corroborated the documentary evidence. On the other hand, M/s Ghani Glass Limited, represented by its legal counsel, denied the allegations, asserting that it has installed state-of-the-art pollution control equipment, including electrostatic precipitators and a wastewater treatment plant. The respondent produced internal compliance reports and maintenance logs, arguing that any exceedance was either temporary due to unforeseen operational malfunctions or attributable to flawed sampling and analytical methodologies employed by the EPA. An expert witness, an environmental consultant engaged by the respondent, testified regarding the general efficiency of the installed pollution control systems and suggested potential inaccuracies in the complainant's data collection. However, the respondent failed to provide independent, third-party laboratory analyses to counter the EPA's specific findings of non-compliance on the dates in question.
The legal framework governing this complaint primarily stems from the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997. Section 11 of the Act prohibits the discharge or emission of any effluent or waste or air pollutant in an amount, concentration, or level that is in excess of the NEQS. Section 12 mandates that no person shall commence construction or operation of any industrial activity likely to cause adverse environmental effects without filing an Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and obtaining approval from the EPA. Section 17 prescribes penalties for contravention of the Act, Rules, or Regulations. Furthermore, the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Gaseous Emissions and NEQS for Industrial Effluents, notified under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, serve as the benchmark for permissible limits of pollutants. The Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010, also provide for the imposition of environmental charges.
Upon careful consideration of the pleadings, documentary evidence, and testimonies presented by both parties, this Tribunal finds the allegations leveled by the complainant against M/s Ghani Glass Limited to be substantially proven. The consistent exceedance of NEQS for air emissions (PM, NOx, SO2) from the respondent's facility, as evidenced by multiple independent laboratory reports presented by the EPA, cannot be dismissed as isolated incidents or methodological errors without compelling counter-evidence. While the respondent claims to have installed pollution control equipment, the effectiveness of such equipment is measured by the actual quality of emissions, which in this case, repeatedly failed to meet the prescribed standards. The lack of independent verification from the respondent
further weakens its defense. The Tribunal also finds strong corroboration for the allegations concerning inadequate treatment and disposal of industrial wastewater and improper storage and disposal of hazardous waste, supported by photographic and videographic evidence and the testimonies of EPA inspectors. The respondent's general assertions about installed pollution control systems, without specific evidence of their efficacy and compliance on the dates of alleged violations, are insufficient to rebut the consistent and
specific evidence of non-compliance presented by the complainant. The onus was on the respondent to demonstrate compliance, particularly after repeated notices from the EPA, which it has failed to do effectively. Operating an industrial unit carries a fundamental responsibility to adhere to environmental laws and protect public health and the environment. The persistent nature of the violations, as established by the evidence, indicates a systemic failure on the part of M/s Ghani Glass Limited to adequately manage its environmental impact. This Tribunal cannot overlook such documented disregard for environmental protection statutes and standards, which are designed to safeguard the ecological balance and the well-being of the populace.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, this Tribunal holds that M/s Ghani Glass Limited has contravened the provisions of Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for Industrial Gaseous Emissions and Industrial Effluents. The respondent's actions constitute a clear breach of its statutory obligations and have contributed to environmental pollution.
Therefore, this Tribunal issues the following order:
1.  **Complaint Allowed:** The complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency, Lahore, is hereby allowed.
2.  **Cessation of Non-Compliance:** M/s Ghani Glass Limited is directed to immediately cease all activities that are in violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards and the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
3.  **Corrective Measures:**
    *   The respondent shall, within **ninety (90) days** from the date of this order, upgrade its existing air pollution control devices (specifically for Particulate Matter, Oxides of Nitrogen, and Sulphur Dioxide emissions) and its industrial wastewater treatment plant to ensure consistent compliance with the NEQS.
    *   The respondent shall submit a detailed Environmental Management Plan (EMP) to the EPA within **thirty (30) days** outlining the specific actions, timelines, and responsible personnel for achieving and maintaining NEQS compliance for air emissions, wastewater discharge, and hazardous waste management. This EMP must include provisions for independent third-party environmental monitoring, with reports to be submitted to the EPA quarterly.
    *   The respondent shall implement proper storage, treatment, and disposal mechanisms for all hazardous waste, including cullet and refractory waste, in accordance with the Hazardous Substances Rules, 2003, and other relevant regulations, ceasing all open dumping forthwith.
4.  **Penalty:** Pursuant to Section 16(1) of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, M/s Ghani Glass Limited is hereby fined a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** for persistent violations of environmental laws and standards. This penalty shall be deposited with the Environmental Protection Agency, Punjab, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order.
5.  **Environmental Charges/Damages:** In addition to the penalty, M/s Ghani Glass Limited is directed to pay environmental charges amounting to **PKR 1,000,000/- (Rupees One Million only)** into the Provincial Sustainable Development Fund, as provided under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010, within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this order, as compensation for the environmental degradation caused.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.

Announced in open Tribunal this **16 January, 2023**.
