**JUDGMENT**
This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Packages Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent"), alleging contraventions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS). The complaint, bearing No. 17/2018, was lodged following a series of inspections and monitoring activities indicating the Respondent's non-compliance with prescribed environmental standards, thereby causing environmental pollution. The Tribunal, presided over by Mr. Justice (R) Chaudhry Muhammad Iqbal, heard the parties and examined the evidence presented.
The Complainant's allegations against M/s Packages Limited primarily revolve around the discharge of industrial effluent and emission of air pollutants exceeding the NEQS. Specifically, it was alleged that during an inspection conducted on March 1, 2018, by a team from the EPA, samples of industrial wastewater discharged from the Respondent's facility located at Shahrah-e-Room
, Lahore, were collected. Subsequent laboratory analysis confirmed that several key parameters, including Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, significantly exceeded the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluents. Furthermore, the complaint detailed observations of black smoke emissions from the Respondent's stacks, indicative of air pollution, which were also alleged to be in contravention of NEQS for industrial gaseous emissions. Following these findings, the EPA issued several show-cause notices and directions under Sections 16 and 17 of PEPA, 1997, requiring the Respondent to adopt appropriate pollution control measures and submit a compliance plan. Despite repeated communications and opportunities provided, the Complainant asserted that the Respondent failed to adequately address the non-compliance issues, necessitating the filing of the present complaint before this Tribunal.
The Respondent, M/s Packages Limited, through its learned counsel, vehemently denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. While acknowledging
the routine inspections conducted by the EPA, argued that the alleged exceedances were either temporary aberrations or were being actively addressed through substantial investments in pollution control technologies. The learned counsel submitted that M/s Packages Limited is a responsible corporate entity with a long-standing commitment to environmental stewardship and had already initiated a comprehensive plan to upgrade its effluent treatment plant (ETP) and air emission control systems prior to the filing of the complaint. It was further contended that some of the parameters might have been affected by factors beyond their immediate control or that the sampling methodology employed by the EPA was not entirely representative. The Respondent presented evidence of purchase orders for new equipment, ongoing construction work for ETP expansion, and internal monitoring reports showing efforts towards compliance. They sought to demonstrate a proactive approach rather than a negligent disregard for environmental regulations, requesting the Tribunal to consider their ongoing efforts and provide reasonable time for full compliance rather than imposing punitive measures.
The Tribunal meticulously reviewed the documentary evidence presented by both parties, including the EPA's inspection reports, laboratory analysis certificates, show-cause notices, and the Respondent's replies, as well as the Respondent's submissions regarding their compliance efforts. The laboratory reports submitted by the Complainant clearly indicated that the samples collected on March 1, 2018, and subsequent dates, consistently showed levels of COD, BOD, TSS, and pH that significantly exceeded the NEQS. While the Respondent's counsel argued for temporary aberrations or issues with sampling methodology, no concrete evidence was presented to discredit the EPA's certified laboratory results or its standard operating procedures for sampling. The visual observations of black smoke emissions, as recorded by the inspection team, further corroborated the allegations of air pollution.
The Tribunal appreciates the Respondent's stated commitment to environmental stewardship and acknowledges the steps taken *after* the initial notices were issued, such as the initiation of ETP upgrades and purchase of new equipment. However, these actions, while commendable for future compliance, do not absolve the Respondent of past and ongoing non-compliance that necessitated the EPA's intervention and the filing of this complaint. The primary duty of an industrial unit under PEPA, 1997, is to ensure continuous compliance with NEQS, and any failure to do so constitutes a contravention of the Act. **IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

Section 11 of PEPA, 1997 creates an absolute prohibition against the discharge of pollutants in excess of NEQS. This prohibition is reinforced by the principle of strict environmental liability recognized in the jurisprudence of the superior courts of Pakistan. Non-compliance with an EPO issued under Section 16 aggravates the culpability of the Respondent and justifies the imposition of an enhanced penalty.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,400,000/- (Rupees One Million Four Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 280,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
