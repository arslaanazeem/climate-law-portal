**JUDGMENT**
**Introduction**
This judgment pertains to Complaint No. 35/2024, filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (hereinafter referred to as "the Complainant" or "EPA"), Lahore, against M/s Nishat Mills Limited (hereinafter referred to as "the Respondent" or "Nishat Mills"), a textile manufacturing unit located in Ferozepur Road, Lahore. The complaint seeks redressal for alleged violations of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") and the National Environmental Quality Standards (hereinafter referred to as "NEQS") for industrial effluents. The Tribunal assumed jurisdiction under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997, to adjudicate upon the matter.
**Allegations**
The Complainant, through its filed complaint and supporting documents, alleged that the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, has been consistently discharging untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent into the public sewerage system/open drain, thereby causing significant environmental pollution. Specifically, the allegations include:
1.  **Violation of Section 12 of PEPA, 1997**: The Respondent is accused of discharging environmental pollutants in excess of the NEQS, particularly concerning parameters such as Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels, as stipulated in S.R.O. 549(I)/2000 dated June 5, 2000, and its subsequent
amendments from time to time.
2.  **Failure to Install/Operate an Adequate Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP)**: The Complainant asserted that despite repeated warnings and directives, the Respondent has either failed to install an effective ETP or is operating an ETP that is inadequate for treating the volume and nature of industrial waste generated, leading to continuous discharge of pollutants.
3.  **Non-compliance with EPA Directives**: It was further alleged that the Respondent failed to comply with various notices and directives issued by the EPA prior to the filing of this complaint, which had mandated corrective measures, including the submission of an environmental management plan and regular monitoring reports.
4.  **Environmental Degradation and Public Health Risk**: The Complainant highlighted that the persistent discharge of untreated effluent is causing severe environmental degradation to the aquatic ecosystems, soil, and groundwater in the vicinity, posing significant health risks to the local population and livestock relying on these resources.
**Proceedings**
Upon receipt of the complaint, the Tribunal took cognizance of the matter. Summons were issued to the Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, requiring their appearance and submission of a written reply. The Respondent, through its authorized representative and counsel, appeared before the Tribunal and filed a written statement denying the allegations, generally asserting compliance with environmental standards and claiming that their existing ETP was functional and effective. They also contended that the samples taken by the EPA were not representative or were taken without proper procedure.
During the course of proceedings, the Complainant submitted detailed laboratory analysis reports of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's discharge points on multiple occasions, duly attested by the environmental laboratories recognized by the EPA. These reports consistently indicated parameters exceeding the NEQS for BOD, COD, TSS, and abnormal pH levels. The Complainant also presented photographic and videographic evidence depicting the discharge of colored and foul-smelling effluent.
The Respondent, in rebuttal, produced some internal monitoring reports and asserted that their processes were environmentally compliant. However, they failed to present independent, credible laboratory reports that could effectively controvert the findings of the EPA's accredited laboratories. Arguments were heard from both sides, and all submitted documents were thoroughly examined.
**Findings of the Tribunal**
Having meticulously reviewed the complaint, the Respondent's reply, the documentary evidence, particularly the laboratory analysis reports submitted by the EPA, and the oral arguments presented by the learned counsel for both parties, the Tribunal has arrived at the following findings:
1.  The laboratory analysis reports presented by the Complainant, conducted by accredited environmental laboratories, consistently demonstrate that the effluent discharged by M/s Nishat Mills Limited on various dates exceeded the prescribed National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for several critical parameters, including BOD, COD, TSS, and pH. The Respondent's mere denial or unsubstantiated claims of compliance, without producing robust countervailing evidence from accredited sources, cannot negate the scientific findings presented by the EPA.
2.  The Tribunal finds that the Respondent has failed to install and/or effectively operate an Effluent Treatment Plant capable of treating its industrial discharge to the NEQS. The evidence clearly indicates a pattern of non-compliance, suggesting either an inadequate ETP or a failure to properly maintain and operate the existing one.
3.  The contention by the Respondent regarding the sampling procedure or representativeness of samples was not substantiated with any credible evidence or expert opinion. The EPA is statutorily empowered to collect samples for monitoring environmental compliance, and in the absence of specific procedural irregularities proven by the Respondent, the samples and their analyses are deemed valid.
4.  Consequently, the Tribunal concludes that M/s Nishat Mills Limited has indeed violated Section 12 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997, by discharging environmental pollutants in excess of the NEQS, thereby causing environmental pollution and posing a threat to public health and the environment. This persistent non-compliance warrants stringent action under the law.
**ORDER AND PENALTY**
In light of the foregoing findings, the Punjab Environmental Tribunal hereby holds M/s Nishat Mills Limited (the Respondent) guilty of violating Section 12 of the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997.
It is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Cease and Desist Order**: The Respondent, M/s Nishat Mills Limited, is immediately directed to cease and desist from discharging any untreated or inadequately treated industrial effluent that does not conform to the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) into any public sewerage system, open drain, or water body.
2.  **Installation/Upgradation of ETP**: The Respondent shall, within a period of **ninety (90) days** from the date of this judgment, install a new, state-of-the-art Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP) or thoroughly upgrade its existing ETP to ensure that all discharged effluents consistently meet the NEQS. A detailed plan for the installation/upgradation, including timelines and technical specifications, must be submitted to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, within **fifteen (15) days** of this order for approval.
3.  **Submission of Compliance Reports**: Upon completion of the ETP installation/upgradation, the Respondent shall submit quarterly compliance reports, including independent laboratory analysis reports of its treated effluent, to the EPA for a period of two years, demonstrating continuous adherence to NEQS.
4.  **Environmental Penalty**: For the established violations of Section 12 of PEPA, 1997, and considering the persistent nature of non-compliance and the potential harm caused to the environment, a penalty of **PKR 5,000,000/- (Rupees Five Million only)** is hereby imposed on M/s Nishat Mills Limited. This penalty shall be deposited into the Punjab Environmental Protection Fund within **thirty (30) days** from the date of this judgment.
5.  **Cost of Proceedings**: The Respondent shall also bear the costs of these proceedings, assessed at **PKR 100,000/- (Rupees One Hundred Thousand only)**, to be paid to the Complainant (EPA) within **thirty (30) days**.

**IV. TRIBUNAL'S ANALYSIS**

This Tribunal observes that the Respondent has been served with an Environmental Protection Order (EPO) under Section 16 of PEPA, 1997 and was granted adequate time to achieve compliance. The persistence of NEQS violations after expiry of the EPO compliance period demonstrates willful non-compliance and disentitles the Respondent from any lenient treatment. The defense of temporary malfunction is rejected as an afterthought unsupported by contemporaneous records.

**V. LEGAL FRAMEWORK**

The jurisdiction of this Tribunal is established under Section 21 of PEPA, 1997. The complaint has been filed in the prescribed manner and the Respondent has been afforded full opportunity to be heard. Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997 empowers this Tribunal to impose a penalty of up to PKR 1,000,000 for a first violation, with an additional PKR 100,000 per day for each day the violation continues.

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 1,500,000/- (Rupees One Million Five Hundred Thousand only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 300,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
