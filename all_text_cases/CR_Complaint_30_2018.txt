This judgment addresses the complaint filed by the Assistant Director, Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Lahore, against M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, alleging persistent violations of environmental laws and standards. The Tribunal is seized of the matter under the provisions of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997 (hereinafter referred to as "PEPA, 1997") to adjudicate upon the alleged contraventions and issue appropriate orders for environmental protection and compliance.
The Complainant, through its representative
, Mr. Asif Ali, Assistant Director, EPA, presented a detailed account of the Respondent's alleged environmental transgressions. It was contended that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited had consistently failed to comply with the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge, as prescribed under the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its associated rules and regulations
. The Complainant further substantiated its claims by presenting multiple inspection reports, laboratory analyses of effluent samples collected from the Respondent's premises, and copies of several Environmental Protection Orders (EPOs) and show-cause notices issued to M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited. These documents, it was argued, consistently demonstrated the Respondent's failure to meet the prescribed NEQS for various parameters, including but not limited to Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD), Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and pH levels. The Complainant highlighted that despite repeated warnings, opportunities for compliance, and the issuance of statutory directions, the Respondent had failed to implement effective pollution control measures, thereby posing a continuous threat to public health and the environment, including the contamination of water bodies in the vicinity.
In response, Mr. Tariq Mehmood, the learned Legal Counsel representing M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, categorically denied the allegations of persistent non-compliance. He contended that the Respondent company had made significant investments in upgrading its effluent treatment plant (ETP) and had undertaken various measures to improve its environmental performance. While acknowledging certain occasional deviations from NEQS, he argued that these were isolated incidents, often attributable to technical glitches or unforeseen operational challenges, rather than a deliberate disregard for environmental laws. The Respondent's counsel presented evidence of capital expenditure on new machinery and pollution control technologies, asserting that the company was genuinely committed to achieving full compliance. He further requested the Tribunal to consider the economic implications of any stringent orders and to grant the Respondent reasonable time to fully stabilize its advanced ETP operations.
The Tribunal conducted multiple hearings, during which both parties were afforded full opportunity to present their arguments, adduce evidence, and cross-examine witnesses. Expert opinions were also solicited and considered. After a thorough review of the entire record, including the inspection reports, laboratory analyses, the history of EPOs issued by the EPA, and the Respondent's submissions regarding its efforts and investments, the Tribunal has reached its findings.
**FINDINGS OF THE TRIBUNAL:**
The Tribunal finds that the Complainant, the Assistant Director, EPA, Lahore, has successfully established, through cogent and overwhelming documentary evidence, that M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited has been in persistent violation of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge, as mandated by the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, and its subsidiary rules and regulations. The consistent exceedance of critical parameters in the effluent samples, as evidenced by the laboratory reports, coupled with the Respondent's failure to achieve sustained compliance despite the issuance of multiple Environmental Protection Orders and show-cause notices, clearly demonstrates a pattern of non-adherence to environmental laws. While the Tribunal acknowledges the Respondent's claims of investment in pollution control technologies, the actual on-ground performance, as reflected in the continuous monitoring data, indicates that these measures have not yet resulted in consistent compliance with NEQS. The Respondent's explanations for non-compliance, while noted, do not sufficiently mitigate the gravity of the persistent environmental transgressions.
**FINAL ORDER AND PENALTY:**
In light of the foregoing findings, and exercising the powers vested in this Tribunal under Section 17 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, it is hereby ordered as follows:
1.  **Declaration of Guilt:** M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited is hereby found guilty of persistent violations of the National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge, as alleged by the Complainant.
2.  **Immediate Compliance:** The Respondent, M/s Ittehad Chemicals Limited, is directed to forthwith ensure full and continuous compliance with all applicable National Environmental Quality Standards (NEQS) for industrial effluent discharge

**FINAL ORDER**

Having found the Respondent, the Respondent, guilty of violating the National Environmental Quality Standards under Section 11 of the Pakistan Environmental Protection Act, 1997, this Tribunal hereby passes the following order:

**1. Declaration of Guilt**

The Respondent, the Respondent, is hereby declared guilty of violating NEQS standards under Section 11 of PEPA, 1997, read with applicable NEQS Regulations (SRO 742(I)/2000).

**2. Penalty**

The Respondent is directed to pay a penalty of **PKR 2,000,000/- (Rupees Two Million only)** under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997, payable to the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency within thirty (30) days of this order.

**3. Environmental Charges (PSDF)**

The Respondent shall additionally deposit **PKR 400,000/-** into the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) under the Punjab Environmental Protection (Provincial Sustainable Development Fund) Rules, 2010.

**4. Compliance Directive**

The Respondent is directed to achieve full compliance with NEQS standards within sixty (60) days of this order and to submit documentary evidence of compliance to the Punjab EPA.

**5. Monthly Compliance Reports**

The Respondent shall submit monthly compliance reports to the Punjab EPA for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this order.

**6. Daily Fine for Non-Compliance**

In the event of failure to comply within the stipulated period, the Respondent shall be liable to pay a daily fine of **PKR 100,000/-** per day of continued non-compliance under Section 17(1) of PEPA, 1997.

**7. EPA Monitoring**

The Punjab EPA is directed to conduct monthly inspection of the Respondent's facility for one (1) year and report findings to this Tribunal.

**8. Costs**

The Respondent shall bear costs assessed at **PKR 50,000/-**, payable to the Punjab EPA.

**9. Right of Appeal**

Any party aggrieved by this order may prefer an appeal before the Lahore High Court under Section 22 of PEPA, 1997 within thirty (30) days of receipt of this order.
